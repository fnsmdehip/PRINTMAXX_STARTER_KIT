- Bebas Neue (LARGE HEADLINE) + Sacramento (smaller accent)
- Works for: Faith, women's apparel, inspirational quotes

**Pairing 2: Modern + Bold**
- Montserrat Bold (ALL CAPS main text) + Montserrat Light (subtitle)

- Sacramento or Pacifico (main phrase in script) + Playfair Display (small supporting text)
- Works for: Faith, gifts for women, wellness

## PLACEMENT GUIDE (Garment Location)

### Standard Print Placement Options

| Placement | Size | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Left chest | 3.5" × 3.5" | Subtle, professional, solopreneur |
| Center chest | 12" × 12" | Full statement, most common |
| Full front | 14" × 18" | Bold all-over impact |
| Back center | 12" × 14" | Second placement (add $3-5 to price) |
| Sleeve | 3" × 10" | Premium feel, charges more |
| Oversized front | 16" × 20" | Streetwear aesthetic |

**Printify placement specs:**
- Recommended resolution: 300 DPI minimum
- File format: PNG with transparent background
- Maximum file size: 200MB (rarely hit this)
- Color mode: sRGB (NOT CMYK — POD printers use RGB)
- For dark garments: PNG with white background sometimes needed for DTG

**Most common placement:** Center chest, 12" wide. This is the default that sells.

### Placement Psychology

**Left chest only:** Signals premium/brand. Good for solopreneur/founder identity designs. Feels like a "real" brand vs. a graphic tee.

**Center chest, large:** Signals statement/identity. Prayer warrior, mental health, dog breed — these all work as full-chest statements.

**Back print:** Usually a secondary placement. "Front + back" listing can justify $5-8 higher price point. Faith designs: Front = "Faith Over Fear", Back = scripture reference in small text.

## MOCKUP STRATEGY

### Why Mockups Matter More Than You Think

Etsy conversion rate data: Listings with 5+ lifestyle mockups convert 30-40% better than product-only mockups.

Buyers don't imagine themselves in the product — they need to SEE it on someone like them.

### Image Strategy: 8 Images per Listing

**Image 1 (Hero/Thumbnail):** Flat lay or model front-facing. Clean background. Shows full design clearly.
**Image 2:** Model wearing the product — realistic body type for your niche
**Image 3:** Close-up of print (shows quality, resolution, detail)
**Image 4:** Back view or secondary placement
**Image 5:** Size guide (prevents negative reviews from sizing issues)
**Image 6:** Lifestyle shot — product in context (coffee mug + coffee shop, tee + casual outdoor)
**Image 7:** Variation options (color swatches, multiple product types)
**Image 8:** Care instructions + brand note (builds trust)

### Mockup Sources

**Free mockups:**
- Printify built-in: Functional, gets the job done, looks slightly generic
- Freepik.com: Search "t-shirt mockup" — 100s of free options
- Mockup World: Free PSD mockups (need Photoshop/GIMP)

**Paid mockups (worth it for top designs):**
- Placeit ($14.99/mo): 10,000+ mockups, models in real environments, lifestyle scenes
- Smartmockups ($9/mo): Clean, high-quality, easier UI than Placeit
- Creative Market: One-time purchase bundles ($20-50 for 50 mockups)

**When to invest in Placeit:** Once a design gets its first 5 organic sales. Upgrade its mockups to premium lifestyle images → watch conversion rate jump.

### Mockup Sizing Requirements

| Platform | Recommended Size | File Format |
|---|---|---|
| Etsy thumbnail | 2000 × 2000px | JPG or PNG |
| Etsy listing images | 2000 × 2000px min | JPG or PNG |
| Pinterest pin | 1000 × 1500px | JPG or PNG |
| TikTok/IG square | 1080 × 1080px | JPG |
| TikTok/IG story | 1080 × 1920px | JPG |

## DESIGN PRODUCTION WORKFLOW

### Batch Design Process (10 Designs in 1 Day)

**Morning block (3 hours): Design production**
1. Open Canva Pro (or Kittl for badge/vintage designs)
2. Create Master Template: artboard at 4500 × 5400px (standard print size)
3. Design 1 concept from scratch — takes ~45 min including variations
4. Once template structure is locked, batch-customize for remaining designs
5. Key insight: Designs 2-10 that reuse the same template structure = 10-15 min each
6. Export all as PNG, transparent background, 300 DPI

**Afternoon block (2 hours): Listing creation**
1. Open Printify → Products → Add new
2. Select product (Bella Canvas 3001 or Gildan 18500)
3. Upload design to print area
4. Generate mockups (select 3-4 from Printify's generator)
5. Add to Etsy — Printify auto-populates basic listing
6. Edit: Title (SEO-optimized), Description, Tags (all 13), Category, Price
7. Publish

**End of day: Pinterest + social**
1. Export mockup images from Printify/Placeit
2. Create 2-3 Pinterest pins per design (resize in Canva)
3. Schedule pins in Tailwind for the week
4. Post 1 TikTok/IG Reel showing design process

**Daily targets:**
- Solo: 2-3 new listings/day consistently = 60-90 listings in 30 days
- With virtual assistant: 5-8 listings/day = 150-240 listings in 30 days

## COLOR STRATEGY

### Color Psychology for POD Niches

| Niche | Primary Colors | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Faith/Christian | Navy, forest green, burgundy, gold, dusty rose | Neon, garish |
| Solopreneur/Tech | Black, white, electric blue, terminal green | Pastels |
| Mental health | Lavender, sage, dusty blue, terracotta | Stark red/yellow |
| Military/Veteran | OD green, coyote tan, navy, black, red/white/blue | Pastels |
| Dog breeds | Match breed colors + neutral | All white (shows dirt) |
| Outdoor/hiking | Forest green, burnt orange, slate, cream | Pastels, neon |
| Meme/funny | Bright primary colors, black/white | Muted earth tones |

### Garment Color Recommendations by Design

**Dark designs (white/light text):** Use black, navy, forest green, burgundy garments. DTG print on dark = $1.50-2.00 more per item from most suppliers.

**Light designs (dark text):** Use white, cream, sport grey garments. Cheapest to produce. Best for Printify's base pricing.

**Multi-color designs:** Keep to 4 colors max for DTG. More colors = more ink = more variables = more inconsistency between batches.

**Premium garment colors that sell well:**
- "Heather grey" — universally popular, hides print inconsistencies
- "Natural" or "cream" — currently trendy, aesthetic appeal
- "Slate" or "storm" — modern alternative to black/grey
- "Cardinal red" — faith, patriot, medical niches

### Seasonal Color Trends
- Spring/Summer: Pastels, coral, sage, lavender
- Fall/Winter: Burnt orange, forest green, burgundy, mustard
- Always evergreen: Black/white, navy/white, forest/cream
- Ramadan season: Emerald, gold, teal
- Christmas: Red/green (obvious), but also "winter white" / "pine" for premium feel
