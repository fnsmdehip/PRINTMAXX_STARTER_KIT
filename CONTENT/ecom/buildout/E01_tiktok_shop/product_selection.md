# E01 TikTok Shop — Product Selection System

## What Makes a TikTok Shop Winner

Five filters. In this order. Product fails any one = skip.

1. **Demo-able in 30 seconds** — you can show the problem and solution visually in a single clip
2. **Wow factor** — comment section should fill with "where do I get this??" reactions
3. **$10-50 retail price** — impulse buy territory, low return rate, fast payment hold release
4. **3x-5x margin minimum** — COGS under $12 for a $40 product after all fees
5. **Reorderable** — same supplier, consistent quality, reshipable inventory

## Product Research SOP (Weekly Routine)

### Step 1 — Mine TikTok Creative Center (15 min)
1. Go to TikTok Creative Center → Products → Trending
2. Filter: US region, last 7 days, sort by GMV growth
3. Note any products with >50% week-over-week GMV increase
4. Cross-reference with Kalodata to verify GMV is real

### Step 2 — Scan Competitor Stores (20 min)
1. In Kalodata → Stores → filter by category and GMV $50K-500K/mo
2. These are mid-tier operators making real money without being saturated
3. Look at their product lineup — what's driving 80% of their revenue?
4. Find their suppliers via CJ Dropshipping reverse image search

### Step 3 — Hashtag Trend Check (10 min)
Check these hashtags weekly for emerging products:
- #TikTokMadeMeBuyIt (always active)
- #cleantok (cleaning gadgets)
- #amazonfind (cross-platform signal)
- #gadget #kitchengadget #homehack
- #[yourcategory]tok

### Step 4 — Margin Calculation (5 min per candidate)

```
Gross Margin = (Retail Price - COGS - Shipping - TikTok Fee - Return Reserve) / Retail Price

TikTok platform fee: 2-8% (category dependent)
Shipping cost: $3-6 per unit (CJ standard)
Return reserve: ~5% of revenue
Payment hold: 30 days post-delivery

Example:
Product sells for $35
COGS: $7
Shipping: $4.50
TikTok fee (5%): $1.75
Return reserve (5%): $1.75
Net profit: $20
Net margin: 57%
```

### Step 5 — Virality Score Assessment

Score each candidate 1-5 on these dimensions:
- Visual demo potential (does it look impressive in 15 seconds?)
- Problem clarity (does everyone immediately understand the problem it solves?)
- Affordable enough to impulse-buy without thinking
- Shareable moment (would someone send this to a friend?)
- Trend alignment (fit with current viral aesthetics)

Only launch products scoring 18+ out of 25.

## Winning Product Archetypes (Current Winners as of Q1 2026)

### The "Why Didn't This Exist Before" Product
Problem everyone has. Solution that feels obvious in hindsight.
Examples: silicone sink strainer, desk cable organizer, pet hair remover lint roller

### The Transformation Demo
Before/after where the transformation is dramatic in 10 seconds.
Examples: rust remover paste, grout cleaning brush, wrinkle spray

### The Satisfying Process
Product used in a way that's ASMR-level satisfying to watch.
Examples: food slicers, organizer sets, compression vacuum bags

### The "I Have That Problem" Hook
Opens with a relatable pain point. 80% of viewers nod along.
Examples: tangled earbuds, sticky phone screen, dull kitchen knives

## Validation Before Full Inventory Commitment

1. Order 20-30 units from supplier
2. Create 3 test videos (different hooks, same product)
3. Run TikTok ads at $20/day for 3 days — target: $1 CPC or lower
4. Track: add-to-cart rate, checkout rate, return rate
5. If ROAS >2 on cold traffic: scale to 100 units, recruit 5 affiliates
6. If ROAS 1-2: test new hooks before scaling
7. If ROAS <1: kill it, move to next candidate

---

## Kill Signals — When to Drop a Product

- Return rate >8% — quality or expectation mismatch
- Comment section negative >30% — stop immediately
- Supplier out of stock >2 weeks — find alternative or pause
- GMV declining >20% week-over-week — trend is dying
- Major competitor enters with 100K+ followers — reassess margin viability
