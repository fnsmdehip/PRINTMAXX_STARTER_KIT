# E01 TikTok Shop — Product Demo Content Templates

## The TikTok Shop Content Formula

TikTok Shop videos that convert follow a tight pattern:
- Hook (0-3 seconds): Stop the scroll
- Problem (3-8 seconds): Make them feel the pain
- Demo (8-25 seconds): Show the transformation
- Result (25-30 seconds): Prove it works
- CTA (last 3 seconds): Link in bio / tap the bag

Videos should NOT look like ads. They should look like organic content that happens to have a product in it.

---

## Template 1: The Problem/Solution Demo (Best Converter)

**Best for:** Cleaning tools, kitchen gadgets, organizational products

**Script structure:**

```
HOOK (text overlay + voiceover):
"[Common problem statement] — I found the fix"

PROBLEM SETUP (3-5 seconds):
Show the messy/broken/frustrating thing. Let it breathe.
No talking. Just the problem visible. Viewer goes "I have that."

PRODUCT INTRO (2 seconds):
Casual reveal — "found this on TikTok Shop, had to try it"
Hold product up briefly

DEMO (10-15 seconds):
Use the product. Show it working. Real time or 2x speed.
No cuts that make it look fake.
Show the transformation happening.

RESULT (5 seconds):
Clean, organized, fixed. Wide shot. Let result speak.

CTA (3 seconds):
"link in my bio" OR "tap the bag to get it"
Show price naturally — "was only $[price]"
```

**Example (desk cable organizer, $24.99):**

```
HOOK: "my desk looked like a disaster zone until I got this"
[show tangled cables under desk, chaos visible]
PROBLEM: 3 seconds of the cable mess
INTRO: "this cable organizer from TikTok Shop — $25"
DEMO: Installing it, routing cables, 2x speed
RESULT: Clean desk, cables hidden, satisfying shot
CTA: "link in bio, game changer if you WFH"
```

## Template 2: The Transformation Before/After

**Best for:** Beauty tools, skincare, home improvement products

**Script:**

```
HOOK (text overlay):
"POV: [relatable situation that sets up transformation]"

BEFORE (4-5 seconds):
Show the before state. No product visible yet.

TRANSITION:
Jump cut or wipe to product. "used this for 30 days"

AFTER (5-7 seconds):
Show the result. Let it be dramatic. Music drop.

PROOF (5 seconds):
Real detail shots — not filtered. Close up.

PRODUCT SHOT + CTA (4 seconds):
Hold up product
"everything linked in bio"
```

**Example (LED facial wand, $39.99):**

```
HOOK: "POV: you've been sleeping on this for years"
BEFORE: Dull skin, uneven tone, no filter
TRANSITION: "30 days with this"
AFTER: Noticeably brighter, glowing — real lighting, no filter
PROOF: Side-by-side still in video
PRODUCT SHOT: Hold up wand
CTA: "honest review — $40 on TikTok Shop"
```

## Template 3: The Educational "Did You Know" Hook

**Best for:** Products solving problems people don't know they have

**Script:**

```
HOOK (first 2 seconds text + voiceover):
"did you know [surprising fact about common problem]?"

CONTEXT (5 seconds):
Briefly explain why this matters to viewer.
Stats or relatable moment.

SOLUTION INTRO (3 seconds):
"this is why I got [product]"

DEMO (12-15 seconds):
Full product demo. Every key feature.

Comparison moment (optional, 5 seconds):
"before I had this I was [old method]"

CTA (3 seconds):
"gets the job done for $[price], link in bio"
```

**Example (air purifier with UV, $45.99):**

```
HOOK: "did you know indoor air is 5x more polluted than outdoor air?"
CONTEXT: "dust mites, pet dander, cooking fumes — your lungs are filtering all of it"
SOLUTION: "ran this for 2 weeks and look at the filter"
DEMO: Show filter before (clean) and after (visibly grey)
CTA: "changed it every month. $46 on TikTok Shop, link in bio"
```

## Template 4: The Honest Review (High Trust, Lower Volume)

**Best for:** Higher-priced items ($40-80), skeptical buyers

**Script:**

```
HOOK: "real talk about [product type] — [counterintuitive claim]"

SETUP (5 seconds):
"I bought this thinking [expectation]"
"here's what actually happened"

Pros walkthrough (10 seconds):
3 specific things that work well
Real demos of each

Cons walkthrough (5 seconds):
1-2 honest things you'd improve
This builds trust — ads never say cons

Verdict (5 seconds):
"worth it for [specific person/use case], not for [who it's not for]"

CTA (3 seconds):
"linked it — decide for yourself"
```

**Example (standing desk converter, $65):**

```
HOOK: "real talk about standing desks — they're not for everyone"
SETUP: "bought this expecting to stand all day. that was naive."
PROS: "wobble is minimal at sitting height. cable management built in. assembles in 15 min."
CONS: "at full standing height, slight wobble. not for monitors heavier than 20lbs."
VERDICT: "if you sit 6 hours a day and want to break it up — yes. if you want full standing workstation — skip it"
CTA: "link in bio, $65, still using it 3 months later"
```

## Template 5: The Satisfying Process Video

**Best for:** Organizational products, cleaning tools, anything with a satisfying transformation

**Script:** Mostly visual, minimal narration

```
Opening shot:
Messy/cluttered/dirty state. No words.

Process (20-25 seconds):
Use the product. Slow enough to be satisfying.
ASMR-style sounds if applicable.
No explanation needed — the demo IS the content.

Final shot (5 seconds):
The organized/clean result. Satisfying wide shot.
Text overlay: "POV: your [space] after [product]"
Small text: "[price] on TikTok Shop"

CTA:
"link in bio" text overlay fades in
No voiceover needed
```

## Affiliate Creator Brief Template

When you recruit creators to promote your product, send this brief:

```
PRODUCT: [name + link]
PRICE: $[X]
YOUR COMMISSION: [X]% per sale ($[dollar amount] per sale)
FREE SAMPLE: Yes / No (select up front whether you're sending samples)

WHAT WORKS:
- Show the problem first (3-5 seconds of pain)
- Real unfiltered demo — no filters on the transformation
- Mention the price naturally in conversation, not as an ad
- Say "link in bio" or "tap the bag" at end

WHAT TO AVOID:
- Scripted-sounding reads
- Fake reactions
- Hiding that it's paid (must disclose per FTC)

BEST PERFORMING HOOKS (pick one):
- "I bought this so you don't have to"
- "you don't realize you need this until you have it"
- "[problem] — this fixed it in [timeframe]"
- "honest review of [product] after [time period] of use"

TARGET LENGTH: 30-45 seconds

SHOOT FORMAT: Vertical 9:16, good natural lighting preferred
```

## Comment Response Templates

Responding to comments within the first hour is key for the TikTok algorithm.

**"Where do I get this?"**
"link in my bio! also if you tap the little bag icon you can get it right on TikTok"

**"Does this really work?"**
"used it for [X time] — [specific result]. not gonna lie it surprised me"

**"How much is it?"**
"$[price] — linked in bio. cheaper than I expected honestly"

**"How long did shipping take?"**
"got mine in [X] days. pretty fast"

**Negative comment:**
"fair point — works better for [specific use case]. if you're [different situation] probably not worth it"

Engaging with negative comments publicly builds more trust than ignoring them.
