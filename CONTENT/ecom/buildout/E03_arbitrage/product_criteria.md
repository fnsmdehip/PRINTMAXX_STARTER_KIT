# E03 Ecom Arbitrage — Product Selection Criteria

## The 6-Filter Gate System

Every product must pass ALL 6 gates before you buy. Fail one = skip.

```
Gate 1: Margin Gate        → ROI ≥ 30%, Net profit ≥ $3.00/unit
Gate 2: Velocity Gate      → BSR < 200,000 in main category
Gate 3: Competition Gate   → No Amazon as seller (or price > your cost + fees)
Gate 4: Risk Gate          → Not IP-gated, not hazmat, not restricted
Gate 5: Trend Gate         → BSR not crashing, price not crashing
Gate 6: Volume Gate        → Enough units available to make it worth the buy
```

---

## Gate 1: Margin Requirements

### Minimums (non-negotiable)

| Metric | Minimum | Target | Excellent |
|---|---|---|---|
| ROI % | 30% | 40-60% | 80%+ |
| Net profit/unit | $3.00 | $5-10 | $15+ |
| Monthly profit potential | $100 | $300+ | $1,000+ |

### By category risk level

| Risk Level | Min ROI | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Low (stable prices, slow-moving) | 30% | Safe to buy in volume |
| Medium (moderate competition) | 40% | Buffer for price compression |
| High (seasonal, trendy, perishable) | 50%+ | You need the extra margin for risk |
| Very high (electronics, clothing) | 60%+ | Returns, rapid price drops, IP risk |

## Gate 2: Sales Velocity (BSR)

BSR = Best Seller Rank. Lower = faster selling.

### Category BSR Benchmarks

| BSR Range | Approx Monthly Sales | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Top 1,000 | 5,000-50,000+ units | Very fast, high competition, price wars |
| 1,001 - 10,000 | 500-5,000 | Fast mover, good balance |
| 10,001 - 50,000 | 100-500 | Moderate, solid reliable seller |
| 50,001 - 100,000 | 30-100 | Slow but still viable at 30%+ ROI |
| 100,001 - 200,000 | 10-30 | Borderline — only buy if very high margin |
| 200,001 - 500,000 | 1-10 | Skip — capital too tied up |
| 500,001+ | < 1/month | Hard no |

### Category-Specific BSR Cutoffs

Different categories have different scale:

| Category | Max BSR (Pass) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Toys & Games | 100,000 | Huge category, BSR 200K still dead |
| Sports & Outdoors | 100,000 | |
| Kitchen & Home | 100,000 | |
| Health & Beauty | 75,000 | Faster turnover expected |
| Electronics | 50,000 | Fast or nothing |
| Books | 500,000 | Books sell much slower per rank |
| Office Products | 75,000 | |
| Grocery | 50,000 | |
| Baby | 75,000 | |

**Critical: Check 90-day BSR average, not just current BSR.**

A product with current BSR 30,000 that averaged 8,000 three months ago is CRASHING.
A product with current BSR 80,000 that averaged 150,000 is IMPROVING.

## Gate 3: Competition Analysis

### Who Is Selling This Item?

**Check with Keepa or RevSeller:**

1. **Is Amazon (the brand/retailer) actively selling?**
   - Amazon selling = they will ALWAYS undercut you
   - Amazon goes in/out of stock = monitor, sometimes you can sell in the gap
   - If Amazon is consistently selling at a price that leaves you 30%+ ROI: very rare, be suspicious

2. **How many other FBA sellers?**

| FBA Seller Count | Risk | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1-3 | Low | Good, limited competition |
| 4-8 | Medium | Watch for price spiral, buy small first |
| 9-15 | High | Price war likely, need 50%+ ROI to enter |
| 16+ | Very High | Skip unless BSR is extremely low and price stable |

3. **Seller count trend (Keepa):**
   - Sellers increasing month-over-month = product being flooded, avoid
   - Sellers stable for 3+ months = safe category
   - Sellers decreasing = good sign, others giving up

4. **Buy Box percentage:**
   - If one seller has 80%+ of Buy Box → they're likely the brand/manufacturer → IP complaint risk
   - Shared Buy Box rotation among 3-6 sellers = healthy competition

### IP and Brand Risk

**High-risk brands (avoid entirely for arbitrage):**

- Nike, Adidas (aggressively IP-protected)
- Apple, Sony, Bose (electronics with authorized dealer requirements)
- LEGO (sometimes, check per set)
- Disney, Warner Bros (licensed merchandise)
- Many supplement brands (proprietary formulas, fake complaint abuse)
- Most direct-to-consumer brands launched post-2020 (they enforce hard)

**Low-risk for arbitrage:**
- Generic household brands (Rubbermaid, Sterilite)
- Store brands (Great Value, Up&Up) — actually can't sell these
- Established CPG (Procter & Gamble, Unilever products sold at retail)
- Books, board games, toys from established brands

**Check before buying: Amazon IP Accelerator page + Brand Registry lookup**
Just search "[brand name] MAP policy" or "[brand name] authorized sellers" to see how tight they are.

## Gate 4: Restricted + Hazmat + Gated Categories

### Automatically Gated Categories (need approval)

| Category | Gating Level | How to Get Approved |
|---|---|---|
| Grocery & Food | Category approval needed | Submit invoices from distributor |
| Health & Beauty | Some subcategories | Invoice from authorized supplier |
| Jewelry | Category gate | More complex, photos required |
| Fine Art | Full gate | Not for arb |
| Automotive | Not gated but complex | |
| Video Games | New products only gated | |
| Collectible Coins | High gate | Not for arb |

**Check if you're gated:** Add item to Seller Central → see if it shows "Apply to sell"

### Hazmat (FBA will reject)

**These items cannot go to FBA without hazmat certification:**
- Lithium batteries (standalone)
- Aerosols, spray cans
- Flammable liquids (nail polish, paint thinner)
- Certain cleaning products with bleach
- Most pesticides

**Hazmat doesn't mean skip:** You can sell hazmat items FBM (you ship), just not FBA.

### Check ASIN Restrictions Before Buying

In Seller Central: Inventory → Add a Product → enter ASIN → if "Apply to Sell" button appears, you're restricted.

Better: Use **OAXray** or **RevSeller** — they check your account's approval status in real-time during scanning.

## Gate 5: Price and BSR Trend Analysis

### Price Health Check (Keepa 90-day chart)

**GREEN LIGHT patterns:**
- Price stable or trending up slightly over 90 days
- Price dips during sale events but recovers
- BSR improving (lower) over time
- Amazon price consistently above your cost + 40% ROI threshold

**YELLOW LIGHT — investigate more:**
- Price dropped 15-25% in last 30 days (possible new entrant)
- BSR degrading 20-30% (might be seasonal)
- Multiple price spikes up and down (volatile, unpredictable)

**RED LIGHT — skip:**
- Price fell 30%+ in 30 days (collapsing)
- BSR going from 50K to 300K = product dying
- Heavy price variance week-to-week (race to bottom happening)
- Seasonal product outside its season (buy in Oct, not Dec, for Christmas items)

### Seasonal Awareness Calendar

| Month | Buy For | Avoid Buying |
|---|---|---|
| Jan | Valentine's Day (Feb 14), tax season prep | Christmas leftover at high prices |
| Feb | Easter (early-mid April) | Valentine's Day items |
| Mar | Spring/Garden season | Easter already bought |
| Apr | Mother's Day (early May), graduation | Spring items past peak |
| May | Summer, Father's Day (June) | Mother's Day leftover |
| Jun | Back to School (Jul-Aug), 4th of July | Father's Day leftover |
| Jul | Back to School, early Halloween | Summer at peak |
| Aug | Halloween (Oct 31) | Back to School near peak |
| Sep | Christmas prep, Thanksgiving | Halloween already bought |
| Oct | Christmas (best window), Cyber Monday deals to resell | |
| Nov | Post-holiday clearance buys for next year | Anything at full pre-holiday prices |
| Dec | Post-Christmas clearance (sell next year) | Most items — prices inflated |

## Gate 6: Volume Availability

You need enough units to make the buy worthwhile.

### Minimum Purchase Thresholds

| Net Profit/Unit | Min Units to Buy | Why |
|---|---|---|
| $3-5 | 20 units | Need $60-100 profit minimum per sourcing trip |
| $5-10 | 10 units | $50-100 minimum total profit |
| $10-20 | 5 units | Still worth $50-100 total |
| $20+ | 1-2 units | Even 1 is worth it |

**Avoid:** Buying 3 units at $3 profit each = $9 total. Not worth the time, FBA fees, and administrative overhead.

### Unit Availability Check

**Online arb:**
- Check if item has "Add to Cart" limit restrictions
- Check if multiple fulfillment methods available (ship, pickup, etc.)
- Check if other store locations have stock (use BrickSeek to see nearby store quantities)
- Buy in one transaction — placing 50 separate orders flags retailer accounts

**In-store arb:**
- Never clear out an entire shelf (leaves evidence, gets you banned)
- Leave 20-30% on shelf
- Buy across multiple store locations
- Space out visits (don't hit same store weekly)

## Winning Product Archetypes

### Archetype 1: The Forgotten Basic

**Description:** Boring, unglamorous item with steady demand — cleaning supplies, batteries, basic kitchen tools. Nobody's excited about it, so competition is lower.

**Characteristics:**
- BSR 20K-100K, stable for 12+ months
- 3-8 FBA sellers
- No brand enforcement
- 35-50% ROI consistently available

**Example categories:** Cleaning products, pantry basics, pet supplies, office supplies

---

### Archetype 2: The Clearance Window Play

**Description:** Major retailer puts something on 50-70% clearance that still sells fine on Amazon. You're not buying for long-term resale — you're buying a specific batch, flipping quickly.

**Characteristics:**
- BSR 10K-50K on Amazon
- Buy within 48 hours of clearance discovery (others are scanning too)
- Limited time opportunity, not a "replens" play
- ROI often 60-150% because clearance is deep

**Example:** Target marks 2024 Stanley cups to $7.49 at clearance. Amazon price: $22.99. That's $10+ profit per unit. Buy everything in stock across 5 local stores.

---

### Archetype 3: The Replens Play

**Description:** An item you can reliably source at the same source for the same price repeatedly. Not a one-time clearance — a permanent spread.

**Characteristics:**
- Source price is structural (member pricing, store brand pricing)
- Amazon price stays elevated because competitors haven't found the source
- You buy monthly, or however fast it sells

**Example:** Costco member pricing on a specialty supplement. $18.99 at Costco, $34.99 on Amazon. Sell-through in 2 weeks. This is a replens play — restock every 3 weeks.

---

### Archetype 4: The Near-Amazon-Out-of-Stock Play

**Description:** Amazon (the retailer) sells this item but keeps going out of stock. When they're OOS, 3P sellers clean up at higher prices.

**How to catch it:**
- Keepa alert: Set alert for Amazon = "Out of Stock"
- When Amazon goes OOS, your listing wins Buy Box at premium pricing
- Restock fast before Amazon comes back

**Risk:** Amazon restocks unpredictably. Don't get caught with 50 units when Amazon comes back at half your price.

## Products to Avoid Entirely

| Category | Reason |
|---|---|
| Clothing/Shoes | Size variation complications, 20%+ returns |
| Electronics with batteries | Hazmat complications + fast depreciation |
| Perfume/cologne | Hazmat + high IP risk |
| Pre-workout supplements | IP risk + changing formulas = authenticity questions |
| Baby formula | Gated category + recall risk + strict requirements |
| Anything Disney-licensed | IP enforcement is brutal |
| Single-use consumables sold in multipacks | Breaking apart multipacks = listing violations |
| Items with 100+ FBA sellers | Price war math never works |
| Melt/freeze products | Temperature-controlled shipping required |
| Anything with "As Seen on TV" | Usually brand-protected |

## Product Research Checklist (Before Every Buy)

```
[ ] ROI ≥ 30% AND net profit ≥ $3.00/unit
[ ] BSR < 200K in main category (check 90-day average)
[ ] Amazon NOT actively selling (or gap play confirmed)
[ ] FBA seller count < 15
[ ] Not gated for my account (RevSeller/OAXray check)
[ ] No hazmat flag
[ ] Not on brands-to-avoid list
[ ] Keepa 90-day price trend = stable or up
[ ] Keepa 90-day BSR trend = stable or improving
[ ] Enough units available to clear minimum profit threshold
[ ] Checked for IP complaints on brand (search "[brand] Amazon IP complaint")
[ ] Cashback portal checked for this retailer
[ ] Credit card category bonus checked
```

If all 12 boxes checked → buy.
Missing 1-2 boxes → investigate further.
Missing 3+ boxes → skip and move on.
