# Amazon Associates Strategy — Niche Selection, Content, Links

## Overview

Amazon Associates pays 1-10% commission depending on category. Traffic + conversion rate is the game.
At 2% average commission, 5% conversion, 3K visitors/mo per article: ~3K × 0.05 × avg $50 order × 0.02 = $150/mo per article.
At 30 articles, that's $4,500/mo. The math is slow but compounds.

## Content Strategy (What Actually Ranks)

### 3 Content Types That Convert

**1. Best [X] under $[price] round-ups**
- "Best standing desk mats under $50"
- "Best air fryers under $100"
- High purchase intent, specific price anchor
- Target: 1,500-2,500 words, 5-8 products

**2. [Product A] vs [Product B] comparisons**
- "Kindle Paperwhite vs Kindle Oasis"
- "Instant Pot vs Ninja Foodi"
- Catches buyers who are already comparing
- Target: 2,000-3,000 words, head-to-head table

**3. Best [Product Category] for [Specific Person/Use Case]**
- "Best running shoes for flat feet"
- "Best budget mechanical keyboards for typing"
- Hyper-specific intent = high conversion
- Target: 2,000 words, 6-10 products, one winner per sub-niche

### Content That Doesn't Convert (Avoid)
- "What is [product]" informational posts — no purchase intent
- Generic "gift guides" — too broad, no specificity
- Review posts for products with 50+ existing reviews — too competitive

## Keyword Research Workflow

### Step 1: Find the head term
Example: "standing desk mat"

### Step 2: Find modifiers that signal purchase intent
- best + [product]
- [product] + review
- [product] + vs + [product]
- [product] + under $[price]
- [product] + for + [specific use case]

### Step 3: Check volume and difficulty
Use: Ahrefs (paid), Ubersuggest (free), or search Google and check "People Also Ask"
Target: KD under 40, volume 500-5,000/mo

### Step 4: Check Amazon BSR (Best Seller Rank)
Products with BSR under 10,000 in their category sell well.
High BSR = proof of commercial intent. Products nobody buys = no affiliate sales.

## Article Templates

### Round-up Template
```
Title: Best [Product] in [Year]: I Tested [X] to Find the Winner
Word count: 1,800-2,500

Structure:
1. Quick answer (first 100 words — winner, runner-up, budget pick)
2. Comparison table (all products, 5 columns: product, price, best for, rating, buy link)
3. Full review per product (150-200 words each, specific pros/cons)
4. Who should buy each one
5. How we chose (testing methodology)
6. FAQ (3-5 questions, 2-3 sentences each)
```

### Comparison Template
```
Title: [Product A] vs [Product B]: Which One Is Actually Worth Buying?
Word count: 2,000-3,000

Structure:
1. Quick verdict (first 100 words — pick A if X, pick B if Y)
2. At-a-glance table (side-by-side specs + price + rating)
3. Design & Build
4. Performance (the thing the product actually does)
5. Features
6. Price & value
7. Verdict (specific recommendation with reasoning)
8. FAQ
```

## Link Placement Rules

### Where to Put Links
- **Product mention on first appearance** — link the product name
- **Buy button at end of each product review** — "Check price on Amazon"
- **Comparison table** — every product row has a link
- **FAQ answer** — link naturally when answering "where to buy"

### Link Format
- Use Amazon Associates SiteStripe link generator
- Links expire after 24 hours for cookies (conversions tracked for 24h after click)
- Use text links, not image links — text links convert better

### What to Avoid
- Don't put "affiliate link" or "sponsored" on every link — one disclosure at the top is enough (FTC compliant)
- Don't link to out-of-stock products — check quarterly
- Don't link to products with under 3.5 stars — converts poorly, hurts trust

### Link Maintenance Schedule
- Monthly: Check 5 random articles for broken/out-of-stock links
- Quarterly: Check all articles against current Amazon bestsellers — swap in better products
- Annually: Update all "[Year]" in titles and refresh prices

```

Top-level pages:
├── Home (category overview with top picks)
├── [Category 1]/ (e.g., /home-office/)
│   ├── best-standing-desk-mats/
│   ├── standing-desk-vs-sitting-desk/
│   └── best-ergonomic-chairs-under-500/
├── [Category 2]/ (e.g., /camping-gear/)
│   ├── best-lightweight-camping-chairs/
│   ├── yeti-vs-hydro-flask/
│   └── best-headlamps-for-hiking/
├── About (establishes credibility, discloses affiliate relationship)
└── Deals (curated Amazon deals — updates weekly)
```

### Internal Linking Rules
- Every article links to the category hub page
- Every category hub links to 5+ sub-articles
- Comparisons link to both individual product round-ups
- Round-ups link to comparison articles ("can't decide? see our [A] vs [B] comparison")

## Operations SOP

### Publishing Schedule
- Month 1-3: 2 articles/week (24 total)
- Month 4-6: 3 articles/week (36 more = 60 total)
- Month 7+: 2 articles/week + monthly update cycles on top performers

### Per-Article Checklist
- [ ] Keyword research done (volume + KD confirmed)
- [ ] Top 3 Google competitors read and beat on depth/specificity
- [ ] All Amazon products have 4+ stars with 100+ reviews
- [ ] All links working and in stock
- [ ] Comparison table complete
- [ ] Quick answer in first paragraph
- [ ] FTC disclosure at top
- [ ] Meta title ≤60 chars, meta description ≤160 chars
- [ ] Internal links: 2-3 to related articles on site
- [ ] Images: product images from Amazon PA-API or embedded Amazon product links (compliant)

### Compliance Rules (Amazon Associates TOS)
- NEVER say "click here to buy" — say "check current price" or "see on Amazon"
- NEVER show Amazon prices in your article (prices change, TOS violation to show stale price)
- ALWAYS say "prices are accurate as of the time of publication" near any price reference
- ALWAYS include FTC affiliate disclosure above the fold
- NEVER cloak Amazon affiliate links
- NEVER use Amazon images downloaded to your server — use Amazon's embed code or PA-API
