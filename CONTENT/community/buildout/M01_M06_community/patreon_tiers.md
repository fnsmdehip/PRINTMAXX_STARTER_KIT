# Patreon Tiers — PRINTMAXX

## Why Patreon (vs Substack Paid / Whop / Skool)

Patreon works when you have existing audience loyalty (YouTube, podcast, Twitter) and want to offer tiered patron support. 8% platform fee. Payout via Stripe.

**Best for:** content creators who already produce regular content. The community pays to support the creator + gets perks.

**Not ideal for:** pure info products or course-style communities (use Skool for those).

**Recommended use case:** run Patreon alongside YouTube/podcast as a patron support + behind-the-scenes layer.

## Launch Strategy

### Week 1: Pre-launch
- Announce to existing audience: "launching Patreon next week"
- Post a "founding member" tease: first 50 patrons get locked in at current price forever
- Collect emails of interested people (Beehiiv form or Twitter DM)

### Launch Day
- Email existing list: "Patreon is live. founding member spots open."
- Twitter thread: "here's exactly what you get and why i'm building this"
- Pin the Patreon link to your Twitter profile + Discord

### Month 1 target: 20-30 patrons

### Ongoing growth levers:
1. Every YouTube video outro: "support on Patreon" with specific benefit mention
2. Every podcast episode: "patron tier has the full breakdown"
3. Monthly income report post publicly (shows it works) → drives upgrades

## Patreon vs Alternatives (Decision Matrix)

| Platform | Best For | Fee | Payout |
|----------|----------|-----|--------|
| Patreon | Creator support + tiered perks | 8% | Stripe monthly |
| Substack | Newsletter + paid posts | 10% | Stripe monthly |
| Skool | Course + community bundle | $99/mo flat | Stripe monthly |
| Whop | Digital product community | 3% | Daily |
| Buy Me a Coffee | One-time tips + light memberships | 5% | Instant |

**Recommended stack:** Patreon ($5-35 support tiers) + Skool ($97+ course community) + Whop (product sales) = three different price points, three different buyer types.
