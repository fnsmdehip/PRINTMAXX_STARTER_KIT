## DELIVERABILITY CHECKLIST

getting into the inbox is the entire game. 50% of cold emails land in spam in 2026. follow this or your sequences are worthless.

### DNS Authentication (do this FIRST)

| Record | What It Does | Setup Time |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| **SPF** | tells servers which IPs can send from your domain | 10 min |
| **DKIM** | cryptographic signature proving email is legit | 15 min |
| **DMARC** | tells servers what to do with failed SPF/DKIM | 10 min |

**Steps:**
1. Buy a separate domain for cold email (never use your main domain)
   - Example: if your site is `acmedev.com`, buy `acmedev.io` or `tryacmedev.com`
   - Cost: $10-$15/year on Namecheap or Cloudflare
2. Set up Google Workspace or Outlook on the cold email domain ($6/month/mailbox)
3. Add SPF record in DNS: `v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all`
4. Enable DKIM in Google Workspace Admin > Apps > Gmail > Authenticate Email
5. Add DMARC record: `v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com`
6. Verify all 3 with [MXToolbox](https://mxtoolbox.com/SuperTool.aspx)

### Email Warmup (14-21 days minimum in 2026)

**Why:** New email domains have zero reputation. ISPs flag unknown senders. You MUST warm up before sending cold.

**Timeline:**
| Days | Daily Volume | What to Do |
|------|-------------|------------|
| 1-3 | 5-10 emails | Send to friends/team, get replies |
| 4-7 | 10-20 emails | Use warmup tool (auto-send + auto-reply) |
| 8-14 | 20-40 emails | Continue warmup + add 5-10 real cold emails |
| 15-21 | 40-60 emails | Ramp cold emails while keeping warmup running |
| 22+ | 30-80 cold emails/day | Full send. Keep warmup tool running forever. |

**Tools for warmup:**
| Tool | Cost | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Instantly.ai** | $97/month | Warmup + sending + analytics. Best all-in-one for cold email in 2026. |
| **DeliverOn** | $49/month | Pre-warmed inboxes ready to send Day 1. Saves 2-3 weeks. |
| **Smartlead** | $39/month | Warmup + rotation + unified inbox. Budget option. |
| **Lemwarm** (Lemlist) | $29/month | Warmup only. Pairs with any sending tool. |

**Recommendation:** Start with DeliverOn ($49/month) for pre-warmed inboxes if you want to skip the 14-21 day wait. Use Instantly ($97/month) for the sending + warmup combo once you scale past 3 mailboxes.

### Daily Send Limits

| Mailbox Age | Max Cold Emails/Day | Notes |
|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| < 14 days | 0-10 | Still warming up |
| 14-30 days | 30-50 | Ramp slowly |
| 30-60 days | 50-80 | Sweet spot |
| 60+ days | 80-100 | Max. Don't go higher per mailbox. |

**Scaling rule:** To send 200 cold emails/day, you need 3-4 mailboxes. To send 500/day, you need 7-10 mailboxes. Rotate across mailboxes using Instantly or Smartlead.

### Quick Deliverability Checks

- [ ] Separate domain for cold email (not main domain)
- [ ] SPF, DKIM, DMARC all pass on MXToolbox
- [ ] Warmup tool running for 14+ days before first cold send
- [ ] Under 80 cold emails per mailbox per day
- [ ] Custom tracking domain set up (not default Instantly/Smartlead tracking domain)
- [ ] No image-heavy emails (text-only preferred for cold)
- [ ] No more than 1 link per email (calendly link only)
- [ ] Unsubscribe line in every email (CAN-SPAM)
- [ ] Bounce rate under 3% (clean your list)
- [ ] Reply rate above 1% (below = check copy or list quality)

## A/B TESTING PLAN

test one variable at a time. run each test for minimum 100 sends per variant (200 total) before drawing conclusions.

### Phase 1: Subject Lines (Week 1-2)

**Test 1 - Dental:**
- A: `your practice loses $4,100/month to no-shows`
- B: `{{PRACTICE_NAME}} no-show rate`
- Hypothesis: personalization (B) will get higher open rate. pain point (A) will get higher reply rate.

**Test 2 - Legal:**
- A: `you're paying $200/lead. your competitor pays $40.`
- B: `{{FIRM_NAME}} lead cost`
- Hypothesis: competitive angle (A) wins on open rate.

**Test 3 - Fitness:**
- A: `50% of your members quit in 90 days`
- B: `{{STUDIO_NAME}} member retention`
- Hypothesis: stat-shock (A) wins on open rate.

**Metric:** Open rate. Winner = subject line with higher unique open rate after 200+ sends.

### Phase 2: Send Times (Week 3-4)

| Test | Time A | Time B | Industries |
|------|--------|--------|------------|
| Day of week | Tuesday 9am | Thursday 9am | All 3 |
| Time of day | 8:30am local | 2:00pm local | All 3 |
| Early week vs late | Monday 10am | Thursday 10am | All 3 |

**Hypothesis:**
- Dental: Tuesday/Wednesday 8-9am (before patient appointments start)
- Legal: Tuesday-Thursday 9-10am (after morning court if applicable)
- Fitness: Monday/Tuesday 10-11am (studios slow before afternoon classes)

**Metric:** Reply rate. Winner = time slot with higher reply rate after 100+ sends per slot.

### Phase 3: CTA Type (Week 5-6)

| Test | CTA A | CTA B | CTA C |
|------|-------|-------|-------|
| Dental Email 1 | "book a 10-min call" [CALENDLY] | "reply 'interested'" | "want a free mockup?" |
| Legal Email 1 | "book a 10-min call" [CALENDLY] | "reply 'show me'" | "free competitive audit" |
| Fitness Email 1 | "book a 10-min call" [CALENDLY] | "reply 'show me'" | "free retention audit" |

**Hypothesis:** Low-friction reply CTAs ("reply 'interested'") will outperform Calendly links 2-3x on reply rate. Calendly converts faster to calls. Test which produces more revenue, not just more replies.

**Metric:** Reply rate AND booked call rate. Winner = CTA that produces most booked calls (not just most replies).

### Phase 4: Email Length (Week 7-8)

Test short (50-75 words) vs current (75-150 words) for Email 1 across all 3 sequences.

**Short variant example (Dental):**
```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

dental practices lose 23% of appointments to no-shows. at $178/visit, that's $4,100/month gone.

we build booking systems that cut no-shows 40-60%. one-time cost. no monthly fees.

worth 10 minutes? [CALENDLY]

{{SENDER_NAME}}
```

**Hypothesis:** Shorter email wins on reply rate for dental and fitness. Legal may prefer slightly longer (credibility matters more in legal services).

### Ongoing Optimization Rules

1. **Kill losers fast.** If a variant has < 1% reply rate after 200 sends, kill it.
2. **Roll winners into all sequences.** Winning subject line pattern in dental? Test it in legal and fitness.
3. **Track revenue, not vanity.** Opens don't matter. Replies kinda matter. Booked calls matter. Signed deals = the only real metric.
4. **Re-test quarterly.** What works in Q1 may not work in Q3. Email fatigue is real.
5. **Log everything.** Track in `LEDGER/OUTREACH_PIPELINE.csv` with columns: sequence, variant, sends, opens, replies, calls_booked, deals_closed, revenue.

## SEQUENCE PERFORMANCE BENCHMARKS

know what "good" looks like so you can diagnose problems fast.

| Metric | Bad | Average | Good | Great |
|--------|-----|---------|------|-------|
| Open rate | < 30% | 30-50% | 50-65% | > 65% |
| Reply rate (total sequence) | < 1% | 1-3% | 3-5% | > 5% |
| Reply rate (Email 1 only) | < 0.5% | 0.5-2% | 2-4% | > 4% |
| Positive reply rate | < 0.3% | 0.3-1% | 1-2% | > 2% |
| Bounce rate | > 5% | 3-5% | 1-3% | < 1% |
| Unsubscribe rate | > 2% | 1-2% | 0.5-1% | < 0.5% |
| Call booking rate (from replies) | < 20% | 20-40% | 40-60% | > 60% |

**Diagnosis guide:**
- Low open rate? Subject line problem. A/B test new subjects.
- Good opens, low replies? Body copy problem. Shorten, add more proof, change CTA.
- Good replies, low bookings? Follow-up speed problem. Respond within 5 minutes.
- Good bookings, low closes? Sales call problem. Not a cold email issue.
