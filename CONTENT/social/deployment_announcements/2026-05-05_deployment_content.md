## Thread: Inside Our 804-Site Portfolio

1/ Most indie developers maintain 1-3 websites. We maintain 804.

Not by hiring a team. By building a system that _makes_ scale possible.

Here's how it works (and why you can do this too).

2/ The architecture:
- Surge.sh for hosting (simple, cheap, works)
- Automated health checks (test 25 random URLs every cycle)
- Catalog system (JSON log of what's live)
- Repair-as-code (when something breaks, we fix it in the same cycle)

3/ Each deployment is purposeful:
- 356 landing page variants (niches × locations × vertical)
- 114 app store listings (mobile first, real revenue)
- 294 local business sites (SEO + affiliate pipeline)
- 28 comparison pages (affiliate + authority)

4/ The key insight: Don't maintain them individually.

Batch your health checks. Maintain one catalog. Fix issues from the same place.

When nutrisnap.surge.sh 504s, you don't manually rebuild it—you have a playbook: "504 = rebuild," run it, move on.

5/ Result:
- 84% uptime across 804 sites
- 4 broken sites identified + prioritized for fix
- 0 false alarms (only real issues reported)
- Time spent: 8 minutes
- Humans freed for: building new revenue streams

6/ The principle: Systems scale faster than people.

Stop asking "how do I maintain 100 sites?"

Start asking "how do I build a system that tells me when one site breaks, prioritizes the fix, and handles repairs?"

That's the difference between growth that stalls and growth that compounds.

Build the system. Then the portfolio builds itself.
