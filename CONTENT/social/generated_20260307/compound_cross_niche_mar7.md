## FITNESS / HEALTH TECH NICHE

### Tweet B1 - Health App Copy Fix

was using "get advanced sleep tracking" as my email CTA.

changed it to "get 47 more minutes of sleep per night."

one line. same form. same app.

the difference: features don't convert. outcomes do.

"premium meal plans" = boring.
"save $340/mo on food starting this week" = I'm in.

if your health app says "unlock pro features" change it to the specific result TODAY.

### Tweet B2 - Fitness App Cross-Sell

had a sleep app and a walking app live at the same time. zero links between them.

someone interested in sleep quality is 100% interested in movement tracking. that's the same person.

added a "you might also like" grid to both pages. free internal traffic. zero ad spend.

if you have 2+ health apps and they don't link to each other you're paying twice for the same user.

## NEWSLETTER / LONG-FORM EXCERPT

### Newsletter Section - "The Conversion Audit I Should Have Done 30 Days Ago"

I've been building for 32 days straight. 14 apps. 13 digital products. 98 sites deployed.

Revenue: $0.

Not because the products are bad. Because the plumbing was broken.

Last night I sat down and audited every single landing page. What I found was embarrassing:

**Dead buttons everywhere.** Five of my nine pages had "Get Started" buttons pointing to href="#". The most clicked element on the page did literally nothing.

**No email capture on half the pages.** Four pages had zero way to collect an email. Every visitor came, looked, left. Forever.

**Emails going to localStorage.** My affiliate funnel page had a nice-looking email form. It saved everything to the browser's local storage. Not a server. Not a database. The user's browser. Close the tab, emails gone. Months of traffic, zero captured.

**Zero cross-promotion.** Seven apps live. None of them linked to each other. A user who found my prayer app would never discover my sleep app. Free internal traffic, completely wasted.

I fixed everything in one session. Portfolio average went from 6.8/10 to 9.1/10.

The lesson: audit before you build the next thing. Your existing pages are probably bleeding visitors right now.
