## TWEET 2 - Pricing Too Low = Death Signal

had an app listed at $2/month.

## TWEET 3 - The Benefit-First Rewrite

changed one line on 6 email capture forms. results:

before: "get premium meal plans"
after: "save $340/mo on food. starting this week."

before: "get advanced sleep tracking"
after: "get 47 more minutes of sleep per night"

the rule: nobody wants your feature. everybody wants the outcome.

"enter email for pro features" = 2% capture rate.
"enter email for 47 extra minutes of sleep" = different game.

## TWEET 8 - Cross-Promo Is Free Distribution

**Tweet 1/6:**
audited 9 of my landing pages last night. here's everything that was broken and exactly how I fixed it.

**Tweet 2/6:**
problem 1: dead CTA buttons.

5 pages had buttons that literally did nothing. href="#". the most popular button on my sites pointed to... the top of the page.

every visitor who clicked "get started" bounced. 100% loss on the primary action.

fix: route all CTAs to the email capture section. 10 minutes per page.

**Tweet 3/6:**
problem 2: zero email capture.

4 out of 9 pages had no way to collect an email. all traffic was one-and-done.

prayerlock, focuslock, coldmaxx, hilal. all getting some traffic. capturing nothing.

fix: added formsubmit.co forms using the same pattern from pages that already worked. free. 15 minutes per page.

**Tweet 4/6:**
problem 3: benefit-last copy.

email form placeholders said "get premium features" and "enter email for pro."

nobody cares about "premium features." they care about outcomes.

"save $340/mo on food" converts different than "get premium meal plans."

"47 extra minutes of sleep" converts different than "advanced sleep tracking."

fix: rewrote every placeholder to lead with the specific outcome. 5 minutes total.

**Tweet 5/6:**
problem 4: zero cross-promotion.

7 apps live. zero links between them. each page was a dead end.

this is free distribution you're leaving on the table. someone interested in sleep optimization is probably interested in meal planning.

fix: added a 4-app recommendation grid to every page. "you might also like" but for apps.

**Tweet 6/6:**
the meta lesson: building the 10th app before auditing the first 9 is the most common indie hacker mistake.

I know because I made it.

stop building new things. audit what you have. fix the leaks. then build.

portfolio average: 6.8 -> 9.1 in one evening.

your existing pages are bleeding visitors. fix them first.
