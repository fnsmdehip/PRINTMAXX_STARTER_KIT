## LINKEDIN POST 1, Pricing Data (from Post 4)

Pricing optimization is 2-4x more impactful than customer acquisition.

That number comes from aggregated SaaS benchmark data across thousands of companies.

Most founders spend 80% of their time on growth. The 25-60% revenue increase is sitting in their pricing page.

Three changes with outsized impact:
- Animated paywalls convert 2.9x better than static ones
- Contextual upgrade prompts (triggered by usage) beat random popups
- 96% of users watch onboarding videos. Most apps don't have one.

The shift happening right now: per-seat pricing is declining. Credit-based and outcome-based models grew 126% year over year.

Charge for results, not access.

The 25-60% revenue increase most founders are missing is sitting in their pricing page, not their growth budget.

## LINKEDIN POST 3, Subscription App Data (from Post 2)

RevenueCat just published data from 100K+ apps and 1 billion transactions.

The finding that stood out: AI-powered apps convert 40% better than non-AI apps. But they also churn 30% faster.

Translation: AI is incredible for getting users in the door. It's less effective at keeping them.

The companies winning are combining both:
- AI-powered conversion funnels
- Traditional retention mechanics (onboarding, community, direct outreach)

Another data point: subscription apps are winner-takes-most. The top 25% grew 80% YoY. The rest are flat or declining.

The strategic implication: pick a sub-niche small enough to dominate. Then build retention, not just acquisition.
