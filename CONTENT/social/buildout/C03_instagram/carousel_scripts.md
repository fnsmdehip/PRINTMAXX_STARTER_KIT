### Carousel 8: "how to price your freelance services (stop undercharging)"

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
you're probably charging 50-60% of what the market will pay.
here's how to find the real number.

**Slide 2:**
the mistake: pricing based on your time
"it takes me 3 hours so I'll charge $150"
clients don't buy time. they buy outcomes.

**Slide 3:**
the right formula: value-based pricing
what is the outcome worth to them?
cold email sequence that books 5 calls → 1 close at $3K → worth $600 to them minimum

**Slide 4:**
how to find market rate in 20 minutes:
search "[service] freelancer" on:
- LinkedIn (filter by Open to Work)
- Fiverr Pro listings
- Upwork top-rated profiles
write down the top 10 prices. you want the top 25%.

**Slide 5:**
the price floors for common services (2026 rates):
- copywriting: $0.15-0.30/word | $100-500/page
- web design: $1,500-8,000 per site
- social media management: $500-2,500/mo
- cold email: $500-2,000/month retainer
- SEO: $750-3,000/month

**Slide 6:**
the 3-tier pricing trick:
never show one price. show three.
basic: $500 / standard: $900 / premium: $1,500
80% of clients pick the middle. you want them to.

**Slide 7:**
how to raise prices without losing clients:
"I'm raising my rates to $X on [date]. existing clients stay at current rate if you pre-pay 3 months."
you'll keep 70%+ of clients and get 90-day cash flow upfront.

**Slide 8:**
the undercharging spiral:
low price → need more clients → less time per client → lower quality → clients leave
the opposite also works: high price → fewer clients → more attention → better results → referrals

**Slide 9:**
the script when a client says "that's too expensive":
"I understand. what budget were you thinking?"
stop talking. wait.
most of the time they name a number 20-30% below yours. counter at 10% below. done.

**Slide 10 (CTA):**
share with a freelancer who's undercharging.
save for next time a client pushes back on your rate.
