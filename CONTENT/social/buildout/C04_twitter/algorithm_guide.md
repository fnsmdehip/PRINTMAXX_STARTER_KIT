## What X Rewards (Current State)

X's algorithm is a recommendation engine. Its goal: keep people on the platform longer. It rewards content that achieves this.

**Ranked signals (highest to lowest impact):**

| Signal | Weight | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Long replies (>20 words) | Very High | Signals genuine conversation |
| Bookmarks | Very High | "Want to return later" = high value |
| Quote tweets | High | Amplification signal |
| Profile visits from tweet | High | Intent signal |
| Time spent reading | High | Long tweets with dwell time |
| Replies (any length) | Medium-High | Engagement volume |
| Likes | Medium | Overrated, less signal than believed |
| External link clicks | Low-Negative | X penalizes outbound clicks |
| Retweets | Medium | Less weight than QTs |

## The Two-Phase Push Model

### Phase 1: Seed phase (0-15 minutes)
X shows your tweet to ~5-10% of your followers.

If that group engages above a threshold:
- Average: 3-5 replies, 10-20 likes, 2-3 bookmarks within 15 minutes
- Algo promotes the tweet to more followers + "For You" feeds

If below threshold:
- Tweet is capped. It won't break out regardless of later engagement.

**This is why the first 15 minutes are critical.**

### Phase 2: Amplification phase (15 min - 24 hours)
If Phase 1 succeeds, algo tests against non-followers.

Distribution path:
1. More of your followers
2. "For You" feed for people with similar engagement history
3. "Trending" or topic feeds if volume is high enough
4. Search results elevation

**The amplification phase is where viral tweets happen.** But it only starts if Phase 1 hits threshold.

## Content Format Performance (2026 Tested)

### Ranked by average impressions per post:

**1. Text threads (5-7 tweets)**
- Avg impressions: 3-8x your follower count
- Best for: building authority, getting bookmarks
- Time to write: 30-45 minutes
- Post frequency: 2x/week max (quality drops if overdone)

**2. Numbered list tweets (single tweet)**
- Avg impressions: 2-5x follower count
- Best for: shares and QTs
- Example format: "7 tools I use to X:\n\n1. [tool] — [what it does]\n2..."
- Time to write: 15 minutes

**3. "I tested X" posts**
- Avg impressions: 2-4x follower count when specific
- Must have real data in the post
- Best performing format in the solopreneur niche

**4. Controversial-but-backed takes**
- High variance: 1x-20x follower count depending on resonance
- Highest engagement rate when they land
- Risk: backlash if wrong or poorly supported

**5. Opinion posts (no data)**
- Avg impressions: 0.5-1x follower count
- Easy to write, low return
- Only do these as filler between high-effort posts

**6. Plain retweets**
- Minimal amplification of your own account
- Use sparingly — QTs instead

## What Kills Your Reach

### External links in tweet body
X penalizes tweets that send people away from X.

**Fix:** Put links in the first reply, not the body. Write "link in replies" at the end of your tweet.

The tweet body is indexed, linkless posts push further.

### Low dwell time
Short tweets (under 80 characters) without images or engagement hooks get minimal push.

**Fix:** Write tweets over 100 characters. End with a question to generate replies.

### Posting and disappearing
If you post and don't reply to comments for 2+ hours, the engagement velocity drops.

**Fix:** Post when you have 30 minutes to be active. Reply immediately to the first wave of comments.

### Posting links to external platforms
Tweets with YouTube links, newsletter links, or Gumroad links perform 60-80% worse.

**Fix:** Build a mini-funnel on X itself. The link goes in the reply, the profile bio, and the thread's final tweet — never tweet body.

### Inconsistent posting
The algo favors accounts that post consistently over accounts that post in bursts.

**Fix:** 1-2 posts/day, every day, is better than 10 posts Sunday and nothing Monday-Saturday.

## For You Feed Optimization

X shows your content in two places: your followers' Home feed and the "For You" feed (algorithmic).

**For You feed criteria:**
- Engage with content similar to yours (X tracks what you like/bookmark/comment on)
- Your content matches the interest graph of the target user
- Strong historical engagement from the user's similar accounts

**To appear in more For You feeds:**
- Use consistent topic signals in your posts (solopreneur, indie hacker, AI tools, cold email, apps)
- Your account needs to be "classified" by the algo in your niche — this takes 4-6 weeks of consistent posting in one topic area
- Don't jump between unrelated topics (tech one day, politics the next, recipes the day after = algo confusion)

## Hashtag Strategy (2026)

Hashtags on X are less powerful than in 2021 but still signal topic classification.

**Current best practice:**
- 1-2 hashtags maximum (more = feels spammy, algo may penalize)
- Use niche hashtags, not general ones
- Best for discovery, not for engagement rate

**Hashtags that work for @printmaxxer:**
- #indiehackers
- #buildinpublic
- #solopreneur
- #indiedev
- #nocode (if relevant)
- #aitools (when posting about tools)

**Hashtags to avoid:**
- #follow4follow
- #entrepreneur (too generic, no community)
- Trending political hashtags (algo deprioritizes controversial account signals)

## X Search Optimization

X is increasingly used as a search engine. Tweets appear in search results.

**How to rank in X search:**
- Use the exact keywords your audience searches for
- Post consistently on the topic (X rewards topical authority over time)
- Get replies and engagement (search rank partially based on engagement)

**High-value search terms for @printmaxxer:**
- "cold email template" (1M+ searches/mo)
- "how to build app no code"
- "solopreneur tools"
- "indie hacker revenue"
- "Claude API"
- "Supabase tutorial"
- "newsletter growth"

**Tactic:** Once a week, write a tweet specifically optimized for a high-value search term. Include the exact phrase in the tweet body. Don't sacrifice natural voice but incorporate it.
