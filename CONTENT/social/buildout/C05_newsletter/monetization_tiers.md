## FREE TIER, The Engine

**What it includes:**
- Weekly issue (full access)
- Archive access (last 4 issues)
- Basic lead magnet on signup

**Purpose:** Max reach, max deliverability, feed sponsor inventory, referral pool.

Free tier is NOT a loss, it generates:
- Sponsor revenue (CPM-based, need subscriber count)
- Affiliate revenue (every tool mention = link)
- Content distribution (readers share = free growth)
- Paid conversion funnel (everyone starts here)

**Do NOT gate too much.** Free subscribers should get real value. 80% of content stays free. The paid gate is the upgrade, not the entry requirement.

## PAID TIER, $9/mo (or $79/year = 27% discount)

**Positioning:** "The complete playbook, not just the highlights."

**What's included:**

1. Issues 24 hours early
Free subscribers get the issue Tuesday. Paid subscribers get it Monday morning.
Psychology: "insider access" feels valuable even when practical difference is small.

2. Monthly Deep-Dive (exclusive to paid)
One 2,000-3,000 word breakdown per month. Not published anywhere else.
Examples:
- "The complete Clay workflow for finding 500 hot leads per week"
- "How I'd build a $5K/mo newsletter from scratch in 2026"
- "The solo operator's complete tax setup (S-Corp, Augusta rule, home office)"

3. Swipe File Library (updated monthly)
- Cold email templates (growing library, 10 new per month)
- Landing page copy templates
- Social media caption swipe file
- Subject line bank

4. Direct Email Access (1 question per month)
- Submit a question via email
- Get a personalized breakdown within 5 business days
- Response is a mini-issue written specifically for their situation

5. Deal Roundup (every Friday)
- 3-5 SaaS lifetime deals worth buying
- Affiliate programs worth joining this week
- Tools on discount

**How to pitch the upgrade:**

In welcome sequence email 5 (day 9):
"The free version gives you the tactics. The paid version gives you the full system."

In footer of every issue:
"[X] founding members at $9/mo. Becomes $15/mo after 200 members. [Link]"

In anniversary email (day 30 and day 90):
"You've been on the list for [X] days. Here's what paid members got this month that free members missed: [list]. Still $9/mo. [Link]"

**Conversion mechanics:**
- Preview paid-only content in free issues (tease)
- Monthly "what paid members got this month" email to free subscribers
- Referral reward: refer 10 people → get 1 free month

**Pricing note:** $9/mo optimized for impulse buys. Under $10 requires no approval process in most corporate card setups. Annual plan at $79 removes monthly churn (pay upfront = stuck for a year).

## VIP TIER, $29/mo (or $249/year = 29% discount)

**Positioning:** "Direct access + the full operator toolkit."

**What's included (everything in Paid, plus):**

1. Bi-weekly live Q&A (Zoom or Twitter Space)
- 60-minute call, max 30 attendees
- Ask anything about your specific business situation
- Recording included if you miss it

2. Priority Response
- Questions answered within 24 hours (vs 5 days for Paid)
- Direct Telegram or email access
- First look at new tools and opportunities before published in newsletter

3. Deal Flow Access
- Pre-publish version of the deal roundup (Friday morning vs Friday night)
- Exclusive deals not shared with free or paid subscribers
- Discount codes negotiated exclusively for VIP members

4. Monthly Teardown
- Full audit of one VIP subscriber's business, funnel, or content strategy
- Submitted via form. Selected and published (with permission) as case study.
- Observer access even if not selected, real value either way.

5. Template Vault (Full Library)
- All swipe files + templates
- Airtable/Notion system templates (pre-built)
- Spreadsheet models (revenue projections, cold email trackers, content calendar)

**Who this is for:**
VIP buyers are operators doing $2K-20K/mo who want to compress their learning curve. The bi-weekly Q&A alone at $29/mo = $14.50 per call. That's cheaper than any coaching.

**How to pitch:**
"If you're already doing $2K/mo and want to get to $10K, the VIP tier pays for itself in one answer."

## UPGRADE TRIGGERS (Behavioral)

Automations to add in Beehiiv:

**Trigger 1: High engagement → paid pitch**

**Trigger 2: Click on paid content link → immediate pitch**
If subscriber clicks any "paid members get X" link: trigger paid upgrade sequence (3 emails over 5 days)

**Trigger 3: Day 30 active**
If subscriber still active after 30 days and not converted: send "what paid members got this month" recap

**Trigger 4: Annual plan push in November**
Pitch annual plan in November before year-end (people spend on business expenses before Dec 31)

## CHURN PREVENTION

Average newsletter churn: 3-8% per month.
Strategies to reduce:

1. Monthly value recap email (remind them what they got)
2. "Pause" option before cancel (30-day pause reduces churn by 25%)
3. Annual plan discounting (locked in subscribers churn 4× less)
4. New content formats quarterly (prevents content fatigue)
5. Community element (VIP Slack or Telegram), social lock-in reduces churn significantly

Target: <2% monthly churn. At $5,600/mo subscription revenue and 2% churn = $112/mo loss. Manageable.
