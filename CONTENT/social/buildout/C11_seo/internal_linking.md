# C11 SEO — Internal Linking Strategy
# Topic Clusters + PageRank Flow

---

## Why Internal Linking Matters (the numbers)

A well-linked internal page gets 30-50% more organic traffic than an identical page with no internal links. Google uses internal links to:
1. Discover new pages faster (crawl efficiency)
2. Understand page importance (PageRank flow)
3. Understand topic relationships (topical authority)
4. Determine which page should rank for which keyword

Cost: $0. Time: 15 minutes per article if done right.

---

## The Linking Rules

**Rule 1: Every new article gets 3-5 links TO existing articles on publish day**
- Choose the most relevant existing pages
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- Place links naturally within body paragraphs

**Rule 2: Update existing articles within 48 hours of publishing new content**
- Add 1-2 links FROM relevant existing articles TO the new article
- This creates bidirectional linking = stronger signal

**Rule 3: Every article links to its pillar hub page**
- "For the full breakdown, see our [Money Methods guide]"
- Ensures pillar pages accumulate maximum PageRank

**Rule 4: Pillar pages link to all spokes**
- Every cluster article gets at least one link from its pillar
- Pillar page = distribution hub for PageRank

**Rule 5: Anchor text variety**
- 40%: exact match keyword ("best affiliate programs")
- 30%: partial match ("these affiliate programs")
- 20%: natural phrasing ("check out this guide")
- 10%: brand/generic ("click here" — sparingly)

## Internal Linking Checklist (Per Article, On Publish)

- [ ] Links to pillar hub page (1 link)
- [ ] Links to 2-4 related cluster articles
- [ ] Anchor text is descriptive (not "click here")
- [ ] Links placed naturally in body text (not footer dumps)
- [ ] Existing related articles updated to link back to new article
- [ ] No broken links (check with Broken Link Checker plugin)
- [ ] No more than 1 link per 200 words (avoid over-linking)

## Orphan Page Prevention

An orphan page = no internal links pointing to it. Google rarely ranks orphan pages.

**How to find orphan pages:**
- Screaming Frog → Internal → filter for pages with 0 inlinks
- Google Search Console → Pages → Not indexed (often = no internal links)

**Fix: The New Article Update Protocol**
Within 48 hours of publishing any new article:
1. Search site for related keywords
2. Find 2-3 existing articles that naturally relate
3. Add 1 sentence + link to the new article in each
4. This ensures zero orphan pages from day one
