# AI UGC Pricing Guide, What to Charge Brands

## Market Rate Reality Check (2025-2026)

Human UGC creators charge $50-500 per video depending on platform and niche.
AI-produced UGC undercuts on cost but must match quality.
Target pricing: $35-250/video depending on specs + volume.

- 1:1 delivery (no exclusivity)

## How to Charge More Without Pushback

### 1. Offer the math
"If one video gets you a 2% conversion on 1,000 clicks and your AOV is $80, that's $1,600 in revenue from a $150 video. I'd be undercharging."

### 2. Bundle revisions
Don't offer unlimited revisions at base price. Each round outside the included revisions = $25-50. This protects your time and makes the initial offer feel premium.

### 3. Anchor on human creator rates
"A human UGC creator with 10K followers charges $200-400 for a 30-second video. I deliver the same quality, more consistently, and faster."

### 4. Show production quality first
Before quoting, send a spec reel or sample video. Visual proof kills price objections before they start.

### 5. Offer an à la carte upgrade menu
Base: $150 for 3 videos
+ Script writing (if not provided): +$30
+ Rush delivery (under 3 days): +$50
+ Extra format variants: +$20 each
+ Exclusivity (brand-only for 30 days): +$100

## What Brands Pay For (Their Mental Model)

Brands aren't buying "video production." They're buying:
1. Ad creative that doesn't look like an ad (authenticity)
2. Speed (human creators take 7-21 days, you deliver in 3-5)
3. Volume (can't get 12 videos from one creator per month easily)
4. Consistency (same quality every time, no mood variability)
5. A/B testing material (same script, different deliveries)

Lead with these in pitches, not the fact that it's AI-generated.

---

## Red Lines, What to Avoid

- Don't disclose it's AI unless asked directly (controversial but standard practice, legal, not deceptive when creative quality is genuine)
- Don't guarantee specific revenue outcomes
- Don't produce content for: MLM, gambling (check jurisdiction), unverified health claims, political campaigns
- Don't offer exclusivity without pricing it into the contract
- If a brand asks about AI disclosure: "The creative is AI-assisted, same as most professional video production uses AI editing tools."
