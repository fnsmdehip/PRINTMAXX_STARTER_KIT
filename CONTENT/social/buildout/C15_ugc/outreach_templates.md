# Brand Outreach Templates — UGC Video Service

## Target Identification

### Where to find brands that need UGC:
1. **TikTok Ads Library** — brands running paid ads = already buying creative
2. **Meta Ads Library** — search niche keywords, filter for active ads
3. **Crunchbase** — filter for DTC brands with recent funding ($1M-$20M seed/series A)
4. **Shopify store directories** — MyIP.ms, BuiltWith filtered by Shopify
5. **r/entrepreneur, r/ecommerce** — founders posting about growth struggles
6. **LinkedIn** — search "Head of Growth," "Performance Marketing Manager," "Creative Strategist" at DTC brands
7. **Product Hunt** — recently launched products with traction
8. **App Store top charts** — apps spending on user acquisition

### Prioritize brands that:
- Are already running UGC-style ads (proven willingness to pay)
- Have 10K-100K social followers (big enough to have budget, small enough to say yes quickly)
- Are in: DTC, apps, SaaS, supplements, finance tools, productivity tools
- Recently launched or raised funding in the last 6 months

## Objection Handling Scripts

### "We only work with real human creators"
"Understood. Most brands start there. The shift I'm seeing is teams using both — human creators for hero content and authenticity-driven campaigns, AI production for volume and split-testing. Would you be open to a test batch to run alongside your current creators?"

### "How is this different from just hiring a freelancer?"
"Two ways: speed and consistency. A freelancer has a life — delays, revisions, creative blocks. I deliver the same quality every time in under 5 days. Also, I can produce 12 videos in the time most creators do 3."

### "Can you prove it performs?"
"I can't guarantee your specific performance — too many variables (landing page, offer, audience). What I can do is send you samples and let you A/B test against your current creative. If my stuff doesn't match or beat it in CTR, you don't re-order."

### "AI content gets flagged / platforms hate AI"
"The platforms don't flag AI voiceovers or AI avatars — they flag low-quality content. The distinction they care about is engagement quality, not production method. Human creators use AI editing tools on every video."

### "It's too expensive"
"What are you paying per video now? [Wait.] So for [X], you're getting [Y] — one revision, [Z] day turnaround? I'm at [PRICE] for [BETTER TERMS]. The comparison usually looks different when you put it next to the full cost."
