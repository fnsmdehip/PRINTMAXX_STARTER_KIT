# Automation Setup: FB Reels Crosspost

## Two Paths: Paid Automation vs Free Manual

You can automate crossposting with $25/mo (Repurpose.io) or do it manually in 15 minutes per video with free tools. Both work. The choice depends on volume.

At <10 videos/week: manual is fine.
At 10+ videos/week: automation pays for itself.

---

## Path A: Repurpose.io (Semi-Automated, $25/mo)

Repurpose.io connects TikTok → other platforms with automatic uploads.

### What it does:
- Monitors your TikTok for new videos
- Auto-downloads (without watermark)
- Uploads to IG, FB, YouTube Shorts automatically
- Applies saved caption templates per platform

### Setup Steps:

1. Sign up at repurpose.io ($25/mo, 14-day free trial)

2. Connect accounts:
   - TikTok (source)
   - Instagram (destination)
   - Facebook Page (destination)
   - YouTube Channel (destination)

3. Create a "Workflow" for each destination:
   - Name: TikTok → Instagram Reels
   - Source: TikTok account
   - Destination: Instagram (Reels)
   - Caption: [TikTok caption] + your template additions
   - Toggle: Remove TikTok watermark (critical — enabled by default)

4. Create three workflows total:
   - TikTok → Instagram Reels
   - TikTok → Facebook Reels
   - TikTok → YouTube Shorts

5. Test with one video. Verify:
   - No watermark on destination uploads
   - Caption applied correctly
   - Video is posted as Reel/Short (not regular post)

### Repurpose.io Limitations:
- Can't add custom thumbnails per platform automatically
- Caption is templated, not fully unique per platform
- YouTube Shorts requires manually adding #Shorts to description (Repurpose does this by default if configured)
- Scheduling delay: 15-60 min after TikTok post

### Cost math:
$25/mo for 10 videos/week = $0.063/video.
Alternative: 15 min manual per video × 10 videos = 2.5 hours/week.
If your time is worth >$10/hour, Repurpose.io pays for itself.

## Path B: Manual Workflow (Free)

No monthly cost. More control over per-platform optimization. Takes 15 min per video.

### Tools:
- CapCut Desktop (free) — export without watermark
- Meta Business Suite (free) — post to FB + IG simultaneously
- YouTube Studio (free) — upload Shorts
- Buffer free tier (3 channels, 10 posts each) — optional scheduling

### Manual SOP Per Video:

**Total time: ~15 minutes**

#### Step 1: Export from CapCut (3 min)
- Open TikTok project in CapCut Desktop
- Export 1080x1920, H.264, no watermark
- Save as: `[date]_[topic]_CLEAN.mp4`

#### Step 2: Post to TikTok (2 min)
- Upload `CLEAN.mp4` to TikTok
- Add TikTok-specific caption from your template
- Post or schedule

#### Step 3: Post to Meta (5 min)
- Open business.facebook.com
- Create → Reel
- Upload `CLEAN.mp4`
- Write FB caption (copy from template, add question)
- Toggle "Also share to Instagram"
- Write IG caption (different from FB — add save CTA)
- Add hashtags in IG caption (or plan to add in first comment after post)
- Schedule for +30 min after TikTok post time

#### Step 4: Post to YouTube Shorts (5 min)
- Open studio.youtube.com
- Upload → `CLEAN.mp4`
- Title: [your YouTube-optimized title]
- Description: [topic paragraph] + hashtags including #Shorts
- Set to "Shorts" format (auto-detected if vertical + under 60s)
- Schedule for +60 min after TikTok post time

#### Step 5: Log it (1 min)
- Add to your crosspost tracking sheet (see template below)

---

## Tracking Sheet Template

Create this as a Google Sheet. One row per video.

```
Columns:
- Date
- Topic
- TikTok URL
- TikTok posted (Y/N)
- IG Reels posted (Y/N)
- FB Reels posted (Y/N)
- YT Shorts posted (Y/N)
- TikTok views (7-day)
- IG views (7-day)
- FB views (7-day)
- YT views (7-day)
- Best performer
- Notes
```

Review weekly. Kill formats that consistently underperform. Double down on what works.

Buffer's free plan: 3 social accounts, 10 scheduled posts per account.

At 7 videos/week × 4 platforms = 28 posts/week — you'll need the paid Essentials plan ($15/mo) for full automation.

For 3-5 videos/week: free tier works.

Setup:
1. buffer.com → connect TikTok, IG, YouTube
2. Set posting schedule (match the optimal times table)

Note: Buffer doesn't remove watermarks. You still need CapCut exports before queuing.

## Common Failures and Fixes

**Problem: IG is suppressing my Reels**
Fix: You're posting with TikTok watermark. Re-export from CapCut with watermark off.

**Problem: YouTube isn't categorizing video as a Short**
Fix: Make sure video is under 60 seconds AND vertical (1080x1920). Add #Shorts to description.

**Problem: FB Reels has zero views**
Fix: Your Facebook Page has no audience. FB Reels primarily surfaces to existing Page followers before going wide. Build Page followers via IG bio link or cross-promo.

**Problem: TikTok posts every day but IG/FB/YT lag behind**
Fix: Build your upload queue in advance. Don't upload to TikTok until you have the clean export ready for all platforms.

**Problem: Repurpose.io uploads but captions look wrong**
Fix: Edit the workflow template. Repurpose uses variables: {{caption}}, {{hashtags}}, {{url}}. Test with one video before scaling.

## Priority Platform by Revenue Potential

Not all platforms are equal for monetization. Here's where to focus energy:

| Platform | Monetization Speed | Revenue Type | Priority |
|----------|------------------|--------------|----------|
| YouTube Shorts | Slowest (1K subs required) | Ad revenue + affiliate | High (long-term) |
| TikTok | Medium (Creator Rewards at 10K) | Affiliate + Creator Fund | High |
| IG Reels | Slow for ads, fast for affiliate | Affiliate + DM funnels | High |
| FB Reels | Fastest for in-stream ads | Ad revenue | Medium |

Facebook has the most accessible in-stream ads program (lower follower threshold). If you want ad revenue quickest, FB Reels plus consistent content is the path.

For affiliate revenue, IG and TikTok win — link-in-bio traffic converts better on those platforms.
