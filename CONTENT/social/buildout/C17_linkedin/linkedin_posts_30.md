### Post 8: LinkedIn Profile Audit Framework
Most LinkedIn profiles lose clients before the first conversation.

Here's the 6-point audit:

1. Headline: Does it say what you do + who you help + what outcome? Not your job title.
   BAD: "Founder at XYZ"
   GOOD: "I help B2B SaaS companies book 15+ qualified demos/month via cold email"

2. Banner: Does it visually reinforce your positioning? Most people leave it blank.

3. About section: First line must be a hook. Who you help. Proof. CTA with specific next step.

4. Featured section: Your best case study, not your website homepage.

5. Experience: Bullet points with numbers. Not responsibilities. Results.

6. Creator mode: Turn it on. Adds a Follow button. Changes your profile to content-first.

Run your own profile through these 6 checks.

Volume is not the answer. Specificity is.

### Post 25: AI Changed Cold Email (Here's What Still Works)
6 months ago, AI ruined cold email.

Everyone started sending AI-generated slop. Open rates dropped industry-wide from 42% to 28%.

But something interesting happened.

The bar for "personalized" just got higher.

What works now:
1. Reference something specific from their LinkedIn posts in the last 30 days
2. Name a competitor and something they're doing better
3. Lead with insight, not your service ("I noticed your pricing page has no social proof, costing you ~15% conversions")
4. 3 sentences or less

What died:
- Generic first lines ("I love what you're building at [company]!")
- AI-obvious openers ("As a busy founder, I know your time is valuable")
- The 6-paragraph intro email

The AI wave created an opportunity: be the one who writes with actual research and human specificity. You'll stand out in a flooded inbox.
