# LinkedIn Lead Generation Strategy
# Content → Warm DMs → Discovery Calls → Clients

---

## The Core Model

LinkedIn lead gen works in 3 phases:

**Phase 1 (Days 1-60):** Build trust with content. Warm your audience before any outreach.
**Phase 2 (Days 30-90):** Strategic commenting to get on prospects' radar.
**Phase 3 (Days 60+):** DMs and inbound inquiries convert at 15-30%.

Most people skip Phase 1 entirely and cold DM immediately. Open rate: <5%.
This system: inbound + warm outbound at 15-30% response rate.

---

## Phase 1: Content Foundation (Days 1-60)

**Goal:** Establish expertise signal. Get prospects to recognize you before you reach out.

**Week 1-2 tasks:**
1. Complete profile optimization (see profile_optimization.md)
2. Identify 50 target prospects, people who match your ideal client profile
3. Follow them. Comment thoughtfully on their posts 3x before any DM.
4. Post your own content 3-5x/week (use content_pillars.md rotation)

**Content → Inbound timeline:**
- Month 1: 0-2 inbound inquiries (building foundation)
- Month 2: 2-5 inbound inquiries
- Month 3: 5-10 inbound inquiries
- Month 4+: 10-20+ inbound per month (compounding effect)

---

## Phase 2: Strategic Commenting (Days 30-90)

**Goal:** Appear in your target client's notifications 2-3 times before DMing.

**The targeted comment strategy:**

Step 1: Build your prospect list (50-100 people matching your ideal client)
- Use LinkedIn search: filter by title, company size, location, industry
- Example filter: "VP Marketing" + "11-50 employees" + "SaaS" + "United States"
- Save them to a Notion CRM database

Step 2: Set up notifications
- Follow each prospect
- Click the notification bell on each profile
- You'll now see when they post

Step 3: Comment within 30 minutes of their posts
- Add value with your comment (not just "Great post!")
- Reference something specific from their content
- Add a relevant insight or follow-up question
- Target: 5-10 comments/day, 15-20 minutes total

**Comment formula that works:**

BAD: "Great insights! Really agree with this perspective."
BAD: "So true! Thanks for sharing."

GOOD: "The part about [specific point] resonates, I saw similar results when I [specific parallel experience with number]. The one thing I'd add: [specific insight]. Have you tested [specific approach]?"

Good comments: 40-80 words, adds new information, ends with question.

Step 4: After 3 meaningful comments on their posts over 2-3 weeks, DM.

## Phase 3: The DM Strategy

**Rule 1:** Never DM without prior content/comment interaction.
**Rule 2:** Never pitch in the first message.
**Rule 3:** Lead with insight or value, not a question about them.

### DM Sequence (3 messages):

**Message 1: The observation (after 3 prior comments on their posts)**
```
Hey [Name], I've been following your posts on [topic].

Your point about [specific thing from their content] caught my attention because I just ran an experiment on the exact same thing.

Results were surprising, open rate went from 2% to 11% when I added [specific tactic].

Happy to share the full breakdown if useful.
```

*Wait for reply. If no reply in 5 days, send Message 2.*

**Message 2: The follow-up (5 days later)**
```
Hey [Name], dropped you a message a few days ago.

Quick context: I [specific relevant thing you've done] for companies like [1-2 relevant examples].

If [specific pain point they've mentioned in their posts] is on your radar, I put together a quick breakdown of what's working right now.

Happy to send it over, no strings attached.
```

*Wait 7 days. If no reply, final message.*

**Message 3: The exit (7 days after Message 2)**
```
Not going to keep following up, I know you're busy.

Last thing: I put together a [specific free resource relevant to their work] that I think would actually help with [specific problem].

Leaving it here: [link]

Good luck with [specific thing from their content].
```

*This message has the highest response rate of the three, people appreciate the clarity.*

## The Diagnostic Offer (Entry Point)

High-ticket services are hard to sell cold. Low-ticket diagnostics aren't.

**The diagnostic model:**
- Price: $200-500
- Deliverable: 60-minute audit + written action plan
- Who it's for: Warm leads who aren't ready for full engagement
- Conversion rate to full client: 25-35%

**The diagnostic DM:**
```
[Name], based on [specific thing I've observed about their business], I think there are 3-4 quick wins in your [area].

I do a 60-minute [area] diagnostic for $300. I look at [specific things], then deliver a written action plan with exact steps.

People who implement it see [specific result] within [timeframe].

If that sounds like it fits where you are, I have a slot open [day]. If not, no worries.
```

**Why it works:**
- Low risk ($300 vs $3K)
- Specific deliverable (they know what they're getting)
- Time-limited (creates urgency without fake scarcity)

## The Inbound Funnel (Passive Lead Gen)

Once content is running, set up inbound triggers:

**Trigger 1: The CTA post**
Every 8-10 posts, include a direct offer:
"If you want [specific outcome], DM me 'SYSTEM' and I'll send the [free resource / diagnostic offer / call link]."

**Trigger 2: The featured lead magnet**
Add a free resource to your Featured section.
- Works while you sleep
- Collects emails (Beehiiv landing page)
- Warms people before they DM you

**Trigger 3: The About CTA**
Last paragraph of About section: "DM me '[trigger word]' and I'll send [specific free thing]."
People read About sections when they're already warm. CTA converts at 15-25%.

## Lead Tracking System

Build this in Notion:

**Database: LinkedIn Leads**
Columns:
- Name
- Company
- Title
- LinkedIn URL
- First interaction date (first time you commented on their post)
- DM sent date
- DM status (not sent / sent / replied / call booked / closed / not interested)
- Deal value
- Notes (what you discussed, what they're struggling with)
- Next action

Review this database every Monday.
Any lead with "replied" status who hasn't converted to "call booked" in 7 days: follow up.

## What Not to Do

1. **Don't cold DM immediately**, wait for 3 prior comment interactions
2. **Don't pitch in the first message**, value first, ask second
3. **Don't send voice notes unsolicited**, text-only until relationship is warm
4. **Don't connect + immediately message**, wait 48 hours after they accept
5. **Don't post more than 2x/day**, kills reach per post
6. **Don't use LinkedIn automation for DMs**, account restriction risk is high
7. **Don't external link in post body**, put all links in first comment
