### Solopreneur Money Angle (Market Sizing + Pricing Ladders)

two market sizing lessons from today's alpha that should change how you think about pricing:

lesson 1: the religious app market. $300k/mo from a Bible chatbot. total addressable market is 5+ billion religious people globally. the "app" market isn't saturated. specific verticals within it are. the broad ones are wide open.

lesson 2: the VA market. $19.5B today, $55B by 2035. but most VAs are stuck at $20/hr because they sell hours instead of outcomes.

the pricing ladder that works for any service business:
- tier 1: hourly ($20-40/hr) for trial clients
- tier 2: monthly retainer ($2-5k/mo) for recurring clients
- tier 3: revenue share or equity for high-growth clients

same person, same skills, same hours. tier 3 makes 5-10x tier 1. the difference isn't the work. it's the packaging.

if you're building a product, pick a market bigger than your ego. if you're selling a service, build a ladder taller than your current rate. the math works. you just have to stop pricing like everyone else on Fiverr.
