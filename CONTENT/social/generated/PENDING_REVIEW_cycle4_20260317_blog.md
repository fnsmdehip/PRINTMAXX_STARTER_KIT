## Title: The 10x Margin Play Hiding in AI Video Production

## Meta description: Solo operators sell AI videos for $75. Agencies resell at $1,500. Here's the full arbitrage playbook.

---

Solo creators on Fiverr pump out AI-generated explainer videos, product demos, and social ads for $50-150.

Agencies buy those videos, add a brand brief and a single revision call, then invoice clients at $500-2,000.

The margin is 83%. The moat is the client relationship. Not the AI skill.

### The supply side is commoditized

Search "AI video" on Fiverr right now. Sort by newest. You'll find hundreds of operators delivering HD AI-generated content at rock-bottom prices.

Quality is 80% there. The missing 20% is branding, strategy, and someone the client trusts enough to hand a credit card to.

That 20% is where all the money lives.

### The agency playbook

Step 1: Find businesses spending $2K+/mo on video content. LinkedIn job posts for "video editor" or "content manager" are your lead list.

Step 2: Cold email offering 60% cheaper with faster turnaround. Position as "AI-powered production studio."

Step 3: Outsource to solo operators at $75-100/video. DM 20 operators on Fiverr. Offer guaranteed monthly volume. 15 will say yes immediately.

Step 4: Add brand guidelines, licensed music, one revision round. Deliver via Google Drive.

Step 5: Invoice at $500-1,500/video.

Your cost per video: $75 production + $50 your time = $125.
Your price: $750 average.
Margin: 83%.

### Why this won't get disrupted

Clients don't want to learn Runway, Kling, or Pika. They want to send a brief and get a video back.

Same reason restaurants exist even though everyone has a kitchen. Convenience is a permanent moat.

### Scaling past $10K/mo

Build a Loom library of brand guideline templates for client onboarding. Create a Notion board where operators grab briefs and upload deliverables. Automate invoicing with Stripe. Add a $200/mo retainer tier for priority turnaround.

Total overhead: $30/mo in tools. Everything else is margin.

### The real play

The business isn't being the best AI video creator. It's being the person between the creator and the check.

Relationship arbitrage. Always has been.

If you're already making AI videos for cheap, stop undercharging. Package it. Name it. Charge 5x.
