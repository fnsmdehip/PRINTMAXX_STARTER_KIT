## TWEET 4: Short Form First Second Rule
**Source:** ALPHA18439 (@SimonasDip, short form clarity)
**Account:** @PRINTMAXXER
**Platform:** Twitter/X
**Status:** PENDING_REVIEW

short form content fails in the first second. not the script. not the edit. the first second.

3 things must be obvious immediately:
- who is speaking
- what is happening
- why should i care

if a viewer can't answer all 3 in under 1 second, they scroll. doesn't matter how good the rest is. you lost them before they knew you existed.

test it: mute your video. watch the first frame. would you stop scrolling?

## TWEET 5: Cold Email Diagnostic
**Source:** ALPHA18378 (@seanb2b cold email signal)
**Account:** @PRINTMAXXER
**Platform:** Twitter/X
**Status:** PENDING_REVIEW

cold email not booking calls? it's one of these 4 things:

1. wrong list. you're emailing people who don't have the problem you solve. fix targeting before touching copy.
2. bad subject line. under 5 words. no caps. looks like a reply, not a pitch.
3. too long. 50-75 words max. nobody reads paragraph 3 of a cold email. nobody.
4. weak CTA. "would love to chat" is weak. "can I send you a 2-min video showing exactly how this works for [company]?" is specific.

diagnose before you rewrite. most people rewrite when they should re-target.
