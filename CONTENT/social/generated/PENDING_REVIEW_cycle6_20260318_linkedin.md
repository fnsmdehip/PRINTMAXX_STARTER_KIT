## LINKEDIN POST 1, SaaS Replacement Wave

35% of enterprises already replaced at least one SaaS product with custom builds.

The economics shifted faster than most people realize.

$50k/year vendor seat vs $3-5k one-time custom build. No feature bloat. No renewal negotiations. No "enterprise tier" upsell.

The opportunity for independent builders:

Mid-market companies (50-500 employees) are the sweet spot. They hate their tools. They don't have internal dev capacity. They have budget.

The playbook: cold email a VP of Ops. Ask what software they'd replace if they could. Build it in 5-7 days. Charge $15-25k.

Most solo devs are building consumer apps hoping for virality. The money is in B2B replacement contracts that nobody else is pitching.

## LINKEDIN POST 2, Vibe Coding Economics

A mobile app studio hit $900k/month.

Their "developers" can't write traditional code.

The model: ship 30 apps per quarter. Each costs under $200 in API credits to build. Kill 28. Double down on the 2 that get traction.

Google Stitch converts screenshots to working UIs. Rork deploys full apps from English descriptions. Templated infrastructure means app #31 takes 6 hours.

This is what "vibe coding" actually looks like in production: not a curiosity, a factory.

The cost of building software dropped 99%. The people winning aren't the best coders. They're the fastest at identifying what to build and shipping experiments.

## LINKEDIN POST 3, Premium Info Products

Someone charges $700/month for a data visualization dashboard.

$700. Monthly. For charts.

But their users are traders making $20k+/month from the data those charts show. $700 looks cheap when it directly improves your decisions on six-figure positions.

The pricing lesson most builders miss: anchor to the buyer's revenue from using your product, not your cost to build it.

Ahrefs charges $200/month for SEO data. Their users make $5k+/month from that data. The ratio is the same.

Find a niche where people earn money from information. Package that information 10% better. Price it at 3-5% of what they earn. The conversion math handles itself.
