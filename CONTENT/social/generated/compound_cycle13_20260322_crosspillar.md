### LinkedIn Post 1 - The Niche App Revenue Data

I analyzed the revenue data across every major habit tracker app. The findings challenged my assumptions.

Top 5 by revenue:
- Hallow (Catholic prayer): $51M ARR
- Finch (virtual pet self-care): $36M ARR
- Streaks (generic habit tracker): ~$360K ARR

The pattern: niche apps serving specific communities earn 100x more than generic tools serving everyone.

The most successful "habit tracker" isn't even marketed as one. It's a prayer app. The second most successful is a virtual pet.

Takeaway for builders: specificity isn't a limitation. It's a 140x revenue multiplier. The generic "habit tracker" category is a race to the bottom. The niche "daily prayer companion" category has one dominant player making $51M with room for every denomination they don't serve.

Building 30+ niche streak trackers targeting specific communities (yoga, photography, coding, prayer). Day 45 of building in public.

### LinkedIn Post 2 - Distribution vs Building

Day 45 stat: 171 websites built. 147 of them have zero distribution.

Not low distribution. Zero. No Reddit post. No tweet. No HN submission. No community engagement.

The 24 sites with at least one distribution channel get all the traffic. The correlation is 1:1.

This is the most expensive lesson in my building journey: speed of building has almost zero correlation with speed of revenue. Distribution speed is the bottleneck.

Restructuring my entire system around this. Every new build now ships with a distribution plan before the first line of code.
