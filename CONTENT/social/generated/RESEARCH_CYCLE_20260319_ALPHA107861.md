## THREAD: The Bottleneck Trap (LinkedIn/X)

---

You hire a consultant to fix your accounting firm's founder dependency.

Week 2: The consultant IS the new bottleneck.

This is one of the most underrated failure modes in service scaling:

**The Bottleneck Transfer Problem:**
- Founder was the constraint
- You replace founder with "systems"
- The person building systems becomes the new founder
- You've just delayed the problem, not solved it

**Why this happens:**
Knowledge work doesn't scale like manufacturing. When the bottleneck is a PERSON's skill (tax strategy, client relationships, audit judgment), you can't replace them with a SOP.

You need REDUNDANCY:
- 2 people who can do the job
- Checklists so either can execute
- Authority to make judgment calls at their level

**The math:**
- 1 founder = 100% dependent, zero use
- 1 founder + 1 consultant = still 1 person critical path (now it's the consultant)
- 2 consultants + systems = finally resilient

Service firms underestimate this. They try to replace a $500K founder with a $80K ops hire + checklists.

The checklists fail because judgment calls still need a human, and now there's only one.

The answer isn't better checklists. It's second opinions built into the process.

### Tweet 2: The Frame (Why)
"Service business scaling fails because founders try to replace JUDGMENT with CHECKLISTS.

Tax strategy. Client negotiations. Deal decisions.

These need a human brain.

Once you recognize this, the solution is obvious: hire TWO people who understand it, not one."

### Tweet 3: The Actionable (How)
"If you're service business founder scaling to 2+ people:

Don't optimize for 'can person X do this solo'

Optimize for 'can EITHER person Y or Z do this equally well'

Redundancy is use. Single points of failure kill margins."
