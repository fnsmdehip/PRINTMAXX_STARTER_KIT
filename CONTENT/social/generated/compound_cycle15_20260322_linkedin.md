## Post 3 - Platform Dependency Is a Business Risk (LinkedIn)

Apple terminated an entire developer account this week because of one employee's actions.

Not a warning. Not a 30-day grace period. Full termination. Every app removed. Every user relationship severed. Years of work gone overnight.

The developer team didn't violate any policy themselves. But Apple's enforcement doesn't distinguish between the account holder and their team members.

This is a structural risk that most founders underestimate:
- If 100% of your revenue flows through one app store, you don't have recurring revenue. You have recurring permission.
- If your only customer relationship is through a platform's notification system, you don't own that relationship.
- If you can't accept payment directly, you can't survive a policy change.

The mitigation is straightforward:
1. Web version of every mobile app (PWA at minimum)
2. Direct payment option alongside in-app purchase
3. Email list from day one (you own this, platforms don't)
4. Revenue diversified across at least 2 distribution channels

Building on platforms is smart. Depending entirely on them is a business continuity risk.
