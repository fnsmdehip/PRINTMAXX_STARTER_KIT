# Your fitness app is a tracking device (and 4 other things builders should know this week)

A French nuclear aircraft carrier was tracked in real-time by journalists this week. Not with satellites. Not with radar. With fitness app data.

Sailors were logging their workouts. The GPS coordinates leaked the carrier's exact position. This made the front page of Hacker News with 546 points because, somehow, people are still surprised when this happens.

The Strava military base leak was 2018. Same pattern. Eight years later. Still not fixed.

## Why this matters if you're not operating a warship

You probably don't command a nuclear carrier. But the same data leakage applies to founders, freelancers, and anyone running a business:

- **Your morning run** reveals your home address to anyone with access to your fitness app data
- **Your coworking check-ins** reveal your daily schedule
- **Your travel patterns** reveal your client meetings
- **Your phone's auto-joined Wi-Fi networks** map every location you visit regularly

You don't need to be hiding military secrets. You just need to understand that your routine is a data product someone is buying.

## The 10-minute fix

1. Audit app permissions on your phone (Settings > Privacy > Location Services)
2. Set fitness apps to "only while using" instead of "always"
3. Strip EXIF data from photos before posting (most phones have this in camera settings)
4. Use a VPN on public Wi-Fi
5. Check haveibeenpwned.com every 90 days

Basic operational security. Takes 10 minutes. 95% of tech founders skip it entirely.
