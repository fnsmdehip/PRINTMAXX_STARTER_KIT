## NEWSLETTER DRAFT: "The Kill Decision"
**Subject:** 1,300 users and $35/month. kill it or fix it?
**Account:** PRINTMAXXER newsletter
**Status:** PENDING_REVIEW

---

someone posted this week: "1.3k users, but only $35 MRR. is it time to kill my project?"

the comments were split. half said kill it. half said pivot.

both sides missed the real question.

**1,300 people are actively using your thing.** that's not a failure. that's signal. strong signal.

$35 MRR on 1,300 users means your monetization is broken, not your product. the math:

- 1,300 users at 3% paid conversion = 39 paying users
- 39 users at $9.99/mo = $389 MRR
- 39 users at $29.99/mo = $1,169 MRR
- 39 users at $49.99/yr = $162/mo (still 4.6x what they're making)

the problem is almost never "nobody wants this." the problem is you put the paywall in the wrong place, priced too low, or never asked for money at all.

**Three things to test before you kill anything with real users:**

1. **Move the gate.** if your premium features are behind a settings menu, move them to the main flow. users can't buy what they don't see.

2. **Test a price increase.** most indie devs price at $4.99 because it "feels fair." fair to who? your users already told you the product has value by showing up 1,300 times. test $9.99. test $14.99. track what happens.

3. **Add a trial.** 7-day free trial of everything, then gate. users experience full value, then face loss aversion. conversion rates on trials are 2-3x higher than hard paywalls.

---

meanwhile, someone else made $50k in 2 weeks from a Zakat calculator app. launched right before Ramadan. 2 billion Muslims, 30 days of daily app usage. the timing was the strategy.

two apps. one with 1,300 users making $35. one making $50k in a window. the difference isn't the code. the difference is pricing and timing.

the kill threshold matters. if an app can't clear $100 MRR after 60 days, consider killing it. but "60 days" means 60 days of actually trying to monetize. not 60 days of hoping users figure out how to pay you.
