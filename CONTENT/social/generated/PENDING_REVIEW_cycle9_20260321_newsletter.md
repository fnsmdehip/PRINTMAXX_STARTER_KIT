## Subject: 20 million users. zero apps. the food habit blue ocean.

**Preview text:** coffee, grilling, baking, cooking. massive communities. zero habit apps.

---

i spent this week scanning Reddit communities for app opportunities using competitive intel scrapers.

the pattern i keep finding: communities with millions of active users being served by passive tools (recipe browsers, hardware companions, static references) but zero daily habit-tracking apps.

here are the numbers:

| niche | community size | existing apps | habit apps |
|-------|---------------|---------------|------------|
| coffee brewing | 4.61M | Acaia, Fellow, recipe refs | 0 |
| grilling/BBQ | 4.44M | thermometer utils, recipe browsers | 0 |
| cooking | 4.41M | Allrecipes, Tasty, video apps | 0 |
| nutrition ed | 5.38M | MyFitnessPal, Cronometer (loggers, not educators) | 0 |
| baking | 3.44M | recipe databases | 0 |
| meal prep | 3.42M | Mealime, Plan to Eat (planners, not habit) | 0 |
| vegan cooking | 2.09M | HappyCow, Forks Over Knives (finders, not habits) | 0 |

that's 25+ million users across food hobbies with zero Duolingo-style daily streak apps.

### why this matters

Duolingo proved the model: daily streaks convert free users to paid at 8-12%. their revenue hit $531M in 2024. Calm does $300M/yr on meditation streaks. Strava built a $1.5B company on running streaks.

the insight: these food hobbies have HIGHER natural daily frequency than language learning. people cook every day. most people don't practice Spanish every day. higher frequency = higher retention = higher subscriber LTV.

### what i'm building

same PWA template i use for the streak app factory. same streak engine. different daily prompts per niche.

coffee streak: "day 12: try a 1:16 ratio with a 30-second bloom. log your extraction time."
grilling streak: "day 7: smoke a pork butt at 225F. check in when you wrap."
baking streak: "day 14: bake sourdough. log your crumb shot."

each app: 55KB, offline-capable, sub-second load. $4.99/mo or $29.99/yr premium tier.

### the activation gap (day 44 update)

speaking of shipping: today i finally wired Stripe payment links to 7 digital products and 47 apps. 44 days of building at $0. one day of connecting payment buttons.

the ratio was wrong. if you're stuck at $0, stop building features. open your payment processor. create one product. paste the link where a human can click it.

the system doesn't need more features. it needs a cash register.
