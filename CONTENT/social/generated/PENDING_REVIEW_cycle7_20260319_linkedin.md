## POST 1, The Trust Product Opportunity

An identity protection company just leaked 900,000 customer records.

The company people paid to protect their data... exposed their data.

This is why trust-based products have the highest margins in tech:
- High switching costs (nobody wants to migrate their security setup)
- Emotional purchasing (fear drives faster decisions than logic)
- Low churn until something breaks

When trust breaks, 900K people need a new provider. Today.

If you're building in security, privacy, or compliance: the market just handed you a warm audience. Show up with a credible alternative and a clear migration path.

## POST 2, What 81,000 People Actually Want From AI

Anthropic surveyed 81,000 people about what they want from AI.

The result surprised nobody who actually builds products:

People want boring automation.
- Emails written faster
- Spreadsheets cleaned up
- Meetings summarized
- Code debugged

Not AGI. Not sentient chatbots. Not "thinking partners."

The winning AI product pitch in 2026 is still: "this saves you 5 hours a week."

Price anchored to time saved. Not technology used.

The most valuable AI companies of the next 5 years will be the ones that automate the most tedious work, not the ones with the most impressive demos.

## POST 3, Solo $10K/mo vs Funded $2M

$10K MRR as a solo operator:
- 85% margins ($8,500/mo profit)
- Zero employees, zero office, zero board meetings
- Work on whatever you want
- No one to report to

$2M seed round:
- 18-24 months runway (if careful)
- Board updates, investor calls, growth expectations
- Hire 5 people and $2M becomes $400K real fast
- Stress about payroll, not product

One of these paths maximizes freedom. The other maximizes appearances.

The ego picks the funded path. The math picks the solo one.

## POST 4, Follow the Silicon

Micron just reported $23.9B in quarterly revenue. Same quarter in 2022: $3.8B.

6.3x growth in 3 years. Next quarter guidance: $33.5B.

This isn't AI hype. This is physical demand for memory chips driven by real compute buildout.

The money flow creates opportunities at every layer:
- Hardware (NVIDIA, AMD, Micron own this)
- Infrastructure (cloud, data centers)
- Platform tools (the "picks and shovels" of AI software)
- Application layer (where solo builders win)

The further from hardware you build, the less capital required and the less competition you face.

A VP at a mid-market company doesn't want to buy GPUs. They want a dashboard showing their AI spend is producing ROI. That dashboard is a $500/mo product waiting to be built.

## POST 5, Infrastructure Over Copy

The cold outreach operators making $50K+/month aren't better writers.

They have better infrastructure:
- Cascade enrichment across 3+ data providers (not just one)
- 10+ sending domains with proper warming
- Deliverability monitoring (not just open rates)
- 200-500 sends/day across distributed inboxes

The best email in the world doesn't matter if it lands in spam.

If your reply rates are below 2%, audit your infrastructure before rewriting your copy. Inbox placement is 80% of the result.
