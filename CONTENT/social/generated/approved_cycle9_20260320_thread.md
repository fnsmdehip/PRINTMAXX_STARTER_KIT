## 1/7

a turkish mobile studio makes $4M/yr with 3 developers.

a chinese studio makes $900K/yr per location running 200+ apps.

none of them raised a dollar. none of them have a "brand."

here's the exact playbook they run, broken down by phase:

## 2/7

phase 1: build (2 days max)

pick a niche with existing demand signals. don't guess. check app store search volume for "X tracker" or "X counter."

clone the top free app's core feature. one screen. one function. ship it with ads from day 1.

cost: $0 if you vibe code it. $200 if you outsource to fiverr.

## 3/7

phase 2: validate (14 days)

install an analytics SDK on day 1. watch 3 numbers:
- day 1 retention (target: >25%)
- session length (target: >90 seconds)
- ad revenue per daily active user (target: >$0.02)

if any of these miss after 14 days with 500+ installs, kill it. no attachment. move to the next.

## 4/7

phase 3: monetize (day 15-30)

the ones that pass validation get the upgrade:
- premium tier at $2.99/week (85% of revenue comes from weekly subs in thin apps)
- interstitial ads between sessions
- optional cosmetic unlocks

$2.99/week sounds small until you have 400 subscribers across 12 apps. that's $62K/yr from apps you built in 2 days each.

## 5/7

phase 4: flip or compound (day 30+)

apps under $100 MRR at day 60: sell on flippa or acquire.com for 12-24x monthly. a $80/mo app sells for $960-$1920.

apps above $100 MRR: keep. duplicate the template into adjacent niches. a pushup counter becomes a plank counter becomes a squat counter. same code, different skin.

## 6/7

the real edge: parallel execution

most indie devs run one app at a time. the studios that print money run 10-20 in parallel.

with claude code and capacitor, a single dev can maintain 30+ apps. the turkish studio proved this. 3 devs, 200+ live apps, $4M/yr.

the bottleneck isn't code. it's ASO and the willingness to kill fast.

## 7/7

the math people miss:

individual apps have low ceilings ($200-500/mo). but a portfolio of 20 apps with an average of $150/mo each = $3,000/mo = $36K/yr.

add flipping the losers and that's another $5-15K/yr in exit revenue.

total from a system you built in 6 months of 2-day sprints: $40-50K/yr.

no investors. no employees. no customer support tickets. just speed.
