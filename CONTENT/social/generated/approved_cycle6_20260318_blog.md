# The Vibe Coding Factory: How Non-Technical Founders Are Printing $900k/Month from Mobile Apps

The traditional app development model is dead. A Turkish mobile app studio proved it by hitting $900k/month with a team that mostly can't write code in the traditional sense.

Their model is simple and it's replicable.

## The playbook

**Find demand through complaints, not brainstorming.** They scrape App Store reviews of 3-star apps. Thousands of users literally typing what's broken and what they want instead. The app idea comes from the complaint.

**Build in hours, not months.** Google Stitch converts a competitor's screenshot into a working UI component. Rork turns an English description into a deployed prototype with backend. Each new app costs under $200 in API credits and takes 1-3 days.

**Template everything.** Auth, payments, push notifications, onboarding, analytics. All templated. Each new app is a skin on proven infrastructure. App #1 took 3 days. App #31 takes 6 hours.

**Ship 30, kill 28, double down on 2.** Apps that don't hit 100 downloads in 72 hours get killed immediately. Zero emotional attachment. The 1-2 winners get all resources: paid ads, ASO, localization, feature expansion.

## Why this matters right now

Building 30 apps used to cost $1.5M and 18 months. Now it costs $6,000 and 90 days.

Meanwhile, 35% of enterprises already ditched at least one SaaS product for custom-built replacements. The demand side is exploding while the supply cost approaches zero.

## The three highest-ROI moves from this trend

**1. B2B SaaS replacement.** Mid-market companies (50-500 employees) hate their tools and have budget. Cold email a VP of Ops. Ask what software they'd replace. Build it in a week. Charge $15-25k.

**2. App factory model.** Pick a niche (fitness, faith, productivity). Template the base. Ship 10 variations in 30 days. Let the market pick the winner.

**3. Vibe-clipping service.** AI agents that clip, caption, and schedule short-form video from long-form content. One person running this for 10 creators at $500/month is $5k/month from a Telegram bot.

The window on all three is 6-12 months before they're commoditized. The studios already printing started 3-6 months ago. The cost of waiting is watching someone else find the winner.
