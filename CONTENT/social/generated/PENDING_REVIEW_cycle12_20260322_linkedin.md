# LinkedIn Content -- Cycle 12

## Post 1: The Tracker vs Teacher Gap

Every health app on your phone is a tracker.

MyFitnessPal counts calories.
Strava logs miles.
Whoop measures HRV.

None of them teach you the science behind what they measure.

I ran competitive analysis across 15 health categories this week.

Combined community: 80M users.
Apps that teach daily science as a streak: 0.

The insight: when every competitor tracks, the blue ocean is teaching.

Recovery science (14.2M community, zero education apps).
Nutrition science (8.5M community, zero education apps).
Flow state (6.5M community, zero education apps).

The content bottleneck that kept this category empty? AI solved it. 365 accurate daily lessons per niche is now a weekend project.

We're building all 15.

The pattern works beyond health: every category that's full of tools has room for a daily educator.

## Post 2: Apple, PWAs, and the Native Bet

Apple is actively crippling PWAs on Mobile Safari.

pwa.gripe documents the ways Apple intentionally degrades the web app experience on iOS.

For context: we have 47 PWAs deployed. This directly affects us.

The uncomfortable truth: Apple does this because PWAs bypass the 30% App Store cut. It's not a technical limitation. It's a business decision.

Our response: Capacitor + RevenueCat for native iOS. Keep PWAs as the free tier. Native app as the premium path.

The teams that survive platform risk are the ones that don't bet on a single distribution channel.
