## Subject: the $51M prayer app and what it teaches about niche software

---

i spent this week analyzing the revenue data behind every major habit tracker and wellness app. the numbers tell a story that contradicts most of what indie hackers believe about building apps.

**the revenue ladder, ranked:**

- Hallow (catholic prayer app): $51M/yr
- Finch (virtual pet self-care): $36M/yr
- Streaks (generic habit tracker): $360K/yr
- Habitify (cross-platform habits): ~$200K/yr

notice anything? the two apps making real money aren't marketed as habit trackers. one is a prayer app. one is a virtual pet. the "habit tracker" category barely cracks six figures annually.

**niche beats generic by 100x.** Hallow serves 1.3 billion catholics. Streaks serves "anyone who wants to track habits." the specific audience wins by two orders of magnitude.

**this week's most interesting finding:**

a brand new app called Habi just launched with a single feature: streak protection. miss a day, don't lose your streak. that's the entire product.

and it's working. because if you read the 1-star reviews on every habit tracker, they all say the same thing: "i was sick for one day and lost my 200-day streak."

hundreds of reviews. same complaint. for years. nobody built the fix until now.

the lesson: don't build 50 features nobody asked for. build 1 feature everyone's been screaming about. the signal is in the complaints, not the feature requests.

**what i'm doing with this data:**

i've built 30+ niche streak trackers. yoga, pushups, photography, coding, prayer, water intake, beat-making, music theory. each one targets a specific reddit community with 500K to 5.4M users.

the strategy: Hallow proved niche works. Habi proved one killer feature works. reddit proved the distribution channel exists. now i'm connecting those three dots.

**the honest update:**

day 45. 171 live websites. 363 automation scripts. 33 autonomous agents running competitive intel, distribution, and content generation.

$0 revenue.

the build-to-sell ratio is literally infinity (division by zero). this is the most documented version of the builder trap you'll find anywhere.

but the shift happened this week. ran a distribution audit: 147 of 171 sites have zero promotion. the 24 sites with at least one distribution channel get all the traffic.

the constraint was never building speed. it's distribution speed. restructuring everything around that.

**one thing to try this week:**

if you have a product with zero distribution, pick one community where your users hang out. write one genuine post that helps someone in that community. don't link your product. just be useful.

the signups come from your profile, not your pitch.
