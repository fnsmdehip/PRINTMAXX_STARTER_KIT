## The shift from 10 blue links to 1 answer

50% of consumers now use AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Mode) for product discovery. They ask a question. They get one synthesized answer.

The brands inside that answer win. Everyone else is invisible.

This is Generative Engine Optimization (GEO). McKinsey projects $750B in consumer spending will route through AI-generated answers by 2028. The distribution rules are changing faster than most businesses can adapt.

## What 680 million citations revealed

A complete analysis of 680 million citations across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Mode showed clear patterns in what gets cited.

### Content that gets cited

| Factor | Impact | Implementation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Structured data tables | +35% citation rate | Use HTML tables with real numbers, not prose |
| FAQ sections | +30% citation rate | Direct one-sentence answers to common questions |
| Specific statistics | +40% citation rate | "12,847 users" not "many users" |
| Schema markup | +25% citation rate | JSON-LD structured data on every page |
| Reddit presence | +3-4x Perplexity citations | Detailed answers with data in relevant subreddits |

### Content that gets ignored

- Generic blog posts without data points
- Listicles with no specific numbers
- AI-generated content that reads formulaic
- Pages without schema markup
- Thin content under 500 words

## Platform-specific strategies

Each AI engine has different citation preferences.

**ChatGPT** prioritizes authority. Well-cited content from established domains with structured data wins. If you have proprietary data or original research, format it in tables and cite your sources.

**Perplexity** prioritizes community signals. Reddit threads get cited 3-4x more than equivalent blog posts. Real user experiences with specific numbers (revenue, conversion rates, time saved) are gold. A detailed Reddit answer in your niche is now a distribution strategy.

**Google AI Mode** uses a hybrid model. It combines structured web data with community signals. Schema markup and Core Web Vitals compliance are table stakes. The "Helpful Content" filter penalizes thin, AI-generated pages aggressively.

## The Princeton study

Princeton researchers tested GEO tactics systematically and found three changes that boost AI citation rates by 30-40%:

1. **Add data tables** to landing pages. AI engines extract tabular data more reliably than prose.
2. **Write direct FAQ answers.** One question, one sentence. No preamble.
3. **Cite specific statistics** from named sources. Generic claims ("studies show") get ignored.

These three tactics require no technical expertise. Just content discipline.

## Measuring GEO performance

Traditional SEO metrics (rankings, impressions, click-through rates) don't capture GEO performance.

Track these instead:

- **AI citations:** How often your brand appears in AI-generated answers
- **Citation context:** Are you cited as authority, example, or source?
- **Platform distribution:** Which AI engines cite you most?
- **Query coverage:** Which questions in your niche trigger your content?

Tools like Ahrefs and Semrush are adding AI citation tracking. Manual spot-checking works for now: search your brand and key terms in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Mode weekly.
