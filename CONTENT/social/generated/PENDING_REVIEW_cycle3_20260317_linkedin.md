## POST 2, Gov Micro-Purchases (LinkedIn Format)

A number most solopreneurs never hear: $700B.

That's annual US government purchasing spend. For purchases under $10,000, there's no formal bidding process required. You register on SAM.gov, find open requests, and submit.

No past performance requirement. No competitive bid process. Just capability and availability.

I've been watching IT consulting requests under $10K. Agencies that can't hire fast enough post micro-purchase requests for website audits, data cleanup, PDF accessibility fixes, and report generation.

These aren't enterprise contracts. They're task-level work orders. Perfect for one-person operations.

The 90% who don't know about SAM.gov are leaving money on the table. The 10% who do are billing $5-8K per engagement.

## POST 3, Cold Email 2026 (LinkedIn Format)

Something shifted in cold email this year.

The {first_name} merge token, the foundation of every outbound sequence for the past decade, now triggers spam filters. Gmail's pattern detection caught up. Every AI outreach tool uses the same merge fields. "Personalization" became the anti-personalization signal.

What works in 2026: business context personalization. Reference their recent product launch. Mention a feature they shipped last Tuesday. Cite a blog post from 3 days ago.

It's effort-based personalization vs. template-based personalization.

The people sending 10,000 emails per day are hitting spam folders. The people sending 50 deeply researched emails per day are getting 15-20% reply rates.

Volume died. Relevance won.
