## LinkedIn Post 1 - Platform Ecosystem Timing

4 companies launched products built on Claude Code on the same day.

Scheduled automation. Session sharing. Context compression. AI-readiness scoring.

This is the exact pattern that created billion-dollar ecosystems around Shopify, Stripe, and Notion:

1. Tool gets popular
2. Power users notice gaps
3. They build the missing pieces
4. Those pieces become standalone businesses

The playbook hasn't changed in 15 years.

If you're using any developer tool daily, open its GitHub issues. Read the top 20 feature requests. If any of them could be a product, you just found your next opportunity.

The best businesses aren't inventions. They're missing features someone finally ships.

## LinkedIn Post 2 - The AI Bloat Backlash

Microsoft just rolled back Copilot features from Windows.

The company spending $10B+ on AI admitted users don't want it forced into every interaction.

Two types of AI integration are emerging:

Pushed AI: chatbots bolted onto every feature, sparkle emojis everywhere, users annoyed.

Pulled AI: invisible backend processing, automation, analysis. Users never think about it. Product just works better.

The market rewards the second type. The VC pitch deck rewards the first.

Build for the market.

## LinkedIn Post 3 - The Real SaaS Timeline

Someone just hit $1K MRR after 9 months and shared everything that went wrong.

The actual timeline:
- Month 1-3: Built a product nobody wanted
- Month 4-5: Rebuilt from scratch using real feedback
- Month 6-7: Got the first paying customer
- Month 8-9: Ground to $1K/month

5 months of building the wrong thing. Then 4 months of iteration to the first meaningful milestone.

This is the timeline nobody shares in launch tweets. The "we just hit $10K MRR" posts skip the months of silence.

The difference between founders who make it and those who don't isn't talent. It's whether they're still iterating at month 5 when nothing is working.
