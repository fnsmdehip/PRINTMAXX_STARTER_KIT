## 4. POST SCHEDULE (Post-Warmup, Day 22+)

### Daily Cadence: 4-6 Posts/Day

Do not exceed 6 posts per day in the first month of full ops (Days 22-52). Scale to 8-10 only after you see consistent engagement velocity.

Space posts minimum 2 hours apart. The algorithm applies a decaying multiplier to repeated posts from the same author:
- Post 1: 100% score
- Post 2 (within 2 hrs): ~80% score
- Post 3 (within 2 hrs): ~60% score
- Post 4+: diminishing returns

### Daily Posting Schedule (ET)

| Time | Content Type | Format | Purpose |
|------|-------------|--------|---------|
| 7:30 AM | Build update / value drop | Single tweet, 2-5 lines | Catch morning scrollers. Specific number from yesterday's agent runs. What happened overnight. |
| 11:00 AM | Engagement bait | Poll, binary choice, question, or "rate my stack" | Drive replies. Algorithm weights replies highest. Midday is peak engagement window. |
| 2:30 PM | Thread or quote tweet | 5-7 tweet thread OR value-add QT | Authority building. Deep dive on a method, tactic, or system you built. |
| 6:00 PM | Industry commentary / hot take | Single tweet, 1-3 lines, opinion format | Ride the day's conversation. Reference something from your feed with your data. |

**Optional 5th and 6th posts:**

- Weekend bonus: Meme or visual post (screenshot of terminal output, dashboard, agent logs)

### Engagement Loops

An engagement loop is when your reply to someone's reply creates a sub-thread that other people join.

**How to create engagement loops:**
1. Post reply bait (binary choice works best)
2. When first 3-5 replies come in, reply to EACH ONE with a genuine follow-up question
3. Your follow-up creates a sub-thread
4. Other people see the sub-thread and join
5. The algorithm counts each sub-reply as additional engagement on YOUR original post

**Example loop:**

Your post: "claude code or cursor? for autonomous agents, only one works."

Reply from @user1: "cursor all day. the UI is better."

Your reply to @user1: "curious. have you tried running a claude code session for 6+ hours straight? genuine question. i can't do that in cursor."

This creates a debate sub-thread. Every reply in that sub-thread boosts your original post's algorithm score.

## 8. GREY GROWTH EDGE (Medium Aggression)

### What Twitter Actually Detects (Specific Numbers)

**Rate limits that trigger automated review:**

| Action | Safe Limit (New Account <30 days) | Safe Limit (Aged Account 30d+) | Triggers Review | Triggers Restriction |
|--------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Follows/day | 50 | 200 | 100 (new) / 300 (aged) | 200 (new) / 400 (aged) |
| Unfollows/day | 50 | 200 | 100 (new) / 300 (aged) | 200 (new) / 400 (aged) |
| Likes/day | 200 | 500 | 300 (new) / 800 (aged) | 500 (new) / 1000 (aged) |
| Tweets/day | 10 | 25 | 15 (new) / 40 (aged) | 20 (new) / 50 (aged) |
| DMs/day | 20 | 50 | 30 (new) / 80 (aged) | 50 (new) / 100 (aged) |
| Retweets/day | 50 | 200 | 100 (new) / 300 (aged) | 200 (new) / 500 (aged) |

**The 70% Rule:** Never exceed 70% of the "Triggers Review" column. That gives you safety margin for burst activity.

### What Triggers Shadowban

A shadowban means your replies don't show up for others, your posts don't appear in search, and you get zero impressions on For You feed. It's invisible to you but fatal to growth.

**Confirmed shadowban triggers:**

1. **Rapid follow/unfollow** -- Following 100 accounts and unfollowing 80 within 24 hours. The algorithm detects the ratio and pattern.
2. **Copy-paste replies** -- Sending the same text (or 80%+ similar text) to multiple accounts. Even if personalized slightly.
3. **URL spam** -- Posting the same link in replies to 5+ different tweets in an hour.
4. **Hashtag stuffing** -- Using more than 3 hashtags per tweet. On X in 2026, hashtags actively hurt reach beyond 1-2 relevant ones.
5. **Follow velocity spikes** -- Going from 10 follows/day to 200 follows/day overnight. The change rate matters, not just the absolute number.
6. **Engagement from suspicious accounts** -- If your engagement pods include accounts that are themselves flagged, their engagement on your posts can flag YOU.
7. **Keyword spam** -- Repeatedly using the same marketing phrases ("DM me for...") in replies across multiple threads.

**Check if shadowbanned:** `shadowban.eu` -- run your handle through it weekly.

### What's SAFE and Gives Edge

**Strategic follow/unfollow (Safe if done correctly):**
- Follow 20-30 niche accounts per day (never more than 50)
- Wait 3-7 days before unfollowing anyone
- Never unfollow more than you follow in a given week
- Unfollow accounts that don't follow back after 7 days in batches of 10-15 per day
- Never follow/unfollow the same account within 30 days
- Mix in some accounts you'll keep permanently (never unfollow the Tier 1 accounts)

**Timing patterns that look natural:**
- Vary your posting times by +/- 30 minutes each day (don't post at exactly 8:00 AM every day)
- Take 1 day per week where you post less (Saturday or Sunday)
- Space actions throughout the day (not 50 likes in 5 minutes)
- Have "off" periods of 2-3 hours where you don't engage at all (simulate sleeping, working, eating)

**Engagement velocity management:**
- First 15 minutes after posting: do NOT like your own post from other accounts or ask friends to engage immediately. Let 2-3 organic engagements happen first.
- Ramp engagement gradually. Post, wait 5 min, first reply comes in, reply to it, wait 5 min, another comes in.
- If a post gets zero engagement in 30 minutes, don't panic-boost it. Let it die. Post a better one later.

### What's RISKY but Worth It

**Reply-guy strategy to big accounts:**
- Risk: Twitter may flag your replies as "This reply may be spam" if you reply to the same large account too frequently
- Mitigation: Don't reply to the same account more than 2x per day. Rotate through your Tier 1-3 lists. Vary reply length and format.
- Worth it because: Being in the first 5 replies of a 300K+ account is the single fastest follower acquisition method that doesn't cost money.

**Thread hijacking (adding to trending threads):**
- Risk: If your addition looks spammy or self-promotional, you get reported
- Mitigation: Only add genuinely valuable content. Reference specific data. Never drop a link.
- Worth it because: A well-placed reply in a viral thread (10K+ likes) can get you 50-100 profile visits in a day

**Aggressive posting cadence:**
- Risk: Author diversity scorer reduces individual post scores if you post too frequently
- Mitigation: Space 2+ hours between posts. Quality over quantity. Better to post 4 bangers than 10 mediocre tweets.
- Worth it because: More at-bats = more chances for something to hit. The one tweet that goes semi-viral pays for 50 that don't.

### What to NEVER Do

1. **Follow 200+ accounts in a day** -- Instant review trigger, possible restriction
2. **Copy-paste the same reply** -- Even changing 1-2 words isn't enough. Rewrite from scratch.
3. **Use hashtag spam** -- More than 2 hashtags per tweet actively hurts reach on X
4. **Post links in your first 10 tweets** -- New accounts posting links get flagged as promotional
5. **Buy followers** -- Ruins your engagement ratio permanently. 10K followers with 2 likes per post = algorithmic death.
6. **Use third-party Chrome extensions** -- Detected on all platforms. Instant flag.
7. **Automate DMs** -- Auto-DM on follow is detectable and hated by users. Manual only.
8. **Like spam** -- Liking 500 posts in an hour. The algorithm detects burst patterns.
9. **Reply to your own alt accounts** -- Twitter detects shared IP/device patterns between accounts
10. **Use automation before Day 22** -- The warmup exists for a reason. Don't shortcut it.

### Mobile Proxy Benefits

Using a mobile proxy makes your session look like a real mobile user on a cell network. This matters because:
- X assigns higher trust to mobile sessions (real users use phones)
- Mobile IPs rotate naturally (carrier-grade NAT)
- Reduces fingerprinting risk if using multiple accounts later

**Setup for @PRINTMAXXER (single account):**
- Use your actual phone on your actual mobile data for the warmup period (Days 1-21)
- This is the cheapest and most effective approach for a single account
- Only need a proxy if running multiple accounts or automating

**If you add Account B later:**
- Different device (separate phone, or separate browser profile on desktop)
- Different IP (mobile proxy from Proxy-Seller at $90/mo or your second phone's data)
- Never log into both accounts from the same IP or device

### Multiple Session Patterns

Different IP + same natural behavior = Twitter sees two separate genuine users. This is how multi-account works safely.

**What matters:**
- Consistent IP per account (don't bounce between WiFi and mobile)
- Consistent device per account (don't switch between phone and desktop mid-session)
- Natural timing per account (each account has its own posting schedule)
- Different content per account (not cross-posting the same tweets)

3. Vary your reply length and format significantly
4. Do NOT delete the flagged replies (deleting makes it worse)
5. Post 2-3 original tweets to balance your reply ratio
6. Check shadowban.eu

**If shadowbanned (replies invisible, zero impressions, no search presence):**
1. STOP all posting and engagement for 48-72 hours
2. Do NOT delete posts (makes it worse)
3. After 72 hours, post 1 text-only tweet per day for 5 days
4. Manual engagement only (likes, scrolling, reading) for 7 days
5. No links for 14 days after shadowban clears
6. Resume at 30% previous volume
7. If shadowban persists after 7 days, check if a specific post triggered it and appeal

**If account suspended:**
1. Appeal immediately through Twitter's form (don't wait)
2. Be polite. Claim you're a new account building a community.
3. Do NOT mention automation, bots, or growth tactics
4. Provide ID if requested
5. If denied: the account is dead. Start Account B from scratch.
6. Never create a new account from the same device/IP/email as the suspended one

**If your engagement suddenly drops 50%+ overnight:**
1. Check shadowban.eu first
2. Check if Twitter had an algorithm update (follow @TwitterDev, @elonmusk for announcements)
3. Review your last 5 posts for possible triggers (links, hashtags, flagged content)
4. Post 2-3 pure text tweets with high-engagement hooks
5. Increase manual engagement on other accounts for 48 hours
6. If persists for 5+ days with no shadowban detected, likely an algorithm shift. Adjust content strategy.
