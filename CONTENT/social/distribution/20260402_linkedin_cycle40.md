## POST 1 — Building in public: 388 sites, $0 revenue (professional reflection)
**Target audience:** Founders, solopreneurs, indie builders, automation practitioners
**Asset:** printmaxx-site.surge.sh (brand page, general)

```
I built 388 websites and deployed them all.

Most of them rank. Some are on page 1.

$0 in revenue.

Here's what happened, and why I think it's actually useful data for anyone building automated systems:

The automation worked exactly as designed. 33 agents running. 530 scripts. 17,000+ hot leads identified and scored. SEO comparison pages generating organic traffic.

The failure was sequencing, not execution.

I automated distribution before I had a payment path. I automated lead generation before I had a working landing page with a buy button. I automated content generation before I had an account to post from.

The lesson isn't "automation is bad." The lesson is: automate the middle of the funnel only after both ends are working manually.

If I were starting over:
Day 1 — Stripe account. One product. One landing page with the buy button tested.
Day 2 — One channel with a working post.
Day 3 — Automate day 1 and day 2.

The infrastructure I built is genuinely powerful. But infrastructure without endpoints is just complexity.

Fixing the endpoints this week. Will report back.

---

Anyone else who's fallen into the "build before validate" trap for automation systems? Curious what the unlock was.
```

## POST 2 — NutriAI launch: real vs. fake data in health apps (professional angle)
**Asset:** nutriai.surge.sh
**Target audience:** Health tech professionals, startup founders, product managers, iOS developers

```
The nutrition app market has a real data problem.

Most apps use Harris-Benedict — a formula from 1919, validated on 239 subjects, with a known error rate of 10-15% for moderately active adults.

The clinical standard since 1990 has been Mifflin-St Jeor, which reduces that error to 5-8% in most populations.

The apps you use every day almost certainly aren't using it.

I built NutriAI to fix this:
→ Mifflin-St Jeor TDEE calculation (your actual baseline, not a generic estimate)
→ AI food scanning via photo or text (Gemini Flash — far faster than manual entry)
→ Macro split that adjusts to your specific goal, not a static 40/30/30
→ No account required, works in browser

Live: nutriai.surge.sh

More broadly: this is a recurring pattern in consumer health apps. The barrier to using real clinical formulas is low. The app developers choosing not to isn't a technical limitation — it's a product decision.

Interested in the clinical accuracy differences between TDEE methods? Happy to share the research.
```
