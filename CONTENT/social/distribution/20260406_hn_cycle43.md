## INDIE HACKERS POST — cnsnt Launch

**Title:**
```
Launched cnsnt-web: a client-side consent form tool. No server. AES-256-GCM. Here's what I built and why.
```

**Body:**
```
I launched cnsnt-web.surge.sh today.

The problem it solves:

I needed digital consent forms for a project. The options were:
- DocuSign ($25/mo minimum, stores everything on their servers)
- Google Forms (free, but Google owns your signature data)
- Jotform (bloated, phone-home analytics by default)
- Paper forms (not searchable, not auditable)

None of these are "you own your data, fully encrypted, works offline."

So I built one.

**Technical choices I'm happy with:**

Web Crypto API instead of a JS crypto library — the browser's built-in crypto is audited, doesn't add supply chain risk, and is available in every modern browser without a CDN dependency.

PBKDF2 at 100,000 iterations — slower key derivation means brute force attacks are expensive. The right tradeoff for a consent form tool where the passphrase is the only authentication factor.

IndexedDB for storage — structured, queryable, local-first. The audit log is hash-chained so tampering with a record breaks the chain.

**What I shipped:**
- 11 consent form templates
- Full audit log per form and signature
- PDF export with embedded HMAC integrity signature
- Optional S3-compatible backup (you provide the endpoint)
- Free tier (3 forms/mo) + paid tier ($4.99/mo, $29.99/yr via Stripe)

**Metrics so far:**
- Day 1 (today)
- Traffic source: this post

**Channels I'm testing:**
- r/privacy and r/selfhosted (technical audience, appreciates encryption specifics)
- LinkedIn (professionals who need consent forms: photographers, coaches, healthcare practitioners)
- Direct cold outreach to freelance photographers (frequent consent form users, pay for tools that save admin time)

Feedback welcome — especially on the pricing and whether the self-hosting story is clear enough for the audience that would actually use it.

Link: cnsnt-web.surge.sh
```
