## POST 1 — Day 57 / builder psychology (thought leadership)

57 days. 398 deployed products. $0 revenue.

That number is both impressive and damning.

The building isn't the bottleneck. I've got streak trackers, habit apps, cold email tools, supplement comparison pages, PDF guides, desktop apps. Built and live.

The bottleneck is 4 accounts that need to be created: Stripe, Gumroad, 2 affiliate programs.

Each one takes 20-40 minutes.

None of them have been done because building the next thing is more interesting than making an account.

This is a documented founder trap. The activation energy for admin is disproportionately high relative to its actual difficulty. You can ship a working app in 2 hours. You can delay making a Stripe account for 2 months.

I'm naming it publicly so I have to fix it this week.

## POST 2 — supplement affiliate cluster (B2B angle, SEO case study)

Built a 6-page content cluster in the men's health supplement space.

Pages: testosterone, prostate, blood pressure, joint health, memory, sleep — all targeting men over 55.

Each page links to the others. Every page has clinical evidence tables, dosage comparisons, and drug interaction warnings (which almost no affiliate sites include).

Traffic source: organic search (no paid ads, no social).

The drug interaction angle is the edge. Most supplement comparison sites are thin content. Adding actual pharmacology detail (what supplements conflict with blood thinners, BP meds, diabetes meds) creates something that clinical search queries find.
