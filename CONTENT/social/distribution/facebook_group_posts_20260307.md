## POST 5 — Solopreneur/Indie hacker groups (version A)
Target groups: Indie Hackers Community, Solopreneur Society, Build in Public, Side Projects & Indie Hacking
Tone: build-in-public honesty, zero guru framing, real numbers, real lessons

---

day 42 of building in public.

122 sites live on the internet.
$0 revenue.

before anyone says "build something people want" — fair. but the harder lesson i've learned over 6 weeks isn't about what to build. it's about distribution being a completely different skill than building.

here's what i got wrong:

i assumed "deployed = discoverable." it doesn't. surge.sh gives you a URL. google doesn't know that URL exists for months unless you actively build links to it.

i thought volume would compensate for targeting. 122 sites in 7 niches sounds like a lot until you realize you need to find where those 7 audiences actually spend time online and show up there. a site nobody sees is the same as no site.

i underestimated how long it takes for free tools to compound. the apps that are getting traction now (focuslock, habitforge) started getting shared in small communities about 3 weeks after launch. the ones i dropped into big generic groups went nowhere.

the build to distribute ratio should probably be 40/60 not 80/20 like i was doing.

122 sites live. still at $0. following along to see if this turns into something by day 90.

## POST 6 — Solopreneur/Indie hacker groups (version B)
Target groups: Bootstrapped Founders, One Person Business, Ship It Weekly, Indie Makers
Tone: slightly more tactical, specific lessons structured for people who want frameworks, honest failure accounting

---

built 122 sites in 42 days. made $0.

the honest post-mortem on what went wrong with distribution:

mistake 1: i shipped to reddit first. r/webdev gives feedback on code quality, not market validation. learned the group targeting matters more than the platform. should have gone to niche subreddits for each tool's audience from day 1.

mistake 2: cold-posting links without context. links in posts get buried by most FB and reddit algorithms. the posts that actually drove traffic were the ones where i talked about the problem the tool solves and mentioned the link almost as an afterthought.

mistake 3: no email capture on day 1. focuslock-app.surge.sh has 0 emails collected because i didn't add a capture form until week 3. every early visitor is gone with no way back.

what's actually working now: posting in small specific communities (under 5k members) where i can actually participate in conversations before dropping a link. conversion rate is probably 10x higher vs big groups.

122 sites. $0. day 42. following along.
