## POST 1

**Title:** Show IH: SEMrush vs Ahrefs comparison page — getting organic traffic from Google

**Body:**

Built a comparison page for SEMrush vs Ahrefs: semrush-vs-ahrefs.surge.sh

Honest about what this is: a page targeting comparison keywords with affiliate links on the side. Not trying to dress it up as pure educational content.

The strategy is straightforward. "SEMrush vs Ahrefs" gets searched thousands of times a month by people who are actively deciding between two $100-200/mo tools. Those people are ready to buy. A comparison page that actually answers the question well can capture that intent.

What makes a comparison page actually rank vs. just exist:

The page needs to take a position. "Both are good, it depends" gets no traffic. "Ahrefs wins for backlink analysis, SEMrush wins for PPC research" gives Google something to serve.

It needs specific data, not vague claims. I included actual pricing tiers, specific feature comparisons, and use case breakdowns for solo founders vs. agencies vs. ecommerce teams.

The content gap I saw: most existing comparison content is either written by affiliates who clearly haven't used both tools, or by the tools themselves. There's a gap for someone who actually ran both and wrote it plainly.

Current status: page is live, indexed, building authority. Not seeing meaningful traffic yet. Will report back in 30 days with organic data.

If you've built comparison pages that rank, I'd want to know what moved the needle fastest for you.

Week 7. Three apps live. Zero revenue. Here's where things stand.

The apps:

Quran Streak: daily Quran reading tracker, streak counter, offline-first, no account required. quran-streak-landing.surge.sh

Gita Streak: same concept for Bhagavad Gita. Daily reading goals, streak tracking. gita-streak-landing.surge.sh

Why faith communities? The App Store for faith tools is genuinely underserved. Most existing apps are either paid with aggressive upsells, require accounts for basic features, or haven't been updated since 2017. There's no technical barrier to building something better. The barrier was just someone deciding to do it.

The distribution strategy I'm testing:

Phase 1 (now): community seeding. Reddit threads in r/islam, r/hinduism, r/Sikh. Facebook groups for each community. Discord servers. Posting directly where these communities already talk about faith practice. No ads.

Phase 2: App Store listings. Takes longer but compounds. The PWA gets first users fast. The app store presence brings organic discovery over time.

Phase 3: If any of the three gets 1k+ active users, I'll test a premium tier. Calendar integration, streaks with social sharing, export to PDF. Nothing that breaks the free core.

Honest status: the apps are built and deployed. The community seeding starts this week. I don't have user numbers yet because I'm reporting before the distribution push rather than after. Will post numbers in week 9.

## POST 3

**Title:** ColdMaxx: My free cold email tool — early feedback request

**Body:**

Built ColdMaxx and it's live: coldmaxx.surge.sh

What it does: cold email copy generation and outreach sequence builder. You put in your product, target persona, and goal. It outputs a 3-step sequence with subject lines, body copy, and follow-up timing.

Why I built it: I kept writing cold email sequences manually and noticed I was following the same structure every time. Problem statement, relevance hook, specific offer, low-friction CTA, two follow-ups spaced 3 days and 7 days apart. Once I saw the pattern, I built a tool around it.

The things I want feedback on:

1. Output quality. Does the generated copy feel like something you'd actually send, or does it feel like AI copy that needs a full rewrite before it's usable?

2. Persona specificity. Right now it takes a text description of the target persona. I'm considering adding a dropdown of common ICP types (B2B SaaS buyer, ecommerce brand owner, agency owner) to give it more to work with. Is that useful or does it constrain the output too much?

3. Sequence length. 3-step is the default. Should 5-step be an option? Or does more variety on the first email matter more than more touches?

The tool is free. No account needed. I'm collecting feedback before deciding whether to add a paid tier for higher volume usage.

Link: coldmaxx.surge.sh

Tear it apart.

## POST 4

**Title:** Transparent: Day 44, $0 revenue, 47 apps live. Here's what I'm fixing.

**Body:**

Day 44. $0 revenue. 47 apps and tools live. This is what's actually happening.

The honest version first: I built a lot. I haven't sold anything yet. Those are two separate problems and I've been treating them like one.

What's working:

Organic traffic is building. Pages like semrush-vs-ahrefs.surge.sh, klaviyo-alternative.surge.sh, and smartlead-vs-instantly.surge.sh are indexed and picking up long-tail keywords. This is a slow game but it compounds.

The tools themselves are functional. pdfmaxx.surge.sh (free PDF tools), roicalc.surge.sh (ROI calculator), coldmaxx.surge.sh (cold email tool) all work. They're not broken. They're just not monetized yet.

What's not working:

Distribution requires accounts I don't have yet. Posting on Product Hunt needs a real PH account with history. Getting to the front page of r/SideProject requires karma. Twitter requires an aged account. These are the actual blockers.

I've been building under the assumption that "if you deploy it, traffic will find it." That's partially true for SEO pages. It's completely false for app tools where discovery depends on community presence.

The fix:

Starting this week, I'm front-loading distribution work. Community seeding on Reddit (organically, not spam). Outreach to niche newsletters. Building the accounts that gate the distribution channels.

The apps don't need more features. They need more eyeballs.

If you've been in a similar position, i.e. built things but not yet sold anything, I want to know what the unlock was for you. Was it one distribution channel? A specific post format? A pivot to a paying audience?
