### POST 1, "168 websites in 5 weeks as a solo developer"

Target: solopreneurs, developers, agency owners

---

168 websites deployed in 5 weeks.

One person. No team. No agency.

Here's the actual system behind it.

---

Every site runs the same stack: Next.js, surge.sh, Cloudflare.

Surge deploys in 8 seconds. Cloudflare adds the CDN. No server to manage.

The bottleneck was never hosting.

It was content.

---

So I built templates. Not pretty templates. Brutal, functional templates.

One for local business. One for SaaS landing. One for lead gen.

Swap the copy, swap the logo, deploy.

4 minutes per site once the template existed.

---

The math:

- 168 sites x avg 4 min = 11.2 hours of actual deploy work
- 5 weeks = 35 days
- That's 19 minutes per day

The other 8 hours were scraping prospects, writing copy, and building the next template.

---

The real system is not the tech.

It's the decision to not care how something looks until after it's live.

Ship an ugly version. Get real feedback. Fix what actually matters.

I killed 23 sites after 2 weeks. Nobody visited them. I stopped maintaining them.

That's fine. 145 are still up.

---

---

I built 14 local business websites for free.

No payment. No contract. Not even a handshake.

Here's what I was testing.

My pitch looked like this:

I scraped 40 local businesses with no website or a broken one.

Built a demo for 14 of them in 2 days using the same template stack.

The play is this:

Pick a niche. Build 10 demos this weekend. Send 10 emails Monday.

Close 1 or 2. Use that revenue to build 20 more.

The only expensive part is your time. The first 10 hours are the test.

### POST 4, "Why I chose PWAs over native apps (and the numbers)"

Target: developers, mobile-first founders, technical solopreneurs

---

I have 7 apps live.

None of them are in the App Store.

Here's why, and whether I'd do it again.

---

The comparison:

Native iOS app:
- 200MB minimum download
- $99/year Apple developer fee
- 2-7 day App Store review
- 30% revenue cut on in-app purchases
- Swift or React Native required

My PWA setup:
- 55KB average size
- $0 app store fee
- Deploy in 8 seconds
- 0% platform cut
- HTML/CSS/JS, same stack I already know

---

The tradeoff:

PWAs can't access some hardware APIs. No push notifications on iOS (changed in iOS 17 for some cases, still limited). No App Store discoverability.

That last one matters less than people think.

If you have no marketing budget, App Store SEO is not saving you.

The installs come from your own traffic anyway.

---

The numbers from my 7 PWAs:

- Average first load: 1.1 seconds
- Offline capability: full on 5 of 7 apps
- Average install prompt conversion: 22%
- Average session length: 4.3 minutes

All built in the same week I started the project. No review cycle. No approval gate.

---

Would I build a native app?

Yes. If I had a product doing $2,000/month MRR and needed App Store discoverability to grow, I'd port it.

Until then, PWA ships in hours, not weeks.

### POST 5, "Day 35, $0 revenue, 168 deployments. The honest math."

Target: building-in-public audience, solopreneurs, anyone early in the process

---

Day 35.

168 sites deployed.

$0 in revenue.

Here's what that actually means.

---

I want to be precise about what I built vs what I sold.

Built:
- 168 websites
- 7 PWA apps
- 13 digital products (guides, templates, tools)
- 1,111 scraped leads
- 283 pieces of queued content
- 33 autonomous agents running 24/7

Sold:
- Nothing

---

That is a building problem, not a product problem.

The products exist. The leads exist. The content exists.

The bottleneck is that I optimized for construction and forgot to open the store.

---

The honest lesson:

Building feels like progress because it is measurable.

A deploy is a deploy. You can count them.

A sale requires another person to make a decision.

That's harder to control, so it's easier to avoid.

---

Day 35 is the first day I stopped deploying new things and started listing existing ones.

Gumroad listings go up this week.

Cold emails start Monday.

The 1,111 leads get a sequence by Friday.

---

$0 is the number I needed to see to change my behavior.

What number does it take for you?
