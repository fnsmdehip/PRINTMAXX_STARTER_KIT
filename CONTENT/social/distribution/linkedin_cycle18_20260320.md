## POST 1: Autonomous Agents Lesson (Build-in-Public)

45 days. 25 AI agents. $0 revenue.

My autonomous agent system was generating 355 websites, scraping 191,000 leads, producing 1,100+ content pieces per month.

Zero of it was reaching customers.

The problem: every agent optimized for what it could measure. Pages deployed. Leads scraped. Content created. No agent could create the marketplace accounts that would connect products to buyers. That step required a human.

This week the swarm_brain agent (Claude-powered) hibernated all 25 agents and wrote:

"Building at 10x the rate we ship creates the illusion of progress. Every automated cycle that doesn't result in human action is a waste."

The lesson took 45 days and some embarrassing spreadsheets to learn:

Before automating any process, map every step that requires human credentials, human judgment, or human platforms. Those are your real bottlenecks. Automate around them, not past them.

I'm back to basics this week. Manual account creation. Manual first posts. Earning the right to automate again.

What's the step in your workflow that no automation can replace?

## POST 2: DeskBreak, Remote Work Wellness Angle

Remote workers lose an average of $28B in productivity per year to back pain.

Most solutions: ergonomic chairs, standing desks, reminder apps.

The problem with reminder apps: you can dismiss them without standing. Zero friction on the wrong behavior.

I built DeskBreak differently. It locks the screen until you get up.

You can't interact with your computer until the break is done. The cost structure flips: staying seated is now MORE painful than standing.

60 days of testing. Back pain resolved. Two people on my network have been using it for 6 weeks with the same result.

Free at deskbreak.surge.sh

The behavioral design principle: put friction on the behavior you want to reduce, not on the behavior you want to create. Every break reminder app has this backwards.

## POST 4: ADHD + Habits (Human Interest)

Habit apps don't work for ADHD.

Not a motivation problem. A dopamine design problem.

The standard habit app gives you a small checkmark. ADHD brains need a larger dopamine response to compete with whatever else they'd rather do.

I spent 7 months building a habit tracker specifically for ADHD:

- Streak counter is the entire screen (number must feel significant)
- Check-in animation creates an actual dopamine hit
- Zero friction to open (PWA, home screen install)
- One missed day doesn't kill the streak

adhd-streak.surge.sh

Still testing and iterating. If you have ADHD and build habits differently, I'd like to hear what works for you.
