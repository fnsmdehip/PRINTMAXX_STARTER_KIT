```
One of the quiet risks of AI-assisted writing: you don't know your own tells.

LLMs have detectable patterns that differ from human writing. Burstiness (sentence length variation), perplexity (word choice predictability), lexical diversity. Human writers naturally vary these. AI outputs cluster in tighter ranges.

The problem: if you edit AI output without understanding these signals, you might be editing for tone while leaving behind the structural signals that flaggers look for.

I built a tool that shows you exactly which signals are present in your writing, so you can edit with precision rather than guessing.

It detects:
- Burstiness patterns
- Perplexity variance
- Filler phrase density
- Lexical diversity across windows
- Paragraph entropy

Client-side only. Nothing transmitted.

Useful for: content teams doing QA, professionals who use AI assistance but want their voice to come through, anyone trying to understand what makes their writing sound human.

Free tool: ai-slop-detector.surge.sh
```
