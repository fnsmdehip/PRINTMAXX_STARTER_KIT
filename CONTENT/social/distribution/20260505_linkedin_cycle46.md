One pattern I see repeatedly in professional services:

Client consent forms collected via cloud tools — DocuSign, HelloSign, Google Forms — store sensitive client data on third-party servers.

For photographers collecting model releases, therapists getting patient consent, or freelancers signing NDAs with clients, that's a compliance consideration that often goes unexamined.

I built cnsnt as an alternative: a consent form manager that keeps everything encrypted locally.

- AES-256-GCM encryption for stored documents
- PBKDF2 key derivation with 100K iterations
- Backup = an encrypted file you control entirely

11 templates included. Desktop app (Tauri, 3.4MB). Web PWA version.

Not saying this is the right choice for everyone — there are legitimate reasons to use cloud tools. But for solo professionals who want to own their client data without cloud dependency, it's worth knowing this option exists.

## POST 3 — Day 44 honest reflection [THOUGHT LEADERSHIP]
**Format:** LinkedIn post with lesson framing

---
Day 44 of building an automated product portfolio. $0 in revenue.

The honest reflection:

I spent 44 days optimizing the wrong constraint.

538 automation scripts. 76 live apps. 192K scraped leads. 20 Gumroad products drafted.

Revenue path blocked by: no Stripe account, no Gumroad account, no Apple Dev Portal setup.

Three account setups. Roughly 70 minutes of human time. Would have unlocked the revenue path on day 1.

The lesson isn't "build less." The lesson is: identify your actual bottleneck early, and it's almost never the code.

For anyone building a product business solo:
- Set up your payment processor before your product
- Create marketplace accounts before your listings
- The code will finish. Accounts don't create themselves.

This is the kind of mistake that's obvious in hindsight and invisible in the moment because building feels productive.

The building is not the bottleneck. It almost never is.
