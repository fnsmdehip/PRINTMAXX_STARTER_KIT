## POST 1: The 45-Day Agent Autopsy (Highest Priority, Authentic Story)

**Title:** 45 days, 25 autonomous agents, $0 revenue. Here's the autopsy.

**Body:**
In January I decided to build a fully autonomous revenue system. 25 Claude-based AI agents running 24/7. CEO agent orchestrating everything. Venture agents for different revenue streams. Content agents generating and distributing. Scraper agents pulling leads and intel.

I was going to watch it make money while I slept.

**What it built in 45 days:**
- 355 websites deployed to surge.sh
- 191,000 leads scraped
- 1,100+ content pieces generated
- 384 alpha intelligence entries processed
- 337 automation scripts written
- 17 content distribution cycles completed

**What it generated:**
- $0

**The diagnosis (from the agent itself):**

This week the swarm_brain agent ran its cycle and hibernated all 25 agents. In its report it wrote:

"Building at 10x the rate we ship creates the illusion of progress. Every automated cycle that doesn't result in human action is a waste."

I didn't write that. The agent did. And it was right.

**What actually happened:**

Every agent optimized for what it could measure. Content created. Pages deployed. Leads scraped. None of them could create a Gumroad account. None could post to Reddit with an authenticated account. None could send from my Gmail. None could sign up for affiliate programs.

Those steps required a human. I never completed them.

The agents built a very sophisticated content archive. Not a business.

**What I'm doing now:**

Manual. Everything. Create the accounts. Post the first 5 pieces manually. Send the first 5 cold emails manually. List the first product manually.

No new automation until I've done 50 things by hand. Earning the automation back.

**The lesson:**

Before building any autonomous system, map every step that requires:
- A human account on an external platform
- Human judgment that can't be delegated
- A human signature or identity verification

Those are your real constraints. Build around them or remove them BEFORE automating.

I automated past mine instead of through them.

## POST 2: DeskBreak Launch Post

**Title:** Launched DeskBreak: screen locks until you get up. 60 days of testing.

**Body:**
Built this for myself. Sharing now because the results are real.

Problem: I was dismissing desk break reminders without standing up. Every reminder, every time. Then staying seated for 4 more hours.

The issue isn't motivation. It's friction. Dismissing a reminder has zero cost. So I dismiss it.

DeskBreak locks the screen. You can't interact until the break timer runs. The cost of not getting up is now higher than the cost of standing.

60 days of use. Back pain is largely gone. Two people from my network have been using it for 6 weeks with the same result.

**Technical notes:**
- PWA, installs to home screen
- Under 200 lines of code total (localStorage timer + fullscreen API)
- No server, no account, no data collection
- Works on mobile and desktop

**Live:** https://deskbreak.surge.sh

The behavioral design insight is more interesting than the code: friction belongs on the wrong behavior, not the right one. Every break reminder app has this backwards. DeskBreak just moves the friction.
