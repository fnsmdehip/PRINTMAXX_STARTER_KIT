## Post 1, PageScorer

I spent 3 months building a product nobody knew existed.

Then I wrote a landing page in a weekend and sent traffic to it.

Zero conversions. Not low conversions. Zero.

I assumed the product was wrong. Spent another month adding features. Sent more traffic. Same result.

The problem wasn't the product.

When I finally sat down and looked at the page objectively, the issues were obvious:

The headline said "The smarter way to manage your workflow." That tells you nothing. What does it do? Who is it for? What changes after you use it?

The CTA said "Get started." Started doing what? Will I need a credit card? Is there a trial? What am I agreeing to?

The page loaded in 5.8 seconds on mobile. Most visitors left before the hero section was visible.

Three fixable problems. I fixed them in a day. Conversions started happening the following week.

The experience stuck with me. Most landing page failures aren't product failures. They're communication failures that are measurable and fixable.

So I built PageScorer.

You paste any URL. It audits the page across 5 criteria that correlate with conversion: value prop clarity, CTA specificity, social proof presence, form friction, and load time. It gives you a letter grade from A to F, and for anything below a B, it gives you specific line-level fixes.

Not "improve your copy." Specific: "Your headline doesn't answer what the product does. Add the core action and the primary user type."

It takes about 8 seconds. It's free.

I've had founders tell me the grade was wrong. Sometimes they're right. Conversion is complex and context-dependent and no rule-based tool captures all of it.

But I've also had founders come back and say: I ran the audit, made 3 changes, and my conversion rate went from 0.4% to 2.1%.

That's the ceiling a landing page audit can hit. Most pages are leaving that kind of gain on the table.

If you want to run your page: pagescorer.surge.sh

No signup. Takes 8 seconds. The grade will either confirm what you already know or show you something you've been too close to see.

## Post 2, Cold Email ROI Calculator

I was running cold email for 4 months before I calculated what it was actually costing me per closed deal.

The number was embarrassing.

$680 per closed deal. At a $500 average deal size.

I was losing money on the channel and calling it "marketing."

The mistake wasn't the copy or the targeting. The mistake was that I was measuring the wrong things.

I tracked open rates. I tracked reply rates. I tracked positive reply rates. I knew my numbers on all of those.

What I didn't track: how many hours I was spending per week on research, personalization, writing, and follow-up. And how that time cost mapped against revenue closed.

When I finally did the math, it looked like this:

150 emails per week at 4% reply rate: 6 replies.
30% of replies were positive: 1.8 positive replies.
20% of positive replies closed: 0.36 deals per week.
Average deal: $500. Revenue: $180/week.
Time spent: 10 hours per week.
Effective hourly rate: $18/hr. Before expenses.

That's a bad channel for a $500 product. It's a potentially good channel for a $2,500 product with the same performance. The math changes completely.

I built a calculator that does this calculation properly.

7 inputs: emails per week, open rate, reply rate, positive reply rate, close rate, average deal value, hours per week spent.

Outputs: cost per closed deal, effective hourly rate, breakeven deal volume, and a 90-day revenue projection at current performance.

The most useful output is breakeven volume. It tells you exactly how many emails per week you'd need to send, at your current conversion rates, to make the channel profitable at your current deal size.

For most solopreneurs with sub-$500 deals: the math doesn't work below 400-500 emails per week.

For people with $2k+ deals: it often works at much lower volume, which is why high-ACV B2B outbound makes sense and low-ACV consumer outbound usually doesn't.

The calculator is at cold-email-roi-calculator.surge.sh. Free, no signup, takes about 2 minutes.

I wish I'd run this math in month 1 instead of month 4.

The channel isn't broken. The deal size was.
