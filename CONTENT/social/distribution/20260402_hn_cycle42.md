## HN POST 1 — Show HN: TruthScope [LAUNCH DAY PRIORITY]

**Title:**

```
Show HN: TruthScope – stress signal analyzer using phone camera PPG and facial detection
```

**Body:**
```
I built TruthScope, a web app that analyzes physiological stress signals using your phone's camera.

What it does:

1. PPG (Photoplethysmography) from rear camera: detects heart rate and HRV by measuring subtle red-channel color changes in your fingertip. Accurate to ±4 BPM under controlled conditions.

2. Facial micro-expression detection: runs face-api.js (MIT license, entirely client-side) on your front camera at ~15fps. Detects 68 facial landmarks and flags emotional suppression patterns.

3. Multi-signal fusion: when PPG + HRV + facial signals align, confidence is weighted up. When they disagree, confidence drops. You see the raw score, not a binary "lie/truth" output.

What I want to be upfront about (competitors won't say this):

- This is a stress detector, not a lie detector. Deception causes stress. So do nerves, anxiety, medical conditions, and caffeine.
- Accuracy under controlled conditions is meaningful. Accuracy in a real-world conversation is lower.
- No readings are stored. Everything runs in the browser. Nothing sent to a server.

Technically interesting parts if you want to dig in:
- AVCaptureSession for camera access with locked exposure
- Bandpass filter (0.75–3Hz) to isolate heart rate frequency from PPG signal
- face-api.js configured with SSD Mobilenet v1 for detection and landmark model for expression inference

Link: https://truthscope.surge.sh

The app has a 14-screen onboarding, Party Mode (group use case), and a paid tier for unlimited sessions via Stripe. But the core detection is free.

Happy to go deep on any of the signal processing choices.
```

## HN POST 2 — Ask HN: What phone camera biometrics actually work at 30fps?

**Title:**
```
Ask HN: What phone camera biometrics are actually reliable at 30fps resolution?
```

**Body:**
```
I've been building a biometric signal analyzer using phone cameras and ran into the hardware limits. Curious what others have found.

What I got working:
- PPG from fingertip (rear camera): extractable heart rate and HRV signal. Degrades with motion and lighting changes.
- Facial micro-expression detection (front camera, face-api.js): 68-landmark detection works at 15fps in browser without killing performance.

What I cut as unreliable at phone camera specs:
- Voice formant analysis (mic input too variable without controlled conditions)
- Pupil dilation (front camera resolution insufficient)
- Respiratory rate from chest movement (works in clinical setups, not arm's-length phone video)

Are there other biometrics that are more viable than I gave them credit for? Or ones that seemed promising but didn't work out?

Background: building truthscope.surge.sh — disclosing in case anyone's curious about the use case.
```

```
I launched TruthScope today — a web app that analyzes physiological stress signals using your phone's camera.

Every competitor claims 90%+ accuracy. I know from my testing that accuracy under real-world conditions is lower. I shipped with every limitation labeled.

The reason: the utility isn't "perfect lie detection." The utility is helping people understand their own physiological responses — for interview prep, negotiation coaching, self-awareness training. For that use case, imperfect accuracy + honest labeling is more useful than a black-box number.

The monetization:
- Free tier: core detection, session limits
- Paid: Stripe checkout, unlimited sessions, monthly + annual plans
- No RevenueCat yet (adding at $1K MRR)

Revenue so far: $0. Day one.

What I did differently from my other 4 apps:
- Documented every limitation upfront (not buried in fine print)
- Built a Party Mode for group use (viral mechanic)
- Published technical blog post with signal processing details

The biggest remaining blocker: App Store submission. The web version is live but the native iOS app needs Apple review.

Link: truthscope.surge.sh
```
