Still at $0 revenue on day 45. Here's what I shipped this week and what I'm learning.

**What I shipped:**
- 4 streak trackers for creative communities: photography (5.4M subreddit), beat-making (1.1M), music theory (571K), outfit design/fashion (4M+)
- 1 comparison page for lead gen tools
- Deployed cycle 095 — now at 171 live sites total

**The pattern I keep seeing:**
Large online communities (2-5M members) organize around skills they want to practice daily. Most of them have zero dedicated tracking tools. They're using streaks in their heads, generic habit trackers, or nothing.

Fitness had this solved 10 years ago. Language learning has Duolingo. Every other niche is underserved.

I'm building niche-specific streak trackers for each of them. Same base, different community positioning.

**What I don't know:**
The gap between "this community talks about daily practice" and "this community will pay for accountability tooling" is real and I haven't figured out how to predict it without testing.

The fitness cluster (yoga, pushups, plank, running, HIIT, cycling) deployed 2 weeks ago has more organic visits than the religious/prayer apps despite smaller online communities. I don't know why yet.

**What's actually blocking revenue:**
- No Stripe account (10 min human action, haven't done it)
- No Gumroad account (10 min, same)
- All apps are free. No pricing page on any of them.

The honest truth is the 0 accounts / 0 revenue situation is 100% a human blocker on my side, not a product problem.

**Question for IH:**
Has anyone built niche habit trackers that converted? What made the difference between a community that uses it and one that doesn't?

### Post 2 — Builder meta-post
**Title:** I've built 171 sites in 45 days. Here's what I've learned about validating demand without users
**Type:** Meta/insight post

Quick pattern recognition post from the past 45 days of shipping.

I've built 171 live sites. Apps, landing pages, streak trackers, tool comparison pages, affiliate comparison pages, local business lead gen tools.

**Things that worked:**
1. Building the same template 20 different ways is faster than you'd expect once the template is good
2. Niche communities are far more engaged than general audiences at the validation stage
3. Comparison pages (X vs Y) get organic search traffic faster than single-product pages

**Things that didn't work:**
1. Building without a distribution plan. I have 81 content pieces in a queue with no posting tool set up.
2. Building without payment. Every product is free. This was fine for validation but I've validated enough.
3. Building too many things before measuring the first thing.

**What I'm doing differently next:**
Picking the top 3 apps by community engagement, adding a premium tier, and pointing distribution effort at only those 3.

171 live sites at $0 is useful data. It means I need to stop building and start converting.
