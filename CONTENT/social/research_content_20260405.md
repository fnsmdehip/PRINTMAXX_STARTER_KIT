Just scanned 28 ecom products with 25-54% margins.

Beauty, health, kitchen categories are 50%+ margins consistently.

But here's the catch nobody mentions: most fail at *customer LTV* not *unit margin*.

One-time flips are noisy. Build collections.

## Thread: The Research Velocity Problem (this thread)

I process ~18K alpha entries across Twitter, Reddit, HN, ProductHunt daily. Here's what I'm learning:

1/ Most "research" is volume theater. Watching 100 HN posts to find ONE that has a business model.

2/ Signal is in PATTERN not NOVELTY.
- Recurring freelance > one-off gigs
- Arbitrage at 50% margins > below 35%
- Tools with 40+ HN points > 7-20 point "neat ideas"

3/ Twitter's become noisy (we're seeing duplicates now). Reddit is still firing (4 new entries today) with patterns nobody's watching:

- r/AppBusiness: builders sharing real revenue (not inflated)
- r/passive_income: the REAL financial questions (salary vs fulfillment trade-offs)
- r/slavelabour: highest-frequency job postings (fastest market rotation)

4/ The insight: Scale your RESEARCH before you scale your execution.

Most people: do 10 jobs, learn from 10 jobs.
Better: learn from 10,000 jobs via research, then pick the top 10.

That's the difference between $0-1K/mo and $5K-50K/mo ventures.

5/ My research pipeline (18K entries, 27 new HN, 4 new Reddit, 0 Twitter actionable):
- Alpha staging + scoring + routing
- Decision engine filters for high-ROI opportunities
- Master ops tracks what's ready vs blocked
- Capital Genesis ranks everything by expected revenue

Without this, you're guessing.

6/ If you're running ventures (apps, content, ecommerce, outbound), build a research layer FIRST.

Not because data is fun to look at. Because it's 10-100x faster than trial-and-error.

The teams making $10K+ MRR all do this. The teams at $0 don't.
