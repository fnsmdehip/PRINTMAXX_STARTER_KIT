## 1. STANDALONE POSTS (14 posts - 2 per day, 7 days)

### Day 1 (Monday)

**Post 1:**
your phone screen at 11pm is stealing 45 minutes of deep sleep. the blue light suppresses melatonin for 90 minutes after you stop scrolling. put it in another room. let the quiet do its work.

**Post 2:**
there's a version of tonight where you fall asleep in eight minutes. cold room. dark room. no screen. just your breathing and the weight of the blanket.

---

### Day 2 (Tuesday)

**Post 3:**
the 4-7-8 breathing pattern. inhale 4 seconds. hold 7. exhale 8. three cycles. you'll feel it by the second one. your nervous system doesn't know how to stay anxious when you breathe like this.

**Post 4:**
no one talks about how good it feels to wake up before your alarm. not because you slept less. because your body finished on its own. that's what real rest feels like.

---

### Day 3 (Wednesday)

**Post 5:**
the rain doesn't care what time it is. it just falls. that's the energy for tonight. stop managing tomorrow. let it be 2am for a while.

**Post 6:**
your bedroom has somewhere between 8 and 14 tiny lights you've never noticed. charger LEDs. router blinks. standby glow. every one of them is telling your brain it's still daytime. cover them. see what total darkness actually feels like.

---

### Day 4 (Thursday)

**Post 7:**
the best sleep aid costs nothing. same bedtime. same wake time. seven days a week. your body will start winding down on its own after two weeks. it just needs a pattern it can trust.

**Post 8:**
some nights the only thing keeping you awake is the effort of trying to fall asleep. get up. sit somewhere dim. read something slow. go back when your eyelids get heavy. don't fight it. let it come to you.

---

### Day 5 (Friday)

**Post 9:**
65 degrees. that's the temperature your body wants at night. it needs to drop two or three degrees to fall asleep. most bedrooms are set to 72. you're making your body work against itself every night.

**Post 10:**
wind down. the last hour before sleep shouldn't look like the rest of your day. dim the lights. close the laptop. let the house get quiet. your brain needs a signal that the day is finished.

---

### Day 6 (Saturday)

**Post 11:**
sleep is not a reward for finishing your to-do list. it's the thing that makes tomorrow's list possible. go to bed. the work will still be there.

**Post 12:**
the sound of rain on a window at 1am. a warm blanket in a cold room. nowhere to be until morning. some moments don't need improving. just staying in.

---

### Day 7 (Sunday)

**Post 13:**
the military sleep method. relax your face. drop your shoulders. let your hands go limp. clear your mind for 10 seconds. if a thought comes, let it pass like a cloud. two minutes. most people are out by 120 seconds.

**Post 14:**
you don't need to earn rest. you don't need to deserve quiet. tonight is not a negotiation. close your eyes. let the day end.

## 6. INSTAGRAM AESTHETIC GUIDE

| Background alt | Charcoal warm | #151018 | Stories backgrounds, alt grid squares |

### Photo/Visual Style

**Source:** AI-generated + stock from Pexels/Unsplash (commercial license, free)

**Subject matter:**
- Cozy bedrooms at night (unmade linen sheets, dim lamp, no people)
- Night sky (moon, stars, no harsh light)
- Rain on windows (close-up, bokeh city lights behind glass)
- Candles in dark rooms (single flame, minimal setup)
- Ocean at night (dark water, silver moonlight line)
- Empty roads or paths at night (streetlamp glow, fog)
- Steam rising from a mug (dark background, warm tone)
- Snow falling at night (streetlight, silence)
- Misty forests at dawn/dusk (blue and purple tones)
- Minimal bedroom interiors (Scandinavian, Japanese, low light)

**What to avoid:**
- Bright daylight photos
- People's faces
- Busy compositions with many elements
- Neon or saturated colors
- Stock photo energy (posed, overlit, commercial)
- Text-heavy graphics
- Anything that feels like marketing

### Typography

- **Primary font:** A clean sans-serif (Inter, Karla, or DM Sans)
- **Weight:** Light or regular. Never bold for body text.
- **Case:** All lowercase. Always.
- **Size:** Small relative to image. Text should feel secondary to the visual.
- **Color:** #d4d8e8 (moon white) on dark backgrounds. Never pure white.
- **Overlay text placement:** Bottom third of image, left-aligned, with padding.

### Grid Layout Strategy

**Pattern:** Dark / Visual / Dark / Visual -- alternating posts

- **Dark posts:** Solid dark background (#0b1120 or #1a1025) with small centered text (the standalone posts from Section 1). Minimal, typographic.
- **Visual posts:** Atmospheric photos (bedroom, rain, ocean, candles). Caption carries the message, image carries the mood.
- **Occasional:** A soft gradient post (navy to purple, horizontal) for variety.

**Grid feel when viewed as a whole:** A dark, cohesive mood board. Someone should scroll your profile and immediately feel calmer. No visual noise. No clashing tones. Everything whispers.

### Stories Style

- Background: #0b1120 or #151018
- Text: Lowercase, small, moon white (#d4d8e8)
- Music: From our own Spotify playlists
- Polls/questions: Minimal ("are you in bed yet" / "what's keeping you up")
- No stickers, GIFs, or bright overlays
- Occasional: 15-second ambient video clip with no text

### Reels Style

- Same visual language as TikTok scripts (Section 2)
- Always dark, always slow
- Text overlays in the same minimal font
- No trending audio unless it fits the mood (ambient/lo-fi only)
- Cover image for grid: should match the dark aesthetic (custom cover, not auto-selected frame)
