## THREADS

### Thread 1: "what i learned tracking my ibadah for 30 days"

1/ i tracked every prayer, every quran page, every dua for 30 days last ramadan. here's what the data showed me about my own worship patterns.

2/ the first finding was brutal: i was missing asr more than any other prayer. not intentionally. i just got busy at work and "forgot." tracking made the pattern impossible to ignore.

3/ second finding: my quran reading peaked in week 1 (motivation high) and crashed in week 3 (routine fatigue). the fix was connecting it to salah, not willpower. 4 pages per prayer = automatic.

4/ third finding: my best duas came at tahajjud and iftar. not because those times are more "blessed" (they are) but because those were the moments i was most vulnerable and honest.

5/ fourth finding: the days i journaled after fajr, i maintained all 5 prayers 95% of the time. the days i didn't journal, it dropped to 60%. self-awareness is half the battle.

6/ final finding: consistency > intensity. the person who prays 5 basic prayers every day for 30 days gets more from ramadan than the person who does 3 hours of worship day 1 and burns out by day 10.

7/ i built a free app to track all of this. no account. works offline. your data stays on your device. ramadan-tracker.surge.sh. the streak counter alone changed my consistency.
