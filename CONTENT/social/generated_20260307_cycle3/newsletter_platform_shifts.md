## 1. tiktok: completion rate is king (again, but harder)

tiktok raised the completion rate threshold. your video needs to be watched to the end by a higher percentage of viewers to get pushed.

what this means in practice:
- shorter videos win (under 60 seconds)
- tutorials are outperforming entertainment content
- text overlays boost discoverability (tiktok is becoming a search engine)
- first hour after posting = your entire distribution window

the counterintuitive move: post LESS but make each video completable. one 45-second tutorial that 80% of viewers finish beats three 3-minute vlogs that get 25% completion.

also new: tiktok is testing follower-first distribution. your followers see your content before the algorithm decides whether to push it wider. this means: building a real audience matters more than going viral.

---

## 2. youtube shorts: search seo now matters

this is the big one nobody is talking about.

youtube shorts are now discoverable via search. not just the shorts feed. actual youtube search results.

what to do:
- add keyword-rich titles to your shorts (not just catchy hooks)
- 50-60 seconds is the sweet spot
- trending audio in the first 5 seconds boosts distribution
- optimal posting: 18-22 shorts per month

the play: take your existing blog content, turn the key points into 50-second shorts with searchable titles, and you've got a second discovery channel that didn't exist 3 months ago.

---

## 3. instagram: DM shares are the new likes

instagram's algorithm now weights DM shares as a priority metric. not likes. not comments. shares via DM.

why this matters: the content that gets shared privately is different from the content that gets liked publicly. it's the "you need to see this" content. the utility content. the "save this for later" content.

other intel:
- first 2 seconds of a reel are make-or-break
- instagram has an "originality score" now. cross-posting from tiktok with watermarks = penalty
- optimal posting: 3-5 reels per week
- watch time is still the #1 metric

the move: create content people want to SEND to a friend. that means: specific, useful, slightly controversial, or personally relevant. generic motivation content dies in the DM metric era.
