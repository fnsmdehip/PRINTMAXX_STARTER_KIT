## REDDIT POST -- r/selfhosted or r/automation

**Title:** Built a comparison page for n8n vs Zapier vs Make. Here's what I actually think after using all three.

**Body:**

Just published a breakdown of all three automation platforms. The short version:

**Zapier:** Best integration library (7,000+). Worst pricing at scale. Fine for non-technical users who need 5-10 automations.

**Make (Integromat):** Better pricing than Zapier. More visual workflow builder. Steeper learning curve. Good middle ground.

**n8n:** Open-source. Self-hosted option. 400+ integrations. Free tier is genuinely useful. Best for developers who want control.

**My honest take:** If you can self-host, n8n wins. If you can't, Make beats Zapier on price. Zapier only wins on pure integration count and ease of use for non-technical users.

For what it's worth, I run 289 daily automations and none of them use Zapier, Make, or n8n. They're all Python + cron. But that's a different conversation.
