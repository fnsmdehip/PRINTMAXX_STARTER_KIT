1/ Hallow raised $157M in VC funding. 10 million downloads. $33.4M revenue. celebrity narrators like Mark Wahlberg.

I built PrayerLock in one session with Claude Code. it's 55KB. it tracks prayer streaks.

here's what I learned comparing them:

2/ Hallow does things PrayerLock never will:

- 5000+ guided meditation sessions

- audio rosary with professional narration

- celebrity partnerships

- prayer groups and community

that's what $157M buys you. and it's genuinely good.

3/ but Hallow also has things users complain about:

- $9.99/mo or $69.99/yr paywall

- 200MB+ app size

- requires account creation

- Catholic-focused only (no Orthodox, no Lutheran, no non-Christian options)

4/ PrayerLock does one thing: keeps your prayer streak alive.

no audio. no celebrities. no community. just you and your daily commitment.

55KB. works offline. no account. data stays on your device. 13+ denominations.

5/ the lesson isn't "free beats funded."

the lesson is: the market isn't one app. it's layers.

Hallow owns the premium layer. nobody owns the free accountability layer. that's the gap.

6/ same pattern with Opal ($99/yr) vs FocusLock ($0).

Opal's Deep Focus mode is nuclear -- you literally can't bypass it. worth $99 for some people.

but most people just need basic app blocking during work sessions. FocusLock does that for free.

7/ if you're building in a space with funded competitors, don't compete on their terms.

find the layer they can't profitably serve. build there.

$0 indie app can coexist with $157M-funded app. different customers. different jobs-to-be-done.
