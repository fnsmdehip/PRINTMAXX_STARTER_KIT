THREAD: the actual science behind desk breaks (and why most productivity advice ignores it)

1/ the 20-20-20 rule is real, but nobody explains WHY it works

every 20 min, look 20 feet away for 20 sec. that specific distance forces your ciliary muscles to relax. it's the closest thing to resetting your eyes mid-session.

2/ posture isn't about "sit up straight"

it's about resetting the s-curve in your spine. after 30+ min of slouching, your ligaments stretch and don't spring back. a 30-sec posture reset every 30 min prevents this from compounding all day.

3/ dehydration hits your cognition before you feel thirsty

mild dehydration (1-2% body weight in water loss) cuts concentration by 10-15%. by the time you notice you're thirsty, you're already impaired. hourly water reminders aren't wellness theater, they're cognitive maintenance.

4/ the 90-min movement break is the one most people skip

human ultradian rhythms run in ~90 min cycles. your body literally wants a state change every 90 min. 2 min of movement resets cortisol and extends the next focus cycle.

5/ the problem isn't knowing this. it's remembering it under deep work.
