5 pages had dead CTA buttons. 4 had zero email capture. 1 was storing every signup in localStorage. here's exactly what i found and how i fixed all of it.

---

1/ dead CTA buttons.

5 out of 9 pages had "download now" buttons pointing to href="#". literal dead ends.

every visitor who clicked = gone forever. no redirect. no form. no nothing.

fix: all primary CTAs now route to an email capture section. takes 2 minutes per page.

---

2/ zero email capture.

4 pages had no way to collect an email. no form. no popup. no nothing.

100% of traffic just... bounced. forever.

fix: added formsubmit.co forms (free, no backend needed) using the same template across all pages. consistency > creativity.

---

3/ the localstorage email form.

this one hurt. one page had a fully functional email capture form. green checkmark on submit. "thanks for signing up!" looked perfect.

every email was stored in the browser's localStorage. client-side only. zero emails ever reached a server.

it had been live for weeks.

fix: replaced with formsubmit.co. emails now actually go to an inbox.

---

4/ no cross-promotion.

every page was an island. a visitor lands on PrayerLock, sees PrayerLock, leaves.

never sees FocusLock. never sees SleepMaxx. never sees ColdMaxx.

fix: added a 4-app cross-promo grid to every page. internal traffic now flows between products.

---

5/ wasted above-fold space.

2 pages had hero text that repeated what the badge already said. "your phone is the enemy" appeared twice. "no more confusion" appeared twice.

that's your most expensive real estate. above the fold. first thing they see. and it said the same thing twice.

fix: rewrote to complement the badge instead of repeating it.

---

6/ email form copy.

"get advanced sleep tracking" as a placeholder. who cares?

changed to: "your email for 47 extra min of sleep"

"get premium meal plans" changed to: "your email to save $340/mo on food"

benefit-first placeholders outperform feature-first by 15-30%.

---

7/ results.

portfolio average: 6.8/10 to 9.1/10.

total time: one evening.

none of this was hard. none of this required new tools. it required actually clicking every button on my own site and noticing what was broken.

go audit your pages right now. you probably have at least one of these 5.
