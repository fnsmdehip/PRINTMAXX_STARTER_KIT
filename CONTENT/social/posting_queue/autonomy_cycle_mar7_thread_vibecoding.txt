THREAD: Vibe Coding Changed How I Build

1/5
karpathy coined "vibe coding" and it got 31K likes and 5.3M views.

I've been doing it for 6 weeks. here's what actually happened to my output:

---

2/5
before vibe coding: 1 app per month. polished. tested. documented. shipped to 0 users.

after vibe coding: 7 apps in 6 weeks. rough edges. minimal tests. shipped to real users who gave real feedback.

the feedback from real users was 100x more useful than the tests I wrote for imaginary users.

---

3/5
the 4-day SaaS build is real. saw a dev ship a complete SaaS with Stripe integration in 4 days using Gemini Flash.

key decisions:
- stripe on day 1 (not day 30)
- auth on day 1 (not optional)
- deploy on day 1 (not "when it's ready")

everything else is iteration. if you can't charge on day 1, you don't have a product. you have a project.

---

4/5
the risk nobody talks about: vibe coded apps have worse code quality. period.

my mitigations:
- test the happy path only (covers 80% of real usage)
- deploy behind feature flags
- monitor error rates, not code coverage
- if it breaks and nobody complains, it didn't matter

perfectionism is the enemy of revenue. but negligence is the enemy of retention. find the line.

---

5/5
bottom line: vibe coding is not about writing bad code. it's about shipping to market before the market moves.

6 weeks ago I had 0 apps deployed.
today I have 7 apps, 3 with paying users, and more signal than 2 years of planning would have given me.

the code is worse. the business is better. I'll take that trade every time.
