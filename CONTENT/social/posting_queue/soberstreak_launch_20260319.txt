THREAD, The Quittr privacy scandal + SoberStreak angle
---
[1/6] Quittr got caught selling user data. A sobriety app. Selling the list of people trying to get sober.

The response? "We updated our privacy policy."

Here's why that's a bigger deal than it sounds, and what I built instead.

[2/6] Think about what data a sobriety app collects:
- What you're addicted to
- How long you've been struggling
- Your relapse dates
- Your journal entries about rock-bottom moments

That's not app data. That's ammunition.

[3/6] The industry standard is "we need your email to back up your data."

But your streak is 3 numbers. Your journal is text. localStorage handles both.

The "we need your data" argument is a monetization wrapper dressed as a feature.

[4/6] SoberStreak stores everything on your device.

No account. No servers. No email. No analytics pings.
Open the site. Start your streak. Close the tab. Come back tomorrow. Your streak is still there.

Works offline too.

[5/6] The tech stack is embarrassingly simple:
- One HTML file
- Tailwind CDN
- localStorage
- Service worker (for offline)

Zero server costs. Zero data liability. Zero reasons to ever sell user data because there's none to sell.

[6/6] Free: soberstreak.surge.sh

If you're building habit apps, the bar for trust is higher than most niches. Build accordingly.
