SUBJECT: the $0.32/user habit app and the 0.8 star trick

---

one timing change. 3.2 stars to 4.0.

a dev on Reddit shared something that sounds too simple to matter: they moved their app review prompt from early in the user path to right after the user gets value.

that's it. 0.8 stars. no code rewrite. no new features.

most apps beg for reviews before the user knows what the app does. you're asking a stranger for a favor. of course they give you 3 stars.

but ask right after they complete a streak, hit a goal, or finish a session? they're already feeling good. the review writes itself.

---

meanwhile in the habit app space:

HabitSwipe hit 2500 users and $799 in revenue in 2 months. that's $0.32 per user. not life-changing but it's real money from a solo dev building a simple product.

another dev built "Ban It" -- an app for tracking bad habits. their first serious paying user was using the app in a way they never intended. that's the product-market fit discovery moment every founder is chasing.

the pattern across both: ship something simple, watch how people actually use it, then double down on the behavior you didn't expect.

---

what's dying in outbound:

someone posted about sending 200 LinkedIn messages for their B2B bookkeeping practice. zero conversions. the 39 comments were unanimous: LinkedIn cold outreach is cooked for most B2B services.

what's working instead: cold email (still), warm referrals, niche community presence, and content that demonstrates expertise before the ask.

if you're still optimizing LinkedIn outreach templates, you're optimizing a dead channel.

---

one more: the first marketing hire at a $680M startup shared that they spent $60K/month on PR agencies. most of it was waste. what actually worked: specific founder story angles, relationships with niche journalists, and timing around industry events. not spray-and-pray press releases.

the insight: whether you're spending $60K/mo or $0/mo on press, the same principles apply. specific story + right journalist + right timing beats volume every time.

---

3 things to do this week:

1. audit your review prompt timing in every app you have live. move it to trigger after value delivery. free 0.8 star improvement.

2. if you're doing LinkedIn outreach, test cold email as a replacement channel. same messaging, different delivery.

3. if you sell a digital product, test $17 price point. it's in the impulse zone and one dev got 93 comments of real feedback at that price.
