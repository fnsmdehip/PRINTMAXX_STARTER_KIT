### THREAD - The Boring App Factory Model (from ALPHA18297 deep dive)
STATUS: APPROVED
SCHEDULED: next available slot
SOURCE: r/AppBusiness ALPHA18297

TWEET 1/6:
I found someone making $4,200/mo from 65 "boring" apps.

none went viral. most make $10-120/mo each.

here's the exact playbook (and why it validates the approach I've been building):

TWEET 2/6:
step 1: the "boring keyword" hunt

look for highly specific long-tail keywords with:
- decent search volume
- terrible existing apps

think "PDF compressor for [niche]" or "unit converter for [specific industry]"

the uglier the competition, the better your odds.

TWEET 3/6:
step 2: the 48-hour MVP

if it takes more than a few days, drop it.

the app does ONE thing slightly better than the outdated garbage currently ranking.

no feature creep. no "V2 roadmap." ship it.

TWEET 4/6:
step 3: pure ASO

spend 80% of time on:
- title optimization
- subtitle with keywords
- screenshot design

that's the entire marketing strategy. publish, tweak keywords once a month, forget about it.

no paid ads. no TikTok. no content marketing.

TWEET 5/6:
the math:

65 apps × $65/mo avg = $4,200/mo

even if half die, 32 apps × $65 = $2,080/mo passive income

even if numbers are inflated by 50%, the MODEL works:
- near-zero marginal cost per app
- compound portfolio over time
- ASO = free distribution

TWEET 6/6:
the takeaway most people miss:

this is a hedge fund approach to apps. diversification > concentration.

you don't need one $4K/mo app. you need forty $100/mo apps.

same math. way less risk. way less drama.

I'm building this exact model. 22 apps live. targeting 50 by Q3.

stop chasing unicorns. build donkeys.
