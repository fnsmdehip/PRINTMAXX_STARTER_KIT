# 4 Conversion Fixes From This Week (With Real Data)

I scan hundreds of solopreneur posts every week looking for alpha -- findings with specific numbers, not motivation.

This week had 4 posts with actual conversion data. Here's what they found.

## 1. Free Trial = 5x Conversion

One dev had a hard paywall on their app. 2% conversion. Added a 7-day free trial. 11%.

Same app. Same features. Same price. Just let users experience the value before asking for money.

If your app has a hard paywall, this is the single highest-ROI change you can make today.

## 2. Personalized Email = 7.2x CTR

Solo founder with 322 users. Generic emails got 2.5% CTR. Personalized emails with 39 data attributes synced from Supabase got 18% CTR.

Setup: Brevo free tier + Supabase sync every 4 hours. 8 automations. Total cost: $0.

The takeaway isn't "personalize your emails." Everyone knows that. The takeaway is the specific stack that makes it possible at $0 cost with no marketing team.

## 3. Channel ROI, Not Channel Traffic

Founder tracked every marketing channel for a $39 digital product.

- Email newsletter: 28% buyer conversion
- Twitter organic: 8%
- Reddit: 6%
- Paid ads: negative ROI

Most channels send traffic. Very few send buyers. If you're not tracking conversion by channel, you're optimizing for vanity metrics.

## 4. Reposition, Don't Rebuild

400 signups in 30 days. $0 revenue. Dev tools for developers.

The fix: stopped targeting developers-as-hobbyists and started targeting developers-at-work. Same product, different positioning. Revenue started flowing.

Before you rebuild your product, ask: "Am I talking to people who actually pay for things?"

## The Pattern

None of these founders rebuilt their product. None raised money. None hired a marketing team. They each changed one thing about how people experience or discover what they already built.

Conversion is a positioning problem, not a product problem.

## Tool Alpha: 2 Finds Worth Looking At

**Context-Compactor** (github.com/HalfEmptyDrum/Context-Compactor) -- open source tool that summarizes agent context instead of truncating it. If you run AI coding agents, this solves the context window problem.

**RunAutoCo** (runautoco.com) -- autonomous AI company that ran 22 business cycles for $36 total. Interesting architecture to study if you're building agent infrastructure.
