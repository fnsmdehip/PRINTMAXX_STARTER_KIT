TITLE: Cold email reply rates by subject line type - data from 11,000 sends

BODY:

Ran cold email campaigns across 3 different niches over 6 months. Total sends: 11,247. Here's what the data showed on subject lines specifically.

Worst performers (under 8% open rate):
- "Quick question" and its variants
- First name only subject lines
- Anything with "following up" in the first send
- Subject lines over 8 words
- Questions that require domain knowledge to parse

Best performers (over 22% open rate):
- Specific number + specific claim ("3 things I noticed about [Company]'s homepage")
- Implied social proof without stating it ("Why [Similar Company] switched their outbound approach")
- Curiosity gaps tied to their actual situation ("Found something odd on your careers page")
- Pattern interrupts that are true and verifiable ("You rank #4 for [keyword], not #1")

The pattern across all high-performers: they require the recipient to already know the answer or assume you do. Vague subject lines get deleted. Specific ones get opened because people want to know if you actually did your research.

Reply rate is a different metric and depends much more on the body and CTA than the subject line. But if they're not opening it, nothing else matters.

Happy to share more breakdowns if this is useful.

the 6 phases:

1. spec (plain english, no jargon)
2. architecture (file map before any code)
3. component build (one at a time)
4. edge case audit (explicit failure hunt)
5. payment wiring (Stripe as a separate pass)
6. deployment (platform-specific prompt)

doing 1-6 in order cuts build time by about 60%.
