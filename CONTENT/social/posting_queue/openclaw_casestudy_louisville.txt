TITLE: I scraped Louisville KY for local businesses with no website. Found 35. Here's the breakdown.

---

I've been building a local business prospecting system called OpenClaw for the past few weeks. Ran a new cycle this morning targeting mid-size southern cities. Louisville was the biggest find.

Here's what the data showed:

LOUISVILLE KY - THIS MORNING'S CYCLE
- Niches scanned: junk removal, tree service, window cleaning, pressure washing
- Total businesses found: 35
- Businesses with zero web presence: 35 (100%)
- BBB grade: F (most had never claimed their listing)
- Average job ticket I observed from their social posts: $300-500
- My price per site: $500 flat

$17,500 pipeline from one city in one morning.

HOW THE SCRAPER WORKS

The system runs a 4-tier search fallback:
1. DuckDuckGo search
2. DDG Lite (when main DDG throttles)
3. Bing
4. BBB Playwright scraper (the real goldmine)

BBB turned out to be far more useful than I expected. Every business has a structured data entry with: name, address, phone, category, rating (A-F), years in business, complaint count. When a business has an F rating, it almost always means they never engaged with their listing, and usually means no website either.

The scraper cross-references the BBB data against a web presence check. If there's no indexed site, they go into the hot leads queue.

This cycle across all 4 cities (Knoxville TN, Chattanooga TN, Birmingham AL, Louisville KY): 81 new leads, 44 D/F grade with zero web presence. Running total: 240 leads in pipeline.

THE PREVIEW SITE PLAY

Before I send a cold email, I build a preview site from a base template. Takes about 2 hours for a full batch. Deploy to surge.sh.

Cold email is 4 lines:

"Found [Business Name] on BBB. You don't have a website yet. I already built one for you to look at: [link]. $500, live in 48 hours. Want it?"

That's it. The preview does the selling. No portfolio needed, no proposal, no discovery call.

WHAT'S ACTUALLY INTERESTING HERE

These businesses aren't broke. A junk removal operator running 5 jobs a week at $400/job is making $100k/year in revenue with zero digital infrastructure. They just never prioritized it or didn't know where to start.

When you show up with the thing already built, you're removing every friction point. They don't have to imagine what it looks like. They don't have to trust a stranger's pitch. They just have to say yes or no to something they can already see.

The conversion rate on preview site cold emails is way higher than spec work requests or generic "do you need a website" outreach.
