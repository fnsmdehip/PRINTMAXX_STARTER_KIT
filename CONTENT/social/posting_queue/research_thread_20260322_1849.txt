THREAD: What 3 founders who actually hit revenue have in common (and what Twitter gurus leave out)

---

1/ A guy built an app for his girlfriend. Not for "the market." Not for a TAM slide. For one person.

She used it. Her friends used it. $500 MRR in 6 weeks. $0 in ads.

The lesson: your first user should be someone who will text you at midnight when it breaks.

---

2/ Someone hit $100K ARR and posted what it ACTUALLY feels like.

Spoiler: not private jets and Bali.

Infrastructure costs, churn eating your growth, support tickets multiplying, and still doing everything yourself.

Revenue is vanity. Margin is sanity.

---

3/ $1K MRR. Took 9 months.

Month 1-3: Built and launched to absolute silence.
Month 4-6: Rebuilt everything based on actual user feedback.  
Month 7-9: Finally cracked distribution.

That's $3.70/day for 270 days of grinding.

---

4/ The pattern across all three:

- Built for a real person, not a persona
- Ignored vanity metrics early on
- Distribution was harder than building
- Revenue took 3-9 months, not 3-9 days
- None of them bought a course to learn this

---

5/ What they all AVOIDED:

- "Just validate with a landing page" (they built the thing)
- "Raise a seed round" (they bootstrapped)
- "Scale fast" (they found 10 users who loved it first)
- Posting revenue screenshots before month 6

Build something small. Make it great. Let it compound.
