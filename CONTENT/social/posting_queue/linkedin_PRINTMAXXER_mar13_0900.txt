RevenueCat just published data from 100K+ apps and 1 billion transactions.

The finding that stood out: AI-powered apps convert 40% better than non-AI apps. But they also churn 30% faster.

Translation: AI is incredible for getting users in the door. It's less effective at keeping them.

The companies winning are combining both:
- AI-powered conversion funnels
- Traditional retention mechanics (onboarding, community, direct outreach)

Another data point: subscription apps are winner-takes-most. The top 25% grew 80% YoY. The rest are flat or declining.

The strategic implication: pick a sub-niche small enough to dominate. Then build retention, not just acquisition.
