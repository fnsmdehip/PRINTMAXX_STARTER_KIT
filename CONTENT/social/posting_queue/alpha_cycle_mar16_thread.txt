THREAD: why 99% of vibe-coded apps die in 48 hours (and the 3 things that actually matter now)

1/ AI made building apps a commodity. Claude Code, Cursor, Replit Agent. a 14 year old can ship a functional app before dinner.

so why are 99% of these apps dead within a week?

2/ because building was never the hard part. distribution was.

@StevenCravotta made $3M from apps. not because he coded better. because he understood: marketing + conversion is the moat.

3/ the playbook that works in 2026:

- build something in 3 hours
- spend the NEXT 3 WEEKS on distribution
- 1 working content format, templated, repeated 50x
- retention > acquisition (10K users once > 1K users monthly)

4/ short form content is pattern recognition, not creativity.

the accounts growing fastest right now run 3-4 formats on loop. they found what works and they just... keep doing it.

stop trying to be original. start trying to be consistent.

5/ templates beat talent every time.

one working video format can power dozens of variations. one proven email subject line can split-test into 20 winners.

find the template. fill in the blanks. distribute.

6/ the indie dev graveyard is full of beautiful apps nobody ever saw.

if you're building right now: stop at 80% done. spend the other 20% on getting it in front of people.

marketing is not a nice-to-have. it's the product.
