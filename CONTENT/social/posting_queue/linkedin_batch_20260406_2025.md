## POST 1: "I Audited 395 Websites With an AI Agent"

I deployed 395 websites over 62 days.

Then I built an AI agent to audit every single one for conversion gaps.

The finding that changed my approach:

378 out of 395 had zero email capture.

Not "bad" email capture. Literally nothing. No form. No field. No pop-up.

The most embarrassing category: 50+ habit-tracking apps where the entire product mechanic is "user returns daily." Every return visit is a capture opportunity. I had zero capture on any of them.

The agent also found:
- 6 cold email tools, each on its own URL, none linking to each other
- 12 affiliate pages earning zero commissions (placeholder IDs)
- A 17-article research blog with no subscribe option

The fix for most of this is adding one input field per page. 15 minutes each.

Estimated impact: 3,000-8,000 email subscribers within 90 days.

The lesson: Before you build more, audit what you have.

The gap between "lots of traffic" and "lots of revenue" is usually one form field and 15 minutes.

What conversion gaps have you found hiding in plain sight?

## POST 2: "The Conversion Rate Nobody Talks About"

A simple UX change that converts 3-5x better for free web tools:

Replace "Subscribe to our newsletter" with "Save your results."

Why it works:

The user just spent 3 minutes entering data into your calculator, grader, or audit tool. They have a result they care about.

"Save your results" is aligned with what they already want.

"Subscribe to our newsletter" is a non-sequitur. They came for a tool, not a mailing list.

The data:
- "Subscribe" = 1-3% opt-in
- "Free ebook" = 3-8% opt-in
- "Save your results" = 15-25% opt-in

Other high-intent captures:
- "Email yourself this report"
- "Get notified when your score changes"
- "Benchmark against others"

The principle: ask for email AFTER value delivery, not before.

The email isn't the lead magnet. The result they already generated is.

## POST 3: "Know How Your Customer Physically Interacts With Content"

An AI audit of my supplement review sites (targeting men 55-70) returned one line that changed my entire strategy:

"This demographic prints things."

They literally print web pages.

So a "get the comparison as a PDF" lead magnet isn't a nice-to-have. It's matching how this audience actually consumes content.

Most marketers optimize for the audience they are, not the audience they're targeting.

A 25-year-old SaaS marketer would never print a supplement comparison. Their 62-year-old father would.

The purchase cycle is different too. Supplement buyers don't impulse buy. They research, compare, wait, come back. If your only conversion point is an affiliate link, you get one shot. With an email list, you get 52 per year.

Understanding the physical habits of your customer is an unfair advantage that most digital marketers completely ignore.
