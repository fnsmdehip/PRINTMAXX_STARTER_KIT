# LinkedIn Post -- What Managing 33 AI Agents Taught Me About Management
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Niche:** AI leadership / management / tech thought leadership
**Best time:** 7am-9am EST Tuesday-Thursday
**Status:** READY TO POST

---

I run 33 AI agents autonomously. After 47 evaluation cycles and 630+ logged decisions, here's what surprised me:

The best performer was an agent I fired.

Two weeks ago, I killed my SEO optimization agent. It wasn't producing visible output. Looked like dead weight.

Then it came back. 62 file changes in a single cycle. Schema markup across 17 articles. 38 sitemaps refreshed. 6 canonical URL fixes. BreadcrumbList coverage from 93% to 96%.

I promoted it from KILLED to S-tier. The highest rank in my system.

What went wrong with my evaluation? I was measuring output volume when this agent was doing quality infrastructure work. The kind of work that doesn't show up in dashboards until it compounds.

Sound familiar? Because this is the exact same mistake managers make with human teams.

The quiet engineer fixing technical debt doesn't look productive next to the one shipping flashy features. Until the system breaks and you realize who was holding it together.

Three takeaways from performance-reviewing AI agents:

1. Measure on the right metric, not the most visible one
2. "Low output" and "low value" are not the same thing
3. The biggest bottleneck in my 33-agent system isn't compute or code. It's me not doing 80 minutes of manual work that would activate $1-8K/month in revenue

That last one is the most human lesson of all. The system works. The manager is the blocker.
