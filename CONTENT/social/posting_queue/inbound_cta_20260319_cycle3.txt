--- COMMUNITY POST (r/indiehackers, r/solopreneur) ---

Title: I validated 114 app niches in 6 months. here's the research process.

After deploying 47 apps, the research phase is the most important and least-discussed step.

Most indie devs find ideas by browsing ProductHunt. The problem: ProductHunt = everyone sees the same thing = competitive.

The process that found underserved niches:

1. Reddit complaint mining, search "is there an app" + niche. threads where nobody has a great answer are your opportunity.

2. App Store gap analysis, 1-star reviews reveal your product requirements: "requires account," "doesn't work offline," "too expensive for what it does."

3. Keyword check, Ubersuggest free tier, target 500-5K searches/mo, kd under 20. niche keywords have less competition than you think.

Example: "running streak tracker" = 1,900/mo, kd 8. "habit tracker" = 90,500/mo, kd 62.

Build for the niche. Win.
