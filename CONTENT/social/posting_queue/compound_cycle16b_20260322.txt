## REDDIT POST

### Reddit Post
Subreddit: r/indiehackers
Title: 249 content pieces across 5 platforms in 3 days - here's the automation architecture

Body:
I built a content distribution engine that generates platform-specific posts from research signals. Here's how it works and the actual numbers from the last 3 days.

**Output (3 days, zero manual writing):**
- Twitter: 114 posts (singles + threads)
- Reddit: 65 posts (matched to subreddit tone)
- Hacker News: 28 posts
- LinkedIn: 25 posts
- Indie Hackers: 17 posts

**The architecture:**

1. **Alpha scraper** runs on cron every 6 hours. Scrapes Twitter, Reddit, HN for trends, methods, and opportunities. Scores each finding on a 0-100 scale.

2. **Intelligence router** takes scored findings and routes them to the right content type. A market gap becomes a Twitter thread. A trending tool becomes a Reddit post. A revenue method becomes a LinkedIn breakdown.

3. **Content generator** uses templates matched to each platform's tone. Reddit gets longer, more educational posts. Twitter gets shorter, punchier hooks. LinkedIn gets professional framing.

4. **Posting queue** holds everything for human review before posting. Nothing goes out without a quick scan.

**What I learned:**

- Platform-specific tone matters more than volume. A Twitter-style post on Reddit gets ignored.
- The 80/20 is in the scraper quality, not the generation quality. Garbage in, garbage out.
- 249 pieces sounds like a lot but maybe 30 of them are actually worth posting. The filter is the real product.
- Engagement bait detection is harder than I expected. Had to build a 5-point scoring system to separate real alpha from recycled platitudes.

**Stack:** Python, Claude Code agents, cron scheduling. No paid tools except the Claude subscription.

Happy to share the scoring methodology if anyone's interested.
