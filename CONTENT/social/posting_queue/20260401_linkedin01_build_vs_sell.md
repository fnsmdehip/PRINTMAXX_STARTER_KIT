Day 57 of building a revenue system.

I decided to audit myself. Actually, I built an AI agent that audits me automatically every 4 hours. Here's what it found.

The good: 202 deployed websites. 14 digital products. 529 automation scripts. 33 AI agents running autonomously. 192,700 scored business leads. Everything built for under $200/month.

The bad: $0 revenue.

The audit found 5 specific revenue leaks:

1. 19 working Stripe payment links that appear on zero pages
2. 5 affiliate pages sending traffic to placeholder IDs
3. 192,700 leads scored and ranked -- zero contacted
4. 14 products sitting in a folder for 57 days
5. 11 tech comparison pages with $0 in commissions

The agent calculated the fix: 80 minutes of human work would unlock an estimated $1,100-7,800/month in revenue.

The bottleneck was never the code. It was me signing up for 3 websites.

This is what most technical founders get wrong. We optimize for building because building feels productive. But the marginal value of the 530th automation script is zero when the 19 payment links you already have aren't visible to anyone.

Three lessons:

1. Wire payments before features. If nobody can pay you, nothing else matters.

2. Your revenue tracker should be automated. I didn't notice these leaks for 57 days. The AI caught them in 4 hours.

3. Know your $/minute. Sending 3 cold emails = $100-1,000/min of revenue potential. Refactoring a scheduler = $0/min. Act accordingly.

Building the machine is the fun part. Turning it on is the work.
