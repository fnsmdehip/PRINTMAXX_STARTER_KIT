- COMPETITIVE_INTEL_MASTER: 262 rows, 182 dupes

### 2/6

the worst offender: CONTENT_FACTORY_QUEUE.

2,200 rows. 2,199 duplicates.

basically the whole file was one row copied 2,199 times.

i've been "generating content" for weeks from a file that was 99.95% duplication.

automation without data hygiene is just error at scale.

### 3/6

what the janitor actually does:

1. scans every CSV in the LEDGER/ directory
2. identifies exact-match duplicate rows
3. preserves the first occurrence, removes the rest
4. creates timestamped backups BEFORE touching anything
5. logs every removal with before/after counts

total time to build: 2 hours.
value per run: prevents every downstream automation from eating bad data.

### 4/6

the insight that made me build this:

my lead scoring was off. my content generator was repeating topics. my trend signals were stale.

turns out the problem wasn't the algorithms.

it was the data underneath them.

classic "garbage in, garbage out" except with 337 automation scripts processing the garbage at high speed.

### 5/6

lesson for anyone building autonomous systems:

you need three layers:

1. scrapers that COLLECT data
2. processors that ACT on data
3. janitors that CLEAN data

most people build 1 and 2 and skip 3.

then wonder why their automations drift.

### 6/6

the janitor runs every 24 hours now via cron.

it's the least exciting script in my system. nobody would tweet about it. there's no viral hook.

but it's the one that keeps the other 336 scripts honest.

boring infrastructure > flashy features.
