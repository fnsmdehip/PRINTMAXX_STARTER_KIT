## Tweet 1 (Hook)

I coined a term for what's killing most solopreneur automation projects.

automation dysmorphia.

here's what it looks like, why it happens, and how to fix it.

---

## Tweet 2

the symptoms:

- you have more scripts than customers
- your dashboard has 15 metrics and zero revenue
- you spend more time optimizing the system than using it
- your agents produce beautiful reports that nobody reads
- you automated everything except the part that makes money

## Tweet 4

how it happens:

building systems feels productive. the code compiles. the agents run. the reports populate. the dashboard lights up green.

selling feels uncomfortable. it requires human contact. rejection. iteration on messaging. talking to strangers.

so you keep building.

---

## Tweet 5

the cognitive trap:

"once this automation is done, revenue will flow."

it never flows. because the next automation always looks more important than sending the first email.

you're not building infrastructure. you're building a procrastination engine with really good architecture.

---

## Tweet 6

the cure (what I'm implementing now):

1. freeze all new script creation. 530 is enough.
2. identify the 3 actions closest to a dollar entering a bank account
3. do those actions manually. no automation. just action.
4. only THEN automate what worked.

for me those 3 actions are:
- send 6 cold emails (15 min)
- list 5 PDFs on Gumroad (45 min)
- sign up for 3 affiliate programs (30 min)

90 minutes. should have done it on day 1.

---

## Tweet 7

the brutal reframe:

my 530 scripts are not assets. they're liabilities until they produce revenue.

my 33 agents are not employees. they're overhead until their output generates income.

my 388 sites are not a portfolio. they're hosting costs until they convert visitors.

if it doesn't make money, it's a hobby with impressive infrastructure.

---

## Tweet 8

lesson: automate the last mile first.

don't automate lead scraping before you've manually closed one deal.
don't automate content creation before you've manually posted for a week.
don't automate product listing before you've manually sold one copy.

the system comes after the sale. not before.

building the opposite way is how you get automation dysmorphia.
