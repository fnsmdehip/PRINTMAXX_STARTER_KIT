THREAD 4 (simplicity wins):

1/
307 upvotes for a concept called "minimum viable day." the idea: stop tracking 10 habits. just answer one question. "was today good enough?" here's why this changes how we build streak apps.

2/
the failure mode of every habit tracker: day 1 you track 10 things. day 7 you track 6. day 14 you track 2. day 30 you deleted the app. complexity is the enemy of retention. the data proves it.

3/
"minimum viable day" flips the model. instead of 10 checkboxes (and the guilt of missing 3), you get one binary toggle. today was viable or it wasn't. zero guilt. zero decision fatigue. just show up.

4/
this directly applies to our streak apps. scripture streak, fitness streak, reading streak. every one of them has a "did you do the thing today" core mechanic. the simpler that mechanic, the longer the streak.

5/
the counterintuitive lesson: fewer features = higher retention = higher LTV. the app that does less keeps users longer. and users who stay longer are 3x more likely to convert to paid.

6/
practical implementation: strip the onboarding to one screen. strip the daily check-in to one action. strip the stats to one number (current streak). everything else is settings, not core UX.

7/
the best apps feel like they took 2 days to build. they probably did. the complexity is in the backend, the notifications, the re-engagement. not the UI. keep the surface dead simple.
