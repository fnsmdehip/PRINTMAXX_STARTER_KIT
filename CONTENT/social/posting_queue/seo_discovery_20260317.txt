THREAD (5 tweets - full technical breakdown)
---

TWEET 1 (hook)

i audited hundreds of deployed pages today and found something that should make every indie builder angry.

every site on surge.sh free tier has this in robots.txt:

User-agent: *
Disallow: /

Google can't see a single page. not one.

---

TWEET 2 (the mechanism)

here's why it's brutal.

surge.sh injects that robots.txt at CDN level before your files are served.

you can deploy your own robots.txt to the project root. it doesn't matter.

theirs wins. every time. the CDN intercepts the request and returns their block file.

this isn't a bug they forgot to fix. it's a free-tier restriction.

---

TWEET 3 (the damage)

what this actually costs you:

zero organic discovery on any page.
zero indexing. Google sees the Disallow and stops.
zero structured data picked up even if your schema markup is perfect.
zero long-tail keyword traffic no matter how good your on-page work is.

you could have the best title tags in your niche. doesn't matter.

the crawlers never got past the front door.

---

TWEET 4 (the audit process)

how to check your own sites fast:

curl https://yoursite.surge.sh/robots.txt

look for "Disallow: /"

then open Google Search Console, check coverage. if you see 0 indexed pages on a site you built months ago, this is probably why.

do this for every surge.sh deployment you own. takes 5 minutes.

---

TWEET 5 (the fix + close)

the fix is a 10-minute migration to vercel or netlify.

both free. both zero crawl restrictions. both deploy from the same dist folder.

surge.sh is a great tool for fast internal prototypes and demos.

for anything you want Google to find, it's the wrong call.

hosting choice is SEO strategy. most builders don't find out until months of traffic are already gone.
