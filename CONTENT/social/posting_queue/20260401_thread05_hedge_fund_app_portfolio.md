## THREAD (8 tweets)

**Hook:**
I manage my iOS app portfolio like a hedge fund.

weekly rebalancing. kill rules. position sizing. Monte Carlo scoring.

here's the actual system:

**Tweet 2:**
most indie devs ship an app and pray.

hedge fund managers don't pray. they have kill rules.

mine:
- app < $100 MRR after 60 days = kill
- engagement < 500 users after 90 days = kill
- 20%+ growth at $500+ MRR = double down

no emotion. just numbers.

**Tweet 3:**
position sizing = how much time to allocate per app.

traditional approach: work on whatever feels exciting today.

quant approach: rank every app by (revenue potential x automation potential x market gap) / (effort x competition).

I score on 7 dimensions. the top 3 get 80% of dev time. the rest get maintenance mode.

**Tweet 4:**
the scoring dimensions:

1. revenue potential (TAM x conversion x price)
2. speed to first dollar
3. automation potential (can agents maintain it?)
4. downside risk
5. competition density
6. synergy with other apps in portfolio
7. capital efficiency

every app gets a composite score weekly.

**Tweet 5:**
the portfolio effect is real.

1 app has a 30% chance of hitting $1K MRR.
10 apps with 30% each = 97% chance at least one hits.

this is basic probability but almost nobody applies it to app development.

diversification isn't a strategy. it's math.

**Tweet 6:**
rebalancing = the part everyone skips.

every Monday:
- pull revenue data for each app
- compare to projection
- any app below the kill line? stop all work on it.
- any app above double-down threshold? shift resources from bottom performers.

sounds obvious. almost nobody does it systematically.

**Tweet 7:**
the meta-insight: building apps is now commoditized. Claude Code can ship a full iOS app in a day.

the moat isn't building. it's portfolio management.

knowing WHICH apps to build, WHEN to kill, and WHERE to double down.

that's quant thinking applied to indie dev.

**Tweet 8:**
I built a script that does this automatically.

`portfolio_optimizer.py` runs weekly. scores every app. generates kill/scale/boost recommendations.

the system manages itself. I just review the output.

this is what happens when you treat apps like a hedge fund treats positions.
