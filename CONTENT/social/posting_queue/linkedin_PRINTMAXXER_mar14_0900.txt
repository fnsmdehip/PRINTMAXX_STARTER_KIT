Most cold email advice tells you to personalize. Almost none of it tells you how to do it at scale.

Here's what actually works: personalize the first line only. Everything else can be templated.

I tested 3 approaches across 400 emails this month:

Approach A: fully personalized, hand-written. 14% reply rate. 8 minutes per email.
Approach B: first-line custom, rest templated. 11% reply rate. 90 seconds per email.
Approach C: fully templated, zero personalization. 4% reply rate. 20 seconds per email.

Approach B wins on ROI by a wide margin. You get 80% of the reply rate at 20% of the time cost.

The first line has to reference something real: a specific post they wrote, a product launch, a hire they made. One sentence. Then transition to your template.

That's it. Stop writing essays. Start writing openers.
