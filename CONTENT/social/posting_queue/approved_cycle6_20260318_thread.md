## 1/7

a Turkish app studio is printing $900k/month. they ship 30+ apps per quarter. most of their "developers" can't write a for loop.

here's the playbook they're running and how anyone can replicate it.

---

## 2/7

step 1: find the demand.

they don't brainstorm app ideas. they scrape App Store reviews of apps with 3-star averages. thousands of users literally typing what's broken and what they want instead.

the app idea comes from the complaint. not from a whiteboard.

---

## 3/7

step 2: vibe code it.

Google Stitch: screenshot of a competitor's UI, get a working React Native layout in 30 seconds.

Rork: describe the app in English, get a deployed prototype with backend in under an hour.

Bolt/Cursor: assemble the backend, connect APIs, handle auth.

total build time per app: 1-3 days. total cost: under $200 in API credits.

---

## 4/7

step 3: template everything.

they don't rebuild from scratch. base template handles auth, payments, push notifications, onboarding, analytics. each new app is a skin on proven infrastructure.

the 31st app takes 6 hours. the 1st took 3 days. that's the compounding advantage.

---

## 5/7

step 4: spray and pray (with data).

ship 30 apps. 25 will flatline. 3-4 will get traction. 1-2 will print.

but "pray" isn't random. they A/B test app store screenshots, keywords, and pricing within 48 hours of launch. apps that don't hit 100 downloads in 72 hours get killed. zero emotional attachment.

---

## 6/7

step 5: double down on winners.

the 1-2 apps that hit get all the resources. paid ads, ASO optimization, feature expansion, localization into 12 languages.

$900k/month doesn't come from 30 apps. it comes from 2 apps that got 100% of the attention after the other 28 proved the market.

---

## 7/7

the entire model works because the cost of failure dropped to near zero.

building 30 apps used to cost $1.5M and 18 months. now it costs $6,000 and 90 days.

the studios printing aren't smarter. they just ship 10x more experiments at 1/100th the cost.

vibe coding isn't a meme. it's a factory.
