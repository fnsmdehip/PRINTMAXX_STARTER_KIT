PLATFORM: indie hacker communities (r/SideProject, r/EntrepreneurRideAlong, Indie Hackers)
TOPIC: OpenClaw local biz case study

built a system that scrapes local businesses, grades their websites, and generates replacement sites automatically.

first city: Nashville.

results:
- 30 businesses discovered via Google Maps API
- 15 graded (8 scored A-, 3 scored B+, 2 scored D, 2 scored F)
- 2 preview replacement sites built and deployed to surge.sh in under 2 hours
- 2 cold emails drafted referencing specific problems (no SSL, not mobile-friendly, 9.6 second load times)

the D and F graded businesses are the leads. their sites are objectively bad. the cold email writes itself: "your site takes 9.6 seconds to load. here's a replacement i already built. it loads in 0.8 seconds."

next city: Memphis.

stack: Python scraper, Google PageSpeed API for grading, HTML templates, surge.sh for instant deployment, Claude for email drafts.

total cost: $0. everything runs locally.
