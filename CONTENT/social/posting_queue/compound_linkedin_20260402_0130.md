## POST 1 — AI Agent Economics
**Type:** thought_leadership

Running 33 autonomous AI agents costs $200/month.

They collectively analyze 192,700 leads, scan 8 data sources daily, generate content, cross-pollinate data between business lines, and audit live websites for conversion issues.

The cost per agent: $6.06/month.

The cost of an equivalent human team doing this manually: somewhere between $150K-300K/year in salary.

But here's the thing nobody talks about: the bottleneck in an AI agent system isn't the agents. It's the human operator.

My system found 8 qualified business opportunities today, scored them all 8+/10 on market viability, and generated briefs with revenue projections. One has zero commercial competitors (agent observability dashboards, 166 GitHub stars in a week).

Revenue from all of this: $0.

Because every revenue path ultimately requires a human to click "submit." Create an account. Send an email. Upload a file.

The agents can research, analyze, score, generate, and optimize. They can't log into Stripe. They can't sign up for affiliate programs. They can't submit an app to the App Store.

If you're building AI agent systems for your business, plan for the human handoff points from day one. The agent layer is the easy part.

## POST 2 — CTA Optimization Data
**Type:** tactical

Ran a conversion audit on 6 affiliate comparison pages today.

Before/after on CTA button copy:

"Try Instantly Free Trial" → "Start sending with Instantly. 14-day free trial."
"Try Apollo Free" → "Start Apollo free. 275M+ contacts, no credit card."
"Try Smartlead Free Trial" → "Start with Smartlead. Unlimited mailboxes from day 1."

Pattern: move the differentiating benefit INTO the button. Don't make people click to find out why they should click.

Page conversion score: 42/100 → 68/100.

Also found every affiliate link had ?ref=PLACEHOLDER — generating zero commissions on every click. Real clicks, real traffic, zero tracking.

Two lessons:
1. Audit your affiliate links. Right now. grep for PLACEHOLDER in your codebase.
2. Benefit-first CTAs outperform feature-first CTAs every time. Put the "why" in the button, not behind it.
