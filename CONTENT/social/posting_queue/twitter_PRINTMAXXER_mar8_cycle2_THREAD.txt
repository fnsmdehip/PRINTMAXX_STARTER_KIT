1/6
Alex Becker posted this to his private FB group in 2020:

"What's that, 5 hours of work? You have 195 more to go!"

he sold Hyros for $110M+ this year. here's what that mindset actually looks like when you break it down.

---

2/6
Hyros is ad tracking software. not sexy. not viral. not consumer.

B2B SaaS solving one boring problem: which ad actually drove the sale. attribution. every agency and brand needs it. low churn because switching tracking is painful.

boring problems, massive TAM.

---

3/6
"5 hours of work? you have 195 more to go."

200 hours a week is obviously impossible for one person. the point isn't literal. the point is: when you finish something, you're not done. you just cleared space for the next thing.

most people ship one feature and call it a day. exit founders ship one feature and immediately start the next three.

---

4/6
the $110M playbook, simplified:

1. find a boring B2B problem with high switching costs
2. build the most focused solution possible
3. charge premium (Hyros is $500-$2,000+/mo)
4. compound customers for 5+ years
5. let private equity find you

no growth hacks. no viral moments. just compounding.

---

5/6
what solopreneurs get wrong: they chase consumer apps with 0.5% conversion rates and $4.99 price points.

what exit founders build: B2B tools with 85%+ margins, 12-month contracts, and customers who literally can't switch without losing data.

same amount of code. wildly different outcomes.

---

6/6
the filter: would this problem still exist if there were no social media?

ad attribution tracking? yes. payroll? yes. invoicing? yes. CRM? yes.

these boring problems don't disappear because an algorithm changed. that's the moat. build for problems that outlast platforms.
