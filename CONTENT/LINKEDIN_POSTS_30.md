Real progress was uncomfortable. Writing and publishing when nobody reads it. Emailing strangers who might ignore you. Posting on Reddit and getting 3 upvotes.

The tools gave me the feeling of building without the risk of building.

When I cut 17 tools, I lost the safety net. And I had to actually ship.

Month 1: $0 revenue. Built 3 Notion templates.
Month 2: $400. First sales. Terrifying and exhilarating.
Month 3: $4,127. Templates, consulting, affiliates.

The turning point wasn't a strategy or a tool or a framework.

It was the decision to stop preparing and start doing.

If your tool stack is growing faster than your revenue, the tools aren't the investment. They're the procrastination.
