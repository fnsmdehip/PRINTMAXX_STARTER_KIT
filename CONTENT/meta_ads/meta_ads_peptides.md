## COMPLIANCE NOTES (all ads)
- Peptides are prescription medications when used therapeutically
- No claims about specific disease treatment without clinical citation
- "Research peptides" framing is NOT allowed for human use marketing
- No anti-aging "reversal" claims - use "support" language
- Must state "Physician-supervised" or "Prescription required"
- BPC-157, TB-500, etc. are NOT FDA-approved drugs - never imply otherwise
- Focus on the physician oversight and clinical setting, not the peptide alone

## CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE NOTES

**Budget Allocation:**
- PAS ads: 30% (cold traffic education)
- Social Proof: 20% (authority-building for newer category)
- Price Comparison: 20% (vs. expensive alternatives)
- Educational: 20% (peptides need MORE education than GLP-1 or TRT)
- UGC: 10%

**Key Insight:** Peptide therapy has lower consumer awareness than GLP-1 or TRT. Run educational ads as first touch at higher frequency. Retarget educated visitors with PAS and price comparison. The funnel is longer: 5-7 touchpoints average.

**Custom Audiences:**
- Lookalike 1%: based on peptides-101 page visitors (>60 sec)
- Retarget: visited any education page, didn't book
- Interest stack: biohacking + longevity + NOT interested in steroids/TikTok wellness
- Exclude: under 25 (waste of spend, low intent)

**Placement:**
- Feed: All variants
- Stories: PEP-PAS-05 and UGC variants
- Reels: Educational variants with text overlay (PEP-ED-01, PEP-ED-02)
- Right column: Price comparison (data-heavy, works in smaller format)

**Meta Policy Notes:**
- Peptide ads may trigger manual review more frequently
- Avoid words: "inject," "injection," "needle" in ad copy
- Frame as "physician-supervised therapy" not "self-administered treatment"
- Landing pages must have clear medical disclaimers
