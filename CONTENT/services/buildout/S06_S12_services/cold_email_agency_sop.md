# Cold Email Agency — Complete Operating Procedures

## Business Model

You run the entire cold email infrastructure for clients. They pay you to:
1. Build their sending infrastructure (domains, inboxes, DNS records)
2. Write their sequences (5-7 email campaigns)
3. Source and enrich their target leads
4. Manage daily operations (monitoring, replies, optimization)
5. Book meetings directly into their calendar

**Pricing:**
- Setup fee: $1,500 one-time (infrastructure + first campaign)
- Monthly retainer: $1,000-2,500/mo (ongoing ops + new campaigns)
- Performance add-on: $200/meeting booked (optional, for high-trust clients)
- DIY course: $197 (passive, teaches the system)

**Unit economics:**
- 5 clients × $1,500/mo avg retainer = $7,500/mo
- + $200/meeting × 10 meetings/mo avg = $2,000/mo
- Total: $9,500/mo with 5 active clients
- Client acquisition: 1-2 new clients/month via your own cold outbound (eat what you cook)

## Infrastructure Setup (New Client Onboarding)

### Day 1: Domain Purchase + DNS Setup

**Domain strategy:**
- Never send from client's main domain
- Buy 2-4 variation domains: if client is acmecorp.com, buy acmehq.com, getacme.com, meetacme.com
- Register with Namecheap or Cloudflare (avoid GoDaddy — lower deliverability reputation)
- Cost: $12-15/domain/year

**DNS records (required for every domain):**

```
# SPF Record (TXT)
Name: @
Value: v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all
# Replace Google with your ESP: Instantly uses _spf.instantlyai.com

# DKIM Record (TXT) — generated by your ESP during inbox setup
Name: google._domainkey  (or instantly._domainkey)
Value: [generated by ESP — copy from ESP dashboard]

# DMARC Record (TXT)
Name: _dmarc
Value: v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com; pct=100; adkim=r; aspf=r

# MX Records (for Google Workspace)
Name: @
Priority 1: aspmx.l.google.com
Priority 5: alt1.aspmx.l.google.com
Priority 5: alt2.aspmx.l.google.com
Priority 10: alt3.aspmx.l.google.com
Priority 10: alt4.aspmx.l.google.com

# CNAME (for click tracking — Instantly)
Name: tracking
Value: tracking.instantlyai.com
```

**Inbox setup:**
- Google Workspace: $6/inbox/mo — best deliverability
- 2 inboxes per domain (first.last@domain.com and firstlast@domain.com)
- Enable 2FA on every inbox
- Connect to Instantly.ai or Lemlist via OAuth

### Day 2-30: Inbox Warmup

**Never skip this. Sending cold from a fresh inbox = instant spam folder.**

Warmup process:
1. Connect inboxes to Instantly warmup pool (or use Mailreach.io $25/mo)
2. Warmup sends automated human-like emails between inbox pool members
3. These emails get marked "not spam" + replied to = inbox reputation builds
4. Start 0 manual sends during warmup
5. Warmup schedule:

| Week | Warmup Volume/Day | Manual Cold Sends/Day |
|------|------------------|----------------------|
| 1-2 | 30 automated | 0 |
| 3-4 | 50 automated | 10 |
| 5-6 | 60 automated | 20 |
| 7-8 | 70 automated | 30 |
| 9-12 | 80 automated | 40 |
| 12+ | 80 automated | 50 (max) |

**Never exceed 50 cold sends/day per inbox. Ever.**

### Day 30+: Campaign Launch

1. Upload verified lead list to Instantly (or Lemlist)
2. Create sequence (see templates below)
3. Set sending window: 8am-5pm M-F in prospect's timezone
4. Enable open tracking (for Lemlist) — Instantly tracks by default
5. Set reply detection to pause sequence on reply
6. Launch with 20 leads/day per inbox, scale by 5/day each week

## Daily Operations SOP

### AM Checklist (30 min)
- [ ] Check Instantly / Lemlist dashboard for bounces > 5% (pause campaign if so)
- [ ] Check spam rate (should be <0.1% — if above, pause sending, investigate)
- [ ] Review new replies from overnight (EU timezone lands overnight in US)
- [ ] Forward hot leads (interested replies) to client via Slack/email
- [ ] Update lead sheet: mark replied, booked, not interested, out of office

### Midday (15 min)
- [ ] Check warmup health (Mailreach or Instantly warmup score should be >80%)
- [ ] Any deliverability alerts? Check Google Postmaster Tools for each domain
- [ ] Book any "let's chat" replies into client's Calendly (or client's AE's cal)

### End of Day (15 min)
- [ ] Log daily stats to client report sheet: sent, opens, replies, meetings
- [ ] Flag any domains hitting >2% bounce rate (means list quality issue)
- [ ] Queue next day's leads if volume is running low

## Deliverability Monitoring

**Tools to use:**

| Tool | Cost | What to Monitor |
|------|------|----------------|
| Google Postmaster Tools | Free | Spam rate, IP/domain reputation |
| Mail-Tester.com | Free (3/day) | Email score before launch |
| MXToolbox | Free | SPF/DKIM/DMARC validation |
| Instantly Warmup Score | Included | Inbox health per email |
| GlockApps | $9/mo | Inbox placement test across 30 providers |

**Danger signals:**
- Spam rate > 0.1% on Google Postmaster → pause sending, investigate list quality
- Bounce rate > 5% → stop, re-verify entire list before resuming
- Open rate < 20% → deliverability problem, not copy problem
- Domain reputation "Low" in Postmaster → that domain is toast, retire it, buy new one

## Offboarding / Handoff SOP

When a client ends the engagement (or wants to bring in-house):
1. Export all campaign data from Instantly → CSV → deliver to client
2. Export all lead lists with engagement status
3. Document all sequences with performance notes
4. Provide DNS records guide for their IT team
5. Transfer domain management to client's Namecheap account
6. 30-day transition window included in every contract

## Contracts + Legal

**Required in every client contract:**
- Scope of work and deliverables
- Client owns all their leads and campaign data
- No guarantee of specific meeting counts (results vary)
- Client responsible for CAN-SPAM/GDPR compliance of their offer
- 30-day notice to cancel
- IP: sequences created during engagement are client's property after contract ends
- No poaching clause: client cannot hire your operators for 12 months after contract end

**Rate limit liability:** If client's domain gets blacklisted due to client-provided bad lists or client-initiated sends outside the system, that's on client. Document this explicitly.
