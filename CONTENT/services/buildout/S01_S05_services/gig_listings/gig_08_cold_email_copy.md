## GIG DESCRIPTION

### The reason you're not getting replies has nothing to do with your offer.

It's the email. Most cold emails fail for 3 reasons:
1. The opener is about you, not them
2. The value prop is vague
3. The CTA asks for too much too soon

I write cold emails using frameworks proven across 500K+ sends. Not generic templates. Emails that sound like a real person wrote them, because a real person did.

**Frameworks I use:**

**Pattern Interrupt** — Open with something they don't expect (observation, specific data point, genuine compliment on something specific)

**FOMO + Social Proof** — Name-drop similar companies you've helped. Nobody wants to be last.

**Consequence-First** — Lead with what happens if they don't solve this problem. Most cold emails lead with the solution — backwards.

**Trojan Horse** — Give them something valuable in the email itself (a data point, a quick insight, a resource). Then ask.

**"Are you the right person?"** — For targeting companies where you're not sure who to contact. Disarms immediately.

---

**What I write:**
- Initial cold email (under 100 words — longer doesn't work)
- 3 follow-ups (each different angle, shorter than the last)
- Breakup email (pattern interrupt, often gets the most replies)
- LinkedIn connection request + follow-up messages
- Subject line variants (A/B test ready)

A: Your ICP (ideal customer profile), your offer/product description, any previous sequences that got replies or didn't, and 2-3 case studies or results if you have them.

**Q: Will these work with any email tool?**

**Q: How do I know if the copy is working?**
A: Track reply rate (target: 8%+) and positive reply rate (target: 3%+). I'll tell you exactly which metrics to watch and what benchmarks mean the sequence is broken.
