The Problem

Why most local business sites don't work

You paid someone to build a site. It looks fine. But it's not getting you any business. Here's why.

🐌

Loads too slow

53% of mobile visitors leave if a site takes over 3 seconds to load. Most local sites take 6-10 seconds. Google penalizes this in rankings too.

📵

Broken on mobile

75% of local business searches happen on phones. If your site isn't built mobile-first, you're losing most of your potential customers before they even read a word.

🔍

Doesn't rank on Google

Being on page 2 of Google is the same as not existing. Without proper on-page SEO and local schema markup, you won't show up when people search for what you do.

📞

No clear path to contact

Most sites bury the phone number. The call-to-action is vague. Visitors don't know what to do next so they leave. Every confused visitor is a lost customer.

How long does it actually take?

First draft is ready in 48 hours. Total project (including revisions and launch) is typically 7-10 business days. If you need it faster, message us — we've done 72-hour turnarounds for $500 rush fee.

Who owns the website?

You do. 100%. We build it, hand over all the code, and you own it forever. If you want to move it somewhere else, you can. No lock-in.

What does hosting cost?

Vercel's free tier handles most local business traffic (up to 100GB bandwidth/month). If you need more, it's $20/mo. Domain registration is $10-15/yr through Namecheap or GoDaddy — you manage that directly.

Do I need to provide content and photos?

Ideally yes — your photos convert better than stock. If you don't have them, we use high-quality free stock (Unsplash) and can write basic copy for an additional $200. You can always swap content later.

Will this help me rank on Google?

Yes, significantly, but not overnight. On-page SEO fixes (which we do at launch) typically show results in 4-8 weeks. Clients in low-competition local markets see movement in 2-3 weeks. We don't guarantee rank 1 — nobody ethically can — but we eliminate every technical barrier.
