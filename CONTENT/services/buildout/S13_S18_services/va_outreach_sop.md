# VA Outreach SOP — S14

## Overview

Hiring a VA to run your cold outreach at scale is the unlock between $2K/mo and $10K/mo.
The VA doesn't close — they fill the pipeline. You close.

Target: VA runs 200-400 personalized outreach messages/day across email + LinkedIn.
Cost: $300-600/mo (Philippines-based VA, 4-6 hours/day)
Expected pipeline: 15-30 warm leads/week at this volume.

---

## Phase 1: VA Sourcing

### Where to Hire

**Option A — Onlinejobs.ph (recommended)**
- Cost: $69/mo job posting or $99/mo for premium
- Quality: Best for long-term VAs (4+ months average tenure vs Upwork's 2)
- Search terms: "lead generation VA", "cold email specialist", "LinkedIn outreach"
- Red flags: Only 1-2 months experience, references not provided, no English sample

**Option B — Upwork**
- Cost: 10-20% platform fee added to rate
- Best for: Short-term testing before committing
- Rate: $4-8/hr Philippines-based

**Option C — VA Catalogs (FastVA, VirtualStaff.ph)**
- Cost: $299-499/mo for pre-vetted VAs
- Best for: Fast start, less sourcing work

### Job Description Template

```
Title: Lead Generation & Outreach VA (Cold Email + LinkedIn)

About the role:
We run outreach campaigns for a digital services business. You'll research
leads, personalize cold emails, and manage LinkedIn outreach. No sales calls.
This is research + writing + sending.

What you'll do:
- 4-6 hours/day, Monday-Friday
- Research 50-80 new leads/day using [tools provided]
- Send 30-50 cold emails/day via [tool provided]
- Send 30-50 LinkedIn connection requests + messages/day
- Log all activity in our tracking sheet (Google Sheets)
- Flag any warm responses for my review

Requirements:
- Strong written English (you'll write personalized first lines)
- Experience with Apollo.io, Hunter.io, or similar
- Reliable internet, consistent availability 8AM-2PM Manila time
- Portfolio or test results from previous outreach work

Pay: $350-450/month (based on experience)
Please reply with: (1) your outreach experience, (2) your best English writing sample
```

### VA Evaluation Test

Before hiring, send 5 candidates the same test:

**Test:**
"Here are 3 company profiles from LinkedIn/Crunchbase. Write a personalized cold email opener (2 sentences) for each — the kind of first line that would make the founder actually read the email. Do NOT use templates or generic phrases."

**Score on:**
- Specificity (did they mention something real about the company)
- Grammar and naturalness (reads like a human, not a template)
- Creativity (does it stand out vs "I noticed your company does X")

**Hire only if 2+ out of 3 openers are genuinely strong.**

## Phase 2: Onboarding VA

### Day 1 (2-hour Zoom call)

1. Walk through your offer + ICP (ideal customer profile) in detail
   - "We help [type of company] get [outcome] by [method]. They're usually [revenue range], [team size], [tech stack or growth signal]"
2. Show them the lead research process (screen share)
3. Show them the email templates + how to personalize first lines
4. Show them the tracking sheet structure
5. Set KPI expectations: 50 leads researched + 40 emails sent on Day 1 (end of week: 200 leads, 180 emails)
6. Set check-in schedule: daily Slack/WhatsApp status update at end of their workday

### Onboarding Notion Page (Share With VA)

Create a single Notion page with:
- [ ] ICP description (2 paragraphs)
- [ ] Lead sources (where to find leads)
- [ ] Disqualification criteria (who to skip)
- [ ] Email templates (2-3 versions per campaign)
- [ ] Personalization examples (good vs bad first lines with notes)
- [ ] LinkedIn message templates
- [ ] Tracking sheet link
- [ ] "If you're unsure, don't guess — Slack me"

### Week 1 Protocol

- Check VA's daily log every morning (takes 5 minutes)
- Review 10 random emails they wrote for quality
- Flag anything that feels templated or off-brand
- Give written feedback via Loom or Notion comments
- Don't micromanage — set expectations, check output, not process

## Phase 3: Daily Operations

### VA Daily Workflow (SOP Document)

**Hour 1: Lead Research**
- Open Apollo.io / LinkedIn Sales Navigator / Hunter.io
- Filter by: [ICP criteria — saved as search in tool]
- Extract 50-80 leads to Google Sheet (columns: First Name, Last Name, Email, LinkedIn URL, Company, Title, Recent news/trigger, Notes)
- Verify emails with Hunter.io verify or NeverBounce before adding to send queue

**Hour 2-3: Email Outreach**
- Open Instantly.ai / Smartlead / Lemlist (whichever client uses)
- Pull today's send list from Google Sheet
- For each lead: paste template, customize first line based on "Notes" column
- Send 40-60 emails (platform limits + warmup stage determine ceiling)
- Log: sent count, sequences added, any notable personalization done

**Hour 4-5: LinkedIn Outreach**
- Open LinkedIn (on client's sub-account if using Sales Navigator)
- Send 30-40 connection requests with personalized note (2-sentence max)
- Check pending connections from yesterday — if accepted, send follow-up message
- Log: requests sent, accepts from prior day, responses (copy paste any responses into Slack for review)

**Hour 5-6: Admin + Reporting**
- Update daily tracking sheet (leads researched, emails sent, LinkedIn sent)
- Flag any positive responses or "not right now" replies (don't touch — hand to you)
- Send end-of-day Slack update: "Day X: XX leads researched, XX emails sent, XX LinkedIn, X replies (see sheet)"

## Quality Control

### Weekly QA (15 minutes)

- Pull 20 random emails VA sent from Instantly.ai sent folder
- Score 1-5 on: personalization quality, grammar, subject line strength
- Any score below 3: record Loom with specific feedback
- Monthly: calculate response rate by VA's personalization quality tier

### Metrics to Track

| Metric | Benchmark | Flag if |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Emails sent/day | 40-60 | Under 30 (speed issue) |
| Open rate | 45-60% | Under 35% (subject lines) |
| Reply rate | 4-8% | Under 3% (copy issue) |
| Positive reply rate | 30-40% of replies | Under 20% (ICP issue) |
| LinkedIn accept rate | 25-35% | Under 15% (message issue) |

## Compensation + Retention

**Base:** $350-450/month
**Performance bonus:** $50 per booked call that converts to client
**Retaining a good VA:** Give raises after 3 months if metrics are solid. Raising from $350 to $450 costs you $100 — replacing a trained VA costs 40+ hours.

**Common VA churn reasons:**
- Better-paying client found (counter: raise pay proactively)
- Burnout from repetitive work (counter: rotate lead sources, give small wins)
- No feedback = no improvement = no motivation (counter: weekly 10-min Loom review)

---

## Scaling from 1 VA to a Team

**1 VA:** 40-60 emails/day, 30-40 LinkedIn/day → 15-25 warm leads/week
**2 VAs:** 80-120 emails/day, 60-80 LinkedIn/day → 30-50 warm leads/week
**3 VAs + 1 SDR (closer):** Full agency pipeline, $25K-50K/mo revenue possible

**When to hire VA #2:** When you're personally handling 8+ warm leads/week and booking 4+ calls/week consistently.
