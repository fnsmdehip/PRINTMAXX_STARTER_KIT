# Fractional CMO Offer — S34

## The Offer

You become the outsourced marketing brain for companies that can't afford a $200K/year CMO
but are growing fast enough to need one. 1-3 clients, $3K-8K/month each, 20-40 hours/month total.

**The gap you fill:**
- Series A startup: has $2M in runway but no marketing leadership
- 7-figure business: founder-led sales but no repeatable marketing engine
- PE-backed SMB: just acquired, needs a growth plan before scaling

**Why they pay $3K-8K/month:**
A full-time senior marketing director costs $120K-180K/year + benefits + equity.
You're $36K-96K/year with zero overhead, no benefits, and results-focused.
For a $5M revenue company, that's a 20-40x leverage difference.

---

## Ideal Client Profile

**Revenue:** $1M-$20M/year
**Team:** 3-20 employees, no dedicated marketing leader
**Growth stage:** Post-product-market-fit, pre-scalable-marketing
**Budget for marketing:** $5K-50K/month in ad spend or content budget
**Decision maker:** Founder, CEO, or board-appointed operator

**Red flags to avoid:**
- Below $1M revenue (they can't really afford this or need execution more than strategy)
- Companies with existing CMO or VP Marketing (you're filling a gap, not stepping on toes)
- Founder who wants to stay involved in every marketing decision (will undermine your work)

## Service Definition

### What a Fractional CMO Actually Does (Month-by-Month)

**Month 1: Audit + Strategy**
- Full marketing audit (channels, spend, conversion rates, CAC, LTV, attribution)
- Competitive landscape analysis
- ICP refinement (who's your actual buyer vs who you think it is)
- Channel prioritization matrix (where to invest next quarter)
- 90-day roadmap delivery (document + presentation)
- Weekly 60-min strategy calls (4 calls)
- Unlimited async Slack access

**Month 2: Systems Build**
- Set up or rebuild tracking infrastructure (GA4, CRM, attribution)
- Establish reporting cadence (weekly marketing dashboard, monthly board report format)
- Hire/brief team members or contractors as needed
- Launch first 1-2 campaigns based on 90-day roadmap
- A/B test cadence established (minimum 2 tests running at all times)
- Weekly 60-min calls continue

**Month 3+: Execution and Optimization**
- Weekly: review metrics, adjust bids/creative/messaging
- Monthly: channel performance review, budget reallocation decisions
- Quarterly: full strategy refresh, new 90-day plan
- Ongoing: manage marketing team/contractors, review all major campaigns before launch
- 2 weekly calls per month (every other week)

**What you don't do:**
- Write copy (you brief and review, not write)
- Design (same — brief and QA)
- Run ads manually (you set strategy and review performance, not daily ops)
- Admin tasks (you're not a VA — escalate this if a client tries it)

## Discovery Call Framework

**Length:** 45-60 minutes

**Structure:**
1. **Context** (10 min): Tell me about the business. Revenue, model, team, how you acquire customers now.
2. **Problem** (15 min): What's working, what's not, what's the marketing-specific challenge you're trying to solve?
3. **Gap** (5 min): Who's responsible for marketing decisions right now? What's missing?
4. **Fit check** (10 min): Explain your role, what you do and don't do, typical client outcome at 90 days
5. **Commercial** (5 min): Show pricing, ask "based on what you've told me, which engagement level makes sense?"
6. **Close or next step** (5 min): Either close on the call or send a proposal within 24 hours

**Deal-breaker questions to ask on call:**
- "What happened with marketing attempts before? Why didn't they work?"
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What does success look like at 6 months?"
- "What's your current marketing budget per month?"

If budget is under $2K/month in ad spend AND under $3K/month for the retainer — they're not ready.

## Deliverable Templates

### 90-Day Marketing Roadmap Template

**Section 1: Audit Findings (2 pages)**
- Current state: channels, spend, conversion rates, CAC, LTV
- What's working (double down)
- What's broken (fix or kill)
- What's untested (explore)

**Section 2: ICP + Positioning (1 page)**
- True ICP definition (data-backed, not founder intuition)
- Messaging hierarchy (what to lead with, what to support)
- Competitive differentiation (1-sentence version)

**Section 3: 90-Day Roadmap (2 pages)**
- Month 1 priorities: [3 specific initiatives with KPIs]
- Month 2 priorities: [3 specific initiatives]
- Month 3 priorities: [3 specific initiatives]

**Section 4: Metrics Dashboard Setup (1 page)**
- KPIs you'll track: CAC, LTV, MQL→SQL rate, revenue by channel

- "Stop measuring" list (vanity metrics to drop)

## Revenue Math + Exit Strategy

**3-client fractional setup:**
- 3 × $4,997 = $14,991/month → $179,892/year
- Hours: ~75/month (lean if you're systematic)
- Hourly effective rate: ~$200/hour

**Scaling options:**
1. Add 1 advisory client: +$2,997/month at only 10-12 hours
2. Build team: hire junior strategist at $4K/month, take on 2 more clients at $4,997 each = +$5,994 net
3. Productize: turn your frameworks into a course or consulting group ($997-2,997 one-time)
4. Exit: sell client base to a marketing agency (common — agency pays 6-12 months of retainer value for established client relationships)

**Typical 12-month ramp:**
- Month 1-2: first client at $3,997 (below-market to get first testimonial)
- Month 3-4: second client at $4,997 (now have proof of concept)
- Month 5-6: third client at $4,997
- Month 7+: raise month 1 client to $4,997 or add advisory client
- Month 12: $14,991-18,000/month
