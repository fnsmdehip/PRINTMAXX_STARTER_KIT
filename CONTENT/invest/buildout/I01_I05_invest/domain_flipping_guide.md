# Domain Flipping Guide — I02

## The Business Model

buy domains for $8-15, sell for $500-50,000. the spread is real.
average successful flip: $1,200. average hold time: 6-18 months.
this is not passive. it's pattern recognition + patience.

## Where to Buy (In Priority Order)

### 1. Expired Domain Auctions (Best ROI)

**GoDaddy Auctions** (godaddy.com/domain-auctions)
- Largest auction platform. Closeouts = ending in 24h, often cheap.
- Filter: .com, under $100, check metrics before bidding

**NameJet** (namejet.com)
- Pre-release auctions — domains dropping from registration
- Often catches premium drops before public

**DropCatch** (dropcatch.com)
- Specialized in catching expiring domains
- Backorder service for domains you want at drop

**Dynadot Auctions** (dynadot.com)
- Less competition than GoDaddy, similar quality

**Expired Domains** (expireddomains.net)
- Free list aggregator for all dropping domains
- Filter by DA, backlinks, age — finds gems before auctions

### 2. Marketplace Buys (Instant Inventory)

**Sedo** (sedo.com) — largest secondary market, $0 listing fee, 15% commission on sale
**Afternic** (afternic.com) — GoDaddy-owned, massive buyer network
**Dan.com** — clean interface, lower fees (9% vs 15-20% elsewhere)
**Flippa** (flippa.com) — more developed sites, higher prices

### 3. Hand Registration (Lowest Cost, Slowest Return)

**Namecheap** — $8.88/yr .com registration
**Porkbun** — $9.73/yr .com, clean UI
**Cloudflare Registrar** — at-cost pricing (~$9.77/yr), no markup

**Hand reg opportunity signals:**
- Emerging trend + .com available (e.g., "AIReceipts.com" before the trend hits)
- Brandable 5-6 letter .com (CVCVC pattern = most liquid)
- Location + industry combos (e.g., "AustinSolarInstall.com")
- Exact-match keywords with commercial intent

## Domain Valuation Framework

### The 4 Metrics That Matter

**1. Domain Age**
- Pre-2005: premium (grandfathered trust signals)
- 2005-2015: solid
- Post-2020: no age premium

**2. Backlink Profile** (check Ahrefs or Moz)
- Domain Authority (DA) 30+: valuable for SEO buyers
- Referring domains > 50: potentially worth $500+
- Spam backlinks: devalues significantly

**3. Keyword Value** (check GKP or Semrush)
- Search volume > 1,000/mo: commercial potential
- CPC > $2: advertisers pay for this traffic
- Buyer intent keywords ("buy X", "X services", "best X"): premium

**4. Brandability**
- Pronounceable: yes = good
- Memorable: 2 syllables = ideal
- Meaning clear without context: premium
- Dictionary words: highest liquidity

### Quick Valuation Formula

```
Base value estimate:
- 1-word .com dictionary word: $2K-50K+
- 2-word .com with commercial intent: $500-5K
- Exact-match keyword .com: search vol × $0.10 to $1
- Brandable 5-letter .com: $200-2K
- Hyphenated domains: 70% discount
- Non-.com TLDs (.net, .org): 50-80% discount vs .com
```

**Comparable sales research:**
- NameBio (namebio.com) — database of 2M+ domain sales with prices
- DNJournal — weekly sales recap with prices
- Search "[domain type] sold price" on NameBio before buying

## Buying Strategy

### Auction Sniping (GoDaddy Auctions)

1. Set up daily filter: .com, $10-150 range, DA 20+, age 5+ years
2. Check expireddomains.net every morning for same-day drops
3. Use NameBio to check if type has sold before and for how much
4. Check Ahrefs (or free Moz) for DA and backlink spam ratio
5. Bid in final 30 minutes only — avoid driving up price early
6. Max bid = 30% of estimated sale price

### Hand Registration Strategy

Focus on 6-month ahead trends:
- Monitor what VC funds are investing in (Crunchbase, AngelList)
- Watch what's trending on Product Hunt in new categories
- Monitor legislation that will create new industries
- Track what terms Gary Vee, Tim Ferriss, or similar are coining

**Register immediately when you see:**
- New regulation creating a market (e.g., "AI compliance" category)
- Viral term without a matching .com
- New product category before mainstream adoption

## Where to Sell

### Passive Listing (Set and Forget)

List on ALL of these simultaneously:
1. **Afternic** — $0 listing, 15-20% commission, GoDaddy partnership gets you exposure
2. **Sedo** — $0 listing, 15% commission, European buyers
3. **Dan.com** — $0 listing, 9% commission (lowest fees)
4. **Epik** — $0 listing, 10% commission

**Pricing strategy:** List at 3-4x what you'd accept. Dan.com supports offer system (best for negotiating).

### Active Outreach (2-5x faster sales)

**Who to target:**
- Startups that would benefit from exact-match domain
- Businesses currently using a hyphenated or .net version
- Companies paying for ads on your domain's keyword

**How to find buyers:**
1. Google "yourdomainkeyword" and note who's bidding on ads
2. LinkedIn search companies in the domain's industry
3. Check if any funded startup uses a less-good version of your domain
4. Search for businesses named exactly what your domain says

**Outreach template:**
```
Subject: [Domain] available — you might want this

Hi [Name],

noticed [Company] is in the [keyword] space.

[yourdomain].com is available and I own it.

might be useful if you're thinking about [specific use case].

asking $[price]. respond if interested.

[Your name]
```

**Volume:** 20-30 outreach emails per domain. 2-5% response rate. close rate on responses: 20-30%.

## Portfolio Management

### Build Toward 50+ Domain Portfolio

**Target allocation:**
- 40% brandable short .coms (quickest liquidity)
- 30% keyword exact-match .coms (slower, higher ceiling)
- 20% geographic + niche combos (steady demand)
- 10% speculative trend plays (hand regs, high risk/reward)

**Monthly budget model:**

| Budget Level | Domains/Mo | Avg Buy | Expected Sales/Mo | Net Monthly |
|-------------|-----------|---------|------------------|-------------|
| $200 | 3-4 | $50 | 0.3 sales | avg $360/sale = $108/mo avg |
| $500 | 8-10 | $55 | 0.8 sales | avg $500/sale = $400/mo avg |
| $1,500 | 15-20 | $75 | 2 sales | avg $800/sale = $1,600/mo avg |
| $5,000 | 20-30 | $170 | 3-4 sales | avg $2,000/sale = $6,000-8,000/mo avg |

Year 1 realistic: $200/mo budget → 36 domains → 4-6 sales → $2,000-4,000 gross

### Renewals Math

Every domain costs $9-15/yr to renew. Portfolio of 50 = $450-750/yr overhead.
Drop anything not sold in 2 years that has no active buyer interest.

## The 90-Day Quick Start

**Days 1-7:**
- Create accounts: GoDaddy Auctions, Afternic, Dan.com, Sedo
- Set up expireddomains.net account with filters
- Read 1 month of DNJournal sales reports to calibrate values
- Buy 0 domains — just watch and learn pricing

**Days 8-30:**
- Buy first 3-5 domains ($50-150 total) from auctions
- Immediately list on Dan.com, Afternic, Sedo
- Send 10 outreach emails per domain

**Days 31-90:**
- Grow to 15-20 domain portfolio
- Expect first sale around day 60-90
- Reinvest 100% of proceeds back into better quality domains
