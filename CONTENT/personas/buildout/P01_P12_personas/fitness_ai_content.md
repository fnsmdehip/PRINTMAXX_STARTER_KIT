## POST 11 — Hook: Gym frequency myth

the frequency compounds.

## POST 16 — Hook: Progressive overload explained
progressive overload = doing slightly more than last time.

last week: 3 sets × 10 reps × 135 lbs bench
this week: 3 sets × 11 reps × 135 lbs (or 3 × 10 × 140 lbs)

that's it. that's the secret. that's why people who train for 5 years look completely different from people who train for 5 years doing the same weight every session.

track your lifts. it doesn't need to be complicated. notes app works.

## POST 17 — Hook: Cutting vs bulking
"should i cut or bulk first?"

if you're under 15% body fat (men) or 25% (women): lean bulk
if you're over those numbers: cut first

why: high body fat reduces insulin sensitivity, which blunts muscle growth anyway. get leaner first, then build.

the "dirty bulk" strategy is just gaining fat faster than necessary and spending the next 6 months cutting it off.

## POST 21 — Hook: Training when sick
mild cold (above-the-neck symptoms): light training is fine. research shows it doesn't prolong illness.

fever, body aches, below-the-neck symptoms: full rest. training while feverish suppresses immune response and can extend illness by days.

the rule: "neck check." above = light training ok. below = rest.
