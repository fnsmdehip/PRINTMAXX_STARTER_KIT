## X / Twitter Algorithm (2026)

### How the For You Feed Works

X uses a two-phase ranking system:

**Phase 1: Candidate retrieval (first 15 minutes)**
- Algorithm pulls 1,500 candidate tweets from your network and interest graph
- Scores each on: likes, retweets, replies, bookmarks, profile clicks, link clicks, video watch time
- Heavy weight on replies (signals genuine engagement, not passive consumption)

**Phase 2: Mixing and filtering**
- Combines candidates from: followed accounts (50%), interest graph (35%), trending topics (15%)
- Applies safety filters (spam, hate speech, sensitive content)
- Final feed shown to user

### What X Rewards in 2026

| Signal | Weight (estimated) | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| Replies | Very High | Real replies, not "great post!" |
| Bookmarks | High | X tracks saves heavily |
| Quote tweets | High | Especially with added commentary |
| Long dwell time | High | Screenshot stops, expanded view |
| Profile clicks | Medium | Shows interest in the author |
| Likes | Medium-Low | Became less predictive over time |
| Retweets (without comment) | Low | Reduced weighting since 2024 |
| Link clicks | Very Low | X deprioritizes external traffic |

### Growth Tactics That Work (March 2026)

1. **Reply bait** — Ask a genuinely interesting question at the end of threads. "What would you add?" outperforms "RT if you agree" 3x.

2. **First 15-minute velocity** — If a tweet gets 10+ engagements in first 15 minutes, it enters amplification phase. Schedule posts when your audience is online. For US solopreneurs: 8-9 AM EST, 12-1 PM EST, 7-9 PM EST.

3. **Thread structure that converts** — Tweet 1 must stand alone and be the hook. Tweets 2-6 expand. Tweet 7+ is where most drop off. Keep under 10 tweets unless exceptional content.

4. **Community posting** — X Communities get their own feed. Target: OpenClaw, Indie Hackers, Claude Code, Startup communities. Posts there can be promoted to your main feed.

5. **Controversial-but-substantiated takes** — Not rage bait. Real counter-intuitive claims with data. "Cold email is dead" (false) gets replies. "Cold email is 10x more effective with 40% shorter emails" (true, with data) gets bookmarks AND replies.

6. **Long-form posts (Premium)** — Since X allows 25K character posts for Premium subscribers, long-form threads can be a single note. These get different algorithmic treatment and often outperform thread format.

### X Algorithm Anti-Patterns (Will Kill Reach)

- External links in first tweet of a thread (move to replies or last tweet)
- Posting same tweet multiple accounts simultaneously (signals coordination)
- Mass follow/unfollow cycles (triggers shadow ban)
- Reply-only accounts with no original content
- Posting 20+ times in one day (looks spammy regardless of quality)
- Hashtag stuffing (more than 2-3 hashtags hurts reach on X now)

### X Monetization Unlocks by Follower Count

| Threshold | Unlock |
|-----------|--------|
| 500 followers, Premium subscriber | Ads revenue share (min 5M impressions/3 months) |
| 10,000 followers | Creator subscriptions |
| Any size | Tips/Super Follows |

## TikTok Algorithm (2026)

### How TikTok Decides Who Sees Your Video

TikTok uses a layered audience expansion model:

**Layer 1 (0-200 views):** Shown to a test audience of your existing followers + highly correlated interest group
**Layer 2 (200-1K views):** If completion rate >60%, pushed to broader interest category
**Layer 3 (1K-10K views):** If like rate + share rate positive, pushed to trending pool
**Layer 4 (10K+):** Potential For You page at scale

### Critical Signals (2026 Priority Order)

1. **Completion rate** — Most important. Watch to end > 60% = signal to expand. Under 30% = buried.
2. **Rewatch rate** — Videos that get rewatched 1.5x+ average are amplified heavily
3. **Shares** — Highest signal. More valuable than likes by ~5x
4. **Comments** — Especially comments with questions (signals genuine engagement)
5. **Profile visits after video** — Indicates long-term interest
6. **Saves/Favorites** — Strong signal for educational content
7. **Likes** — Lowest quality signal but still matters

### What Works on TikTok (March 2026)

1. **Hook in first 0-2 seconds** — Visual hook (something happening), not "hey guys today I'm going to..."
2. **Text overlay throughout** — 40% of users watch without sound
3. **Series format** — "Part 1 of 5" explicitly told in video gets saves and follows from people who want Part 2
4. **Duet and stitch bait** — End with a question or controversy that invites other creators to respond
5. **Trending sounds** — Use within 48 hours of trending, not after it peaks
6. **"For education purposes" niche** — Finance, tech, how-to niches get boosted by TikTok's "TikTok for Business" push
7. **Posting cadence** — 3-5 videos per day is the documented sweet spot for growth phase; 1/day for maintenance

### TikTok Shadow Ban Triggers

- Words on TikTok's restricted list (changes monthly — check via TikTok Studio Insights)
- Links to non-approved platforms in comments
- Multiple similar videos posted in quick succession
- Purchased followers or engagement (detected quickly)
- Community guideline strikes (3 strikes = permanent restriction)

### TikTok Shop Integration (2026)

- Products with a video showing them get 3-5x more organic reach
- Commission rate for creators: 5-20% depending on category and negotiation
- Live shopping shows have separate algorithm; being live for 60+ min triggers discoverability boost

## Instagram Algorithm (2026)

### Feed, Explore, and Reels are Different Algorithms

**Home Feed:**
- Ranked by: past engagement with account, relationship strength, freshness
- Post within 2 hours of when your audience is most active
- Carousels get reshown to users who didn't engage on first serve (second chance mechanic)

**Explore:**
- Based on interest graph, not follower graph
- High saves and shares → Explore eligibility
- Explore has its own CPE (cost per engagement) signals separate from feed

**Reels:**
- Most similar to TikTok — completion rate dominates
- Watch to end + watch again = strong signal
- Reels shown to non-followers by default (unlike feed posts)
- Reels with original audio do NOT get penalized for music copyright (unlike TikTok)

### What Drives Growth on Instagram (March 2026)

1. **Reels are the growth engine** — Feed posts maintain audience, Reels grow it. Ratio: 3 Reels per 1 carousel per 1 static post.

2. **Saves are the highest signal for carousels** — Design carousels as "save this for later" reference material. Best carousel topic: step-by-step guides, comparisons, lists.

3. **Stories drive retention** — Post 5-7 Stories per day. Accounts with consistent Stories have 30% lower unfollow rate.

4. **Collab posts** — Tag another creator as collaborator. Their audience + your audience both see the post. Only way to genuinely double reach without paid amplification.

5. **Comment pods (careful)** — Groups of 5-10 creators who comment on each other's posts within 30 minutes of posting. Organic-feeling, algorithm-friendly, but requires reciprocity. Don't use automated pods.

6. **Hashtags are declining** — Instagram has been transparent that hashtags are no longer the primary discovery mechanism. Use 5-8 relevant hashtags max, not 30.

### Instagram Algorithm Anti-Patterns

- Buying followers (detected by follower/engagement ratio, limits organic reach permanently)
- Inconsistent posting (algorithm penalizes gaps > 3 days)
- Follow/unfollow cycles (flag, may lose Creator account status)
- Reposting exact same content from TikTok with watermark (explicitly suppressed)
- Using all 30 hashtags (associated with spam accounts)

## Reddit Algorithm (2026)

### How Reddit Ranks Posts

Reddit uses Hot, New, Top, and Rising feeds — each sorted differently:

**Hot score formula (approximation):**
```
score = (upvotes - downvotes) * time_decay_factor
time_decay = 1 / (hours_since_post + 2)^1.8
```

Upvotes in first 2 hours matter most. A post with 100 upvotes in hour 1 beats a post with 500 upvotes after 24 hours.

**Rising feed:** Recent posts with unusual upvote velocity. Best opportunity for content discovery.

### Reddit Growth Tactics (March 2026)

1. **Post when moderators are asleep** — US subs: post 6-8 AM EST (East Coast mods awake but audience hasn't arrived; upvote velocity starts). International subs: adjust accordingly.

2. **Title is 80% of the post** — Redditors upvote titles. Front-load the value in the title. Question format outperforms statement format in most subs.

3. **Comment your own post** — Post the body as the first comment (if post is long). Top-level comment from OP appears first and gets votes separately from the post itself.

4. **Cross-post strategically** — Post to primary sub first. Wait 2-4 hours. Then cross-post to related subs. Cross-posts get a link back, driving votes to original.

5. **AMA format** — "Ask me anything about [topic I know well]" posts get massive engagement if topic is relevant to sub. Set up as a 2-hour event.

6. **Flair exploitation** — Some subreddits have flairs that get pinned or featured. r/Entrepreneur has "Seeking Advice" which gets pinned to top.

### Reddit Traffic Method to Other Platforms

- Add URL in first comment (not in post body — mods remove it)
- Use link shortener that shows destination domain (bit.ly, not obscured links)
- Wait until post has 50+ upvotes before adding link (reduces mod removal risk)
- Add value in post first — link is supplementary, not the point

## YouTube Algorithm (2026)

### What YouTube Optimizes For

YouTube's stated goal: maximize watch time. Everything is in service of this.

**Homepage feed ranking:**
1. Personalized to viewer (what they watch, click, finish)
2. Fresh (uploaded within last 2 weeks)
3. High CTR + high average view duration

**The CTR/AVD balance:**
- High CTR, low AVD = clickbait. YouTube will stop serving after 100-200 impressions.
- Low CTR, high AVD = good video, bad thumbnail. YouTube will serve more once it detects AVD.
- High CTR, high AVD = YouTube's ideal. Serves indefinitely.

**Target metrics for growth:**
- CTR: 4-8% (anything above 10% unsustainable)
- Average View Duration: >50% of video length
- Likes/Comments per 1K views: >30 (strong signal)

### YouTube Shorts vs Long-Form (March 2026)

| Metric | Shorts | Long-form |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Subscriber conversion | Low | High |
| Ad revenue per view | Very Low ($0.01-0.05 RPM) | High ($3-20 RPM) |
| Growth speed | Fast (millions of views possible) | Slow (requires SEO + loyalty) |
| Watch time credit | Minimal | Full weight |
| Algorithm connection | Separate feed | Main feed |

**Strategy:** Use Shorts to grow subscribers fast. Long-form to monetize them.

### YouTube SEO (2026)

1. **Title keyword placement** — Target keyword in first 60 characters of title
2. **Description front-loading** — First 150 characters (shown in search) must contain keyword + value prop
3. **Tags** — Still matter for Suggested Videos algorithm (appear in "Up Next"). Use 15-20 specific tags.
4. **Chapters/timestamps** — Improves AVD by letting viewers jump to relevant sections. YouTube indexes these for search.
5. **Pinned comment** — Add pinned comment with timestamp guide and related video link within 1 hour of publish
6. **End screens + cards** — 20+ seconds of end screen with next video card. Drives session time, which YouTube rewards.

## Pinterest Algorithm (2026)

Pinterest is a search engine, not a social network. Works like SEO, not viral spread.

**Key factors:**
1. **Pin quality score** — Based on historical performance of your account
2. **Keyword relevance** — Title, description, board name all indexed
3. **Fresh content** — Pinterest explicitly favors new pins over repins
4. **Video pins** — 3-5x more reach than static pins since 2025
5. **Idea pins** — Stories-format. Highest organic reach on Pinterest right now.

**Pinterest SEO strategy:**
- Board name = primary keyword ("Make Money Online Ideas" not "My Ideas")
- Pin title = searchable phrase ("How to Make $500 This Week From Home")
- Description = 150-300 words, naturally includes 3-5 variations of keyword
- Post 10-25 pins per day (mix of your own + repins from relevant accounts)
- Best times: Saturday morning, evenings weekdays

## LinkedIn Algorithm (2026)

### LinkedIn Feed Priority

1. **Connection posts** (from 1st-degree connections)
2. **Follows** (pages and people you follow)
3. **Content you engage with** (interest-based)

### What LinkedIn Rewards

- **Dwell time** — If users stop scrolling and read, LinkedIn serves to more
- **Early comments** — Comments in first 30 minutes are weighted heavily
- **Native content** — External links suppress reach by ~40%. Put link in first comment.
- **Polls** — Get 5-10x more reach than text posts. Use for research + engagement.
- **Document posts (carousels)** — PDFs uploaded as carousel posts. High saves = high reach.
- **Text-only posts** — High dwell time if content is good. No link = full reach.

### LinkedIn Anti-Patterns

- External links in post body (use first comment instead)
- Hashtag spam (more than 5 hashtags = reduced reach)
- Engagement pods (LinkedIn detects coordinated engagement, suppresses it)
- Cross-posting identical content from other platforms (performs poorly)
- Posting more than 1x per day (LinkedIn limits distribution to avoid fatigue)
