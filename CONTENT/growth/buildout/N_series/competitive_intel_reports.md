## Monitoring Systems

### Level 1: Free (Zero Cost)

**Visualping.io (free tier: 5 pages)**
Set up monitoring on competitor pricing pages:
- [competitor].com/pricing
- Alerts when any text changes on the page
- Ideal for catching: price changes, new tier additions, feature changes

Priority pages to monitor:
1. Instantly.ai/pricing
2. Apollo.io/pricing
3. Clay.run/pricing
4. lemlist.com/pricing
5. Your own pricing page (for sanity check)

**Google Alerts**
Set up for:
- "cold email 2026" (trending topics)
- "[competitor name]" (mentions)
- "[your product name]" (monitoring your own brand)
- "MCP server marketplace" (your market category)

**Twitter List (X)**
Create a private X list called "Competitors" — add all competitor founders and their company accounts. Check daily in 5 minutes.

## Intelligence Extraction Methods

### From Their Content

What a competitor publishes tells you:
- Who their target customer is (who they're writing for)
- What problems they think are worth solving (what topics they cover)
- Where they're investing (if they publish 10 SEO articles on "cold email templates" — that's a bet)
- What's working for them (check their top-performing articles via Ahrefs)

**Action:** If a competitor consistently ranks #1 for a keyword in your space, either: (a) write a better article and steal the traffic, or (b) pivot to a different keyword cluster they're not dominating.

---

### From Their Job Postings

What they're hiring for = where they're investing:
- Hiring 5 "content writers"? They're doubling down on content.
- Hiring "head of partnerships"? They're moving to B2B channel sales.
- Hiring "ML engineer"? They're building AI features.

**Where to check:** LinkedIn jobs, AngelList, their own careers page, Greenhouse/Lever job boards.

**Monitor via:** Google Alert for "[competitor] careers" or LinkedIn job alerts for company name.

---

### From Their Customer Complaints

What are their users complaining about = your opportunity to be better.

**Where to find:**
- Reddit: search "[competitor name] sucks" or "[competitor name] problem"
- G2 / Capterra reviews: filter by 1-3 stars, read the "cons" sections
- Twitter: search "[competitor name] -from:[competitor account]" to see what others say
- Their own community (if they have Discord/Slack — join as a user)

**What to look for:**
- "I wish they had..." → feature idea
- "Their support is terrible" → service differentiator
- "Too expensive for what you get" → pricing angle
- "Doesn't work for [specific use case]" → your niche opportunity

---

### Reverse Engineer Their Ads

**Facebook Ad Library (free):** facebook.com/ads/library → search any company name → see all their active ads

What to extract:
- What problem do they lead with in their ad copy?
- What image/video style are they using?
- How long have they been running this ad? (longer = it's working)
- What's their CTA?

**Google Ads (via SpyFu or SEMrush):**
- Which keywords are they bidding on?
- What's their ad copy?
- What landing pages do they send traffic to?

## Quarterly Competitor Analysis

Every 90 days, deeper review:

1. **Traffic comparison:** Pull their estimated traffic (SimilarWeb/Ahrefs). How does their growth rate compare to yours?

2. **Content analysis:** What are their top 10 articles by traffic? How could you create better versions?

3. **Pricing analysis:** Have their prices moved? Did they add/remove tiers?

4. **Product analysis:** What features did they ship? What's on their public roadmap?

5. **Social analysis:** Follower growth rate, engagement rate, content format bets

6. **Team size:** Check LinkedIn for company size changes (growing fast = raise incoming or big bet; shrinking = trouble)

7. **SEO keyword gaps:** Use Ahrefs "Content Gap" to find keywords competitors rank for that you don't

**Deliverable:** 1-page summary in `LEDGER/COMPETITIVE_INTEL_QUARTERLY_[YYYY-Q#].md`
