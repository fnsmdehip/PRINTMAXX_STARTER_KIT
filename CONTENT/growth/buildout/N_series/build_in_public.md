# Build in Public Strategy — N28

**Concept:** Document your building process publicly. Share progress, failures, revenue numbers, and lessons as you go. This builds an audience before you have a product to sell, creates trust that converts to customers, and generates content from work you're doing anyway.
**Model:** Not motivational content. Raw, specific, numbers-first documentation.
**Platforms:** X/Twitter primary, LinkedIn secondary, newsletter tertiary

---

## Why Build in Public Works

1. **Social proof before launch:** When you tweet "day 23 of building X — 47 beta users" and then launch, those followers already trust you. Conversion rate is 3-5x vs cold launch.

2. **Accountability:** Public commitments are harder to abandon. Your audience becomes your accountability partner.

3. **Inbound interest:** People DM you asking to buy before you've announced anything. Best signal you have a product worth building.

4. **SEO:** Your build-in-public threads rank for "[product name] review" before you even ask for reviews.

5. **Content from free:** You're building anyway. The only cost is documenting. 30 minutes of writing = 30 days of content.

---

## What to Share (and What Not to)

### Share This

| What | Example | Why |
|------|---------|-----|
| Revenue milestones | "first $100 from [product]" | Social proof |
| User count | "hit 100 beta users today" | Traction signal |
| Technical decisions | "switched from X to Y because Z" | Dev audience appeal |
| Failures | "tried X, got 0 conversions, pivoting to Y" | Trust + engagement |
| Behind-the-scenes | "what my actual daily schedule looks like" | Parasocial connection |
| Revenue math | "at $29/mo × 12 users = $348 MRR" | Aspirational + specific |
| Decisions | "choosing between [option A] and [option B]" | Engagement bait + transparency |
| Lessons | "3 things I learned building [feature]" | Value + shareable |

### Don't Share This

- Exact customer names or emails (privacy)
- Contract/service details (confidentiality)
- Code that could be exploited (security)
- Negative things about named individuals
- Revenue from sources you can't substantiate (sets expectations you can't meet)
- Anything you're not comfortable with competitors seeing

## What Drives Engagement in Build in Public

**Highest engagement formats:**

1. **The honest number reveal** — Real revenue figures, even small ones, outperform almost any other content. People are curious about actual numbers.

2. **The specific mistake post** — "I spent $200 on Facebook ads and got 0 conversions. Here's what I did wrong." This gets shares from people who learned from your mistake.

3. **Before/after** — "before: 0 subscribers. after 90 days: 847." Transformation with specifics.

4. **Decision transparency** — "choosing between two approaches: [A] or [B]. here's my thinking." Invites comments, builds parasocial investment.

5. **Daily update threads** — Some builders post every single day. The compounding effect of consistent posting builds a loyal audience faster than sporadic high-quality posts.

## Building an Audience Before Launch

The best product launches start with an audience that's been watching you build.

**3-month pre-launch plan:**

**Month 1: Ideation phase posts**
- "thinking about building [X] because [specific problem]"
- "researched the market. found [specific numbers]"
- "decided to build [X]. here's my reasoning."
- "week 1 progress: [what exists]"

**Month 2: Building phase posts**
- Weekly progress updates
- Technical decision posts (2-3)
- Beta user recruitment post (ask for early access signups)
- "100 people signed up for early access" update

**Month 3: Pre-launch phase posts**
- "launching in [X days]"
- "sneak peek" screenshots or demos
- "beta users are using it. here's what they're saying"
- Launch day post

**Result:** By launch day, you have an audience that's been watching for 90 days. They know the product, they trust you, and they're rooting for you. This audience converts at 3-7x the rate of cold traffic.

## Platforms and Prioritization

**X/Twitter (primary):**
- Most concentrated indie hacker / solopreneur audience
- Thread format works for longer updates
- Quote tweeting your own milestone posts keeps them visible
- Use #buildinpublic hashtag for discoverability

**LinkedIn (secondary):**
- Professional tone version of same content
- "I'm a solopreneur building [X]" format works well here
- Slightly longer intros (professional audience expects more context)
- Document posts (PDF carousels) of your monthly reviews perform well

**Newsletter (tertiary, highest value per subscriber):**
- Monthly "build in public" letter covering the full month
- Goes deeper than tweets — what you're thinking, struggling with, excited about
- This is where your most engaged followers live
- They become early customers and advocates

**Indie Hackers (occasional):**
- Monthly "making of" post
- High-quality audience, low noise
- Best format: honest case study with real numbers
- Top posts get 10,000+ reads and stick in Google search

## The Long Game

At 6-12 months of consistent build in public:

- **Account growth:** 2,000-10,000 followers who are your exact ICP
- **Newsletter:** 500-2,000 subscribers who've followed your journey
- **Product launch conversions:** 3-8% of audience converts (at 5,000 followers = 150-400 customers on launch)
- **Inbound leads:** People DM asking about services you haven't even announced
- **Press:** Your honest revenue posts get picked up by newsletters, podcasts, blogs

The math at 12 months, conservative:
- 5,000 X followers → 200-400 newsletter subscribers from build-in-public content
- 3 products launched → average 50-100 customers each × $20-50 avg → $3,000-15,000 in product revenue directly attributable to the audience

Build in public is a compounding asset. The first 90 days feel like shouting into the void. Day 180 it starts clicking. Day 365 it's your distribution engine.
