A — "Private Bank"

Dark, serif typography, gold accents. Feels like a high-end financial institution. Signals trust, exclusivity, and competence. No gradients, no glow effects, no AI cliches. The anti-startup look.

Think: Goldman Sachs meets automation. Serif fonts (Georgia/Playfair) signal permanence. Gold (#c9a227) on black is timeless — never goes out of style and never looks AI-generated.

B — "Stripe/Linear"

Light, clean, modern SaaS aesthetic. Warm white background, black text, no color accents. Minimal and functional. Feels like a product, not a service. Professional without trying hard.

Think: Stripe's website meets consulting. System fonts signal "we're engineers, not marketers." Warm white (#faf8f4) avoids the sterile blue-white of AI sites. Hard to mistake for AI-generated because it's deliberately plain.

C — "Dev Tool"

Dark with monospace accents and blue highlights. Feels technical and competent. Like a developer tool's marketing site. Signals "we actually build things" not "we make slide decks."

Think: Vercel/Railway meets consulting. Monospace code snippets mixed with clean sans-serif. Blue (#3b82f6) is engineering-coded. Appeals to technical buyers and CTOs. Risk: may feel too technical for dental offices.

Why these three?

Every AI/automation company in 2026 uses the same look: dark background, neon gradients, floating particles, and sans-serif fonts. Your current site is close to this (dark green + gold glow). These three directions each break that pattern differently. Option A goes old-money. Option B goes anti-design minimalist. Option C goes technical-credible. None of them look like "I told ChatGPT to make a website."
