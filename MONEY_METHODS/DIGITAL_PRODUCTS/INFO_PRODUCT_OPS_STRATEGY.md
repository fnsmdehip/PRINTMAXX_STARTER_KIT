# Info Product Ops Strategy

**Created:** 2026-02-12
**Purpose:** Full operational playbook for building, launching, and scaling info products from $0 to $50K+/mo
**Status:** ACTIVE

---

## the thesis

info products are the highest-margin business on earth. zero inventory. zero shipping. zero COGS after creation. a PDF that took 2 hours to write sells for $29 forever. a Notion template that took 45 minutes generates $500/mo on autopilot.

the game is volume x quality x distribution. build 20+ products across 4 price tiers. stack them into bundles. wire upsell paths between them. distribute through 5+ channels. let marketplace organic traffic do 60% of the work.

most people build one course, spend $5K on production, price it at $497, sell 3 copies, and quit. that's the wrong model.

the right model: build fast, price low-to-mid, ship in volume, let winners emerge, double down on what sells, kill what doesn't. portfolio approach. not single-bet gambling.

## SECTION 1: Product Type Taxonomy

### Tier 1: Micro Products ($1-9)

**what they are:** single-use assets. one template. one checklist. one swipe file. solves one specific problem in under 5 minutes of reading.

**creation time:** 15-45 minutes
**format:** PDF (1-5 pages) or Notion doc
**quality bar:** clean formatting, specific content, zero fluff. every sentence delivers value. if you can cut a sentence without losing information, cut it.
**purpose in portfolio:** lead magnets, email list builders, impulse buys, gateway to higher-tier products
**margin after platform fees:** 85-90%

**examples:**
- $3: 10 cold email subject lines that got 40%+ open rates
- $5: the exact Canva template I use for Gumroad covers
- $7: funnel teardown of a $70K/mo creator (already built, see PRODUCTS/GUMROAD_READY_LISTINGS.md)
- $0 (lead magnet): 5 AI prompts for content creation (already built)

**when to build these:** always. every session that produces research should also produce a micro product. a tweet thread that performs well becomes a $5 PDF. a cold email that gets replies becomes a $3 template.

**revenue ceiling per product:** $200-800/mo (volume play, 50-200 buyers/mo at $3-7)
**total tier capacity:** 15-20 micro products = $3,000-12,000/mo

---

### Tier 2: Standard Products ($19-49)

**what they are:** complete systems for one problem domain. 2,000-8,000 words. includes templates, frameworks, copy-paste assets. buyer implements in 1-3 days.

**creation time:** 1-4 hours
**format:** PDF (15-40 pages) + Notion template + bonus assets (scripts, spreadsheets, prompt libraries)
**quality bar:** specific numbers throughout. real examples, not hypotheticals. actionable steps, not theory. buyer should be able to start implementing within 30 minutes of purchase.
**purpose in portfolio:** primary revenue drivers. high enough price to matter, low enough for impulse buys. this is where 60% of revenue comes from.
**margin after platform fees:** 85-90%

**examples:**
- $27: cold email playbook with 10 industry sequences (already built)
- $27: Twitter/X growth playbook with 30 templates (already built)
- $29: 73 cold email subject lines organized by industry (spec'd in MICRO_PRODUCT_SPECS.md)
- $34: local biz cold email script pack for 10 industries (spec'd)
- $34: PWA money machine with starter template code (spec'd)
- $47: AI automation toolkit with 20 workflows (already built)
- $47: vibe coding playbook (already built)
- $47: AI content farm blueprint (already built)

**when to build these:** every time you have a working system that produces results. cold email pipeline that books calls? that's a $27 product. app that ships in 2 hours? that's a $47 playbook.

**revenue ceiling per product:** $1,500-8,000/mo (30-200 buyers/mo at $27-47)
**total tier capacity:** 8-12 standard products = $12,000-50,000/mo

---

### Tier 3: Premium Products ($79-199)

**what they are:** comprehensive systems with implementation support. 10,000-25,000 words. multiple deliverables. video walkthroughs optional but high-converting. buyer gets a complete business-in-a-box or advanced skill system.

**creation time:** 8-20 hours
**format:** PDF guide (50-100 pages) + video walkthroughs (optional, 2-5 hours) + template library + community access (Discord/Skool) + email support for 30 days
**quality bar:** must look like a $500 product sold at a discount. professional cover design. table of contents. chapter structure. case studies with real numbers. implementation checklists per chapter. this is the product people screenshot and share on Twitter.
**purpose in portfolio:** high-margin anchor products. create perceived value for lower tiers ("if his $97 product is this good, the $27 one is a steal"). also the bridge to Tier 4 services.
**margin after platform fees:** 85-90%

**examples:**
- $97: the local business client acquisition system (already built, see GUMROAD_READY_LISTINGS.md listing 1)
- $97: the solopreneur automation stack (20 workflows + video setup guides + community)
- $149: the content empire blueprint (5-account content farm + monetization + 90-day plan)
- $149: the cold outbound machine (email + LinkedIn + AI calling + CRM + reporting)
- $199: the app factory playbook (build 5 apps in 30 days + ASO + monetization + App Store submission)

**when to build these:** after a Tier 2 product sells 50+ copies and you have proof the system works. upgrade with more depth, video, community, and support. or when you have a system generating $5K+/mo and can document the full thing.

**revenue ceiling per product:** $3,000-15,000/mo (20-100 buyers/mo at $97-199)
**total tier capacity:** 3-5 premium products = $9,000-50,000/mo

---

### Tier 4: High-Ticket ($500+)

**what they are:** done-with-you implementation. live calls. personalized feedback. cohort-based or 1:1. the buyer pays for your time and expertise, not just information.

**creation time:** 2-5 hours of curriculum design (content already exists in lower tiers). ongoing time per client: 2-5 hours/month.
**format:** live Zoom calls (weekly or biweekly) + private Slack/Discord channel + all lower-tier products included + personalized action plans + accountability check-ins
**quality bar:** must deliver measurable results. "John closed $8K in local biz clients in 30 days" type testimonials. if clients don't get results, the product dies. this tier lives and dies on proof.
**purpose in portfolio:** highest margin per customer. 5-10 clients at $500-3,000/mo = $2,500-30,000/mo from a handful of people. also generates testimonials and case studies that sell everything else.
**margin:** 90%+ (your time is the only cost)

**examples:**
- $500: 4-week cold outbound implementation sprint (1:1 calls, inbox setup, first campaign live)
- $750: local biz client acquisition setup (pipeline configured, first 50 leads generated, first cold emails sent)
- $1,500: 90-day solopreneur launch program (cohort of 5-10, weekly calls, full system implementation)
- $3,000: done-for-you content system setup (5 accounts configured, 30 days of content scheduled, monetization live)

**when to build these:** after you have 3+ testimonials from lower-tier products. someone buys the $97 local biz system, closes their first client, DMs you "this works" -- now offer them the $750 implementation.

**revenue ceiling per offer:** $5,000-30,000/mo (5-10 clients at $500-3,000)
**total tier capacity:** 2-3 high-ticket offers = $10,000-60,000/mo

## SECTION 2: Creation Workflow by Tier

### Tier 1 Micro Product Workflow (15-45 min)

```
1. identify the asset (5 min)
   - what did you build/discover/test this session?
   - can it be extracted into a standalone file?
   - is it specific enough to solve one problem?

2. extract and format (10-20 min)
   - pull the relevant content from source files
   - add brief intro (2-3 sentences: what, who, why)
   - format with clean headers and bullet points
   - add 1-2 real examples or screenshots
   - no padding, no filler, no "in this guide you'll learn"

3. export (5 min)
   - save as .md in DIGITAL_PRODUCTS/micro_products/
   - export to PDF via Notion or pandoc
   - save PDF to DIGITAL_PRODUCTS/pdfs/

4. list (10 min)
   - write 3-sentence Gumroad description
   - set price ($3-7)
   - enable PWYW with minimum
   - add to appropriate category and tags

5. wire upsell
   - connect to relevant Tier 2 product as "want the full system?"
```

**tools needed:** text editor, Notion (free) or pandoc, Gumroad account
**quality check:** read the whole thing in under 3 minutes. if any sentence doesn't add value, delete it. if a stranger in your niche read this, would they implement something today?

---

### Tier 2 Standard Product Workflow (1-4 hrs)

```
1. identify the system (10 min)
   - what complete workflow do you have that produces results?
   - what are the specific steps someone else would need?
   - what's the one-sentence promise? ("book 10 calls/week from $37/mo infrastructure")

2. outline (15 min)
   - 5-8 sections maximum
   - each section = one major step in the system
   - identify templates/scripts/assets to include
   - identify real numbers you can cite

3. write (1-3 hrs)
   - section 1: the problem + why existing solutions fail (200-400 words)
   - sections 2-6: the system, step by step (300-600 words each)
   - each section ends with an action item
   - include copy-paste templates inline
   - include real numbers: costs, conversion rates, timeframes
   - final section: implementation timeline (week 1, 2, 3, 4)

4. add bonus assets (30 min)
   - Notion template with pre-built structure
   - spreadsheet tracker (Google Sheets template)
   - prompt library (if AI-assisted workflow)
   - script files (if code involved)

5. package and list (30 min)
   - compile to PDF with cover
   - write Gumroad listing (use existing listing templates as model)
   - price at $19-47 based on depth and asset value
   - configure upsells (Tier 3 products, related Tier 2)

6. create launch content
   - 3 tweets: hook with specific result, tease method, link
   - 1 Reddit post: value-first in relevant sub
   - 1 newsletter section
```

**tools needed:** text editor, Notion (free), Canva (free tier for covers), Gumroad, pandoc or browser PDF export
**quality check:** show it to one person in the target niche before listing. ask "would you pay $X for this?" if they hesitate, add more specific content.

---

### Tier 3 Premium Product Workflow (8-20 hrs)

```
1. validate demand (1 hr)
   - check: did the related Tier 2 product sell 50+ copies?
   - check: do you have 3+ unsolicited testimonials?
   - check: have 5+ people asked "do you offer something more advanced?"
   - if no to all three, don't build this yet. go back to Tier 2.

2. curriculum design (2 hrs)
   - 8-12 modules/chapters
   - each module: lesson + implementation exercise + template/asset
   - identify where video adds value (setup walkthroughs, live demos)
   - plan community element (Discord channel, Skool group, or email support)

3. write core content (6-12 hrs)
   - expand Tier 2 content with deeper examples
   - add case studies (anonymized client results, your own results)
   - add advanced tactics not in the standard version
   - add troubleshooting guide for common failures
   - add "what to do when X goes wrong" sections

4. create video content (optional, 3-5 hrs)
   - screen recordings of actual implementation
   - use Loom (free for <25 videos) or OBS (free)
   - 5-15 minute videos, one per module
   - host on Vimeo or unlisted YouTube
   - embed links in PDF

5. build community (1 hr)
   - create Discord server or Skool community
   - set up channels: introductions, wins, questions, resources
   - pin welcome guide with community rules
   - plan first 30 days of engagement prompts

6. package and launch (2 hrs)
   - professional PDF with cover, TOC, chapter headers
   - separate assets folder (templates, scripts, spreadsheets)
   - access instructions for video + community
   - Gumroad listing with extensive description
   - price at $97-199
   - launch sequence: 7-day pre-launch, launch day, 48-hour window

7. post-launch
   - collect testimonials from first 10 buyers
   - iterate based on questions in community
   - update content quarterly
```

**tools needed:** everything from Tier 2 + Loom/OBS (video), Discord/Skool (community), Vimeo (hosting)
**quality check:** would you buy this at full price from a stranger? print the table of contents. does each chapter promise a specific outcome? if a chapter says "mindset" or "getting started" with no specific deliverable, rewrite it.

---

### Tier 4 High-Ticket Workflow (2-5 hrs setup)

```
1. define the offer (30 min)
   - what specific result do you deliver? ("5 local biz clients in 30 days")
   - what's your time commitment per client? (2-5 hrs/mo)
   - what's included? (calls, support, all products, done-for-you elements)
   - what's the price? ($500-3,000)

2. create application process (30 min)
   - Typeform or Google Form: 5-7 qualifying questions
   - screen for: budget, commitment level, niche fit
   - auto-reject obvious bad fits (no budget, unrealistic expectations)

3. create onboarding (1 hr)
   - welcome email with expectations and schedule
   - intake questionnaire (their business, goals, current state)
   - access to all included products
   - first call agenda template

4. build delivery system (1-2 hrs)
   - Calendly for call scheduling (free tier)
   - private Slack or Discord channel per client
   - shared Notion workspace for tracking
   - weekly check-in template
   - progress dashboard

5. sell
   - add CTA to all Tier 2 and Tier 3 products: "want me to implement this for you?"
   - DM funnel: reply to positive feedback with offer
   - limited availability (3-5 clients at a time, creates urgency, also real)
```

**tools needed:** Calendly (free), Slack/Discord, Notion, Zoom (free for 40 min), Typeform/Google Forms
**quality check:** can you guarantee a specific result? if not, don't sell it at this tier. refine your system until you can.

## SECTION 4: Launch Sequence Playbook

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (7 days before)

**day -7:** announce you're building something. don't reveal the product. tease the problem.
```
"spent 6 hours reverse-engineering how a 22 year old makes $70K/mo.
writing up the full teardown now. dropping it this week."
```

**day -5:** share one specific insight from the product. give real value. make them want more.
```
"58% of all cold email replies come from email 1.
if your first email doesn't convert, follow-ups won't save you.
full system dropping Thursday."
```

**day -3:** show a preview. screenshot of the PDF. Notion page in progress. make it tangible.

**day -1:** final tease. specific promise. "tomorrow" creates urgency.
```
"dropping the full cold email playbook tomorrow.
10 industry sequences. 14,000+ sends tested. 42% open rate.
$27. cheaper than one cold email tool."
```

### Phase 2: Launch Day

**hour 0:** publish the product on Gumroad. verify purchase flow works. test download.

**hour 1:** post the launch tweet/post. lead with the result, not the product.
```
"cold emailed 200 dentists last month. 14% reply rate.
closed $7.5K in web design projects.
i packaged the exact system: sequences, scripts, DNS setup, everything.
$27. link below."
```

**hour 2:** cross-post to relevant communities.
- Reddit: value-first post in r/Entrepreneur, r/SideProject, r/coldemail (give 80% of value, link product for the full system)
- LinkedIn: professional angle, link in comments
- IndieHackers: launch post with revenue story
- Hacker News: only if product is technical enough (PWA, automation toolkit)

**hour 4-8:** engage with every comment and reply. answer questions publicly. each reply is free content that sells the product.

**hour 12:** post a follow-up with early buyer feedback or a specific result.

### Phase 3: Post-Launch (days 2-7)

**day 2:** value post related to the product topic. don't pitch. just be useful. mention the product in passing.

**day 3:** share a customer win or testimonial (even if it's from a free beta tester). specific numbers.

**day 4:** create a comparison or "before/after" post. show what changes when someone implements the system.

**day 5:** bundle announcement. "grabbed product X? add product Y for $20 more."

**day 6:** objection-handling post. address the #1 reason people didn't buy.
```
"'i can figure out cold email on my own'
sure. that's what i thought too.
spent 4 months and $600 in tools before landing my first client.
or you could spend $27 and start sending tomorrow."
```

**day 7:** last call post. not a hard close. just a reminder with a specific benefit.

### Phase 4: Evergreen (week 2+)

**weekly cadence:**
- 1 value post per week that naturally leads to the product
- 1 customer result or testimonial per week (collect via DMs and reviews)
- 1 "behind the scenes" post about product updates or additions

**monthly actions:**
- review Gumroad analytics: which products sell, which don't
- update product content with new insights, tools, or data
- create new micro products from frequently asked questions
- test price changes on underperformers ($27 not selling? try $19)

**quarterly actions:**
- bundle top sellers into a new package
- upgrade best Tier 2 to Tier 3 (add video, community, more depth)
- kill products that sold <10 copies in 90 days
- launch 3-5 new products based on what sold

**evergreen distribution:**
- Gumroad Discover marketplace (organic search traffic from 15M+ buyers)
- Pinterest pins linking to products (long shelf life, SEO-friendly)
- Auto-responder email sequence for new subscribers (welcome email -> free product -> paid product pitch on day 3)
- blog posts/Medium articles with product CTAs at the end
- YouTube video descriptions with product links

## SECTION 5: Distribution Channels Ranked

### 1. Gumroad (PRIMARY)

**why first:** 15M+ buyers already on the platform. they search for "cold email templates" and your product appears. zero audience required.

**fees:** 10% flat (no monthly fee on free plan)
**conversion rate benchmark:** 2-5% of page views
**best for:** PDFs, templates, prompt packs, playbooks, bundles
**features that matter:** Gumroad Discover (marketplace), upsells, discount codes, affiliate program, email integration

**optimization:**
- enable Discover on every product
- set PWYW with minimum price (increases average order value 15-25%)
- configure upsell chains between products
- use "suggested price" anchoring (show ~~$197~~ now $47)

### 2. Whop (SECONDARY MIRROR)

**why:** 3% fee vs Gumroad's 10%. on $1,000 revenue, that's $70 more in your pocket.

**fees:** 3% transaction fee
**best for:** same products as Gumroad. duplicate every listing.
**limitation:** smaller marketplace, less organic discovery
**strategy:** list everything on both. link to Whop from direct traffic (lower fee). link to Gumroad from organic (bigger marketplace).

### 3. Lemon Squeezy (SECONDARY MIRROR)

**why:** 5% + $0.50 per transaction. handles EU VAT automatically (Gumroad doesn't).

**fees:** 5% + $0.50
**best for:** international sales, especially EU. also good for software/SaaS products.
**strategy:** list everything that sells on Gumroad. use for EU-targeted traffic.

### 4. Etsy (MARKETPLACE ORGANIC)

**why:** 90M+ active buyers. they search for templates, printables, planners, spreadsheets.

**fees:** $0.20 listing + 6.5% transaction + 3% payment processing
**best for:** Notion templates, spreadsheets, planners, checklists. NOT playbooks or guides (Etsy buyers want templates, not reading material).
**conversion rate benchmark:** 1-3%
**strategy:** list all template-style products. use Etsy SEO (long-tail keywords in title and tags).

### 5. Own Website (LONG-TERM)

**why:** zero platform fees (just Stripe 2.9% + $0.30). full control. build email list.

**fees:** Stripe only (2.9% + $0.30)
**best for:** high-ticket products ($97+), bundles, and capturing email subscribers
**when to build:** after you have 5+ products selling on marketplaces and an email list of 500+
**stack:** Carrd ($19/yr) or Next.js + Stripe Checkout + SendGrid/Resend for email delivery
**strategy:** drive direct traffic here (from social, email, referrals). keep marketplace listings for organic discovery.

### 6. Amazon KDP (SPECIFIC USE CASE)

**why:** largest book marketplace. works for journals, planners, workbooks.

**fees:** 40% royalty on list price (60% on $9.99+)
**best for:** formatted journals, planners, log books. NOT digital downloads.
**strategy:** convert any template product into a physical printable format. $9.99-14.99 price point. passive after listing.

### Conversion hierarchy (most to least):
1. Own website with warm email list: 5-15% conversion
2. Gumroad with Discover traffic: 2-5%
3. Etsy with SEO-optimized listing: 1-3%
4. Whop marketplace: 1-3%
5. Social media direct links: 0.5-2%
6. Cold traffic (ads, Reddit): 0.3-1%

## SECTION 7: Content-to-Product Pipeline

### the conversion map

every piece of work generates products. this is not optional. if you built something useful and didn't extract a sellable asset from it, you left money on the table.

```
RESEARCH SESSION
  -> tweet thread summarizing findings (free, drives traffic)
  -> micro product: key data points as swipe file ($3-7)
  -> standard product: full playbook with implementation steps ($19-47)
  -> premium product: system + video + community ($97-199)

BUILDING SESSION
  -> "how I built X" tweet thread (free, drives traffic)
  -> micro product: the exact prompts/scripts used ($5-7)
  -> standard product: step-by-step recreation guide ($27-47)
  -> premium product: full walkthrough + template code + support ($97-149)

CLIENT WORK
  -> anonymized case study (free content)
  -> micro product: the template/framework used ($5-7)
  -> standard product: the complete system ($27-47)
  -> high-ticket: done-for-you version of same service ($500-3,000)

FAILURE / EXPERIMENT
  -> honest failure post (free, high engagement)
  -> micro product: "what I learned from $X mistake" ($3-5)
  -> standard product: "the complete guide to avoiding X" ($19-27)
```

### pipeline triggers (automate these)

| trigger | product action |
|---------|---------------|
| cold email sequence gets >10% reply rate | extract as micro product ($3-7) |
| tweet gets 500+ likes | expand into standard product ($19-27) |
| reddit post gets 100+ upvotes | compile into playbook ($27-47) |
| client pays $500+ for a service | productize the system ($47-97) |
| same question asked 5+ times in DMs | create product that answers it ($5-27) |
| complete a 10+ hour build session | create "how I built this" guide ($19-47) |
| AI prompt produces great results consistently | add to prompt vault, consider standalone ($3-7) |

## SECTION 11: Competitive Positioning

### how to win in a crowded market

info products are not a new idea. thousands of people sell cold email playbooks, tweet templates, and AI guides. the market is saturated with generic garbage. that's actually good news.

**your edge:**

1. **specificity beats generality.** "cold email playbook" competes with 500 products. "cold email sequences for dental practices that book consultations" competes with 3. niche down on every product.

2. **real numbers beat theory.** "14,000+ sends tested, 42% open rate" beats "proven framework for cold email success." include your actual data. if you don't have data, run the system first, then sell the results.

3. **working assets beat guides.** a PDF that explains cold email theory is worth $7. a PDF with 10 copy-paste email sequences ready to send is worth $47. include things people can use immediately, not things they have to figure out.

4. **ugly authenticity beats polished generic.** a cover designed in Canva free tier with a specific promise ("$2K-5K per client") outsells a $500 custom-designed cover with a generic promise ("the ultimate guide"). spend zero on design. spend everything on content.

5. **portfolio beats single product.** one product is a lottery ticket. 20 products across 4 tiers with upsell paths and bundles is a business. the portfolio approach means you win on volume even if individual products underperform.

## SECTION 14: Risk Mitigation

### platform risk

**problem:** Gumroad changes fees or shuts down your account.
**mitigation:** list on 3+ platforms simultaneously. build email list from day 1. own the customer relationship, not the platform.

### refund risk

**problem:** high refund rates eat into revenue.
**mitigation:**
- set clear expectations in product descriptions (exactly what's included)
- price Tier 1-2 low enough that refund effort exceeds product cost (nobody refunds a $7 PDF)
- for Tier 3+: add "30-day money-back guarantee" to increase purchase confidence (refund rate drops when guarantee is explicit, paradoxically)
- typical digital product refund rate: 2-5%. budget for 5%.

### content theft

**problem:** someone buys your $27 product and shares it for free.
**mitigation:** accept it. seriously. if someone shares your product, that's free marketing. the people who pirate $27 products were never going to buy. the people who buy want the updates, support, and credibility of being a paying customer.

### quality dilution

**problem:** shipping too fast, quality drops, reviews go negative.
**mitigation:** every product passes the quality checklist in Section 10. no exceptions. one bad product damages the brand. 20 good products compound the brand.

## APPENDIX C: Pricing Psychology

### anchoring
always show a "value" price crossed out above the actual price. ~~$197~~ $47. this is standard on Gumroad and increases conversions 15-25%.

### odd pricing
$27 outperforms $25. $47 outperforms $50. $97 outperforms $99. odd numbers feel calculated and intentional. round numbers feel arbitrary.

### PWYW (pay what you want)
Gumroad's PWYW with minimum price increases average order value by 15-25%. set minimum at your target price. set "suggested" at 1.5-2x. some buyers pay more voluntarily.

### bundle math
bundles should save 30-40% vs buying individually. less than 30% savings doesn't feel worth it. more than 50% savings devalues individual products.

### the $7 gateway
$7 is the sweet spot for first-time buyers. low enough to impulse purchase. high enough to qualify as a real product (not a freebie). a $7 buyer who has a good experience becomes a $27, $47, $97 buyer. the $7 product is your cheapest customer acquisition channel.
