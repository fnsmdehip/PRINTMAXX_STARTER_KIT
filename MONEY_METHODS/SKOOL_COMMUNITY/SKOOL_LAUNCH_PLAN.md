## growth tactics

### 1. TWITTER FUNNEL
this is the engine. every piece of content you post on twitter has one job: make people curious about the inner circle.

structure:
- 70% pure value threads (how to build X, how I made $X, breakdown of Y)
- 20% social proof (member wins, screenshots, results)
- 10% direct promotion (open enrollment, challenge launches, live session announcements)

the CTA is never "join my community." the CTA is always: "I teach this stuff step by step inside the inner circle. link in bio." soft. confident. inevitable.

### 2. FREE 5-DAY CHALLENGE
(see FREE_CHALLENGE_5DAY.md for the full breakdown)

run this monthly. it's a public event — anyone can join. they get 5 days of guided building. by day 3, they're hooked. by day 5, they realize they need the community to keep the momentum going. conversion target: 15-20% of challenge participants join the paid community.

### 3. REFERRAL PROGRAM
- refer 1 person: exclusive template pack
- refer 3 people: free month
- refer 5 people: free VIP upgrade for a month
- refer 10 people: VIP for life

make the referral link easy to share. make the rewards visible. celebrate referrers publicly.

### 4. GUEST EXPERT SESSIONS
once a month, bring in someone with credibility. not a big name necessarily — someone who's done the thing. built a product, hit $10K/mo, scaled with automation. they do a 30-min session + Q&A. you promote it as a free event, but only community members get the recording and the follow-up resources.

### 5. CONTENT REPURPOSING MACHINE
every live session, hot seat, and build stream gets:
- clipped into 3-5 short clips for twitter
- transcribed and turned into a written post
- key insights pulled into a template or checklist
- added to the course library

one event = 10+ pieces of content. this is how you stay visible without burning out.

## KPIs to track

### monthly
- total members (free trial + paid)
- MRR (monthly recurring revenue)
- churn rate (target: under 8%)
- free trial → paid conversion rate (target: 25%+)
- $47 → $97 upgrade rate (target: 10%+)

### weekly
- new members joined
- members who posted in the war room thread
- live session attendance rate
- template downloads
- challenge signups (when running)

### daily
- new posts in community
- comments/replies per post
- DMs received asking about the community
- twitter impressions on community-related content

### the numbers that matter most
- **LTV (lifetime value):** if average member stays 6 months at $47, LTV = $282. your CAC needs to be under $50.
- **activation rate:** what % of new members post within their first 48 hours? if it's under 60%, your onboarding is broken.
- **90-day retention:** if someone makes it 90 days, they'll likely stay 12+ months. focus everything on getting people through the first 90 days.

## competitor analysis

### SHIP30 (ship 30 for 30)
- **what they do right:** clear outcome (write online for 30 days), social proof everywhere, cohort model creates urgency
- **what they get wrong:** expensive ($500+), focused only on writing, no ongoing community after the cohort
- **how we win:** we're ongoing, not cohort-based. we're cheaper. we cover building AND selling, not just content creation. and we don't disappear after 30 days.

### HORMOZI'S SKOOL COMMUNITY
- **what they do right:** massive social proof (hormozi's brand), free tier drives insane volume, gamification keeps people engaged
- **what they get wrong:** too big — you're a number, not a name. the advice is generic at scale. the leaderboard rewards activity, not results.
- **how we win:** we're small and intense. every member gets seen. we reward shipping, not commenting. and our advice is specific to solopreneurs building digital products — not generic "business" advice.

### INDIE HACKERS
- **what they do right:** huge community, great case studies, free access
- **what they get wrong:** no structure, no accountability, too much "what should I build?" not enough "here's how to build it." the energy is contemplation, not action.
- **how we win:** we're the action layer on top of the indie hacker mindset. you come to us when you're done reading and ready to build.

### VARIOUS $997+ MASTERMINDS
- **what they do right:** high ticket = high commitment, small groups, personalized advice
- **what they get wrong:** price excludes 95% of the market. often run by people who make money teaching, not doing. the ROI is unclear.
- **how we win:** $47/mo is a no-brainer. you can join for one month, ship something, make money, and decide if you want to stay. zero risk. and we're in the trenches building alongside you, not lecturing from a stage.
