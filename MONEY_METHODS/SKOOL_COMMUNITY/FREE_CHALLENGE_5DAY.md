## DAY 1: PICK YOUR WEAPON
### idea validation in 2 hours

**morning prompt (posted at 8 AM):**

stop scrolling. today you pick what you're building this week. not tomorrow. not "after more research." today.

here's the framework — answer these 3 questions for any idea:

1. **are people searching for it?** check google trends, reddit, twitter search. if nobody's talking about the problem, there's no market.
2. **are people paying for alternatives?** check gumroad, product hunt, chrome web store, app stores. if there are paid alternatives, there's money in the space. if there are NO alternatives, that's a warning sign — not an opportunity.
3. **can you build it in 2 days?** be honest. not 2 days if everything goes perfectly. 2 days including the part where you get stuck for 3 hours on a CSS bug. if the answer is no, scope it down until it's yes.

if you don't have any ideas, here are 10 to steal:
- a single-purpose calculator for a specific niche (freelancer rate calculator, saas churn calculator)
- a curated resource list with daily updates (best AI tools, best free APIs, best no-code templates)
- a simple timer/tracker for a specific workflow (pomodoro for writers, sprint timer for developers)
- a template pack (notion templates, email templates, landing page templates)
- a browser extension that solves one annoyance
- a micro-course (1 hour, 5 lessons, solves one specific problem)
- a simple API wrapper with a pretty front-end
- a mobile-first PWA tool (habit tracker, expense logger, meal planner)
- a "awesome list" website with search and filtering
- a simple SaaS dashboard that displays data from a public API

**action items:**
- [ ] brainstorm 5 ideas (15 minutes max — set a timer)
- [ ] run each through the 3-question framework
- [ ] pick the strongest one
- [ ] write a 1-sentence description: "it's a [type of product] that helps [specific person] do [specific thing]"
- [ ] post your pick in the community thread with the 1-sentence description

**evening share (posted at 6 PM):**

## DAY 2: BUILD THE MVP
### the single-file app or landing page

**morning prompt (posted at 8 AM):**

yesterday you picked your weapon. today you build it.

the rules of the MVP:
- one feature. not two. not "well, it also does..." — ONE.
- one screen. if it needs more than one screen, you're overbuilding.
- no frameworks. no npm install. no dependencies. one HTML file or one landing page builder. that's it.
- it doesn't need to be pretty. it needs to WORK.

if you're building an app:
- open a text editor
- create index.html
- build the core feature in HTML + CSS + vanilla JS
- test it in your browser
- it works? you're done for today.

if you're building a landing page (for a course, template, service):
- use carrd.co ($19/year), super.so, or raw HTML
- headline that states the outcome
- 3 bullet points of what they get
- one CTA button
- a screenshot or mockup of the product
- done.

if you're building a template pack or digital download:
- create the templates
- write a 1-page sales description
- take screenshots
- package it as a zip or notion link
- done.

**action items:**
- [ ] open your editor or page builder at 9 AM sharp
- [ ] set a 4-hour timer. when it goes off, stop building features.
- [ ] the remaining time goes to testing and fixing bugs
- [ ] deploy to github pages, netlify, vercel, or carrd — whatever gets it live fastest
- [ ] post a screenshot of your MVP in the thread

**evening share (posted at 6 PM):**

post your progress. format:

> **screenshot:** [image]
> **what works:** [1-2 sentences]
> **what's broken/ugly:** [be honest]

## DAY 3: ADD MONETIZATION
### stripe, gumroad, or waitlist

**morning prompt (posted at 8 AM):**

your product works (mostly). today it starts making money (potentially).

most people skip this step. they launch free, "just to get feedback." that's fear talking. you're not afraid of feedback — you're afraid of asking for money. get over it today.

pick your monetization path:

**option A: stripe checkout link**
- create a stripe account (takes 10 minutes)
- create a product with a price
- generate a checkout link
- paste it behind your "buy" or "upgrade" button
- test it with a $1 charge to yourself
- done.

**option B: gumroad**
- create a gumroad account
- list your product
- set a price (or "pay what you want" with a suggested price)
- embed the gumroad widget on your page
- done.

**option C: waitlist (if the product isn't ready for paid yet)**
- add an email capture form (tally.so, google forms, or a simple mailto link)
- the pitch: "this launches [date]. join the waitlist for early access + a launch discount."
- this is the minimum. if you're doing option C, you better have A or B ready by launch day.

pricing guidance:
- digital downloads/templates: $9-29
- simple tools/apps: $5/mo or $29 lifetime
- courses/guides: $27-97
- services/done-for-you: $250+ per project
- if you don't know, charge $19. it's low enough that impulse buyers will click, high enough that it's not a joke.

## DAY 4: LAUNCH DAY
### product hunt, twitter, reddit

**morning prompt (posted at 8 AM):**

it's launch day. everything you built this week goes live today. if it's not perfect, good — it shouldn't be. ship it anyway.

the launch checklist (do these in order):

**9:00 AM — final check**
- product works on mobile? check.
- payment flow works? check.
- link is live and accessible? check.
- no embarrassing typos on the main page? check.

**9:30 AM — twitter launch**
write a tweet with this structure:
- line 1: what you built (simple, clear)
- line 2: who it's for
- line 3: one specific feature or benefit
- line 4: the link
- line 5: "built this in 4 days. feedback welcome."

example: "built a freelancer rate calculator that tells you exactly what to charge based on your expenses, goals, and market rates. took 4 days. it's free. [link]"

no thread. no hype. one tweet. let the product speak.

**10:00 AM — reddit post** (pick 1-2 relevant subreddits)
title format: "I built [thing] because [problem I had]"
body: 3-4 paragraphs. what problem you had, what you tried, what you built, and the link. be genuine. reddit can smell marketing from a mile away.

**10:30 AM — product hunt** (optional but recommended)
submit as a "Show" or have it ready for a proper launch. if you don't have a product hunt presence yet, submit it and engage with any comments.

**11:00 AM — DM 10 people**
find 10 people who would genuinely find your product useful. DM them. not "check out my product" — "I built something that solves [problem you've talked about]. want to try it? no strings."

**rest of the day — engage**
respond to every comment, every reply, every DM. thank people for feedback. fix bugs in real-time if you can. this is the day you earn trust.

## DAY 5: ANALYZE & ITERATE
### what worked, what to double down on

**morning prompt (posted at 8 AM):**

the launch adrenaline is fading. good. today is about clear eyes and cold numbers.

pull your data:
- how many people saw your product? (impressions, page views)
- how many engaged? (clicks, signups, comments)
- how many paid? (revenue, email captures)
- where did they come from? (twitter, reddit, DMs, organic)

now do the math:
- **conversion rate:** visitors → action (signup, purchase, waitlist). if it's above 3%, you have product-market fit for this channel. below 1%, something's broken.
- **channel performance:** which channel drove the most engaged visitors? that's where you double down next week.
- **feedback themes:** what did people love? what confused them? what did they ask for?

the three decisions:
1. **kill it** — nobody cared, the numbers are flat, and you're not excited to keep working on it. that's fine. you built the muscle. move to the next product.
2. **coast it** — it got traction but it's not going to be your main thing. keep it live, make small improvements, let it earn passively.
3. **double down** — people are paying, people are sharing, and you see a clear path to more. this is your focus for the next 30 days.

**action items:**
- [ ] pull all metrics into a single document
- [ ] calculate conversion rate per channel
- [ ] list top 3 pieces of feedback and how you'd address each
- [ ] make the kill/coast/double-down decision
- [ ] write your "week in review" — what you learned, what surprised you, what you'd do differently

**evening share (posted at 6 PM):**

> **total metrics:** [views / signups / revenue]
> **best channel:** [where most traction came from]
> **decision:** [kill / coast / double down]
