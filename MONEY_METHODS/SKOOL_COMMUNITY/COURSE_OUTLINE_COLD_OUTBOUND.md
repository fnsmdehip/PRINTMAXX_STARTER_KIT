## $0 to $10K/mo in 90 days

you have no audience. no brand. no warm leads. good.

cold outbound doesn't care about any of that. cold outbound cares about one thing: can you find someone with a problem, write a message that makes them care, and close them before they forget you exist?

## MODULE 1: THE OUTBOUND MACHINE
*before you send a single message, build the infrastructure that makes it work.*

### lesson 1.1: the outbound mindset — rejection is data
- **objective:** reframe cold outreach from "begging strangers" to "solving problems at scale"
- **content:** why most people fail at outbound: they make it about themselves. "I'm reaching out because I..." — nobody cares. outbound works when you lead with the prospect's problem, not your solution. the rejection math: 100 messages → 10 replies → 3 calls → 1 client. that means 99 rejections per client. if that scares you, this isn't for you. if that excites you, keep reading.
- **deliverable:** write down your "rejection number" — how many no's you need per week to hit your income goal. print it. put it on your wall.

### lesson 1.2: infrastructure setup — domains, inboxes, warmup
- **objective:** set up the technical foundation so your emails actually reach inboxes
- **content:** buy 3 domains similar to your main domain (not exact — variations). set up google workspace on each. that's 3 inboxes minimum. why: sending volume limits. one inbox = 30-50 emails/day max before you get flagged. 3 inboxes = 90-150/day. email warmup tools: instantly.ai, warmbox, or manual warmup (send/reply to real people for 2 weeks). SPF, DKIM, DMARC — set them up or your emails go to spam. check with mail-tester.com.
- **deliverable:** 3 domains purchased, 3 inboxes created, warmup started. SPF/DKIM/DMARC configured. score 9+ on mail-tester.

### lesson 1.3: your offer — the thing that makes them reply
- **objective:** craft an offer so specific and valuable that ignoring it feels like a mistake
- **content:** "I do marketing" is not an offer. "I'll write 30 days of twitter content for your SaaS and guarantee 50 new signups or you don't pay" is an offer. the offer formula: [specific outcome] + [specific timeline] + [risk reversal]. price anchoring: your offer should feel like a steal compared to the alternative (hiring someone, doing it themselves, losing money by not doing it). the offer stack: primary deliverable + 2-3 bonuses that cost you nothing but feel valuable.
- **deliverable:** write 3 versions of your offer. test each in 50 messages. keep the one with the highest reply rate.

### lesson 1.4: the daily outbound routine (60 minutes/day)
- **objective:** build a repeatable daily process that generates leads without consuming your entire day
- **content:** the 60-minute outbound block: minute 0-15: find 20 new leads (see module 2). minute 15-45: write and send 20 personalized messages. minute 45-60: follow up on yesterday's messages and respond to replies. that's it. 60 minutes. every day. no excuses. the compound effect: 20 messages/day = 600/month = 60 replies = 18 calls = 6 clients. adjust the math based on your close rate.
- **deliverable:** block 60 minutes on your calendar every day for the next 90 days. non-negotiable. set a phone alarm.

### lesson 1.5: tools of the trade
- **objective:** set up your outbound tech stack for under $100/month
- **content:** the stack: instantly.ai or lemlist for email sequences ($30-50/mo). apollo.io or hunter.io for lead finding (free tier to start). notion or google sheets for CRM (free). loom for video outreach (free). calendly for booking calls (free). you don't need salesforce. you don't need hubspot. you need discipline and a spreadsheet.
- **deliverable:** sign up for each tool. connect your inboxes. send a test sequence to yourself. confirm it lands in the primary inbox, not spam.

## MODULE 2: LEAD GENERATION
*if you can't find people to sell to, nothing else in this course matters.*

### lesson 2.1: defining your ICP (ideal customer profile)
- **objective:** describe your perfect customer so specifically that you could find them in a crowded room
- **content:** demographics are lazy targeting. "small business owners" is not an ICP. "shopify store owners doing $10K-50K/mo in revenue who post on twitter and don't have a dedicated marketing person" is an ICP. the 5-layer ICP framework: industry, revenue range, team size, tech stack, and pain signal. pain signals: what are they doing (or not doing) that tells you they need your help? hiring posts, bad websites, no social presence, outdated content — these are entry points.
- **deliverable:** write your ICP document. one page. specific enough that you could hand it to a stranger and they could find 20 leads in an hour.

### lesson 2.2: scraping leads — the manual method
- **objective:** find 100 qualified leads in under 2 hours using free tools
- **content:** linkedin search operators for surgical targeting. twitter advanced search for finding people who are talking about their problems right now. product hunt for finding founders of recently launched products (they need help — they just launched). job boards as lead signals: a company hiring a marketer means they need marketing. you are the marketing. google maps for local business outreach. the "steal from your competitors" method: find your competitor's followers and customers.
- **deliverable:** build a lead list of 100 qualified prospects. columns: name, company, email, linkedin, pain signal, personalization hook.

### lesson 2.3: scraping leads — the automated method
- **objective:** set up systems that generate fresh leads while you sleep
- **content:** apollo.io for B2B lead data. hunter.io for email finding. phantombuster for linkedin automation (be careful — linkedin will ban you if you're aggressive). google alerts for trigger events (funding rounds, new hires, product launches). RSS feeds for monitoring target companies. building a lead generation workflow that adds 20 new leads to your list automatically every day.
- **deliverable:** set up at least 2 automated lead generation flows. test them for 1 week. target: 20 new qualified leads per day without manual work.

### lesson 2.4: qualification — don't waste messages on dead leads
- **objective:** quickly filter your list so you only message prospects who can actually buy
- **content:** the 4-question qualification filter: (1) do they have the problem you solve? (2) can they afford your price? (3) are they the decision maker? (4) is there a trigger event (something that makes this urgent right now)? if the answer to any of these is "no" or "I don't know," skip them. your time is worth more than a low-probability message. the "warm lead" vs "cold lead" spectrum and how it changes your approach.
- **deliverable:** run your 100-lead list through the qualification filter. cut it down to the top 50. those are your week 1 targets.

### lesson 2.5: organizing your pipeline
- **objective:** build a simple CRM system that tells you exactly what to do every morning
- **content:** you need 5 columns: new lead, contacted, replied, call booked, closed/lost. that's your pipeline. every lead moves through it or gets removed. the weekly pipeline review: every friday, look at your numbers. how many in each stage? where are leads getting stuck? if no one's replying, your copy is broken. if they reply but don't book, your CTA is broken. if they book but don't close, your sales call is broken. the pipeline tells you where to focus.
- **deliverable:** set up your CRM (notion template or google sheet). add your 50 qualified leads. start moving them through the pipeline tomorrow.

## MODULE 3: COPY THAT CONVERTS
*your message has 3 seconds to earn 30 seconds. make them count.*

### lesson 3.1: the 6-question framework
- **objective:** write cold emails and DMs that get replies using a repeatable framework
- **content:** every message must answer 6 questions in the prospect's mind: (1) who is this person? (2) why are they messaging ME specifically? (3) what's in it for me? (4) is this credible? (5) what do they want me to do? (6) is this easy? the framework in practice — a cold email is: personalization (1-2 sentences proving you know them) → pain point (name their problem) → offer (your specific solution) → proof (one result or credential) → CTA (one easy next step). 5 lines max. no paragraphs. no novels.
- **deliverable:** write 5 cold emails using the 6-question framework. each under 75 words. each with a different personalization angle.

### lesson 3.2: subject lines and openers that get opened
- **objective:** write subject lines and first lines that achieve 50%+ open rates
- **content:** subject lines that work: short (3-5 words), lowercase, look like they came from a friend, not a marketer. "quick question about [company]" — works. "UNLOCK YOUR GROWTH POTENTIAL WITH OUR..." — spam folder. the first line is more important than the subject line in mobile preview. it must be personal. "saw your tweet about X" or "noticed you just launched Y" — proof that this isn't a mass blast. what to avoid: fake personalization ("I love your company!"), compliment sandwiches, anything that sounds like a template.
- **deliverable:** write 10 subject lines and test them across your next 100 emails. track open rates. kill the bottom 5. iterate.

### lesson 3.3: sequences — the follow-up is where money lives
- **objective:** build a 5-touch email sequence that converts over 2 weeks
- **content:** 80% of deals close after the 5th touchpoint. most people give up after 1. your sequence: email 1 (day 1): the cold open — 6-question framework. email 2 (day 3): the bump — "just floating this back up, did you get a chance to see my note?" keep it to 2 sentences. email 3 (day 7): the value add — share something useful (article, insight, tool) related to their problem. no pitch. email 4 (day 10): the case study — "helped [similar company] achieve [specific result]. thought it might be relevant." email 5 (day 14): the breakup — "looks like the timing isn't right. no worries — I'll stop reaching out. if [problem] ever becomes a priority, here's my calendar link." the breakup email often gets the highest reply rate.
- **deliverable:** write your complete 5-email sequence. load it into your email tool. set the timing. it runs automatically from here.

### lesson 3.4: personalization at scale — the 80/20 method
- **objective:** make every email feel personal without spending 10 minutes per message
- **content:** you can't write a fully custom email for every prospect. you can't send a fully templated email to every prospect. the answer is the 80/20 method: 80% of your email is templated (the offer, the proof, the CTA). 20% is personalized (the first 1-2 sentences). batch your personalization: spend 15 minutes researching 20 leads at once. write one custom line for each. paste into your template. done. tools that help: crystal knows for personality insights, their twitter/linkedin for recent activity, their website for obvious gaps.
- **deliverable:** create your master email template with a [PERSONALIZATION] placeholder. practice writing the custom lines. time yourself: you should be able to personalize 20 emails in under 15 minutes.

### lesson 3.5: DMs, linkedin, and multi-channel outreach
- **objective:** extend your outreach beyond email to increase touchpoints and reply rates
- **content:** email is the foundation. but adding 1-2 other channels increases your reply rate by 30-50%. linkedin connection request + message: keep it short, don't pitch in the connection request. twitter DM: only if they're active on twitter. even better — engage with their content for a week before DMing. loom video: a 60-second personalized video gets 3x the reply rate of text. the multi-channel sequence: day 1 email, day 2 linkedin connect, day 4 email follow-up, day 7 linkedin message, day 10 email with loom video. don't be everywhere at once — be strategically present.
- **deliverable:** add linkedin and one other channel to your sequence. create a loom video template (record the structure once, customize the first 15 seconds for each prospect).

## MODULE 4: SYSTEMS & AUTOMATION
*stop doing the same thing manually every day. build the machine, then feed it.*

### lesson 4.1: CRM — your single source of truth
- **objective:** set up a CRM that tracks every interaction and tells you exactly what to do next
- **content:** your CRM is not optional. it's the brain of your outbound machine. every prospect, every message, every reply, every call — logged. the minimum viable CRM: prospect name, company, email, status (new/contacted/replied/booked/closed/lost), last contact date, next action, notes. notion template walkthrough. google sheets template walkthrough. when to upgrade to a real CRM (hint: when you're closing 5+ deals/month). the daily CRM ritual: open it first thing, check what's due today, do those things.
- **deliverable:** set up your CRM with the template provided. migrate your existing leads into it. set a daily reminder to check it.

### lesson 4.2: automated follow-ups — never let a lead go cold
- **objective:** set up systems that follow up automatically so you never forget a prospect
- **content:** instantly.ai sequence setup walkthrough. lemlist sequence setup walkthrough. conditional logic: if they reply, stop the sequence. if they don't, continue. if they click a link, tag them as warm. booking link in every email. auto-reminders for calls. the "re-engagement" sequence: for leads that went cold 30+ days ago, a 3-email sequence that says "hey, still interested? things have changed since we last talked."
- **deliverable:** set up your primary outbound sequence with automated follow-ups. set up a re-engagement sequence for cold leads. test both end-to-end.

### lesson 4.3: tracking — know your numbers or die guessing
- **objective:** track every metric in your outbound process so you can find and fix bottlenecks
- **content:** the outbound dashboard: emails sent, open rate, reply rate, positive reply rate, calls booked, close rate, revenue. track weekly. if your open rate is below 40%, your subject lines are broken. if your reply rate is below 5%, your copy is broken. if your booking rate is below 30% of replies, your CTA is broken. if your close rate is below 20% of calls, your sales process is broken. every number points to a specific fix. the weekly metrics review: 15 minutes every friday. look at the numbers. identify the weakest link. fix it next week.
- **deliverable:** build your outbound dashboard (spreadsheet or notion). populate it with this week's data. identify your weakest metric.

### lesson 4.4: the sales call — closing without being sleazy
- **objective:** run a 30-minute sales call that closes 30%+ of qualified prospects
- **content:** the call structure: (1) rapport — 2 minutes of genuine human connection, not forced small talk. (2) diagnosis — ask about their problem. let them talk. listen for 80% of the call. (3) prescription — present your offer as the solution to the specific problem they just described. (4) objection handling — price objection: "what's it costing you NOT to solve this?" timing objection: "when would be a better time? what changes between now and then?" need-to-think objection: "totally understand. what specific questions can I answer right now to help you decide?" (5) close — "would you like to move forward?" then shut up. the first person to talk loses.
- **deliverable:** practice the call structure with a friend or on a recording. do at least 3 mock calls before your first real one. write your objection handling scripts and have them visible during calls.

### lesson 4.5: contracts, invoicing, and getting paid
- **objective:** set up the boring-but-essential systems that make sure you actually get the money
- **content:** a handshake is not a contract. use a simple service agreement (template provided). scope, timeline, price, payment terms, cancellation policy. invoicing: stripe invoicing, paypal invoicing, or a simple notion/google doc invoice. payment terms: 50% upfront, 50% on completion. or monthly retainer, paid at the start of each month. never start work before receiving the first payment. never. the "retainer vs project" decision: retainers = predictable revenue but you're on the hook for ongoing work. projects = lumpy revenue but clear end dates.
- **deliverable:** customize the contract template for your offer. set up your invoicing system. send a test invoice to yourself.

## MODULE 5: SCALE & OPTIMIZE
*you have a system that works. now make it work harder.*

### lesson 5.1: A/B testing your outreach
- **objective:** systematically improve every element of your outbound process through testing
- **content:** test one thing at a time. subject line test: send version A to 50 people and version B to 50 people. measure open rates. keep the winner. now test the first line. then the offer. then the CTA. the 100-send rule: you need at least 100 sends per variant to know if a result is real or random. the testing calendar: test a new element every 2 weeks. in 90 days, you've optimized 6 elements and your reply rate has probably doubled.
- **deliverable:** set up your first A/B test (subject line recommended). run it for 1 week or 100 sends per variant, whichever comes first. document the result.

### lesson 5.2: hiring VAs — your first team member
- **objective:** hire and train a virtual assistant to handle lead generation and initial outreach
- **content:** when to hire: when you're consistently closing deals and the bottleneck is finding more leads, not closing more deals. where to find VAs: onlinejobs.ph (philippines), upwork, or twitter. what to delegate first: lead scraping and list building. NOT copywriting or personalization — that's your competitive advantage. the training process: record yourself doing the task 3 times. create a checklist. have them do it while you watch. then let them loose. cost: $400-800/mo for a full-time VA who does 4+ hours of lead gen per day.
- **deliverable:** write the job description for your VA. create the training documentation (screen recordings + checklists). even if you're not ready to hire yet, having this ready means you can scale overnight when the time comes.

### lesson 5.3: multi-channel at scale
- **objective:** expand from email-only to a coordinated multi-channel outbound machine
- **content:** the channels ranked by ROI for cold outbound: (1) cold email — highest volume, lowest effort per contact. (2) linkedin — highest reply rate, but low volume and easy to get restricted. (3) twitter DMs — only works if they're active on twitter. (4) cold calling — highest conversion rate per contact, but most people would rather eat glass. (5) direct mail — physical mail stands out because nobody does it. costs more but 10x the response rate for high-value prospects. the multi-channel play: don't do all 5. pick email + 1 other. master those two before adding a third.
- **deliverable:** add one new channel to your outbound machine. set up the tools. send your first 20 messages on the new channel. compare results to email after 1 week.

### lesson 5.4: scaling to $10K/mo — the math
- **objective:** reverse-engineer your exact path to $10K/month in recurring revenue
- **content:** the math depends on your price. at $2K/mo retainer: you need 5 clients. at $1K/mo: 10 clients. at $500/project: 20 projects. pick the model that fits your offer. the pipeline math: if you close 20% of calls and book 30% of replies, you need 167 positive replies to get 10 clients. at a 5% reply rate, that's 3,340 emails. over 90 days, that's 37 emails per day. very doable with 3 inboxes. the real constraint is usually not volume — it's offer quality. if your close rate is 40% instead of 20%, you need half the emails. fix your offer before you increase your volume.
- **deliverable:** fill in the $10K math worksheet with your actual numbers. what's your price? what's your close rate? how many emails per day do you need? this is your roadmap.

### lesson 5.5: beyond outbound — building the inbound flywheel
- **objective:** transition from pure outbound to a hybrid model where leads also come to you
- **content:** outbound funds the business. inbound scales the business. once you're at $5K/mo from outbound, start building inbound: (1) case studies from your outbound clients. (2) content that teaches what you do (not how — what). (3) a simple website that ranks for "[your service] for [your niche]." (4) referrals — ask every happy client: "who else do you know who has this problem?" the end state: outbound generates 50% of revenue, inbound generates 50%. outbound is your controllable, predictable engine. inbound is your scalable, compounding engine. together, they're unstoppable.
- **deliverable:** write 2 case studies from your client work. publish them on your website. ask 3 existing clients for referrals this week. plant the seeds for the inbound flywheel while your outbound machine keeps printing.
