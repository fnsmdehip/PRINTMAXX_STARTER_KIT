### What subject lines work for local business cold email

**Top performers (8-15% reply rates):**
1. "3 things hurting {{BUSINESS_NAME}}'s website" - audit-specific, creates curiosity
2. "I built a new website for {{BUSINESS_NAME}}" - bold, already-done frame
3. "{{BUSINESS_NAME}} vs [competitor name]" - competitive angle
4. "quick question about {{BUSINESS_NAME}}'s site" - low-commitment
5. "noticed something about {{BUSINESS_NAME}}" - curiosity gap

**What bombs (<2% reply):**
- "Grow your business with a new website" (generic pitch)
- "Website redesign services" (sounds like spam)
- "I can help {{BUSINESS_NAME}}" (me-focused, not them-focused)
- Anything with "free", "limited time", "special offer" (spam filter triggers)

## Scraper scoring system (savvy criteria)

The scoring system identifies HIGH CONVERSION POTENTIAL, not just bad websites. A bad website owned by a broke business = waste of time. A bad website owned by an established business with 100+ reviews = money.

### Lead score breakdown (0-100 points)

| Signal | Points | Why it predicts conversion |
|--------|--------|--------------------------|
| No website at all (Google listing only) | +20 | They know they need one. Zero friction to sell. |
| Website exists but no mobile viewport | +15 | Losing 60%+ of traffic. Easy to demonstrate value. |
| Website loads >5 seconds | +10 | Measurable, shareable, embarrassing when you show them. |
| No SSL certificate | +8 | Chrome shows "Not Secure" warning. Scares customers away. |
| 50+ Google reviews, bad website | +15 | Established business, proven revenue, CAN pay. |
| High-margin industry (dental, legal, HVAC) | +10 | Higher willingness to pay for professional services. |
| Actively posts on Google Business | +5 | They care about marketing, just bad at web. Easy sell. |
| Competitor in same area has better site | +8 | Competitive pressure = urgency to act. |
| Owner email findable (not info@) | +10 | Direct line to decision maker. |
| Has Facebook but bad/no website | +8 | They try to market online. Web is the missing piece. |
| Copyright year is 3+ years old | +5 | Stale site, clearly neglected. |
| No schema.org / structured data | +3 | AI search optimization gap (ChatGPT, Perplexity won't recommend them). |

**Score interpretation:**
- 80-100: FIRE lead. Contact immediately. High close rate.
- 60-79: Strong lead. Include in outreach campaigns.
- 40-59: Moderate lead. Include in high-volume campaigns.
- 20-39: Weak lead. Low priority, only email if you have bandwidth.
- 0-19: Skip. Good website or not a real business.

## Legal compliance

### CAN-SPAM requirements (cold email)
- Include physical mailing address (use virtual office if needed)
- Include clear unsubscribe mechanism ("reply REMOVE")
- Don't use deceptive subject lines
- Identify message as advertisement (not required if email is "primarily commercial" to business address, but good practice)
- Honor unsubscribe within 10 business days

### State-specific cold email laws
- California (CCPA): Must honor opt-out requests, maintain do-not-email list
- Most states follow federal CAN-SPAM, no additional requirements for B2B

### FTC guidelines
- Don't make unsubstantiated claims ("double your revenue")
- Don't use fake testimonials
- Be truthful about what's included in packages
- Disclose any affiliate relationships

### Contracts
- Use simple service agreement
- 50% deposit, 50% on delivery
- 3 rounds of revisions included
- Client owns domain and content
- We retain portfolio usage rights
- No guaranteed results (rankings, revenue)
