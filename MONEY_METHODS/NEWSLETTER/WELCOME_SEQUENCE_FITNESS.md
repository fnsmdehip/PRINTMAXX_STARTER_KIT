### Email 2 - Day 1
**Subject Line:** Walking burns more than the gym (here's the math) | The data that made me quit my gym membership
**Preview Text:** 10K steps = 400-500 calories. 30 min lifting = 150-250. Do the math.
**From Name:** Step By Step

Let's talk numbers.

A 30-minute weight training session burns roughly 150-250 calories depending on intensity. That's what the research shows (Falcone et al., 2015, Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research).

Walking 10,000 steps burns 400-500 calories for most men. That's not a special walk. That's just hitting the step count throughout a normal day.

But here's where it gets interesting.

NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis) accounts for up to 15% of your total daily calorie burn. For desk workers, that number drops to almost nothing. You're sitting 10-12 hours a day. Your metabolism adjusts downward.

Walking fixes this without requiring willpower.

The gym requires you to:
- Drive there (20 min)
- Change (5 min)
- Workout (45 min)
- Shower and change (15 min)
- Drive back (20 min)

Total time commitment: ~105 minutes for 200 calories burned.

Walking 10K steps throughout the day:
- Split across morning, lunch, evening
- No commute, no changing, no shower needed
- 400-500 calories burned
- Zero willpower required after the first week

I cancelled my $50/month gym membership 14 months ago. I've walked every day since. I'm in better shape now than when I was "going to the gym" 3x/week (which was really 1.5x/week if I'm honest).

Walk first. Add the gym later if you want. But walk first.

**Evening (3,000 steps):**
- 20-minute walk after dinner. No podcast. No phone. Just walk. 2,400 steps.

**Total: 8,000 steps.** On a day where you didn't "exercise" once.

Add a 15-minute morning walk outside and you're at 10K without trying.

The key: make walking the default, not the exception. Park far away. Take stairs. Walk during calls. These are not fitness tips. They're architecture changes to your day.
