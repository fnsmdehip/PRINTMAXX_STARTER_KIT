WHAT IS BROWN NOISE?
Brown noise (also called red noise) is a deeper, bass-heavy version of white noise. It emphasizes lower frequencies, creating a sound similar to a waterfall, heavy rain, or strong wind. Many people find brown noise more soothing than white noise because it's less harsh on the ears.

WHAT YOU'LL HEAR:

BROWN NOISE VS WHITE NOISE:
• White noise: All frequencies at equal intensity (sounds like static)
• Pink noise: Balanced frequencies (sounds like rain)
• Brown noise: Emphasizes low frequencies (sounds like waterfall or wind)

Brown noise is particularly effective for people who find white noise too "hissy" or harsh. The deeper frequencies are more pleasant for extended listening and can feel more natural.
