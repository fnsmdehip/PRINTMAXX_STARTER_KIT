## CRITICAL: The July 2025 Crackdown (READ THIS)

YouTube renamed "repetitious content" to **"inauthentic content"** and suspended thousands of AI channels in Jan 2026.

**What gets you flagged:**
- AI slideshow videos with no real narration or editing
- Template clones where only the title changes between videos
- Upload frequency + format similarity + minimal editing (risk signals stack)
- Pure AI generation without human editorial layer

**What still works:**
- AI-ASSISTED content with human editorial judgment
- AI voiceover with unique scripts and editorial perspective
- Stock footage videos with real analysis/commentary
- AI tools for productivity (scripts, captions, research)

**Survival rule:** Every video needs a "creative fingerprint" -- unique tone, opinion, structure, or editorial angle. AI is the tool, not the creator. You are the creator using AI tools.

## THE 10-VIDEO TEST SPRINT (Before Committing)

Don't build a 100-video content calendar before validating. Run a 10-video sprint.

### Week 1: Videos 1-5

1. Research 5 proven topics from competitor channels
2. Create using AI workflow below
3. Upload daily (Mon-Fri)
4. Track which topics get traction (views, CTR, retention)

### Week 2: Videos 6-10

1. Double down on best-performing topic from Week 1
2. Test different title/thumbnail approaches (A/B test)
3. Study retention analytics (where do people drop off?)
4. Improve scripts based on what worked

### Decision Point (End of Week 2)

| Result | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Average views > 1,000 | Continue. Scale to 12-16 videos/month. |
| Average views 300-1,000 | Optimize packaging (titles, thumbnails, hooks). Run another 10. |
| Average views < 300 | Pivot niche or format. Don't waste 3 months on a dead niche. |

**Cost of test sprint:** $38-71 (one month of tools) + 30-40 hours of work. Cheap validation.

## THE AI PRODUCTION WORKFLOW (3-4 Hours Per Video)

**Original playbook said 2.5 hours.** After the July 2025 crackdown, budget 3-4 hours to add the human editorial layer that keeps you safe from demonetization.

### Step 1: Topic Research (20-30 min)

1. Find competitor channel in your niche (10K-100K subs sweet spot)
2. Sort their videos by "most popular"
3. Pick video with 50K-500K views (proven demand, not viral outlier)
4. Analyze: What question does it answer? What emotion does it trigger?
5. Create similar topic with **different angle** (not a copy, a perspective shift)

**Tool:** Use ChatGPT to analyze competitor titles and suggest unique angles.

### Step 2: Script Generation (30-40 min)

**ChatGPT prompt template:**

```
Write a YouTube script for [topic]. Target length: 1,500 words (10 minutes).

Structure:
- Hook (0-30 sec): Start with a surprising stat, bold claim, or question
- Main content: 3-5 key points with specific examples
- Conclusion: Recap + CTA

Tone: [casual/professional/educational]
Include: specific examples, statistics, smooth transitions
Avoid: fluff, repetition, generic advice, AI vocabulary (delve, landscape, leverage, comprehensive)
```

**Then the critical step (what separates surviving channels from banned ones):**

- Rewrite the intro and conclusion in YOUR voice
- Add 2-3 contrarian opinions or strong takes
- Replace generic examples with specific ones
- Cut "AI fluff" (anything that sounds like it could be in any video on this topic)
- Add personality: humor, frustration, excitement, skepticism

**This editorial pass is NON-NEGOTIABLE.** Raw ChatGPT scripts will get you flagged.

### Step 3: Voiceover (10 min)

1. Copy edited script to ElevenLabs
2. Select voice (pick ONE, use it for every video for brand consistency)
3. Add pauses manually: commas in script create natural pauses
4. Emphasize key words: CAPS in script = emphasis
5. Vary sentence length (short + long mixed = natural rhythm)
6. Generate audio, download MP3

**Pro tip:** Test 5-10 voices before committing. Some sound robotic at scale. Find one that sounds like "a person talking," not "an AI reading."

### Step 4: Video Assembly (75-90 min)

1. Import voiceover to CapCut
2. Add stock footage every 3-5 seconds (must match what's being said)
3. Add text overlays for key points and numbers
4. Background music at 10-15% volume (never overpower voice)
5. Simple transitions (cuts work. don't overcomplicate with fancy effects)
6. Add captions (CapCut auto-generates, clean up errors)

**Stock footage rules:**
- If talking about "email marketing" don't show random beach footage
- Footage should match what's being discussed (viewers notice disconnect immediately)
- Use a mix of stock footage + screen recordings + simple graphics
- Avoid using the same 10 clips that every AI channel uses

### Step 5: Thumbnail (15-20 min)

**Canva template approach:**

1. Create ONE master template in Week 1
2. Duplicate for every video
3. Change: background image, text (3-5 words MAX), accent color
4. Keep: layout, font, style (brand consistency)

**Thumbnail rules:**
- 3-5 words max (readable at phone size)
- High contrast colors
- Face or expressive image (even stock faces work)
- Curiosity gap (make them want to click to learn the answer)

### Step 6: Upload & Optimize (10-15 min)

- **Title:** Curiosity or problem-solution format. "Why [Thing] Is Secretly [Surprising Fact]"
- **Description:** First 150 characters = most important (include keywords naturally)
- **Tags:** 5-8 relevant tags (use competitor analysis)
- **Thumbnail:** Upload custom (never use auto-generated)
- **End screens:** Link to 2 related videos
- **Cards:** Add 1-2 mid-video cards to relevant content

### Total Time Per Video: 3-4 Hours

At 3 videos/week = **9-12 hours/week.** Sustainable with a full-time job.

At 4 videos/week = **12-16 hours/week.** Only if you've streamlined the workflow.

**Original playbook suggested 4 videos/week at 2.5 hours each.** After the quality crackdown, 3 videos/week at 3-4 hours each is safer and more sustainable. Quality > quantity now.

## WHAT ACTUALLY KILLS AI CHANNELS (Avoid These)

### 1. Robotic Voiceover
ElevenLabs quality is good but you need to:
- Add pauses manually (commas and periods in script)
- Emphasize key words (CAPS in script)
- Vary sentence length (short and long mixed)
- Test 5-10 voices until you find one that sounds natural
- Never use default/stock voices that thousands of other channels use

### 2. Stock Footage Mismatch
- If talking about "email marketing" don't show random beach footage
- Footage must match what's being discussed
- Viewers notice disconnect in seconds and click away
- Use mix: stock footage + screen recordings + simple graphics

### 3. Zero Personality
AI scripts are bland by default. You MUST edit to add:
- Contrarian opinions ("everyone says X but actually Y")
- Specific examples from real cases (not generic "studies show")
- Strong statements ("this works" not "this might work")
- A consistent perspective/worldview that makes you recognizable

### 4. Inconsistent Upload Schedule
- Uploading 10 videos Week 1 then nothing for 3 weeks kills momentum
- Better: 3 videos every week for 3 months straight
- Algorithm rewards consistency, not bursts
- Set a schedule and stick to it

### 5. Template Sameness (NEW - Post-Crackdown)
- Every video using identical format/structure = "inauthentic content" flag
- Vary intro styles, segment structures, content types
- Mix formats: how-to, analysis, comparison, case study, news commentary
- YouTube's Gemini AI now detects template patterns across your uploads

### 6. Ignoring Retention Analytics
- If 60% of viewers leave in the first 30 seconds, your hook is broken
- If there's a consistent drop at minute 3, your content sags there
- Study retention graphs for every video
- Fix the weak points in the next video

## SHORTS STRATEGY (Discovery, Not Revenue)

**Shorts RPM: $0.01-0.07/1K views** (vs $1-30 for long-form). Revenue is 50-1000x lower.

**Use Shorts for:**
- Subscriber acquisition (funnel to long-form)
- Topic testing (cheap validation before full video)
- Repurposing (clip best moments from long-form)
- Algorithm boost (YouTube favors multi-format creators)

**Do NOT use Shorts for:**
- Primary revenue (1M Shorts views = $10-60)
- Standalone strategy (no moat, no retention)

**Tool:** Opus Clip ($9/mo) auto-clips your long-form into Shorts. Upload 2-3 Shorts per long-form video.

## AI DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS

YouTube requires disclosure when content includes:
- Synthetic voices resembling real people
- Deepfake face swaps
- AI-generated realistic scenes that could be mistaken for real footage

YouTube does NOT require disclosure for:
- AI-generated scripts or outlines
- AI-assisted editing or auto-captions
- Using AI for productivity (research, ideation)

**For faceless channels with AI voiceover:** If using a synthetic voice that doesn't impersonate a real person, disclosure is likely not required. But adding an AI disclosure label proactively is low-risk insurance.
