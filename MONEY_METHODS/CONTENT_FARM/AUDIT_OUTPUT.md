## SECTION F: Content Multiplication Framework

### The 1-to-15+ System

Every piece of content you create should generate at minimum 15 derivative pieces. Here is the exact workflow.

### Starting Point: One Long-Form Piece

Write one thread, one article, one video script, or one deep analysis. That becomes the "source content."

**Example source:** A 10-tweet thread about "gained 12lbs of muscle in 8 months training 3 hours per week"

### The 15 Derivatives

**From 1 thread, generate:**

| # | Derivative | Platform | Time to Create |
|---|-----------|----------|----------------|
| 1 | Original thread | X/Twitter | 30 min (already done) |
| 2 | TikTok reel (hook from tweet 1) | TikTok | 10 min (screen record reading it, or text overlay on stock footage) |
| 3 | IG reel (same video) | Instagram | 2 min (repost, adjust aspect ratio if needed) |
| 4 | YouTube Short (same video) | YouTube | 2 min (repost) |
| 5 | FB Reel (same video, no watermarks) | Facebook | 2 min (repost, remove TikTok watermark) |
| 6 | IG carousel (key data points as slides) | Instagram | 15 min (Canva, 5-7 slides) |
| 7 | LinkedIn post (professional rewrite) | LinkedIn | 10 min (adjust tone for B2B) |
| 8 | Reddit post (value-first, link in comments) | Reddit | 10 min (adjust for subreddit norms) |
| 9 | Medium article (expand to 1,500 words) | Medium | 30 min (add context, studies, examples) |
| 10 | Substack post (same as Medium, different audience) | Substack | 5 min (cross-post) |
| 11 | Pinterest pin (graphic with key stat) | Pinterest | 5 min (Canva template) |
| 12 | Email newsletter issue | Beehiiv | 15 min (add personal context) |
| 13 | Gumroad product spec (if topic is deep enough) | Gumroad | 30 min (outline product) |
| 14 | Quote graphic for stories | IG Stories | 5 min (Canva) |
| 15 | Reply/follow-up thread (next day) | X/Twitter | 15 min (add what people asked) |

**Total time for 15 pieces: approximately 3 hours**
**Time per piece: approximately 12 minutes average**

### Platform-Specific Reformatting Rules

**X/Twitter to TikTok/Reels:**
- Take the hook tweet (tweet 1)
- Record yourself reading it OR use text overlay on stock footage
- Add trending sound at 10% volume
- Keep under 60 seconds
- Strong hook in first 3 seconds
- Caption text on screen (watched muted)

**X/Twitter to LinkedIn:**
- Rewrite in professional tone (remove lowercase casual style)
- Add "what I learned" framing
- Include specific business outcome
- Use line breaks liberally (LinkedIn favors readability)
- Add 3-5 relevant hashtags
- End with question (drives comments)

**X/Twitter to Instagram Carousel:**
- Slide 1: Hook (same as tweet 1)
- Slides 2-5: Key data points (one per slide, large text)
- Slide 6: Summary/takeaway
- Slide 7: CTA (follow, save, share, link in bio)
- Use Canva template for brand consistency
- Square format (1080x1080)

**X/Twitter to Reddit:**
- Rewrite as self-post (not link post)
- Lead with value (no self-promotion in body)
- Use markdown formatting
- Link to product/profile in comments only
- Match subreddit culture (r/fitness = data-driven, r/SideProject = humble, r/Entrepreneur = numbers)
- Respond to every comment for first 2 hours

**X/Twitter to Medium/Substack:**
- Expand to 1,500-2,000 words
- Add introduction with context
- Add supporting data/studies
- Add examples and case studies
- Add actionable takeaways at end
- Include images/charts where relevant
- Cross-post between Medium and Substack

**X/Twitter to Email Newsletter:**
- Personal intro (1-2 sentences about why this matters to you)
- Content (adapted from thread)
- One CTA (reply, visit link, buy product)
- PS line for secondary offer
- Keep under 500 words (newsletters that are too long get skimmed)

**X/Twitter to Pinterest:**
- Create tall graphic (1000x1500)
- Key stat or quote from thread in large text
- Brand colors and logo
- Link to full article/thread
- Use relevant keywords in pin description
- Pin to niche-specific board

### Repurposing Workflow (Daily)

**Step 1: Create source content (30-60 min)**
Write one thread OR one reel script OR one article.

**Step 2: Platform adaptation (60-90 min)**
Reformat for 5-6 platforms using rules above.

**Step 3: Schedule (15 min)**
Upload to Buffer/Publer. Set platform-specific times.

**Step 4: Engage (30 min)**
Reply to comments on all platforms for first 2 hours after posting.

**Step 5: Measure (10 min next day)**
Check which platform performed best. Double down on that format next time.

### Content Multiplication Math

If you create 1 source piece per day and multiply to 15:
- 1 source piece x 15 derivatives = 15 posts/day
- 15 posts/day x 30 days = 450 posts/month
- 450 posts across 6+ platforms = massive surface area

At even 0.1% conversion to product sale:
- 450 posts x average 500 impressions = 225,000 impressions/month
- 225,000 x 0.1% CTR = 225 link clicks
- 225 clicks x 3% conversion = 6-7 sales
- 6-7 sales x $27 average = $162-$189/month

Scale to 3 niches running simultaneously:
- $162-$189 x 3 niches = $486-$567/month
- This is before ad revenue, before affiliate revenue, before growing audience

The content multiplication framework is how you go from "posting" to "operating a content machine."

### Zero Waste Integration

Every content piece should trigger at minimum:
1. Social post (posted within 24 hours)
2. Newsletter mention (next issue)
3. Gumroad product consideration (does this become a product?)
4. Affiliate link opportunity (can you link a relevant tool?)
5. Lead magnet hook (can you offer a free template/guide in exchange for email?)

If you finish a research task and don't generate content from it, you did half the job.

**Monday:** Research + write 3 source pieces (threads/scripts)
**Tuesday:** Multiply Monday's content to all platforms (45 pieces)
**Wednesday:** Research + write 3 source pieces
**Thursday:** Multiply Wednesday's content (45 pieces)
**Friday:** Schedule next week's queue + analyze performance

**Weekly output:** 6 source pieces x 15 derivatives = 90 posts/week + 2 long-form pieces
