# SaaS / Startup Founders Sequence
**Category:** SaaS founders, tech startups, HN hiring leads
**Offer:** Contract dev — AI automation, web apps, agentic pipelines — 2-week delivery
**Signal:** Posted on HN Who's Hiring, Product Hunt launch, LinkedIn founder post
**Price:** $2,500-8,000 per project / $150/hr hourly
**Sequence:** 3 emails over 10 days (E1 → +4 days E2 → +6 days E3)

---

## EMAIL 1 — Cold Intro

**Subject:** contract instead of hire — 2wk delivery

**Body:**
```
hey [First Name],

saw your [HN / Product Hunt / LinkedIn] post about [role or product].

instead of a 60-day hire cycle, one option: contract dev. you get the output in 2 weeks, no equity, no benefits negotiation.

i build [specific stack: TypeScript/Next.js/Python/n8n/agentic pipelines]. recent work: [specific example relevant to their stack — see notes].

what i'd tackle first for [Company]: [1-2 sentences on their specific problem based on what i found].

worth a 20-minute call to scope it?

[Your First Name]
```

**Notes:** Personalize "[specific example]" to match their stack. If they need AI automation → mention agentic pipelines. If they need web → mention Next.js + Supabase. Never say "I'm a developer" — show the thing. "[1-2 sentences on their specific problem]" is key — this requires reading their site/post before sending.

---

## EMAIL 2 — Follow-Up

**Subject:** re: [Company] — what i'd build first

**Body:**
```
[First Name],

followed up on [Company] a bit more.

quick read of your site: [specific observation — what's missing or what could ship faster with contract help].

i can have [specific deliverable] live in 2 weeks. here's how that works:
- week 1: build + daily updates
- week 2: your feedback + revisions + deploy
- you own everything on day 14

cost: $[specific amount] flat.

if you're still in the hiring process, contract doesn't block that — some founders run both.

[Your First Name]
```

**Notes:** The "you own everything on day 14" line removes the biggest objection (lock-in). Flat rate beats hourly for founders — they hate variable costs. The "doesn't block hiring" line is a genuine objection-handler.

---

## EMAIL 3 — Breakup

**Subject:** last note on this

**Body:**
```
[First Name],

closing this thread — don't want to spam you.

if you're still looking for contract help later: [your contact method]. i specialize in [2-3 word niche: AI agents / Next.js SaaS / agentic pipelines] and usually have 1-2 slots open.

one thing i noticed that might be useful regardless: [genuinely useful observation about their product/site in 1-2 sentences].

good luck with [Company].

[Your First Name]
```

**Notes:** The "one thing i noticed" at the end is the most replied-to part of breakup emails. Even if they don't hire you, you built goodwill and they'll remember you. Keep it genuinely useful — not a pitch in disguise.

---

## STACK MATCHING GUIDE

| Their Stack | Lead With |
|-------------|-----------|
| TypeScript / Next.js / Supabase | "i've shipped 3 Next.js + Supabase apps in the last 60 days" |
| Python / LLMs / vector DBs | "built 4 agentic pipelines last quarter, Pinecone + Claude + n8n" |
| React Native / mobile | "can replicate your app in Capacitor + Ionic in 2 weeks vs 8-week native build" |
| n8n / Zapier | "built custom n8n workflows that cut ops time by 70% for 2 clients" |
| AI / automation | "my automation stack processes 1,500+ alpha entries/day with zero human intervention" |

---

## FOLLOW-UP CADENCE AFTER SEQUENCE

If no reply: wait 45 days. Reach back out with a specific observation:
- "saw you launched [feature/update] — noticed [specific thing]. if you ever want help with [X], i'm available."
- Founders remember people who pay attention to their product.
