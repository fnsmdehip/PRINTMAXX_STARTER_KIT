# E-Commerce Arbitrage Playbook

## What is Retail/Online Arbitrage?

Retail arbitrage: buying products from retail stores (Walmart, Target, etc.) at clearance/discount prices and reselling online at market price.

Online arbitrage: buying discounted products from one online marketplace and reselling on another at higher prices.

**The core concept:** exploit pricing inefficiencies between platforms. Product sells for $15 at Walmart clearance, $35 on Amazon. You pocket the difference minus fees and shipping.

**Why it works:**
- Big retailers have inventory they need to move (clearance, seasonal, overstock)
- Different platforms have different buyer behaviors and price expectations
- Information asymmetry: not everyone checks all platforms
- Local inventory varies wildly (Walmart clearance in Oklahoma differs from NYC)

## Best Product Categories for Beginners

### 1. Electronics Accessories ($15-50 range)
**Why:** High margin, low risk, fast turnover, small/light shipping

**Examples:**
- Phone cases and screen protectors
- Charging cables and power banks
- Bluetooth speakers and earbuds
- Gaming accessories (controllers, headsets)
- Computer mice and keyboards

**Where to source:** Walmart clearance, Target clearance, Amazon Warehouse Deals
**Where to sell:** eBay (13.25% fee), Facebook Marketplace (5% fee)
**Typical margin:** 40-60%

### 2. Beauty & Personal Care
**Why:** Consumable = repeat buyers, brand loyalty, high perceived value

**Examples:**
- High-end skincare (The Ordinary, CeraVe, Neutrogena)
- Makeup palettes and sets
- Hair tools (brushes, straighteners)
- Fragrance gift sets
- Men's grooming products

**Where to source:** TJ Maxx, Marshalls, Ross, CVS clearance
**Where to sell:** Mercari (10% fee), Poshmark (20% for beauty)
**Typical margin:** 50-80%

**WARNING:** Check expiration dates. Never sell expired products. Factor in authentication concerns for high-end brands.

### 3. Toys & Games
**Why:** Seasonal spikes (holidays), impulse purchases, collectibility

**Examples:**
- LEGO sets (especially retired/discontinued)
- Board games
- Action figures and collectibles
- Educational toys
- Outdoor play equipment

**Where to source:** Walmart clearance (January, July), Target clearance (post-holiday)
**Where to sell:** Amazon FBA (15% + fulfillment), eBay
**Typical margin:** 30-50%

**Best timing:** Buy clearance January-February, sell November-December.

### 4. Books
**Why:** Easy to start, low capital, standardized products (ISBN), light weight

**Examples:**
- Textbooks (high value, predictable demand)
- Popular fiction bestsellers
- Niche non-fiction (business, self-help, technical)
- Children's books (board books, series)

**Where to source:** Library sales, thrift stores, Amazon Warehouse, Goodwill
**Where to sell:** Amazon FBA (15%), eBay
**Typical margin:** 20-40% (textbooks can hit 100%+)

**Tool:** BookScouter.com to compare buy prices across platforms.

### 5. Home & Kitchen (Small Items)
**Why:** Consistent demand, gift-giving occasions, aesthetic trends

**Examples:**
- Kitchen gadgets under $50
- Organizational products
- Decorative items (candles, frames)
- Small appliances
- Bedding and bath accessories

**Where to source:** HomeGoods, Target clearance, Walmart
**Where to sell:** Facebook Marketplace (best for local, 0% pickup), eBay
**Typical margin:** 40-60%

### Platform-Specific Buyer Psychology

**Amazon:** Buyers prioritize speed (Prime) and trust. Pay premium for convenience. Best for: consumables, electronics, household staples.

**eBay:** Buyers hunt deals, compare prices, patient with shipping. Best for: collectibles, used electronics, books, toys.

**Facebook Marketplace:** Local buyers, instant gratification, avoid shipping. Best for: large items, furniture, local pickup, fast cash.

**Mercari:** Mobile-first, younger demographic, fashion-focused. Best for: clothing, accessories, beauty, small electronics.

**Poshmark:** Fashion and beauty exclusively, social selling. Best for: clothing, shoes, designer items, beauty products.

## Fee Calculations for Every Platform

### eBay
- **Final value fee:** 13.25% of total sale (item + shipping)
- **Payment processing:** Included in final value fee
- **Optional fees:** Promoted listings (cost per sale), store subscription ($7.95-299.95/mo)
- **Net calculation:** Sale price × 0.8675 = your cut

**Example:**
- Sell item for $50 + $5 shipping = $55 total
- eBay fee: $55 × 0.1325 = $7.29
- You receive: $55 - $7.29 = $47.71
- Minus shipping cost ($5) = $42.71 net

### Facebook Marketplace
- **Fee:** 5% per shipment, $0.40 flat fee
- **Local pickup:** 0% fee
- **Payment processing:** Included
- **Net calculation:** (Sale price × 0.95) - $0.40

**Example:**
- Sell for $50 shipped
- Fee: ($50 × 0.05) + $0.40 = $2.90
- You receive: $47.10
- Minus shipping ($5) = $42.10 net

**Pro tip:** Offer local pickup for 0% fees. Price slightly higher for shipped.

### Amazon FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon)
- **Referral fee:** 15% (most categories)
- **FBA fulfillment fee:** $3.22-$5.42 for small/light items (<1 lb)
- **Monthly storage:** $0.75/cubic foot (standard), higher for oversized
- **Long-term storage:** $6.90/cubic foot if >365 days

**Example:**
- Sell item for $50
- Referral fee: $50 × 0.15 = $7.50
- FBA fulfillment (small): $3.50
- Total fees: $11.00
- You receive: $50 - $11 = $39.00
- Minus your cost ($15) = $24 profit

**Calculator:** Use Amazon FBA Calculator (sellercentral.amazon.com/fba/calculator)

### Mercari
- **Selling fee:** 10% + $0.30 payment processing
- **Shipping:** Buyer pays (you set), or you offer free shipping
- **Promotions:** Optional discounted shipping rates

**Example:**
- Sell for $50
- Fee: ($50 × 0.10) + $0.30 = $5.30
- You receive: $44.70
- Minus shipping ($5) = $39.70 net

### Poshmark
- **Fee structure (tiered):**
  - Sales under $15: flat $2.95
  - Sales $15+: 20%
- **Shipping:** Flat $7.67 prepaid label (up to 5 lbs)

**Example:**
- Sell for $50
- Fee: $50 × 0.20 = $10
- You receive: $40
- Shipping: prepaid label, buyer pays
- Net: $40 (minus your cost)

**Note:** High fees, but fashion/beauty buyers expect it. Price accordingly.

## Shipping Cost Optimization

### Calculated vs. Flat Rate

**Calculated shipping:** Based on weight, dimensions, destination
- Best for: heavy items, large items, variable weights
- Tools: Pirate Ship, ShipStation
- Rates: USPS, UPS, FedEx (compare all)

**Flat rate shipping:** Fixed price regardless of weight (up to limit)
- USPS Flat Rate boxes: $9.65 (small), $16.95 (medium), $22.80 (large)
- Best for: dense/heavy items that fit the box

### Shipping Cost Hacks

1. **Use Pirate Ship or ShipStation** - 20-40% cheaper than retail USPS rates
2. **USPS First Class** - cheapest for items under 1 lb
3. **USPS Priority Mail** - 1-3 day delivery, free boxes/envelopes from USPS
4. **Facebook Marketplace local pickup** - zero shipping cost
5. **Buy shipping supplies in bulk** - Amazon, Uline, UPS Store
6. **Reuse Amazon boxes** - free, eco-friendly
7. **Polymailers for soft goods** - $0.10-0.20 each vs. $0.50+ for boxes

### Dimensional Weight Pricing

Large but light items (pillows, lampshades) get charged by dimensional weight: (L × W × H) / 139 for USPS.

**Avoid:** Bulky low-margin items. Shipping will kill profit.

## Photography Tips (Phone Camera is Fine)

### The 3-Photo Minimum
1. **Main photo:** Product front/center, good lighting, plain background
2. **Detail photo:** Close-up of brand label, condition, features
3. **Context photo:** Product in use, size reference, packaging included

### Lighting Matters More Than Camera
- **Natural light wins:** Near window, indirect sunlight (avoid harsh shadows)
- **White background:** White poster board ($1 at Dollar Tree) or white sheet
- **No flash:** Makes colors look bad, creates glare

### Quick Setup
- Stack books or boxes to create height
- Lean poster board against wall for seamless background
- Use phone's portrait mode for background blur
- Take 10-20 photos, pick best 5

### What to Capture
- All sides of product
- Any flaws or wear (be honest, avoid returns)
- Size reference (next to common object like phone or ruler)
- Sealed packaging if new
- Brand labels and tags

**Pro tip:** Batch photography. Set up once, photograph 10-20 items in one session.

## Listing Optimization Per Platform

### eBay Listing Optimization
- **Title:** 80 characters max, stuff with keywords (brand, model, size, color, condition)
  - Good: "Apple AirPods Pro 2nd Gen White Wireless Earbuds MagSafe Case New Sealed"
  - Bad: "Awesome Earbuds!"
- **Condition:** Be accurate (New, New Other, Refurbished, Used - Like New/Good/Acceptable)
- **Shipping:** Offer free shipping, bake cost into price (buyers filter by free shipping)
- **Returns:** 30-day returns = higher visibility in search
- **Photos:** 12 free photos, use all 12
- **Description:** HTML allowed, use bullet points for features
- **Item specifics:** Fill out ALL fields (eBay uses for search ranking)

**eBay SEO formula:** Title keywords + item specifics + competitive price + free shipping + fast handling time = top of search.

### Amazon Listing (if not FBA)
- **UPC/EAN required:** Must match manufacturer UPC
- **Condition notes:** Be thorough for used items
- **Competitive pricing:** Amazon buy box goes to lowest price + good metrics
- **Fulfillment speed:** Set realistic handling time (1-2 days ideal)
- **Photos:** High-res, white background for main image

**Amazon Pro seller ($39.95/mo) needed for:** More than 40 sales/month, access to bulk tools, advertising.

### Facebook Marketplace
- **Photos:** First photo is EVERYTHING (70% of clicks)
- **Title:** Short, descriptive (50 chars)
- **Description:** Bullet points, dimensions, condition, pickup/shipping options
- **Price:** Slightly negotiable (buyers will offer less)
- **Response time:** Fast replies = higher ranking

**Facebook hack:** Cross-post to local buy/sell groups, increase visibility 10x.

### Mercari
- **First photo:** Lifestyle shot if possible (product in use)
- **Hashtags:** Use 5 relevant hashtags
- **Description:** Measurements mandatory for clothing
- **Shipping:** Offer seller-paid shipping for items under $25 (increases sales)
- **Promote:** Mercari's promotion tool (drop price 10% = 5% off fee)

### Poshmark (Fashion/Beauty Only)
- **Cover photo:** Model or styled flatlay
- **Measurements:** Required for clothing (bust, waist, length, inseam)
- **Brand:** Select from dropdown (enables brand filters)
- **Sharing:** Share your listings 3-5x per day (Poshmark is social)
- **Bundles:** Enable bundle discounts (3+ items = discount)
- **Parties:** Join Posh Parties to increase visibility

**Poshmark is time-intensive:** Must actively share, follow, engage. Best if focusing only on fashion.

## Tools: Keepa, BrickSeek, Tactical Arbitrage

### Keepa (Amazon Price History Tracker)
**Cost:** $19/month
**What it does:**
- Tracks Amazon price history for every product (3-month, 1-year, all-time graphs)
- Shows sales rank history (how often product sells)
- Price drop alerts (get notified when product hits target price)
- Shows Amazon direct vs. 3rd party seller prices

**How to use:**
1. Install Keepa browser extension (Chrome, Firefox)
2. Visit any Amazon product page
3. See price history graph below main product image
4. Look for: consistent sales rank + recent price drop = buy opportunity

**What to look for:**
- Sales rank under 50,000 in category = sells multiple times per day
- Price dropped 30%+ recently = clearance or overstock
- Historical high price 2x current price = margin potential

**ROI:** Pays for itself if you find 2-3 good deals per month.

### BrickSeek (Walmart/Target Local Inventory Checker)
**Cost:** Free (limited), $9.99/mo (unlimited)
**What it does:**
- Check real-time inventory and prices at local Walmart/Target stores
- Find clearance items before going to store (saves gas)
- See which stores have stock of specific SKU

**How to use:**
1. Get SKU from Walmart.com or Target.com product URL
2. Enter SKU on BrickSeek
3. Enter your ZIP code
4. See inventory levels and prices at nearby stores

**Example:**
- Product shows $15 online, BrickSeek shows $5 at Store #1234
- Drive to that specific store, buy clearance item, resell for $30+

**Limitations:** Inventory not always accurate (updated nightly). Call store to confirm before driving 30+ minutes.

### Tactical Arbitrage (Advanced Product Sourcing)
**Cost:** $27-97/month (tiered plans)
**What it does:**
- Automated product sourcing across 200+ online retailers
- Bulk price comparison (scan thousands of products in minutes)
- Profit calculator built-in (accounts for Amazon fees)
- Reverse search (find where products are cheaper)

**How to use:**
1. Product search: Enter Amazon category, TA scans Walmart, Target, etc. for price differences

3. Set filters: Min profit $5, min ROI 30%, etc.

**Best for:** Scaling to 100+ orders per month. Overkill for beginners.

**Alternative:** Manual research for first 3 months, add TA when revenue hits $500+/month.

## Legal Considerations

### Business Structure
**Month 1-3:** Operate as individual, no LLC needed (test the model first).

**After $1K/month consistently:** Consider LLC ($50-500 to file, varies by state).

**Why LLC:**
- Liability protection (separate personal and business assets)
- Tax benefits (deduct business expenses)
- Professionalism (can open business bank account)

### Sales Tax
**You must collect sales tax if:**
- Selling to buyers in states where you have nexus (physical presence)
- Amazon FBA creates nexus in states with FBA warehouses
- Each state has different thresholds ($100K sales or 200 transactions common)

**Solution:**
- Use platform's automatic sales tax collection (eBay, Amazon)
- Or use TaxJar ($19+/month, automates sales tax filing)
- Set aside 8-10% of revenue for sales tax remittance

### Resale Certificate
Get a resale certificate (free from your state) to buy wholesale without paying sales tax upfront. Only applicable if buying from wholesalers, not retail arbitrage.

### Record Keeping
**Track EVERYTHING:**
- Every purchase (receipt, date, product, cost)
- Every sale (platform, date, price, fees)
- Shipping costs
- Mileage if driving to stores (56¢/mile deductible)

**Tools:**
- Google Sheets or Excel (free)
- QuickBooks Self-Employed ($15/mo, connects to bank)
- Wave (free accounting software)

**Tax time:** Profit = revenue - (product costs + shipping + fees + mileage + tools)

### Trademark/Brand Restrictions
**Amazon gated categories:** Some brands require approval (Nike, Apple, Disney, etc.)
- Need 3 invoices from authorized distributor
- Retail receipts (Walmart, Target) often NOT accepted

**Counterfeit risk:** Never buy from shady sources. Stick to major retailers.

**First sale doctrine:** You CAN resell authentic products you legally purchased. No trademark violation.

## Tax Tracking

### What's Deductible
- Product costs (your purchase price)
- Shipping supplies (boxes, tape, labels)
- Shipping costs (postage)
- Platform fees (eBay, Amazon, etc.)
- Software/tools (Keepa, Tactical Arbitrage)
- Mileage (56¢/mile for 2026, driving to source products)
- Home office (if you have dedicated space, percentage of rent/mortgage)
- Phone/internet (percentage used for business)

### What to Save
- All receipts (photo on phone, store in Google Drive folder)
- Platform sales reports (download monthly CSV from each platform)
- Mileage log (MileIQ app auto-tracks, $6/mo)

### Quarterly Estimated Taxes
If you make $1K+ profit per quarter, you OWE estimated taxes (due April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15).

**How much to set aside:** 25-30% of profit (federal + state + self-employment tax).

**Payment:** IRS Direct Pay (free online), or EFTPS.

**Penalty for not paying:** Underpayment penalty (small, but annoying). Pay quarterly to avoid.

### Year-End
- Form 1040 Schedule C (business profit/loss)
- Form 1040 Schedule SE (self-employment tax, 15.3% on profit)
- Platform will send 1099-K if you exceeded $5K (2026 threshold)

**Hire CPA for first year ($200-400)** to set up correctly. DIY with TurboTax Self-Employed ($119) after that.

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Buying Too Much Inventory Too Fast
**Mistake:** "I found 50 of these on clearance for $5 each, bought them all!"
**Reality:** Product doesn't sell, you're stuck with $250 in dead inventory.
**Fix:** Test with 1-3 units first. Confirm sell-through before going deep.

### 2. Ignoring Fees in Profit Calculation
**Mistake:** "I bought for $10, sold for $30, made $20 profit!"
**Reality:** After 13.25% eBay fee, $5 shipping, $3 supplies, profit = $8.
**Fix:** Use profit calculator BEFORE buying. Net profit = sell price - (cost + fees + shipping + supplies).

### 3. Bad Photos = No Sales
**Mistake:** Dark, blurry photos. Messy background. No detail shots.
**Reality:** Buyers scroll past, item sits for weeks.
**Fix:** Spend 10 minutes per item on good photos. White background, natural light, multiple angles.

### 4. Overpricing or Underpricing
**Mistake:** "I paid $15, so I'll sell for $50 to make good profit."
**Reality:** Market price is $25. Item doesn't sell.
**Fix:** Research sold listings (eBay) or current prices (Amazon) BEFORE listing. Price competitively.

### 5. Slow Shipping / Poor Communication
**Mistake:** Wait 5 days to ship. Don't respond to messages.
**Reality:** Bad reviews, account health drops, sales plummet.
**Fix:** Ship within 1-2 business days. Respond to messages within 24 hours. Under-promise, over-deliver.

### 6. No Inventory Tracking
**Mistake:** "Where did I buy this? How much did I pay?"
**Reality:** Can't calculate actual profit, overpay on replenishment, miss trends.
**Fix:** Simple spreadsheet. Columns: Date purchased, product, source, cost, sell price, platform, fees, net profit, date sold.

### 7. Chasing Every Shiny Object
**Mistake:** Try every category, every platform, constantly switching.
**Reality:** Never get good at anything, spread too thin.
**Fix:** Pick ONE category + ONE platform for first month. Master it. Then expand.

### 8. Ignoring Returns/Customer Service
**Mistake:** "Not my problem, no returns."
**Reality:** Platforms force returns, bad reviews kill sales.
**Fix:** Accept 30-day returns. Be honest in descriptions. Most returns are avoidable with accurate listings.

### 9. Not Scaling When Working
**Mistake:** Found profitable item, sell 3 units, move on to something else.
**Reality:** Left money on table.
**Fix:** When something works, DO MORE OF IT. Buy 10, 20, 50 units. Ride the wave.

### 10. Quitting Too Early
**Mistake:** "I've been doing this for 3 weeks and only made $50 profit, this doesn't work."
**Reality:** Takes 60-90 days to dial in, build feedback, understand what sells.
**Fix:** Commit to 90 days minimum. Track metrics. Adjust based on data, not emotions.

**Strategy:**
- Source from Walmart clearance (BrickSeek to find deals)

**Key:** Became expert in one category. Knew exactly what sold fast. Built reputation with fast shipping and good photos.

**Background:** Started with $100, focused on TJ Maxx/Marshalls beauty finds.

**Strategy:**
- Visit TJ Maxx 2x per week (20 minutes from home)
- Buy high-end beauty products at 50-70% off
- Resell on Mercari and Poshmark
- Excellent photos (styled flatlays, natural light)

**Results:**
- Month 1: $280 revenue, $70 profit
- Month 3: $1,100 revenue, $350 profit
- Month 6: $2,800 revenue, $900 profit

**Key:** Found niche with built-in audience (beauty buyers on Mercari/Poshmark). Low competition. High margins.

### Case Study 3: Book Flipper
**Background:** Started with $50, focused exclusively on textbooks.

**Strategy:**
- Source from library sales ($0.50-2/book)
- Scan ISBN with Amazon Seller app
- Only buy if Amazon sell price $15+
- Ship via USPS Media Mail (cheapest)

**Results:**
- Month 1: $320 revenue, $180 profit (bought 30 books for $40, sold 12 for $320)
- Month 3: $1,400 revenue, $800 profit
- Month 6: $3,600 revenue, $2,000 profit

**Key:** Textbooks have predictable demand (semesters start/end). High margins. Low capital requirements. But time-intensive to source.
