## Best POD Platforms (February 2026)

### 1. Merch by Amazon
**Pros:**
- Massive built-in audience (Amazon traffic)
- No upfront cost
- Print-on-demand, no inventory
- Amazon handles production, shipping, customer service

**Cons:**
- Application approval required (waitlist)
- Strict content policy (no trademark infringement, no controversial)
- Tier system (start at 10 designs, level up with sales)
- Lower royalty (typically $2-5 per shirt)

**Best for:** Evergreen designs, trending niches with high search volume
**Approval tips:** Apply with portfolio of original designs, professional application

### 2. Redbubble
**Pros:**
- No approval required, instant setup
- 70+ products (shirts, mugs, stickers, phone cases, wall art)
- Global marketplace
- Artist-friendly community

**Cons:**
- Lower traffic than Amazon
- High competition (millions of designs)
- Lower margins (15-25% typical)

**Best for:** Niche designs, artist portfolio, diverse product types
**Tip:** Upload to multiple products per design (maximize revenue per design)

### 3. TeeSpring (now Spring)
**Pros:**
- Create your own storefront
- Higher margins (set your own prices)
- Integrates with YouTube, TikTok, Instagram (sell directly to followers)
- Fast payouts

**Cons:**
- You drive your own traffic (no marketplace)
- Need audience or ad spend to succeed

**Best for:** Influencers, content creators with existing audience
**Tip:** Promote on TikTok with storefront link in bio

### 4. Printful + Etsy
**Pros:**
- Full control over branding
- Integrates with Etsy (built-in marketplace)
- High quality prints
- More products (home decor, apparel, accessories)

**Cons:**
- Base costs higher ($12-15 per shirt)
- You handle marketing
- Etsy fees (6.5% transaction + $0.20 per listing)

**Best for:** Branded shops, higher-end designs, Etsy search traffic
**Tip:** Optimize Etsy SEO (titles, tags, descriptions)

### 5. Zazzle
**Pros:**
- No upfront cost
- 1000+ products
- Established marketplace

**Cons:**
- Lower royalties (5-15%)
- Old-school interface
- Lower traffic than competitors

**Best for:** Passive income, set-and-forget designs

## Pricing Strategy Per Platform

### Merch by Amazon
- **Retail price:** $19.99-24.99 (Amazon sets base)
- **Your royalty:** $2-6 depending on price
- **Strategy:** Price competitively (check similar designs). Don't race to bottom.

### Redbubble
- **Base cost:** $15-18 (Redbubble sets)
- **Your markup:** 15-25% typical
- **Retail price:** $20-25
- **Your profit:** $3-6 per item
- **Strategy:** 20% markup standard, 30% for niche designs with no competition

### TeeSpring
- **Base cost:** $10-12 (production + shipping)
- **Your markup:** You set (full control)
- **Retail price:** $19.99-29.99
- **Your profit:** $8-18 per item
- **Strategy:** Price at $24.99 for quality perception. Higher markup because you're driving traffic.

### Printful + Etsy
- **Base cost:** $12-15 (Printful charges)
- **Etsy fees:** 6.5% + $0.20
- **Retail price:** $25-35
- **Your profit:** $7-15 per item
- **Strategy:** Premium pricing. Etsy buyers expect handmade/higher quality. Use "free shipping" (bake cost into price).

## Niche Selection Criteria

### High-Demand Niches (Proven Winners)
1. **Dog breeds:** "Golden Retriever Mom," "German Shepherd Dad"
2. **Nursing:** Endless variations on nurse pride/humor
3. **Faith-based:** Christian market is huge, loyal, repeat buyers
4. **Teacher gifts:** End of year, back to school, appreciation week
5. **Sarcastic humor:** Introverts, overthinkers, office workers

### Avoid These Niches (Oversaturated or Low Margin)
1. **Generic motivation:** "Be Kind," "Stay Strong" (too much competition)
2. **Zodiac signs:** Played out, low margins
3. **Sports teams:** Trademark nightmares
4. **Band/celebrity:** Instant takedown

### The Niche Sweet Spot
- **Specific enough:** "Labrador Mom" not just "Dog Mom"
- **Large enough:** At least 10K monthly searches on Amazon
- **Passionate buyers:** People who identify strongly (occupations, faith, hobbies)
- **Low competition:** Under 1,000 existing designs

**How to validate:**
1. Search niche on Amazon/Etsy
2. Check number of results
3. Look at best-seller ranks
4. Check review counts (gauge sales volume)
5. If top results have <50 reviews = opportunity

## Design Creation Workflow (Canva Free Tier)

### Step 1: Set Up Canva
- Create free account (Canva.com)
- Use custom dimensions: 4500x5400px (standard t-shirt print area)
- Transparent background (PNG export)

### Step 2: Typography Design
- **Font pairing:** Bold headline font (Bebas Neue, Montserrat Bold) + script accent (Pacifico, Dancing Script)
- **Hierarchy:** Main phrase large, subtitle small
- **Spacing:** Generous padding, don't cram text

### Step 3: Add Simple Graphics
- Use Canva's free elements library
- Search: "line art," "minimalist," "icon"
- Avoid copyrighted clipart (Disney, Marvel, etc.)
- **Safe sources:** Flaticon.com (free tier), Noun Project (attribution)

### Step 4: Color Schemes
- **High contrast:** Black text on white, white text on black
- **Complementary colors:** Navy + coral, sage green + rust, mustard + charcoal
- **Avoid:** Neon (looks cheap), too many colors (messy)

### Step 5: Export
- Download as PNG
- Transparent background
- 4500x5400px minimum (high-res for printing)

### Time Per Design
- Simple text design: 10-15 minutes
- Text + icon: 20-30 minutes
- Complex layout: 45-60 minutes

**Goal:** Create 10 designs in one session (2-3 hours). Batch work is faster.

### Design Variations
Create multiple colorways:
- "Dog Mom" in black text → also create white text version, rose gold version, navy version
- Same design, 4 color options = 4 separate uploads = 4x revenue potential

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Too Broad
"Dog Lover" has 500K designs. "Bernese Mountain Dog Mom" has 200. Niche down.

### 2. Ugly Designs
Cramped text, bad fonts, low contrast. Use Canva templates as starting point.

### 3. Trademark Violations
Disney, Marvel, sports teams, brand names. Instant takedown + account risk.

### 4. Not Uploading Consistently
Upload 5-10 designs per week minimum. Consistency compounds.

### 5. Ignoring SEO (Etsy, Amazon)
Titles and tags matter. "Funny Dog Shirt" gets buried. "Golden Retriever Mom Shirt Gift for Dog Lover" ranks.

### 6. Giving Up Too Early
First 20 designs: crickets. Designs 30-50: a few sales. Designs 100+: consistent income. It's a numbers game.
