## The Naming Rules That Actually Matter

Before the audits, here's what the data says about app naming patterns across all successful consumer apps:

### What Top Apps Do

| Pattern | Examples | Why It Works |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| **One evocative word** | Calm, Forest, Strava, Hallow, Streaks, Tide, Pillow, Rise | Feels like a brand, not a feature list. Memorable. Clean App Store listing. |
| **Short action/state word** | Done, Focused, Centered, Session, Abide | Implies what you become, not what the app does |
| **Nature/object metaphor** | Flora, Forest, Tide, Pillow, Oak, Daylio | Emotional resonance without explaining mechanics |
| **Invented but phonetic** | Habitify, Mealime, Yummly, Strava, Whisk | Feels like a real word you haven't heard. Trademark-friendly. |
| **Person/character name** | Headspace, Noom, Fabulous, Woebot | Gives the app personality |

### What Failing Apps Do (The Cringe Patterns)

| Pattern | Examples | Why It Fails |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| **[Thing]Max/Maxx/Ultra/Pro/360** | SleepMax, FitPro, HealthUltra | Sounds like a supplement brand or infomercial product. The double-x makes it worse. |
| **Compound verb phrases** | WalkToUnlock, RunAndEarn, ScanToSave | Sounds like a feature description, not a brand. Nobody says "I use WalkToUnlock." |
| **[Thing]Lock/[Thing]Forge/[Thing]Hub** | FocusLock, HabitForge, ProductivityHub | Reads like a SaaS landing page from 2018. "Forge" is peak LinkedIn energy. |
| **Aggressive/bro suffixes** | GrindMaxx, HustleForge, BeastMode | Alienates 80% of the market. Reads like a preworkout flavor. |
| **AI/Smart/Bot prefix** | SmartSleep, AI Habits, BotTracker | Screams "I let ChatGPT name my app" |

### The "Would a normie say this out loud?" Test

If someone wouldn't comfortably say "I use [AppName]" to a coworker, the name fails.

- "I use Calm" -- natural
- "I use SleepMaxx" -- sounds like you're describing a mattress from a truck commercial
- "I use Forest" -- natural
- "I use HabitForge" -- sounds like you're LARPing as a blacksmith

### 1. PrayerLock

**VERDICT: AUTHENTIC**

**Niche Landscape (Actual Top Prayer Apps):**
- Hallow (Catholic, $100M+ raised, clean single word)
- Glorify (Christian, single evocative word)
- Abide (Christian, single evocative word)
- Muslim Pro (Muslim, functional name)
- Pray.com (domain-as-name)
- Lectio 365 (liturgical reference)
- iPray (dated but functional)
- Athan (Islamic call to prayer)
- Al Quran (direct reference)

**Why PrayerLock Works:**
- "Lock" implies commitment and discipline, which deeply resonates with faith communities
- The faith niche actually uses compound names more often than other niches (Muslim Pro, Pray.com, Prayer Mate)
- "Lock" is a mechanical metaphor that matches the app's core mechanic (phone locking during prayer)
- The faith community values accountability language. "Lock" = covenant language
- Two syllables. Clean. Says what it does without sounding generic

**Potential Concern:** The "Lock" suffix is overused in the broader app market. But within faith apps specifically, nobody uses it, so it actually differentiates.

**Alternative Names (If You Ever Wanted To Rebrand):**
1. **Selah** -- Hebrew/Arabic musical direction meaning "pause and reflect." Deep insider reference. 2 syllables. Beautiful.
2. **Vesper** -- evening prayer in Christian tradition. Single word. Evocative. Already some use.
3. **Adhan** -- similar to Athan but differentiated. The call to prayer itself.
4. **Vigil** -- implies watchfulness, dedication. Works cross-faith.
5. **Anchor** -- "daily anchor" already used in your social handle. Strong metaphor.

**Final Call:** Keep PrayerLock. It works. The mechanic-as-name approach (lock your phone to pray) is clear and differentiated within the faith app market.

## The "Maxx" Problem: A Deeper Analysis

Three of seven app names use "Maxx." This deserves its own section.

**The Maxxing Meme Lifecycle:**
- 2020-2022: "Looksmaxxing" emerges on lookism/incel forums
- 2023: Goes mainstream TikTok via jawline/mewing content
- 2024: Peak saturation. Everyone adds "-maxx" to everything. Becomes ironic.
- 2025: Still recognized but increasingly eye-roll territory. "Maxxing" is what brands say when they want to sound young.
- 2026 (now): It's the "on fleek" of self-improvement language. Insiders still use it, but it's no longer insider.

**The Problem with Meme-Based Naming:**
When a meme goes mainstream enough for brands to adopt it, the insiders have already moved on. You end up with a name that's too "internet" for normies and too "corporate trying to be internet" for the actual community.

**Exception: @PRINTMAXXER**
This handle works BECAUSE the "maxxer" suffix is part of the brand's deliberate ironic energy. The entire brand is "this is unhinged and I know it." SleepMaxx, MealMaxx, and BioMaxx don't have that self-awareness built in. They just sound like they're trying to be trendy.

**Rule of Thumb:** Use "maxx" once in your brand portfolio (the meta-brand itself). Using it 4 times makes you look like you only know one naming convention.

## The Brand Family Strategy

If you want a cohesive portfolio, you have two paths:

### Path A: The "Lock" Family
PrayerLock, Steplock, Vault (focus), SleepLock...
Unified by the mechanic: locking your phone to build habits.
This is clean and descriptive. Users immediately understand what the apps do.

### Path B: Individual Brands
Each app has its own identity based on niche naming conventions.
PrayerLock, Stride, Vault, Drift, Stack, Prep, Circadian.
More App Store optimized. Each name competes in its own niche rather than being part of an obvious portfolio.

### Path C: Hybrid
Keep the "Lock" name for apps that actually lock your phone (PrayerLock, Steplock, SleepLock).
Give independent names to apps that don't have a lock mechanic (Stack for habits, Prep for meals, Circadian for biohacking).

**My Recommendation: Path C.** The "lock" mechanic is your differentiator for the phone-locking apps. Own it. But don't force "Maxx" or "Lock" onto apps where it doesn't fit.

## Indie App Naming Trends 2025-2026 (Research Synthesis)

### data sources
Product Hunt launches (top 100 per quarter, 2024-2026), App Store "New Apps We Love" features, r/SideProject naming discussions, Indie Hackers community threads, YC batch company names, Hacker News Show HN posts.

### Trend 1: One-word real English words dominate

the most successful indie apps launched in 2024-2025 overwhelmingly use single real words:
- Arc (browser), Linear (PM), Notion (workspace), Raycast (launcher), Warp (terminal), Cursor (IDE), Craft (docs), Things (to-do), Camo (webcam), Loom (video), Grain (meeting notes), Pitch (presentations)

**why it works:** one real word is easier to remember, easier to search, easier to type, and carries emotional/conceptual weight. "Arc" implies curvature, trajectory, ambition. "Linear" implies simplicity, directness. the word does marketing work for free.

**the problem:** most good one-word .com domains are taken. solution: use .app, .so, .dev, or .co TLDs. the modern consumer does not care about .com vs .app for mobile products.

### Trend 2: Lowercase is the new premium

many 2024-2026 launches present their names in all-lowercase: linear, notion, raycast, arc, cal, loom. this signals confidence and modernity. all-caps (@PRINTMAXXER) works for a hustle brand but not for consumer apps trying to feel calm and premium.

**for your portfolio:** consider presenting app names in lowercase in all marketing materials. "drift" not "Drift." "mise" not "Mise." this is a visual branding choice, not a naming choice, but it affects perception.

### Trend 3: Nature and physical object metaphors are thriving

Forest (focus), Bloom (mental health), Opal (screen time), Flora (focus), Pillow (sleep), Bear (notes), Panda (reading), Oak (meditation). physical objects give apps tangibility and warmth in an increasingly digital world.

**for your portfolio:** Pantry, Simmer, Drift, Cocoon all fit this pattern. these names "exist in the physical world" which makes them feel more real than abstract compounds like HabitForge or FocusLock.

### Trend 4: Compound "NounVerb" and "VerbNoun" names are declining

the era of ScanPay, TaskDone, FocusKeeper, HabitBull is fading. these names feel like 2016-2019 App Store clutter. the exception is when the compound is genuinely clever or creates a new word (Superhuman, Basecamp, Headspace).

**for your portfolio:** PrayerLock is on the acceptable edge of this pattern because "Lock" is the core mechanic. WalkToUnlock, FocusLock, and HabitForge are firmly in the declining pattern.

### Trend 5: Meme-based naming has a 2-year shelf life

"Maxxing" as a suffix follows the same lifecycle as "on fleek" (2014-2016), "no cap" (2019-2021), and "bussin" (2021-2023). by the time a meme is adopted by brands, the insiders who originated it have moved on. the people who still say "looksmaxx" in 2026 are either: (a) deeply embedded in the community and using it sincerely, or (b) normies who picked it up late.

**the risk:** naming an app "SleepMaxx" in 2026 is like naming an app "SleepFleek" in 2018. it dates the product instantly.

**the exception:** @PRINTMAXXER works because the entire brand is self-aware about the meme. the consumer apps are not self-aware about it. they just sound like they are trying to be trendy.

### Trend 6: AI-generated naming patterns to avoid

Claude, GPT, and other LLMs have characteristic naming patterns that are becoming recognizable to consumers:
- "[Adjective][Noun]" with CamelCase (SmartFocus, QuickPrep, DeepHabit)
- Adding "AI" or "Smart" prefix (AI Meals, Smart Sleep, Smart Habits)
- Compound words that are too literally descriptive (WalkToUnlock, MealPlan, SleepTrack)
- Using "Hub," "Forge," "Lab," "Studio," "Factory" suffixes

if a human would not name their app this way, do not let AI name it this way.

### Trend 7: successful indie naming formula

**the pattern that works in 2025-2026:**
```
1 real English word (1-3 syllables)
+ the word should evoke a feeling or sensory experience
+ NOT the literal function of the app
+ available as .app domain
+ 7 characters or fewer for the icon
```

examples that fit this formula: Calm, Drift, Mise, Dusk, Nudge, Cocoon, Grain, Warp, Arc, Craft

## ASO/SEO Keyword Analysis Per Rename

### why this matters
App Store search accounts for 65-70% of app discovery. the app name carries the most weight in ASO. but you also have a 30-character subtitle field and a 100-character keyword field. the NAME does not need to contain every keyword -- it needs to be memorable AND searchable.

### strategy: brand name in title, keywords in subtitle

| App | Recommended Name | Title (30 chars) | Subtitle (30 chars) | Top 3 Keywords |
|-----|-----------------|-------------------|---------------------|----------------|
| PrayerLock | PrayerLock | PrayerLock | prayer timer & streaks | prayer timer, prayer streak, qibla compass |
| SleepMaxx rename | Drift | Drift | circadian sleep tracker | sleep tracker, circadian rhythm, blue light |
| WalkToUnlock rename | Steplock | Steplock | walk to unlock your day | step counter, walking tracker, fitness lock |
| FocusLock rename | Vault | Vault | deep work focus timer | pomodoro timer, focus app, deep work |
| HabitForge rename | Streakr | Streakr | daily habit streaks | habit tracker, streak counter, daily habits |
| MealMaxx rename | Mise | Mise | meal prep planner | meal planning, meal prep, recipe organizer |
| BioMaxx rename | Circadian | Circadian | light & rhythm tracker | circadian rhythm, sunlight tracker, sleep optimization |

**key insight:** the brand name (first word in title) does NOT need to be a keyword. it needs to be memorable. the subtitle handles keyword duty. Calm does not have "meditation" in its name. Forest does not have "focus" in its name. they use the subtitle for that.
