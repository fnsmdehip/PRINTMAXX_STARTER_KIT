## Why Government Contracts

- $700B+ in annual federal spending
- Small business set-asides: 23% of federal contracts reserved for small business
- Micro-purchases under $10K: no formal bidding required, simplified process
- Simplified acquisitions $10K-$250K: limited competition, faster awards
- State and local governments spend another $3.5T+ annually
- Most solopreneurs ignore this because it "seems complicated"
- Claude Code makes fulfillment 5-10x faster than traditional approaches
- Government pays on time (NET 30 standard, often NET 15)
- Recurring contracts: once you're in, renewals are easier than new business

## Step-by-Step Setup (Do This Week)

### Day 1: Registration (2-3 hours)

**1. Get a UEI (Unique Entity Identifier)**
- Go to: https://sam.gov/content/entity-registration
- Replaced the old DUNS number in 2022
- Free to obtain
- Takes 1-2 business days

**2. Register on SAM.gov**
- Go to: https://sam.gov
- Create account, complete entity registration
- Required for ALL federal contracts
- Free. Takes 7-10 business days for initial registration.
- Annual renewal required.
- Select your NAICS codes (key ones below)

**Relevant NAICS codes for our services:**
| Code | Description | Our Service |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| 541511 | Custom Computer Programming | Web dev, software |
| 541512 | Computer Systems Design | IT consulting |
| 541519 | Other Computer Related Services | AI/ML, data |
| 541611 | Administrative Management Consulting | Business consulting |
| 541613 | Marketing Consulting | Content, social media |
| 541810 | Advertising Agencies | Content creation |
| 541820 | Public Relations Agencies | Comms |
| 541910 | Marketing Research | Research reports |
| 541990 | Other Professional Services | Catch-all |
| 561410 | Document Preparation Services | Technical writing |
| 611430 | Professional Development Training | Training materials |

**3. Register on Additional Platforms**
- **GovWin** (Deltek): https://iq.govwin.com - contract intelligence ($99/mo but free trial)
- **FPDS** (Federal Procurement Data System): https://www.fpds.gov - historical contract data
- **USAspending.gov**: Free. Shows all federal spending. Use for competitive intel.
- **State procurement portals**: Each state has one. Start with your state.

### Day 2: Intelligence Gathering (2-3 hours)

**4. Set Up SAM.gov Alerts**
- Search for opportunities using NAICS codes
- Set email alerts for new postings matching your codes
- Filter by:
  - Set-aside type: "Total Small Business"
  - Contract value: "$10K-$250K" (simplified acquisition)
  - Place of performance: your state or "any"
  - Keywords: "technical writing", "web development", "data analysis", "content creation"

**5. Run FOIA Intelligence (from ALPHA015 @pipelineabuser)**
```
Steps:
1. Go to USAspending.gov
2. Search for contracts in your NAICS codes
3. Note: who won, what they charged, when it expires
4. File FOIA request for:
   - Losing bidders (these are hungry for alternatives)
   - Evaluation criteria (know exactly what they score on)
   - Budget breakdowns (know the real budget)
   - Winning proposal (see exactly how winner positioned)
5. Target: contracts expiring in 60-90 days (incumbent may not rebid)
6. Target: losing bidders as subcontract partners
```

FOIA request template:
```
Subject: FOIA Request - [Contract Number]

Dear FOIA Officer,

Under the Freedom of Information Act (5 U.S.C. 552), I request access to the
following records related to contract [CONTRACT NUMBER]:

1. The winning proposal (technical and pricing volumes)
2. Source selection evaluation documentation
3. Names of all offerors (winning and losing)
4. Award amount and period of performance

I am willing to pay reasonable duplication fees up to $25. Please contact me
if costs will exceed this amount.

I request a fee waiver as this information will contribute to public understanding
of government operations.

Thank you,
[Name]
[Contact Info]
```

**6. Scan State and Local Opportunities**
- Your state procurement portal
- City/county purchasing departments
- School districts (huge IT and content needs)
- Community colleges (training materials, web dev)
- Public libraries (tech modernization)

### Day 3-7: First Bid Preparation

**7. Create Capability Statement (1-pager, required)**

Every government buyer will ask for this. Format:

```
[COMPANY NAME] - CAPABILITY STATEMENT

CORE COMPETENCIES:
- Technical Writing & Documentation (SOPs, manuals, reports)
- Web Development & Digital Solutions (responsive sites, portals, apps)
- Data Analysis & Visualization (dashboards, reports, insights)
- AI-Powered Content Creation (training materials, marketing content)
- Research & Policy Analysis (deep research, literature reviews)

DIFFERENTIATORS:
- AI-augmented delivery: 3-5x faster turnaround vs traditional consultancies
- Solopreneur overhead: competitive pricing, direct communication
- Modern tech stack: cloud-native, accessible, mobile-responsive
- Rapid prototyping: working demos in days, not months

PAST PERFORMANCE:
[List any relevant projects - even non-government work counts for first bids]

COMPANY DATA:
UEI: [Your UEI]
CAGE Code: [Assigned during SAM registration]
NAICS: 541511, 541519, 541611, 541910, 561410
Small Business: Yes
[State] Certified: [If applicable]

CONTACT:
[Name, email, phone]
```

**8. Identify First 5 Opportunities to Bid On**

Start with:
- **Micro-purchases** ($0-$10K): Government Purchase Card buys. No formal bidding. Find by networking with contracting officers.
- **Simplified acquisitions** ($10K-$250K, small business set-aside): Limited competition. RFQ (Request for Quote) format, shorter proposals.
- **Task orders** under existing IDIQs: Subcontract to a prime contractor. They handle compliance, you do the work.

Search terms on SAM.gov:
```
"technical writing" + "small business set-aside"
"website redesign" + "small business"
"data analysis" + "simplified acquisition"
"content development" + "small business"
"training development" + "small business"
```

### Day 7-14: Submit First Bid

**9. Proposal Structure (for simplified acquisitions)**

Simplified acquisition proposals are SHORT. Don't over-engineer.

```
1. COVER LETTER (1 page)
   - Reference solicitation number
   - State you meet all requirements
   - Highlight key differentiator

2. TECHNICAL APPROACH (3-5 pages)
   - Understanding of requirement
   - Approach and methodology
   - Timeline/milestones
   - Quality assurance plan

3. PAST PERFORMANCE (1-2 pages)
   - 2-3 relevant projects
   - Client contact info
   - Results achieved

4. PRICING (1-2 pages)
   - Firm-fixed price breakdown
   - Labor categories and rates
   - Any ODCs (Other Direct Costs)

5. REQUIRED ATTACHMENTS
   - Capability statement
   - SAM.gov registration proof
   - Any certifications
```

- [ ] 8(a) certification (if minority-owned, takes time)
- [ ] GSA Schedule contract (for larger, ongoing work - 6-12 month process)

### FAR (Federal Acquisition Regulation) Basics

Key FAR clauses you'll see in every contract:
- **FAR 52.212-4**: Standard commercial terms
- **FAR 52.219-1**: Small business program representations
- **FAR 52.222-3**: Convict labor prohibition
- **FAR 52.222-26**: Equal opportunity
- **FAR 52.232-33**: Payment by EFT (direct deposit)

Don't let FAR scare you. For simplified acquisitions, it's mostly standard clauses. Read them, understand the basics, comply.

## Subcontracting Strategy (Fastest Path to Revenue)

**Don't bid on your own first.** Subcontract to a prime.

Why:
- Prime handles all compliance/paperwork
- You just do the work
- Lower risk, faster payment (often)
- Builds past performance for future solo bids

**How to find primes looking for subs:**
1. SubNet on SBA.gov: https://eweb1.sba.gov/subnet/
2. SAM.gov subcontracting directory
3. LinkedIn: search "[NAICS code] government contractor"
4. Attend virtual matchmaking events (SBA hosts these)
5. GovWin: shows which primes are bidding on what

**Pitch to primes:**
```
Subject: Small Business Subcontractor - [Your Specialty] - [NAICS Code]

Hi [Name],

I'm [Name], a small business specializing in [technical writing / web development / data analysis]. I noticed [Company] holds contracts in [NAICS code] and wanted to explore subcontracting opportunities.

I can deliver [specific capability] at competitive rates with fast turnaround. AI-augmented workflow means I deliver in days what traditionally takes weeks.

Happy to share my capability statement and discuss how I can support your team on upcoming proposals.

[Name]
[UEI: XXXX]
[NAICS: XXXX]
```
