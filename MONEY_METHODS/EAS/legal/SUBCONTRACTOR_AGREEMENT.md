## 2. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR RELATIONSHIP

### 2.1 Status

Contractor is an independent contractor and is **not** an employee, partner, agent, or joint venturer of EAS. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment relationship, partnership, or agency between the Parties.

### 2.2 Tax Obligations

Contractor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes arising from compensation received under this Agreement, including self-employment taxes. EAS will issue IRS Form 1099-NEC to Contractor for compensation paid in each calendar year as required by law.

### 2.3 No Benefits

Contractor is not entitled to any employee benefits including but not limited to: health insurance, retirement plans, paid time off, workers' compensation, unemployment insurance, or any other benefits that EAS provides to its employees.

### 2.4 Equipment and Workspace

Contractor shall provide their own equipment, tools, software, internet access, and workspace necessary to perform the Services. EAS may provide access to EAS-owned software tools and platforms as needed for specific projects, but such access does not create an employment relationship.

### 2.5 Schedule

Contractor controls their own schedule and working hours. EAS does not dictate when or where Contractor performs the work, provided that Contractor meets all agreed-upon deadlines and milestones. Contractor may work for other clients simultaneously, subject to the restrictions in Sections 6 and 7.

## 3. SERVICES

### 3.1 Project Assignments

EAS will assign projects to Contractor via written Project Assignment documents that reference the applicable client SOW and specify:

- Project name and client (identified by code name until NDA execution)
- Assigned deliverables and responsibilities
- Applicable EAS playbook(s)
- Timeline and milestone deadlines
- Compensation for the assignment

Contractor may accept or decline any Project Assignment at their discretion. Acceptance of a Project Assignment creates a binding obligation to complete the assignment in accordance with this Agreement.

### 3.2 Playbook Compliance

Contractor agrees to follow EAS's designated playbooks, quality standards, and delivery processes for each assigned project. Playbooks define the methodology, templates, quality checkpoints, and deliverable formats to be used. Deviation from assigned playbooks requires prior written approval from EAS.

### 3.3 No Authority to Bind

Contractor shall not represent themselves as an employee or agent of EAS. Contractor shall not enter into agreements, make commitments, or incur obligations on behalf of EAS or any EAS client without express written authorization.

Compensation for each Project Assignment is milestone-based, not hourly. The total project fee for each assignment is specified in the applicable Project Assignment document.

| Milestone | Percentage | Trigger |

### 4.3 Payment Terms

Expenses are not reimbursed unless pre-approved in writing by EAS. Pre-approved expenses require itemized receipts and will be reimbursed at cost with no markup.

### 4.5 No Guaranteed Volume

This Agreement does not guarantee any minimum number of Project Assignments. EAS assigns projects at its discretion based on project needs, Contractor availability, and past performance.

## 5. NON-DISCLOSURE AND CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed to Contractor by EAS or by EAS clients in connection with Project Assignments, including but not limited to:

(a) **Client Information:** Client business data, customer lists, financial information, operational processes, strategies, and any information obtained during project execution;

(b) **EAS Business Information:** EAS pricing, fee structures, profit margins, client lists, pipeline information, and business strategies;

(c) **EAS Methodology:** EAS playbooks, templates, frameworks, tools, training materials, proprietary processes, and standard operating procedures;

(d) **Proprietary Tools:** Any software, scripts, automation workflows, or technical tools developed by or proprietary to EAS; and

(e) **Observed Information:** Information learned through observation, conversation, or participation in EAS operations, even if not formally documented or designated as confidential.

### 5.2 Obligations

Contractor shall:

(a) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing Services under this Agreement;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent from EAS;

(c) Protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care Contractor uses for their own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care;

(d) Not copy, reproduce, or store Confidential Information except as necessary to perform the Services; and

(e) Return or destroy all Confidential Information upon termination of this Agreement or upon EAS's request.

### 5.3 Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Contractor;

(b) Was known to Contractor prior to disclosure, as demonstrated by written records;

(c) Is independently developed by Contractor without use of Confidential Information; or

(d) Is required to be disclosed by law or court order, provided Contractor gives EAS prompt written notice and cooperates in seeking a protective order.

### 5.4 Duration

The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of this Agreement and remain in effect for three (3) years following the date of termination, or for so long as the information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law, whichever is longer.

## 6. NON-COMPETE

### 6.1 Client Non-Solicitation

During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months following the termination of Contractor's involvement in each specific client engagement, Contractor shall not directly solicit, accept, or perform work for any EAS client that was introduced to Contractor through an EAS engagement, where such work is substantially similar to the services provided under the EAS engagement.

### 6.2 Scope Limitations

This non-compete provision is intentionally narrow in scope:

(a) **Client-specific:** The restriction applies only to specific clients with whom Contractor worked through EAS, not to the industry at large.

(b) **Time-limited:** The twelve (12) month period runs from the completion or termination of Contractor's involvement in each specific client engagement, not from the termination of this Agreement as a whole.

(c) **Activity-limited:** The restriction applies only to services substantially similar to those provided under the EAS engagement. Contractor is not restricted from providing unrelated services to such clients.

(d) **No geographic restriction:** There is no geographic limitation, as EAS clients are nationwide and services are delivered remotely.

(e) **No general industry restriction:** Contractor is free to perform automation consulting, AI implementation, or any other services for any other clients, companies, or organizations not introduced through EAS.

### 6.3 Enforceability

The Parties acknowledge that this non-compete provision is reasonably necessary to protect EAS's legitimate business interests in its client relationships and is not unduly restrictive of Contractor's ability to earn a livelihood. If any court of competent jurisdiction determines that this provision is overly broad, the Parties agree that the court shall reform the provision to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable.

## 7. NON-SOLICITATION

### 7.1 Restriction

During the term of this Agreement and for twenty-four (24) months following termination, Contractor shall not actively solicit, recruit, or induce any EAS employee, contractor, or client to terminate their relationship with EAS.

### 7.2 Inbound Inquiries

This restriction applies to active solicitation only. If an EAS employee, contractor, or client independently contacts Contractor without solicitation, responding to such inbound inquiry does not constitute a violation of this Section 7. However, Contractor shall not encourage, suggest, or facilitate such inbound contact.

### 7.3 Non-Circumvention

Contractor shall not, directly or indirectly, bypass, circumvent, or attempt to circumvent EAS to deal directly with any party introduced through EAS engagements, including but not limited to clients, vendors, partners, subcontractors, and service providers. This obligation survives termination for twenty-four (24) months.

### 7.4 Liquidated Damages

The Parties acknowledge that a breach of Sections 5 (Confidentiality), 6 (Non-Compete), or 7 (Non-Solicitation/Non-Circumvention) would cause EAS irreparable harm that is difficult to quantify. Accordingly, in the event of any such breach, Contractor agrees to pay EAS liquidated damages in the amount of [FIFTY THOUSAND DOLLARS ($50,000)] per violation, in addition to any other remedies available to EAS at law or in equity. This liquidated damages provision represents a genuine pre-estimate of EAS's likely loss and is not a penalty.

### 7.5 Technical Access Revocation

Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, EAS shall immediately revoke Contractor's access to all EAS systems, including but not limited to: API key proxy access, workflow platforms, code repositories, project management tools, communication channels, and client-related systems. Contractor acknowledges that all EAS-provided tools, platforms, and API access are the property of EAS and that Contractor has no independent right to access, copy, or replicate such systems after termination.

## 9. QUALITY STANDARDS

### 9.1 Playbook Adherence

Contractor shall perform all work in accordance with the EAS playbook designated for each Project Assignment. The playbook defines the required methodology, deliverable templates, naming conventions, documentation standards, and quality criteria.

### 9.2 Quality Checkpoints

Each Project Assignment includes three (3) quality checkpoints at which EAS reviews Contractor's work for conformance with playbook standards and project requirements:

| Checkpoint | Timing | Review Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **QC-1** | After discovery/setup | Requirements capture, tool access, approach validation |
| **QC-2** | Draft delivery | Deliverable completeness, accuracy, playbook adherence |
| **QC-3** | Pre-launch / final | End-to-end testing, documentation, client readiness |

### 9.3 Defect Remediation

If EAS identifies defects or quality issues at any checkpoint, Contractor shall remediate such defects within forty-eight (48) hours of written notification. "Defect" means any deviation from the playbook requirements, project specifications, or reasonable professional standards.

### 9.4 Quality Failure Escalation

Quality performance is tracked across Project Assignments:

- **First quality failure:** Written notice and documented remediation plan.
- **Second consecutive quality failure:** Written warning and mandatory review call.
- **Third consecutive quality failure:** Grounds for immediate termination for cause under Section 10.2.

A "quality failure" means a deliverable that fails to pass a quality checkpoint after one remediation attempt.

### 9.5 Client Feedback

EAS may share relevant client feedback with Contractor for improvement purposes. Contractor shall incorporate constructive feedback into subsequent work.

## 10. TERMINATION

### 10.1 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement with fourteen (14) days' prior written notice. If Contractor has an active Project Assignment at the time of termination, Contractor shall complete the current milestone before the termination takes effect, unless EAS agrees otherwise in writing.

### 10.2 Termination for Cause

EAS may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Contractor:

(a) Commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within seven (7) days of written notice;

(b) Accumulates three (3) consecutive quality failures as defined in Section 9.4;

(c) Engages in dishonesty, fraud, or misrepresentation in connection with the Services;

(d) Is the subject of a client complaint that is upheld after EAS's good-faith investigation;

(e) Violates the confidentiality, non-compete, or non-solicitation provisions of this Agreement; or

(f) Fails to meet milestone deadlines on two (2) or more consecutive Project Assignments without prior written notice to EAS of extenuating circumstances.

### 10.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:

(a) Contractor shall immediately cease all work on active Project Assignments (unless completing a current milestone under Section 10.1).

(b) Contractor shall deliver to EAS all Work Product, including work in progress, within five (5) business days.

(c) EAS shall pay Contractor for all completed and EAS-approved milestones as of the termination date. No payment is due for incomplete or unapproved milestones.

(d) Contractor shall return or destroy all Confidential Information, client data, and EAS materials within five (5) business days and provide written confirmation.

(e) Sections 5, 6, 7, 8, 11, and 14 shall survive termination.

## 14. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 14.1 Good Faith Negotiation

The Parties agree to attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising out of this Agreement through direct discussion for a period of fifteen (15) days from written notice of the dispute.

### 14.2 Binding Arbitration

If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation, the dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered in accordance with the Wyoming Arbitration Act (Wyo. Stat. Ann. 1-36-101 et seq.). The arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator in Cheyenne, Wyoming (or such other location as mutually agreed, including by video conference). The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding.

### 14.3 Attorneys' Fees

The prevailing Party in any arbitration or legal proceeding shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing Party.

### 14.4 Injunctive Relief

Nothing in this Section 14 prevents either Party from seeking injunctive or equitable relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to protect its rights under Sections 5, 6, 7, or 8 of this Agreement.

### 14.5 Forum Selection

For any dispute not subject to arbitration (including requests for injunctive relief), the Parties irrevocably consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in the State of Wyoming. Contractor waives any objection to venue in such courts, including any argument that such courts are an inconvenient forum.

### 14.6 Equitable Relief

Contractor acknowledges that a breach of Sections 5, 6, or 7 would cause irreparable harm to EAS and that monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, EAS shall be entitled to seek temporary restraining orders, preliminary injunctions, and permanent injunctions to enforce such provisions, without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting bond (to the extent permitted by law).
