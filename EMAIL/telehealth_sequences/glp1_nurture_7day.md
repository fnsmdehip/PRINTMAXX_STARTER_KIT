## Day 3: Side Effects - The Honest Version

**Subject A:** GLP-1 side effects: what the brochure leaves out
**Subject B:** I asked 200+ GLP-1 users about side effects. Here's what they said.

**Preview text:** Nausea is the headline. But there are 4 more you should know about.

**Body:**

{{FIRST_NAME}},

Every GLP-1 program says "mild nausea, usually subsides in a few weeks." That's true. It's also incomplete.

Here's the full picture from clinical data and real user reports:

**The common ones (30-50% of users):**
- Nausea. Peaks in weeks 1-3, usually fades by week 6. Eating smaller meals helps.
- Constipation or diarrhea. GI system is adjusting. Fiber and water fix most of it.
- Injection site reactions. Minor redness. Goes away on its own.

**The ones nobody mentions until you're already on it (10-20%):**
- Fatigue. Your body is burning fewer calories and eating less. You'll feel tired for 2-4 weeks.
- Muscle loss. This is the big one. You lose weight, but 30-40% of it can be muscle if you don't resistance train. Every good program should mention this. Most don't.
- "Ozempic face." Rapid fat loss in the face. Happens more at higher doses.
- Sulfur burps. Tirzepatide specifically. Harmless but annoying.

**The serious ones (rare, <1%, but you should know):**
- Pancreatitis. If you have a history, tell your provider.
- Gallstones. Rapid weight loss increases risk. Know the symptoms.
- Thyroid concerns. Contraindicated if you have a history of medullary thyroid carcinoma.

Here's what separates a good program from a bad one: a good program tells you all of this BEFORE you start, monitors your blood work, and adjusts your dose when side effects are too much.
