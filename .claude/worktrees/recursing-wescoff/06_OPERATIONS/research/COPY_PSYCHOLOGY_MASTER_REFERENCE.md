# Copy Psychology Master Reference

**Purpose:** Battle-tested psychological frameworks for content that converts
**Use:** Reference when writing posts, threads, emails, landing pages, CTAs

## REPLY BAIT FRAMEWORKS

### The "Comment Keyword" Funnel

**Pattern:** Give value → Create curiosity gap → Offer more via keyword comment

**Template:**
```
[Hook with result]

[3-5 bullet value points - enough to be useful, not enough to fully replicate]

reply "[KEYWORD]" and I'll send you [the full breakdown/template/guide]
```

**Why it works:**
- Comments boost algorithmic reach 10x vs likes
- Each reply = notification to replier's followers
- Creates lead qualification (people who engage = warm leads)
- Reply is lower friction than clicking link

**Examples:**
```
"I scraped 74 YouTube automation accounts.

the stack that prints:
- ChatGPT (free)
- ElevenLabs ($5/mo)
- Leonardo AI (free)

reply 'STACK' for the full breakdown"
```

```
"$17K/month from videos of people sleeping.

the channel: Hollow Stares
the niche: paranormal compilations
the strategy:

reply 'PARANORMAL' and I'll DM the full analysis"
```

### The Self-Reply Thread

**Pattern:** Hook post → Self-reply with deeper content → Final reply = product CTA

**Structure:**
1. Tweet 1: Hook (result or contrarian take)
2. Tweet 2-5: The "how" (value, but not everything)
3. Tweet 6: CTA (product, DM offer, or call to action)

**Why it works:**
- Algorithms favor threads with engagement
- Each tweet = new surface area for discovery
- Builds momentum toward CTA
- Self-replies often outperform original

### The Contrarian Hot Take

**Pattern:** Disagree with common belief → Provide reasoning → Invite debate

**Template:**
```
[common advice] is wrong.

here's what actually works:

[your take with specifics]

disagree? reply with your experience.
```

**Why it works:**
- Disagreement triggers engagement
- "Prove me wrong" energy drives comments
- People share to support OR refute
- Controversy without being offensive

**Examples:**
```
"VidIQ and TubeBuddy are useless in 2026.

Viewstats and 1of10 are what the winners use now.

the algorithm changed. the tools didn't.

fight me in the replies."
```

### The "DM Me" Qualifier

**Pattern:** Specific offer → DM keyword → Only serious people engage

**Template:**
```
[specific service/offer description]

DM me "[KEYWORD]" if you want:
- [specific outcome 1]
- [specific outcome 2]
- [specific outcome 3]

only doing [X] this month.
```

**Why it works:**
- DMs = highest intent leads
- Keyword filters out tire-kickers
- Scarcity creates urgency
- Direct path to conversion

## SALES PSYCHOLOGY TRIGGERS

### Loss Aversion (2x stronger than gain)

**People fear losing more than they desire gaining.**

**Application:**
- "You're losing $X every month by not doing this"
- "Most creators are leaving money on the table"
- "Your competitors are already doing this"

**Don't say:** "You could make $10K"
**Say:** "You're losing $10K every month by not doing this"

### Social Proof (Following the crowd)

**People do what others do, especially similar others.**

**Application:**
- Specific numbers: "74 accounts use this stack"
- Named examples: "@levelsio built $79K MRR this way"
- Relatable peers: "Creators with <10K followers are making..."

**Hierarchy of proof:**
1. Named, verifiable examples (strongest)
2. Specific numbers with screenshots
3. Aggregate statistics
4. Vague "many people" (weakest - avoid)

### Curiosity Gap (Open loops)

**Incomplete information creates psychological tension.**

**Application:**
- Start with result, withhold method
- "Here's what no one's telling you..."
- "The part they don't share..."
- Self-replies that promise "more below"

**Don't close loops in the hook. Open them.**

### Authority (Expertise signals)

**People follow perceived experts.**

**Application:**
- Specific numbers signal expertise
- Industry jargon (used correctly)
- "I've tested 50+ tools..."
- Screenshots of results

**Don't claim authority. Demonstrate it with specifics.**

### Reciprocity (Give to get)

**When you give value first, people feel obligated to give back.**

**Application:**
- Free value in posts (reply bait works because of this)
- "Usually I charge $81, but today it's free"
- Lead magnets before selling

**The more specific your free value, the more obligation you create.**

### Scarcity (Limited availability)

**Rare things are more valuable.**

**Application:**
- "Only doing 5 this month"
- "Free for 24 hours only"
- "Last day to join"
- Beta access, early bird

**Real scarcity > fake scarcity. People can smell BS.**

### Commitment Consistency (Small yes → big yes)

**People want to be consistent with past actions.**

**Application:**
- Get small engagement first (like, comment, follow)
- Then bigger ask (DM, sign up, buy)
- "If you liked this, you'll love..."

**The reply bait keyword is a micro-commitment.**

## CONTROVERSY FRAMEWORKS (Safe Controversy)

### The Spicy But Defensible Take

**Pattern:** Opinion that's controversial but you can back up with data

**Safe zones:**
- Tool preferences ("VidIQ is dead")
- Strategy debates ("Long form > shorts for revenue")
- Industry observations ("Most courses are scams")

**Avoid:**
- Politics (unless your niche)
- Personal attacks
- Anything you can't defend with specifics

### The "Unpopular Opinion" Format

**Template:**
```
unpopular opinion: [contrarian take]

here's why:

[reasoning with specifics]

[invite engagement]
```

**Examples:**
```
"unpopular opinion: faceless YouTube is harder than showing your face

- no personal brand moat
- easier to copy
- algorithm favors personality

the money is real but the competition is brutal. change my mind."
```

### The "They Won't Tell You This" Exposé

**Pattern:** Reveal something the industry gatekeeps

**Template:**
```
[gurus/experts/industry] won't tell you this:

[insider knowledge]

why they hide it:

[incentive explanation]
```

**Examples:**
```
"YouTube gurus won't tell you this:

most of their revenue is from selling courses, not from YouTube.

the channels are proof-of-concept for the course, not the business model.

follow the money."
```

### The Pattern Interrupt

**Pattern:** Start with something that doesn't match expectations

**Examples:**
- Starting with failure: "I lost $40K last month. Here's what happened..."
- Counter-intuitive: "Stop posting every day. Here's why..."
- Admission of wrong: "I was wrong about [thing I said before]..."

## HOOK FORMULAS

### The Consequence-First Hook

**Pattern:** Start with what happened, not what you'll explain

**Don't:** "I'm going to explain how to make $10K/month"
**Do:** "$10K/month from a channel with 25 videos. here's the breakdown."

### The Specificity Hook

**Numbers > adjectives. Specifics > vague.**

**Don't:** "A lot of money from YouTube"
**Do:** "$17,109/month from paranormal compilations"

### The Contrast Hook

**Pattern:** Unexpected comparison that creates curiosity

**Examples:**
- "This 7-day-old channel is outperforming 3-year-old channels"
- "$5/month tool stack making $50K/month"
- "925 subscriber channel, $50K video"

### The "Here's What" Hook

**Pattern:** Direct promise of valuable information

**Examples:**
- "Here's what no one's telling you about..."
- "Here's the exact stack..."
- "Here's why [common thing] doesn't work..."

## CTA FRAMEWORKS

### The Soft CTA (For trust building)

**Pattern:** Offer value, don't demand action

**Examples:**
- "link in bio if you want more"
- "I wrote a full guide (link in profile)"
- "Bookmark this for later"

### The Direct CTA (For conversion)

**Pattern:** Clear instruction with specific outcome

**Examples:**
- "Reply 'STACK' and I'll DM you the full guide"
- "Click the link. Buy the template. Make money."
- "DM me 'AUDIT' for a free channel review"

### The Urgency CTA (For action NOW)

**Pattern:** Time or quantity limit

**Examples:**
- "Only available for 24 hours"
- "First 10 people only"
- "Taking this down tomorrow"

### The Qualifier CTA (For high-intent leads)

**Pattern:** Make them prove they're serious

**Examples:**
- "DM me 'SERIOUS' if you actually want to do this"
- "Only DM if you have [specific requirement]"
- "Not for everyone. DM if you're ready to [action]"

## ENGAGEMENT AUTHENTICITY CHECKS

### Bot Detection Signals

| Red Flag | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| 10K likes, 5 comments | Botted likes |
| Comments are all generic | Engagement pod or bots |
| New account, huge followers | Bought followers |
| Engagement spikes then dies | Viral manipulation |

### Earnings Claim Skepticism

| Claim Type | Trust Level |
|------------|-------------|
| Screenshot of revenue dashboard | Medium (fakeable) |
| Specific non-round number | Higher (harder to fake) |
| "I make $50K/month" no proof | Low |
| Selling to people who want money | Very low |

**Rule:** Extract the METHOD even if numbers are BS. Tactics can be real even with inflated proof.

## QUICK REFERENCE CHEAT SHEET

### When writing a post:
1. Hook = consequence/result first
2. Body = specific value (not everything)
3. CTA = reply keyword or soft bio link

### When writing a thread:
1. Tweet 1 = hook (open curiosity gap)
2. Tweets 2-5 = value (close some gaps, open others)
3. Final tweet = CTA (close the sale)

### When writing a DM funnel:
1. Public post = reply bait with keyword
2. DM response = more value + qualifier question
3. Follow-up = offer with clear next step

### When writing landing copy:
1. Headline = biggest outcome
2. Subhead = who it's for + what they get
3. Body = specific features with proof
4. CTA = clear action + urgency
