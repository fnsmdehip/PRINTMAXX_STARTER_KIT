## What They're Doing

Ali Abdaal runs a massive education business teaching people how to become successful YouTubers. His Part-Time YouTuber Academy (PTYA) is a cohort-based course priced at $997-$4500 per student, with 500 students per cohort. He's generated $4.5M from PTYA alone since 2021, teaching 3K+ students. YouTube AdSense brings in $130K/mo, but his courses and products account for $350K+/mo.

## Funnel Map

YouTube Free Content (productivity, studying, tech reviews) → Email List / Newsletter → Free Course Lead Magnet → Part-Time YouTuber Academy Cohort ($997-4500) → Circle Community Access → Coaching Upsells / Advanced Products

## Monetization Stack

1. **Part-Time YouTuber Academy** - $497K-2.25M per cohort (500 students × $997-4500)
2. **YouTube AdSense** - $130K/mo
3. **Skillshare Courses** - Unknown but substantial (he has multiple top courses)
4. **Newsletter Sponsorships** - Unknown
5. **Podcast Revenue** - Unknown
6. **Affiliate Deals** - Tool recommendations, productivity products

**Total Est. MRR:** $350K+ from courses/products alone

## Unique Mechanism

**Part-Time YouTuber Academy** - The framing is critical. Not "YouTube course" but "Part-Time" = accessible to people with jobs. Not "how to go viral" but "systematic approach to building a channel while working." The cohort model creates urgency (limited seats) and community value (learn with peers, not alone).

## Replicable Frameworks

1. **Cohort-Based Course Model** - Instead of evergreen course, run cohorts with limited enrollment. Creates scarcity, urgency, and higher perceived value. Community becomes part of the product.

2. **YouTube SEO Strategy** - Ali's channel grew through search traffic, not virality. He optimized for keywords people actively search (e.g., "how to study effectively," "Notion tutorial"). Evergreen content that ranks = consistent discovery.

3. **Free Course to Paid Funnel** - Give away 80% of value free. Use free course as lead magnet to qualify leads. Only pitch paid cohort to people who completed free training = warmer audience.

4. **Multi-Platform Distribution** - YouTube is discovery, newsletter is nurture, podcast is authority building, Twitter/IG are amplification. Each platform serves a different funnel stage.

5. **Premium Pricing + Volume** - $997-4500 is high-ticket for online course, but 500 students per cohort = massive revenue. Price reflects transformation (starting a YouTube channel) not just information.

## Gap/Weakness

Ali's weakness: **generalist positioning.** He teaches productivity, YouTube, medicine, tech reviews, philosophy. He's not THE authority on any single thing. For PRINTMAXX, we can go DEEP on one-person businesses, app building, or content stacking. Niche focus = clearer value prop.

Also: **cohort model requires high touch.** Ali has a team of 16+ people. We can start with smaller cohorts (20-50 students) and automate more via pre-recorded content + async community + occasional live Q&A.

## Key Takeaway

The cohort model is a pricing arbitrage opportunity. People will pay 3-5x more for a cohort (community, accountability, limited enrollment) than an evergreen course (available anytime, solo consumption). Ali's PTYA at $997-4500 would be worth maybe $297 as evergreen. The cohort structure unlocks premium pricing + urgency.
