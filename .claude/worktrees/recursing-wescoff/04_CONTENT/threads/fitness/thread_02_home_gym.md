# Thread: Built a home gym for under $500

## Tweet 1 (Hook)
Gym was 20 minutes away. I was skipping too many sessions.

Built a home gym for $487 total.

Paid for itself in 10 months. Here's the exact setup:

## Tweet 2
The essentials I bought:

- Adjustable dumbbells (5-52 lbs): $299 (Amazon Basics)
- Pull-up bar (doorframe): $35
- Resistance bands (set of 5): $25
- Yoga mat: $20
- Dip bars (freestanding): $89
- Timer app: free

Total: $468

## Tweet 3
Exercises these cover:

- Squats (goblet, Bulgarian split)
- Presses (bench on floor, overhead, push-ups)
- Pulls (rows, pull-ups, band work)
- Deadlifts (single leg, RDLs)
- Core (everything)

Full body, no gaps.

## Tweet 4
What I skipped:

- Bench: Floor press works fine
- Barbell: Dumbbells hit the same muscles
- Cable machine: Bands do the job

You don't need commercial gym equipment to build muscle.

## Tweet 5
My weekly routine:

Monday: Push (chest, shoulders, triceps)
Wednesday: Pull (back, biceps)
Friday: Legs (quads, hamstrings, glutes)

45 minutes each session. In my garage. No commute.

## Tweet 6
The real math:

Gym membership: $50/month x 12 = $600/year
Home gym: $487 once

Broke even in month 10.

Year 2 and beyond: $0 ongoing cost.

Plus: No waiting for equipment. No 40-minute round trip.

## Tweet 7
Upgrades I added later (optional):

- Weight vest ($60): Makes pull-ups harder
- Kettlebell ($40): Swings and carries
- Foam roller ($25): Recovery

Still under $700 total. Still using it 3 years later.
