## Email 2: The practice

**Send timing:** Sunday, 6pm
**Week:** 2 of nurture cycle

### Subject line
One practice for anxious days

**A/B variants:**
- A: When anxiety won't let go
- B: The 60-second reset

### Preview text
I use this almost daily

### Body

Anxiety follows me. It probably follows you too.

Here's a practice that helps on the hard days:

**The 60-second reset:**

1. Stop whatever you're doing (10 seconds)
2. Take 3 slow breaths (15 seconds)
3. Name what you're anxious about, out loud if possible (10 seconds)
4. Say "I release this to You" (5 seconds)
5. Sit in silence (20 seconds)

That's it. 60 seconds.

I don't claim this erases anxiety. It doesn't. But it interrupts the spiral. It creates space.

Sometimes I do this 5 times in a day. No shame in that.

The goal isn't perfection. It's presence. Remembering that you're not alone in the anxiety.

Try it this week. When the anxiety spikes, take 60 seconds before reacting.

## Email 3: The question

**Send timing:** Sunday, 6pm
**Week:** 3 of nurture cycle

### Subject line
A question for your week

**A/B variants:**
- A: What are you avoiding?
- B: Honest question inside

### Preview text
Takes 2 minutes to answer

### Body

One question for your week:

**What conversation is God trying to have with you that you've been avoiding?**

Maybe it's about:
- A relationship that needs attention
- A decision you've been delaying
- A habit you know needs to change
- A calling you've been ignoring

I've noticed that God is patient. He'll keep bringing up the same thing until we engage with it.

Sometimes I avoid the conversation because I already know what He'll say. And I'm not ready to hear it.

But the avoiding doesn't make it go away. It just delays the peace that comes from obedience.

This week, find 10 minutes of quiet. Ask God: "What do you want to talk about that I've been avoiding?"

Then listen.

**A/B variants:**

- B: Grace for imperfect weeks

### Preview text
A word before Monday

### Body

Before the new week starts, a reminder:

You showed up this week. Maybe imperfectly. Maybe inconsistently. But you showed up.

God doesn't measure faithfulness by perfection. He measures it by direction.

Are you moving toward Him, even slowly? That counts.

Did you have moments of connection this week, even brief ones? That counts.

Did you think about Him at all during the chaos? That counts.

The enemy wants you to start Monday feeling like a failure. Like you need to "do better" to earn God's attention.

That's a lie.

God's grace isn't earned. It's given. And it's new every morning.

Start tomorrow knowing that. Let it change how you approach the week.
