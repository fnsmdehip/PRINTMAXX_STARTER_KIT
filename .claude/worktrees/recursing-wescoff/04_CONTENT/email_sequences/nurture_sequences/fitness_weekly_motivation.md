**Block 1: Lower body (10 min)**
- Bodyweight squats: 3 sets of 15
- Reverse lunges: 3 sets of 10 each leg

**Block 2: Upper body (10 min)**
- Push-ups: 3 sets of 10-15
- Pike push-ups: 3 sets of 8

**Block 3: Circuit (15 min)**
- 40 sec burpees
- 20 sec rest
- 40 sec mountain climbers
- 20 sec rest
- 40 sec plank hold
- 20 sec rest
- Repeat 5 rounds

**Cool down (5 min)**
- Static stretches, focus on legs and shoulders

Total: 45 minutes

Do this Monday, Wednesday, and Friday. Or whenever works for your schedule. Consistency beats timing.

## Email 2: The mindset shift

**Send timing:** Monday, 6am
**Week:** 2 of nurture cycle

### Subject line
The real reason you skip workouts

**A/B variants:**
- A: Not motivation. This.
- B: Why discipline fails

### Preview text
What actually works

### Body

Motivation is unreliable. You know this.

Discipline is better but still fails. You can't willpower your way through everything.

Here's what actually works: making the decision smaller.

When you skip a workout, the decision you're actually making is:

"I need to change clothes, drive to the gym, work out for an hour, shower, drive back, and then continue my day."

That's a big decision. Your brain says no.

Try this instead:

"I'll put on my workout clothes."

That's it. Just the clothes. If you still don't feel like working out after that, fine. Take them off.

But 90% of the time, once the clothes are on, you'll do something.

Even if it's just 15 minutes instead of 45. That's still a win.

The goal isn't perfect workouts. It's showing up more often than you skip.

## Email 4: The check-in

**Send timing:** Monday, 6am
**Week:** 4 of nurture cycle

### Subject line
How was last month?

**A/B variants:**
- A: Quick fitness check-in
- B: 4 questions for yourself

### Preview text
Honest assessment time

### Body

End of the month. Time to check in.

Answer honestly:

**1. How many workouts did you do last month?**
- 12+ = Great consistency
- 8-11 = Solid
- 4-7 = Room to improve
- 0-3 = Time to restart

**2. Did you try anything new?**
New exercise, new workout, new time of day? Variety helps motivation.

**3. What got in the way?**
Identify the pattern. Work? Family? Energy? Knowing the obstacle helps you plan around it.

**4. What's one small change for next month?**
Not a complete overhaul. One thing. Maybe it's adding 5 minutes to each workout. Maybe it's scheduling workouts in your calendar. One change.

Progress isn't linear. Some months are better than others.

What matters is that you keep going.
