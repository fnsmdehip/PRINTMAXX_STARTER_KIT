## Common mistakes to avoid

1. **Tool overload:** Don't list 50 tools. Pick the best 10-15.
2. **No examples:** Show exactly how you use each tool.
3. **Vague time savings:** "Saves time" is weak. "Saves 5 hours per week" is strong.
4. **Forgetting the learning curve:** Acknowledge setup takes time upfront.
5. **Overpromising:** "Automate your entire business" kills trust.

## Segmentation strategies

**By engagement level:**
- High engagement, no purchase: Send case study email
- Opened all, clicked course link: Offer payment plan
- Low engagement: Send survey asking what they need

**By use case:**
- Content creators: Focus on writing and video tools
- Service providers: Emphasize client management and proposals
- Ecommerce: Highlight customer service and email automation

**By technical skill:**
- Beginners: Emphasize easy setup and templates
- Advanced: Show integration possibilities and API access

## Common objections + how to handle

**"I don't have time to learn new tools"**
Response: Setup takes 15 minutes per tool. You'll save that in the first week.

**"AI content is low quality"**
Response: AI drafts, you edit. Faster than starting from scratch.

**"Too expensive"**
Response: Calculate time saved. At $50/hour, saving 10 hours per week = $500/week value.

**"I tried AI tools, they didn't work"**
Response: Most people use wrong tools or wrong prompts. Show specific examples.
