# Case study email templates (4 variations)

Use this for: Sharing client/customer success stories to build credibility and show what's possible. Demonstrates real results without hard selling.

## Overview

Send case study emails 1-2x per month. Rotate through these four formats. Show different angles of success. Under 400 words each.

Expected open rate: 40-60%
Expected click rate: 8-15%

---

**Example (Fitness niche):**

Subject: How Jessica finally lost the last 15 lbs

{{firstName}},

Quick case study about solving plateau problems.

**The situation:**
Jessica, 34, accountant, was stuck at 165 lbs for 6 months. Wanted to hit 150 lbs but nothing was working.

**Why it mattered:**
Felt like she was doing everything right but seeing zero progress. Started to think her body "couldn't" lose more weight. Considering giving up entirely.

**What didn't work:**
- Cutting calories lower (made her exhausted and binge on weekends)
- Adding more cardio (injured her knee)

**The solution:**
Track protein instead of calories. Hit 120g protein per day. Keep workouts the same. Stop restricting food types.

**Implementation:**
- Week 1-2: Just tracked protein, no other changes
- Week 3-4: Adjusted meals to hit target consistently
- Week 5-12: Continued with occasional diet breaks

**Timeline:** 12 weeks

**Result:**
Lost 14 lbs (164 to 150). Gained visible muscle. Energy increased. No more weekend binges.

**Unexpected benefit:**
Saved time (simpler tracking). Enjoyed food more (no restrictions). Maintained social life (eating out was fine if protein was high).

If you're dealing with a plateau, try tracking one thing (protein, steps, or sleep) before adding more restrictions.

**Example (SaaS/Consulting niche):**

Subject: Mike's 6-month journey to $10K MRR

{{firstName}},

Long-form case study: Mike's journey to $10K monthly recurring revenue.

**Background:**
Mike, 29, developer. Built a project management tool for freelancers. Launched with 0 customers. Goal: $10K MRR in 6 months.

**Month 1: $0 MRR**
Launched. Posted on Product Hunt. Got 200 signups, 3 paid customers ($97/month each = $291 MRR). Realized free tier was too generous. Lots of users, no revenue.

**Month 2: $873 MRR**
Changed pricing. Free tier more limited. Lost half the free users but gained 6 paid customers. Total: 9 customers. Started cold emailing freelancers.

**Month 3: $1,455 MRR**
First churn (2 customers canceled). Hurt, but not fatal. Added 8 new customers from cold email. Total: 15 customers at $97/month. Learned why people canceled, fixed those issues.

**Month 4: $2,716 MRR**
Raised price to $147/month for new customers. Grandfathered existing at $97. Added annual plan ($1,397/year). Got 3 annual customers = instant $4,191 cash. MRR from monthly: $2,716.

**Month 5: $4,899 MRR**
Finally felt some momentum. Referrals started coming. Added 18 customers (mix of $97 and $147 plans). Only 1 churn. Fixed a major bug that was blocking growth.

**Month 6: $10,473 MRR**
Hit goal. 71 customers total. Mix of grandfathered ($97), current ($147), and annual plans. Inbound started working. Cut cold email from 100/day to 20/day.

**Final results:**
- $10,473 MRR (exceeded $10K goal)
- 71 customers
- $147 average per customer
- 8% monthly churn rate
- 40% of new customers from referrals

**What made the difference:**
Month 4 pricing change. Raising price to $147 meant needing fewer customers to hit goal. Also filtered for serious users who stuck around longer.

**What Mike would do differently:**
"I should have started with cold outreach on day 1, not after launch. And I definitely underpriced initially. $147 should have been my starting price."

**Advice to someone starting today:**
"The first 10 customers are the hardest. After that, if your product works, referrals do a lot of the work. Focus on those first 10. Do things that don't scale. Reach out manually. Get feedback. Fix problems fast."

The journey isn't always linear. This is what real progress looks like.

## When to send case studies

- After welcome sequence (Days 10-14)

- When you have fresh wins
- In response to specific objections

**Frequency:**
- 1-2 per month in regular nurture
- More during launch periods
- Feature in sales sequences
- Share on social media too

## How to gather case studies

**Best sources:**
- Paying customers/clients
- Successful students
- Free users with notable wins
- Beta testers

**How to ask:**
"Hey [Name], saw you got [result]. Would you be open to me sharing your story? I'd feature you in my newsletter and tag you. Takes 10 minutes on your end."

**What to collect:**
- Before/after metrics
- Timeline
- What they tried before
- What worked
- Direct quotes
- Permission to share (written)

**Incentive:**
- Free exposure to your audience
- Link to their business
- Testimonial they can use
- Free access to paid product

---

## Making case studies credible

**Include:**
- Real names (first name + last initial if needed)
- Specific numbers and metrics
- Timeline (how long it took)
- What didn't work (shows honesty)
- Direct quotes in their voice
- Permission statement

**Avoid:**
- Vague results ("made more money")
- Unrealistic timelines ("in just 3 days")
- Only positives (no challenges mentioned)
- Stock photos
- Made-up testimonials (illegal and unethical)

---

## FTC compliance for case studies

**Required disclosures:**
- "Results not typical"
- "Individual results may vary"
- If compensated for testimonial, disclose it
- Can't imply everyone gets these results

**Safe approach:**
- Share real stories with real numbers
- Don't promise readers will get same results
- Show the work required
- Be honest about difficulty

**Example disclaimer:**
"This is [Name]'s actual result. Your results will vary based on your situation, effort, and implementation."

---

## Soft selling with case studies

**Don't:**
- Turn entire email into sales pitch
- Include aggressive CTAs
- Oversell your role in their success

**Do:**
- P.S. mention of how you helped
- Link to relevant paid resource
- Natural segue to offering

**Example soft sell:**

P.S. The strategy [Name] used is what I teach in [Product name]. If you want the same framework, check it out here: [link]

## Formatting for readability

**Use:**
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Bullet points for metrics
- Bold for key results
- Quotes for direct speech
- White space

**Avoid:**
- Long blocks of text
- Walls of metrics
- Tiny font sizes
- Complex formatting

**Mobile-first:**
- 60%+ read on mobile
- Test how it looks on phone
- Keep lines short
- Use clear hierarchy

## Tracking performance

**Key metrics:**
- Open rate: Target 40-60%
- Click rate: Target 8-15%
- Replies: Track engagement
- Conversions: Track sales/signups after

**What to test:**
- Different case study formats
- Subject line styles
- Length (short vs detailed)
- Before/after vs journey vs problem-focused

**Double down on:**
- Formats that get best open rates

## Common mistakes

1. **Too long:** Keep under 400 words
2. **Vague metrics:** "Got better results" is weak
3. **No permission:** Always ask before sharing
4. **Fake stories:** Illegal and destroys trust
5. **Only positives:** Show challenges too
6. **No takeaway:** Reader should learn something
7. **Too salesy:** Focus on the story, not your pitch
