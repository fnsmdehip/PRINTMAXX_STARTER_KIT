# Weekly value email templates (4 variations)

Use this for: Regular weekly newsletters that keep your audience engaged without selling. Build trust and stay top-of-mind.

## Overview

Send one value-first email per week. Rotate through these four templates. Each under 300 words. One clear takeaway.

Expected open rate: 35-55%
Expected click rate: 5-12%

---

---

**Example (AI/Productivity niche):**

Subject: Save 2 hours per week on emails

{{firstName}},

Quick tip for managing email overload.

**The problem:**
Spending 30+ minutes per day writing similar emails.

**The fix:**
Create a swipe file of your 10 most common email types. When you write a good one, save it. Next time, copy and customize in 2 minutes instead of writing from scratch.

**Why it works:**
Most business emails are 80% the same. You're recreating the wheel every time.

Tried this last month. Inbox time dropped from 90 minutes to 45 minutes per day.

Takes 20 minutes to set up your swipe file. Saves 2 hours per week after that.

[Your name]

P.S. Works for Slack messages too. Copy-paste > rewriting.

{{firstName}},

Quick story about overtraining.

**What I did:**
Trained 6 days per week for 8 weeks. Thought more volume = faster results.

**What happened:**
Lost strength. Constantly tired. Sleep got worse. Actually gained fat despite working out more.

**The lesson:**
Recovery matters more than volume. Your muscles grow during rest, not during workouts.

**What to do instead:**
3-4 hard sessions per week. Prioritize sleep and protein. Take rest days seriously.

## Sending cadence

**Weekly schedule:**
- Week 1: Quick tip (Template 1)

- Week 3: Lesson learned (Template 2)

- 12-1 PM: Lunch break
- 6-8 PM: Evening wind-down

Test your audience's patterns.

## Engagement tactics

**Encourage replies:**
- End with question
- Ask for their experience
- Invite them to share results
- Request feedback on topic

**Example endings:**
- "What's your biggest [problem]? Hit reply."
- "Tried this? Let me know how it goes."
- "What topic should I cover next week?"
- "Reply with one word: [your current situation]."

**Why replies matter:**
- Improves deliverability
- Builds relationship
- Generates content ideas
- Identifies potential customers

## Soft selling in nurture emails

**Never do:**
- Weekly product pitches
- Long sales copy in every email
- Aggressive CTAs
- Fake urgency

**Do occasionally (1-2x per month):**
- P.S. mention of product
- Natural segue to paid offering
- Link to relevant paid resource
- Soft reminder of how you can help

**Example soft sell:**

P.S. If you want the complete [topic] system, I built a course with [specific details]. [Link]. No pressure.

**When to sell harder:**
- Launch weeks
- Special promotions
- When they ask how to work with you
- Re-engagement campaigns

**3. Send day:**
- Tuesday vs Thursday vs Sunday

- Morning vs lunch vs evening

## Measuring success

**Key metrics:**
- Open rate: Target 35-55%
- Click rate: Target 5-12%
- Reply rate: Target 1-3%
- Unsubscribe rate: Keep under 0.5%

**Quality signals:**
- People reply to your emails
- Forward to friends
- Reference past emails
- Ask when next one is coming

**Red flags:**
- Opens declining over time
- High unsubscribes
- Zero replies
- Low clicks

If metrics drop, refresh content or survey audience.

---

## Handling unsubscribes

**Don't take it personally:**
- 0.3-0.5% unsubscribe per email is normal
- People's needs change
- Better engaged list than big list

**Make it easy:**
- Clear unsubscribe link
- One-click process
- No guilt trips
- No "Are you sure?" pages

**Learn from it:**
- Track which emails get most unsubscribes
- Survey people who leave (optional)
- Adjust content if pattern emerges

## Re-engagement for inactive subscribers

**If someone hasn't opened in 60 days:**

**Email 1: The check-in**
Subject: "Still interested?"
Body: "Haven't heard from you in a while. Still want these emails? Click here to stay on the list. No response = I'll remove you in 7 days."

**Why this works:**
- Cleans your list
- Re-engages interested people
- Improves deliverability

**If they re-engage:**
- Keep sending
- Note they're interested
- Maybe survey what they want

**If they don't:**
- Remove from list
- Better for everyone

## Growing your nurture list

**Best sources:**
- Lead magnets
- Content upgrades
- Webinar signups
- Free tool/resource
- Newsletter-only content

**Don't:**
- Buy email lists
- Add people without consent
- Scrape emails from internet
- Auto-subscribe trial users (ask first)

**Quality over quantity:**
- 1,000 engaged subscribers > 10,000 cold ones
- Focus on attracting right people
- Set expectations at signup
- Deliver what you promise

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Selling too often:** Nurture means provide value, not pitch
2. **Too long:** Most people skim. Keep it tight.
3. **No personality:** Generic tips are forgettable
4. **Irregular sending:** Weekly = expected. Monthly = forgotten.
5. **No call to action:** Give them something to do (even if just reply)
6. **Boring subjects:** Subject line determines if they open
7. **Not mobile-friendly:** 60%+ read on phone

## Monetization strategy

**Months 1-3:**
- Pure value, rare selling
- Build trust and relationship
- Establish expertise
- Grow list

**Months 4-6:**
- Occasional soft pitches (P.S. lines)
- Launch sequences for products
- Survey what they need
- Create based on feedback

**Months 6+:**
- Regular value + strategic selling
- Launch new products
- Affiliate recommendations
- Premium tier options

The nurture list becomes your most valuable asset. Treat it well.
