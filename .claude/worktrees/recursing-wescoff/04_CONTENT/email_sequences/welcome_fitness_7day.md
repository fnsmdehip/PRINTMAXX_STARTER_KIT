## What makes this sequence work

**Day 1-2: Credibility**
- Real transformation story
- Not a fitness model or gym owner
- Relatable struggle

**Day 3-4: Education**
- Identifies real problem (unsustainable plans)
- Provides practical solution
- Shows it's simpler than they think

**Day 5-6: Social proof + pitch**
- Results from normal people
- Clear offer with specific value
- Not pushy or scammy

**Day 7: More free value**
- Reinforces generosity
- Sets weekly email expectation
- Leaves door open for questions

---

## Performance benchmarks

Track these metrics:

**Expected rates:**
- Email 1 open: 70-85% (they just opted in)
- Email 2-3 open: 50-65%
- Email 4-7 open: 40-55%
- Download rate: 60-75% (Day 1 resource)
- Program conversion: 3-7%

**Red flags:**
- Opens below 35% after email 3: Subject lines too salesy or boring
- High opens but low clicks: Offer not clear or compelling
- High unsubscribes on pitch email: Too aggressive or mismatched audience

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Overpromising:** "Lose 20 lbs in 30 days" kills trust
2. **Too complicated:** They want simple, not advanced
3. **No personality:** Generic fitness advice is everywhere
4. **Pitching too hard:** Fitness audience is skeptical of scams
5. **Ignoring replies:** Engagement builds customers

---

## Segmentation ideas

**Based on engagement:**
- Opened all 7 but didn't buy: Send personal email asking what stopped them
- Bought within 3 days: Move to customer onboarding
- Clicked program link but didn't buy: Send payment plan option or testimonial

**Based on fitness level:**
- Beginners: Focus on simplicity and sustainability
- Intermediate: Emphasize progression and results
- Advanced: Offer specialized training or coaching

---

## Customization by offer type

**Digital product (course, program):**
- Emphasize transformation and time savings
- Show before/after results
- Include money-back guarantee

**Coaching (1-on-1 or group):**
- Focus on personalization and accountability
- Show client success stories
- Offer discovery call instead of direct purchase

**Physical product (equipment, supplements):**
- Demonstrate practical use
- Compare to gym membership cost
- Include risk-free trial

---

## After sequence

Move them to:
- Weekly newsletter (1 tip per week)
- Launch sequence (new product releases)
- Re-engagement sequence (if they go quiet)

Keep providing value. Most people won't buy immediately. They'll buy later when they're ready if you stay top of mind.
