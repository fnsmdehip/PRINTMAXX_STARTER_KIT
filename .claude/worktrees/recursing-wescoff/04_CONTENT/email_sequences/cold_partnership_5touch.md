# Partnership cold outreach (5 emails)

Use this for: Reaching out for strategic partnerships, collaborations, joint ventures, or affiliate relationships.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Value proposition opener |
| 2 | Day 4 | Specific partnership idea |
| 3 | Day 8 | Example partnership |
| 4 | Day 12 | Alternative approach |
| 5 | Day 16 | Break-up email |

Expected reply rate: 12-22%
Expected partnership discussions: 6-14%

---

## Email 1: Value proposition

**Subject:** Partnership idea for [Company]
**Subject B:** Potential collaboration with [Company]
**Preview:** Overlapping audiences. Might be worth exploring.

---

{{firstName}},

Quick partnership idea for [Company name].

I run [Your company/brand]. We help [your audience] with [your value prop].

Noticed our audiences overlap:
- Your customers: [their customer description]
- Our customers: [your customer description]
- Overlap: [what they have in common]

Been thinking about ways we could work together that benefit both audiences.

Worth a conversation?

[Your name]
[Your title]
[Calendar link]

P.S. No pitch. Just want to explore if there's a fit.

---

**Why it works:**
- Clear about what you do
- Shows audience alignment
- Collaborative tone (not asking for favor)
- Low-pressure invitation

---

## Email 2: Specific idea

**Subject:** Re: Partnership idea (see specific proposal)
**Subject B:** One way we could work together
**Preview:** Thought through a specific approach. Might work.

---

{{firstName}},

Following up on partnership idea.

Here's one specific approach:

**What:** [Specific partnership type: co-marketing, affiliate, integration, etc.]

**How it works:**
- [Step 1 of partnership]
- [Step 2 of partnership]
- [Step 3 of partnership]

**Value for [Company name]:**
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]

**Value for us:**
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]

**Effort:** [Time commitment or resources needed]

Similar thing with [other partner] drove [result] for both sides.

Open to exploring this?

[Calendar link]

[Your name]

---

**Why it works:**
- Concrete proposal (not vague "let's partner")
- Shows you thought it through
- Mutual value is clear
- Proof it works

## Email 3: Example partnership

**Subject:** How we partner with [Similar company]
**Subject B:** Example that might be relevant
**Preview:** Working with [Similar company]. Structure might work for you too.

---

{{firstName}},

Wanted to share a relevant example.

We've been partnering with [Similar company] for [timeframe].

**What we do:**
[Description of partnership structure]

**Results:**
- For them: [Metric 1], [Metric 2]
- For us: [Metric 1], [Metric 2]

**Time investment:** [Hours per month or similar]

Could see something similar working with [Company name] because [specific reason].

If you're open to it, let's schedule 20 minutes to explore.

[Calendar link]

[Your name]

P.S. Happy to intro you to [Similar company] to ask about their experience.

---

**Why it works:**
- Social proof from similar partner
- Specific results reduce risk
- Shows track record
- Offer to verify with current partner

## Email 4: Alternative approach

**Subject:** Different partnership angle

**Preview:** Original idea might not work. How about this?

---

{{firstName}},

Haven't heard back, so maybe [original partnership idea] isn't the right fit.

Different angle: [Alternative partnership structure]

This would be:
- [How it's different from first idea]
- [Lower commitment or different value]
- [Specific benefit to them]

Example:
[Company] does this with us. Takes them [time investment]. Generated [result] in [timeframe].

More their speed than full partnership.

Worth discussing?

[Calendar link]

[Your name]

---

**Why it works:**
- Shows flexibility
- Offers lower-commitment option
- Another proof point
- Keeps conversation alive

## Email 5: Break-up

**Subject:** Last one from me
**Subject B:** Calling it
**Preview:** Haven't heard back. Will stop emailing.

---

{{firstName}},

Haven't heard back, so I'm guessing partnership isn't a priority for [Company name] right now.

No problem. I'll stop emailing.

If anything changes and you want to explore working together, here's my calendar: [link]

Keep building.

[Your name]

P.S. If you know someone else at [Industry] companies who might be interested in partnerships, I'd appreciate the intro.

---

**Why it works:**
- Break-up emails get responses
- Respects their decision
- Leaves door open
- Asks for referrals

## Finding partnership prospects

**Where to look:**
- Companies serving same audience (non-competing)
- Complementary product/service providers
- Companies in your distribution channels
- Brands your customers already use
- Communities where your audience hangs out

**Good partner signals:**
- Similar company size/stage
- Active in partnerships already
- Visible online presence
- Growing customer base
- Values alignment

---

## Partnership types to pitch

**Co-marketing:**
- Webinar together
- Content collaboration
- Joint lead magnet
- Cross-promotion
- Value: Audience access

**Affiliate/Revenue share:**
- Promote their product to your audience
- They promote yours
- Commission on sales
- Value: Revenue + social proof

**Product integration:**
- Technical integration between products
- Embedded in each other's platforms
- Value: Product stickiness

**Content partnership:**
- Guest posts
- Podcast appearances
- Co-created resources
- Value: SEO + audience

**Distribution:**
- They resell your product
- You resell theirs
- White label
- Value: Market expansion

---

## Research before reaching out

**Minimum research (5 minutes):**
- What do they sell?
- Who are their customers?
- Do they already have partners?
- What partnerships have they done?
- Who decides on partnerships?

**Strong pitches reference:**
- Specific overlap in audience
- Gap in their offering you fill
- Mutual connection or partner
- Their recent growth or launch

---

## Personalization tactics

**Level 1: Basic**
- Company name
- Industry
- General value prop

**Level 2: Researched**
- Specific product/service they offer
- Named audience overlap
- Reference existing partner

**Level 3: Custom**
- Analyze their customer base
- Propose specific partnership structure
- Show detailed mutual value
- Include projected results

Use Level 3 for high-value partnerships. Level 2 for most.

## Subject line testing

**Test these patterns:**

**Direct:**
- "Partnership idea for [Company]"
- "Collaboration opportunity"
- "[Your Company] x [Their Company]"

**Specific:**
- "[Partnership type] partnership?"
- "Co-marketing idea"
- "[Specific benefit] for [Company]"

**Curious:**
- "Overlapping audiences"
- "Idea for [Company]"
- "Quick question about partnerships"

**Avoid:**
- "Amazing opportunity"
- All caps
- Too vague
- Desperate tone

## Timing and cadence

**Send schedule:**
- Email 1: Day 0 (Tuesday-Thursday best)
- Email 2: Day 4
- Email 3: Day 8
- Email 4: Day 12
- Email 5: Day 16

**Why 4-day spacing:**
- Partnership decisions take time
- Not pushy
- Catches them on different days
- Shows patience

**Best times:**
- Mid-week (Tuesday-Thursday)
- Mid-morning (9-11 AM)
- After lunch (2-3 PM)

## Response handling

**"Tell me more"**
→ Send partnership one-pager
→ Book call to discuss details

**"What's in it for us?"**
→ Break down specific value
→ Share results from similar partnerships

**"Not interested in partnerships"**
→ "What would make you consider one?"
→ Add to long-term nurture

**"Talk to [other person]"**
→ "Can you intro me?"
→ Or ask for their email

**"Maybe later"**
→ "When should I check back?"
→ Set reminder, add to follow-up list

---

## Partnership call framework

**30-minute structure:**

**Minutes 0-5: Intro**
- Brief background on both companies
- Set agenda
- Establish goals

**Minutes 5-15: Discovery**
- Their partnership goals
- What's worked before
- What hasn't
- Decision process

**Minutes 15-25: Proposal discussion**
- Walk through partnership idea
- Adjust based on their needs
- Discuss mutual value
- Address concerns

**Minutes 25-30: Next steps**
- If interested: Propose written agreement
- If not sure: Send examples/case studies
- If not interested: Thank and move on

---

## Partnership proposal essentials

**Include in written proposal:**
- Overview of both companies
- Partnership objective
- Specific activities/deliverables
- Timeline and milestones
- Resource requirements
- Success metrics
- Financial terms (if applicable)
- Next steps and approval process

**Keep it:**
- Under 3 pages
- Visually clean
- Specific (not vague intentions)
- Focused on mutual value

---

## Negotiation tips

**Be flexible on:**
- Specific tactics (as long as goal is met)
- Timeline (within reason)
- Resource split
- Testing period before full commitment

**Be firm on:**
- Mutual value (not one-sided)
- Clear metrics
- Written agreement
- Regular check-ins

**Red flags:**
- They want you to do all the work
- Vague on their contribution
- No clear decision maker
- Keep delaying without reason

---

## Partnership agreement basics

**Always include:**
- Scope of partnership
- Duration and renewal terms
- Each party's responsibilities
- Success metrics
- Financial terms (if any)
- IP and brand usage guidelines
- Termination clause
- Contact points

**Even for informal partnerships:**
- Email agreement at minimum
- Clear on expectations
- How to measure success
- How to exit if not working

---

## Objection handling

**"We don't do partnerships"**
→ "What's held you back before?"
→ Offer low-commitment test

**"Too busy right now"**
→ "When's a better time?"
→ Offer to do most of the work

**"Need approval from [person]"**
→ "Happy to present to them"
→ Or ask for warm intro

**"Worried about brand fit"**
→ Show existing partners
→ Offer test campaign first

**"What if it doesn't work?"**
→ Propose trial period
→ Clear exit terms

## Volume strategy

**To close 2 partnerships per quarter:**
- Need ~8-10 partnership calls
- Need ~80-200 cold emails
- Need ~5-10 emails per week

**Quality over quantity:**
- Better to target 50 perfect fits
- Than blast 500 random companies
- Partnership sales are higher touch

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Vague value prop:** "Let's collaborate" is meaningless
2. **One-sided:** Only benefits you
3. **No examples:** Show track record
4. **Too formal:** Partnerships are relationship-based
5. **No follow-up structure:** Keep momentum
6. **Wrong contact:** Email partnerships@ instead of decision maker
7. **Too complicated:** Start simple, scale later

## After partnership launches

**First 30 days:**
- Weekly check-in
- Track metrics closely
- Quick iterations

**First 90 days:**
- Monthly review
- Assess value delivered
- Plan next phase or scale

**Ongoing:**
- Quarterly business reviews
- Update agreement if needed
- Explore expansion opportunities

**If not working:**
- Honest conversation
- Try adjustments
- Exit gracefully if no fit

---

## Tracking partnership performance

**Key metrics:**
- Leads/customers generated
- Revenue attributed
- Content engagement
- Brand reach
- Time invested vs value

**Review frequency:**
- High-touch: Monthly
- Medium-touch: Quarterly
- Low-touch: Annually

**Decision criteria:**
- Keep if: Positive ROI, low effort, good relationship
- Adjust if: Value exists but structure needs work
- End if: One-sided, too much effort, no results

---

## Scaling partnerships

**At 3-5 partnerships:**
- Document processes
- Create partner toolkit
- Regular partner updates

**At 10+ partnerships:**
- Dedicated partner manager
- Partner portal
- Tiered partnership levels

**At 25+ partnerships:**
- Partner program
- Co-marketing budget
- Partner summit/event

---

## Turning partnerships into referrals

**After successful partnership:**
- Ask who else they know
- Offer to intro them to your partners
- Request testimonial
- Feature them in content

**Build ecosystem:**
- Partner directory on site
- Partner-only Slack
- Cross-referral rewards
- Annual partner summit

Good partners become your best source of new partners.
