## How to outsource A/B testing with AI as a solo founder

You're running a SaaS. Your landing page converts at 2%. Industry standard is 4%.

You could hire a conversion optimizer. That costs $5-15k per month.

Or you could use AI to do it for you.

Here's how.

## The AI-powered testing loop

Normal A/B testing takes weeks. You pick a variable. Run it for 2 weeks. Analyze. Iterate.

AI-powered testing runs in days. Here's why.

**Step 1: AI generates variations.**

You give ChatGPT or Claude your landing page copy. You ask: "Generate 5 variations of this headline that test urgency, clarity, benefit, and authority."

It generates 5 variations instantly. Cost: free (or $20/month Claude subscription).

**Step 2: AI runs the test.**

You set up tests in Google Optimize, Unbounce, or Variant. You don't write variations manually. You paste AI-generated ones.

Each variation gets 500 visitors. Let the tool handle traffic splitting.

**Step 3: AI analyzes results.**

You give the results to Claude. "Here's my test data. What's statistically significant? What should I test next?"

Claude tells you. "Variation 2 won with 4.8% conversion (vs 2.1% baseline). The pattern: social proof + urgency works. Next test benefit + risk reversal."

**Step 4: Repeat.**

Winning variation becomes your new baseline. AI generates 5 more. Run again.

In 3 weeks, you go from 2% to 3.5%. In 6 weeks, 4.2%.

## Tools for AI A/B testing

**Variation generation:**

Claude or ChatGPT. Prompt: "I need 10 landing page headline variations for [your product]. Each should test a different angle: urgency, benefit, social proof, authority, price value, exclusivity, simplicity, specificity, risk reversal, free trial."

Cost: Free or $20/month.
Quality: 8/10 variations are actually usable.

**Test running:**

Google Optimize (free), Optimizely ($1000+), Unbounce ($80-200/month), or Variant ($250-500/month).

Pick Unbounce or Variant if you're not technical. They have drag-and-drop builders.

Cost: $50-200/month for solo founders.
Quality: Industry standard.

**Analysis:**

Claude analyzes your results. Or Statsig (free tool with better statistical analysis than most platforms).

Cost: Free (Claude) or free (Statsig).
Quality: Saves you hours of math.

**Full-stack automation:**

Causal (formerly Causal) lets you build automated A/B test flows. You set up workflows where results automatically trigger next tests.

Example: "If variation 2 beats baseline by >15%, pause other variations and run variation 2 vs variation 3."

Cost: $50-200/month.
Quality: Removes manual testing overhead.

## What variables to test

**Headline variations:**
- "Solve [problem]" vs "Get [benefit]"
- Specific number vs vague ("Save 10 hours" vs "Save time")
- Question vs statement ("Tired of wasting time?" vs "Stop wasting time")
- Authority + benefit ("Used by 500 companies to save 10 hours/week")

**CTA button text:**
- Action-first ("Start now") vs benefit-first ("See my savings")
- Urgency ("Get access today") vs comfort ("Learn more")
- Specific ("Start my free trial") vs vague ("Click here")

**Form fields:**
- Minimal (email only) vs comprehensive (email + name + company)
- Progressive profiling (start with email, ask more later)
- Phone vs email priority

**Page length:**
- Long form (3000+ words) vs short form (500 words) vs medium (1500)
- Video vs text
- Social proof position (top vs bottom vs middle)

**Risk reversal:**
- No guarantee vs 30-day money-back vs risk-free trial
- Visible vs hidden

## Common mistakes

Testing too many variables at once. Pick one. Run it. Learn. Then test another. Otherwise you won't know what caused the change.

Not running tests long enough. 100 visitors per variation isn't enough. Get to 500+.

Ignoring segment performance. Maybe the headline works great for company buyers but not solo founders. Use analytics to segment and test accordingly.

Not archiving winners. Keep a document of what works: "Headlines with specificity win 15% higher." "Shorter forms always win." Build a playbook.
