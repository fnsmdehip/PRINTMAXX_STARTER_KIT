# GEO strategy for app idea validation: how to get cited by AI overviews

You have an app idea. You want to know: is there real demand? GEO content can prove it.

Write content about solving the problem your app solves. If it ranks in AI overviews, demand is real.

## The validation flow

**Step 1:** Choose your app idea (e.g., "Sales follow-up automation")

**Step 2:** Identify 20 search queries people ask about it
- "How to automate sales follow-ups"
- "Best sales follow-up tools"
- "Cheapest sales automation"
- etc.

**Step 3:** Write content answering each query

**Step 4:** Track if content ranks in AI overviews

**Step 5:** If 50%+ of content ranks, demand is validated

**Step 6:** Build the app with confidence

## The content strategy

Don't write "my app is cool." Write "here's how to solve this problem today."

Examples:
- "Best tools for sales follow-ups" (lists 5 competitors)
- "How to automate follow-ups with Zapier" (step-by-step)
- "Why follow-ups matter" (data-backed)
- "Sales follow-up scripts that convert" (templates)

All educational. No selling. AI ranks educational content better.

## Why this validates demand

If 10k people search "sales follow-up automation" monthly, Google shows it in AI overviews.

If your content ranks there, you get:
- 100-500 monthly clicks (conservative)
- Interest signal verified
- Search volume proven

Then you know: this problem is big enough for an app.

## Real timeline

**Month 1:** Write 10 pages

**Month 2:** Track rankings. If 5+ rank, continue. If 0 rank, rethink idea.

**Month 3:** Write 10 more pages. Refine based on what converts.

**Month 4:** Full content set ranks well. Demand validated. Start building.

Total time: 4 months of validation = 1 month of development savings (you'd have built the wrong thing).

## Tools needed

- Google Search Console ($0) - track rankings
- Claude ($20/month) - content generation
- GitHub ($0) - host pages
- Sheets ($0) - track search volume

Total: $20 for validation.

## Common patterns in ranking content

Content that ranks in AI overviews for app validation:

1. **Comparison:** "Sales tools ranked by automation capability"
2. **How-to:** "How to set up 5-email sales sequence"
3. **Data:** "Sales follow-up ROI: do sequences actually work?"
4. **Templates:** "Sales follow-up email templates (37 examples)"
5. **Cost:** "How much does sales automation cost?"

All answer real questions. All drive toward your app's value prop.

## Measuring demand signals

Track these metrics:

- Rank position in AI overviews (aim for top 3 sources)
- Click volume to your site (500+ monthly = strong signal)
- Average session duration (2+ minutes = real interest)
- Scroll depth (70%+ = readers value content)
- Return visitors (30%+ = validation signal)

If all 5 are strong, demand is there.

## Real example

You want to validate "cold email automation for agencies."

Content you'd write:
- Best cold email tools for agencies
- How to automate email sequences safely
- Cold email compliance guide
- Cold email templates that work
- Why agencies need automation

After 3 months:
- 5 of 5 pages rank in AI overviews
- 2k monthly clicks
- 40% are agencies (based on email subscribers)

Result: Validated. Build the app.

## If content doesn't rank

Red flags that demand is weak:

- 0 of 10 pages rank
- Content gets 50 clicks/month
- Wrong audience finding you (finance people when you target salespeople)

These mean: rethink the idea. Different niche. Different angle. Different problem.

Better to learn this via content than after building.

## Expansion after validation

Once first niche validates:

Write content for adjacent niches:
- Cold email for B2B
- Cold email for SMBs
- Cold email for freelancers

Each becomes a user segment for your app.
