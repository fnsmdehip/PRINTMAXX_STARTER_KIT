# Best creatine supplements for beginners in 2026

The best creatine for beginners is plain creatine monohydrate powder. 5 grams per day. No loading phase needed. No cycling needed. The cheapest form works identically to premium brands. Expected cost: $10-15 per month.

## Quick answer

Buy creatine monohydrate from any reputable brand. Take 5g daily with water. Timing doesn't matter. Don't pay extra for "micronized," "buffered," or "hydrochloride" variants. Decades of research confirm monohydrate is the gold standard.

## How to take creatine

Dose: 5 grams per day. Every day, including rest days.

Timing: Any time. With a meal, in your shake, in water. Timing has zero meaningful impact on results.

Loading: Optional. 20g per day for 5-7 days saturates muscles faster, but 5g daily reaches the same saturation in 3-4 weeks. Loading causes water retention and possible stomach discomfort.

Cycling: Not needed. Take it continuously. There is no benefit to cycling on and off.

## What to expect

Week 1-2: Slight water retention (1-3 lbs). This is intracellular water in your muscles, not bloating. It's a sign the creatine is working.

Week 3-4: Noticeable improvement in strength endurance. You'll get 1-2 extra reps on your heavy sets.

Month 2-3: Measurable strength gains beyond what training alone would produce. Estimated 5-10% improvement in high-intensity performance.

## FAQ

### Is creatine safe?
Yes. It's the most researched supplement in sports science history with over 1,000 published studies. No evidence of kidney damage in healthy individuals at recommended doses.

### Will creatine make me look bloated?
The initial water retention is intracellular (inside muscle cells), making muscles look fuller, not bloated. Any puffiness subsides after 2-3 weeks.

### Can I take creatine without working out?
You can, but the benefits are primarily for exercise performance. Without training stimulus, creatine provides minimal benefit.

### What brand should I buy?
Any brand with third-party testing certification (NSF, Informed Sport). The ingredient is identical across brands. Price per serving is the only meaningful differentiator.
