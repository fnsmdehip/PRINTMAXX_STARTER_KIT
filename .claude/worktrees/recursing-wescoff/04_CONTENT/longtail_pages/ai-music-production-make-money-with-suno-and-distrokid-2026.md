# AI music production: make money with Suno and DistroKid 2026

## Quick answer

Suno AI generates complete songs (vocals, instruments, mixing) in 30 seconds. DistroKid distributes to Spotify, Apple Music, and 150+ platforms for $22/year. Revenue: $0.003-0.005 per Spotify stream. To make $1,000/month from streaming, you need 200,000-333,000 monthly streams. The play: volume of lo-fi/ambient tracks + playlist placement + sync licensing.

## How it works

1. Generate songs with Suno ($10/month for unlimited, free tier: 10 songs/day)
2. Edit and select the best (1 in 5 generations is good enough)
3. Distribute via DistroKid ($22/year) to all streaming platforms
4. Get on playlists (organic and paid placement)
5. Collect streaming revenue + sync licensing income

## Production workflow

### Daily routine (1 hour)

1. Generate 20 songs in Suno (10 min)
2. Listen and select top 4 (20 min)
3. Edit metadata (title, genre tags, description) (10 min)
4. Upload to DistroKid (10 min)
5. Submit to playlists (10 min)

### Monthly output

20 tracks/week = 80 tracks/month. Over 6 months: 480 tracks. Each track is a lottery ticket for playlist placement and algorithmic discovery.

## Playlist placement strategy

### Organic (free)

- Submit to Spotify editorial playlists via Spotify for Artists (submit 7 days before release)
- Contact independent playlist curators (find via PlaylistPush, SubmitHub)
- Create your own playlists featuring your tracks + similar popular tracks

### Paid placement

- SubmitHub ($1-10 per submission to curators)
- PlaylistPush ($150-500 per campaign)
- Daily Playlists ($10-50 per submission)

Budget $50-200/month on playlist placement until you find what works.

## Sync licensing (higher revenue per placement)

Sync licensing = your music used in videos, ads, podcasts, games.

Platforms:
- Epidemic Sound (apply as creator)
- Artlist (submit catalog)
- Musicbed (higher-end placements)
- Pond5 (stock music marketplace)
- AudioJungle (Envato marketplace)

One sync placement can pay $50-500, more than 100K streams.

## Legal considerations

- Suno Pro/Premier licenses allow commercial use
- Free tier Suno: check terms (may be non-commercial)
- DistroKid: you are the "artist" and rights holder
- Some platforms are developing AI music policies (monitor changes)
- Always disclose AI generation where required by platform

## FAQ

### Can Spotify detect AI music?

Spotify has flagged some low-effort AI tracks. Quality AI-generated music that sounds professional has not been targeted. Focus on quality over quantity.

### Is AI music copyrightable?

US copyright law is evolving. Pure AI output may not be copyrightable. Adding human creative input (editing, arranging, selecting) strengthens your claim. The commercial use right from Suno's license is separate from copyright.

### How long until I see revenue?

DistroKid releases are live in 1-3 days. First revenue trickles in within 2-4 weeks. Meaningful revenue ($100+/month) typically takes 3-6 months of consistent uploading and playlist placement.

### What about Udio as a Suno alternative?

Udio is another AI music generator. Test both. Different tools produce different styles. Use whichever generates better output for your target genre.
