# How to validate SaaS idea in one week with $100

## Quick Answer

Build a landing page with waitlist form. Run $100 in Google Ads. If you get 20+ signups with 5%+ conversion, you have signal. If not, pivot or kill the idea.

## The Validation Framework

**Goal:** Determine if people want your product before building it.

**Not validation:** Asking friends if your idea is good.

**Real validation:** Strangers giving you their email (or money) based on your pitch.

## Day-by-Day Plan

### Day 1: Research + Positioning

**Hours:** 4

**Tasks:**
1. Define specific problem your SaaS solves
2. Identify target audience (be specific)
3. Find where they search online
4. Check competitor pricing
5. Write your unique angle

**Example:**

Bad: "Project management tool for teams"

Good: "Project management for remote design agencies with 5-15 people. Integrates with Figma. $49/mo."

**Deliverable:** One-sentence pitch + target keywords.

### Day 2: Build Landing Page

**Hours:** 4

**Tool:** Carrd (free tier supports 3 sites)

**Landing page structure:**

1. **Headline:** Promise in 8 words
   - "Get clients to approve designs 50% faster"

2. **Subhead:** Who it's for
   - "For remote design agencies tired of endless revision rounds"

3. **3 bullet points:** Key features
   - Figma integration for instant feedback
   - Version control built in
   - Client-friendly interface (no design knowledge needed)

4. **Social proof (if you have):**
   - "Join 47 agencies on the waitlist"

5. **Email form:** Tally embed
   - "Get early access + 50% off launch price"

6. **Founder note:**
   - Your photo + 2 sentences about why you're building this

**No fancy design needed.** Clean, fast, clear.

**Deliverable:** Live landing page + waitlist form.

### Day 3: Set Up Ads

**Hours:** 3

**Platform:** Google Ads

**Why Google Ads?**
- People are actively searching (high intent)
- Cheaper than Facebook for B2B
- Fast results

**Campaign setup:**

1. **Campaign type:** Search
2. **Goal:** Website traffic
3. **Budget:** $15/day for 6 days = $90 (save $10 for overages)
4. **Keywords:** 5-10 specific searches

**Example keywords for design project management:**
- "project management for design agencies"
- "design collaboration tool"
- "client feedback tool for designers"
- "figma project management"

5. **Ad copy:**

```
Headline 1: Project Management for Design Agencies
Headline 2: Get Client Approvals 50% Faster
Description: Built for remote teams. Figma integration. Join 47 agencies on waitlist.
```

6. **Target:** US only (or your primary market)

**Deliverable:** Active Google Ads campaign.

### Days 4-6: Run Ads + Monitor

**Hours:** 1/day

**Tasks:**
- Check daily ad spend
- Pause low-performing keywords
- Track conversion rate
- Reply to any waitlist signups asking what they want

**What to look for:**

| Metric | Good | Bad |
|--------|------|-----|
| Click-through rate (CTR) | 3%+ | <1% |
| Cost per click (CPC) | $1-3 | $5+ |
| Conversion rate | 5%+ | <2% |
| Total signups | 20+ | <10 |

If bad after 3 days, pause and iterate.

### Day 7: Analyze Results

**Hours:** 2

**Decision matrix:**

**Scenario 1: Strong signal (20+ signups, 5%+ conversion)**
- People want this
- Build MVP
- Email waitlist for feedback on features

**Scenario 2: Weak signal (5-10 signups, 2-3% conversion)**
- Some interest but not strong
- Interview signups (what's missing?)
- Iterate positioning or features
- Test again

**Scenario 3: No signal (<5 signups, <1% conversion)**
- Kill idea or major pivot
- Save time/money by not building

## What Good Validation Looks Like

**Real example from solopreneur:**

- Landing page: "Email warmup for cold outreach ($29/mo)"
- Ads spend: $100
- Results: 34 signups, 7.2% conversion
- Action: Built MVP, launched in 6 weeks
- Outcome: 12 paying customers at launch

**Signal was there.** People clicked and signed up.

## What Bad Validation Looks Like

**Real example:**

- Landing page: "AI-powered note-taking app"
- Ads spend: $100
- Results: 3 signups, 0.8% conversion
- Action: Killed idea before building
- Outcome: Saved 3 months of dev time

**No signal.** Market spoke clearly.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1:** Vague positioning.

**Fix:** Be specific about who it's for and what problem it solves.

**Mistake 2:** Running ads to homepage instead of dedicated landing page.

**Fix:** Single-purpose landing page = higher conversion.

**Mistake 3:** Not setting a kill threshold.

**Fix:** Decide upfront: "If I don't get 15 signups, I kill this."

**Mistake 4:** Asking for money too early.

**Fix:** For validation, email signup is enough. Monetize after you build.

## Advanced: Offering Founding Member Pricing

If you want stronger validation, offer pre-purchase.

**Landing page addition:**
"Reserve your spot. $99 for lifetime access (regular $29/mo)."

If people pay $99 for something that doesn't exist yet, you have very strong validation.

But this only works if you're 90% sure you'll build it.

## After Validation

**If you get signal:**

1. Email waitlist: "Thanks for signing up. What's the #1 feature you need?"
2. Build MVP based on responses
3. Launch to waitlist first
4. Convert 10-20% to paying customers

**If you don't get signal:**

1. Email waitlist anyway: "We're pivoting based on feedback. What problem should we solve instead?"
2. Learn from failures
3. Iterate on positioning or idea
4. Test again with new angle

## Is One Week Enough?

**For initial signal:** Yes.

**For complete validation:** No.

One week tells you if there's demand. To fully validate, you need to:
- Build MVP
- Get paying customers
- Retain them for 3+ months

But one week saves you from building the wrong thing.

## Can You Do This for $0?

Yes, but slower.

**Free version:**
- Post about your idea daily on X/LinkedIn (no ads)
- Build landing page (free)
- Track organic signups
- Takes 4-6 weeks instead of 1 week

Trade time for money.
