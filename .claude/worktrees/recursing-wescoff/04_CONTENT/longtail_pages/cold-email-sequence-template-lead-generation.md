# Cold email sequence template for lead generation

## Quick Answer

5 emails over 10 days. Email 1 soft intro. Email 2 value. Email 3 proof. Email 4 urgency. Email 5 last chance.

Open rate: 30-40%. Response rate: 2-5%.

Copy templates below. Personalize with [Name], [Company], [Specific pain].

## Why This Sequence Works

Most cold emails die at email 1 because they ask too much.

This sequence builds trust first:
- Email 1: Who you are
- Email 2: Why you're relevant
- Email 3: Proof it works
- Email 4: Time-sensitive reason
- Email 5: Final offer

By email 5, they know you and trust you.

## The 5-Email Sequence

### Email 1 (Day 1): Soft Introduction
```
Subject: [Name] + [Company] = good fit?

Hi [Name],

I work with companies like [Competitor/Similar] to [specific outcome].

Quick question: Are you still looking to [pain point]?

If yes, worth a quick call.
If no, no worries!

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Short (2 sentences)
- Complimentary (you're like [good company])
- Question (makes them think)
- No pressure (if no, okay)

**Target open rate:** 15-25%
**Target reply rate:** 2-5%

### Email 2 (Day 3): Value + Social Proof
```
Subject: [Value metric] in 30 days

Hi [Name],

Some quick wins I see in [their industry]:
- [Company A] did [specific result]
- [Company B] improved [metric] by [%]
- [Company C] saved [time/money]

Worth exploring?

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Specific results (not vague)
- Multiple examples (shows pattern)
- They're similar companies (relevant)

**Target open rate:** 20-30%
**Target reply rate:** 3-7%

### Email 3 (Day 5): How It Works
```
Subject: The [specific approach] we use

Hi [Name],

Here's how we typically work:

1. We audit [their current state] (30 min call)
2. We show gaps they're missing (custom report)
3. We suggest 3 fixes (no pressure)

Most people do 1 and 2, then decide.

Interested in a quick audit?

[Calendar link]

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Clear process (no mystery)
- Low commitment first (audit only)
- Path is obvious (audit → report → decision)

**Target open rate:** 25-35%
**Target reply rate:** 2-4%

### Email 4 (Day 7): Urgency
```
Subject: Slot opening up next week

Hi [Name],

Noticed you haven't replied. Completely understand.

I have 2 audit slots opening next week [specific dates].

If interested, book here: [link]

Otherwise, I'll stop bugging you.

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges silence (not pushy)
- Limited slots (creates urgency)
- Gives out date (makes it real)
- Permission to stop (respects their time)

**Target open rate:** 30-40%
**Target reply rate:** 1-3%

### Email 5 (Day 10): Last Chance
```
Subject: Last message [Name]

Hi [Name],

This is my final note.

I help [specific type] company solve [specific problem].

You seem like a fit. I'd rather not leave money on the table for you.

Last offer: 15-min call to see if it's worth your time.

[Calendar link]

If not, good luck with [their goal].

[Your name]
```

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges this is final (honest)
- Emotional appeal (money on table)
- One more ask (clear CTA)
- Permission to leave (respectful ending)

**Target open rate:** 35-45%
**Target reply rate:** 1-2%

## Subject Line Variations (A/B Test)

If Email 1 not working, try:

```
Original: [Name] + [Company] = good fit?

Variation 1: Quick question about [Company]
Variation 2: [Name], one idea for [pain point]
Variation 3: Help with [specific challenge]?
Variation 4: [Company] doing [outcome]?
Variation 5: 3 things I noticed at [Company]
```

Test 50/50. See which gets higher open rate.

## Timing

**Send schedule:**
- Email 1: Monday 9am
- Email 2: Wednesday 2pm
- Email 3: Friday 10am
- Email 4: Monday 3pm (next week)
- Email 5: Thursday 9am (next week)

Vary timing so you're not hitting inbox same time daily.

## Personalization (Critical)

Don't send generic. Fill in:

- [Name]: Their first name
- [Company]: Their company name
- [Competitor/Similar]: Company doing similar thing
- [Specific outcome]: What they can achieve
- [Pain point]: What keeps them up at night
- [Specific result]: Exact number/metric
- [Metric]: Something they care about
- [Industry]: Their industry/niche
- [Specific approach]: How you do it
- [Their current state]: What they use now
- [Specific challenge]: Their problem
- [Specific problem]: What you solve

Example with personalization:

```
Hi Sarah,

I work with marketing directors at companies like HubSpot
and Gong to get 40% more leads through LinkedIn.

Quick question: Are you still looking to increase inbound leads?

If yes, worth a quick call.
If no, no worries!

Alex
```

That's personalized. Generic version:

```
Hi there,

I work with companies to get better results.

Interested in a call?

Alex
```

Generic = 0.5% response rate.
Personalized = 3-5% response rate.

Do the work.

## Metrics to Track

Send 100 emails:

```
Email 1: 35 opens (35%), 2 replies (2%)
Email 2: 28 opens (28% of openers), 1 reply
Email 3: 20 opens (20% of openers), 1 reply
Email 4: 22 opens (22% of openers), 0 replies
Email 5: 25 opens (25% of openers), 1 reply

Total: 5 replies = 5% response rate
```

Benchmark:
- Open rate: 25-40% is good
- Reply rate: 2-5% is good
- Meeting rate: 50% of replies → meetings

## Red Flags

- Open rate below 15%: Subject lines need work
- No replies by email 3: Value proposition is bad
- People unsubscribing: Too aggressive tone
- Calendar links not clicked: Offer is weak
