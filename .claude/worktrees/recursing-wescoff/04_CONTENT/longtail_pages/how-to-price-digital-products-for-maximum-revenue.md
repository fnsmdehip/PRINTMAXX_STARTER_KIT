# How to price digital products for maximum revenue

Price digital products at $7-27 for templates and guides, $27-97 for courses and comprehensive systems, and $97-497 for premium coaching or done-for-you solutions. The sweet spot for impulse purchases on Gumroad is $9-19 with pay-what-you-want enabled.

## Quick answer

Set your price based on the value of the problem solved, not the time it took to create. A Notion template that saves someone 5 hours per week is worth $12-27 even if it took you 4 hours to build. Enable pay-what-you-want with a minimum at 60% of list price to capture budget-conscious buyers while allowing generous ones to pay more.

## Psychological pricing tactics

End prices in 7 or 9. $17 outperforms $15 and $20 in conversion tests. The brain processes $17 as "teens" while $20 feels like a different category.

Use anchoring. Show the "value" ($150/mo personal trainer) next to your price ($17 one-time). The comparison makes your price feel small.

Bundle products. Three $12 products bundled at $29 (save $7) converts better than any individual product. Bundles increase average order value by 40-60%.

## Testing your price

Start at the higher end of your range. It's easier to lower a price than raise it. If conversion rate is below 2%, the price might be too high or the landing page copy needs work. If conversion rate is above 8%, you're probably undercharging.

## FAQ

### Should I offer free products?
Yes, as lead magnets only. A free PDF or mini-template captures emails. Then upsell to paid products in your welcome sequence. Never give away your full product for free.

### How do I know if I'm undercharging?
If more than 50% of pay-what-you-want customers pay above your minimum, you're undercharging. Raise your listed price by 20-30%.

### Do discounts work for digital products?
Sparingly. A launch discount (20% off for first 48 hours) creates urgency. Permanent discounts train your audience to wait for sales.

### What about refunds?
Offer a 30-day refund guarantee. It increases conversions by 15-25% and actual refund rates are typically under 5% for quality digital products.
