# How to write cold emails that don't sound like spam

## Quick Answer

Research the person for 5 minutes. Write a specific subject line referencing something they did. Keep email under 100 words. One clear ask. No buzzwords. Reply rate: 15-25%.

## What Makes Email Sound Like Spam

**Spam signals:**
- Generic subject lines ("Quick question" "Following up")
- No personalization ("Dear Sir/Madam")
- Buzzwords ("synergy" "game-changer" "disruptive")
- Long emails (200+ words)
- Multiple asks
- Fake urgency ("Limited time offer")
- Template language ("I came across your profile")

**What actually works:**
- Specific subject lines
- Research-based personalization
- Plain language
- Short emails
- One clear ask
- Real reason for reaching out

## The Formula That Works

### Subject Line

**Bad:**
- "Quick question"
- "Following up"
- "Partnership opportunity"

**Good:**
- "Your post about [specific thing]"
- "Question about [their recent work]"
- "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out"

**Best:**
Reference something specific they did recently.

### Email Body Structure

**Line 1: Why you're emailing**
Mention something specific about them.

**Line 2-3: What you do**
One sentence. No jargon.

**Line 4: The ask**
Specific, easy to say yes to.

**Line 5: Sign off**
No fluff.

## Real Examples

### Example 1: Cold Outreach for SaaS

**Bad version:**

> Subject: Partnership Opportunity
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I hope this email finds you well. I came across your profile and was impressed by your work in the productivity space. Our company, [SaaS Name], is revolutionizing the way teams collaborate with cutting-edge AI technology.
>
> I'd love to schedule a 30-minute call to discuss how we can create synergies and drive mutual value. Are you available next week?
>
> Looking forward to connecting!
>
> Best,
> [You]

**Why it fails:** Generic subject, buzzwords, vague ask, too formal.

**Good version:**

> Subject: Your TikTok about productivity tools
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Saw your TikTok about time tracking tools. You mentioned wanting something simpler than Toggl.
>
> I built a minimal time tracker for solopreneurs. 3 clicks to track, auto-categorizes by project.
>
> Want to try it? I'll give you free access.
>
> [Your name]

**Why it works:** Specific reference, plain language, clear offer, short.

### Example 2: Outreach for Content Collab

**Bad version:**

> Subject: Collaboration
>
> Dear [Name],
>
> I am reaching out to explore potential collaboration opportunities between our brands. I believe there is significant synergy between our audiences and we could create compelling content together.
>
> Would you be interested in discussing this further? I have some ideas I'd love to share.
>
> Best regards,
> [You]

**Why it fails:** Vague everything. No specific reason. Buzzwords.

**Good version:**
>
> Subject: Your LinkedIn post on cold outreach
>
> [Name],
>
> Your post on cold email templates got 1.2k likes. Would you want to co-write a follow-up post? I have data from 5000 cold emails we sent.
>
> I'll do the writing. You review/edit. We both share it.
>
> In?
>
> [Your name]

**Why it works:** References specific post, clear mutual benefit, easy ask.

### Example 3: Job/Freelance Pitch

**Bad version:**

> Subject: Available for Projects
>
> Hi,
>
> I am a highly skilled developer with expertise in React, Node.js, and Python. I have worked with numerous clients and delivered exceptional results. I am currently available for new projects.
>
> Please let me know if you have any openings.
>
> Thank you,
> [You]

**Why it fails:** No research, generic skills list, passive ask.

**Good version:**

> Subject: Your job post for React developer
>
> [Name],
>
> Saw your post looking for a React dev. Built something similar for [Company]: [link to live project].
>
> Available to start this week. $75/hour or project rate.
>
> Want to see more work?
>
> [Your name]

**Why it works:** References job post, shows proof, clear rate, direct ask.

## Research First

**Spend 5 minutes per person:**

1. Check their recent posts (X, LinkedIn)
2. Visit their company site
3. Look at recent work/projects
4. Find one specific thing to reference

**What to reference:**
- Recent post they made
- Project they launched
- Article they wrote
- Company milestone
- Mutual connection

**Don't reference:**
- Generic profile info
- Job title
- Company description
- Their "impressive background"

## The One Clear Ask

**Bad asks:**
- "Can we schedule a call?" (vague)
- "Would you be interested in learning more?" (pointless)
- "Let me know your thoughts" (no action)

**Good asks:**
- "Want to try it?" (specific)
- "Can I send you the first draft?" (actionable)
- "Free for 15 minutes Thursday?" (concrete time)

Make it easy to say yes.

## Length Matters

**Data from 5000 cold emails:**

| Word Count | Reply Rate |
|------------|------------|
| <50 words | 22% |
| 50-100 words | 18% |
| 100-150 words | 12% |
| 150-200 words | 8% |
| 200+ words | 4% |

**Target:** Under 100 words. Shorter = better.

## Words to Avoid

**Spam triggers:**
- Synergy, game-changer, disruptive, innovative
- Revolutionizing, cutting-edge, world-class
- Solution, leverage, utilize
- Partnership opportunity, limited time
- Free money, guarantee, act now

**Use instead:**
- Plain English
- Specific terms
- Numbers and data
- Your actual product name

## Follow-Up Strategy

**When to follow up:**
- After 3-4 days (no reply)
- After 1 week (second follow-up)
- Kill after 2 follow-ups

**Follow-up template:**

> [Name],
>
> Following up on my email about [specific thing].
>
> Still interested?
>
> [Your name]

Even shorter than first email.

## Timing and Deliverability

**Best times to send:**
- Tuesday-Thursday
- 8-10 AM or 1-3 PM (recipient's time zone)
- Avoid Monday AM and Friday PM

**Deliverability tips:**
- Warm up new email domains
- Don't send 100+ per day from one account
- Use plain text (not HTML templates)
- Personalize every email
- Avoid attachments in first email

## Testing and Iteration

**Track these metrics:**

| Metric | Good | Bad |
|--------|------|-----|
| Open rate | 40%+ | <20% |
| Reply rate | 15%+ | <5% |
| Positive reply rate | 10%+ | <3% |

**What to test:**
- Subject lines (A/B test different hooks)
- Email length (try shorter)
- CTA wording (direct vs soft)
- Send time

Test 50 emails per variation. Compare results.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1:** Using templates without personalization.

**Fix:** Spend 5 minutes researching each person.

**Mistake 2:** Writing a novel.

**Fix:** Cut your email in half. Then cut it again.

**Mistake 3:** Vague subject lines.

**Fix:** Reference something specific they did.

**Mistake 4:** Asking for too much.

**Fix:** One small, easy ask.

**Mistake 5:** Following up too soon.

**Fix:** Wait 3-4 days minimum.

## When to Stop

**Kill the campaign if:**
- Open rate <20% after 50 emails
- Reply rate <5% after 100 emails
- Multiple spam reports

**Iterate instead:**
- Try different subject lines
- Make emails shorter
- Improve targeting
- Test different ask
