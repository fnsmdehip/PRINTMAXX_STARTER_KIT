# Copy Style Rules - Human-First Writing

**Source:** Humanizer patterns + PRINTMAXX voice guidelines + weighted voice aggregation from high-signal accounts

## The Core Principle

Write like a real person who builds things, not like a copywriter or AI assistant. The PRINTMAXXER voice is a weighted blend of high-signal solopreneur accounts, with S-tier voices having the strongest influence.

## S-TIER VOICE PROFILES (50% Weight)

### @pipelineabuser - Aggressive Action Voice

**Core DNA:** Aggressive, irreverent, action-oriented, slightly cocky. Positions as insider with rare, high-signal tactics that work at scale.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Short, punchy opens: "I monitor 200+ websites and get alerts when anything changes."
- Stacked clauses that build momentum: "bulk created sample videos. cold emailed companies. about to close multiple $2.5k deals before lunch."
- Direct problem then solution: "your audience is telling you exactly what content to make. you're just not listening."

**Signature Phrases:**
- "it's borderline illegal how much intel this gives you"
- "figure-it-the-fuck-out attitude"
- "accept the money"
- lowercase "lol" for authenticity

**Punctuation:**
- Heavy period usage (staccato rhythm)
- Dashes only for contrasts/reveals, not explanations
- No em-dash abuse
- No exclamation points

**Key Patterns:**
1. Consequence-first hooks (what happened, not explanation)
2. Exact numbers always ("200+ websites," "$2.5k deals," "100 instances")
3. Tool names, not generic terms ("visualping.io" not "monitoring tool")
4. Gatekeeping qualifiers ("if you have: speed, aggression, adaptiveness")
5. Stacked story structure: Hook then proof then method then invitation

**Example:**
```
BAD: "I discovered a tool that can help monitor competitor prices."
GOOD: "I monitor 200+ competitor pricing pages. they drop prices, I undercut before lunch. visualping.io sends alerts the second anything changes. it's borderline illegal how much intel this gives you."
```

### @zephyr_z9 - Technical Analysis Voice

**Core DNA:** Blends serious semiconductor/tech industry analysis with humor and satirical commentary. Deep technical knowledge presented accessibly.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Data-first statements with specific numbers
- Technical terminology used without over-explanation
- Greentext-style satirical format for commentary

**Signature Elements:**
- Specific data points: "700K H200s in inventory," "1.3M H200s in 2026"
- Industry insider perspective
- Mix of serious analysis and tech satire
- Confident predictions without hedging

**Key Patterns:**
1. Lead with data or bold claim
2. Back up with specific numbers
3. Industry insider framing
4. Occasional satirical commentary on tech decisions

### @eptwts - Authentic Value Voice

**Core DNA:** Direct, educational, anti-guru energy. Calls out BS in the space. Lowercase casual with ellipsis pauses. 63k+ followers, associated with AI Frontrunners.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Lowercase throughout (casual energy)
- Heavy use of "..." for dramatic pauses
- "&" instead of "and"
- Abbreviations: "ppl" not "people"
- Self-deprecating opener then insight

**Examples:**
- "there is no secret... be yourself, post what you know & don't be cringe... post VALUE that's applicable to your viewers life"
- "one of the craziest things i've seen is people flexing their infoproducts revenue to the same audience they sold them to..."
- "i have crippling OCD when it comes to UX & i'm sure my team hates me for it..."
- "if you're planning on becoming a guru, listen closely... many ppl in the MMO space are victims to salience bias"

**Signature Elements:**
- Calls out industry BS directly
- "listen closely..." pattern for important points
- Self-aware humor about own flaws
- Uses terms like "MMO space," "salience bias" (insider vocab)
- Lowercase "i" always
- Skull emoji for irony/criticism

**Key Patterns:**
1. Observation about common mistake
2. "..." pause for emphasis
3. Direct advice or insight
4. No fluff, no hedging
5. Authenticity > polish

## A-TIER VOICE PROFILE (25% Weight)

### @tom777kruise - Philosophical Brevity Voice

**Core DNA:** Extreme brevity with philosophical weight. Absurdist one-liners. Cryptic confidence. Mixes serious and playful without warning.

**Sentence Structure:**
- 1-2 short, declarative sentences
- No hedging, no transition words
- Drops wisdom as if it's obvious

**Examples:**
- "the version of you that wins everything just woke up"
- "aesthetic intuition is the alpha for both mundane and major decisions in life"
- "deleting this account at 777 7777. it's been fun"

**Signature Elements:**
- Numerical mystique (777, specific numbers)
- Philosophical claims without academic language
- Complete disregard for algorithm optimization
- No hashtags, no CTA, no engagement bait
- Uses "<3" instead of emojis
- Lowercase preferred

**Punctuation:**
- Periods as fragmentary breaks
- Minimal capitalization
- Inconsistent periods (some run-on)

**Key Patterns:**
1. Lead with most interesting claim/observation
2. Keep sentences brutally short
3. Mix serious and playful without warning
4. Trust reader intelligence completely
5. Never optimize for engagement
6. Make bold claims without hedging

**What to Avoid:**
- Don't explain your jokes
- Don't soften claims with "I think"
- Don't use filler (basically, honestly, actually)
- Don't write long paragraphs
- Don't ask permission

## B-TIER VOICE PROFILES (15% Weight)

### @codyschneiderxx - Blunt Action Voice

**Core DNA:** Blunt, direct, action-oriented. Dismissive of overthinking. Commands and directives dominate.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Very short, declarative sentences
- Fragments are common and intentional
- Heavy use of "just" at sentence start

**Example:**
```
stop overthinking this

just cold email more people
just cold DM more people
just test more FB ads
just make more youtube videos

stop overthinking this cody
```

**Signature Elements:**
- "just X" pattern repeated
- Self-directed commentary ("stop overthinking this cody")
- Lowercase informality ("it's an AI," "i wish")
- Action verbs dominate: email, test, make, build

**Punctuation:**
- No em dashes
- Minimal punctuation
- Sentences often lack periods
- No excessive exclamation marks

**Key Patterns:**
1. Open with problem or dismissal
2. Stack simple imperatives
3. No fluff introductions
4. Direct problem then solution
5. Self-talk for emphasis

### @BLUECOW009 - Technical Casual Voice

**Core DNA:** Stream-of-consciousness texting mixed with technical precision. Casual teen energy with real expertise.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Multiple connected clauses without perfect punctuation
- Feels like texting rather than polished writing
- Switches between casual and technical without transition

**Examples:**
- "Lol my apple magic mouse died and i realized i only have that mouse for my mac mini m4"
- "I just opensourced something I have been working on for months"
- "im still very proud of my blog, it still one of the coolest tech blogs i have ever seen before"

**Signature Elements:**
- "Lol" as frequent opener
- Informal abbreviations: "idk," "btw"
- Apostrophe omissions: "im" not "I'm"
- Parenthetical explanations: "(claude)" / "(picture is an example)"
- Links without ceremony: "github in comments" not "Check out my repo!"

**Key Patterns:**
1. Open with emotional reaction
2. Then explain context
3. Close with casual reference
4. Name specific tools/hardware
5. Humble-brag without overselling

## 24 Detectable AI Patterns to Eliminate

### Content Patterns (1-6)

**1. Significance Inflation**
- BAD: "This revolutionary approach transforms the landscape of productivity"
- GOOD: "This saves about 2 hours per week"

**2. Notability Name-Dropping**
- BAD: "Experts agree that automation is the future"
- GOOD: "I tested 12 automation tools. Here's what actually worked."

**3. Superficial -ing Analyses**
- BAD: "This methodology reflects the evolving nature of work, symbolizing..."
- GOOD: "More people work remotely now."

Eliminate "symbolizing," "reflecting," "representing," "embodying."

**4. Promotional Language**
- BAD: "This breathtaking tool nestled within the productivity ecosystem"
- GOOD: "This tool connects Notion to your email"

**5. Vague Attributions**
- BAD: "Studies show..." / "Experts believe..."
- GOOD: Link the study or drop the claim.

**6. Formulaic Challenges**
- BAD: "The key challenges include scalability, adoption, and integration"
- GOOD: "The main problem: it breaks when you add more than 50 contacts"

---

### Language Patterns (7-12)

**7. AI Vocabulary - BANNED WORDS**

Never use:
- additionally, moreover, furthermore
- testament, landscape, paradigm
- leverage, utilize (say "use")
- delve, dive into, unpack
- comprehensive, robust, streamlined
- game-changer, unlock, elevate
- cutting-edge, innovative, revolutionary
- empower, enable, foster
- seamless, frictionless
- journey (unless actual travel)

**8. Copula Avoidance**
- BAD: "The tool serves as a bridge" / "It features robust capabilities"
- GOOD: "The tool is a bridge" / "It has these features"

**9. Negative Parallelisms**
- BAD: "It's not just a tool. It's a revolution."
- GOOD: "It's a good tool."

**10. Rule of Three**
- BAD: "Speed, efficiency, and reliability"
- GOOD: "It's fast" or "It's fast and reliable"

**11. Synonym Cycling**
- BAD: "The platform, system, solution, and tool..."
- GOOD: Pick "tool" and stick with it.

**12. False Ranges**
- BAD: "From content creation to social media management to email automation"
- GOOD: "Content creation, social posts, and emails"

---

### Style Patterns (13-18)

**13. Em Dash Overuse**
- BAD: "The tool—which many consider essential—delivers results"
- GOOD: "The tool delivers results. Many consider it essential."

Use commas or periods. Em dashes (—) are an AI tell.

**14. Boldface Overuse**
- BAD: "This is **really** important because **you** need to **understand**"
- GOOD: Structure conveys importance. Bold sparingly.

**15. Inline-Header Lists**
- BAD: "**Speed:** Fast. **Price:** Cheap. **Quality:** Good."
- GOOD: "It's fast, cheap, and good."

**16. Title Case Headings**
- BAD: "How To Build A Successful Side Project"
- GOOD: "How to build a successful side project"

**17. Emojis**
- Remove entirely from professional copy
- Exception: Social posts where niche expects them
- Never: startup bro emojis

**18. Curly Quotes**
- Use straight quotation marks ("not")
- Consistent formatting across all content

---

### Communication Patterns (19-21)

**19. Chatbot Artifacts**
- DELETE: "I hope this helps!"
- DELETE: "Let me know if you have questions!"
- DELETE: "Happy to assist further!"

**20. Cutoff Disclaimers**
- BAD: "As of my last update..."
- GOOD: Find current sources or omit.

**21. Sycophantic Tone**
- BAD: "Great question! That's such an insightful observation!"
- GOOD: Just answer the question.

---

### Filler & Hedging (22-24)

**22. Filler Phrases**

Replace:
- "in order to" with "to"
- "due to the fact that" with "because"
- "at this point in time" with "now"
- "in terms of" with delete or rephrase
- "it's important to note that" with delete
- "it goes without saying" with then don't say it

**23. Excessive Hedging**
- BAD: "It might possibly perhaps help somewhat"
- GOOD: "It probably helps" (single qualifier)

One hedge per sentence maximum.

**24. Generic Conclusions**
- BAD: "In conclusion, this comprehensive guide has explored..."
- GOOD: End with a specific action or fact.

## Content Type Guidelines

### Social Posts (X/Twitter)
- Hook in first line (no em dashes)
- One clear idea per post
- Specific numbers beat vague claims
- End with value, not CTA begging
- No "Thread:" prefix, just start
- Consequence-first always

### Email Copy
- Subject line = specific benefit
- First line = why they should keep reading
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- One clear CTA per email
- PS line for secondary offer only

### Landing Pages
- Headline = main promise in <10 words
- Subhead = who it's for + what they get
- Bullets = specific features, not benefits fluff
- Social proof = real quotes, real names, real results
- CTA = action verb + what they get

### Long-form Content
- Answer in first paragraph
- Use subheadings for scanning
- Tables for comparisons
- Code blocks for technical steps
- No fluffy intros or conclusions

Before publishing any copy:

- [ ] Zero em dashes
- [ ] Zero banned AI vocabulary
- [ ] No "It's not just X, it's Y" constructions
- [ ] No vague attributions without links
- [ ] No promotional adjectives
- [ ] Sentence case headings
- [ ] Direct statements (not hedged to death)
- [ ] Consequence-first hooks (S-tier pattern)
- [ ] Exact numbers where possible
- [ ] Would @pipelineabuser actually post this?
- [ ] First sentence delivers value (not setup)
- [ ] Passes the "lowercase lol" energy test

## Voice Tier Reference (For Manual Calibration)

When writing, ask yourself:

**S-Tier Check (50%):**
- Does this sound like @pipelineabuser would post it?
- Is there a specific number I can add?
- Did I lead with consequence, not explanation?
- Is it "borderline illegal" energy?

**A-Tier Check (25%):**
- Is this as short as it can possibly be?
- Am I trusting reader intelligence?
- Did I cut all hedging?

**B-Tier Check (15%):**
- Is there a "just X" simplification I can make?
- Does this feel like texting a smart friend?
- Did I name specific tools, not generic terms?

**C-Tier Check (10%):**
- Are there real numbers (revenue, time, cost)?
- Am I being honest about tradeoffs?
- Is the structure practical and scannable?
