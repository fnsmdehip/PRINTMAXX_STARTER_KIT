1. Equipment reviews ("Best drivers under $300 in 2026")

3. Deal alerts ("Harbor Freight's best kept secrets")
4. Problem solvers ("Fix a running toilet in 10 minutes")
5. Workshop tours/setups ("$500 workshop setup that does everything")

**Content tone rules (non-negotiable for this demo):**

- Speak WITH authority, not AT the audience. They've been alive longer than most content creators. Respect that.
- No hype. No "INSANE deal." No caps lock. No fire emojis.
- Data and specifics beat enthusiasm. "This driver adds 12 yards on average" beats "This driver is AMAZING."
- Calm voiceover. Think narrator, not salesperson. ElevenLabs voice: use "Daniel" or "Adam" (baritone, measured pace). Avoid "Rachel" or any voice that sounds under 35. Test 3 voices with a sample video before committing.
- Slow pacing. 2-3 seconds between points. Let information land.
- "Here's what I found" framing, not "You NEED this." This demo hates being sold to but loves being informed.
- Price transparency. Always mention cost. This demo respects directness about money.
- Comparison format wins. "A vs B" content gets the highest engagement because this demo researches before buying.

**Content production workflow:**

1. Monday: Claude generates 7 video scripts + 21 Facebook posts per page (batch for the week)
2. Tuesday: ElevenLabs generates voiceovers for all 7 videos. Stock footage pulled from Pexels/Pixabay.
3. Wednesday: Assemble videos (CapCut or DaVinci Resolve free). Schedule all Facebook posts via Meta Business Suite.
4. Thursday-Sunday: Posts auto-publish. Monitor engagement. Reply to comments within 2 hours (Facebook's algorithm weights reply speed. This demo comments 3-4x more than 18-34 on interest-based pages, creating organic reach amplification).

**Content split:** 70% educational / 20% product comparison / 10% deal alerts. The educational content builds trust and drives email signups (lead magnet: "Free PDF: Top 10 [Drivers/Reels/Drills] Under $200, Tested"). The comparisons drive affiliate clicks. The deal alerts create urgency and train the email list to open fast. Every educational post ends with email capture CTA, not just affiliate links.

- Goal: be recognized as a helpful member BEFORE ever sharing your content.

**Step 4: Seed content (Day 21+)**

- Share YouTube videos by answering questions: "Great question. I actually made a video breaking this down: [link]." Only when genuinely relevant.
- Never post the same link to multiple groups on the same day. Stagger over 3-4 days.
- If a group allows self-promotion threads (many have "Share your content Sunday" etc.), use those.
- 80/20 rule: 80% of your group activity is helping others. 20% is your content.

5. Click-through rate on affiliate links is below 0.5% after 90 days of testing across 100+ posts with links. The thesis is wrong. This audience doesn't click affiliate links from faceless pages.

## APPENDIX: Why This Demo is Mispriced

The digital marketing industry defaults to 18-34. Every course, every guru, every case study optimizes for this demo. Meta's own advertiser reports show 78% of ad spend targets 18-44. This creates a pricing anomaly:

- Facebook ad CPMs for 55-70: $3-8
- Facebook ad CPMs for 18-34: $15-25
- The 55-70 demo has 2-3x the disposable income
- The 55-70 demo returns products 38% less
- The 55-70 demo has a compressed sales cycle (fewer touchpoints to conversion)
- The 55-70 demo spends MORE time on Facebook than any other age group

You're paying 3-5x less to reach an audience that spends 2-3x more and converts in 3x fewer touchpoints. That's a 9-15x efficiency gap. This is a market inefficiency. It won't last forever. The content creator industrial complex will eventually notice. First mover advantage: 6-18 month window before CPMs normalize as more marketers discover the arbitrage.

The PRINTMAXX system already produces faceless content at scale. The change: tone (calm, authoritative), platform (Facebook/YouTube instead of TikTok/X), and product selection (supplements, gear, tools instead of SaaS and courses). Same pipeline, different output. $3 CPM reaching people with $1.7M net worth vs $20 CPM reaching people with $12K in savings.
