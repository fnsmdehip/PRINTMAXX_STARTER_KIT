# Distraction-Free Social Media App (iOS/Android)
Date: 2026-04-02
Score: 8/10
Status: PENDING_REVIEW

## What
A mobile app that wraps Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and X into distraction-free views -- stripping Reels, Shorts, algorithmic feeds, and infinite scroll. Shows only content from accounts you follow, in chronological order. The "Dull" app (64 HN points, 46 comments in hours) validated massive demand, but it only covers Instagram and YouTube.

## Why Now
The backlash against algorithm-driven content is at peak intensity in 2026. HN commenters said "the fact someone had to build a separate app just to get the version of Instagram from 5 years ago says a lot." Digital wellness is a $7B market growing 15% YoY. Apple's Screen Time features are weak. Parents are desperate for tools. The Dull app proved demand exists but only covers 2 platforms and is iOS-only. Cover 4 platforms on both iOS and Android.

## Revenue Path
- Free: 1 platform, basic feed cleanup
- Premium: $4.99/mo or $29.99/yr -- all platforms, custom filters, screen time limits, family sharing, focus modes
- Affiliate: partner with digital wellness brands, therapy apps
- B2B: enterprise license for companies wanting to reduce employee social media distraction

## Expected ROI
- Startup cost: $0 (Expo/React Native, Stripe)
- Time to revenue: 7 days (build MVP with Instagram + YouTube using web views with CSS injection)
- Monthly potential: $1,500-$5,000 (300-1000 subscribers at $4.99)
- Competition: LOW -- Dull is iOS only, 2 platforms. No comprehensive cross-platform solution exists.

## First 3 Steps
1. Build React Native/Expo app with WebView-based platform wrappers. Inject CSS/JS to hide Reels, Shorts, Explore, and algorithmic elements. Start with Instagram and YouTube
2. Add TikTok (show following-only feed) and X (chronological timeline only). Wire Stripe for subscriptions. Deploy via TestFlight
3. Launch on Product Hunt, post "I built the anti-doomscroll app" on HN, Reddit r/digitalminimalism, r/productivity. Target parents and knowledge workers
