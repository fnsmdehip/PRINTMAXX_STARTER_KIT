### The Credibility Trap Angle (new pitch refinement)
The netlify.app CPA case (Summit Financial) opened a new angle: **the credibility mismatch**. High-trust professions (accountants, tax preparers, lawyers, financial advisors) running on free hosting subdomains have an intrinsic trust problem with their own clients. This angle is MORE compelling than the SEO angle because it's about existing client trust, not just new client acquisition.

**Apply to future cycles:** Search for lawyers, financial advisors, insurance agents, real estate brokers on:
- `.netlify.app`
- `.vercel.app`
- `.glitch.me`
- `.replit.dev`
- `.github.io`

These are even worse than GoDaddy subdomains for credibility signals.

### New Pattern: Domain-in-Email vs No Website
Perfectly Fitted Bridal (Charlotte) had a real domain in their email address (info@perfectlyfittedbridalnc.com) but the Wix subdomain as their actual site. This = they bought the domain but never built a real site. Strong pitch angle: "You already own the domain. The only thing missing is a real site on it."

**Search pattern for future cycles:** Find businesses where email domain ≠ website domain. These are warm leads — they KNOW they should have a real site, they just never finished the job.
