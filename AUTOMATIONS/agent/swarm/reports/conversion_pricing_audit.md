## SECTION 3: PRICING PSYCHOLOGY ANALYSIS

### What's Working

1. **$7 ending pattern**: All primary Gumroad products end in $7 ($7, $17, $27, $47, $97, $197). Research supports this - $7 outperforms $5 and $10 on Gumroad. This is well-executed.

2. **Anchoring**: Every product has a crossed-out "Value" anchor at 3-5x the price (e.g., ~~$497~~ $97). The multipliers are in the believable zone. Good.

3. **PWYW with minimums**: PWYW at 50-60% of list price is smart. It captures price-sensitive buyers without tanking perceived value. Gumroad data shows ~15% of buyers pay above list price on PWYW products.

4. **Bundle savings escalation**: 23% -> 39% -> 44% savings as bundle size increases. This creates a natural pull toward the Everything Bundle.

5. **Tripwire -> upsell chain**: The $0 -> $7 -> $27 -> $47 -> $97 -> $197 ladder follows proven direct response sequences. Each upsell on the thank-you page is logical.

6. **Fiverr 3-tier structure**: Basic/Standard/Premium with 3-4x price jumps. The "Standard" tier at the middle of Basic and Premium naturally draws buyers away from Basic (decoy effect). Average expected order of $171 means the tier structure works.

### What's Missing or Broken

1. **No charm pricing on Etsy**: Etsy listings use $9.99, $7.99, $14.99 etc. This is correct for Etsy culture (99 cent ending works on marketplace platforms). No issue here.

2. **No urgency/scarcity on any product page**: No limited-time pricing, no cohort limits, no early-bird pricing. Every product is available forever at the same price. This is fine ethically but leaves conversion money on the table.

3. **No risk reversal beyond "30-day money-back"**: No "keep the product even if you refund" or "double your money back if X" or "unconditional" language. The current guarantee is standard but not remarkable.

4. **No social proof pricing**: No "join 500+ solopreneurs" or "237 copies sold" or "4.8 stars from 52 reviews". These would dramatically increase conversion. Obviously can't add until first sales, but the infrastructure for displaying this should be built.

5. **No payment plans**: The $97 and $197 products have no pay-in-3 or pay-in-2 options. Adding "3 payments of $67" on the $197 Everything Bundle would capture buyers who balk at $197 upfront.

6. **Storefront says "Save $246" on Everything Bundle ($443 separately -> $197)**: But the primary line individual total is $355 for all 9 paid products, not $443. The storefront appears to include the 3 micro products (#11, #12, #13 at $29 + $19 + $39 = $87) making the total $442 which rounds to $443. This is fine but should be verified. The GUMROAD_READY_LISTINGS.md says $355 individual total, $197 bundle price, save $158. The storefront says $443 individual, $197 bundle, save $246. These are different because the storefront bundles ALL 12 paid products including micro products, while the original listing bundled only the 9 core products. This needs to be standardized.
