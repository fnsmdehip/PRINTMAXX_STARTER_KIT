## Detailed Page-by-Page Analysis

### 1. ColdMaxx vs Instantly (07_LANDING/) -- Grade: A

**URL:** https://coldmaxx-vs-instantly.surge.sh/

**Value Prop (A):** "$0/mo vs $30-97/mo for the same job" badge is immediately clear. The h1 asks the exact question the searcher has. Savings banner ("save $360-$1,164/year") quantifies the benefit above the fold. Specific, benefit-focused, consequence-first.

**CTA (A):** Multiple CTAs throughout. Split CTA at bottom gives both options (ColdMaxx free vs Instantly). Email capture with a specific lead magnet ("73 cold email subject lines that got 34%+ open rates, tested across 464K sends"). CTAs use action verbs ("try ColdMaxx free", "send the PDF").

**Social Proof (C):** Claims "1,200+ cold emailers already have it" but no named testimonials, no case studies, no logos. The comparison data itself acts as implicit proof but lacks human voices.

**Copy Quality (A):** Lowercase PRINTMAXXER voice throughout. No em dashes. No banned AI words. Consequence-first hooks. Specific numbers everywhere ($360/year, 5,000 emails/month, 464K sends). Decision tree format is scannable. "tl;dr for people who don't read" is on-brand. FTC affiliate disclosure present.

**Mobile (A):** Responsive breakpoints at 768px. Grid collapses to single column. Table font-size reduces. Decision tree stacks vertically.

**Lead Capture (A):** FormSubmit.co email capture with specific lead magnet. Hidden fields track source page. Redirect to store page after submission.

**Urgency (C):** No time-limited offers or scarcity elements. "Updated mar 2026" timestamp is the only freshness signal.

**Top Fix:** Add 2-3 real testimonials from cold email users. Even short quotes would dramatically increase trust.
