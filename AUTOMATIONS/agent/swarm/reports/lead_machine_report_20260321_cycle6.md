## New Cold Email Tactics Used in Cycle 6

**Three new angles NOT used in previous cycles:**

**1. Platform dependency angle (cycle 6 innovation):**
Instead of "your site doesn't rank," use "you're paying [Houzz/Yelp/Rover] $X/month for leads your site would capture for free."
- Used in: Blind Ambition (Houzz), Croach Pest (Yelp), Happy Tails (Rover), Fresh Start Cleaning (Yelp)
- Why it works: makes the ROI case without criticizing their current site. Owners know they're paying too much for leads.

**2. Platform-specific failure naming:**
Named the exact platform (Pixieset, Webs.com, Square.site, GoDaddy builder) and explained WHY it fails.
- "Pixieset is great for gallery delivery, not for Google acquisition. You need a front door."
- "Webs.com is a platform that stopped active development years ago."
- This is more credible than generic "your site is bad." Shows we did the homework.

**3. Future search intent framing:**
Instead of "you're missing traffic NOW," use "X is when buyers/parents/owners make decisions — that's in Y weeks."
- Used in: Daycare (fall enrollment), Real estate (spring buying season), Deck contractor (quote season)
- Creates urgency that feels helpful, not pressurized. "I'm telling you before the window closes."
