## Findom Business Model — Platform Economics Summary

### 1. Platform Revenue Splits

| Platform | Creator Cut | Primary Function | Discovery |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| OnlyFans | 80% | Subscription + tips | Low (SEO only) |
| LoyalFans | 80% | Subscription + PPV | Medium (built-in) |
| iWantClips | 60% | Clip sales + tributes | High (findom-specific) |
| Throne | ~95%* | Wishlist/gifts only | Medium |
| CashApp | 100% | Direct tribute | None (external traffic required) |

*Throne charges buyers, not creators. Creator receives full gift value minus processing.

### 2. Posting Frequency by Tier

- **5K–15K followers:** 1–2x/day free content (Twitter/X), 3–5x/week on paid platform
- **15K–50K followers:** 2–3x/day free, daily on paid platform, heavier DM engagement
- Findom is relationship-density over content volume — engagement rate matters more than post count

### 3. Account Setup Requirements

**Universal (all platforms):**
- Government ID (age 18+ verification)
- Selfie with ID for some platforms
- Bank account or payment processor for payouts
- Tax forms (W-9 US / W-8BEN international)

**Platform-specific:**
- OnlyFans/LoyalFans: ID + face match, 3–5 day approval
- iWantClips: ID verification, content must be self-produced
- Throne: Email only to create wishlist, no adult content restrictions

### Economics Summary

Findom monetizes through **layered revenue**: free platform (Twitter) drives traffic → subscription on OF/LoyalFans ($15–50/mo) → PPV upsells ($20–200) → direct CashApp tributes (variable, $5–$500+) → Throne wishlist gifts. Top earners stack all 5 streams. iWantClips works best for clip-based content sales; CashApp is pure margin but requires an existing audience.
