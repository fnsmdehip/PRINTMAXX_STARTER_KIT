## Findom Business Model — Platform Economics Summary

---

### Top Monetization Platforms

| Platform | Revenue Split | Key Mechanic | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **OnlyFans** | 80/20 | Subs + PPV + tips | Largest audience, most discoverability |
| **LoyalFans** | 80/20 | Same as OF + better UX | Growing alternative, lower saturation |
| **iWantClips** | 60/40 | Clip sales + tributes | Findom-native, tribute buttons built-in |
| **Throne** | ~0% (gifting) | Wishlist fulfillment | No cash, physical gifts from verified brands |
| **CashApp** | 0% (direct) | Direct tribute/transfer | No platform fees but no discovery layer |

**Platform stack reality:** Most operators run 2-3 simultaneously. OF for recurring subs, CashApp/Venmo for direct tributes, iWantClips for clip catalog.

---

### Posting Frequency (5K-50K accounts)

- **Twitter/X:** 3-7x/day (free content + tribute demands, primary funnel)
- **OF/LoyalFans:** 1-2x/day (gated content, sub retention)
- **Clips platforms:** 2-4 clips/week (evergreen catalog builds passive income)
- **TikTok/IG:** 1-3x/day if not banned (top-of-funnel, no explicit content)

Revenue is heavily front-loaded toward **catalog depth** — a 200-clip back catalog outperforms a new account with same follower count.

---

### Account Setup Requirements

**Age Verification (all platforms):**
- Government-issued ID required (OF, LoyalFans, iWantClips)
- 18 U.S.C. § 2257 compliance records mandatory for US-adjacent content
- Some platforms require selfie + ID match

**Compliance:**
- Platforms require W-9 (US) or W-8BEN (international) for payouts
- OF: $20 minimum payout, 7-day hold
- iWantClips: 1099 issued at $600+ annual earnings
- CashApp: Flags repeated large transfers — many operators use business accounts

---

### Business Model Core

**Findom economics differ from standard creator economy:**

- Lower content volume requirement (power dynamic = attention scarcity)
- Higher ARPU ($50-500/sub vs $5-15 typical OF)
- Tribute/gift mechanics replace standard subscription churn math
- Twitter is the primary acquisition channel — banned frequently, account diversification critical

**Key risk:** Platform bans are high-frequency. Operators maintain 2-3 Twitter backups and use Linktree-style hubs to route traffic regardless of which account is live.
