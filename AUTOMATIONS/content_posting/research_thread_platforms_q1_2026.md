**1/7**
The algorithms changed under your feet in Q1 2026 and most creators haven't noticed yet.

TikTok raised completion thresholds. Instagram deprioritized watch time. YouTube Shorts now indexes like search.

Here's what actually changed, platform by platform, and what to do about it:

---

**2/7**
TikTok raised the completion rate threshold.

Videos that hit 85% watch-through used to get pushed. Now that bar is higher. Short-form slop that people swipe past at 70% gets buried.

Fix: Tighter hooks. Cut the first 0.5s of dead air. Front-load the payoff. If your video doesn't earn the replay, it doesn't get distributed.

---

**3/7**
TikTok's first-hour window is now make-or-break.

The initial distribution batch got smaller. If your first 200 viewers don't engage hard in that window, the algo kills reach.

Fix: Post when your audience is active, not when it's convenient. Pin a comment with a question. First hour engagement is the new audition.

---

**4/7**
Instagram quietly elevated DM shares above watch time as a ranking signal.

Content that gets sent in DMs ("you need to see this") now outranks content with high passive view counts.

Fix: Create content people forward to a specific person. "Tag someone who..." is dead. Make content so specific that sharing it IS the engagement.

---

**5/7**
Instagram's Originality Score now penalizes cross-posted content harder.

Reposting your TikTok with the watermark was already bad. Now even clean reposts with identical audio/captions get flagged and suppressed.

Fix: Native creation per platform. Or at minimum, change the hook, swap the caption, use platform-native audio. Same idea, different execution.

---

**6/7**
YouTube Shorts now factors SEO into discoverability.

Titles, descriptions, and tags actually matter for Shorts now. They're indexing Shorts like regular videos.

The sweet spot shifted to 50-60 seconds. Longer = more ad inventory for YouTube = more push from the algo.

Fix: Write real titles. Use keywords. Fill out descriptions. Hit 55 seconds.

---

**7/7**
Twitter now weights engagement depth over impressions.

A tweet with 50 replies and 10 bookmarks outranks one with 500 likes and zero conversation.

Fix: Write tweets that demand a response. Ask questions. Make claims worth arguing. Bookmarks = the silent ranking signal nobody talks about.

The algo rewards depth everywhere now. Surface-level engagement is dying across every platform.

Save this thread. The creators who adapt in the next 30 days eat the ones who don't.
