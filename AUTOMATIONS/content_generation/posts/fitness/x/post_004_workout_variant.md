# Post 004 — Niche Fitness Apps at Scale

**Platform:** X (Twitter)
**Length:** Thread (5 tweets)
**Niche:** Fitness

---

deployed 12 fitness variant apps using the same streak template. each targets a hyper-specific fitness niche: cold plunge, calisthenics, running, yoga, cycling, swimming, rock climbing, parkour, gymnastics, pilates, bouldering, dance.

why variants work:
1. app store treats them as independent apps
2. hyper-specific names rank higher for niche keywords
3. each community is loyal (they'll switch if YOUR app is better)
4. low competition per niche variant

each app:
- daily workout logging
- streak counter (resets on miss)
- community leaderboard
- unlock premium at day 30 ($3.99)

time per app: 1.5 hours (template + niche copy).
cost: $0.
revenue potential: $150-400/mo per app = $2k-4.8k/mo total.

the play: most fitness apps are generic (fitbit, strava, apple health). fitness communities hate generic. they want PURPOSE. "i want to rock climb 365 days" not "log workouts". that specificity is the moat.

scale: 50 fitness variants = $7.5k-20k/mo recurring. the marginal app takes 1.5 hours to build. roi on time is insane.
