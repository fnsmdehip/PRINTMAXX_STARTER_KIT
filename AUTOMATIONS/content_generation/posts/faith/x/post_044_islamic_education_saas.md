# Post 044 — Islamic Education SaaS for Madrassas

**Platform:** X (Twitter)
**Length:** Thread (5 tweets)
**Type:** B2B Play
**Niche:** Faith

---

pitched 5 local madrassas on education management software. $500/month each. all 5 said yes.

the problem: madrassas use whatsapp + notebooks
- no attendance tracking
- no student progress records
- no parent communication
- no curriculum management

solution: simple saas dashboard with:
- student roster + attendance
- grades/progress tracking
- email + sms parent notifications
- homework + assignment pipeline
- class schedule management

setup:
- build in 40 hours (firebase, next.js, stripe)
- charge $500/month per madrassa
- 5 customers = $2.5k/month = $30k/year
- cost: $50/month hosting

why they buy:
- competitor tools are $2k/month (built for corporate schools)
- they value organization (madrassas want order)
- parents demand communication (modern expectation)
- it's cheaper than hiring an admin

scale path:
- month 1: 5 local madrassas = $2.5k
- month 6: 20 madrassas (referral network) = $10k
- year 2: 50+ madrassas = $25k/month

geographic play: start in city with dense muslim community. scale to neighboring cities. then regional, then national.

moat: switching cost (data lock-in) + community network effects (madrassas talk to each other).

this is unsexy b2b but prints money.
