# Post 005 — Scripture Variants Play

**Platform:** X (Twitter)
**Length:** Thread (5 tweets)
**Niche:** Faith

---

deployed 8 scripture reading apps targeting different Christian denominations. each reads a different translation + includes denominational commentary.

apps:
- catholic-scripture (douay-rheims + catechism notes)
- orthodox-scripture (septuagint + patristic readings)
- evangelical-scripture (kjv + topical prayer)
- lutheran-scripture (martin luther notes)
- plus 4 more

why separate apps? denominational readers don't want ecumenical commentary. they want their tradition represented. app store features them separately. each gets a shot at ranking.

time per app: 2 hours (copy template, swap commentary, rebrand).
cost: $0.
revenue potential: $100-300/mo per app (smaller niches than quran).

the play exploits two things:
1. app store treats variants as independent apps
2. religious niches are tribal (they'll choose their tradition first)

scale: 20+ faith-based variants across traditions + niches = $3k-5k/mo passive.

most people don't build variants because it "feels wrong". actually, it's respecting reader preferences and leveraging app store visibility. it's the right move.
