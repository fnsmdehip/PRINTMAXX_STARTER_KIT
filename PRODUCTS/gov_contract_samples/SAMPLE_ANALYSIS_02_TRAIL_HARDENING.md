## 2. Requirement Breakdown

The USDA Forest Service needs a Design-Build contractor to harden and improve the Lake Loop Section of the Multi-Use Trail (MUT) in Sam Houston National Forest, Texas.

**Core Requirements:**
- Design-Build construction contract (FAR Part 36)
- Trail hardening (surface stabilization, material application)
- Drainage improvements
- Structure improvements along the Lake Loop Section
- All labor, materials, equipment, supplies, supervision, and construction

**Contract Type:** Firm-Fixed-Price (FFP) Design-Build

**Procurement Method:** Competitive negotiation under FAR Part 15 (best value, not lowest price)

**Key Legal Note:** Architect-Engineer services, if required, are incidental to construction and NOT procured under the Brooks Act (40 U.S.C. Chapter 11). This means A-E qualifications-based selection does not apply. Price is a factor.

## 5. Competitive Landscape

**Likely competitors:**
- Regional civil engineering firms with outdoor/recreation construction experience
- Trail construction specialists (e.g., firms specializing in national forest trail work)
- General contractors with USDA Forest Service past performance
- SBA 8(a) firms with construction capability

**Competitive advantage factors:**
1. Past performance with USDA Forest Service or National Park Service
2. Experience in Texas (climate, soil, vegetation knowledge)
3. Design-Build experience (not just construction)
4. Small business certifications (8(a), HUBZone, SDVOSB)
5. Knowledge of multi-use trail standards (FHWA, Forest Service trail specs)
6. Environmental compliance experience in national forests

**Key differentiator:** If you have done trail work in Sam Houston National Forest or similar East Texas pine forests before, that is a significant advantage. The soil conditions (sandy loam, high water table near lake) require specific hardening approaches.

## 7. Bid/No-Bid Recommendation

### RECOMMENDATION: BID (if qualified) / NO-BID (if no construction capability)

**BID if you have:**
1. Active trail or outdoor construction experience
2. Design capability (in-house PE or teaming partner)
3. Bonding capacity for estimated contract value ($200K-$800K)
4. Equipment for trail construction (mini excavators, compactors, material haulers)
5. Small business status under NAICS 237990

**Rationale for BID:**
1. **100% small business set-aside** eliminates large competitors. The $45M size standard is generous.
2. **FAR Part 15 best value** means technical quality matters, not just price. Strong past performance and technical approach beat a low bid.
3. **Design-Build** creates higher barriers to entry, reducing competition. Fewer firms have both design and construction capability.
4. **Federal forest trail work is repeat business.** The USDA Forest Service has an ongoing backlog of trail maintenance and improvement projects. Strong performance here leads to future opportunities.
5. **Firm-Fixed-Price** is predictable if you estimate correctly. No cost-plus risk.

**NO-BID if:**
- You have zero construction capability (this is a physical construction contract)
- You cannot secure bonding
- You have no trail, outdoor recreation, or civil engineering experience
- You cannot mobilize to East Texas
