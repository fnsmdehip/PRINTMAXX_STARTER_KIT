# FOIA Request Templates for Government Contract Intelligence

## Overview

The Freedom of Information Act (5 U.S.C. 552) gives any person the right to request access to federal agency records. Government contracts are public records. You can FOIA:
- The original solicitation (RFP/RFQ)
- Technical evaluation summaries
- Names of all offerors (bidders)
- Awarded pricing/rate cards
- Past performance evaluations
- Modification documents

**Cost:** Most FOIA requests are free if you request "fee waiver for media/research purposes" or if processing costs are under $25. Some agencies charge $0.10-$0.25/page for large requests.

**Timeline:** Agencies have 20 business days to respond (often take 30-90 days in practice).

**Where to file:** Each agency has a FOIA office. Use the FOIA.gov portal or email the agency's FOIA office directly.

## Template 1: Request for Solicitation and Evaluation (Primary Template)

Use this to get the full picture on any contract award.

```
Subject: FOIA Request - Contract [AWARD_ID]

[Agency FOIA Office]
[Agency Name]
[Address]

Dear FOIA Officer:

Under the Freedom of Information Act (5 U.S.C. 552), I am requesting
copies of the following records related to contract award [AWARD_ID],
awarded to [RECIPIENT_NAME] on or about [START_DATE]:

1. The original solicitation documents (RFP, RFQ, or equivalent),
   including the Statement of Work (SOW) or Performance Work Statement
   (PWS), and all evaluation criteria.

2. The source selection decision document or evaluation summary,
   including technical scores and evaluation narratives for all
   offerors. I understand that proprietary information may be redacted
   pursuant to Exemption 4, but request that the names of all offerors
   and their respective evaluation ratings be released.

3. The names of all companies that submitted proposals or quotes
   in response to the solicitation.

4. The awarded contract, including all pricing (line items, labor
   rates, and total value), and any subsequent modifications.

5. Any pre-award surveys or market research documents related to
   this procurement.

I am a researcher compiling data on federal procurement patterns for
public interest analysis. I request a fee waiver under 5 U.S.C.
552(a)(4)(A)(iii), as disclosure of this information is in the public
interest and will contribute to public understanding of government
operations.

If the fee waiver is not granted, I agree to pay reasonable fees up
to $50.00 without prior approval. If costs exceed this amount, please
contact me before processing.

I prefer to receive responsive documents in electronic format (PDF)
via email at [YOUR_EMAIL].

If any portion of this request is denied, please identify the specific
exemption(s) relied upon and release any reasonably segregable
non-exempt portions.

Thank you for your prompt attention to this request.

Sincerely,
[YOUR_NAME]
[YOUR_ADDRESS]
[YOUR_EMAIL]
[YOUR_PHONE]
[DATE]
```

## Template 2: Request for Losing Bidder Information Only (Quickest Response)

Use this when you only need competitor names. Simpler request = faster response.

```
Subject: FOIA Request - Offeror List for Contract [AWARD_ID]

Dear FOIA Officer:

Under the Freedom of Information Act (5 U.S.C. 552), I am requesting
the names of all companies that submitted proposals, quotes, or bids
in response to the solicitation that resulted in contract [AWARD_ID],
awarded to [RECIPIENT_NAME].

This is a narrow request for non-exempt information (offeror names
are routinely released). I request a fee waiver as this information
contributes to public understanding of government procurement
competition.

I prefer electronic delivery to [YOUR_EMAIL].

Sincerely,
[YOUR_NAME]
[YOUR_EMAIL]
[DATE]
```

## Template 3: Request for Pricing and Rate Cards

Use this to get the actual pricing the winner charged. Essential for your "40% less" pitch.

```
Subject: FOIA Request - Pricing Information for Contract [AWARD_ID]

Dear FOIA Officer:

Under the Freedom of Information Act (5 U.S.C. 552), I request copies
of the following pricing-related documents for contract [AWARD_ID],
awarded to [RECIPIENT_NAME]:

1. The awarded contract pricing schedule, including all labor category
   rates, fixed prices, and/or cost estimates.

2. Any contract modifications that adjusted pricing.

3. The Independent Government Cost Estimate (IGCE) prepared for this
   procurement, if releasable.

I understand that certain pricing information may be withheld under
Exemption 4 (trade secrets/commercial information), but note that
unit prices in government contracts are generally releasable per
established FOIA case law. I request that the agency release all
reasonably segregable non-exempt pricing information.

I request a fee waiver. I prefer electronic delivery.

Sincerely,
[YOUR_NAME]
[YOUR_EMAIL]
[DATE]
```

## Template 4: Request for Past Performance Information

Use this to understand how well the current vendor is performing. Poor performance = recompete opportunity.

```
Subject: FOIA Request - Performance Information for Contract [AWARD_ID]

Dear FOIA Officer:

Under the Freedom of Information Act (5 U.S.C. 552), I request:

1. Contractor Performance Assessment Reports (CPARs) filed for
   [RECIPIENT_NAME] under contract [AWARD_ID].

2. Any quality assurance surveillance plan (QASP) results or
   performance monitoring reports.

3. Any cure notices, show cause notices, or performance deficiency
   letters issued under this contract.

I understand that CPAR narratives may contain exempt information.
I request release of all non-exempt portions, including overall
performance ratings.

Fee waiver requested. Electronic delivery preferred.

Sincerely,
[YOUR_NAME]
[YOUR_EMAIL]
[DATE]
```

## Template 5: Batch Request (Multiple Awards at Once)

Use this to FOIA multiple related contracts in a single request. More efficient.

```
Subject: FOIA Request - Multiple Contract Awards in [NAICS_CODE/CATEGORY]

Dear FOIA Officer:

Under the Freedom of Information Act (5 U.S.C. 552), I request the
following documents for each of the contract awards listed below:

- Names of all offerors who submitted proposals
- Awarded pricing (labor rates and/or line item prices)
- Source selection decision summary (with proprietary info redacted)

Contract Awards:
1. [AWARD_ID_1] - [RECIPIENT_1] - [AMOUNT_1]
2. [AWARD_ID_2] - [RECIPIENT_2] - [AMOUNT_2]
3. [AWARD_ID_3] - [RECIPIENT_3] - [AMOUNT_3]
4. [AWARD_ID_4] - [RECIPIENT_4] - [AMOUNT_4]
5. [AWARD_ID_5] - [RECIPIENT_5] - [AMOUNT_5]

All contracts are in the [NAICS_CODE] category and were awarded by
[AGENCY_NAME] within the past 12 months.

I request a fee waiver for public interest research. If fees apply,
I agree to pay up to $100.00 without prior approval.

Electronic delivery preferred to [YOUR_EMAIL].

Sincerely,
[YOUR_NAME]
[YOUR_EMAIL]
[DATE]
```

## FOIA Pro Tips

### Speed Up Your Response

1. **Be specific.** "All documents related to contract X" gets a faster response than "all IT contracts from 2025."
2. **Reference the award ID.** USAspending award IDs map directly to agency contract numbers.
3. **Request electronic delivery.** Eliminates copying costs and speeds up processing.
4. **File with the right office.** Sub-agencies (DEA, FAA, DISA) have their own FOIA offices separate from parent agencies.

### Fee Waivers

Agencies must grant fee waivers when disclosure is:
- "Likely to contribute significantly to public understanding of government operations"
- "Not primarily in the commercial interest of the requester"

Frame your request as research/journalism/public interest analysis. If you run a blog or newsletter that publishes government spending analysis, reference it.

### What's Exempt (What You Won't Get)

- **Exemption 4:** Trade secrets and confidential commercial information. Agencies often redact specific technical approaches and proprietary methodologies. But unit prices are generally NOT exempt.
- **Exemption 5:** Deliberative process. Internal agency deliberations before award decision.
- **Exemption 6:** Personal privacy. Names/titles of government employees are usually released, but personal contact info may be redacted.

### What You Will Get (Almost Always Released)

- Names of all companies that bid
- Overall evaluation ratings (Acceptable/Good/Outstanding)
- Awarded pricing (especially unit prices and labor rates)
- Statement of Work
- Contract modifications
- Period of performance details

### Appeal If Denied

If your request is denied or heavily redacted, you have the right to appeal. Most agencies have a simple appeal process. Many release additional documents on appeal.

## Batch FOIA Workflow

For the PRINTMAXX lead gen pipeline:

1. **Run USAspending scraper** weekly to get new awards
2. **Select top 10-20 targets** per week (your NAICS codes, your agency targets)
3. **File batch FOIA requests** (Template 5) to each agency -- group by agency
4. **Track responses** in a spreadsheet (request date, agency, response date, documents received)
5. **Extract leads** from responses (losing bidder names, their capabilities, contact info)
6. **Enrich leads** via SAM.gov (get addresses, POCs, size standards)
7. **Cold outreach** using the cold email templates

Expected timeline: File 20 FOIAs per week, get 10-15 responses within 30 days, extract 3-5 warm leads per response = 30-75 leads per month.
