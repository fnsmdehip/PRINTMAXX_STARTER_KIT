## Section 2: Category Analysis

### IT Services ($15.0M across 30 awards)

**Dominant buyers:** DoD (DISA, DFAS), DOJ (DEA), State Department, Treasury (IRS)
**Common work types:** Software development, system modernization, cybersecurity, Power Platform, data warehousing
**Winner profile:** Small-to-mid IT firms, mostly VA/MD/DC based. Some large (CDW, Deloitte) but majority are 8(a)/HUBZone eligible
**Undercut opportunity:** HIGH. Power Platform, cloud migration, and data analytics work is commoditized. Boutique firms beat big primes on rate cards.

### Management Consulting ($15.0M across 30 awards)

**Dominant buyers:** DoD, DOJ, GSA, USAID
**Common work types:** Strategic planning, construction management advisory, program management, CONOPS
**Winner profile:** Mix of niche consultancies (ProCon, InflowLogistics) and large firms (PAE/Amentum)
**Undercut opportunity:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Strategic planning and program management have high margins. Small teams deliver same quality at lower overhead.

### Janitorial & Facility Services ($14.9M across 30 awards)

**Dominant buyers:** DoD, VA, DHS
**Common work types:** Custodial, grounds maintenance, facility maintenance
**Winner profile:** Regional facility services companies
**Undercut opportunity:** HIGH. Facility services is a relationship business. Losing bidders on one contract often win the next. Perfect for cold outreach.

### Security Services ($14.7M across 30 awards)

**Dominant buyers:** DoD, DHS, DOJ
**Common work types:** Guard services, security systems, access control
**Winner profile:** Mid-size security firms, often veteran-owned
**Undercut opportunity:** MEDIUM. Requires cleared personnel and physical presence. Good for teaming arrangements.

### Staffing ($14.7M across 30 awards)

**Dominant buyers:** HHS, DoD, VA
**Common work types:** Temporary staffing, admin support, technical staffing
**Winner profile:** National and regional staffing agencies
**Undercut opportunity:** HIGH. Staffing markup rates vary wildly (25-80%). Undercutting by 10-15% on markup wins contracts.

### Engineering ($15.0M across 30 awards)

**Dominant buyers:** DoD, DOT, Interior
**Common work types:** Engineering design, environmental assessment, surveying
**Winner profile:** Engineering firms, often PE-licensed
**Undercut opportunity:** MEDIUM. Requires technical certifications but margins are high.

### Training ($14.7M across 30 awards)

**Dominant buyers:** DoD, HHS, VA
**Common work types:** Professional development, IT training, workforce development
**Winner profile:** Training companies, some online-first
**Undercut opportunity:** HIGH. AI-augmented training delivery slashes costs. Perfect for "same quality, 40% less" pitch.

### Marketing & Advertising ($14.2M across 30 awards)

**Dominant buyers:** DoD, HHS, USAID
**Common work types:** Media buying, PR, communications, digital marketing
**Winner profile:** Small creative agencies, some large holding company shops
**Undercut opportunity:** HIGH. Government creative work is overpriced. Digital-first agencies destroy traditional shops on ROI.

## Section 5: How to Use This Report

### Step 1: Identify Targets
Pick 5-10 awards in your service area from this report. Focus on:
- Awards in your NAICS code
- Awards where the winner is a similar-size company (not Deloitte)
- Awards with upcoming renewal dates

### Step 2: File FOIA Requests
Use the FOIA template (included separately) to request:
- Original solicitation documents
- Technical evaluation summary
- List of offerors (losing bidders)
- Awarded pricing (line items, rates)

### Step 3: Build Your Lead List
From FOIA responses, extract:
- Losing bidder company names
- What they proposed
- Why they lost (evaluation feedback)
- Their contact info (SAM.gov lookup)

### Step 4: Cold Outreach
Contact losing bidders with:
- "I saw you competed on [contract]. I can deliver [service] at 40% less."
- Or partner: "Team with us on the recompete. We bring [capability] you were missing."

### Step 5: Position for Recompetes
For expiring contracts:
- Contact the agency's contracting officer (listed in solicitation)
- Attend industry days
- Get on relevant contract vehicles (GSA Schedule, Alliant 2, etc.)
