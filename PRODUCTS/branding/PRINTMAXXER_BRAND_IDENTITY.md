## 1. Logo concept

**Primary mark: PRINTMAXXER terminal wordmark**

text-based logo. no icons, no mascots, no abstract shapes. just the word rendered like it's being typed into a terminal.

- typeface: monospace, heavy weight. think SF Mono Bold or JetBrains Mono ExtraBold
- "PRINT" in neon green (#00FF41) on black (#0D0D0D)
- "MAXXER" in white (#FFFFFF) on black (#0D0D0D)
- underscore cursor blinking after the R: `PRINTMAXXER_`
- slight CRT screen glow effect on the green text (1-2px outer glow, 30% opacity)
- no gradients. no shadows. no 3D. flat and clean

**AI generation prompt (for Midjourney/DALL-E/Leonardo):**
```
monospace terminal text "PRINTMAXXER" with blinking cursor on pure black background,
neon green (#00FF41) glow on "PRINT", white text on "MAXXER", CRT monitor aesthetic,
scanlines subtle, no icons, no symbols, flat design, 16:9 aspect ratio, dark terminal
window chrome at top with three colored dots (red/yellow/green), --ar 16:9 --v 6
```

**Alternate mark: abbreviated**
- `PM>_` in neon green monospace on black
- for favicons, app icons, small spaces
- the `>` suggests terminal prompt, the `_` suggests always typing, always building

**AI generation prompt (favicon):**
```
minimalist terminal icon, letters "PM" in neon green monospace font followed by
">" symbol and blinking cursor, pure black background, 1:1 square, pixel-perfect,
no gradients, CRT glow effect subtle --ar 1:1 --v 6
```

**Usage rules:**
- always on dark backgrounds. never on white or light
- minimum clear space: 1x height of text on all sides
- never stretch, rotate, or add effects beyond the glow
- if background isn't pure black, use #0D0D0D minimum

## 4. Content pillars

### Pillar 1: building in public (40% of content)

what gets posted: real revenue numbers (including $0), what shipped this week, screenshots of dashboards, failed launches, cost breakdowns, time tracking.

why it works: people follow for the honesty. the $0 starting point makes it relatable. the 11 simultaneous methods makes it unique. nobody else is running this many in parallel and documenting it.

content formats:
- weekly revenue updates (screenshot + commentary)
- "shipped today" posts (what went live)
- cost breakdowns ("this method cost $47 to set up. here's what it returned.")
- milestone posts ("first $100" / "first 1,000 followers on X account")

example topics:
- "day 14. 3 of 11 methods are generating traffic. 0 revenue yet. here's what i'm learning."
- "my AI persona got 847 followers in 6 days. cost: $0. time: 4 hours setup. here's exactly how."
- "killed method #7 today. cold calling via VA was burning $200/week with 0 conversions. moving budget to paid ads."

### Pillar 2: automation and AI tools (25% of content)

what gets posted: specific tools with actual costs, automation workflows with screenshots, AI generation comparisons, time saved calculations, stack breakdowns.

why it works: tool content gets saved and shared. people want to know what tools a cracked builder actually uses vs what they're paid to promote.

content formats:
- "tool of the week" with cost, ROI, screenshot
- workflow breakdowns ("input X, press button, get Y")
- AI comparison posts (which model for which task)
- "i automated X. took 3 hours. saves 5 hours/week."

example topics:
- "this $30/mo tool monitors 200+ competitor pages and alerts me the second anything changes. visualping.io."
- "my content pipeline: write 1 post, buffer distributes to 6 platforms, costs $0. here's the exact setup."
- "tested 4 AI image generators for my persona account. here's which one doesn't get flagged."

### Pillar 3: solopreneur tactics with real numbers (20% of content)

what gets posted: specific tactics that worked (with numbers), tactics that failed (with numbers), platform-specific hacks, growth experiments with before/after.

why it works: specificity is rare. most accounts say "post consistently." we say "i posted 6x/day for 14 days on X. engagement went from 23 avg to 847 avg. here's what changed."

content formats:
- experiment results with before/after numbers
- platform algorithm findings
- pricing experiments
- conversion rate breakdowns

example topics:
- "posting at 6am vs 9am on X. tested for 30 days. 6am got 2.3x more impressions. thread:"
- "changed my cold email subject line from question to statement. open rate went 18% to 41%. the exact templates:"
- "TikTok shop nano creators (<10K) get 4.3x higher click-through than 100K+ accounts. targeting small influencers for affiliate."

### Pillar 4: failures and honest lessons (10% of content)

what gets posted: methods that got killed and why, money lost, time wasted, bad decisions, course corrections.

why it works: vulnerability builds trust. and failure content gets higher engagement than success content because people feel seen.

content formats:
- "killed this method" posts
- "biggest mistake this week" posts
- "what i'd do differently" retrospectives
- honest cost of failure breakdowns

example topics:
- "spent 3 weeks building an app nobody wanted. the 4 signals i ignored that should have told me to stop."
- "bought a warmed twitter account for $45. got suspended in 72 hours. here's the red flag i missed."
- "my revenue projection said $2K/mo by month 2. actual: $0. here's why the model was wrong."

### Pillar 5: tool reviews with actual ROI (5% of content)

what gets posted: tools we actually use with real cost, real time-to-value, real ROI calculations. not sponsored, not affiliate (unless disclosed).

why it works: honest tool reviews are rare because everyone's got an affiliate deal. when you show the real numbers, people trust you more.

content formats:
- "i paid $X for Y. here's what happened after Z days."
- tool comparison tables with actual costs
- "i replaced X with Y. saved $Z/month."
- stack audits ("my full stack costs $127/month. here's every tool.")

example topics:
- "my complete $127/mo tech stack for running 11 methods. every tool, every cost, nothing hidden."
- "beehiiv vs substack vs convertkit for a new newsletter. tested all 3. beehiiv won. here's why."
- "i tried 6 AI video generators. only 2 are worth paying for. the rest are demos pretending to be products."

## 6. Color scheme

### Primary palette

| Role | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| Terminal green | #00FF41 | 0, 255, 65 | primary brand color, text highlights, CTAs, logo |
| Deep black | #0D0D0D | 13, 13, 13 | backgrounds, primary surface |
| Pure white | #FFFFFF | 255, 255, 255 | body text on dark, secondary elements |
| Alert red | #FF0040 | 255, 0, 64 | warnings, failures, losses, killed methods |

### Extended palette

| Role | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| Terminal green dim | #00CC33 | 0, 204, 51 | hover states, secondary green |
| Terminal green glow | #00FF4133 | 0, 255, 65, 20% | glow effects, backgrounds at low opacity |
| Dark surface | #1A1A1A | 26, 26, 26 | cards, elevated surfaces, code blocks |
| Medium gray | #666666 | 102, 102, 102 | secondary text, timestamps, metadata |
| Amber | #FFB800 | 255, 184, 0 | warnings, caution states, in-progress |

### Application rules

**backgrounds:** always #0D0D0D or #1A1A1A. never white. never light gray. this is a terminal brand.

**text hierarchy:**
1. #00FF41 for headlines, key numbers, CTAs, emphasis
2. #FFFFFF for body text, descriptions
3. #666666 for timestamps, secondary info, metadata

**accent usage:**
- #FF0040 only for failure content, killed methods, losses, warnings
- #FFB800 only for in-progress status, caution notes
- #00FF41 for everything positive: revenue, growth, shipping, wins

**social media:**
- twitter/X: dark mode default. green links match the brand naturally
- instagram: stories in black with green text overlays
- thumbnails: black background, one line of green text, white subtitle
- never use the brand colors on a light background

### Contrast ratios (WCAG AA compliance)

| Combination | Ratio | Status |

| #00FF41 on #0D0D0D | 14.4:1 | passes AAA |
| #FFFFFF on #0D0D0D | 19.4:1 | passes AAA |

## 8. Tone and personality

### what we are
- a cracked indie hacker who ships relentlessly
- honest about failures (posts losses, not just wins)
- specific with numbers (never vague, never rounded unless the real number is round)
- slightly irreverent (doesn't take the "entrepreneur" label too seriously)
- generous with tactics (shares the actual method, not just the result)
- building a machine, not a personal brand (the system is the star, not the person)

### what we are not
- a guru selling courses about making money
- a marketing agency with polished copy
- an influencer who only posts wins
- a motivational account with quotes over sunsets
- someone who says "just be consistent" without showing how

### voice calibration

before posting anything, run these checks:

1. would @pipelineabuser actually post this? (aggressive, specific, consequence-first)
2. is there a specific number i can add? (always yes. find it.)
3. did i lead with what happened, not what i'm about to explain? (consequence first)
4. would this get saved/bookmarked, not just liked? (save-worthy > like-worthy)
5. does this feel like texting a smart friend? (if it feels like a blog post, rewrite it)

### banned phrases (in addition to copy-style.md)
- "solopreneurship journey"
- "building something special"
- "excited to announce"
- "thrilled to share"
- "stay tuned"
- "drop a comment if"
- "who else?"
- "can I get a"
- "let's gooo"
- any variation of "hustle culture" framing

## 10. Platform-specific posting rules

### Twitter/X
- 5-6 posts per day minimum
- mix of threads (1-2/week), single tweets (daily), quote tweets with takes
- first line is the hook. if the first line doesn't make someone stop scrolling, rewrite it
- use line breaks aggressively. walls of text die on twitter
- no hashtags in main tweets (looks desperate). hashtag replies only if strategic
- reply to bigger accounts with actual value, not "great post"
- schedule via Buffer. batch create weekly

### Instagram
- 3-4 posts per week (carousel + reels)
- carousels: terminal-styled slides, black background, green text, one insight per slide
- reels: screen recordings of building, terminal commands running, dashboards updating
- stories: daily. behind the scenes. polls for what to build next
- no stock photos. no lifestyle shots. terminal aesthetic or raw screenshots only

### TikTok
- 1-2 posts per day
- screen recordings with voiceover
- "how i built X in Y hours" format
- "this tool costs $Z and does [thing]" format
- keep under 60 seconds. front-load the hook in first 3 seconds
- trending sounds are fine here (different audience than twitter)

### LinkedIn
- 2-3 posts per week
- more polished than twitter but same honesty
- focus on: automation wins, revenue experiments, tool stacks, hiring VAs
- no "i'm humbled" energy. just facts and numbers
- longer form is fine here. linkedin rewards length

### YouTube
- 1 video per week minimum
- topics: weekly updates, tool deep dives, method breakdowns, failure postmortems
- thumbnail: black background, one line of green text, white subtitle
- no face cam required. screen recording + voiceover works
- shorts: repurpose twitter threads as 60-second videos

### Reddit
- no self-promotion. contribute to communities first
- share tactics in relevant subreddits (r/SideProject, r/juststart, r/Entrepreneur)
- link to own content only when someone asks or it's genuinely the best answer
- different username than @PRINTMAXXER if possible (reddit hates brands)

## 11. Visual content rules

### Screenshots and dashboards
- always dark mode
- crop out personal info, bookmarks bar, other tabs
- add neon green annotations if highlighting something specific
- no watermarks

### Carousel slides (Instagram/LinkedIn)
- black background (#0D0D0D)
- monospace font only
- one key insight per slide
- green text for numbers and emphasis
- white text for explanations
- slide 1: hook (green text, large)
- slides 2-8: content (white text, normal)
- last slide: CTA or summary (green text)

### Video thumbnails
- black background
- 1 line of green text (the hook)
- 1 line of white text below (the context)
- no face. no arrows. no circles. no red borders
- look like a terminal output, not a youtube thumbnail

### Memes and casual content
- terminal-themed memes allowed
- green/black aesthetic maintained even in humor
- self-deprecating > dunking on others
- reference actual experiences, not generic solopreneur tropes

## 12. Brand don'ts

1. never use light mode aesthetics
2. never use stock photography
3. never use motivational quotes without data backing them
4. never claim revenue you haven't made
5. never hide failures or losses
6. never shill tools without disclosing affiliate relationships
7. never post "just be consistent" type advice without the specific how
8. never use corporate language or marketing speak
9. never pretend to have a team if it's solo
10. never round numbers to sound better ($847 not "about $1K")
