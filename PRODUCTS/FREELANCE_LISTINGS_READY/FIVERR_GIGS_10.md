WHY MOST COLD EMAILS FAIL (AND HOW I FIX EACH PROBLEM):

Problem 1: Too long. Nobody reads a 200-word email from a stranger. Fix: I write emails under 85 words. The entire pitch in 4-5 sentences. Every word earns its place or gets cut.

Problem 2: Generic opener. "I came across your company and was impressed by..." is invisible. Every person in that inbox got the same opener today. Fix: Pattern-interrupt first lines that reference something specific about the recipient. Not "I saw your website." Something that proves I looked at their actual business.

Problem 3: No proof. "We help companies increase revenue" means nothing. Fix: Specific numbers from specific clients in specific industries. "Helped a dental practice in Austin add 23 new patients in 30 days" beats "we help businesses grow."

Problem 4: Weak CTA. "Would love to chat sometime" is not a CTA. It's a wish. Fix: Binary question that's easy to answer. "Worth a 15-minute call next Tuesday or Thursday?" is specific, low-commitment, and easy to reply "yes" to.

Problem 5: No follow-up sequence. 80% of replies come from emails 2-4, not email 1. Most people send one email and give up. Fix: I write full 3-5 email sequences with different angles, different proof points, and a strategic breakup email that often gets the highest reply rate of the entire sequence.

WHAT I DELIVER:

Full cold email sequences (3-5 emails per sequence):

Email 1 - The Opener:
Pattern-interrupt subject line + specific observation about their business + value prop in one sentence + proof point + soft CTA. Under 85 words.

Email 2 - Different Angle:
New subject line + approach from a different pain point + different case study + direct ask. If Email 1 was about revenue, Email 2 is about time savings.

Email 3 - Social Proof:
Third approach + "I helped [similar company] achieve [specific result]" + screenshot or link to case study + "worth a conversation?"

Email 4 - The Breakup:
"Looks like the timing isn't right. No worries." This email consistently gets the highest reply rate because it removes pressure. FOMO activates. People who ignored 3 emails suddenly reply to the one that says you're going away.

Email 5 (Premium only) - 30-Day Re-engagement:
Sent 30 days after the breakup. New angle, new proof point. Catches people whose situation changed since the initial sequence.
