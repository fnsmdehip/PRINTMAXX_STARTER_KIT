Most family history dies with the last person who remembers it.

Your grandparent knows where the family came from, what they left behind, and why. That knowledge disappears the day they're gone — unless someone writes it down first.

- One-time $17 — no annual renewal, no subscription

**What's inside:**

Interview guides for living relatives. 47 specific questions across 6 life categories: childhood, family origin, work and struggle, relationships, beliefs, and what they want remembered. Designed to be asked during a single afternoon visit — not a research project.

Heritage region history templates. Understand what was happening in the region your family came from during the years they were there. Context that transforms names and dates into actual history.

Family timeline. A structured format for mapping births, deaths, migrations, wars, economic events, and decisions against the historical backdrop. Turns scattered memories into a coherent narrative.

Writing prompts for each section. 60+ prompts for turning recorded answers into readable stories. No writing experience needed.

Research checklist. 31 document types to gather: birth certificates, census records, immigration papers, military records, property deeds, photographs. Organized by difficulty and typical location.

Photo documentation guide. How to photograph, label, and preserve physical photographs. The metadata system that makes photos findable 30 years later.

Storage and sharing. Digital formats, cloud backup strategy, and how to share the finished workbook with the full family.
