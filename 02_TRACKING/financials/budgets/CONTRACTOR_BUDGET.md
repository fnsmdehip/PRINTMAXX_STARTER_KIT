# Contractor Budget Guide

## Philosophy

- Hire to buy back time, not to feel like a boss
- Start with single projects, graduate to ongoing
- Pay well for quality (cheap = expensive problems)
- One contractor at a time until systems exist

---

## When to Hire (Decision Framework)

### Hire When You Can Answer YES to 3+

- [ ] I'm turning down revenue due to capacity
- [ ] This task takes 10+ hours/month
- [ ] The task is well-defined and repeatable
- [ ] I can afford 3 months of contractor cost
- [ ] I've done the task myself (can train/review)
- [ ] ROI is clear (time freed x my rate > cost)

### ROI Calculation

```
Should I hire a content writer?

Your time on content: 15 hrs/month
Your hourly value: $75/hr
Value of your time: $1,125

Contractor cost: $600/month
Time saved: 12 hrs (they handle 80%)
Value reclaimed: $900

Monthly ROI: $900 - $600 = $300 positive
Plus: You use freed time to earn more
```

### Don't Hire If

- Task is undefined or constantly changing
- You haven't done it yourself successfully
- It's a core competency (strategy, key decisions)
- Cash reserves < 3 months contractor cost
- You're hiring to avoid learning

## Contractor Roles by Phase

### Phase 1: Bootstrap ($0-1K revenue)

**Recommendation: No contractors yet**

Do everything yourself to:
- Understand all aspects of business
- Know what good output looks like
- Build systems before delegating

**Exception:** One-off design help ($50-100)

### Phase 2: Growth ($1-5K revenue)

**Budget: $100-300/month**

| Role | Hours/Week | Rate | Monthly Cost | Priority |
|------|------------|------|--------------|----------|
| Content writer | 5-8 | $25-40/hr | $100-320 | 1st hire |
| Graphic designer | 2-4 | $30-50/hr | $60-200 | 2nd hire |
| Virtual assistant | 3-5 | $15-25/hr | $45-125 | 3rd hire |

**First Hire: Content Writer**
- Draft blog posts from outlines
- Repurpose long-form to social
- Newsletter draft assistance
- SEO content creation

### Phase 3: Scale ($5-10K revenue)

**Budget: $500-1,200/month**

| Role | Hours/Week | Rate | Monthly Cost |
|------|------------|------|--------------|
| Content writer | 10-15 | $30-50/hr | $300-750 |
| Graphic designer | 5-8 | $35-60/hr | $175-480 |
| Video editor | 5-10 | $25-40/hr | $125-400 |
| Virtual assistant | 8-12 | $18-30/hr | $144-360 |

**Team Structure:**
- Writer handles 80% of content production
- Designer creates all visual assets
- VA handles scheduling, inbox, admin
- You focus on strategy, high-value content, sales

### Phase 4: Expansion ($10K+ revenue)

**Budget: $2,000-5,000/month**

| Role | Hours/Week | Rate | Monthly Cost |
|------|------------|------|--------------|
| Content lead | 20+ | $40-75/hr | $800-1,500 |

## Hiring Process

### Step 1: Define the Role

**Create a clear scope document:**
```
Role: Content Writer
Hours: 8-10/week
Rate: $35-45/hr
Duration: 3-month trial

Responsibilities:
- Write 2 blog posts/week from outlines
- Edit and publish in WordPress
- Create social snippets for each post

Requirements:
- Portfolio of similar work
- Native English writing
- Experience with [your niche]
- Reliable, meets deadlines

Not responsible for:
- Topic research (you provide)
- Graphics (designer handles)
- Promotion (you handle)
```

### Step 2: Source Candidates

| Platform | Best For | Quality | Cost |
|----------|----------|---------|------|
| Upwork | All roles | Variable | 5-20% fee |
| Fiverr | Quick tasks | Variable | 5.5% fee |
| Twitter | Niche experts | High | None |
| LinkedIn | Professionals | High | None |
| Referrals | Trusted | High | None |
| Contra | Freelancers | Medium-High | Lower fees |

### Step 3: Evaluate

**Test project approach:**
- Pay for a small test (1-2 hours)
- Evaluate: quality, communication, deadline adherence
- Give feedback, see how they respond

**Red flags:**
- Misses initial deadline
- Doesn't ask clarifying questions
- Poor communication
- Defensive about feedback
- Price dramatically lower than market

### Step 4: Onboard

**First week:**
- Share brand guidelines
- Walk through tools and processes
- Do first project together
- Provide detailed feedback

**First month:**
- Weekly check-ins
- Clear feedback loops
- Adjust scope as needed
- Build relationship

## Payment Structures

### Hourly

**Best for:** Variable workloads, undefined scope

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Flexibility | Unpredictable costs |
| Pay for actual work | Time tracking needed |
| Easy to adjust | May optimize for hours |

### Project-Based

**Best for:** Defined deliverables, one-time work

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Predictable cost | Scope creep risk |
| Outcome-focused | Hard to adjust |
| No time tracking | May rush quality |

### Retainer

**Best for:** Ongoing relationships, consistent work

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Guaranteed availability | Commitment required |
| Often lower effective rate | Use-it-or-lose-it |
| Simpler budgeting | Less flexibility |

### Recommended Progression

1. **Start:** Small project (test fit)
2. **Continue:** Hourly (build trust)
3. **Scale:** Retainer (lock in good people)

## Managing Contractors

### Communication Cadence

| Frequency | Purpose | Method |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Daily | Quick updates | Slack/async |
| Weekly | Check-in, feedback | 15-30 min call or async |
| Monthly | Review, planning | 30-60 min call |
| Quarterly | Relationship review | Deep conversation |

### Feedback Framework

**SBI Model:**
- **Situation:** When/where
- **Behavior:** What happened (facts)
- **Impact:** Effect on work/business

**Example:**
"In yesterday's blog post (situation), the CTA was missing from the conclusion (behavior), which likely reduced email signups from that post (impact). Can we make sure every post has the standard CTA?"

### Documentation Requirements

- Project briefs (every project)
- Style guides (one-time, update quarterly)
- Process docs (as you build them)
- Feedback logs (continuous)

## Common Contractor Mistakes

1. **Hiring too early** - Do it yourself first
2. **Hiring too cheap** - Quality costs, cheap is expensive
3. **Unclear expectations** - Document everything
4. **Micromanaging** - Hire good people, let them work
5. **No feedback** - They can't improve without it
6. **Single point of failure** - Have backup relationships
7. **Treating like employees** - They're independent, respect that
8. **Not investing in relationship** - Good contractors are assets

## Exit/Transition Planning

### When to End a Contractor Relationship

- Quality consistently below standard
- Deadlines regularly missed
- Communication issues persist
- Business needs change
- Better fit found

### Transition Process

1. Give 2-4 weeks notice (more for long relationships)
2. Document their processes
3. Have them train replacement if possible
4. End professionally (you may need them again)
5. Final payment promptly

### Contractor to Employee

**Consider when:**
- Working 30+ hours/week consistently
- Core to business operations
- Want more control/exclusivity
- Long-term commitment mutual

**Process:**
- Discuss openly
- Consult accountant/lawyer
- Adjust compensation for benefits
- Formal employment agreement
