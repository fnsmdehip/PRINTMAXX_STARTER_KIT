# Agency Services Unit Economics

## Hourly Rate Analysis

### Rate Positioning

| Level | Hourly Rate | Who Charges This |
|-------|-------------|------------------|
| Entry | $25-50/hr | New freelancers, commodity work |
| Mid | $75-125/hr | Experienced freelancers |
| Expert | $150-250/hr | Specialists, consultants |
| Premium | $300-500/hr | Top-tier consultants, agencies |

### Rate by Service Type

| Service | Typical Range | Market Rate |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Content writing | $50-150/hr | $75/hr |
| SEO consulting | $100-200/hr | $150/hr |
| Automation setup | $100-250/hr | $175/hr |
| Technical consulting | $150-300/hr | $200/hr |
| Strategy/advisory | $200-500/hr | $300/hr |

### True Effective Rate

```
Effective Rate = Billed Hours / Total Hours Worked x Hourly Rate

Reality check:
- 8 hour day
- 5-6 billable hours (admin, sales, breaks)
- Utilization: 62-75%

If charging $100/hr with 65% utilization:
Effective Rate = 0.65 x $100 = $65/hr actual
```

## Scaling with Contractors

### When to Hire

**Signals you need help:**
- Turning down work due to capacity
- Working >50 hrs/week consistently
- Project backlogs >4 weeks
- Clients waiting for responses

**Financial threshold:**
```
Hire when: (Your rate - Contractor rate) x Available hours > Contractor cost

Example:
- You charge: $150/hr
- Contractor: $75/hr
- Extra 20 hrs/month available
- Profit: ($150 - $75) x 20 = $1,500/mo
```

### Contractor Economics

| Role | Their Rate | You Charge | Your Cut | Monthly (10hrs) |
|------|------------|------------|----------|-----------------|
| Junior dev | $30/hr | $100/hr | $70/hr | $700 |
| Content writer | $40/hr | $100/hr | $60/hr | $600 |
| Designer | $50/hr | $125/hr | $75/hr | $750 |
| Senior dev | $75/hr | $175/hr | $100/hr | $1,000 |

### Building a Contractor Network

**Phase 1: Test (1-3 months)**
- Hire for single project
- Pay market rate
- Evaluate quality and reliability

**Phase 2: Retain (3-6 months)**
- Offer consistent work
- Slightly above market rate
- Build relationship

**Phase 3: Scale (6+ months)**
- Priority access to your work
- Premium rate for reliability
- Consider profit sharing

### Contractor Management Costs

| Item | Time/Month | Opportunity Cost |
|------|------------|------------------|
| Finding contractors | 4-8 hrs/project | $400-800 |
| Onboarding | 2-4 hrs/person | $200-400 |
| Management | 1-2 hrs/contractor/week | $400-800/mo |
| Quality review | 0.5 hrs/deliverable | Variable |

**Factor in 5-10 hours/month management overhead per contractor**

## Revenue Models Comparison

### Solo Freelancer

```
Max Revenue = Billable Hours x Rate x Utilization

Example:
- 160 hrs/month available
- 75% utilization = 120 billable hours
- $150/hr rate
- Max monthly: $18,000
- Max annual: $216,000
```

**Ceiling:** $15-25K/month (burnout risk above)

### Solo + 2 Contractors

```
Your billable: 80 hrs x $150 = $12,000
Contractor 1: 40 hrs x $75 margin = $3,000
Contractor 2: 40 hrs x $75 margin = $3,000
Total: $18,000/month

With less personal time commitment.
```

**Ceiling:** $20-35K/month

### Small Agency (5 people)

```
Your billable: 40 hrs x $200 = $8,000
4 contractors: 160 hrs x $60 margin = $9,600
Total revenue: ~$40,000/month
Overhead: ~$10,000/month
Net: $30,000/month
```

**Ceiling:** $30-75K/month (higher complexity)

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring

- Show 3 options (cheap, recommended, premium)
- Middle option should be your target
- Premium anchors value perception

### Value Framing

Instead of: "$150/hr for consulting"
Say: "$3,000 to solve your X problem (saves $30K/year)"

### Confidence Pricing

| Signal | Effect on Pricing Power |
|--------|------------------------|
| Case studies | +20-30% |
| Testimonials | +10-20% |
| Niche specialization | +30-50% |
| Published content | +15-25% |
| Speaking/podcast | +20-40% |

## Action Items

1. **Start at $100/hr minimum** - $50/hr is a trap
2. **Productize 2-3 services** - Easier to sell, better margins
3. **Target 70% utilization** - Track weekly
4. **Add first contractor at $10K/mo revenue** - Don't wait too long
5. **Build retainer base** - 3-5 retainer clients = stable income
6. **Raise rates yearly** - 10-20% for existing, 20-30% for new clients
