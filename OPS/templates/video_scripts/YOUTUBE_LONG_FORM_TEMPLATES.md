# YouTube Long Form Script Templates

Templates for 8-20 minute YouTube videos.

---

## Template 1: Tutorial (10-15 min)

**Structure:**
```
[0:00-0:30] HOOK - What they'll learn, why it matters
[0:30-1:30] INTRO - Problem context, your credibility
[1:30-2:00] OVERVIEW - What we'll cover
[2:00-10:00] MAIN CONTENT - Step-by-step tutorial
[10:00-11:00] COMMON MISTAKES - What to avoid
[11:00-12:00] SUMMARY - Quick recap
[12:00-12:30] CTA - Subscribe, next video
```

**Script Framework:**

```
HOOK:
"By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how to [specific outcome].
I'm going to show you the exact [method/process] I use to [result].
No fluff, just the steps."

INTRO:
"If you've ever [struggled with thing], you're not alone.
Most people [common mistake].
I spent [time] figuring this out so you don't have to.
Here's what actually works."

OVERVIEW:
"We're going to cover:
1. [First main point]
2. [Second main point]
3. [Third main point]
Plus, I'll share [bonus] at the end."

MAIN CONTENT:
"Let's start with [step 1]...
[Detailed explanation with examples]

Next, [step 2]...
[Detailed explanation with examples]

Finally, [step 3]...
[Detailed explanation with examples]"

COMMON MISTAKES:
"Before we wrap up, here's what NOT to do:
- Don't [mistake 1]
- Avoid [mistake 2]
- Never [mistake 3]"

SUMMARY:
"Let's recap:
1. First, [step 1 summary]
2. Then, [step 2 summary]
3. Finally, [step 3 summary]"

CTA:
"If this helped, hit subscribe for more [topic] content.
Check out this video next for [related topic].
See you in the next one."
```

## Template 2: List/Ranking (12-18 min)

**Structure:**
```
[0:00-0:45] HOOK - Tease best item, create curiosity
[0:45-1:30] CONTEXT - Why this list matters
[1:30-14:00] LIST ITEMS - Each item with explanation
[14:00-15:00] HONORABLE MENTIONS - Didn't make the cut
[15:00-16:00] WINNER REVEAL - Best pick and why
[16:00-16:30] CTA
```

**Script Framework:**

```
HOOK:
"I tested [X] different [things] over [timeframe].
Number 3 on this list completely changed how I [do thing].
But the number 1 pick? That surprised even me."

CONTEXT:
"There are hundreds of [options] out there.
Most of them are [bad adjective].
I've actually used all of these for [real work/time].
Here's what's actually worth your [money/time]."

LIST ITEMS (for each):
"Coming in at number [X]: [Item Name]

What it does: [Brief explanation]
What I like: [2-3 pros]
What I don't like: [1-2 cons]
Best for: [Specific use case]
Price: [Cost]

The verdict: [One sentence summary]"

HONORABLE MENTIONS:
"Quick shoutout to [items] that almost made the list.
They're good, but [reason they didn't make it]."

WINNER:
"The overall winner for most people is [item].
Here's why: [compelling reason].
If you only get one [thing], this is it."

CTA:
"Let me know your favorite in the comments.
Subscribe for more [topic] comparisons.
Next video: [related content]"
```

## Template 3: Case Study (10-15 min)

**Structure:**
```
[0:00-0:30] HOOK - Impressive result
[0:30-2:00] CONTEXT - Starting point, challenge
[2:00-3:00] STRATEGY - What approach was taken
[3:00-10:00] EXECUTION - Detailed breakdown
[10:00-11:00] RESULTS - Numbers and outcomes
[11:00-12:00] LESSONS - Key takeaways
[12:00-12:30] CTA
```

**Script Framework:**

```
HOOK:
"[Specific result] in [timeframe].
I'm going to break down exactly how."

CONTEXT:
"[X months] ago, I/[subject] was [starting state].
The goal was [specific target].
The challenge: [main obstacle].
Here's what happened."

STRATEGY:
"Instead of [common approach], we decided to [different approach].
The thesis was simple: [core insight].
Here's the plan we put together."

EXECUTION:
"Month 1: [What happened]
We started by [first action].
[Specific details, numbers, screenshots]

Month 2: [What happened]
Then we [next action].
[Specific details, numbers, screenshots]

Month 3: [What happened]
Finally, [last action].
[Specific details, numbers, screenshots]"

RESULTS:
"The final numbers:
- [Metric 1]: [Before] to [After]
- [Metric 2]: [Before] to [After]
- [Metric 3]: [Before] to [After]

Total [result]: [Number]"

LESSONS:
"Here's what I'd do differently:
1. [Lesson 1]
2. [Lesson 2]
3. [Lesson 3]

And what worked perfectly:
1. [Success 1]
2. [Success 2]"

CTA:
"Want the full [template/resource]? Link in description.
Subscribe for more breakdowns like this.
Comment with your questions below."
```

## Template 4: Story/Documentary (15-20 min)

**Structure:**
```
[0:00-1:00] COLD OPEN - Dramatic moment
[1:00-2:00] TITLE/INTRO - Set the stage
[2:00-5:00] ACT 1 - Background and setup
[5:00-10:00] ACT 2 - Conflict and journey
[10:00-15:00] ACT 3 - Resolution
[15:00-17:00] REFLECTION - Meaning and lessons
[17:00-18:00] CTA
```

**Script Framework:**

```
COLD OPEN:
"[Dramatic moment or statement]
But to understand how we got here,
we need to go back to [beginning]."

TITLE/INTRO:
"This is the story of [subject].
From [starting state] to [ending state].
Here's how it happened."

ACT 1:
"It started in [time/place].
[Background context]
[Character/subject introduction]
[Initial situation]
[First challenge appears]"

ACT 2:
"Things started to change when [inciting incident].
[Rising action]
[Obstacles and setbacks]
[Attempts and failures]
[Darkest moment]
[Turning point]"

ACT 3:
"Then [breakthrough moment].
[Rising success]
[Climax]
[Resolution]
[Final state]"

REFLECTION:
"Looking back, here's what this teaches us:
[Lesson 1 with explanation]
[Lesson 2 with explanation]
[Lesson 3 with explanation]

The bigger picture: [Philosophical takeaway]"

CTA:
"If you want more stories like this, subscribe.
Drop a comment with your thoughts.
See you in the next one."
```

## Template 5: Explainer (8-12 min)

**Structure:**
```
[0:00-0:30] HOOK - Why this matters
[0:30-1:30] PROBLEM/QUESTION - What we're solving
[1:30-7:00] EXPLANATION - Core concept breakdown
[7:00-9:00] EXAMPLES - Real-world applications
[9:00-10:00] IMPLICATIONS - What this means for viewer
[10:00-10:30] CTA
```

**Script Framework:**

```
HOOK:
"Understanding [concept] could [dramatic benefit].
Most people get this completely wrong.
Let me explain."

PROBLEM:
"You've probably heard of [concept].
But what does it actually mean?
And more importantly, why should you care?
Let's break it down."

EXPLANATION:
"At its core, [concept] is [simple definition].

Think of it like [analogy].
[Expand on analogy]

The key components are:
1. [Component 1 - explanation]
2. [Component 2 - explanation]
3. [Component 3 - explanation]

Here's how they work together: [synthesis]"

EXAMPLES:
"Let's look at real examples.

Example 1: [Concrete example]
[How concept applies]

Example 2: [Different example]
[How concept applies]

Notice the pattern: [Common thread]"

IMPLICATIONS:
"So what does this mean for you?

If you're [audience segment 1]: [Specific application]
If you're [audience segment 2]: [Specific application]

The key takeaway: [One sentence summary]"

CTA:
"Questions? Drop them in comments.
Subscribe for more [topic] explanations.
Watch this next: [Related video]"
```

## Hooks for Long Form

### The Promise Hook
"By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how to [specific result]."

### The Credibility Hook
"I've [impressive credential]. Here's what I learned."

### The Curiosity Hook
"What if everything you know about [topic] is wrong?"

### The Story Hook
"3 years ago, I [starting point]. Today, [ending point]. Here's how."

### The Controversy Hook
"Everyone says [common belief]. I'm going to show you why that's wrong."

### The Data Hook
"I analyzed [X] [things]. Here's what the data shows."

### The Behind-the-Scenes Hook
"I'm going to show you exactly what happens when [thing]."

### The Mistake Hook
"I made a $[X] mistake. Let me save you from doing the same."

## Retention Techniques

### Pattern Interrupts

Every 2-3 minutes, include:
- Visual change (B-roll, graphics, screen recording)
- Tonal shift (serious to light, explanation to story)
- Direct address ("Now here's where it gets interesting...")
- Recap/preview ("So we've covered X. Next, we're diving into Y.")

### Open Loops

Start topics without finishing:
- "I'll show you the results in a minute, but first..."
- "The third point is the most important, but we need context first..."
- "We'll come back to this, but for now..."

### Engagement Prompts

- "Let me know in the comments which [thing] you use"
- "Before I reveal this, guess in the comments"
- "Hit like if you've experienced this"
- "Subscribe before I reveal the winner"

### Chapter Markers

Add timestamps for:
- Each major section
- Key moments viewers might search for
- Call-to-actions for easy navigation
