# Newsletter Monetization Research

## Executive Summary

Newsletter monetization follows predictable patterns based on niche, list size, and engagement. Core revenue streams: sponsorships (primary), paid subscriptions, affiliate links, product sales. CPMs range from $15 (broad) to $150+ (finance/B2B).

---

## Sponsorship CPMs by Niche

| Niche | CPM Range | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Finance/Investing | $50-150 | Highest CPMs, B2B sponsors pay premium |
| B2B/SaaS | $40-100 | Enterprise tools, productivity software |
| Tech/Developer | $30-80 | Dev tools, cloud services |
| Marketing | $25-60 | Martech, analytics platforms |
| Crypto/Web3 | $30-100 | Volatile, depends on market |
| Business/Entrepreneurship | $25-50 | Courses, tools, services |
| Health/Fitness | $15-40 | Supplements, apps, equipment |
| Lifestyle/General | $10-25 | Consumer products, apps |
| Creator Economy | $20-45 | Tools, platforms, courses |

**CPM Calculation:**
- CPM = (Sponsorship Price / List Size) x 1000
- Example: $500 sponsor / 10k list = $50 CPM

---

## Sponsorship Pricing Formulas

### Basic Formula
```
Price = (List Size x CPM) / 1000
```

### Adjusted Formula (Account for Open Rate)
```
Price = (List Size x Open Rate x Engaged CPM) / 1000
```

Example: 25k list, 45% open rate, $60 engaged CPM
- Price = (25,000 x 0.45 x 60) / 1000 = $675

### Sponsorship Tiers

| Tier | Placement | Typical Pricing |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Primary | Top of newsletter, dedicated section | 100% of rate |
| Secondary | Mid-newsletter, 100-150 words | 50-60% of rate |
| Classified | Bottom, 50 words + link | 20-30% of rate |
| Dedicated Send | Entire email about sponsor | 150-200% of rate |

## Platform Comparison

### Beehiiv
**Pros:**
- Built-in ad network (Beehiiv Boost)
- Referral program tools
- Free tier up to 2,500 subs
- Good analytics
- Boosts for cross-promotion

**Cons:**
- Limited customization on free tier
- Newer platform, smaller ecosystem

**Pricing:** Free to $99/mo

**Best For:** Growth-focused newsletters wanting built-in monetization

### Substack
**Pros:**
- Built-in paid subscription
- Network effects (recommendations)
- Simple setup
- Reader app with high engagement
- No upfront costs

**Cons:**
- 10% cut on paid subs
- Limited design options
- No ad network

**Pricing:** Free (10% on paid subs)

**Best For:** Writers building subscription revenue

### ConvertKit
**Pros:**
- Powerful automation
- Creator-focused features
- Sponsor network (ConvertKit Sponsor Network)
- Landing pages, forms

**Cons:**
- Higher pricing
- Steeper learning curve
- Less social features

**Pricing:** Free to $29-79/mo+

**Best For:** Creators with complex funnels

### Ghost
**Pros:**
- Open source option
- Full ownership
- Beautiful themes
- Membership built-in
- No platform fees

**Cons:**
- Requires more setup
- Self-hosted needs maintenance
- Smaller ecosystem

**Pricing:** $9-199/mo (hosted) or free (self-hosted)

**Best For:** Publishers wanting full control

## Case Studies

### Morning Brew
- **Size:** 4M+ subscribers
- **Revenue Model:** Sponsorships (primary), Courses, Events
- **CPM:** ~$50 for primary
- **Key Tactic:** Daily habit, snackable format, strong referral program
- **Exit:** Sold to Business Insider for $75M

### The Hustle
- **Size:** 1.5M+ subscribers
- **Revenue Model:** Sponsorships, Premium tier (Trends)
- **CPM:** ~$40-60
- **Key Tactic:** Voice-driven, personality in writing
- **Exit:** Acquired by HubSpot for reported $27M

### Milk Road
- **Size:** 250k subscribers in 10 months
- **Revenue Model:** Sponsorships, Events
- **CPM:** $50-80 (crypto premium)
- **Key Tactic:** Speed to market, meme-style formatting
- **Exit:** Acquired for ~$1M in under 1 year

### TLDR
- **Size:** 1.2M+ subscribers
- **Revenue Model:** Sponsorships, Job board
- **CPM:** ~$30-50
- **Key Tactic:** Multi-niche approach (Tech, AI, Marketing, etc.)

## Pricing Strategy

### Starting Out (< 5k subs)
- Don't chase sponsors yet
- Focus on affiliate links for relevant products
- Build case studies (what converts)
- Track click-through rates religiously

### Growth Phase (5k-25k)
- Set rates 20% below market to build testimonials
- Offer 3-issue packages at discount
- Create media kit with open rates, demographics
- Start outbound to relevant sponsors

### Established (25k+)
- Rate card with firm minimums
- Waitlist creates urgency
- Annual deals for premium clients
- Diversify: events, products, paid tier

## Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Good | Great | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|-------|
| Open Rate | 40%+ | 50%+ | Industry avg is 20-25% |
| Click Rate | 3%+ | 5%+ | On sponsor links |
| List Growth | 10%/mo | 20%/mo | Early stage |
| Churn | <2%/mo | <1%/mo | Monthly unsubscribes |
| Sponsor Renewal | 50%+ | 75%+ | Repeat bookings |

---

## Monetization Stack (Recommended)

### Phase 1: 0-5k subs
1. Affiliate links (passive)
2. Build engagement metrics
3. Test what resonates

### Phase 2: 5k-25k subs
1. Sponsorships (1-2 per issue)
2. Affiliate links (curated)
3. Referral program for growth

### Phase 3: 25k+ subs
1. Sponsorships (primary revenue)
2. Paid tier/premium content
3. Products (courses, templates)
4. Events/community
5. Job board (if B2B)

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **CPM is niche-dependent** - Finance pays 5x lifestyle
2. **Open rates matter more than list size** for sponsors
3. **Start monetizing earlier than you think** - 5k is enough
4. **Diversify revenue** - Sponsors + affiliate + products
5. **Build in public** - Share growth, attracts sponsors
6. **Referral programs work** - Milk Road, Morning Brew prove it
