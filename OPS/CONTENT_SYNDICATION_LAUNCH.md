## Section 1: Medium Partner Program

**Setup time:** 20 minutes
**Monthly revenue potential:** $50-1,000/mo (depends on views and niche)
**Break-even:** Immediate ($0 cost)

### Step 1: Create Medium account (2 min)

1. Go to [medium.com/m/signin](https://medium.com/m/signin)
2. Sign up with Google account (use the @printmaxxer email or personal Google)
3. Set username: `@printmaxxer` or `@printmaxx`
4. Bio: "i build apps and revenue streams. sharing what works with real numbers. no theory, no fluff."
5. Profile photo: use the PRINTMAXXER brand avatar

### Step 2: Apply for Partner Program (3 min)

1. Go to Settings (gear icon) > Partner Program
2. Medium requires 100 followers to join the Partner Program as of 2025. Alternative: join immediately by connecting Stripe and publishing consistently. The program auto-enrolls eligible writers.
3. Requirements: valid Stripe account, published at least 1 story, account in good standing
4. Apply and connect Stripe (next step)

### Step 3: Connect Stripe for payouts (5 min)

1. Settings > Partner Program > "Set up Stripe"
2. Connect existing Stripe account or create new one
3. Medium pays monthly via Stripe (minimum $10 payout threshold)
4. Payouts arrive around the 9th of each month for previous month's earnings

## Section 3: Content repurposing pipeline

We have 200+ short-form posts and 20+ newsletter issues. Here's how to turn them into long-form articles.

### Pipeline 1: Long tweets to Medium articles

**Source:** 130 tweets from `AUTOMATIONS/content_posting/` CSVs + 50 faith posts + 50 fitness posts

**Process:**
1. Find tweets with specific numbers or strong hooks
2. Expand to 1,000-1,200 words using this structure:
   - Hook (the tweet itself, 1-2 sentences)
   - Context (why this matters, 100 words)
   - The method (step by step, 400 words)
   - The results (specific numbers, 200 words)
   - What I'd do differently (100 words)
   - Takeaway (1-2 sentences)

**Example expansion:**

Tweet: "cold emailed 200 dentists with a mockup of a website i already built for them. 27% open rate. 4 calls booked."

Becomes a 1,200-word article: "I pre-built websites for dentists before they asked. 27% open rate on the cold emails."
- Section 1: why I built before I pitched
- Section 2: how I scraped dentist contact info (tools, costs)
- Section 3: the mockup workflow (screenshot, personalization)
- Section 4: the email template (exact copy)
- Section 5: results breakdown (open rate, reply rate, calls, close rate)
- Section 6: ROI math ($30 tool cost, $X revenue)

### Pipeline 2: Video scripts to blog posts

**Source:** 30 YouTube scripts from `CONTENT/YOUTUBE_SCRIPTS_30.md` + 20 TikTok scripts

**Process:**
1. Strip video-specific cues (B-roll notes, transition markers)
2. Convert spoken language to written (tighten, add headers)
3. Add data tables and comparison charts
4. Add relevant links and resources
5. Target word count: 1,000-1,500 words

### Pipeline 3: Article outlines to full articles

**Source:** 50 outlines from `CONTENT/ARTICLE_OUTLINES_50.md`

**Process:**
1. Pick outlines that match trending Medium topics
2. Research 2-3 data points per section
3. Write 200-300 words per section
4. Add specific examples and numbers
5. Target: 5 outlines per week turned into full articles

### Pipeline 4: Newsletter sequences to Substack posts

**Source:** 4 newsletter welcome sequences (28 emails total)

**Process:**
1. Each email is already 300-500 words with good hooks
2. Combine 2-3 related emails into one Substack post (800-1,200 words)
3. Remove email-specific CTAs, add Substack-native CTAs
4. Add images (Unsplash stock + screenshots)
5. Publish as "best of" or "start here" posts

**Combinations that work:**

| Substack Post | Source Emails | Word Count |
|---------------|-------------|------------|
| "The complete AI tool audit: 47 tested, 6 survived" | AI Tech emails 2 + 4 | ~1,200 |
| "12 AI prompts that replaced $200/mo tools" | AI Tech email 3 | ~800 |

| "I tracked my sleep for 365 days" | Sleep emails 2 + 3 | ~1,200 |

## Section 4: Cross-posting automation

### Platform 1: dev.to

**URL:** [dev.to](https://dev.to)
**Sign up:** GitHub account or email
**Audience:** 1M+ developers, strong community
**Cost:** Free
**Format:** Markdown (copy-paste from Medium, dev.to supports markdown natively)
**Revenue:** No direct payment, but drives traffic to products and newsletter

**What to post:**
- All tech/programming articles from MEDIUM_BATCH_10
- PWA article, SaaS replacement, AI-assisted dev, content distribution system
- Post 2-3 days after Medium publication (let Medium get initial traction first)

**Submission process:**
1. Create account at dev.to
2. Click "Create Post"
3. Paste markdown content
4. Add cover image (1000x420 recommended)
5. Add tags (max 4): `#webdev`, `#programming`, `#ai`, `#productivity`
6. Set canonical URL to your Medium article (tells Google the original source)
7. Publish

**Key setting:** Always set `canonical_url` to the Medium version. This prevents duplicate content penalties and gives Medium the SEO credit.

### Platform 2: HackerNoon

**URL:** [hackernoon.com](https://hackernoon.com)
**Sign up:** Email
**Audience:** 3M+ monthly readers, tech-focused
**Cost:** Free to publish
**Revenue:** HackerNoon pays writers based on page views (variable, $50-500/mo for active writers)
**Format:** Markdown editor

**What to post:**
- Tech articles with strong data: SaaS replacement, cold email numbers, PWA build
- HackerNoon editors curate submissions, so quality matters

**Submission process:**
1. Create account at hackernoon.com/signup
2. Click "Write" in top nav
3. Paste content, add cover image
4. Submit for editorial review (takes 1-3 days)
5. If accepted, article gets HackerNoon distribution (front page, socials, newsletter)

**Note:** HackerNoon has editorial review. Articles need to be well-formatted with clear sections. Our batch articles already meet this standard.

### Platform 3: LinkedIn articles

**URL:** linkedin.com (click "Write article" from homepage)
**Sign up:** Already have account
**Audience:** 900M+ users, B2B and professional
**Cost:** Free
**Format:** Rich text editor (paste from Medium, reformat slightly)

**What to post:**
- Cold email article (B2B audience will engage)
- SaaS replacement (professionals love cost-saving content)
- $0 marketing stack (startup operators and founders)
- AI-assisted development (managers and tech leads)

**Adaptation for LinkedIn:**
1. Add a hook paragraph (LinkedIn rewards "see more" clicks)
2. Use shorter paragraphs (3-4 lines max)
3. Remove slang, keep professional tone
4. Add a question at the end (drives comments)
5. No hashtags or 1-2 max

**Posting cadence:** 1 LinkedIn article per week, posted Tuesday or Wednesday 8-10am ET.

### Platform 4: Hashnode

**URL:** [hashnode.dev](https://hashnode.dev)
**Sign up:** GitHub account
**Audience:** Developer community, growing fast
**Cost:** Free (includes custom domain blog)
**Format:** Markdown
**Revenue:** No direct payment, drives traffic

**What to post:**
- All developer-focused articles
- PWA article, SaaS replacement, AI dev tools

**Bonus:** Hashnode lets you map your blog to a custom domain for free. Set up `blog.printmaxx.com` or similar.

**Submission process:**
1. Create account, set up blog
2. Paste markdown content
3. Add tags (Hashnode has preset tags)
4. Set canonical URL to Medium original

### Platform 5: Medium cross-post to Substack

Any Medium article can be adapted for Substack. The key differences:

| Feature | Medium | Substack |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Tone | More polished | More personal, conversational |
| CTA | "Follow on Medium" | "Subscribe to get this in your inbox" |
| Length | 1,000-2,000 words | 800-1,500 words |
| Images | More visual | Fewer, more text-focused |
| Monetization | Partner Program (views) | Paid subscriptions |

**Adaptation checklist:**
- [ ] Change title slightly (avoid exact duplicate)
- [ ] Add personal intro paragraph ("here's what I've been thinking about...")
- [ ] Convert CTAs to Substack native
- [ ] Add "If this was forwarded to you, subscribe here" at bottom
- [ ] Set canonical URL if cross-posting
