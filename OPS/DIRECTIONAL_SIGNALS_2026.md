## SECTION 10: RISK FACTORS & CONTRARIAN SIGNALS

### What Could Go Wrong
1. **TikTok US collapse:** If algorithm retraining fails or user exodus accelerates, TikTok Shop revenue projections may not hold. Hedge: diversify across YouTube Shopping and Instagram.
2. **AI regulation:** EU AI Act enforcement in 2026 could constrain certain AI business models. Hedge: build compliant from day one.
3. **Subscription fatigue backlash:** If consumers truly revolt against subscriptions, recurring revenue models face headwinds. Hedge: offer lifetime deals and usage-based pricing alongside subscriptions.
4. **Voice AI quality plateau:** If voice AI doesn't improve enough for reliable production use, the SMB opportunity stalls. Hedge: human-in-the-loop hybrid models.
5. **Google AI Overviews kills SEO:** If AI Overviews capture 50%+ of search intent, content-driven businesses lose their traffic engine. Hedge: build direct audience channels now.

### Contrarian Bets Worth Considering
1. **Long-form content comeback:** While everyone chases Shorts/Reels, YouTube's algorithm now bridges Shorts viewers to long-form. Creators who invest in 20-60 min content build deeper moats.
2. **Email renaissance:** As social algorithms become more pay-to-play and unpredictable, email lists become the most valuable owned asset. The "boring" channel is the safest bet.
3. **Physical products via AI:** While everyone builds digital products, AI-designed physical products (print on demand, custom merchandise, AI-generated art prints) have less competition and higher perceived value.
4. **Anti-AI positioning:** As AI content floods every platform, "handmade," "human-created," and "authentic" positioning may command premium pricing. The artisanal angle.
