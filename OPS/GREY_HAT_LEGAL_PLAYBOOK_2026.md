## 3. Aggressive Outreach

### 3A. Cold Email at Scale

**Legal status:** LEGAL under CAN-SPAM Act. No daily sending cap in the law.

**CAN-SPAM requirements (mandatory):**
1. Accurate "From" and "Reply-To" fields (no impersonation)
2. Non-deceptive subject lines
3. Include valid physical postal address
4. Clear opt-out mechanism in every email
5. Honor opt-out within 10 business days
6. Identify message as advertisement

**What CAN-SPAM does NOT require:**
- Prior consent (opt-in NOT required for B2B cold email in the US)
- Daily volume limits (no legal cap)

**Penalty:** Up to $53,088 per violating email.

**Practical safe limits (ESP-imposed):**

| Provider | Daily Limit |
|----------|-------------|
| Google Workspace | 2,000/day |
| Microsoft Office 365 | 10,000/day |
| Yahoo Mail | 500/day |
| Free Gmail | 100-500/day |

**Expert-recommended safe limits:**
- New domain: Start at 10-20/day, ramp up 20-50/week
- Established domain: 40-100/day per email address
- Conservative forever: 30-40/day per address = always land in primary inbox
- Spam rate threshold: Keep below 0.1% (0.3%+ = reputation damage)

**Scaling strategy (Multi-Sender Technique):**
- Target 2,000 cold emails/day
- Need 20+ email addresses (100 emails per address)
- Each address on separate domain or subdomain
- Warm up each address for 30+ days before sending
- Tools: Instantly, Smartlead, Saleshandy

**FTC compliance for cold email content:**
- Clearly identify yourself and company
- No misleading claims about products/services
- Substantiate any earnings/results claims
- Include physical address and opt-out in every email

**International watch:**
- EU/UK (GDPR): Need legitimate interest for B2B, consent for B2C
- Canada (CASL): Need express or implied consent
- France: B2C consent mandatory from August 2026
- Australia: Opt-in required

**PRINTMAXX fit:** COLD_OUTBOUND (MM007), AGENCY_SERVICES (MM005)

- Florida (FTSA): Broader autodialer definition, limits message volume, no outreach after 8 PM

**Revenue range:** SMS has 98% open rate vs 20% for email. Conversion rates 3-5x higher than email when done legally.

**Risk profile:**
- Legal risk: HIGH if non-compliant (class action lawsuits common)
- Reward: HIGHEST engagement channel when compliant
- Recommendation: Only use for opted-in leads, never cold SMS

**FTC compliance:** Standard product listing requirements. No false claims about condition, origin, or warranty.

**Revenue range:**
- Beginners: $500-$2,000/mo
- Intermediate: $3,000-$10,000/mo

**2026 changes:**

- Must self-prep or use 3PL (extra labor scared off casual flippers)
- Amazon aggressive about high price markups (may deactivate listings)
- Data-driven approach essential (can't just scan randomly anymore)

**Platform rules:**
- Amazon: No "retail dropshipping" (must receive, inspect, repackage). Gated products need approval.
- eBay: Retail arbitrage from Amazon/Walmart to eBay is PROHIBITED
- Legal everywhere but eBay has specific sourcing restrictions

**Key tools:**
- Keepa, Jungle Scout (Amazon data)
- SpamZilla, Domain Hunter Gatherer (expired domains)
- CLOSO, BuyBotPro (deal scanning)

**Risk profile:**
- Legal risk: NONE (First Sale Doctrine protects you)
- Platform risk: LOW if following prep/listing rules
- Financial risk: MEDIUM (unsold inventory, fee changes)

**PRINTMAXX fit:** ECOM_ARB (MM023), ECOM_DROPSHIP (MM022)

## 7. Legal Reference Quick Sheet

### Federal Laws That Matter

| Law | What It Covers | Key Requirement |
|-----|---------------|-----------------|
| CAN-SPAM Act | Commercial email | Opt-out, identification, physical address |
| TCPA | Phone calls and SMS | Prior express written consent |
| FTC Act Sec. 5 | Deceptive practices | No misleading claims, disclose sponsorships |
| TAKE IT DOWN Act (2025) | Deepfakes | No non-consensual intimate deepfakes |
| DEFIANCE Act (2026) | Deepfake civil remedy | Up to $250,000 statutory damages |
| First Sale Doctrine | Product resale | Can resell legally purchased goods |
| ACPA | Domain names | No cybersquatting on trademarked names |
| DMCA | Digital copyright | No reproducing copyrighted content |

### FTC Disclosure Requirements

**When to disclose:**
- Paid sponsorships/brand deals
- Affiliate links (any link where you earn commission)
- Free products received for review
- AI-generated content (recommended best practice, required in some states)
- Virtual influencer status

**How to disclose:**
- "#ad" or "#sponsored" at beginning of post (not buried)
- "Affiliate link" next to links
- "Paid partnership with [Brand]" (platform-native tools)
- "This content is AI-generated" for AI creators
- Clear and conspicuous (visible without scrolling/clicking)

### Platform ToS vs. Law

**Important distinction:** Violating a platform's Terms of Service is NOT illegal. It's a breach of contract that can result in account suspension/ban, but NOT legal prosecution.

| Action | Legal? | Against ToS? | Risk |
|--------|--------|-------------|------|
| Multiple accounts | YES | Varies by platform | Account ban |
| Buying followers | YES | YES (all platforms) | Account ban |
| Buying aged accounts | YES | YES (all platforms) | Account ban |
| Engagement pods | YES | Grey area | Shadowban |
| Automation tools | YES | Varies by platform | Account ban |
| Reply guy strategy | YES | NO (desired behavior) | NONE |
| Cold email | YES (with CAN-SPAM) | N/A | Legal penalty if non-compliant |
| Cold DMs | YES | Varies | Account ban if spammy |
| AI content | YES | Varies by platform | Must disclose |
| Findom | YES | Allowed on specific platforms | Know local laws |
| Retail arbitrage | YES | Allowed on Amazon, prohibited on eBay | Product delistings |

### Income Reporting

ALL income from these activities is taxable:
- Self-employment tax applies
- File Schedule C (Profit or Loss from Business)
- Consider LLC for liability protection
- Keep records of all expenses for deductions
- Quarterly estimated tax payments if earning $1,000+/year
