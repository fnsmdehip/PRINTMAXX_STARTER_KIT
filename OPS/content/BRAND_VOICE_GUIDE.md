# PRINTMAXX Brand Voice Guide

## Core Identity

**Who We Are:** Practical solopreneurs who build, test, and share what actually works.

**Voice Personality:**
- Direct (get to the point)
- Specific (numbers over vague claims)
- Honest (admit failures and tradeoffs)
- Casual (like texting a smart friend)
- Helpful (share what worked, not theory)

---

## Voice Principles

### 1. Lead with the Conclusion

BAD: "In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore the multifaceted approaches to building a sustainable passive income stream through print on demand."

GOOD: "Here's how I make $3k/month from POD spending 5 hours per week."

### 2. Use Specific Numbers

BAD: "We've helped many businesses increase their revenue significantly."

GOOD: "Average customer sees 23% revenue increase in first 60 days."

### 3. Admit Limitations

BAD: "Our revolutionary system works for everyone!"

GOOD: "This works best for niches with 10k+ monthly searches. Smaller niches need different tactics."

### 4. Write Like You Talk

BAD: "Leveraging cutting-edge automation methodologies enables unprecedented operational efficiency."

GOOD: "I automated my posts. Saves 5 hours per week."

## Platform Adaptations

### Twitter/X
- Hook in first line
- One idea per post
- No thread emoji (just start)
- End with value, not CTA begging
- Max 1 emoji if any

**Example:**
"Built a POD store in 3 hours using Printful + Shopify.

First month: $847 revenue, $312 profit.

The trick: Pick ONE niche. I did camping mugs.

Here's the exact setup..."

### Email
- Subject = specific benefit
- First line = why keep reading
- No "Hope this finds you well"
- One clear CTA
- PS for secondary offer only

**Example:**
Subject: 3 print files that made $2,400 last month

First line: "I'm sharing the exact designs, pricing, and where I sell them."

### Landing Pages
- Headline = main promise in <10 words
- Subhead = who it's for + what they get
- Bullets = specific features
- Social proof = real quotes, real names
- CTA = action verb + what they get

### Long-form Content
- Answer in first paragraph
- Subheadings for scanning
- Tables for comparisons
- No fluffy intros or conclusions

## Banned Vocabulary

### AI Tells (Never Use)
- leverage, utilize (say "use")
- delve, dive into, unpack
- comprehensive, robust, streamlined
- game-changer, unlock, elevate
- cutting-edge, innovative, revolutionary
- empower, enable, foster
- seamless, frictionless
- journey (unless actual travel)
- landscape (unless actual land)
- paradigm, testament

### Filler Words (Delete)
- additionally, moreover, furthermore
- in order to (just "to")
- due to the fact that (just "because")
- at this point in time (just "now")
- it's important to note that
- it goes without saying

### Promotional Fluff
- breathtaking, revolutionary, groundbreaking
- best-in-class, world-class
- one-of-a-kind, game-changing
- unlock your potential
- take your X to the next level

---

## Punctuation Rules

### Em Dashes (—)
- NEVER use em dashes
- Use commas or periods instead

BAD: "The tool—which many consider essential—delivers results"
GOOD: "The tool delivers results. Many consider it essential."

### Exclamation Points
- Max one per email
- Zero in professional content
- Okay sparingly in social

### Headings
- Sentence case always
- BAD: "How To Build A Successful Side Project"
- GOOD: "How to build a successful side project"

## Before/After Examples

### Product Description

BEFORE (AI voice):
"Experience the revolutionary comfort of our innovative, premium-quality t-shirts. Leveraging cutting-edge fabric technology, these game-changing garments will transform your wardrobe and unlock unprecedented levels of style and comfort."

AFTER (PRINTMAXX voice):
"6oz ringspun cotton. Pre-shrunk. Runs true to size. Same blank as Gymshark uses. $4.20 landed cost, retails at $28."

### Tutorial Intro

BEFORE:
"In today's rapidly evolving e-commerce landscape, understanding the multifaceted approaches to print-on-demand fulfillment has become essential for entrepreneurs seeking to establish sustainable passive income streams."

AFTER:
"POD fulfillment options ranked by margin:
1. Self-fulfill DTF (65% margin)
2. Printful (35% margin)
3. Printify (30% margin)

Here's when each makes sense..."

### Email Subject Lines

BEFORE:
- "Unlock Your Potential with Revolutionary Strategies!"
- "Don't Miss This Game-Changing Opportunity!"
- "Transform Your Business Journey Today!"

AFTER:
- "3 print files that made $2,400 last month"
- "The $47 tool that replaced my $200/mo subscription"
- "I lost $800 on this mistake (here's how to avoid it)"

## Hedging Rules

### One Hedge Per Sentence Maximum

BAD: "It might possibly perhaps help somewhat with some of your problems."
GOOD: "It probably helps."

### Acceptable Hedges
- probably
- likely
- typically
- often
- in most cases

### Avoid Stacking
BAD: "This could potentially maybe work"
GOOD: "This usually works"

## Pre-Publish Checklist

- [ ] Zero em dashes
- [ ] Zero banned AI vocabulary
- [ ] No "It's not just X, it's Y" constructions
- [ ] No vague attributions without links
- [ ] No promotional adjectives
- [ ] Sentence case headings
- [ ] Direct statements (not hedged to death)
- [ ] Would a real solopreneur say this?
- [ ] First sentence delivers value (not setup)
- [ ] Specific numbers where possible
