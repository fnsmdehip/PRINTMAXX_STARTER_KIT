## 4. Landing Page Swipes

### High-Converting Patterns

| Site | Type | What Works |
|------|------|------------|
| **Opal.so** | App landing | Minimalist, one clear CTA, social proof above fold |
| **Notion.so** | SaaS | Feature showcases with GIFs, templates as hooks |
| **Superhuman.com** | Email app | Exclusivity angle, speed positioning, waitlist |
| **Linear.app** | Dev tool | Dark mode, keyboard-first messaging |
| **Basecamp.com** | PM tool | Opinionated positioning, anti-enterprise |
| **ConvertKit.com** | Email | Creator-focused messaging, earnings calculator |

### Landing Page Formula

**Hero Section:**
- Headline: Primary benefit in <10 words
- Subhead: Who it's for + what they get
- CTA: Single button, action verb
- Visual: Product screenshot or demo

**Trust Section:**
- Logos of customers/press
- Specific number ("50,000+ users")

**Features Section:**
- 3-4 key features max
- Benefit-focused headers
- Visual for each

**Social Proof Section:**
- Real testimonials with photos
- Specific results in quotes

**FAQ Section:**
- 5-7 common objections
- Direct answers

**Final CTA:**
- Repeat main offer
- Add urgency if appropriate

## 5. Email Swipes

### Welcome Sequence Pattern (5 emails)

| Email | Timing | Purpose | Subject Formula |
|-------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | Immediate | Deliver lead magnet | "Here's your [thing]" |
| 2 | Day 2 | Quick win | "Do this first (2 min)" |
| 3 | Day 4 | Story + lesson | "How I [achieved thing]" |
| 4 | Day 6 | Social proof | "[Name] just did [result]" |
| 5 | Day 8 | Soft pitch | "Ready for [next step]?" |

### Cold Email Pattern 1: Observation-Based

```
Subject: Quick question about {Company}

{Name},

Noticed you {specific observation from research}.

Most teams in {industry} struggle with {problem}.

We helped {similar company} achieve {specific result}.

Worth a look?

{Signature}
```

### Cold Email Pattern 2: Trigger-Based

```
Subject: Congrats on the {trigger event}

{Name},

Saw {company} just {trigger: raised funding / launched / hired}.

When companies hit this stage, they usually need help with {problem}.

We've worked with {similar companies} on exactly this.

15 minutes to see if it fits?

{Signature}
```

### Flash Sale Email

```
Subject: 48 hours only

Hey {Name},

For the next 48 hours, I'm opening up [Product] at [discount].

You've been getting my emails for [X weeks/months].

This is the one time I'm going to pitch you.

[Product] helps you [main benefit]:
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]
- [Benefit 3]

[X] people have already grabbed it.

Offer ends [specific time].

[CTA Button]

{Signature}
```

### Subject Line Patterns

- Question: "Quick question about {topic}?"
- Number: "3 ways to {benefit}"
- Curiosity: "This changed everything"
- Personal: "For you, {Name}"
- Result: "How {Name} got {result}"
- Urgency: "24 hours left"
- Pattern interrupt: "Don't open this"

## 8. Conversion Elements

### Paywall Best Practices

| Element | Impact | Implementation |
|---------|--------|----------------|
| Animated paywall | 2.9x conversion | Subtle motion on CTA |
| Personalized name | +17% conversion | Capture name in onboarding |
| Annual default | 2.6x retention | Show annual first |
| Free trial on annual only | Higher LTV | Only offer trial on best plan |
| Dynamic discounts | +35% conversion | Time-limited offers |

### Onboarding Best Practices

| Tactic | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Value before signup | Higher completion |
| Day 0 paywall | 50% of trials |
| Single push notification | 3x retention |
| Streak mechanic | 55% 7-day retention |
| 77% drop in 3 days | Focus all energy on first 3 days |

### Trust Signals

- Harvard/medical citations for health apps
- User count with specific number ("47,392 users")
- Real testimonials with photos and full names
- Press logos (even small publications help)
- Security badges for payment/data apps

## 9. Pricing Psychology

### App Pricing Tiers

| Tier | Price Point | Psychology |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Weekly | $4.99-$9.99/wk | Low commitment, high annualized |
| Monthly | $9.99-$14.99/mo | Standard subscription |
| Annual | $49.99-$99.99/yr | Best value positioning |
| Lifetime | 3-5x annual | Captures "whale" segment |

### Course Pricing

| Format | Price Range | Conversion |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| Mini-course | $27-$97 | Highest, impulse buy |
| Self-paced | $97-$299 | Good |
| Cohort | $350-$1,000 | Lower but higher LTV |
| Premium | $2,000+ | Low but sustainable |

### Price Anchoring

- Show highest price first (crossed out)
- Display monthly equivalent for annual
- Use odd numbers ($97 vs $100)
- Bundle to increase perceived value

## Quick Reference: Pattern Recognition

### High-Signal Indicators
- Paying for ads = making money (FB Ads Library)
- High App Store rank + reviews = proven demand
- Multiple copycat apps = validated market
- Influencer promoting repeatedly = working

### Red Flags
- No visible monetization = hobby project
- Overcrowded with big players = hard to compete
- Requires hardware/logistics = complex
- Regulatory concerns = risky

### Best Clone Opportunities
- Successful app + underserved niche = opportunity
- US app not in other markets = localization play
- Expensive tool + simpler need = cheaper alternative
- Web-only tool + mobile demand = platform port
