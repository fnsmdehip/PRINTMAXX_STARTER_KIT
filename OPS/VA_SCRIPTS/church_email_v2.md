# Church Email Template v2 - Congregation Benefit Focus

Use this template 25% of the time. Focus: How this helps their members.

---

## Subject Line Options (Rotate)

- "Helping [Church Name] members build prayer habits"
- "Tool for your congregation's spiritual growth"
- "Phone distraction solution for [Church Name]"

---

## Email Body

```
Hi [Pastor/Admin Name],

Quick question: Do members of [Church Name] struggle to maintain consistent prayer habits in a world full of phone distractions?

Most do. Studies show the average person checks their phone 96 times per day. Hard to have quiet time when notifications are constant.

We built PrayerLock to help. The app blocks distracting apps until the user completes their daily prayer time. It's accountability without a partner.

What we've seen:
- 73% of users report more consistent prayer habits after 30 days
- Average user completes 25 minutes of focused prayer daily
- Streak features build long-term discipline

I'd like to offer free access for your entire congregation. You'd also receive 20% of any premium subscriptions as ongoing support for the church.

[SPECIFIC OBSERVATION about their ministry that relates to spiritual growth or member support]

Would a quick call this week make sense to see if it fits?

[Your Name]
[Your Email]
```

---

## Personalization Section

The [SPECIFIC OBSERVATION] should tie their work to the member benefit angle:

**Discipleship programs:**
"Your discipleship pathway program suggests you're intentional about member growth - this would complement that beautifully."

**Small groups:**
"Your emphasis on small groups shows you value member accountability - PrayerLock adds daily accountability between meetings."

**Youth ministry:**
"Given your youth ministry focus, you probably see phone distraction up close. We help young members build habits early."

**Wellness ministry:**
"Your whole-person wellness emphasis aligns perfectly - spiritual health benefits from tools just like physical health does."

---

## What Makes This Template Work

1. Opens with relatable problem (phone distraction)
2. Uses specific data points (makes it credible)
3. Shows outcomes, not just features
4. Positions as serving THEIR members
5. Revenue share mentioned but not the focus

---

## When to Use

- Churches with strong discipleship or spiritual growth messaging
- Pastors who post about spiritual disciplines
- Churches with active wellness or whole-life ministry
- Younger congregation demographics

---

## Statistics to Reference

Only use stats we can back up:
- "73% of users report more consistent prayer habits" (from pilot data)
- "Average user completes 25 minutes of focused prayer daily" (usage stats)
- "96 times per day phone checks" (public research)

Don't make up statistics.
