# Church Email Template v3 - Revenue Share Focus

Use this template 25% of the time. Focus: Sustainable income stream for the church.

---

## Subject Line Options (Rotate)

- "New income stream for [Church Name]"
- "Partnership that funds ministry at [Church Name]"
- "Passive revenue opportunity for [Church Name]"

---

## Email Body

```
Hi [Pastor/Admin Name],

Churches are always looking for ways to fund ministry without adding burden to the congregation. Here's one that actually helps your members while generating ongoing revenue.

We created PrayerLock, an app that helps Christians build consistent prayer habits by blocking distracting apps until they complete daily prayer time.

Here's the partnership model:
- Your congregation gets free access through a [Church Name] link
- Any member who upgrades to premium ($50/year) = $10 to your church
- Revenue is automatic, monthly, and ongoing

Real example: A church with 500 members typically sees:
- 10% download rate = 50 users
- 20% premium conversion = 10 paying members
- $100/year to the church from 10 members
- Scales with adoption

No cost to you. No effort from your team. Members benefit spiritually, church benefits financially.

[SPECIFIC OBSERVATION about their church that shows you researched them]

Worth a 15-minute call to see if it fits?

[Your Name]
[Your Email]
```

---

## Personalization Section

Tie the [SPECIFIC OBSERVATION] to their financial or operational situation:

**Building campaign:**
"I noticed your building fund campaign - this could contribute ongoing funds without additional asks."

**Mission focus:**
"Your mission giving shows you invest in kingdom work - this is passive income that funds those efforts."

**Small church:**
"Smaller churches often have tight budgets. This creates revenue that grows with member adoption."

**New church plant:**
"Church plants need every revenue stream possible. This one helps members while funding ministry."

---

## Math Breakdown (Use Selectively)

For churches that respond well to numbers:

```
Here's the math for a 500-member church:

Conservative scenario:
- 5% download = 25 users
- 20% premium = 5 paying members
- $50/year each = $250 total
- Your 20% = $50/year

Moderate scenario:
- 10% download = 50 users
- 25% premium = 12-13 paying members
- $650 total
- Your 20% = $130/year

Strong adoption:
- 20% download = 100 users
- 30% premium = 30 paying members
- $1,500 total
- Your 20% = $300/year

Multiplied across years, this becomes meaningful ongoing support.
```

---

## What Makes This Template Work

1. Leads with their problem (funding)
2. Positions as help for members AND revenue
3. Uses specific, realistic numbers
4. Emphasizes "no effort" - passive income
5. Concrete example makes it tangible

---

## When to Use

- Churches with visible financial campaigns
- Smaller churches with limited budgets
- Church admins (more operational focus)
- Churches that mention stewardship often

---

## Cautions

- Don't make it feel transactional
- Spiritual benefit should still be mentioned
- Avoid pressure language about money
- Some churches will be turned off by revenue focus - that's okay, that's why we A/B test
