# Gym Pitch Template v3 - Competitive Advantage Angle

Use this template 30% of the time. Focus: Stand out from other gyms.

---

## Subject Line Options (Rotate)

- "What [Competitor] doesn't offer - but [Gym Name] could"
- "Differentiate [Gym Name] from the big boxes"
- "Unique member benefit for [Gym Name]"

---

## Email Body

```
Hi [Owner/Manager Name],

What makes [Gym Name] different from the Planet Fitness down the street?

Equipment is similar everywhere. Price wars are a race to the bottom. The gyms winning right now are those offering something their competitors don't.

We partner with select gyms to offer a branded fitness app. Your members get something they can't get at the big boxes:

- [Gym Name]-branded app that blocks distracting apps until they hit activity goals
- In-app challenges exclusive to your members
- Leaderboards that build community
- Engagement dashboard so you see who's active

You also earn 15% of premium subscriptions. But honestly, the differentiation is worth more than the rev share.

When members compare options, "free branded app with challenges" tips the scale.

[SPECIFIC OBSERVATION about their competitive position or unique value]

Free pilot. We handle setup. Worth exploring?

[Your Name]
[Your Email]
```

---

## Personalization Examples

Focus [SPECIFIC OBSERVATION] on their differentiation:

**Boutique/specialty gym:**
"Your focus on functional fitness already sets you apart - this extends that specialized experience to members' phones."

**Independent vs chains:**
"Independent gyms like [Gym Name] win on community and care. This gives you a tech advantage to match."

**Unique programs:**
"Your personal training packages are a differentiator. A branded app adds another layer competitors can't copy."

**Location competition:**
"I noticed there are 4 gyms within 2 miles of you. This gives members a reason to choose [Gym Name]."

---

## What Makes This Template Work

1. Opens with strategic question about differentiation
2. Acknowledges real competitive pressures
3. Positions app as competitive advantage, not just tool
4. Revenue is mentioned but not the focus
5. "Select gyms" creates subtle exclusivity
6. Practical benefits (challenges, leaderboards) are tangible

---

## When to Use

- Independent gyms competing with big boxes
- Gyms in competitive markets (multiple gyms nearby)
- Owners who post about what makes them different
- Boutique/specialty fitness studios
- Gyms with strong brand identity

---

## Additional Angle: Retention

If they seem focused on keeping members:

```
The real win: members with the app are 40% more likely to renew.

Why? The app keeps them engaged on rest days. Engagement drives results. Results drive retention.

Your churn rate drops. Lifetime value goes up. The rev share is bonus.
```

Use this only if you sense retention is a pain point (mentions about member churn, seasonal drops, etc.)

---

## Competitor Mentions

You can reference competitors without naming them:

**Good:**
- "What makes you different from the big boxes?"
- "Your competitors can't offer this"
- "Big chains don't partner like this"

**Don't do:**
- Name specific competitors (Planet Fitness, LA Fitness) negatively
- Make claims about what competitors can't do (we don't know their partnerships)
