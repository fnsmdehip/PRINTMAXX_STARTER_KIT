## Church Follow-Up Sequence

### Day 3 - First Follow-up

```
Subject: Re: [Original subject line]

Hi [Pastor/Admin Name],

Just following up on my note from a few days ago about PrayerLock for [Church Name].

Quick recap: Free prayer habit app for your congregation, plus 20% of any premium subscriptions.

Would 15 minutes this week work to discuss?

[Your Name]
```

### Day 7 - Second Follow-up

```
Subject: Re: [Original subject line]

Hi [Pastor/Admin Name],

Wanted to circle back one more time on the PrayerLock partnership.

Other churches have told us:
- Members appreciate the accountability without needing a partner
- The streak feature builds habits that stick
- Setup takes less than a day

If this isn't a priority right now, I understand. Just let me know either way.

[Your Name]
```

### Day 14 - Final Follow-up

```
Subject: Re: [Original subject line]

Hi [Pastor/Admin Name],

I'll assume the timing isn't right and won't follow up again.

If building consistent prayer habits becomes a focus for [Church Name] in the future, feel free to reach out anytime.

Blessings,
[Your Name]
```

## Response Templates

### When They Ask for More Info

```
Hi [Name],

Happy to share more. Here's a quick overview:

[App Name] helps [target audience] with [core benefit]. The app [how it works in one sentence].

For partners, we offer:
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]
- [Benefit 3]

The easiest next step is a quick 15-minute call where I can show you the dashboard and answer questions: [Calendly link]

What works for you?

[Your Name]
```

### When They're Interested but Need to Check with Someone

```
Hi [Name],

Completely understand - these decisions often involve others.

To help you present it:
- I can send a one-page summary
- Happy to join a call with your [pastor/manager/principal]
- Can provide case studies from similar [churches/gyms/schools]

What would be most helpful?

[Your Name]
```

### When They Ask About Pricing

```
Hi [Name],

Great question. Here's the breakdown:

For [churches/gyms/schools]:
- Partnership is free to join
- Members/students access the app free (basic version)
- Premium version is $[X]/year
- You receive [X]% of premium subscriptions from your [congregation/members/students]

No upfront cost, no minimum commitment. You only benefit when your [congregation/members/students] do.

Make sense? Happy to walk through the math on a call: [Calendly link]

[Your Name]
```

### When They're Hesitant

```
Hi [Name],

Totally understand the hesitation. A few things that might help:

1. The pilot is completely free and no-commitment
2. We handle all setup and support
3. If it doesn't work, you just stop - no penalty

Most [churches/gyms/schools] test it with a small group first to see results before broader rollout.

Would a pilot with just [20-30] [members/students] make sense to start?

[Your Name]
```

### When They Decline

```
Hi [Name],

Completely understand - appreciate you letting me know.

If circumstances change in the future, feel free to reach out.

All the best with [Church Name/Gym Name/School Name],
[Your Name]
```

## Follow-Up Best Practices

### Timing

| Outreach Type | FU 1 | FU 2 | FU 3 | Close |
|---------------|------|------|------|-------|
| Church | Day 3 | Day 7 | Day 14 | After 14 |
| Gym | Day 3 | Day 7 | Day 12 | After 12 |
| School | Day 5 | Day 12 | Day 21 | After 21 |
| Affiliate | Day 3 | Day 7 | - | After 7 |

### Rules

1. **Never send more than 3 follow-ups** - 4+ is spam
2. **Always use "Re:" in subject** - keeps thread together
3. **Keep follow-ups shorter than original** - respect their time
4. **Add new value if possible** - testimonial, stat, new angle
5. **Final follow-up should close the loop** - let them know you're stopping

### What to Track

Log in your tracker:
- Date of each follow-up
- Follow-up number (1, 2, 3)
- Any response received
- Status update after each follow-up

---

## When to Stop Early

Stop the sequence before final follow-up if:

1. They explicitly decline
2. They ask to be removed from outreach
3. Email bounces
4. You get a spam complaint
5. They respond positively (obviously)
