## Interview Questions

### Screening Questions (Written)

Send these after initial application review:

```
1. Why are you interested in this role specifically?

2. Describe your experience with email outreach or cold emailing. What was your response rate?

3. Walk me through how you would research a church to personalize an outreach email.

4. What tools do you use to stay organized when managing multiple outreach campaigns?

5. What's your availability in US Eastern Time? (We need overlap for sync calls)

6. Do you have experience working with faith-based organizations or fitness businesses?

7. What's your preferred payment method? (Wise, PayPal, etc.)
```

### Video Interview Questions (15-20 min call)

```
INTRO (2 min):
- Tell me about yourself and your work background
- Why are you interested in outreach work?

SKILLS ASSESSMENT (5 min):
- I'm going to give you a church name. Walk me through how you'd research them and write an outreach email.
- [Share a church website in chat]
- What would you include in the email?

EXPERIENCE (5 min):
- Tell me about a time you had to send many personalized emails. How did you stay efficient without sacrificing quality?
- What's the worst response rate you've had on an outreach campaign? What did you do about it?
- How do you handle rejection or no responses?

LOGISTICS (3 min):
- What hours are you available? Do you have other jobs?
- What's your internet situation? Any frequent power outages?
- Do you have a backup plan if your computer breaks?

CLOSE (2 min):
- What questions do you have for me?
- When could you start?
```

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Generic application that doesn't mention church/gym outreach
- Poor English grammar in written samples
- No experience with any form of outreach or research
- Unrealistic availability claims
- Can't explain how they'd personalize an email
- Asks about payment before asking about the work

### Green Flags

- Sample email demonstrates personalization
- Mentions specific tools they use (Google Sheets, Hunter.io, LinkedIn)
- Asks clarifying questions about the role
- Has church/faith background or fitness interest
- Previous outreach experience with measurable results

## Onboarding Checklist

### Day 1: Setup

- [ ] Add to team Slack channel (or create WhatsApp group)
- [ ] Share Google Drive folder with templates
- [ ] Grant edit access to tracking spreadsheet
- [ ] Send welcome video (Loom - 5 min intro)
- [ ] Schedule first week check-in call
- [ ] Collect payment information

### Day 1-2: Training

- [ ] Watch training video for their specific role:
  - Church: `OPS/VA_TRAINING/church_outreach_training.md`
  - Gym: `OPS/VA_TRAINING/gym_outreach_training.md`
  - School: `OPS/VA_TRAINING/school_outreach_training.md`
  - Affiliate: `OPS/VA_TRAINING/affiliate_recruitment_training.md`
- [ ] Review email templates in `OPS/VA_SCRIPTS/`
- [ ] Practice 3 sample outreaches (review together)
- [ ] Quiz on key points (informal, on call)

### Day 3-5: Supervised Work

- [ ] VA sends first 10 outreach emails
- [ ] Review all 10 for quality and personalization
- [ ] Provide feedback on specific emails
- [ ] Correct any issues before scaling

### Week 2+: Independent Work

- [ ] Daily update in Slack (leads contacted, responses)
- [ ] Weekly video call (15-30 min)
- [ ] Monthly performance review
- [ ] Adjust targets based on results

## Escalation Process

### When VA Should Escalate

1. **Hot lead** - Pastor/owner very interested, wants call today
2. **Objection** - Specific concern needs addressing
3. **Complaint** - Negative response or unsubscribe request
4. **Technical issue** - Tool not working, can't access resource
5. **Unusual request** - Something not covered in training

### How to Escalate

```
SLACK MESSAGE FORMAT:

ESCALATION: [Hot Lead / Objection / Complaint / Technical / Other]

Contact: [Name, Organization]
Email: [their email]
Summary: [2-3 sentences on situation]
Action needed: [What you need from me]

Urgency: [Today / This week / When available]
```

### Response Time Targets

| Escalation Type | Response Time |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Hot lead | Same day |
| Objection | Same day |
| Complaint | 4 hours |
| Technical | Same day |
| Other | Next business day |

## Quality Assurance

### Email Review Checklist

Every email should have:

- [ ] Correct church/gym name (no copy-paste errors)
- [ ] Pastor/owner name spelled correctly
- [ ] Specific personalization (not generic)
- [ ] No grammar or spelling errors
- [ ] Correct signature and links
- [ ] Sent from correct email account

### Response Rate Benchmarks

| Metric | Church | Gym | School |
|--------|--------|-----|--------|
| Target open rate | 40%+ | 35%+ | 30%+ |
| Target response rate | 10%+ | 8%+ | 5%+ |
| Target positive response | 5%+ | 4%+ | 2%+ |

### Warning Signs

- Response rate below 3% for 2+ weeks
- Declining trend week over week
- Complaints or spam reports
- VA not posting daily updates
- Missed check-in calls

### Course Correction

1. Review email samples together
2. Audit targeting (wrong organizations?)
3. A/B test new subject lines
4. Refresh templates if stale
5. Consider different time zones for sending

## Firing Process

### When to Fire

- Consistent underperformance after coaching (3+ weeks below targets)
- Dishonesty (fabricating data, fake outreach)
- No-shows without communication (2+ times)
- Sharing confidential information
- Spam complaints traced to their outreach

### How to Fire

1. Document all issues with dates
2. Have final video call
3. Be direct but kind: "This isn't working out. Your last day is [date]."
4. Process final payment within 48 hours
5. Revoke all access immediately
6. Update tracking sheet

### What to Say

```
Hi [Name],

I appreciate the work you've done over the past [X weeks]. Unfortunately, this arrangement isn't working out as we need it to.

[Specific reason: The response rates have been below our 5% threshold for three weeks, and we haven't seen improvement after our coaching sessions.]

Today will be your last day. I'll process payment for all hours through today by [date].

Please don't take on any new work. I'll revoke access to our systems by end of day.

Thank you for your time. I wish you the best.

[Your name]
```

## Scaling the VA Operation

### When to Hire More VAs

- Current VA consistently hits targets with capacity to spare
- Proven response rates and conversion process
- Clear ROI ($X spent on VA produces $Y in partnerships)
- Operational systems are documented and repeatable

### Scaling Model

| Stage | VAs | Weekly Outreach | Expected Results |
|-------|-----|-----------------|------------------|
| Test | 1 | 100-150 | Prove the model |
| Growth | 2-3 | 300-500 | Scale what works |
| Scale | 5-10 | 1000+ | Dedicated niche teams |

### VA Specialization

At 3+ VAs, consider specializing:
- VA 1: Church outreach only
- VA 2: Gym outreach only
- VA 3: Follow-up and nurture sequences

### Hiring a VA Manager

At 5+ VAs, consider promoting top performer to manage others:
- Rate: $8-10/hr
- Responsibilities: QA, training new VAs, weekly reporting
- You: Strategy, scripts, partnership calls only
