## ACCOUNT SETUP (30 minutes)

### Step 1: Create Upwork Account

```
1. Go to upwork.com/signup
2. Sign up as Freelancer
3. Use your real name (Upwork verifies identity)
4. Upload professional headshot (not AI-generated, Upwork detects this)
5. Verify identity (government ID required)
6. Set primary category: "Writing" or "Web Development" (can add more later)
7. Set hourly rate: $75/hour (explained below)
8. Add payment method for receiving funds (PayPal, direct deposit, wire)
```

### Step 2: Profile Basics

```
Profile photo: Professional headshot, good lighting, plain background
Location: Your actual city (clients often filter by location)
Title: See specialized titles below
Overview: See specialized overviews below
Skills tags: Add all relevant skills (max 15)
Portfolio: Add 3-5 samples (create them if you don't have client work yet)
Connects: You start with 40 free Connects (each proposal costs 2-6 Connects)
```

### Step 3: Create 5 Specialized Profiles

Upwork lets you create up to 2 specialized profiles beyond your main profile. Rotate your primary profile focus monthly to test which gets the most traction.

**Strategy:** Start with Profile 1 and 2 as specialized profiles. Use main profile for Profile 3. Rotate Profiles 4 and 5 as your main profile in months 2 and 3 based on traction.

## PROPOSAL STRATEGY

### Week 1: Send 20 Proposals (4/day for 5 days)

```
Budget your Connects:
- Start with 40 free Connects
- Each proposal costs 2-6 Connects
- Budget for ~10-15 proposals from free Connects
- Buy additional Connects ($0.15 each) for 5-10 more

Job search filters:
- Payment verified: YES (only clients who have paid on Upwork before)
- Budget: $500+ (skip $50 jobs, waste of time)
- Proposals: <15 (less competition)
- Client hire rate: >80% (they actually hire, not just browse)
- Posted: Last 24 hours (be first to apply)
```

### Proposal Writing Rules

```
DO:
- Read the entire job post (reference specific details they mentioned)
- Lead with a relevant result ("I did this for a similar client...")
- Ask 2-3 questions (shows engagement, not just mass applying)
- Include 1 relevant portfolio sample
- Keep it under 200 words
- Personalize the first 2 sentences to THEIR business

DO NOT:
- Copy-paste the same proposal to every job
- Start with "Dear Hiring Manager" or "I am writing to express my interest"
- List your skills without context
- Send proposals to jobs you're not qualified for
- Bid too low (cheap signals low quality)
- Write more than 300 words
```

### Pricing Strategy

```
First 3 clients: 20% below your target rate (build reviews)
- Blog posts: $150 (target: $200)
- Website redesign: $600 (target: $800)
- Video clips: $100 (target: $150)

After 5-star reviews (3+ clients): Raise to target rates
After 10 reviews: Raise 20% above initial target
After Top Rated badge: Raise to premium rates ($100-150/hour)

The goal: Get to 5 five-star reviews as fast as possible. Reviews are everything on Upwork.
```

## UPWORK TIPS (Learn From Mistakes Others Make)

```
1. RESPOND WITHIN 1 HOUR. Upwork tracks response time. Faster = better ranking.
2. Never bid on jobs with 50+ proposals. You're buried.
3. Upwork charges 10% fee (drops to 5% after $10K with same client). Price accordingly.
4. Video proposals win. Record a 60-second Loom saying "Hey {{CLIENT_NAME}}, watched your project..." and attach to proposal.
5. Your first message after being hired matters. Send: project brief, timeline, first deliverable date.
6. Over-deliver on first projects. Give an extra blog post free. Include bonus audit. This gets 5-star reviews + repeat clients.
7. Ask for reviews explicitly: "If you're happy with the work, would you mind leaving a review? It helps a lot."
8. Upwork's algorithm favors: recent activity, response time, job success score. Stay active daily.
```
