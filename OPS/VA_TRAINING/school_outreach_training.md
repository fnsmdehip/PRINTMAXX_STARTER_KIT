## Understanding the Target

### Who We Contact

**Primary targets:**
- School counselors
- Assistant principals (academics)
- Academic deans
- IT directors (for technology decisions)

**Secondary targets:**
- PTA/PTO presidents
- Parent Facebook group admins
- University student services

**We do NOT cold contact:**
- Individual teachers (unless they're tech coordinators)
- Students directly
- Parents individually

### The Offer to Schools

**Free pilot:**
- 30 students, 30 days
- Focus on students struggling with phone distraction
- Measure screen time reduction and self-reported focus

**After pilot:**
- School-wide licensing ($2-5 per student per month)
- Volume discounts for districts
- Teacher dashboard integration

**Value props:**
- Reduce phone distraction in homework time
- Build study habits that stick
- Data for parents and counselors
- Student self-regulation tool

## Finding Schools

### Criteria for Good School Targets

**Must have:**
- Public or private school in USA
- Middle school, high school, or university
- Website with staff directory
- Some digital presence

**Ideal:**
- 500-3000 students
- Mentions technology or digital citizenship programs
- Recent news about phone policies
- Active parent engagement
- Title I schools (federal funding, may have budget)

**Avoid:**
- Elementary schools (too young for our app)
- Schools with existing phone ban (may not see need)
- Very small schools (<100 students)
- Religious schools that might object to app blocking

### Where to Find Schools

**School directories:**
- https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/schoolsearch/ (public schools database)
- https://www.privateschoolreview.com
- State education department websites

**Google Search:**
- "[City] high school contact"
- "[School name] counselor email"
- "Schools in [district] struggling with phone use"

**LinkedIn:**
- Search "school counselor" + [city]
- Search "assistant principal" + [state]
- Find contact info from profiles

**News:**
- Search "[city] school phone policy"
- Schools in news about tech/phone issues are warm targets

### Research Process (Step by Step)

**Step 1: Find the school**
- Use NCES database or Google
- Verify grade levels (middle/high/university)
- Check enrollment size

**Step 2: Find the right contact**
- Check school website staff directory
- Look for: counselor, assistant principal (academics), dean
- Find email pattern from directory

**Step 3: Research the school**
- Any news about phone policies?
- Technology initiatives mentioned?
- Academic improvement programs?

**Step 4: Note personalization**
- Specific program they run
- Recent achievement or challenge
- Technology they already use

## Writing Outreach Emails

### The Formula

1. **Relevance hook** - why you're reaching out to THEM specifically
2. **Problem acknowledgment** - phone distraction affecting students
3. **Solution introduction** - what StudyLock does
4. **Specific offer** - free pilot program
5. **Low-commitment ask** - 15-minute call

### Tone Guidelines

- Professional but not stiff
- Education-focused, not sales-y
- Empathetic to their challenges
- Data-driven when possible
- Never pushy

### Personalization Examples

**Good personalization:**
- "I saw [School Name] recently implemented a phone-free policy during class. StudyLock helps extend that focus to homework time."
- "Your counseling department's focus on student wellness caught my attention. Phone distraction is a growing factor in student stress."
- "Noticed [School Name] won the STEM award this year - congrats. Technology tools for focus fit well with that innovation culture."

**Bad personalization:**
- "I noticed you're a school counselor." (generic role mention)
- "Your school has students." (worthless)
- "I found your email online." (creepy)

### Template Selection

Different angles for different roles:

**Counselors:** Focus on student wellness, anxiety reduction, study habits
**Administrators:** Focus on academic outcomes, phone policy support
**IT Directors:** Focus on implementation ease, data privacy compliance

Use templates from `OPS/VA_SCRIPTS/` based on who you're contacting.

### Subject Lines

- "Student focus tool - free pilot for [School Name]"
- "Question about [School Name] phone policy"
- "Study habit tool for your students"
- "Supporting [School Name] students with focus"

Avoid:
- "Partnership opportunity"
- "Free offer"
- Anything that sounds like a sales pitch

## Follow-Up Sequences

### Timeline (Longer for Schools)

Schools move slowly. Patience required.

- Day 0: Initial email
- Day 5: First follow-up
- Day 12: Second follow-up
- Day 21: Final follow-up
- After Day 21: Mark closed

### Follow-Up Templates

**Day 5 Follow-up:**

Quick summary: Free 30-day pilot for students struggling with phone distraction during study time.

Worth a quick call to see if it fits?

[Name]

[Name]

If student phone distraction becomes a focus area later, feel free to reach out.

Best,
[Name]

## Handling Responses

### Positive Response

**What it looks like:**
- "This sounds interesting for our students"
- "Can you send more information?"
- "I'd like to discuss with my principal"
- "How do we set up a pilot?"

**What to do:**
1. ESCALATE in Slack: "HOT LEAD - School"
2. Reply with pilot details and Calendly
3. Update tracker: "Positive - Call Pending"

**Reply template:**
```
Hi [Name],

Great to hear from you. Here's how the pilot works:

- 30 students, 30 days, completely free
- We help select students who could benefit (struggling with phone distraction)
- Students download app, set study goals, app blocks distractions until goal met
- End of pilot: report on usage and student feedback

Happy to walk through details: [Calendly link]

What questions can I answer?

[Name]
```

### "I need to check with administration"

Common response. Support them.

```
Hi [Name],

Totally understand. Happy to provide:
- One-page summary for administration
- Data privacy documentation
- Info on pilots at other schools

Let me know what would help you make the case.

[Name]
```

### Objection: Budget Concerns

```
Hi [Name],

The pilot is completely free - no budget needed to start. If [School Name] wants to continue after, we have flexible pricing based on student count. Many schools start with just the counseling department budget.

Happy to discuss options: [Calendly]

[Name]
```

### Objection: Privacy Concerns

```
Hi [Name],

Great question. Data privacy is critical for us:

- COPPA and FERPA compliant
- No personal data sold or shared
- Students control their own settings
- School sees aggregate data only, not individual behavior

Happy to send our full privacy policy and answer specific questions.

[Name]
```

### Negative Response

```
Hi [Name],

Understood, thanks for letting me know. I won't follow up further.

Best wishes to [School Name] for the school year.

[Name]
```

## Common Questions (FAQ)

### "What does the app do?"

StudyLock helps students build focused study habits. Students set a study goal (like 30 minutes). Until they complete focused time, distracting apps stay blocked. Builds self-regulation and reduces phone temptation.

### "Is this a phone ban?"

No. Students choose to use it. It's a tool that helps them follow through on their own intentions to study. Many students want to focus but get pulled into social media - this helps them stick to their goals.

### "What age is this for?"

Designed for middle school and up. Most effective for ages 12-22. Younger students need parent involvement.

### "How do we know it works?"

Pilot includes before/after surveys on study habits and focus. We also track usage data (anonymized) showing time saved from distractions.

### "What about students without phones?"

The app is optional. Students without smartphones or whose parents don't want them using it simply don't participate. No student is required to use it.

### "Is this compliant with FERPA/COPPA?"

Yes. We don't collect educational records. Student data is minimal (usage patterns only) and we never share identifiable data. Full compliance documentation available.

### "What does it cost after the pilot?"

School-wide licensing starts at $2/student/month with volume discounts. Most schools spend less than they do on other ed-tech tools. Pilot is completely free.

## School Calendar Awareness

### Best Times to Reach Out

**August-September:** Administrators planning year, open to new programs
**January:** Post-holiday reset, new semester energy
**Late Spring:** Planning for next year

### Worst Times

**October-November:** Busy with current programs, parent conferences
**December:** Holiday break, no decisions
**May-June:** End of year chaos, testing

### Email Timing

- Tuesday-Thursday: Best response
- Morning (8-10am their time): Before school starts
- Avoid: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, school breaks

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Be patient - schools move slowly
- Focus on student outcomes, not app features
- Provide documentation when asked
- Respect decision-making hierarchy
- Follow up appropriately

### Don't

- Pressure for quick decisions
- Go around the chain of command
- Promise academic results we can't guarantee
- Contact parents directly without school involvement
- Send more than 30 emails/day
