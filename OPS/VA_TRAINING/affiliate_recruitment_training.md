Complete training guide for VAs recruiting micro-influencers and content creators as affiliates.

Build an army of affiliates who promote our apps organically. You're recruiting:

1. Micro-influencers (1k-50k followers)
2. Content creators in faith, fitness, and productivity niches
3. Bloggers and newsletter writers

The offer: 20-30% recurring commission on sales they drive.

## Understanding the Affiliate Model

### What We're Offering

**Commission structure:**
- Bronze tier (1-10 sales/month): 20% commission
- Silver tier (11-50 sales/month): 25% commission
- Gold tier (51+ sales/month): 30% commission

**Example earnings:**
- App subscription: $50/year
- At 25% commission: $12.50 per sale
- 10 sales/month = $125/month recurring
- 50 sales/month = $625/month recurring

**What affiliates get:**
- Unique referral link
- Real-time dashboard showing clicks/conversions
- Monthly payout (PayPal, Wise, or bank transfer)
- Promotional assets (graphics, copy)
- Direct line to support

### Why Creators Join

- Passive income aligned with their content
- Products they can genuinely recommend
- Supporting their audience with useful tools
- No inventory, no customer service
- Recurring commission (not one-time)

## Finding Affiliates

### Target Creators by Niche

**Faith (for PrayerLock):**
- Christian content creators
- Devotional accounts
- Worship leaders with following
- Faith-based lifestyle influencers
- Christian podcasters and bloggers

**Fitness (for WalkToUnlock):**
- Fitness influencers (not huge bodybuilders)
- Running/walking accounts
- Health coaches
- Wellness bloggers
- Home workout creators

**Productivity (for all apps):**
- Productivity YouTubers
- Digital minimalism advocates
- Study/student accounts
- Tech reviewers (smaller ones)
- Newsletter writers on focus/habits

### Ideal Creator Profile

**Must have:**
- 1,000-50,000 followers (sweet spot: 5k-20k)
- Active posting (at least weekly)
- Engaged audience (comments, not just likes)
- Content relevant to our niche
- Based in English-speaking country (or fluent English)

**Red flags:**
- Bought followers (check engagement rate)
- Only promotional content
- Inactive for weeks
- Very different audience (gaming, memes, politics)
- Already promoting competitor

### Where to Find Creators

**Instagram:**
- Search hashtags: #faithblogger, #fitnessjourney, #productivitytips
- Look at accounts tagged by small creators
- Check followers of competitor apps

**TikTok:**
- Search relevant hashtags
- Look for educational/lifestyle content
- Note: TikTok outreach is harder (DMs limited)

**YouTube:**
- Search niche + review, tutorial, routine
- Look at smaller channels (1k-50k subs)
- Check video descriptions for contact email

**Twitter/X:**
- Search bios for niche keywords
- Look at who follows industry accounts
- Check lists curated by others

**Newsletters:**
- Substack discovery for niche topics
- Beehiiv featured newsletters
- Search "[niche] newsletter"

### Research Process

**Step 1: Find the creator**
- Search relevant hashtags/keywords
- Note follower count and engagement

**Step 2: Evaluate fit**
- Does their content align with our app?
- Would their audience benefit from it?
- Do they seem authentic (not just ads)?

**Step 3: Check engagement**
- Comments on recent posts
- Engagement rate (likes/comments vs followers)
- 2-5% engagement = healthy

**Step 4: Find contact**
- Instagram: DM or bio link email
- YouTube: Channel about page email
- Twitter: DM or bio link
- Email in bio or website contact

**Step 5: Note personalization**
- Specific post you liked
- Content theme they focus on
- Recent milestone or achievement

## Writing Outreach Messages

### DM vs Email

**Use DM when:**
- Instagram creator (preferred)
- Twitter with open DMs
- Quick, casual approach fits

**Use email when:**
- DMs are closed
- More professional creator (blogger, podcaster)
- YouTube creators (usually have business email)
- Substack/newsletter writers

### DM Formula (Keep it SHORT)

DMs need to be concise. Creators get tons of messages.

1. **Compliment** - specific, genuine (1 sentence)
2. **Who you are** - brief (1 sentence)
3. **The offer** - quick value prop (1-2 sentences)
4. **Ask** - simple, low commitment (1 sentence)

**Total: 4-5 sentences max for DM**

### Email Formula (Slightly longer OK)

1. **Compliment/hook** (1-2 sentences)
2. **Who you are and what you're offering** (2-3 sentences)
3. **Why them specifically** (1-2 sentences)
4. **Clear next step** (1 sentence)

**Total: 6-8 sentences for email**

### Personalization Examples

**Good:**
- "Your post about building a morning prayer routine was so practical. I sent it to three friends."
- "Love how you break down complex fitness topics without being preachy. Your video on rest days was exactly what I needed to hear."
- "Your productivity newsletter is one of the few I actually open every week. The focus blocks strategy changed how I work."

**Bad:**
- "I love your content!" (too generic)
- "You're an amazing influencer!" (sounds fake)
- "Your account is great." (lazy)

### Template Examples

**Instagram DM - Faith Creator:**
```
Hey [Name]! Just found your account through the #morningprayer hashtag - your content on building consistent quiet time is so needed.

We built PrayerLock, an app that blocks distracting apps until you complete morning prayer. Thought it'd resonate with your audience.

Would you want to check it out + earn 25% on any subscriptions? Free to join, just takes 2 mins.

Let me know!
```

**Instagram DM - Fitness Creator:**
```
Hey [Name], your 30-day walking challenge content is awesome. Simple but actually useful.

We have a fitness app (WalkToUnlock) that blocks social media until you hit step goals. Think your audience would love it.

Want to try it free + get 25% commission on any signups through your link? Super easy to set up.
```

**Email - Productivity Creator:**
```
Subject: Collab idea for [Name/Newsletter]

Hey [Name],

I've been reading [Newsletter Name] for a few months - your piece on deep work strategies actually got me to turn off notifications for good.

We built StudyLock, an app that helps people build focused work habits by blocking distracting apps until they complete a focus session. Given your audience's interest in productivity, thought it might be a fit.

We're looking for a few affiliate partners. You'd get:
- 25% recurring commission on subscriptions ($12.50 per signup)
- Free lifetime access to the app
- Custom landing page if you want one

If you're interested, I can send you a link to sign up (takes 2 minutes) or we can jump on a quick call.

Either way, keep up the great work on the newsletter.

[Name]
```

## Follow-Up Strategy

### Timeline

- Day 0: Initial DM/email
- Day 3: Follow-up (if no response)
- Day 7: Final follow-up
- After Day 7: Mark closed

Creators are busy. Don't spam.

### Follow-Up Templates

**Day 3 Follow-up (DM):**
```
Hey [Name], just bumping my message from a few days ago about the affiliate program. No pressure either way - just let me know if you'd want to try it out!
```

**Day 7 Final (DM):**
```
Last ping on this! If the timing's not right, totally get it. Feel free to reach out later if affiliate stuff ever interests you.
```

**Email Follow-up:**
```
Subject: Re: [Original subject]

Hey [Name],

Just floating this back up in case it got buried.

Quick recap: 25% commission affiliate program for [app name], aligns with your [niche] content.

If you're interested, here's the signup link: [link]

If not, no worries at all. Just wanted to make sure you saw it.

[Name]
```

## Handling Responses

### Interested

**What it looks like:**
- "This sounds cool, tell me more"
- "How do I sign up?"
- "What's the commission structure?"
- "I'd be interested in trying the app"

**What to do:**
1. Note in Slack: "Interested Affiliate - [Name]"
2. Reply with signup link and quick instructions
3. Offer to answer any questions
4. Update tracker: "Interested - Signup Pending"

**Reply template:**
```
Awesome! Here's how to get started:

1. Sign up here: [affiliate signup link]
2. You'll get your unique link immediately
3. Download the app to try it yourself (free access for affiliates)
4. Share your link however works for you - bio, stories, posts

Commission is 25% recurring - so you earn every time someone renews.

Any questions, just ask. Excited to have you!
```

### Questions Before Committing

**"How much can I actually make?"**
```
Depends on your audience and how much you promote. To give you an idea:

- Each sale = $12.50 commission
- 10 sales/month = $125
- 50 sales/month = $625+

Some affiliates treat it as passive (link in bio, occasional mention) and make $50-100/month. Others who actively promote make $500+.

No minimum commitment though - even a few sales covers nice extras.
```

**"Do I have to post about it constantly?"**
```
Nope. Most affiliates just:
- Put link in bio
- Mention it when relevant to their content
- Share occasionally in stories

No posting requirements. Just use it authentically when it fits.
```

**"Is the app actually good?"**
```
I'd say try it first! You get free lifetime access as an affiliate. Use it for a week, see if you like it. Only promote if you genuinely would recommend it.
```

### Not Interested

```
No problem at all! Thanks for letting me know. If things change, feel free to reach out.
```

Mark as "Declined" in tracker. Don't follow up again.

### No Response

After Day 7 with no response, mark as "No Response - Closed" and move on.

## Platform-Specific Tips

### Instagram

- DM from a real-looking account (not brand account)
- Keep DMs short and casual
- Avoid "partnership" language (sounds corporate)
- Request button works better for larger accounts

### Twitter/X

- DMs only if open
- Can quote their tweet with appreciation, then DM
- Keep very casual
- Some prefer email (check bio)

### YouTube

- Always use email (business inquiries)
- Be slightly more professional
- Reference specific video
- They're used to sponsorships, position as affiliate instead

### TikTok

- DMs very limited
- Check if they have Instagram link in bio
- Or email in bio
- TikTok comments can work to initiate

### Newsletter Writers

- Always email
- Reference specific issue you liked
- Position as mutual benefit
- They appreciate revenue diversification

## Creator Red Flags

**Don't recruit if:**
- Engagement seems fake (thousands of likes, zero comments)
- Content is mostly ads/promotions
- Audience seems irrelevant (gaming, memes, etc.)
- They have negative/controversial presence
- They're promoting direct competitors
- They ask for upfront payment (we don't pay to recruit)

**Politely decline:**
```
Thanks for the interest! We're looking for creators in [specific niche] right now, so it might not be the best fit. Appreciate you reaching out though!
```

## Common Objections

### "I only do paid sponsorships"

```
Totally understand. Just to clarify - affiliate is different from sponsorship. No deliverables required, no posting schedule. Just passive income if your audience converts.

Some creators do both - sponsored posts for flat fee, and affiliate link in bio for ongoing passive income.

Either way, no pressure!
```

### "I don't want to spam my audience"

```
100% agree - that's why we like working with people who genuinely care about their audience.

Most affiliates just mention it when it naturally fits their content. No spamming, no constant promotion. Just authentic recommendations when relevant.

Your audience trusts you because you're not spammy - we want to keep it that way.
```

### "I'm already an affiliate for something similar"

```
Got it. Out of curiosity, which one? Just want to understand the space better.

If you ever want to add another option for your audience, let me know. Some creators have multiple affiliate links and let their audience choose.
```

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Personalize every message
- Be genuine and casual
- Focus on their audience benefit
- Respect "no" immediately
- Track everything
- Report quality creators even if they decline

### Don't

- Copy-paste without customization
- Use corporate/marketing language
- Over-promise earnings
- Follow up more than twice
- Recruit creators who don't fit
- Offer upfront payment for joining
