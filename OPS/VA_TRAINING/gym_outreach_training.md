## Finding Gyms

### Criteria for Good Gym Targets

**Must have:**
- Independent gym or small chain (not national franchise HQ)
- Active Instagram presence
- Owner/manager name findable
- USA location

**Ideal:**
- 500-5000 members (estimate from followers)
- Posts member transformations or challenges
- Has app or digital presence
- Engaged social following
- Personal training focus

**Avoid:**
- Big box gyms managed by corporate (Planet Fitness corporate, etc.)
- Gyms with no social presence
- CrossFit boxes (different business model)
- Martial arts/yoga only studios (unless they have fitness component)
- Gyms that already prominently feature a competing app

### Target Categories

**Tier 1 (Best):**
- Boutique fitness studios (F45 franchises, local HIIT studios)
- Personal training gyms
- Independent gyms 1-3 locations

**Tier 2 (Good):**
- Regional chains (5-20 locations)
- Gym franchisees (individual Planet Fitness owners)
- Specialty gyms (powerlifting, climbing)

**Tier 3 (Lower priority):**
- 24-hour gyms
- Large chains
- Hotel gyms

### Where to Find Gyms

**Google Maps:**
- Search "gym" or "fitness center" + [city]
- Filter by reviews (4+ stars preferred)
- Check website and social links

**Instagram Search:**
- Search #[city]gym or #[city]fitness
- Look at gym accounts in results
- Check their follower count and engagement

**Yelp:**
- Search gyms in target city
- Good for finding contact info
- Check reviews for owner names

**LinkedIn:**
- Search "gym owner" + [city]
- Find decision makers directly
- Note: don't cold message on LinkedIn, use email

### Research Process (Step by Step)

**Step 1: Find the gym**
- Start with target city
- Use Google Maps or Instagram
- Verify they're independent or franchisee

**Step 2: Check social presence**
- Instagram: posting frequency, engagement
- Facebook: page activity
- Look for owner/manager posts

**Step 3: Find decision maker**
- Check website "About" or "Team" page
- Look at Instagram bio for name
- Search LinkedIn for gym name

**Step 4: Find email**
- Website contact page
- Instagram bio (sometimes linked)
- LinkedIn (sometimes shown)
- Hunter.io on their domain

**Step 5: Note personalization**
- Recent member challenge they ran?
- Expansion, renovation, anniversary?
- Content focus (transformation stories, etc.)?
- Pain point hints (talking about member retention)?

## Writing the Pitch

### Understand the Value Prop

Gyms care about:
1. Member retention (reduce churn)
2. Engagement (members actually showing up)
3. Differentiation from competitors
4. Revenue opportunities

Your pitch addresses all four:
- App keeps members engaged outside gym
- Challenges create community and retention
- Unique offering vs competitors
- 15% rev share = new revenue stream

### The Formula

1. **Hook question** that identifies the problem (1 line)
2. **What we offer** and why it matters to THEM (2-3 lines)
3. **Specific offer** - free pilot, rev share (1-2 lines)
4. **Simple ask** (1 line)

### Personalization Examples

**Good personalization:**
- "Saw you just launched a 6-week transformation challenge - our app would be perfect for keeping participants accountable between gym visits."
- "Your Instagram shows great member engagement. We help gyms extend that energy outside the four walls."
- "Noticed you're expanding to a second location - congrats. We work with growing gyms on member retention tools."

**Bad personalization (don't do this):**
- "I see you own a gym." (worthless)
- "Your gym looks nice." (generic)
- "I noticed you have Instagram." (so does everyone)

### Template Selection

Use templates from `OPS/VA_SCRIPTS/`:
- gym_pitch_v1.md - Problem/solution angle (use 40%)
- gym_pitch_v2.md - Revenue focus (use 30%)
- gym_pitch_v3.md - Competitive advantage angle (use 30%)

### Subject Line Rules

- Keep under 50 characters
- Focus on benefit, not product name
- Never clickbait or ALL CAPS
- Test subjects we provide

**Good subjects:**
- "Member engagement tool for [Gym Name]"
- "Quick question about [Gym Name] members"
- "Partnership idea for [Gym Name]"

**Bad subjects:**
- "AMAZING OPPORTUNITY!!!"
- "You need to see this"
- "Free app"

### Before Hitting Send

Checklist:
- [ ] Gym name spelled correctly
- [ ] Owner/manager name correct
- [ ] Personalization is specific
- [ ] No grammar errors
- [ ] Correct signature
- [ ] Logged in tracker

## Handling Responses

### Positive Response

**What it looks like:**
- "Tell me more about the pilot"
- "How does the rev share work?"
- "Can we do a call?"
- "Send me more info"

**What to do:**
1. ESCALATE in Slack: "HOT LEAD - Gym"
2. Reply with info and Calendly link
3. Update tracker: "Positive - Demo Pending"

**Reply template:**
```
Hi [Name],

Great to hear from you. Quick answers:

- Pilot: 30 days free, we set up branded app for [Gym Name]
- Rev share: You get 15% of any premium subscriptions ($50/year) from your members
- Dashboard: Track member engagement, run challenges

Let's do a quick call to walk through it: [Calendly link]

What day works best?

[Name]
```

### Question/Objection

**Common questions:**
- "How much does this cost us?"
- "What data do you collect?"
- "We already have an app"
- "How is this different from [competitor]?"

**What to do:**
1. Check FAQ below for answer
2. If covered, respond using FAQ
3. If not, escalate for guidance
4. Log objection type in tracker

### Negative Response

**What it looks like:**
- "Not interested"
- "We're good"
- "Remove me"

**What to do:**
1. Reply politely
2. Mark as "Declined" in tracker
3. Add to do-not-contact list

**Reply template:**
```
Hi [Name],

Got it, thanks for letting me know. I won't follow up again.

Best of luck with [Gym Name].

[Name]
```

## Common Questions (FAQ)

### "What does the app do?"

WalkToUnlock requires users to hit step goals before unlocking social media and other distracting apps. Perfect for gym members - encourages movement even on rest days.

### "What's the cost to us?"

Zero. The pilot is free. We make money from premium subscriptions ($50/year) and share 15% with you.

### "How much could we actually make?"

Depends on member adoption. Example: 500 members, 10% download, 20% go premium = 10 paying users = $500/year to you. Scale with more members.

### "What data do you collect from our members?"

Step counts and app usage only. We never share individual data. You see aggregate stats: active users, average steps, challenge participation.

### "We already have an app / use another service"

This isn't a replacement for your member management app. It's a supplement that focuses specifically on activity motivation through app blocking. Works alongside anything you already use.

### "How is setup handled?"

We handle everything. We create a branded version with your logo and colors. You get a unique link. Share it with members however you want (email, QR in gym, social).

### "What if members don't like it?"

It's optional for members. They choose to download and can disable anytime. No negative impact on members who don't want it.

### "What happens after the pilot?"

If it works, we continue the partnership. You keep earning 15% of premium subs indefinitely. No commitment required - either party can end anytime.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Focus on THEIR benefit, not our product
- Personalize with specific observations
- Respond to positive leads within hours
- Log everything immediately
- Ask when unsure

### Don't

- Pitch features instead of benefits
- Send generic "I noticed you have a gym" emails
- Promise things not in the offer
- Contact corporate offices for franchises
- Send more than 40 emails/day from one account
- Follow up more than 3 times

## Troubleshooting

### "I can't find owner email"

1. Try info@gym.com or contact@gym.com
2. Check LinkedIn for owner
3. Use Hunter.io on their domain
4. Look at Facebook page "About" section
5. As last resort, use website contact form (log differently)

### "They said they're a franchise - should I contact?"

Yes, if they're an independent franchisee (they own their location). No, if they're pointing you to corporate.

### "They asked about pricing I don't know"

Escalate. Don't make up numbers. Say "Let me get you exact details and follow up."

### "They mentioned a competitor"

Note which competitor in tracker. We want to know who we're up against. Don't trash competitors in your response.

## Gym Industry Notes

### Busy Seasons
- January: New Year's rush (owners busy but interested in retention)
- May-June: Summer prep (members active)
- September: Back to school surge

### Slow Seasons
- November-December: Holiday drop-off (owners have time to take calls)
- July-August: Summer slump

### Best Days/Times to Email
- Tuesday-Thursday: Best response rates
- Morning (9-11am their time): Decision makers at desk
- Avoid: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons
