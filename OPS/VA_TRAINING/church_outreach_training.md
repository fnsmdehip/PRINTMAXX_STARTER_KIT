# Church Outreach Training

Complete training guide for VAs doing church partnership outreach.

---

## Your Mission

Help churches discover a prayer habit app that benefits their congregation. You're not selling anything to the pastor. You're offering a free tool that:

1. Helps their members build consistent prayer habits
2. Gives the church 20% of any premium subscriptions
3. Provides a usage dashboard

Your job: Find churches, personalize outreach, get responses, book calls.

## Finding Churches

### Criteria for Good Church Targets

**Must have:**
- Active Facebook page (posted in last 30 days)
- Based in the USA
- Congregation estimate 100-2000 members
- Pastor or admin email findable

**Ideal:**
- Already uses technology (online sermons, giving app)
- Active youth program
- Mentions digital discipleship or spiritual growth
- Pastor has personal social media presence

**Avoid:**
- Giant megachurches (10,000+ members) - too bureaucratic
- Very small churches (<50 members) - limited opportunity
- Churches with no online presence
- Non-English primary language

### Where to Find Churches

**Church Directories:**
- https://www.churchfinder.com
- https://www.faithstreet.com
- Denomination directories (Southern Baptist, PCUSA, etc.)

**Google Search:**
- "[City] [denomination] church"
- "Churches in [City] with online services"
- "[City] church Facebook"

**Facebook Search:**
- Search "Church" + [City name]
- Look for active pages, not just listed

**Google Maps:**
- Search "church" in target area
- Check website links from Maps listing

### Research Process (Step by Step)

**Step 1: Find the church**
- Start with a target city or state
- Use directory or search
- Verify they have online presence

**Step 2: Check Facebook/Instagram**
- Are they posting regularly?
- What content do they share?
- Note anything specific for personalization

**Step 3: Find pastor name**
- Check church website "About" or "Staff" page
- Look at Facebook page "About" section
- Check LinkedIn if needed

**Step 4: Find email**
- Check website contact page
- Look for pastor@church.com or info@church.com
- Use Hunter.io if email not obvious
- Check Facebook "About" for email

**Step 5: Note personalization**
- Recent sermon series topic?
- Upcoming event?
- Something specific on their Facebook?
- Church milestone (anniversary, new building)?

## Writing Personalized Emails

### The Formula

Every email needs:
1. **Specific observation** about their church (1 line)
2. **What we're offering** (2-3 lines)
3. **Simple ask** (1 line)

### Personalization Examples

**Good personalization:**
- "Saw your recent sermon series on Digital Sabbath - love that you're tackling screen time from a faith perspective."
- "Noticed you just celebrated 25 years in your community - congratulations!"
- "Your Easter livestream had great engagement. Clear your congregation is active online."

**Bad personalization (don't do this):**
- "I noticed your church is doing great things." (too generic)
- "I saw you have a website." (meaningless)
- "Your church seems nice." (lazy)

### Template Selection

Use templates from `OPS/VA_SCRIPTS/`:
- church_email_v1.md - Standard approach (use 50% of time)
- church_email_v2.md - Congregation benefit focus (use 25%)
- church_email_v3.md - Revenue share focus (use 25%)

We A/B test these to see what works best. You'll be told if we need to shift percentages.

### Subject Line Rules

- Keep under 50 characters
- Include church name when possible
- Never use clickbait or ALL CAPS
- Test subjects we provide, don't create your own

### Before Hitting Send

Checklist:
- [ ] Church name spelled correctly
- [ ] Pastor name spelled correctly
- [ ] Personalization is specific and accurate
- [ ] No grammar errors
- [ ] Correct signature
- [ ] Logged in tracker before sending

## Handling Responses

### Positive Response

**What it looks like:**
- "Tell me more"
- "This sounds interesting"
- "Can we schedule a call?"
- "Send me information I can review"

**What to do:**
1. ESCALATE immediately in Slack with "HOT LEAD"
2. Reply acknowledging their interest (use template)
3. Share Calendly link for booking
4. Update tracker: "Positive Response - Call Pending"

**Reply template:**
```
Hi [Pastor Name],

Thanks for your interest. Here's a quick overview:

- Congregation members get free access to the prayer habit app
- [Church Name] receives 20% of any premium subscriptions from your members
- You get a dashboard showing engagement

Happy to walk you through it on a quick call. Here's my calendar: [Calendly link]

What time works for you?

[Name]
```

### Question/Objection

**What it looks like:**
- "How does this work exactly?"
- "What's the cost?"
- "Is this a religious organization?"

**What to do:**
1. Note the question in Slack
2. Check if answer is in FAQ (below)
3. If yes, respond with FAQ answer
4. If no, escalate for guidance
5. Update tracker with objection type

### Negative Response

**What it looks like:**
- "Not interested"
- "Please remove me from your list"
- "We already have something"

**What to do:**
1. Reply politely (use template below)
2. Mark as "Declined" in tracker
3. Add to do-not-contact list
4. Note reason if they gave one

**Reply template:**
```
Hi [Pastor Name],

Understood, and thanks for letting me know. I won't follow up further.

Blessings to you and [Church Name].

[Name]
```

### No Response

After Day 14 with no response:
1. Mark as "No Response - Closed"
2. Do NOT contact again (unless specifically told to re-engage later)

## Common Questions (FAQ)

### "What does the app do?"

PrayerLock helps build consistent prayer habits. Users set a prayer goal (like 10 minutes each morning). Until they complete it, distracting apps are blocked. It uses faith-based prompts and tracks streaks.

### "What's the cost to our church?"

Nothing. The app is free for church members. We offer a premium version ($50/year) with extra features. Churches get 20% of premium subscriptions from their congregation.

### "Is this a denominational thing?"

No, we're non-denominational. The app works for Christians of all backgrounds.

### "How do members sign up?"

We give you a unique link or code for your church. Members download the app and enter the code. That's it - they're connected to your church.

### "What data do you collect?"

Basic usage stats only. We never share individual data. Churches see aggregate engagement (how many members active, average streak, etc.), not individual behavior.

### "We already use [other app]."

That's great. PrayerLock is specifically for building prayer habits through app blocking - it's different from Bible apps or giving platforms. Many churches use it alongside existing tools.

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Personalize every email (no copy-paste without customization)
- Log everything in the tracker immediately
- Respond to positive leads same day
- Ask questions when unsure
- Post daily updates in Slack
- Be respectful and professional

### Don't

- Send emails without personalization
- Skip follow-ups
- Create your own templates without approval
- Promise things not in the offer
- Argue with negative responses
- Contact the same church from multiple emails
- Send more than 50 emails per day from one account

## Troubleshooting

### "I can't find pastor's email"

1. Try info@church.com or office@church.com
2. Check LinkedIn for pastor profile with email
3. Use Hunter.io on their domain
4. As last resort, use contact form (log it differently)

### "Email bounced"

1. Mark as "Invalid Email" in tracker
2. Try alternate email if you found one
3. Don't re-send to same address

### "I got a spam complaint"

1. STOP and escalate immediately
2. Screenshot everything
3. Do not contact that church again
4. We may need to adjust approach

### "Not sure if this is a good church to target"

When in doubt, skip it. Better to have quality targets than waste time on poor fits.
