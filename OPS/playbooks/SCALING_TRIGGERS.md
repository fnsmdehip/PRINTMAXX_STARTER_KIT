# PRINTMAXX Scaling Triggers

**Purpose:** Know exactly when to level up operations
**Rule:** Scale from abundance, not desperation

---

## When to Hire First VA

### Quantitative Triggers

Hire when ANY of these are true:

| Trigger | Threshold | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| Revenue | >$3k/month consistent | Can afford $500-1k/month |
| Time on admin | >10 hours/week | Opportunity cost too high |
| Response time | >48 hours to leads | Losing sales |
| Growth rate | Flat for 2+ months | Bottleneck is you |

### Qualitative Triggers

- You're turning down opportunities due to time
- Quality of work is slipping
- Burnout affecting creativity
- Same tasks repeating daily with no variation

### What to Delegate First

**Best first tasks for VA:**
1. Social media scheduling
2. Email inbox triage
3. Customer support (templated responses)
4. Data entry and organization
5. Research and compilation
6. Basic image/content formatting

**Keep yourself:**
- Strategy decisions
- Original content creation
- Sales calls
- Product development
- Customer relationships

### Hiring Checklist

Before hiring:
- [ ] Document every task you'll delegate (SOPs)
- [ ] Calculate time savings vs. cost
- [ ] Set up communication system (Slack, Loom)
- [ ] Create training materials
- [ ] Define success metrics

**Where to hire:**
- Upwork (general VA)
- Belay (US-based)
- OnlineJobs.ph (Philippines)
- Time Etc (UK-based)

**Budget:**
- Philippines: $5-15/hour
- US/UK: $25-50/hour
- Start with 5-10 hours/week

## When to Add Automation

### Quantitative Triggers

| Trigger | Threshold | Automation Type |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Same task 10x/week | Repetitive manual work | Zapier/Make |
| Email responses | >20/day templated | Email sequences |
| Social posting | >3 platforms | Buffer/Typefully |
| Lead handling | >50/week | CRM automation |
| Reporting | >2 hours/week | Dashboard automation |

### Best ROI Automations

**Tier 1 - Set up immediately:**
1. Welcome email sequence (ConvertKit, Beehiiv)
2. Social scheduling (Buffer, Typefully)
3. Lead capture to spreadsheet (Zapier)
4. Invoice/payment (Stripe)

**Tier 2 - When volume increases:**
1. Customer onboarding sequence
2. Follow-up sequences (nurture, cart abandon)
3. Social mention monitoring
4. Reporting dashboards

**Tier 3 - When scaling:**
1. Multi-step customer journeys
2. Dynamic content personalization
3. Predictive analytics
4. Custom integrations

### Automation Decision Framework

For each potential automation, ask:

1. **Frequency:** How often do I do this? (>5x/week = automate)
2. **Consistency:** Is the process the same each time? (yes = automate)
3. **Cost:** What's the tool cost vs. time saved?
4. **Fragility:** Will it break often and need maintenance?
5. **Value:** Does human touch matter here?

**Calculate ROI:**
```
Hours saved per week x Your hourly value = Weekly savings
Weekly savings x 4 = Monthly savings
Monthly savings - Tool cost = Net benefit
```

If net benefit > $100/month, automate it.

### Automation Checklist

Before automating:
- [ ] Process documented in OPS/
- [ ] Manual process works consistently
- [ ] Edge cases identified
- [ ] Error handling defined
- [ ] Monitoring in place

## When to Expand to New Channel

### Quantitative Triggers

| Trigger | Threshold | Consider Expansion |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| Primary channel growth | Slowing (<5%/month) | Add channel |
| Engagement rate | Declining 20%+ | Diversify |
| Audience saturation | Same people, diminishing returns | New audience |
| Revenue per follower | Decreasing | Different channel may monetize better |
| Content repurpose potential | >50% reusable | Add channel |

### Channel Expansion Priority

**If starting from X/Twitter:**

1. **Newsletter** - Own your audience (add first)
2. **LinkedIn** - If B2B component exists
3. **YouTube** - If content is teachable/demonstrable
4. **Threads** - Low effort if already doing X
5. **TikTok** - If product is visual or trend-friendly

**Evaluation criteria:**
- Where does your audience already hang out?
- What content format fits your strengths?
- What's the effort/return ratio?
- Can you repurpose existing content?

### New Channel Launch Checklist

- [ ] Research channel best practices (2-3 hours)
- [ ] Study 5 successful accounts in niche
- [ ] Create channel-specific content plan
- [ ] Batch first 2 weeks of content
- [ ] Commit to 30-day test
- [ ] Define success metrics upfront
- [ ] Don't neglect existing channels

### Channel Effort Guide

| Channel | Setup Time | Ongoing Weekly | Expected Results |
|---------|------------|----------------|------------------|
| Newsletter | 2-4 hours | 2-3 hours | 2-3 months |
| LinkedIn | 1-2 hours | 3-4 hours | 1-2 months |
| YouTube | 5-10 hours | 5-10 hours | 6-12 months |
| Threads | 30 min | 1-2 hours | 1-2 months |
| TikTok | 2-3 hours | 5-10 hours | 1-3 months |
| Podcast | 4-6 hours | 3-5 hours | 6-12 months |

## When to Raise Prices

### Quantitative Triggers

| Trigger | Threshold | Action |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Conversion rate | >10% on offers | Price is too low |
| No price objections | Last 20 sales | Leave money on table |
| Capacity maxed | Can't fulfill more | Price to reduce demand |
| Cost increases | Margin under 60% | Raise to maintain margin |
| Competitor prices | 30%+ higher | Room to increase |

### Price Increase Framework

**Test before committing:**
1. Raise price 20% for new customers
2. Grandfather existing customers
3. Run for 30 days
4. Measure: conversion rate, revenue, complaints

**If conversion drops:**
- Less than 20% drop: Keep new price (higher revenue)
- More than 20% drop: Test middle ground
- Complaints increase: Add value or reduce

**When NOT to raise prices:**
- You're struggling to get ANY sales
- Market is commoditized
- Major competitors just lowered prices
- Product quality isn't proven yet

### Price Increase Communication

**For existing customers:**
"We're raising prices to [price] starting [date]. As an existing customer, you're locked in at your current rate."

**For new pricing:**
Simply update. No explanation needed.
Don't apologize. Don't over-explain.

## When to Cut Losing Products

### Quantitative Triggers

| Trigger | Threshold | Action |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Sales | <5 in 90 days | Cut or pivot |
| Revenue contribution | <5% of total | Evaluate |
| Support burden | >20% of tickets | Cut |
| Refund rate | >10% | Fix or cut |
| Time investment | >20% for <5% revenue | Cut |

### Decision Framework

For each product, calculate:

```
Profit = Revenue - Costs - (Support hours x hourly rate)
Profit margin = Profit / Revenue
Effort ratio = Hours spent / Revenue generated
```

**Cut if:**
- Negative profit margin
- Effort ratio worse than other products
- Opportunity cost of keeping it high

**Keep (but reduce effort) if:**
- Positive margin but low
- Serves as funnel to other products
- Minimal ongoing effort

**Invest more if:**
- High margin
- Good effort ratio
- Growth potential

### Sunsetting Process

1. Stop promoting (remove from homepage, stop ads)
2. Notify existing customers (30-day notice)
3. Offer migration path if applicable
4. Honor existing purchases/support
5. Archive product materials
6. Redirect traffic to alternatives
7. Post-mortem: Why did it fail?

## When to Partner/Collaborate

### Quantitative Triggers

| Trigger | Threshold | Consider Partnership |
|---------|-----------|---------------------|
| Audience size | >5k followers | Enough to trade |
| Revenue | >$2k/month | Can support rev share |
| Skill gaps | Blocking growth | Fill via partner |
| Distribution ceiling | Plateaued 2+ months | Cross-promote |

### Partnership Types

**Content collaborations:**
- Guest posts
- Podcast swaps
- Co-created content
- Shoutout exchanges

**Business partnerships:**
- Affiliate relationships
- Bundle deals
- White label arrangements
- Joint ventures

### Partnership Evaluation

Rate each opportunity:

| Factor | Score 1-5 |
|--------|-----------|
| Audience overlap | |
| Audience quality | |
| Partner reputation | |
| Effort required | |
| Potential upside | |
| Risk level | |

**Proceed if:**
- Total score > 20
- No single factor below 3
- Upside clearly > effort

### Partnership Red Flags

- Want money upfront for "exposure"
- Much smaller/unengaged audience
- History of complaints
- Vague terms
- Rush to commit
- Won't share metrics

## Scaling Decision Tree

```
Revenue consistent for 3+ months?
├─ No → Focus on product-market fit
└─ Yes → Bottleneck is...
         ├─ Time → Hire VA or automate
         ├─ Audience → Expand channels
         ├─ Conversion → Optimize funnel
         └─ Product → Raise prices or add products

Growth slowing?
├─ Engagement down → Content quality or saturation
├─ Traffic same, conv down → Funnel issue
├─ New leads down → Acquisition issue
└─ Churn up → Product or support issue
```

## Scaling Readiness Checklist

Before scaling anything, ensure:

- [ ] Core business profitable (not just revenue-positive)
- [ ] Processes documented (can delegate/automate)
- [ ] Metrics tracked consistently
- [ ] Cash reserve (3+ months)
- [ ] Founder not burned out
- [ ] Customer feedback positive
- [ ] Legal/compliance handled

---

## Scaling Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Scaling too early** - Amplifies broken processes
2. **Scaling without data** - Flying blind
3. **Hiring before documenting** - VA fails, you blame them
4. **Automating broken process** - Faster failure
5. **Expanding channels too fast** - Diluted effort
6. **Raising prices without value** - Churn increases
7. **Partnerships for ego** - Not strategic
8. **Cutting too slowly** - Sunk cost fallacy

**Rule:** Scale what's working. Fix or cut what's not.
