## How to Research Current Trends

Before creating videos, research what's currently trending:

### Sources to Check

1. **TikTok Creative Center** - https://ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter/inspiration/popular/music/pc/en

3. **Tokchart** - https://tokchart.com (best for trajectory data)
4. **YouTube "TikTok Compilation"** - See what songs appear repeatedly

### Scene-Based Music Switching

For longer videos (30-60s), consider mood shifts:

```
Example Structure:
0-8s (Hook): Trending viral sound - grab attention
8-25s (Problem): Darker/tension music
25-40s (Solution): Uplifting, energetic drop
40-50s (CTA): Fade or switch to clean outro
```

### Quick Research Process

1. **Check TikTok Creative Center** for current trending sounds
2. **Note the vibe** (not just the song) - energetic? emotional? aesthetic?
3. **Find royalty-free equivalent** matching that vibe
4. **Test sync** - ensure beats align with your cuts (typically 120-150 BPM)

### BPM Reference for Sync

| Genre | Typical BPM | Cuts Per Beat |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Phonk | 140-150 | Every 2-4 beats |
| House/EDM | 120-130 | Every 4 beats |
| Trap | 130-150 | Every 2 beats |
| Lo-fi | 70-90 | Slower, every 4-8 beats |
| Hyperpop | 140-170 | Fast, every 1-2 beats |

### Sample Prompts for Music Search

When searching Pixabay/Uppbeat:

- "phonk aggressive bass" (for fitness/hype)
- "ethereal dreamy synth" (for aesthetic)
- "club drop energy" (for party vibe)
- "cinematic tension" (for problem/hook)
- "uplifting electronic" (for solution reveal)
