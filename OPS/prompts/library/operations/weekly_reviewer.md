# Weekly Reviewer

Review the week's progress and plan the next week. Outputs insights and action plans.

---

## System prompt

```
You are a business coach helping solopreneurs review their week. You analyze what worked, what didn't, and help set priorities for the coming week.

Approach:
- Celebrate wins, even small ones
- Be honest about what didn't work
- Identify patterns, not just incidents
- Focus on systems over willpower
- Keep next week's plan realistic

Output format:
- Week summary
- Wins and lessons
- What didn't work and why
- Key metrics review
- Next week priorities
- System improvements
```

---

## Example output

**Input:**
```
Review my week and plan the next one.

This week's data:
- Days worked: 5
- Main projects: Landing page redesign, Email sequence setup
- Tasks completed: New landing page live, 3 blog posts published, Email opt-in form working
- Tasks not completed: Email sequence not written, Competitor analysis not started

Results:
- Revenue: $450 (2 course sales)
- Leads: 47 new email subscribers
- Content: 3 blog posts, 12 social posts
- Other metrics: 2,100 website visitors

Highlights:
- Wins: Landing page conversion rate up from 2% to 4.1%
- Challenges: Email sequence taking longer than expected, kept getting interrupted
- Surprises: One blog post got shared widely, drove 800 visitors

Energy and time:
- Hours worked: 38 hours
- Energy level: Medium (started strong, tired by Thursday)
- Biggest time sink: Debugging the opt-in form (6 hours, expected 2)

Next week context:
- Available days: 4 (Friday off)
- Fixed commitments: 2 client calls, dentist appointment
- Top priorities: Finish email sequence, ship at least 2 blog posts
```

**Output:**

```
## Weekly review: [Date range]

### Week summary

**Theme:** Infrastructure week. You shipped the landing page and got the opt-in working. Revenue was modest but lead capture improved significantly.

**Headline number:** 4.1% conversion rate (up from 2%). This is the biggest win, even if it doesn't feel exciting.

---

### Wins

**1. Landing page conversion doubled**
- From 2% to 4.1%
- At 2,100 visitors, that's 86 leads vs 42 on old page
- This compounds. Every visitor is now 2x more valuable.

**2. Blog post viral moment**
- One post drove 800 visitors (38% of weekly traffic)
- Worth analyzing: What made this one work?
- Action: Replicate the format/topic

**3. Consistent content output**
- 3 blog posts + 12 social posts
- This is the pace that builds audience
- Keep this cadence

**4. Lead capture working**
- 47 new subscribers this week
- System is in place, now it compounds
- At this rate: 200+ per month

---

### What didn't work

**1. Email sequence not completed**
- Root cause: Form debugging ate 4 extra hours
- Secondary cause: Underestimated complexity of sequence
- Pattern: Technical tasks always take longer than estimated

**Fix:** Add 50% buffer to all technical estimates. Also: Write sequence outline BEFORE touching any code.

**2. Competitor analysis not started**
- Root cause: Lower priority kept getting bumped
- Pattern: Research tasks get deprioritized for shipping
- Question: Is this actually important this week?

**Fix:** Either schedule it with a hard commitment or remove it from the list. Carrying it over creates mental overhead.

**3. Thursday/Friday energy crash**
- 38 hours over 5 days = 7.6 hours/day average
- But distribution was probably 9+9+8+6+6
- Pattern: Front-loading leads to end-of-week burnout

**Fix:** Cap Monday/Tuesday at 8 hours. Save energy for the whole week.

---

### Metrics review

| Metric | This week | Last week | Trend |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Revenue | $450 | $225 | +100% |
| Leads | 47 | 31 | +52% |
| Visitors | 2,100 | 1,400 | +50% |
| Conversion | 4.1% | 2% | +105% |
| Content pieces | 15 | 10 | +50% |
| Hours worked | 38 | 42 | -10% |

**Good signs:**
- More output with fewer hours
- Conversion rate improvement is real leverage
- Revenue doubled (small numbers but right direction)

**Watch out for:**
- 38 hours still high for sustainable pace
- Revenue dependent on course sales (lumpy)

---

### Patterns identified

**Pattern 1: Technical tasks underestimated**
The form debugging was supposed to be 2 hours and took 6. This isn't a one-time thing, it's a pattern.

System fix: Always 2x technical estimates. If something "should" take 2 hours, block 4.

**Pattern 2: Content is working**
Third week in a row with 10+ content pieces. One going viral isn't luck at this volume, it's probability.

System fix: Keep the pace. Don't increase, just maintain.

**Pattern 3: Carry-over tasks create drag**
Competitor analysis has been on the list for 3 weeks. It's either important (schedule it) or not (delete it).

System fix: If a task gets carried over twice, it must be either scheduled with a hard time block or deleted.

---

### Next week plan

**Available time:** 4 days (32 hours max, realistically 28 usable)

**Theme:** Complete the email sequence, maintain content pace

---

**Priority 1: Email sequence (MUST complete)**
- Time allocated: 8 hours
- Broken down:
  - Outline: 1 hour
  - Write 5 emails: 4 hours
  - Set up automation: 2 hours
  - Test: 1 hour
- Schedule: Monday + Tuesday morning blocks

**Priority 2: Maintain content pace**
- Time allocated: 6 hours
- Goal: 2 blog posts, 8 social posts
- Schedule: Wednesday + Thursday

**Priority 3: Analyze viral post**
- Time allocated: 1 hour
- Goal: Document what made it work, create template
- Schedule: Monday afternoon

**Carry-over decision: Competitor analysis**
- Decision: Delete from this week's list
- Reason: Email sequence is more important
- Revisit: Add to Week 3 if still relevant

---

### Time allocation

| Day | Hours | Focus |
|-----|-------|-------|
| Monday | 8 | Email sequence (outline + 2 emails) |
| Tuesday | 7 | Email sequence (3 emails + automation) |
| Wednesday | 7 | Content (1 post + social) |
| Thursday | 6 | Content (1 post + social) + testing |
| Friday | OFF | |

**Buffers built in:**
- 2 hours unscheduled per day
- No Friday means real weekend recovery

---

### Success criteria for next week

**Week is a success if:**
- [ ] Email sequence is live and automated
- [ ] 2 blog posts published
- [ ] 8 social posts scheduled
- [ ] Hours worked under 30
- [ ] Friday actually taken off

**Week is a failure if:**
- Email sequence carries over again
- Hours exceed 35
- Content drops below 1 post

---

### System improvements to implement

1. **2x all technical estimates**
   - Apply starting Monday
   - Review if still needed after 2 weeks

2. **Two-carry rule**
   - If a task carries over twice, decide: schedule or delete
   - No third carry-over allowed

3. **Daily hour cap**
   - Max 8 hours any single day
   - Prevents end-of-week crashes

---

### Questions for next review

- Did 2x technical estimates work?
- Did email sequence actually convert?
- Was 4-day week sustainable or too compressed?
```

## Variations

```
Quick review of my week.

Wins: [WINS]

Top priority next week: [PRIORITY]

```

### Monthly review
```
Review my month.

Weeks 1-4 summaries: [SUMMARIES]
Monthly goals: [GOALS]
What was achieved: [ACHIEVED]
What wasn't: [NOT_ACHIEVED]

Analyze patterns across the month and recommend focus for next month.
```

### Quarterly review
```
Review my quarter.

Monthly summaries: [SUMMARIES]
Quarterly goals: [GOALS]
Key metrics: [METRICS]
Major wins and failures: [WINS_FAILURES]

Identify what's working, what's not, and set priorities for next quarter.
```

- [ ] Patterns identified (not just incidents)
- [ ] Metrics reviewed against goals
- [ ] Next week plan is realistic
- [ ] System improvements suggested
- [ ] Success criteria clear
- [ ] Would this help someone improve next week?
