# Video Script Writer

Write scripts for short-form video content (TikTok, Reels, Shorts). Follows PRINTMAXX copy-style rules.

---

## System prompt

```
You are a short-form video scriptwriter for educational content. You write scripts that hook fast, deliver value, and don't waste time.

Voice: Direct. Conversational. Like explaining something to a friend.

Rules:
- Hook in the first 3 seconds. No "Hey guys" or "So today I want to talk about"
- One idea per video
- Speak in active voice
- No filler phrases ("basically," "you know," "like I said")
- No AI vocabulary
- Keep under 60 seconds for most content
- End with a specific takeaway, not "Follow for more"
- Write for spoken delivery (contractions, natural rhythm)
```

---

## User prompt template

```
Write a [DURATION]-second video script about [TOPIC].

Platform: [PLATFORM - TikTok, Reels, Shorts]
Format: [FORMAT - talking head, screen recording, B-roll, voiceover]

Context:
- Niche: [NICHE]
- Target audience: [AUDIENCE]
- Goal: [GOAL - teach, entertain, drive traffic, build authority]

Hook angle: [ANGLE - curiosity, contrarian, result, mistake, secret]

Key points:
[POINTS - what must be covered]
```

## Example output

**Input:**
```
Write a 45-second video script about email subject lines.

Platform: TikTok
Format: talking head

Context:
- Niche: Email marketing for creators
- Target audience: Newsletter writers with <5k subscribers
- Goal: Teach one quick win

Hook angle: result

Key points:
- Most people write boring subject lines
- One word change can double open rates
- The word is "you"
```

**Output:**

```
SCRIPT: Email subject line trick

[HOOK - 0:00-0:03]
This one word doubled my email open rates.

[CONTEXT - 0:03-0:10]
I was getting 25% open rates on my newsletter. Fine, but not great.
Then I tested something stupid simple.

[MAIN POINT - 0:10-0:30]
I added the word "you" to my subject lines.

Instead of "5 ways to grow your newsletter"
I wrote "5 ways you can grow your newsletter"

Same content. Different framing.
Open rates jumped to 42%.

[WHY IT WORKS - 0:30-0:40]
People skim their inbox.
"You" makes them feel like the email is actually for them.
Not some mass broadcast.

[TAKEAWAY - 0:40-0:45]
Check your last 5 subject lines.
Add "you" to one and see what happens.

---

VISUAL NOTES:
- Show email inbox on screen during "skim their inbox"
- Display both subject line versions side by side
- Show before/after open rate comparison
```

## Variations

### Tutorial video
```
Write a script teaching [SKILL] in under [DURATION] seconds.

Structure:
1. Hook: What they'll be able to do (3 sec)
2. Why this matters: Pain point or benefit (5 sec)
3. Steps: One action per beat, clear and specific
4. Result: Show what success looks like (5 sec)
5. Takeaway: One sentence summary

Include timing cues and visual suggestions.
```

### Mistake video
```
Write a script about a common mistake in [TOPIC].

Structure:
1. Hook: "Stop doing [X]" or "This mistake is costing you [Y]" (3 sec)
2. The mistake: What most people do wrong (10 sec)
3. Why it's wrong: The consequence (10 sec)
4. The fix: What to do instead (15 sec)
5. Proof: Your result or data (5 sec)

Be specific about the mistake. No vague "not being consistent."
```

### Behind-the-scenes video
```
Write a script showing [PROCESS/ROUTINE].

Structure:
1. Hook: Interesting result or context (3 sec)
2. What you're doing: Quick overview (5 sec)
3. Walk through: Step by step as you do it (30-40 sec)
4. Result: What it produces (5 sec)
5. Tip: One thing viewers can apply (5 sec)

Write as voiceover. Keep energy conversational, not performative.
```

### Contrarian video
```
Write a script challenging [COMMON BELIEF].

Structure:
1. Hook: State the belief, then challenge it (5 sec)
2. Why people believe it: Quick acknowledgment (5 sec)
3. Your experience: What actually happened to you (15 sec)
4. The real answer: What works better (15 sec)
5. Caveat or nuance: When the common advice IS right (5 sec)

Back up claims with specific examples or numbers.
```

## Format guidelines

### Timing markers
```
[HOOK - 0:00-0:03]
[CONTEXT - 0:03-0:08]
[MAIN POINT - 0:08-0:25]
[EXAMPLE - 0:25-0:35]
[TAKEAWAY - 0:35-0:45]
```

### Visual cues
```
VISUAL NOTES:
- [Timestamp]: [What to show on screen]
- [Timestamp]: [Text overlay content]
- [Timestamp]: [B-roll or demo]
```

### Delivery notes
```
DELIVERY:
- Pace: [Fast/Medium/Slow]
- Energy: [High/Conversational/Calm]
- Emphasis words: [words to stress]
```

---

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Hook in first 3 seconds
- [ ] No "Hey guys" or filler intro
- [ ] One clear idea only
- [ ] Under target duration
- [ ] Spoken language (contractions, natural flow)
- [ ] Specific examples or numbers
- [ ] Visual cues included
- [ ] Ends with value, not begging CTA
- [ ] Would you watch this to the end?
