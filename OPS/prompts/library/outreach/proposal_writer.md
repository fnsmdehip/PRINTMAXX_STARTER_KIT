# Proposal Writer

Write client proposals that close deals. Follows PRINTMAXX copy-style rules.

---

## System prompt

```
You are a proposal specialist for freelancers and solopreneurs. You write proposals that clearly communicate value, set expectations, and close deals.

Approach:
- Lead with their problem, not your services
- Be specific about deliverables and timelines
- Make pricing clear and justified
- Set boundaries professionally
- Make it easy to say yes

Rules:
- No fluff or filler
- No AI vocabulary
- No em dashes
- Specific over vague
- Professional but human tone
- Clear next steps
```

```markdown
# Website Redesign Proposal

**Prepared for:** Sarah Chen, DesignFlow
**Prepared by:** [Your name]
**Date:** [Date]

## The challenge

DesignFlow's current website converts visitors to trial signups at 1.8%. For a B2B SaaS in the project management space, industry benchmarks suggest 3-5% is achievable.

Based on our conversation and my review of your current site, the main issues are:

1. **Unclear value proposition.** The homepage doesn't communicate what DesignFlow does or who it's for within the first 5 seconds.

2. **Friction in the signup flow.** The trial signup requires 6 form fields. Industry best practice is 2-3 for initial conversion.

3. **Credibility gaps.** No social proof above the fold. Testimonials are buried on a separate page.

4. **Mobile experience.** 47% of your traffic is mobile, but the current design isn't optimized for smaller screens.

---

## The solution

I'll redesign your homepage, pricing page, and features page with a focus on conversion, not just aesthetics.

### What you'll get

**Homepage redesign**
- Clear hero section with value proposition
- Social proof (logos, testimonials) above the fold
- Streamlined signup flow (2 fields max)
- Mobile-first responsive design

**Pricing page redesign**
- Comparison table that guides decisions
- Clear differentiation between tiers
- FAQ section addressing common objections
- Optimized for conversion, not just information

**Features page redesign**
- Benefit-focused messaging (not feature lists)
- Visual demonstrations of key workflows
- Clear pathways to trial signup

### What's included

- Research and competitive analysis
- Wireframes for review and feedback
- High-fidelity designs in Figma
- Mobile and desktop versions
- Developer handoff documentation
- One round of revisions per page

### What's not included

- Development/coding (happy to recommend developers)
- Copywriting (I'll work with your existing copy or provide direction)
- Additional pages beyond the three specified
- Ongoing maintenance or updates

---

## Process and timeline

**Week 1: Research and strategy**
- Review analytics, competitor sites, and user feedback
- Identify quick wins and key opportunities
- Present findings and align on direction

**Week 2: Wireframes**
- Create low-fidelity wireframes for all three pages
- Review meeting to gather feedback
- Revise based on input

**Week 3: Design**
- High-fidelity designs for desktop and mobile
- Review meeting for feedback
- Revisions

**Week 4: Finalization**
- Final designs delivered
- Developer handoff documentation
- Implementation support (answering questions)

**Total: 4 weeks from project start**

This includes everything listed above: research, wireframes, design, revisions, and handoff documentation.

**Payment terms:**
- 50% ($3,250) due at project start
- 50% ($3,250) due on final delivery

**Additional work (if needed):**
- Extra revision rounds: $500/round
- Additional pages: $1,500/page
- Copywriting support: $800/page

## Why this investment makes sense

Your current site gets approximately 5,000 visitors per month.

At 1.8% conversion: 90 trial signups/month
At 3.5% conversion: 175 trial signups/month

That's 85 additional trial signups per month. If 20% convert to paid at your $50/month starting price, that's an additional $850/month in recurring revenue.

**Payback period: Less than 8 months**

And the improved conversion rate keeps working as your traffic grows.

---

## What makes this different from your previous redesign

You mentioned the last redesign didn't move metrics. Here's how this project is different:

1. **Research-driven, not aesthetic-driven.** Every design decision will be justified by data, user behavior, or proven patterns.

2. **Conversion-focused scope.** We're redesigning three pages that directly impact signups, not the entire site.

3. **Measurement built in.** I'll document what we changed and why so you can measure the impact clearly.

4. **Iterative process.** Multiple checkpoints to ensure we're aligned before moving forward.
```

## Variations

```
Include:
- Problem summary (2-3 sentences)
- What you'll deliver (bullet list)
- Timeline
- Price
- Next steps
```

```
Write a proposal for ongoing [SERVICE] retainer.

Include:
- What's included monthly
- What's not included
- How hours/work are tracked
- Communication expectations
- Minimum commitment
- How to pause or cancel

Focus on setting clear expectations for ongoing relationship.
```

```
Include:
- Reference to current relationship
- New scope
- How it fits with existing work
- Adjusted timeline/price

Keep it brief. They already trust you.
```

## Proposal sections checklist

**Must have:**
- [ ] Problem/challenge summary
- [ ] Proposed solution
- [ ] Deliverables (specific)
- [ ] Timeline
- [ ] Investment/pricing
- [ ] Next steps

**Should have:**
- [ ] What's not included (scope boundaries)
- [ ] Process overview
- [ ] ROI or value justification
- [ ] Payment terms
- [ ] About you (brief)

**Nice to have:**
- [ ] Testimonials or case studies
- [ ] FAQ or objection handling
- [ ] Risk reversal (guarantee, if offered)
- [ ] Multiple pricing options

---

## Pricing presentation options

**Single price:**
```
Investment: $X
```
Simple, clear, no decision fatigue.

**Tiered pricing:**
```
Option A (Basic): $X
Option B (Standard): $Y [Recommended]
Option C (Premium): $Z
```
Gives options, anchors middle tier.

**Itemized pricing:**
```
Research: $X
Design: $Y
Revisions: $Z
Total: $A
```
Shows where money goes, but invites line-item negotiation.

**Value-based pricing:**
```
If this generates Y additional revenue, your ROI is Z%.
Investment: $X
```
Frames price against outcome.
