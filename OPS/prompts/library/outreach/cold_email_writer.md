# Cold Email Writer

Write cold emails that get responses. Follows PRINTMAXX copy-style rules.

---

## System prompt

```
You are a cold email specialist for solopreneurs. You write emails that get opened, read, and replied to without being spammy or manipulative.

Approach:
- Research-based personalization (not fake flattery)
- Lead with value, not your pitch
- Short and scannable
- Clear but soft CTA
- One email does one thing

Rules:
- Under 100 words for initial outreach
- No em dashes
- No AI vocabulary
- No "I hope this email finds you well"
- No fake urgency
- No manipulation tactics
- Be honest about who you are and what you want
```

---

## User prompt template

```
Write a cold email for [PURPOSE].

About me:
- Who I am: [WHO_I_AM]
- What I offer: [OFFER]
- Why I'm reaching out: [REASON]

About the recipient:
- Who they are: [RECIPIENT]
- What they care about: [INTERESTS]
- Connection point: [CONNECTION - mutual contact, their content, their company]

Email details:
- Goal: [GOAL - meeting, response, intro]
- Tone: [TONE - professional, casual, friendly]
- CTA: [CTA - soft ask, hard ask]

Personalization info:
[INFO - specific details about this person to reference]
```

## Example output

**Input:**
```
Write a cold email for freelance web design services.

About me:
- Who I am: Freelance web designer specializing in SaaS landing pages
- What I offer: Landing page redesigns that improve conversion
- Why I'm reaching out: Their current landing page has obvious issues

About the recipient:
- Who they are: Founder of a B2B SaaS tool
- What they care about: Growing the business, improving conversion
- Connection point: Found them through their ProductHunt launch

Email details:
- Goal: Get a response to discuss potential project
- Tone: Professional but friendly
- CTA: Soft ask

Personalization info:
- Launched on ProductHunt 3 months ago
- Landing page has slow load time and unclear value prop
- They seem to be bootstrapped based on their Twitter
```

```
Two quick things I spotted:

I redesign SaaS landing pages for a living. Happy to share a few specific suggestions if you're open to it.

No pitch, just thoughts that might help.

[Your name]
```

## Variations

### Permission-based cold email
```
Write a permission-based cold email.

Instead of pitching directly, ask permission to share more.

Structure:
1. Connection point (why reaching out)
2. Value hint (what you could help with)
3. Permission ask ("Would you be open to...")

This reduces friction and respects their time.
```

### Value-first cold email
```
Write a cold email that leads with value.

Structure:
1. Share something useful (insight, resource, connection)
2. Brief context on why you're sharing
3. Soft ask or no ask at all

The goal is to start a conversation, not close a deal.
```

### Referral request cold email
```
Write a cold email asking for a referral.

Structure:
1. Brief intro and context
2. Specific ask (who you want to be connected to)
3. Why it's relevant to them
4. Make it easy to say yes or no

Be specific about who you want to meet and why.
```

### Follow-up cold email
```
Write a follow-up email to [ORIGINAL EMAIL].

Rules:
- Reference the original email
- Add new value or information
- Keep it shorter than the first
- Don't guilt trip ("just circling back...")
- Give an easy out
```

## Cold email frameworks

### AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)
```
Attention: Hook that stops them
Interest: Why this matters to them
Desire: What they could gain
Action: Clear next step
```

### PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solution)
```
Problem: Name the problem they have
Agitate: Show the cost of the problem
Solution: Hint at how you can help
```

### QVC (Question, Value, CTA)
```
Question: Ask something relevant
Value: Share something useful
CTA: Soft ask for response
```

### BAB (Before, After, Bridge)
```
Before: Their current situation
After: What it could look like
Bridge: How you help get there
```

## Subject line formulas

**Curiosity:**
- "Quick question about [their thing]"
- "Thought about [their company]"
- "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out"

**Value:**
- "Idea for [their problem]"
- "[Specific insight] for [company]"
- "Re: [something they posted/published]"

**Direct:**
- "[Thing] + [their company]"
- "For [Name]: [brief topic]"

**Avoid:**
- "Quick question" (overused)
- "Following up" (as first email)
- Anything clickbaity
- ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation

---

## Personalization levels

**Level 1: Basic (minimum viable)**
- Name
- Company name
- Generic role reference

**Level 2: Research-based (recommended)**
- Specific thing they published/built
- Recent news about their company
- Mutual connection or shared interest

**Level 3: Deep (high-value targets)**
- Reference specific challenges they've mentioned
- Connect their content to your offer
- Show genuine understanding of their situation

Rule: The higher the value of the contact, the deeper the personalization.

---

## Follow-up sequence

**Email 1 (Day 0):** Initial outreach
**Email 2 (Day 3-4):** Add new value, reference original
**Email 3 (Day 7-10):** Different angle or question
**Email 4 (Day 14):** Final follow-up, give easy out

Example Email 4:
```
Subject: Closing the loop

Hi [Name],

I sent a few emails about [topic] but haven't heard back. Totally understand if it's not a fit or not the right time.

I'll assume this isn't relevant and won't follow up again. But if things change, I'm always happy to chat about [topic].

Best,
[Name]
```
