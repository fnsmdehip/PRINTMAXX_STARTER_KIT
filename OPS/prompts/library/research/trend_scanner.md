# Trend Scanner

Find trending topics and capitalize on them. Outputs trend intelligence and action plans.

---

## System prompt

```
You are a trend analyst for solopreneur content and product opportunities. You identify emerging trends before they peak and help creators capitalize on them.

Approach:
- Separate signal from noise
- Focus on actionable trends (not just "AI is growing")
- Consider timing (early, peak, late)
- Assess solopreneur opportunity (can one person capitalize?)

Output format:
- Trend summary with evidence
- Timing assessment
- Opportunity analysis
- Action recommendations
- Risk factors
```

---

## User prompt template

```
Scan for trends in [NICHE/INDUSTRY].

Scan focus:
- Trend types: [TYPES - topics, tools, formats, platforms, behaviors]
- Timeframe: [TIMEFRAME - this week, this month, emerging]
- Opportunity type: [OPPORTUNITY - content, product, service, affiliate]

My context:
- Niche: [NICHE]
- Content focus: [FOCUS]
- Audience: [AUDIENCE]
- What I can create: [CAPABILITIES - blog, video, tool, course]

Data to analyze:
[DATA - any specific sources, posts, or trends you've noticed]

Questions:
1. What's trending that I should cover?
2. What's the timing (early, peak, or late)?
3. How should I approach it?
4. What's the risk if I pursue this?
```

---

## Example output

**Input:**
```
Scan for trends in the solopreneur/indie hacker space.

Scan focus:
- Trend types: topics and tools
- Timeframe: this month
- Opportunity type: content and affiliate

My context:
- Niche: Tools and productivity for solopreneurs
- Content focus: Twitter/X and blog
- Audience: Indie hackers building side projects
- What I can create: Blog posts, threads, tool reviews

Questions:
1. What's trending that I should cover?
2. What's the timing (early, peak, or late)?
3. How should I approach it?
4. What's the risk if I pursue this?
```

**Output:**

```
## Trend scan: Solopreneur/Indie hacker space

### Trend 1: AI-assisted coding tools

**Evidence:**
- Cursor (AI code editor) hit 100K users, constant Twitter discussion
- Copilot alternatives gaining traction (Codeium, Tabnine updates)
- "vibe coding" and "AI pair programming" phrases appearing frequently
- Multiple ProductHunt launches per week in this space

**Timing:** Early-to-peak. Growing fast but not saturated.

**Opportunity analysis:**
- Content: "Best AI coding tools for solopreneurs" comparison posts
- Affiliate: Cursor, Codeium, Replit have affiliate programs
- Thread: "I tried 5 AI coding tools for a week" thread could perform
- Risk: Everyone is covering this. Need unique angle.

**Recommended approach:**
Don't do a generic "top 10 AI coding tools" post. Instead:
- "I built a SaaS with only AI coding tools" (story angle)
- "AI coding tools for non-developers" (underserved audience)
- Specific workflow: "How I use Cursor + Claude for rapid prototyping"

**Risk factors:**
- High: Trend could plateau quickly
- Medium: Hard to differentiate from existing content
- Low: Tools might change rapidly, making content outdated

---

### Trend 2: One-person SaaS transparency

**Evidence:**
- Open startups movement growing (sharing revenue publicly)
- @levelsio, @marclouvion style transparency posts get high engagement
- "Building in public" hashtag still active
- ProductHunt category for open source analytics

**Timing:** Established but still effective. Not oversaturated.

**Opportunity analysis:**
- Content: Interview format posts with indie hackers
- Thread: "X lessons from building a $Y/month SaaS alone"
- Blog: Case studies of successful one-person SaaS
- No direct affiliate opportunity

**Recommended approach:**
- Curate: "How 10 one-person SaaSes hit $10K MRR" (data compilation)
- Template: "The exact tech stack for a one-person SaaS"
- Contrast: "What building alone looks like vs what people think"

**Risk factors:**
- Low: Evergreen interest, not a flash trend
- Medium: Requires research time to do well
- Low: Won't become outdated quickly

---

### Trend 3: Anti-complexity tools

**Evidence:**
- Tools like Cal.com, Linear, Dub.co gaining traction with "simple" positioning
- Backlash against feature-bloated software
- "Does one thing well" is becoming a selling point
- Rising interest in "boring technology" choices

**Timing:** Early. Sentiment is growing but not mainstream yet.

**Opportunity analysis:**
- Content: "Best simple alternatives to [complex tool]" series
- Angle: "Why I ditched Notion for Apple Notes"
- Tool reviews: Focus on simplicity as feature
- Potential product: Simple tool in a complex category

**Recommended approach:**
- Contrarian: "You don't need [popular complex tool]"
- Listicle: "10 simple tools that replaced my entire stack"
- Deep dive: "The hidden cost of software complexity"

**Risk factors:**
- Low: Aligns with solopreneur values
- Medium: Goes against some affiliate opportunities (complex tools pay more)
- Low: Not dependent on external factors

---

### Trend 4: Distribution > product

**Evidence:**
- @levelsio tweet about distribution being harder than building went viral
- Multiple threads on "build vs distribute" ratio
- "Ship fast, market slow" becoming common advice
- Content marketing and SEO discussions increasing in indie spaces

**Timing:** Peak awareness, but content still needed.

**Opportunity analysis:**
- Content: "How to distribute a side project" guides
- Thread: "I spent X hours building, Y hours marketing. Here's what I learned."
- Tool comparisons: Distribution tools (social schedulers, email tools)

**Recommended approach:**
- Tactical: "My exact distribution strategy for [specific type of product]"
- Numbers: "I posted every day for 30 days. Here's the data."
- Contrarian: "Why I don't do [common distribution tactic]"

**Risk factors:**
- Low: Evergreen topic
- Medium: Lots of generic content exists
- Low: Your audience actively needs this

---

### Trend 5: Niche micro-SaaS over broad tools

**Evidence:**
- Specific tools for specific workflows winning (Lemon Squeezy for creators, Gumroad for digital products)
- "SaaS for X" formula successful
- Broad tools getting criticism for feature bloat
- Indie hackers sharing success with hyper-niche products

**Timing:** Established trend, still has runway.

**Opportunity analysis:**
- Content: "Micro-SaaS ideas for [niche]" posts
- Case studies: Profile successful niche SaaS founders
- Product: Build content around your own niche tool idea

**Recommended approach:**
- Listicle: "30 micro-SaaS ideas no one is building"
- Interview: Profile 5 micro-SaaS founders and their niches
- Framework: "How to find a micro-SaaS niche"

**Risk factors:**
- Low: Proven formula
- Low: Evergreen interest
- Medium: Research-intensive to do well

---

### Emerging signals (too early to call)

**Voice/audio content for B2B:**
- Podcasts in niche B2B spaces growing
- Audio newsletters testing (Substack audio)
- Too early: Wait 2-3 months to see if sustains

**No-code AI automation:**
- Zapier AI, Make AI features
- Non-developer AI workflows
- Worth watching but not mature content yet

**Solo founder retreats/communities:**
- IRL meetups for indie hackers
- Paid communities becoming normalized
- Could be content angle if you attend one

---

### Priority recommendations

**This week:**
1. Create comparison content on AI coding tools (Trend 1)
   - Unique angle: For non-developers or specific use case

2. Post a "distribution strategy" thread (Trend 4)
   - Share your actual approach with numbers

**This month:**
3. Write "simple alternatives" blog post series (Trend 3)
   - Target 3-5 popular complex tools

4. Research and publish micro-SaaS case study (Trend 5)
   - Interview 1-2 founders for original content

**Monitor:**
- AI coding tool announcements
- New micro-SaaS launches on ProductHunt
- Community discussion on simplicity vs features

---

### Risk assessment summary

| Trend | Timing Risk | Competition Risk | Relevance Risk |
|-------|-------------|------------------|----------------|
| AI coding tools | Medium | High | Low |
| Transparency content | Low | Medium | Low |
| Anti-complexity | Low | Low | Low |
| Distribution focus | Low | Medium | Low |
| Micro-SaaS niche | Low | Low | Low |

**Best bet for you:** Trend 3 (Anti-complexity) and Trend 5 (Micro-SaaS niche). Lower competition, high relevance to your audience, and not timing-dependent.
```

## Variations

### Weekly content ideas
```
Scan trends for this week's content in [NICHE].

Find:
- What's being discussed right now?
- Any news or launches to comment on?
- Trending hashtags or conversations?
- Contrarian takes gaining traction?

Output: 5 content ideas with trend backing and timing
```

### Platform-specific trends
```
Scan trends on [PLATFORM] for [NICHE].

Analyze:
- What formats are performing?
- What topics are getting engagement?
- What creators are growing fast and why?
- Algorithm or feature changes to leverage?

Output: Platform-specific trend report and tactics
```

### Product opportunity trends
```
Scan for product opportunities in [MARKET].

Look for:
- Complaints about existing tools
- Workarounds people are doing manually
- Requests for features in communities
- Markets with old/stale solutions

Output: Product opportunity assessment with validation steps
```

## Data sources for trend scanning

**Real-time:**
- Twitter/X search and trending
- Reddit (r/programming, r/SideProject, etc.)
- Hacker News front page
- ProductHunt launches

**Weekly:**
- Newsletter roundups (TLDR, Morning Brew, etc.)
- YouTube trending in niche
- Google Trends
- Podcast episode topics

**Monthly:**
- Industry reports
- Tool release notes and changelogs
- Conference topics and speakers
- VC investment announcements
