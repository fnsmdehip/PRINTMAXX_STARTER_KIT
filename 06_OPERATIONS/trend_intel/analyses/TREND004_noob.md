## What They're Doing

Ryan Duncan runs "Ghost Operators," a meta business teaching people to become "Ghost Operators" - growth partners who scale creator-led Skool communities. Instead of building his own audience and monetizing them, he teaches operators to partner with existing YouTube creators who haven't monetized via community yet. The Ghost Operator handles all Skool setup, community management, gamification, funnel optimization, and retargeting ads while the creator focuses on content. Revenue split or retainer model.

This is a classic "picks and shovels" play during the Skool gold rush. While everyone else competes to build communities, Ghost Operators train the infrastructure layer.

## Replicable Frameworks

### 1. Meta Business Model
Don't compete in the creator economy. Sell tools/training to creators trying to win. Ryan doesn't build looksmaxxing or AI communities - he teaches people to build ANY Skool community.

### 2. 90-Day Money-Back Guarantee
"Get your first paying Creator Partner in 90 days or money back." This de-risks the offer and shows confidence. Works because the outreach templates and system are proven (he's won Skool Games 7x).

### 3. Partnership Positioning vs. Service Provider
"Ghost Operator" sounds like strategic partner. "Community Manager" sounds like employee. The language elevates the perception and justifies rev share vs. hourly wage.

### 4. Leveraging Skool Games Wins as Social Proof
7x Skool Games winner = instant credibility. He's not theorizing, he's won the actual competition multiple times. This is transferable: win competitions, tournaments, leaderboards in your niche → use as proof for teaching.

### 5. Templates + Systems Over Theory
Provides outreach scripts, contract templates, onboarding flows, gamification frameworks. Not just "here's what to do" but "copy these exact files."
