## 1. Keyword Research Fundamentals

### Search Intent Types

| Intent | Signal Words | Content Type | Example |
|--------|--------------|--------------|---------|
| Informational | how, what, why, guide, tutorial | Blog posts, guides | "how to start print on demand" |
| Commercial | best, review, vs, compare, top | Comparison posts, reviews | "printful vs printify" |
| Transactional | buy, price, coupon, deal, order | Product pages, pricing | "print on demand pricing" |
| Navigational | [brand name], login, app | Homepage, app pages | "printful login" |

**Intent determines content type.** Don't write a guide for a transactional keyword.

### Keyword Difficulty Assessment

**Manual Difficulty Check:**
1. Google the keyword
2. Count how many:
   - Big brands in top 10 (Amazon, Wikipedia = hard)
   - Small sites/blogs (opportunity)
   - Forums/Reddit (very easy)
3. Check DA/DR of top results (if using tools)

**Difficulty Tiers:**

| KD Score | Difficulty | Strategy |
|----------|------------|----------|
| 0-20 | Easy | Target immediately, quick wins |
| 20-40 | Medium | Good for growing sites, quality content wins |
| 40-60 | Hard | Need strong site, excellent content, some backlinks |
| 60+ | Very Hard | Major authority required, consider alternatives |

**Rule:** New sites should target KD < 30. Established sites can go higher.

### Search Volume Evaluation

**Volume isn't everything.** A keyword with 100 searches but high intent beats 10,000 searches with low intent.

**Better metric: Traffic potential**
A keyword with 500 searches/month might bring 2,000 visitors if you also rank for related terms.

**Volume benchmarks:**

| Volume | Classification | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| 0-100 | Very low | Good for highly specific niches |
| 100-500 | Low | Sweet spot for new sites |
| 500-2,000 | Medium | Target after some authority |
| 2,000-10,000 | High | Competitive, needs strategy |
| 10,000+ | Very high | Usually dominated by brands |

**Rule:** New sites should target 100-500 volume keywords with KD under 30.

### Competition Analysis

**Quick competitive analysis:**
1. Google the keyword
2. Open top 5 results
3. Note: Word count, content depth, backlinks
4. Ask: Can I make something 10x better?

**Competitive signals:**

| Signal | Good Sign | Bad Sign |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Top results | Thin content, old dates | In-depth guides, recent updates |
| Domains | Forums, small blogs | Amazon, Wikipedia, major brands |
| Content type | Text-only, no images | Rich media, interactive tools |
| SERP features | Few featured snippets | Dominated by featured snippets |

## 2. Free Tools

### Google Keyword Planner

**URL:** ads.google.com/keyword-planner

**What it does:** Shows search volume ranges and keyword ideas from Google's own data.

**How to use it:**
1. Create free Google Ads account (no need to run ads)
2. Go to Tools > Keyword Planner
3. Choose "Discover new keywords"
4. Enter seed keywords
5. Filter by location, language

**Limitations:**
- Shows ranges (100-1K) not exact volumes unless you spend on ads
- Focuses on commercial keywords
- Misses many long-tail variations

**Best for:** Initial keyword discovery, validating search volume exists.

### Google Trends

**URL:** trends.google.com

**What it does:** Shows relative search interest over time.

**How to use it:**
1. Enter keyword
2. Set timeframe (12 months for trends, 5 years for seasonality)
3. Compare up to 5 terms
4. Check "Related queries" for ideas

**Power moves:**
- Compare "print on demand" vs "dropshipping" to see which is growing
- Check seasonality (gift-related keywords spike Q4)
- Find rising queries (keywords growing fast)

**Best for:** Trend validation, seasonal planning, comparing topics.

### Google Search Console

**URL:** search.google.com/search-console

**What it does:** Shows what keywords your site already ranks for.

**How to use it:**
1. Verify your site ownership
2. Go to Performance > Search results
3. Click "Queries" tab
4. Sort by impressions or clicks

**Power moves:**
- Find keywords you rank #11-20 for (easy wins with optimization)
- Identify pages cannibalizing each other (same keyword, multiple pages)
- Track position changes over time

**Best for:** Finding low-hanging fruit on existing sites.

### AnswerThePublic

**URL:** answerthepublic.com

**What it does:** Shows questions people ask about a topic.

**How to use it:**
1. Enter seed keyword
2. Get questions organized by who/what/when/where/why/how
3. Export to CSV

**Limitations:**
- 3 free searches per day
- No search volume data
- Can be overwhelming

**Best for:** Finding informational content ideas, FAQ sections.

### Ubersuggest (Free Tier)

**URL:** neilpatel.com/ubersuggest

**Free tier limits:**
- 3 searches per day
- Limited historical data
- Reduced export options

**Best for:** Quick keyword validation when you've used other free searches.

### Additional Free Tools

| Tool | URL | Best For |
|------|-----|----------|
| Keyword Surfer | Chrome extension | Volume data in Google search |
| Keywords Everywhere | Chrome extension | Related keywords |
| AlsoAsked | alsoasked.com | People Also Ask questions |
| Soovle | soovle.com | Autocomplete from multiple engines |

## 4. Research Process

### Step 1: Seed Keyword Generation

Start with 10-20 seed keywords. These are broad terms that define your niche.

**Methods to find seeds:**

1. **Brainstorm your topics**
   - What do you sell/teach?
   - What problems do you solve?
   - What does your audience search for?

2. **Mine your competitors**
   - List 5 competitors
   - Check their page titles, H1s, URLs
   - Note recurring terms

3. **Check existing content**
   - Google Search Console queries
   - Analytics landing pages
   - Blog post topics

4. **Use Google autocomplete**
   - Type each term, note suggestions
   - Add letters (a, b, c...) for more

**Example seed list for print business:**
- print on demand
- custom merchandise
- t-shirt business
- merch design
- dropshipping products
- print fulfillment
- custom apparel
- branded merchandise

### Step 2: Competitor Keyword Analysis

**Process:**
1. Identify 3-5 direct competitors
2. Run their domains through Ahrefs/SEMrush/Ubersuggest
3. Export their top organic keywords
4. Filter for:
   - Volume > 100
   - Position 1-20 (they're ranking)
   - Relevance to your business

**What to look for:**
- Keywords you should target but aren't
- Content types that rank
- Topics with multiple competitor pages (validated demand)

### Step 3: Long-tail Expansion

**Expansion methods:**

| Method | How To | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Add modifiers | seed + year/location/type | "print on demand 2024" |
| Question format | how/what/why + seed | "how to start print on demand" |
| Comparison | seed + vs + alternative | "printful vs printify" |
| For whom | seed + for + audience | "print on demand for artists" |
| Problem-based | seed + problem/issue | "print on demand low quality" |

**Tools for expansion:**
- AnswerThePublic (questions)
- AlsoAsked (People Also Ask)
- Google autocomplete
- Related searches (bottom of Google SERP)

### Step 4: Content Gap Analysis

Find keywords competitors rank for that you don't.

**Process:**
1. Export your current rankings
2. Export competitor rankings
3. Compare lists
4. Identify gaps where:
   - Competitor ranks top 10
   - You don't rank at all or rank 20+
   - Keyword is relevant to your business

## 5. Keyword Organization

### Clustering Strategy

Group related keywords together. One page targets one cluster, not one keyword.

**Cluster structure:**
```
Primary keyword: "print on demand business"
- Variations: "pod business", "print on demand company"
- Long-tail: "how to start print on demand business"
- Questions: "is print on demand profitable"
- Related: "best print on demand sites", "print on demand costs"
```

**Clustering rules:**
- Same search intent = same cluster
- Check if Google shows same results for keywords
- One page per cluster
- 5-20 keywords per cluster typical

### Priority Scoring

**Scoring formula:**
```
Priority Score = (Volume Score x Intent Score x Competition Score) / Effort Score
```

**Scoring criteria:**

| Factor | 1 Point | 2 Points | 3 Points |
|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| Volume | <100 | 100-1000 | 1000+ |
| Intent | Informational | Commercial | Transactional |
| Competition | KD 40+ | KD 20-40 | KD <20 |
| Effort | New content needed | Update existing | Quick optimize |

Higher score = higher priority.

### Content Mapping

**Content mapping template:**

| Cluster | Primary Keyword | Content Type | URL | Status |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|-----|--------|
| POD basics | how to start print on demand | Guide | /guides/start-print-on-demand | Draft |
| POD comparison | printful vs printify | Comparison | /compare/printful-vs-printify | Planned |

**Content type mapping:**

| Intent | Best Content Types |
|--------|-------------------|
| Informational | Guides, tutorials, explainers |
| Commercial | Comparisons, reviews, "best of" lists |
| Transactional | Product pages, pricing pages |
| Navigational | Homepage, feature pages |

## Quick Reference

### Keyword Research Checklist

- [ ] Generate 10-20 seed keywords
- [ ] Run seeds through free tools
- [ ] Analyze 3-5 competitors
- [ ] Expand with long-tail variations
- [ ] Check search intent for each keyword
- [ ] Assess difficulty (aim for KD < 30 for new sites)
- [ ] Group into clusters
- [ ] Score and prioritize
- [ ] Map to content types
- [ ] Set up tracking

### When to Move to Paid Tools

| Situation | Stick with Free | Consider Paid |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Monthly content | < 4 pieces | 8+ pieces |
| Site traffic | < 1,000/month | 5,000+/month |
| Revenue from SEO | < $500/month | $1,000+/month |
| Time spent on research | < 4 hours/month | 10+ hours/month |

### Red Flags (Keywords to Avoid)

- KD > 50 with site DR < 30
- Dominated by Wikipedia, Amazon, or major news sites
- No indie sites in top 20
- Search intent doesn't match your content type
- Volume declining in Google Trends
- YMYL topics without E-E-A-T
