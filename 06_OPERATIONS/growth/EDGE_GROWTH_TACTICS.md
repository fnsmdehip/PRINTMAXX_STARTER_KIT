### LinkedIn Algorithm (2026)

**Ranking factors by weight:**
1. Comments (4x weight vs likes)
2. Dwell time (time spent reading)
3. Saves/bookmarks (high signal)
4. Shares (7x weight vs likes)
5. Profile clicks from post

**What kills posts:**
- External links in post body (instant nerf, put in first comment)
- Editing within first hour (resets algorithm)
- Hashtag stuffing (5+ hashtags)
- Posting > 2x/day
- Engagement from same accounts always (pod detection)

### Account Warming Protocol

**Week 1-2:** Manual only (10-20 connections/day, engage 10+ posts/day)
**Week 3-4:** Light scheduling (20-30 connections/day, start posting)
**Week 5+:** Cloud automation at 50% limits, ramp gradually over 2 weeks

## SECTION 7: Bought Engagement Legal Analysis

### When It's Legal

| Scenario | Legal Status | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|
| Personal account, no marketing | ✅ LEGAL | No commercial misrepresentation |
| Trigger algorithm, don't claim numbers | ⚠️ GREY | No false claims made |
| Internal testing/benchmarking | ✅ LEGAL | Not consumer-facing |

### When It's Illegal

| Scenario | Legal Status | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|
| Claim followers to get sponsorships | ❌ ILLEGAL | Fraud/misrepresentation |
| Fake reviews for your product | ❌ ILLEGAL | FTC banned Aug 2024 |
| Screenshot fake metrics for marketing | ❌ ILLEGAL | Deceptive advertising |
| Use bots with stolen identities | ❌ ILLEGAL | Identity theft |

### FTC Penalties

- Up to $53,088 per violation (2025 rate)
- Each post can be separate violation
- Brands AND influencers liable

### The Safe Approach

If you use any engagement boosting:
1. Never use inflated numbers in pitches
2. Never screenshot fake metrics for marketing
3. Use it to trigger algorithms, not claim influence
4. Keep it off the books for any marketing claims
5. Prioritize methods that convert to real engagement
