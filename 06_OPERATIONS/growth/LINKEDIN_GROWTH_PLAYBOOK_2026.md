## ALGORITHM BREAKDOWN

### CRITICAL: 2026 Algorithm Overhaul (February 2026 Update)

**LinkedIn organic reach dropped 50% vs 2024. Engagement down 25%. Follower growth down 59%.** This is the biggest shift since LinkedIn entered the creator economy in 2023. Everything below reflects the NEW reality.

**The headline change: "Depth Score"** - LinkedIn's new ranking signal measures how long people actually engage with your content, not just whether they clicked or tapped. Reading time, comment depth, saves, and private shares all factor in. The algorithm now distinguishes between someone who stopped scrolling briefly and someone who actually read and found it useful.

**Why LinkedIn introduced Depth Score:** Internal data showed 60% of high-engagement posts in Q3 2025 used optimization tactics, but user satisfaction was declining. Engagement bait worked mechanically but users hated the experience.

**Key implication:** A well-informed post from someone with real expertise now outperforms a perfectly designed carousel from a social media manager who doesn't understand the topic deeply. The "Skills-First" ranking system means your follower count matters less than your verified expertise.

### How the LinkedIn Algorithm Works (2026)

LinkedIn's algorithm evaluates content through four stages:

**Stage 1: Quality Filter (0-60 minutes)**
- Spam detection
- Original content check
- Grammar/formatting scan
- External link detection (penalized ~60% reach reduction)
- AI-generated content detection (47% less organic reach if detected)
- Engagement bait pattern detection (auto-suppressed)
- "Skills-First" relevance check: does your profile expertise match your content topic?

**Stage 2: Initial Test (1-4 hours) - THE GOLDEN HOUR**
- Shown to small segment (~500-1000 of your connections)
- **First 90 minutes determine 70% of your total reach**
- Measures early engagement velocity
- Key metrics: dwell time, reactions, comments, saves, shares
- Responding to comments within 15 minutes = 90% algorithmic boost

**Stage 3: Depth Score Evaluation (4-24 hours)**
- Comment weight: multi-sentence comments are HIGHEST value
- Shares with added context: second highest
- Saves/bookmarks: strong intent to return (huge dwell time signal)
- Comment threads: extended discussion = massive boost
- Simple reactions and generic comments: LOWEST value
- Profile consistency check: your activity must match your bio/headline expertise

**Stage 4: Network Expansion (24+ hours)**
- High-performing posts shown to 2nd and 3rd degree connections
- Personal profiles get 13x more reach than company pages
- Employee reshares reach 561% further than company page posts
- Can resurface for days/weeks if comments continue

### Algorithm Ranking Factors (2026 Updated)

| Factor | Weight | Change vs 2025 | Optimization |
|--------|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| Depth Score (dwell time + read time) | HIGHEST | **NEW** | Long-form, carousels, stories that hold attention |
| Shares with thoughts added | HIGHEST | UP | Make content share-worthy, add unique perspective |
| Comments (5+ words, substantive) | HIGHEST | Same | Ask questions, prompt discussion, respond fast |
| Saves/bookmarks | HIGH | UP | Create reference content, templates, frameworks |
| Private DM shares ("dark social") | HIGH | **NEW** | Content worth sharing 1-on-1 = quality signal |
| Comment threads (extended discussion) | HIGH | **NEW** | Respond to every comment, ask follow-ups |
| Reactions (all types) | LOW | DOWN | Lowest value engagement signal now |
| Profile consistency (expertise match) | MEDIUM | **NEW** | Content must match profile headline/skills |
| External links | NEGATIVE (-60%) | WORSE | Never in main post. First comment only. |
| AI-generated patterns | NEGATIVE (-47%) | **NEW** | Algorithm trained to detect and suppress AI content |

### What Kills Posts (2026 Updated)

- External links in the post body (60% reach reduction, up from ~30-40%)
- AI-generated content with recognizable patterns (47% less reach)
- Editing within first hour (resets algorithm)
- Pods with obvious engagement patterns (ML detection improved)
- Hashtag stuffing (5+ hashtags)
- Engagement bait ("Agree?" "Like if you..." "Type YES")
- Low-quality images/formatting
- Posting frequency > 2/day
- Engagement from same accounts always
- Profile expertise mismatch (fintech headline but motivational content)
- Tagging more than 5 people per post
- Excessive emojis and over-formatting

### Algorithm-Friendly Patterns (2026 Updated)

**Post structure that works:**
```
[Hook - first 2-3 lines visible before "see more"]

[White space]

[Story/content with clear formatting - aim for 1,000-1,300 characters]

[Clear CTA or question to drive comments]

[3-5 relevant hashtags at END of post]
```

**Golden Hour tactics (first 90 minutes are everything):**
- Respond to EVERY comment within 15 minutes (90% algorithmic boost)
- Ask follow-up questions to extend comment threads
- Have 3-5 people ready to engage genuinely in first 10 minutes
- Never post and walk away

**Reshare/re-engagement hack:**
- Comment on or reshare your own post after 8 hours (pushes back into feeds)
- Do it again at 24 hours if engagement is strong
- Alternate between formats to prevent audience fatigue

**Timing optimization (2026 data-backed):**
- **Best times:** 8-11am and 12-1pm (recipient's local time zone)
- **Best days:** Tuesday (top performer), Wednesday, Thursday
- **Worst days:** Weekends (minimal engagement), Monday (catch-up mode)
- **B2B Marketing peak:** 7-9am (morning scroll before work)
- **Tech/SaaS peak:** 12-1pm (lunch break browsing)
- Buffer data: weekday morning posts earn nearly 2x engagement vs off-hours
- Early momentum now influences reach for 48-72 hours (up from 24h)
- Post when target audience is active, not when you're free

**Posting cadence (2026 best practice):**
- 3-4 posts per week beats sporadic bursts followed by silence
- Quality and consistency beat raw frequency
- Mix: anchor content (carousels/video) 2-3x/week + engagement drivers (polls) 1-2x/week + quick-value text posts 1-2x/week
- 1x/day for aggressive growth, but only if quality stays high

## ENGAGEMENT TACTICS

### The Comment Strategy

**Commenting is the highest ROI activity on LinkedIn.**

**Why:**
- Your comment appears on their followers' feeds
- Builds relationship with poster
- Shows your expertise publicly
- No algorithm penalty, only upside

**How to comment effectively:**

**Pattern: Add Value**
```
[Agree/appreciate] + [New insight they didn't mention] + [Question or experience]
```
Example: "This is spot on. I'd add that timing matters too - we saw 40% higher reply rates sending at 9am vs 2pm. What time works best for your audience?"

**Pattern: Challenge Respectfully**
```
[Acknowledge their point] + [Different perspective] + [Your experience/data]
```
Example: "Interesting take. I've found the opposite - shorter emails (under 75 words) consistently outperform. Might depend on the industry though. What's your typical ICP?"

**Avoid:**
- "Great post!" (zero value)
- "Love this!" (generic)
- Long rambling comments
- Self-promotion without relevance
- Arguing/being negative

### Target Accounts for Engagement

**Find your target creators:**
1. Identify 20-30 accounts in your space
2. Turn on notifications for their posts
3. Comment within first 30 minutes (critical)
4. Be consistently helpful

**Good targets:**
- Industry thought leaders with 10K-100K followers
- Customers and prospects who post
- Complementary service providers
- Media/journalist accounts in your space

### Pod Alternatives (Legal)

**Instead of formal pods:**
1. **Engagement buddy system** - 3-5 people who support each other naturally
2. **Community engagement** - Slack/Discord groups where you share posts
3. **Team engagement** - Colleagues engage with each other's content
4. **Cross-platform** - Share LinkedIn posts to email list, Twitter

**Rules:**
- Don't engage within the same minute every time
- Vary your reactions (like, celebrate, insightful)
- Write real comments, not templates
- Don't engage with everything (looks obvious)

### DM Engagement

**Use DMs to deepen relationships, not pitch:**

**After someone comments:**
```
Hey [name], thanks for that comment on my post about [topic].

[Reference their specific point] - that's a really good add.

Are you also working on [related topic]?
```

**After connecting:**
```
Hey [name], thanks for connecting.

Noticed you're working on [their thing] - how's that going?

I'm building [your thing] so our worlds probably overlap.
```

## OUTBOUND STRATEGIES

### Connection Request Philosophy

**The debate: Note vs No-Note**

| Approach | Accept Rate | Best When |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| No note | 35-40% | Large volume, weak connection |
| Short note | 40-50% | Some context, mutual connection |
| Personalized note | 50-70% | High-value prospect, clear relevance |

**The answer:** Personalized for high-value, no-note for volume.

### Connection Request Templates (Under 300 chars)

See `MONEY_METHODS/COLD_OUTBOUND/LINKEDIN_TEMPLATES.md` for full templates.

**Quick reference:**

**Trigger-based:**
```
{{firstName}}, saw {{company}} just {{trigger}}. We help companies at that stage with {{problem}}. Worth connecting?
```

**Mutual connection:**
```
{{firstName}}, noticed we're both connected to {{mutual}}. Working in similar spaces. Let's connect.
```

**Content-based:**
```
{{firstName}}, your post on {{topic}} was solid. Building similar stuff. Would love to connect.
```

**Direct:**
```
{{firstName}}, I help {{their role}} at {{company type}} with {{outcome}}. Thought there might be a fit. Connect?
```

### Follow-Up Sequence Structure

**Message 1: Thank + Question (24-48hr after accept)**
- Thank for connecting
- Reference something specific
- Ask an open question about their work

**Message 2: Value Add (3-5 days later)**
- Share relevant resource
- Insight about their industry
- No pitch, just value

**Message 3: Soft Pitch (7-10 days later)**
- Direct but permission-based
- Reference previous value
- Clear CTA (call, demo, chat)

**Message 4: Breakup (14 days later, if no response)**
- Acknowledge no response
- Leave door open
- Final value offer

### Voice Notes (3x Response Rate)

**Why they work:**
- Pattern interrupt in DM inbox
- Shows effort and personality
- Hard to ignore
- Builds trust faster

**Script structure:**
```
Hey [name], quick voice note for you.

[Personalized observation - 10 seconds]

[What you do - 10 seconds]

[Clear ask - 10 seconds]

Let me know.
```

**Tips:**
- Keep under 60 seconds
- Speak naturally, not scripted
- Record in quiet environment
- Don't ramble

### InMail Strategy (Sales Navigator)

**When to use InMail:**
- Can't reach via connection request
- High-value prospect
- Clear, compelling reason to reach out

**InMail structure:**
```
Subject: [Specific, curiosity-driving]

Hi [name],

[Hook - why reaching out NOW]

[1-2 sentences on problem/solution]

[Specific proof point]

[Clear CTA - question or meeting]

[Signature]
```

**Avoid:**
- Long paragraphs
- Generic subjects
- Pitching without context
- Multiple CTAs

## SALES NAVIGATOR

### Search Filters That Matter

**Boolean Search Syntax:**
- `AND` - Both terms required
- `OR` - Either term
- `NOT` - Exclude term
- `""` - Exact phrase
- `()` - Grouping

**Example searches:**

Finding SaaS founders:
```
("founder" OR "co-founder" OR "CEO") AND ("SaaS" OR "B2B" OR "software")
```

Finding sales leaders:
```
("VP Sales" OR "Head of Sales" OR "Sales Director") AND NOT "recruiter"
```

Finding by company stage:
```
("Series A" OR "seed" OR "raised") AND ("SaaS" OR "tech startup")
```

### Lead List Building

**Process:**
1. Save search with specific filters
2. Review weekly for new leads
3. Export for enrichment (Apollo, Clearbit)
4. Segment by priority
5. Create personalized sequences per segment

**Good filters:**
- Company headcount: 11-50, 51-200
- Geography: Target markets
- Job function: Your buyers
- Posted on LinkedIn: Recent (active users)
- Company growth: Hiring, fundraising

### Account Tracking

**Set up alerts for:**
- Target accounts posting content
- Key contacts job changes
- Company news and updates
- Decision makers joining/leaving

**Weekly review:**
1. Check saved account alerts
2. Engage with their content
3. Note trigger events
4. Update outreach priority

|--------|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|

### What Gets You Banned

1. **Chrome extensions** - All detectable. Instant ban risk.
2. **Mass automation** - Volume + speed + patterns
3. **Connection request spam** - Low accept rate triggers review
4. **Fake accounts** - Instant ban if detected
5. **Inconsistent activity** - 0 for weeks then 500 actions in a day
6. **Multiple accounts same device** - Fingerprint correlation

### Account Warming Protocol

**Week 1-2:**
- Manual only
- 10-20 profile views/day
- 5-10 engagement actions/day
- 10-15 connection requests/day (personalized)
- Complete profile 100%

**Week 3-4:**
- Increase to 20-30 connections/day
- Start posting content (2-3/week)
- Begin light scheduling (Buffer, Hootsuite)
- NO automation tools yet

**Week 5+:**
- Can introduce cloud automation (Expandi, Dripify)
- Start slow: 50% of normal limits
- Monitor for warnings
- Ramp gradually over 2-3 weeks

### Expandi Setup Guide

**Configuration:**
1. Cloud-based (not browser extension)
2. Dedicated IP (residential proxy if needed)
3. Smart limits enabled
4. Human-like delays (random intervals)
5. Working hours only (9am-6pm target timezone)

**Safe sequences:**
```
Day 0: View profile
Day 1: Connection request
Day 3: First message (if accepted)
Day 7: Follow-up (if no response)
Day 14: Final follow-up
Day 21: Withdraw connection request (if not accepted)
```

### If Restricted

**Warning signs:**
- "You've reached the limit" message
- Unusually low accept rates
- Connection requests failing silently
- Profile showing as restricted

**Recovery protocol:**
1. Stop ALL automation immediately
2. Wait 7 days minimum
3. Manual activity only for 2 weeks
4. Resume at 50% previous volume
5. If persists, contact LinkedIn support
