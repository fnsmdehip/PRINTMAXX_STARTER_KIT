## 1. Algorithm Mechanics (Current State)

### How Instagram Uses Multiple Algorithms

Instagram doesn't have one algorithm. It has separate ranking systems for:
- **Feed** - Posts from accounts you follow + recommended content
- **Stories** - Prioritized by relationship and recency
- **Reels** - Discovery-focused, designed to surface content from accounts you don't follow
- **Explore** - Recommendation engine for new content/accounts

### Feed Ranking Factors

The Feed algorithm ranks posts based on:

1. **Relationship** (strongest signal)
   - How often you interact with this account
   - DM history
   - Whether you're tagged in each other's posts
   - Search history for their profile

2. **Interest**
   - Past engagement with similar content types
   - Time spent on similar posts
   - Content category (fitness, food, tech, etc.)

3. **Timeliness**
   - Recency of post
   - Your activity patterns (when you're most active)

4. **Frequency of Use**
   - How often you open Instagram affects what you see
   - More frequent = more chronological
   - Less frequent = more "best of" curated

### Reels Distribution System

Reels have a different distribution model optimized for discovery:

**Phase 1: Initial Test (0-30 min)**
- Shown to small % of your followers
- Measured: Watch time, shares, saves

**Phase 2: Expand or Die (30 min - 2 hours)**
- If signals positive, pushed to non-followers via Explore/Reels tab
- If signals weak, distribution stops

**Phase 3: Viral Scaling (2+ hours)**
- Strong performers get pushed to broader audiences
- Can continue for days/weeks if engagement sustains

**Key Reels Signals (ranked by importance):**
1. Watch time / completion rate
2. Shares (to DMs, Stories)
3. Saves
4. Likes
5. Comments
6. Profile visits after viewing

### Stories Algorithm

Stories prioritize:
1. **Recency** - Newer stories shown first
2. **Engagement history** - Accounts you interact with most
3. **Closeness** - DMs, tags, profile views
4. **Story completion** - If you watch their full stories

**Stories don't affect Feed ranking directly** but build relationship signals.

### Reach Factors (What Hurts/Helps)

**Positive signals:**
- High save rate
- Shares to DMs
- Long watch time on video
- Comments with multiple words
- Profile visits from content
- Follows from content

**Negative signals:**
- "Not interested" reports
- Account mutes
- Unfollows shortly after following
- Low engagement rate relative to followers
- Rapid unnatural growth patterns

## 2. Content Format Performance

### Format Comparison (2025 Data)

| Format | Reach | Engagement | Best For |
|--------|-------|------------|----------|
| Reels | Highest | Moderate | Discovery, new followers |
| Carousels | High | Highest | Saves, education, existing audience |
| Single Image | Lowest | Low-Moderate | Brand moments, announcements |
| Stories | Followers only | High (intimate) | Relationship building, polls |

### Reels Performance

**Pros:**
- 2-3x more reach than static posts
- Pushed to non-followers
- Best for account growth

**Cons:**
- Lower engagement rate (more views = diluted %)
- Followers may miss them (algorithm dependent)
- Higher production effort

**Best for:** Growth, discovery, going viral

### Carousels Performance

**Pros:**
- Highest engagement rate of any format
- Highest save rate (educational content)
- Multiple impressions (each slide = new impression)
- Strong for building authority

**Cons:**
- Limited reach compared to Reels
- Primarily shown to existing followers

**Best for:** Education, how-tos, lists, before/after

### When to Use Each Format

| Goal | Format |
|------|--------|
| Grow followers | Reels |
| Educate existing audience | Carousels |
| Build relationships | Stories |
| Drive saves | Carousels |
| Go viral | Reels |
| Quick announcement | Single image |
| Behind-the-scenes | Stories |

### Carousel Engagement Tactics

1. **Hook on slide 1** - Make them swipe
2. **Cliffhanger transitions** - "But here's the problem..." (swipe)
3. **10 slides optimal** - More slides = more time spent
4. **CTA on last slide** - Save, share, follow
5. **Numbered lists work** - "5 things that..." forces completion
6. **Text-heavy performs** - Educational carousels outperform image dumps
7. **Consistent template** - Brand recognition, faster creation

## 3. Optimal Specs

### Reels Length Sweet Spots

| Length | Performance | Use Case |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| 7-15 sec | Highest completion rate | Trends, quick tips, hooks |
| 15-30 sec | Strong balance | Tutorials, stories |
| 30-60 sec | Good if engaging | Educational, narrative |
| 60-90 sec | Lower completion | Only if content demands |
| 90+ sec | Poor | Avoid unless highly engaged audience |

**Key insight:** Completion rate matters more than length. A 7-second video watched 3x beats a 60-second video watched 50%.

### Carousel Slide Counts

| Slides | Performance |
|--------|-------------|
| 2-3 | Low (not worth carousel format) |
| 4-6 | Moderate |
| 7-10 | Optimal - highest engagement |
| 10 (max) | Best - use all slides |

**Rule:** If you can't fill 7+ slides with value, make it a Reel instead.

### Caption Length Optimization

| Length | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| 1-50 chars | Quick CTAs, single thoughts |
| 50-150 chars | Most posts, punchy value |
| 150-300 chars | Context + CTA |
| 300-500 chars | Storytelling, educational |
| 500-2200 chars | Long-form (use for SEO, searchability) |

**Caption structure that works:**
1. Hook (first line visible)
2. Value/story
3. CTA
4. Hashtags (or in comments)

**First line is everything.** It's what shows in feed before "...more"

### Hashtag Strategy (2025 State)

**What changed:**
- Hashtags now more for SEO/searchability than distribution
- Algorithm uses image/video recognition more than hashtags
- Over-hashtagging can look spammy

**Current best practice:**
- **3-5 relevant hashtags** (not 30)
- Mix of: 1 broad, 2-3 niche, 1 branded
- Place in caption (not comments - no difference now)
- Use hashtags users would actually search

## 4. Growth Tactics

### Posting Frequency

| Frequency | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| 1x/week | Minimal growth, audience forgets you |
| 3x/week | Baseline for growth |
| 5x/week | Strong growth, sustainable |
| 1x/day | Optimal for aggressive growth |
| 2x+/day | Diminishing returns, audience fatigue |

**Recommended mix (1x/day):**
- 3-4 Reels/week
- 2-3 Carousels/week
- Daily Stories
- 1-2 single images/week (optional)

### Best Times to Post (2025)

**General best times:**
- **Weekdays:** 6-9 AM, 12-2 PM, 7-9 PM (local time)
- **Weekends:** 9-11 AM, 7-9 PM
- **Peak day:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday

**BUT: Your audience data > general data**

Check Instagram Insights > Your Audience > Most Active Times

**Posting strategy:**
1. Post 30 min before peak time
2. Engage heavily for 30 min after posting
3. Reply to all comments within first hour

### Story Engagement Tactics

Stories build relationship signals. Use them for:

1. **Polls** - Easy engagement, provides data
2. **Questions** - DM conversations = algorithm boost
3. **Quizzes** - Gamification, completion rates
4. **Sliders** - Low-friction engagement
5. **Add Yours** - Trend participation

**Story hacks:**
- Post 3-7 Stories/day (not 20)
- Use Close Friends for exclusivity
- Story replies lead to DM which builds relationship signal
- Link stickers (drive traffic)
- Post Stories when you post Feed (double exposure)

### DM Automation (Safe Methods)

**What's safe:**
- ManyChat for automated responses to specific keywords
- Auto-replies to Story mentions
- Broadcast Channels (one-to-many)

**What's risky:**
- Mass DMing non-followers
- DM pods/groups
- Third-party tools that automate outreach

**Safe DM strategy:**
1. CTA in post: "DM me 'GUIDE' for the free template"
2. ManyChat auto-sends link when keyword received
3. Starts conversation, builds relationship signal

### Collaboration Strategies

**Collab posts:**
- Use Instagram's Collab feature (post shows on both profiles)
- Both audiences see it
- Engagement aggregated
- Best way to cross-pollinate audiences

**Collab criteria:**
- Similar audience size (0.5x-2x your size)
- Same niche or adjacent
- Complementary, not competitive

## 5. Reels Specific Tactics

### Hook Patterns (First 1-3 Seconds)

The hook determines watch time. Patterns that work:

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Controversy | "Stop doing this immediately..." |
| Curiosity gap | "The thing nobody tells you about..." |
| Bold claim | "I made $10K in 30 days with this" |
| Direct address | "If you're struggling with X, watch this" |
| Pattern interrupt | Unexpected visual/sound |
| List promise | "3 things that changed my..." |

**Hook rules:**
- No logos/intros - start with value
- Face on screen in first frame (human connection)
- Text overlay stating promise
- Movement in first second

### Trending Audio Strategy

**Finding trending audio:**
1. Scroll Reels tab - note repeated sounds
2. Look for upward arrow on audio name (trending indicator)
3. Check audio page - if under 10K uses but growing = optimal
4. Use Instagram's audio suggestions when creating

**Audio strategy:**
- Original audio has longer shelf life, can become your trending sound
- Trending audio gives quick boost, short window
- Mix both: 60% original, 40% trending

### Cover Image Importance

Cover image affects:
- Click-through from profile grid
- Saves (people save pretty covers)
- Brand consistency

**Cover best practices:**
- Custom thumbnail (not random frame)
- Text overlay stating video topic
- Consistent template across all Reels
- High contrast, readable on mobile
- Face + text = optimal

### Cross-Posting from TikTok

**The rules changed:**
- Instagram no longer penalizes TikTok watermarks (Adam Mosseri confirmed)
- BUT - remove watermarks anyway for professionalism
- Download via SnapTik or TikTok's no-watermark option

**Cross-posting strategy:**
- Post to TikTok first (faster trend cycle)
- Wait 24-48 hours
- Post to Instagram with native upload
- Adjust caption for Instagram audience
- Don't mention TikTok in caption

## 7. Account Health

### Shadowban Indicators

**Signs you might be shadowbanned:**
- Sudden engagement drop (50%+)
- Posts not appearing in hashtag feeds
- Explore reach goes to zero
- Non-followers can't find your content

**What a shadowban actually is:**
- Not officially acknowledged by Instagram
- Likely: reduced distribution, not complete ban
- Usually temporary (24 hours - 2 weeks)

### Action Limits (2025)

To avoid triggering spam detection:

| Action | Hourly Limit | Daily Limit |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Follows | 20-30 | 150-200 |
| Unfollows | 20-30 | 150-200 |
| Likes | 60-100 | 500-1000 |
| Comments | 20-30 | 100-200 |
| DMs | 20-30 | 50-100 |

**For new accounts (<3 months):**
- Cut all limits in half
- Build up activity gradually
- Avoid any automation

### Recovery Tactics

**If reach drops suddenly:**

1. **Check Account Status**
   - Settings > Account > Account Status
   - Shows if content violates guidelines

2. **Audit recent content**
   - Delete anything that might violate
   - Check for banned hashtags

3. **Stop all automation**
   - Disconnect third-party apps
   - Settings > Security > Apps and Websites

4. **Reduce activity**
   - Don't post for 24-48 hours
   - Minimal liking/commenting
   - Let account "rest"

5. **Appeal if needed**
   - Settings > Account > Account Status > Request Review

6. **Resume slowly**
   - Post high-quality content
   - Engage genuinely
   - Avoid hashtag spam

### What to Avoid

**Content violations:**
- Nudity/sexual content
- Hate speech
- Misinformation
- Violent content
- Sale of regulated goods

**Behavioral red flags:**
- Follow/unfollow spam
- Comment pods
- Buying followers/likes
- Rapid mass actions
- Multiple accounts doing same actions
- Third-party automation tools (except approved partners)
