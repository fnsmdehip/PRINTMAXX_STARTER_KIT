Lemlist limits you to 3 sending emails on the $59/mo plan. want more? $69/mo gets you 5 on Email Pro. you're paying more for less sending capacity. Lemlist's infrastructure is solid but it's not built for raw volume the way Instantly is.

Bottom line: if your strategy is volume (send more, get more replies), Instantly lets you scale sending capacity for half the price. Lemlist caps your accounts and charges more per inbox.

Lemlist's personalization is where they destroy Instantly. custom images with the prospect's name, logo, or LinkedIn photo embedded right in the email. dynamic landing pages that feel like you built a page just for them. liquid syntax for conditional logic (if prospect is CEO, say X, if VP say Y). multi-channel sequences that hit email, then LinkedIn, then a phone call.

Bottom line: if your cold emails all look the same and reply rates are below 2%, Lemlist's personalization is the fix. custom images alone can boost reply rates 2-3x according to their case studies. Instantly can't match this.

Instantly's approach: volume distribution. spread your sends across unlimited accounts so no single inbox gets hammered. smart rotation, automatic warmup, bounce detection. the idea is that 50 accounts sending 100 emails each lands better than 5 accounts sending 1,000 each.

Lemlist's approach: reputation management. lemwarm does the warmup. custom tracking domains keep your sender score clean. but the real deliverability play is personalization. emails that look hand-written get opened. emails that look mass-sent get flagged. Lemlist's dynamic content makes each email unique enough that spam filters treat it differently.

Bottom line: both take deliverability seriously but from opposite angles. Instantly spreads risk across accounts. Lemlist makes each email look unique. best results? use both strategies. or pick the one that matches your sending style.
