sleep tracking accuracy

winner: Sleep Cycle, they use your phone's sensors

Sleep Cycle uses your phone's accelerometer and microphone to detect movement and sound during sleep. it builds a sleep stage chart showing when you were in light sleep, deep sleep, and REM. the smart alarm uses this data to wake you during your lightest sleep phase within a 30-minute window.

SleepMaxx uses manual tracking. you log when you went to bed and when you woke up. it estimates sleep quality based on duration, consistency, and your daily notes. it's simpler but less precise on the stage-by-stage breakdown.

important caveat: phone-based sleep tracking (like Sleep Cycle) is less accurate than wearables. studies show 60-70% accuracy for sleep stages vs polysomnography. it's better than nothing, but it's not medical-grade. SleepMaxx's manual approach is honest about its limitations.

Bottom line: Sleep Cycle gives you more data points. SleepMaxx gives you honest, simple tracking without pretending phone sensors are medical devices. both are useful, neither replaces a sleep study.
