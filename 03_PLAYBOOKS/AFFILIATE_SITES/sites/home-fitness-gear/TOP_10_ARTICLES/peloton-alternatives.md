### Section 2: Peloton Alternatives Ranked (1,800 words)

**For each alternative:**
- Price (bike + subscription)
- How it compares to Peloton
- App compatibility
- Pros/cons
- Best for whom

**Alternatives to cover:**

1. **Echelon EX-5S** - $999 bike + $40/month
   - Similar experience, lower price
   - Good instructors, decent app
   - Pros: Value, solid build
   - Cons: App not as polished
   - Savings vs Peloton: ~$500

2. **NordicTrack S22i** - $1,999 bike + $39/month
   - Automatic incline/decline
   - iFit library is excellent
   - Better trainer variety
   - Pros: Features, decline
   - Cons: More expensive
   - Best for: Those wanting more than spin

3. **Schwinn IC4** - $799 bike (no required subscription)
   - Works with ANY app
   - Apple Fitness+, Peloton app, Zwift
   - Pros: Flexibility, value
   - Cons: No built-in screen
   - Best for: Apple users

4. **Bowflex C6** - $799 bike + optional JRNY
   - Similar to Schwinn IC4
   - Works with multiple apps
   - JRNY subscription optional
   - Pros: Quality build, flexibility
   - Cons: Basic metrics display

5. **Yosuda Indoor Bike** - $299
   - Budget Amazon option
   - Use any app on tablet
   - Pros: Incredibly cheap
   - Cons: Basic construction
   - Best for: Trying cycling cheaply

6. **Myx Fitness** - $1,299 bike + $29/month
   - Heart rate focused training
   - Good instructor lineup
   - Owned by Beachbody
   - Pros: Different approach
   - Cons: Less community feel

7. **Bowflex VeloCore** - $1,699
   - Unique leaning feature
   - JRNY workouts
   - Pros: Engaging movement
   - Cons: Premium price

8. **DIY: Basic Bike + Peloton App** - $300-500 bike + $13/month
   - Use any spin bike
   - Subscribe to Peloton digital
   - No leaderboard integration
   - But: Same instructors, workouts
