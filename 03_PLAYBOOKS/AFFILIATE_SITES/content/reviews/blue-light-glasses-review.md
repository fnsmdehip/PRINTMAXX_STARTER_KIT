## Do blue light glasses actually work?

The science is mixed. Blue light from screens probably isn't damaging your eyes. But:

1. The glasses reduce glare, which does reduce eye strain
2. The tint can help with sleep if used in evening
3. Placebo effect is real, and if you feel better, that matters

I notice less eye fatigue with Felix Gray and GUNNAR. Whether it's the blue light filtering or the anti-glare coating, my eyes feel better after long sessions.

## FAQ

**Do blue light glasses help with eye strain?**
Many users report less eye fatigue. Whether it's the blue light filtering or anti-glare coating isn't clear. Both help.

**Should I wear blue light glasses all day?**
Fine to wear them during screen time. No need outdoors. Some people wear them 8+ hours daily without issues.

**Clear lenses vs amber lenses?**
Clear lenses (Felix Gray, Zenni standard) filter less but look normal. Amber lenses (GUNNAR) filter more but affect color perception.

**Do I need prescription blue light glasses?**
If you wear glasses normally, yes. Zenni offers prescription at budget prices. Felix Gray and Warby Parker add $50 for prescription.

**Can I use blue light glasses with my phone?**
Yes. Works for any screen: phone, tablet, computer, TV.
