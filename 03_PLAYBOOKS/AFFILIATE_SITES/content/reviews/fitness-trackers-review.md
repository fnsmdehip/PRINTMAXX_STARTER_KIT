## How I tested step accuracy

I manually counted 1,000 steps three times per device and compared to tracker readings.

Results:
- Garmin: 2% average variance (most accurate)
- Fitbit: 3% average variance
- Apple Watch: 3% average variance
- Amazfit: 5% average variance
- Samsung: 7% average variance

All are accurate enough for daily tracking. The Samsung variance was highest, often undercounting by 50-70 steps per 1,000.

## FAQ

**Are fitness trackers accurate for steps?**
Yes, within 3-7% for the devices tested. Good enough for daily tracking goals.

**Fitbit vs Garmin?**
Fitbit is simpler and cheaper. Garmin has more data for serious training. Most people should get Fitbit.

**Do I need GPS for step tracking?**
No. GPS matters for mapping runs and bike rides. Step counting works without it.

**How often should I charge my fitness tracker?**
Depends on device. Fitbit: weekly. Apple Watch: daily. Amazfit: every 2-3 weeks.

**Which tracker syncs with StepUnlock?**
StepUnlock reads from Apple Health and Google Fit. Fitbit, Garmin, and Apple Watch sync to these platforms. Amazfit syncs to Google Fit.
