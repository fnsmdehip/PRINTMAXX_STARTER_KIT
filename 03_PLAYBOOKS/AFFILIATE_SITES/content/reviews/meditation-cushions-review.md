## What to look for in a meditation cushion

**Fill type matters most.**

Buckwheat hulls mold to your body and support sitting bones. Foam compresses over time and loses support. Kapok (cotton-like fiber) is middle ground.

For daily practice, get buckwheat. For occasional use, foam is fine.

**Height depends on your flexibility.**

Tighter hips need more height. Most zafus are 4-6 inches. If your knees float high above the floor when sitting cross-legged, you need a taller cushion or a bench.

**Zabuton (floor mat) helps.**

Knees and ankles on hard floors hurt after 15 minutes. A zabuton cushions them. Halfmoon set includes one. Otherwise, a folded blanket works.

## FAQ

**Do I need a meditation cushion?**
Not strictly. But proper height and support help posture. Bad posture leads to back pain and distraction. A cushion or bench makes longer sessions more comfortable.

**Buckwheat vs foam?**
Buckwheat molds to your body and lasts longer. Foam is lighter but compresses over time. For daily use, buckwheat is worth the extra cost.

**What height cushion do I need?**
Depends on hip flexibility. If your knees are high when sitting cross-legged, you need more height (5-6"). Flexible sitters can use lower cushions (4").

**Can I use a regular pillow?**
Temporarily, yes. But bed pillows compress flat and don't provide consistent support. A proper zafu maintains height and shape.
