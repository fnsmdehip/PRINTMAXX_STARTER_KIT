# WalkToUnlock - Competitive Analysis

## Direct Competitors (Screen Blockers)

### 1. Opal

**Revenue:** ~$600k MRR
**Pricing:** $9.99/mo, $49.99/yr
**Rating:** 4.8 (50k+ ratings)

**What they do well:**
- Clean, premium design
- Session-based blocking
- Family/group features
- Strong brand presence

**What they miss:**
- No fitness integration
- Generic unlock conditions (time-based)
- No health motivation angle
- Doesn't tie screen time to physical activity

**Our differentiation:**
- Step-based unlock (fitness gamification)
- Health data integration
- Walking as the unlock mechanism
- Different positioning (fitness + productivity)

## Market Gap Analysis

### Unserved needs:

1. **Walking accountability**
   - Fitbit tracks but doesn't enforce
   - StepBet requires money commitment
   - Nobody blocks apps until steps hit

2. **Screen time + fitness connection**
   - Opal blocks but ignores fitness
   - Fitness apps track but ignore screen time
   - No one connects the two

3. **Accessible fitness motivation**
   - Running apps intimidate beginners
   - Gym apps require equipment
   - Walking is universally accessible

4. **Simple daily accountability**
   - StepBet is weekly
   - Fitbit has no real consequence
   - Need daily enforcement

## Key Insights

1. **No one owns steps + screen blocking**
   - Clear white space
   - First mover advantage

2. **Walking is underserved in fitness apps**
   - Running/cycling dominate
   - Walking is more accessible

3. **Tracking without consequence doesn't work**
   - Step goals fail because no real consequence
   - Blocking creates real motivation

4. **Screen time anxiety is growing**
   - Everyone knows they scroll too much
   - Tie solution to positive activity (walking)

5. **Price benchmarks**
   - $8-10/mo is acceptable
   - Annual discount standard
   - Lower than premium fitness apps
