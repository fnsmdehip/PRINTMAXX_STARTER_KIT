## Targeting criteria

### Ideal productivity influencers

**Audience size:**
- Sweet spot: 5K-50K followers
- Under 5K: low volume
- Over 50K: harder to reach, more expensive

**Content themes:**
- Deep work and focus
- Time management
- Tool reviews and setups
- Digital minimalism
- Second brain/PKM
- Remote work
- Studying/learning
- Notion/Obsidian content

**Engagement signals:**
- Real comments discussing content
- Responds to DMs
- Posts consistently
- Actually uses tools (not just reviews for money)
- Shares real workflows

### Red flags

- Only posts sponsored content
- No actual productivity system (just motivational quotes)
- Promotes get-rich-quick schemes
- No engagement on posts
- Hasn't posted in 2+ weeks

## Product-specific angles

### Screen time/focus blockers

**Pain points:**
- Can't focus because of phone
- Social media addiction
- Doom scrolling
- Notification overload

**Angle:** "Your audience knows they should focus but can't stop checking their phone. This actually blocks the apps."

### AI productivity tools

**Pain points:**
- Information overload
- Writing takes too long
- Research is tedious
- Organizing notes is a mess

**Angle:** "Your audience wants to work smarter. This uses AI to {{specific_benefit}}."

### Time tracking/management

**Pain points:**
- Don't know where time goes
- Can't estimate tasks accurately
- No accountability
- Procrastination

**Angle:** "Your audience talks about time management but doesn't track time. This makes it easy."

## Platform-specific approaches

### Twitter/X

- Shorter DMs (under 200 characters works best)
- Engage with their tweets first before DM
- Quote tweet their content to get on radar
- Use threads to showcase product

### YouTube

- Email works better than comments
- Offer to be on their channel for a review
- Provide B-roll footage for their video
- Give extended trial for proper review

### Instagram

- DMs work but get buried
- Comment on their posts first
- Stories are good for soft engagement
- Reels work well for product demos

### TikTok

- Short DMs only
- Duet/stitch their content
- Trend participation with product
- Comment engagement first
