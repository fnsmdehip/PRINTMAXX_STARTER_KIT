# WalkToUnlock FAQ

Frequently asked questions for WalkToUnlock users.

---

## Getting Started

### What is WalkToUnlock?

WalkToUnlock is a phone-locking app that blocks distracting apps until you hit your step goal. Set a step target, the app locks selected apps, and you get them back when you walk. Built for people who want to move more and scroll less.

**Related:** How do I set up WalkToUnlock?

### How do I set up WalkToUnlock?

Download, grant health permissions, pick apps to block, set your first step goal. Done in under a minute. The app guides you through setup.

**Related:** What permissions does WalkToUnlock need?

### What permissions does WalkToUnlock need?

Health/step data access to count your steps. Notification permission for goal alerts. Screen Time (iOS) or Usage Access (Android) to block apps. All required for core functionality.

**Related:** Why does WalkToUnlock need health data?

### Why does WalkToUnlock need health data?

To count your steps. We read step counts from Apple Health (iOS) or Google Fit (Android). We don't access any other health data.

**Related:** What health data do you collect?

### Do I need to create an account?

No. The app works fully offline without an account. Creating one lets you sync data across devices and backup your streaks.

**Related:** How do I create an account?

### Does WalkToUnlock work on iPad?

Partially. iPads don't have built-in step tracking, so you'd need an Apple Watch or connected fitness tracker. Works best on iPhone.

**Related:** Does WalkToUnlock work with Apple Watch?

## Step Tracking

### How are steps counted?

We read step data from Apple Health (iOS) or Google Fit (Android). If you have a smartwatch, those steps sync to your phone's health app and we read from there.

**Related:** Steps aren't counting correctly.

### Steps aren't counting correctly.

Check that health permissions are fully granted. Make sure your phone or watch is syncing to Apple Health/Google Fit. Try closing and reopening the app.

**Related:** How do I fix step counting issues?

### Do steps from my smartwatch count?

Yes, if your watch syncs to Apple Health or Google Fit. Fitbit, Garmin, and other devices that sync to these platforms will work.

**Related:** How do I connect my Fitbit?

### Can I use a treadmill?

Yes. Steps on a treadmill count if your phone is in your pocket or your watch is tracking. If neither, you can manually add steps (Premium feature).

**Related:** Can I manually add steps?

### Can I manually add steps?

Premium feature only. Go to Settings > Manual Entry. We trust you to be honest. Manual entries are flagged in your history.

**Related:** Is there a manual entry limit?

### What's the minimum step goal?

500 steps. We recommend starting with 1,000-2,000 if you're new to walking goals. You can increase over time.

**Related:** What's a good step goal?

### What's a good step goal?

Depends on your current activity level. Starting point: aim for 500-1,000 more than your daily average. Build up to 10,000+ over time.

**Related:** How do I change my step goal?

### How do I change my step goal?

Settings > Daily Goal. You can change it anytime, but it won't affect the current day's unlock requirement.

**Related:** Can I have different goals for different days?

## Health Data

### What health data do you access?

Step count only. We read daily step totals from your phone's health app. We don't access heart rate, sleep, weight, or any other health data.

**Related:** Do you share my health data?

### Do you share my health data?

No. Your step data stays on your device and in your encrypted account (if you create one). We never sell or share health data.

**Related:** Can I delete my health data?

### Can I delete my health data?

Yes. Settings > Data > Clear Health Data removes all step history from WalkToUnlock. This doesn't affect your phone's health app data.

**Related:** What's your privacy policy?

### Can I use WalkToUnlock without sharing health data?

Not effectively. The core feature requires step data to know when to unlock your apps. You could use it as a manual timer only, but that defeats the purpose.

**Related:** I don't want to share health data.

### Why are my steps different from my watch?

Your watch may use different algorithms or count partial steps differently. WalkToUnlock reads from Apple Health/Google Fit, which may aggregate differently than your watch's native app.

**Related:** Which step count is more accurate?

### My steps aren't syncing from my watch.

Check that your watch is properly connected to Apple Health or Google Fit. Try opening your watch's companion app to force a sync. Restart both devices if needed.

**Related:** How do I force a sync?

## Features

### What apps can I block?

Any app on your phone. Common choices: social media, games, YouTube, Netflix, messaging apps. You pick what distracts you most.

**Related:** How do I add apps to block?

### How do I add apps to the block list?

Settings > Blocked Apps > Add. Browse the list and tap apps you want blocked until you hit your step goal.

**Related:** Can I block all apps?

### What are walking streaks?

Days in a row where you hit your step goal. Miss a day, streak resets. Longest streak saved. Builds accountability.

**Related:** What happens if I miss my goal?

### What happens if I miss my goal?

Your streak resets. Blocked apps remain blocked until midnight (reset time). Next day starts fresh with new goal.

**Related:** Can I change the reset time?

### Can I change the reset time?

Premium feature. Default is midnight in your timezone. You can change to any hour (example: 4 AM for night owls).

**Related:** I work night shifts.

### What if there's an emergency?

Triple tap the home button (iOS) or power button 3x (Android) to access emergency bypass. Use it only for real emergencies.

**Related:** Emergency bypass isn't working.

### Can I pause my step goal?

Yes. Settings > Pause Goal. Apps stay unblocked during pause. Use sparingly. Pausing too often defeats the purpose.

**Related:** I'm sick or injured.

## Troubleshooting

### Blocked apps are still opening.

Check Screen Time (iOS) or Usage Access (Android) permissions. They must be fully enabled. Try revoking and re-granting permission, then restart the app.

**Related:** How do I fix permission issues?

### Steps aren't unlocking my apps.

Make sure health permissions are granted. Try force-quitting and reopening the app. Check that your step count in the app matches your health app.

**Related:** Why doesn't the app see my steps?

### Battery draining fast.

WalkToUnlock uses minimal battery for step monitoring. If excessive drain, check that you're not running multiple step-tracking apps simultaneously.

**Related:** How do I reduce battery usage?

### My streak reset but I walked yesterday.

Check your timezone. If you traveled, the day boundary might have shifted. Also ensure your steps synced before midnight reset time.

**Related:** Can you restore my streak?

### Can you restore my streak?

If it reset due to a bug or sync issue, yes. Email support@walktounlock.app with your account info and we'll investigate.

**Related:** How do I contact support?

### App is crashing.

Update to the latest version. If crashes continue, uninstall and reinstall. Your data syncs back when you sign in.

**Related:** How do I report a bug?
