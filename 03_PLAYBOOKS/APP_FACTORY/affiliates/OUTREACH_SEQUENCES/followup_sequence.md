# Follow-up Sequence Templates

Follow-up is where most deals happen. 80% of sales require 5+ touches. Most people give up after 1-2.

---

## The golden rules

1. **Always follow up.** No response is not a "no."
2. **Add value each time.** Don't just "check in."
3. **Know when to stop.** 3-4 follow-ups max.
4. **Change the angle.** Same message won't work twice.
5. **Make it easy.** Simple yes/no questions.

---

## DM follow-up sequence (TikTok/Instagram)

### Follow-up 1 (Day 3)

```
Hey, floating this back up. Quick yes/no - any interest in trying [App Name]? I can send free access if you want to test it first.
```

### Follow-up 2 (Day 7)

```
One more ping. If the affiliate thing doesn't fit your content, totally get it.

But if you just want to try the app for yourself, here's free premium: [link]

No strings attached.
```

### Follow-up 3 (Day 14) - final

```
Last message on this. If timing's not right, no stress.

The offer stands whenever: 20% commission + free premium.

Good luck with your content!
```

## Email follow-up sequence (Bloggers/YouTubers)

### Follow-up 1 (Day 4)

**Subject:** Re: [Original subject]

```
Hey [Name],

Just bumping this to the top of your inbox.

Quick recap: [App Name] affiliate program, 25% commission, free premium access.

Worth exploring, or should I check back another time?

[Your name]
```

### Follow-up 2 (Day 9)

**Subject:** Different angle

```
Hey [Name],

Didn't hear back, so trying a different angle:

Would a guest post work better than affiliate? I could write something like "[Post idea related to your app]" for your site. You keep 100% of the traffic, and we just include a mention of [App Name].

Or if now's just not a good time, let me know and I'll follow up in a few months.

[Your name]
```

### Follow-up 3 (Day 16)

**Subject:** Quick question

```
Hey [Name],

One quick question: Is affiliate content something you're interested in generally, or not really your thing?

Just trying to understand if this is a fit or if I should focus elsewhere.

Either way, no hard feelings.

[Your name]
```

### Follow-up 4 (Day 30) - breakup email

**Subject:** Closing the loop

```
Hey [Name],

I've reached out a few times about [App Name] affiliate partnership. Since I haven't heard back, I'll assume the timing isn't right.

If things change, the offer stands:
- 25% commission
- Free premium access
- Affiliate signup: [link]

Thanks for your time. Keep up the great work on [Blog/Channel Name].

[Your name]
```

## Situational follow-ups

### After they showed interest but went quiet

```
Hey [Name],

We chatted about [App Name] a bit back. You seemed interested but things got quiet.

Still open to setting you up as an affiliate? I can have your link ready in 5 minutes.

Let me know.
```

### After they said "maybe later"

```
Hey [Name],

You mentioned checking out [App Name] at a better time. Just circling back.

Any interest now? The offer's still the same: 20% commission + free premium.

No pressure either way.
```

### After they tried the product but didn't promote

```
Hey [Name],

Saw you tried [App Name] - thanks for giving it a shot.

What did you think? If it didn't click, I'd love feedback.

If you did like it and just haven't had time to post, happy to send some content ideas or swipe files.

Either way, curious to hear your take.
```

### After a major update or news

```
Hey [Name],

Quick update: [App Name] just [launched new feature / hit milestone / got featured].

Thought you might find it relevant since we chatted about partnering before.

The affiliate offer still stands if you want to revisit.

[Your name]
```

## What NOT to do

**Don't:**
- Send the same message again
- "Just checking in" with no value
- Follow up more than 4 times without response
- Sound desperate or pushy
- Send automated-looking messages

**Do:**
- Add new information each time
- Change your angle/offer
- Keep it short
- Give them an easy out
- Know when to move on

## Tracking follow-ups

Add these columns to your outreach spreadsheet:

```
Follow-up 1 Sent, Follow-up 1 Response, Follow-up 2 Sent, Follow-up 2 Response, Follow-up 3 Sent, Follow-up 3 Response, Final Outcome
```

Or use a CRM that tracks email sequences.

## Automation options

For email follow-ups at scale:

**Free/cheap:**
- Mailtrack (Gmail tracking)
- Streak (Gmail CRM)
- Boomerang (scheduled follow-ups)

**Paid:**
- Lemlist (cold outreach automation)
- Apollo (email sequences)
- Hunter.io Campaigns (email campaigns)

**For DMs:**
- Mostly manual (automation risks account bans)
- Use a spreadsheet to track timing
- Set calendar reminders for follow-ups

## Response rate benchmarks

| Follow-up number | Expected response rate |
|------------------|----------------------|
| Initial outreach | 10-20% |
| Follow-up 1 | 5-15% |
| Follow-up 2 | 3-10% |
| Follow-up 3 | 2-5% |
| Follow-up 4+ | <2% |

Most responses come from follow-ups 1-2. Beyond that, you're mostly just leaving the door open.
