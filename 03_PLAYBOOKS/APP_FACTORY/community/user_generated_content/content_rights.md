# Content rights

How to legally use content your users create.

---

## The basics

**Problem:** Your user posts a great testimonial. You want to use it in ads. Can you?

**Answer:** Only if you have permission.

Without explicit rights, you can:
- React to it
- Comment on it
- View it

You cannot:
- Reshare it on other platforms
- Use it in marketing materials
- Put it in ads
- Feature it on your website

---

## Types of rights

### 1. Implied license (weakest)

When users post in your community, they implicitly allow:
- Other members to view it
- Discussion and replies
- Platform-native interactions

**Does NOT include:** Use outside the platform.

### 2. Terms of service rights

Your ToS can grant broader rights when users join:

```
By posting content in [Community Name], you grant [Company] a
non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to use, reproduce,
modify, and display your content for promotional purposes.
```

**Pros:** Automatic, applies to all content
**Cons:** Users may not read ToS, can feel sneaky

### 3. Explicit permission (strongest)

Direct consent for specific use:

```
Do you give us permission to use your testimonial in:
[ ] Our website
[ ] Social media posts
[ ] Paid advertisements
[ ] Email marketing
[ ] Press materials

Signature: ____________
Date: ____________
```

**Pros:** Clear, defensible, respectful
**Cons:** Friction, may reduce submissions

## Permission collection system

### For community posts

When you want to feature a post:

**Step 1: DM the user**
```
Hey [name]!

Loved your post about [specific thing]. Would you be okay with us
featuring it on our [website/socials]?

We'd credit you as [display name they choose].

Let me know!
```

**Step 2: Get written confirmation**
```
User: "Yeah sure!"
You: "Thanks! Just to confirm - you're okay with us using it on our
website and social media with credit to you. Reply 'yes' to confirm."
User: "Yes"
```

**Step 3: Save the exchange**
Screenshot and store in permissions folder.

### For testimonials (collected via form)

Include in form:

```
By submitting this testimonial, I grant [Company Name] permission to
use my submission for marketing purposes including but not limited to:
- Website display
- Social media posts
- Paid advertising
- Email marketing
- Press materials

I confirm this testimonial represents my honest experience.

[ ] I agree to the above
[ ] I want to remain anonymous
[ ] You can use my first name and last initial
[ ] You can use my full name
```

### For video content

Use formal release form:

```
VIDEO TESTIMONIAL RELEASE

I, [full name], grant [Company Name] permission to use my video
testimonial for promotional purposes.

I understand this includes:
- Website and app display
- Social media (organic and paid)
- Email marketing
- Press and PR materials
- Trade show displays

I confirm the content is my honest experience and I was not coerced.

Compensation received (if any): [describe or N/A]

I am over 18 years of age.

Signature: ____________________
Print name: ____________________
Date: ____________________
Email: ____________________
```

## Platform-specific rules

### Discord

- Posts are viewable by members only (unless screenshotted)
- Resharing outside Discord requires permission
- Bot messages are owned by you

### Instagram

- Public posts can be shared via official "share" feature
- Embedding requires no permission (uses official embed)
- Downloading and re-uploading requires permission
- Stories require direct permission to save/share

### Twitter/X

- Retweets are built-in sharing (no permission needed)
- Quote tweets are acceptable
- Screenshotting and reposting should credit original
- Using in ads requires permission

### YouTube

- Embedding is allowed (official feature)
- Re-uploading requires permission
- Using clips in your videos: check fair use or get permission

### TikTok

- Duets/stitches use official features (allowed)
- Downloading and re-uploading requires permission
- Using in ads requires explicit permission

## Special situations

### Anonymous testimonials

If user wants to remain anonymous:

**What you CAN say:**
- "A user from California said..."
- "One of our members shared..."
- First name only: "Sarah shared..."

**Keep:**
- Original submission with real name (for verification)
- Permission noting anonymous display preference

### Minors (under 18)

**Rule:** Get parent/guardian consent for anyone under 18.

```
PARENTAL CONSENT FOR MINOR'S TESTIMONIAL

I, [parent name], am the parent/guardian of [minor name], age [X].

I consent to [Company] using my child's testimonial/image for
promotional purposes.

Parent signature: ____________________
Date: ____________________
Contact email: ____________________
```

### Employees / paid ambassadors

Disclose the relationship:

```
[Name] is a [Company] ambassador. They received [free access/payment/
product] but this testimonial reflects their honest experience.
```

FTC requires disclosure of material relationships.

### International users

**GDPR (EU users):**
- Explicit consent required
- Right to withdraw consent
- Right to be forgotten
- Document everything

**Include in permission:**
```
I understand I can withdraw this permission at any time by emailing
[email]. Withdrawal won't affect content already published but will
prevent future use.
```

## Revocation handling

### If a user asks to remove their content

**Step 1: Acknowledge immediately**
```
Got it. I'll remove your testimonial from [locations] within [X days].
```

**Step 2: Actually remove it**
- Website
- Social posts (if possible)
- Ads (pause and replace)
- Marketing materials
- Email templates

**Step 3: Confirm completion**
```
Your testimonial has been removed from all our marketing materials.
Thanks for letting us know.
```

**Step 4: Document**
Note in permissions tracker: "Revoked [date], content removed [date]"

### What you can't undo

- Printed materials already distributed
- Screenshots others have taken
- Archived/cached versions online
- Third-party sites that copied content

Be honest about limitations.

## Legal disclaimer

This guide provides general information, not legal advice. For your specific situation:

1. Consult an attorney familiar with IP and content rights
2. Review platform-specific Terms of Service
3. Consider your jurisdiction's laws
4. When in doubt, get explicit written permission

**Principle:** More permission is always safer than less.

### Permission checklist

Before featuring UGC:

- [ ] Identified the content creator
- [ ] Requested permission in writing
- [ ] Received explicit "yes" confirmation
- [ ] Documented the permission exchange
- [ ] Confirmed approved use cases
- [ ] Stored in permissions folder
- [ ] Noted any restrictions (anonymous, limited use)

### Red flags (don't use)

- User is a minor without parent consent
- Content makes unverifiable claims
- You can't confirm the user is real
- Permission was ambiguous
- User seemed hesitant or pressured
- Content shows identifiable third parties
