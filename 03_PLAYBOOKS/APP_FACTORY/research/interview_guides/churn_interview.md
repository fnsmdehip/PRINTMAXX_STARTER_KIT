# Churn Interview Guide

Understanding why users leave.

**Duration:** 20-30 minutes
**Participants:** 5-10 recent churners (within 30 days)
**Goal:** Find the moment they decided to leave and why

---

## Timing matters

**Best:** 3-7 days after churn
- Fresh memory
- Not too emotional
- Still remember the trigger

**Acceptable:** 7-30 days after churn

**Too late:** 30+ days
- Memory fades
- Rationalization increases

---

## Before the interview

### Prep checklist
- [ ] Review their usage data (last login, features used, subscription length)
- [ ] Check support tickets for any complaints
- [ ] Know their subscription tier and price paid
- [ ] Prepare personalized questions based on data

### What you're looking for
- The triggering moment (not the excuse)
- Unmet expectations
- Competitive alternatives they're using
- What would have kept them

---

## Interview script

### Opening (2 min)

> Thanks for taking the time to chat. I know you've moved on from [product], and I'm trying to understand your experience so we can improve.
>
> There are no wrong answers. Honest feedback helps us most, even if it's critical.
>
> Is it okay if I record this for notes?

---

### Context setting (3 min)

> First, help me understand: what were you hoping to accomplish when you first signed up for [product]?

**Probe:**
- What problem were you trying to solve?
- Where did you hear about us?
- What made you decide to try it?

---

### Usage period (5 min)

> Walk me through your experience using [product].

**Probe:**
- How often did you use it?
- Which features did you use most?
- Did you ever try [specific feature]? Why or why not?

> Was there a moment when you felt like "this is working for me"?

> Was there a moment when you felt like "this isn't working"?

---

### The churn moment (10 min)

This is the critical section. Get specific.

> Tell me about when you decided to stop using [product].

**Dig deeper:**
- What specifically triggered that decision?
- Was there a specific moment or event?
- How long were you thinking about it before you cancelled?

> What were you expecting that you didn't get?

> What would have needed to change for you to stay?

> If you could go back and talk to yourself on day one, what would you tell yourself about [product]?

---

### Alternatives (5 min)

> What are you using now instead?

**Probe:**
- How did you find it?
- What made you choose it over us?
- Is it solving the problem better? How?
- What do they do that we didn't?

> If you're not using anything, how are you solving the problem now?

---

### Recovery attempt (3 min)

> Is there anything that would make you consider coming back?

**Probe:**
- A specific feature?
- A price change?
- A different approach to the problem?

> What would we need to change?

---

### Wrap-up (2 min)

> Any other feedback you want to share?

> Thanks for being honest. This really helps us improve.

## Churn interview vs. exit survey

| Churn interview | Exit survey |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Deep understanding | Quick data |
| 20-30 min | 2 min |
| 5-10 people | Everyone who churns |
| Rich context | Surface patterns |
| Use for insight | Use for tracking |

**Best practice:** Run exit surveys for all churners. Interview a sample monthly.

---

## Exit survey questions (for automated collection)

Add these to your cancellation flow:

1. Main reason for leaving? (single select)
   - Not using it enough
   - Too expensive
   - Missing features I need
   - Found a better alternative
   - Technical issues
   - Other

2. What feature would have made you stay? (open text)

3. Would you recommend [product] to a friend? (1-10)

4. Can we follow up for a quick chat? (Yes/No + email)

## Red flags to watch for

Patterns that indicate systemic problems:

**Onboarding failure:**
- "I never figured out how to..."
- "I didn't have time to set it up"
- Churn in first 7 days

**Value proposition mismatch:**
- "I thought it would do X but it does Y"
- "The marketing said..."
- "I expected..."

**Competitor advantage:**
- "[Competitor] does this better"
- Multiple mentions of same competitor
- Switching to free alternatives

**Price sensitivity:**
- "It's good but not worth $X"
- "I'd pay $Y but not $X"
- Churn spikes after trial ends
