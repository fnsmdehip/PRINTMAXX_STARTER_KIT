# Discovery Interview Guide

Understanding problems worth solving.

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Participants:** 5-8 people in target segment
**Goal:** Find pain points, workflows, and willingness to pay

---

## Before the interview

### Prep checklist
- [ ] Review any existing data on this user segment
- [ ] Test recording setup (Zoom/Loom)
- [ ] Prepare note-taking doc
- [ ] Send calendar invite with video link
- [ ] Confirm 24 hours before

### What you're looking for
- Specific stories, not opinions
- Frequency and intensity of problems
- Current solutions and workarounds
- Emotional language (frustrating, annoying, love, hate)
- Willingness to pay signals

---

## Interview script

### Opening (2-3 min)

> Thanks for taking the time to chat. I'm researching how people [do X] and I'd love to hear about your experience. There are no right or wrong answers. I'm just trying to understand your perspective.
>
> Is it okay if I record this for my notes? The recording won't be shared publicly.

**Wait for consent before recording.**

---

### Background (5 min)

> Tell me a bit about yourself and your [relevant context: work, daily routine, goals].

**Probe:**
- What does a typical day/week look like?
- How long have you been doing [activity]?
- What role does [area] play in your life?

---

### Current behavior (15 min)

> Walk me through the last time you [did the activity we're researching].

**Follow-up questions:**
- What triggered you to do that?
- What did you do first? Then what?
- Who else was involved?
- How long did it take?
- What tools or apps did you use?

> What does success look like when you [do activity]?

> How often do you [do activity]? Daily? Weekly?

---

### Pain points (15 min)

> What's the hardest part about [activity]?

**Probe deeper:**
- Why is that hard?
- Can you give me a specific example?
- What happens when that goes wrong?
- How do you feel when that happens?

> What have you tried to solve this problem?

**Follow-up:**
- What worked about that solution?
- What didn't work?
- Are you still using it? Why or why not?
- How much did you pay for it?

> If you had a magic wand and could fix one thing about [activity], what would it be?

---

### Willingness to pay (10 min)

> What would it be worth to you to solve [the problem they mentioned]?

**If they struggle:**
- Would you pay $5/month? $10? $20?
- What's the maximum you'd consider?
- What would it need to do to be worth that?

> What would make you switch from your current solution?

> Have you ever paid for a tool or app to help with [activity]?
- What made you decide to pay?
- What would make you stop paying?

---

### Wrap-up (5 min)

> Is there anything I didn't ask about that you think is important?

> Do you know anyone else who [does this activity] who might be willing to chat?

> Thanks so much. This was really helpful. I'll follow up with [any promised deliverable or next steps].

## Common mistakes to avoid

**Don't:**
- Ask leading questions ("Don't you think X would be helpful?")
- Ask about future behavior ("Would you use X?")
- Pitch your solution during the interview
- Interrupt when they're telling a story
- Ask yes/no questions

**Do:**
- Ask "Tell me about a time when..."
- Ask "Why?" and "Can you give an example?"
- Embrace silence (they're thinking)
- Focus on past behavior, not hypotheticals
- Take verbatim notes of key phrases
