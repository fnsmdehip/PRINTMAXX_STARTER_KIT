# Power User Interview Guide

Understanding what keeps your best users engaged.

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Participants:** 3-5 top users by engagement or tenure
**Goal:** Understand retention drivers and get feature insights

---

## Identifying power users

Define "power user" for your product. Common criteria:

- **Usage frequency:** Daily active for 30+ days
- **Feature depth:** Uses 5+ features
- **Tenure:** Subscribed 3+ months
- **Engagement:** Top 10% by sessions
- **Evangelism:** Has referred others

---

## Before the interview

### Prep checklist
- [ ] Review their usage analytics in detail
- [ ] Note their most-used features
- [ ] Check if they've contacted support (and what for)
- [ ] Look for patterns that differ from average users
- [ ] Prepare personalized questions

### What you're looking for
- The "aha moment" that hooked them
- Workflows and habits they've built
- Features that matter most
- Pain points (even power users have them)
- Ideas for new features

---

## Interview script

### Opening (2 min)

> Thanks for making time. You're one of our most active users, and I want to understand what's working for you. This helps us make the product better for everyone.
>
> No sales pitch here. Just trying to learn from how you use [product].
>
> Okay to record for notes?

---

### Origin story (5 min)

> Take me back to when you first started using [product]. What was going on in your life that made you look for something like this?

**Probe:**
- What problem were you trying to solve?
- What else had you tried before?
- How did you find us?

> Was there a specific moment when you realized "this is going to work for me"?

**This is gold.** The "aha moment" is what you need to replicate for new users.

---

### Current usage (10 min)

> Walk me through how you use [product] now. What does a typical week look like?

**Probe:**
- When do you open it?
- What's the first thing you do?
- How long do you spend?
- Any routines or rituals?

> Which features do you use most?

**For each feature they mention:**
- Why that one?
- How often?
- What would break if that feature didn't exist?

> Are there features you've tried but stopped using? Why?

> Are there features you've never tried? What's held you back?

---

### Value and stickiness (10 min)

> What's the main benefit you get from [product]?

> What would you miss most if you couldn't use it anymore?

> Have you ever thought about switching to something else?
- What made you consider it?
- What made you stay?

> What keeps you coming back?

**Listen for:**
- Habit formation
- Emotional connection
- Unique value props
- Switching costs

---

### Pain points and improvements (10 min)

Even power users have frustrations.

> What's the most frustrating thing about [product]?

> If you could change one thing, what would it be?

> Is there anything you wish it did that it doesn't?

> What would make you use it even more?

---

### Social proof and referrals (5 min)

> Have you told anyone else about [product]?
- What do you tell them?
- How do you describe it?

> If you were explaining [product] to a friend in one sentence, what would you say?

**Their words = marketing copy gold.**

> What type of person do you think would benefit most from this?

---

### Closing (3 min)

> What should I have asked that I didn't?

> Any final thoughts on how we can improve?

> Thanks for being a power user. Seriously. You're why we keep building.

## Turning insights into action

### For onboarding
- How can we get new users to the "aha moment" faster?
- What features should we highlight first?
- What habits should we try to form?

### For retention
- What's the "core loop" power users have built?
- How can we reinforce it?
- What features drive the most stickiness?

### For marketing
- How do power users describe us?
- What benefits do they emphasize?
- Who do they recommend us to?

### For product
- What's missing that power users want?
- What's broken that they work around?
- What's underused that could be improved?

---

## Power user advisory panel

Consider creating an ongoing relationship:

**Benefits:**
- Early feedback on new features
- Beta testers
- Case studies and testimonials
- Referral sources

**How to structure:**
- Quarterly 30-min calls
- Slack channel for quick questions
- Early access to features
- Small thank-you gifts

**Don't:**
- Over-ask
- Ignore their feedback
- Treat them like free labor
