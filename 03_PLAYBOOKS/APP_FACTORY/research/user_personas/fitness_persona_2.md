# Fitness Persona 2: Casual Exerciser

## Quick profile

**Name:** Jennifer L.
**Age:** 31
**Occupation:** Account Manager at agency
**Location:** Chicago, IL
**Income:** $68,000/year
**Tech comfort:** High (uses apps for everything)

---

## Core identity

Jennifer exercises somewhat regularly but inconsistently. She's not trying to be an athlete, just wants to feel good and look decent.

**Health status:**
- Healthy weight, wants to tone up
- Has energy but not fitness
- No major health issues
- Wants to maintain as she ages

---

---

## Pain points

### Primary frustrations

1. **Inconsistency**
   - Good weeks and bad weeks
   - Social plans derail workouts
   - Travel for work disrupts routine
   - Can't seem to make it stick

2. **Boredom**
   - Same gym routine gets old
   - Doesn't know what to do
   - Cardio feels like punishment
   - Need variety to stay engaged

3. **No visible results**
   - Works out but body doesn't change
   - Not sure if doing exercises right
   - Spending time but not seeing payoff
   - Demotivating cycle

4. **Time crunch**
   - Gym takes 1.5 hours with commute
   - Some days can't fit it in
   - Sacrifices social life for gym
   - Feels like trade-offs

| Solution | Why she tried | Why it failed |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Boutique fitness classes | Fun, social | Too expensive, $30/class |
| Planet Fitness | Cheap | Boring, never goes |

---

## What she actually needs

### Must-have
- Variety (not same thing every day)
- 20-30 minute effective workouts
- No gym required sometimes
- Visible progress tracking
- Flexibility for busy weeks

### Nice-to-have
- Social/community element
- Recommendation engine (what to do today)
- Works at home and gym
- Instructor personality

---

## Jobs to be done

**Primary JTBD:**
"When I have 25 minutes and want to work out but don't know what to do, I want someone to tell me exactly what to do that day, so I actually exercise without wasting time deciding."

**Secondary JTBDs:**
- "Help me see that my workouts are working"
- "Keep things interesting so I don't quit"
- "Fit around my unpredictable schedule"
- "Make me feel like part of a fitness community"

## Willingness to pay

- Currently pays: $25/month gym, $0 apps
- Would consider: $15-20/month for the right app
- Price sensitivity: Medium
- Value trigger: "If it actually kept me consistent and I saw results"

---

## Decision factors

**What would make her try:**
- Friend recommendation (important)
- Pretty UI and good instructors
- Free trial
- Instagram/TikTok presence

**What would make her stay:**
- Actually stuck with it 3+ weeks
- Saw physical changes
- Enjoyed the workouts
- Felt community connection

**What would make her leave:**
- Got boring
- Couldn't fit in schedule
- No visible results after month
- Better option came along

---

## Messaging that resonates

**Yes:**
- "Tell me what to do today"
- "Real results in 20 minutes"
- "Workouts that don't feel like work"
- "Fit fitness into your life"

**No:**
- "Extreme transformation"
- "No pain no gain"
- "Commit to the grind"
- "Elite training program"

---

## User quotes (composite)

> "I want to be someone who works out, not a fitness person. There's a difference."

> "I've started and stopped so many times. I need something that adapts to my chaos."

> "I'd rather do a fun 20-minute workout than force myself through 45 minutes of misery."

> "Show me it's working. I don't feel different, so I stop going."

---

## Design implications

1. **Daily recommendations** - Tell her exactly what to do
2. **Varied workouts** - Different every day
3. **Time options** - 15, 25, 40 minute versions
4. **Progress photos/metrics** - Visible change tracking
5. **No equipment required** - Home or gym options
6. **Instructor personality** - Makes it enjoyable
7. **Social features** - Friends, challenges
8. **Flexible scheduling** - Adjust for busy weeks
