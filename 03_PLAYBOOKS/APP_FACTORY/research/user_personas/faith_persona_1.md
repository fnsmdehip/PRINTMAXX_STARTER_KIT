# Faith Persona 1: Busy Professional

## Quick profile

**Name:** Sarah M.
**Age:** 34
**Occupation:** Marketing Manager
**Location:** Suburbs of Dallas, TX
**Income:** $85,000/year
**Tech comfort:** High (uses 10+ apps daily)

---

## Core identity

Sarah is a practicing Christian who grew up in the church. Her faith is important to her identity, but her career demands often crowd out spiritual practices.

**Faith background:**
- Raised Baptist, now attends non-denominational church
- Believes deeply but struggles to prioritize faith
- Husband and two kids (ages 5 and 7)
- Church attendance: 2-3x per month (down from weekly)

---

## Daily life

**Morning (6:00-8:00 AM):**
- Rushes to get kids ready for school
- Checks email/Slack before leaving house
- Coffee in car during commute
- Zero quiet time

**Workday (8:00-5:30 PM):**
- Back-to-back meetings
- Eats lunch at desk
- Constantly context-switching
- High stress, high stakes

**Evening (5:30-10:00 PM):**
- Kids' activities, homework help
- Dinner prep, family time
- Work emails after kids in bed
- Falls asleep exhausted

**Weekend:**
- Church when family can make it
- Kids' sports, errands
- Tries to rest but rarely succeeds

| Goal | Reality | Gap |
|------|---------|-----|
| Daily Bible reading | Maybe 2x/week | Large |

---

## Pain points

### Primary frustrations

1. **Guilt cycle**
   - Knows she "should" be more consistent
   - Feels guilty when she misses devotions
   - Guilt creates avoidance, not action

2. **Time scarcity**
   - Can't find 30+ minute blocks
   - Traditional devotionals feel too long
   - Mornings are impossible

3. **Shallow engagement**
   - When she does read, feels rushed
   - Doesn't retain what she reads
   - No reflection time

4. **Isolation**
   - Doesn't have Christian friends at work
   - Small group conflict with kids' schedules
   - Feels alone in her struggles

---

| Solution | Why she tried | Why it failed |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| YouVersion Bible App | Free, recommended | Notifications ignored, forgot |
| Daily devotional book | Gifted by friend | Left on nightstand untouched |

---

## What she actually needs

### Must-have
- Content that fits in 5-minute windows
- Works in fragmented moments
- No guilt trips
- Easy to resume after missing days

### Nice-to-have
- Connects to her real-life situations (work stress, parenting)
- Helps her share faith with kids
- Some sense of community/accountability
- Progress tracking without shame

---

## Decision factors

**What would make her try:**
- Recommendation from trusted friend
- Clear "5 minutes a day" promise
- Free trial, no credit card
- Pretty design (matters to her)

**What would make her stay:**
- Actually used it for 2+ weeks
- Felt spiritually nourished
- Didn't add stress
- Kids could use it too

**What would make her leave:**
- Guilt-inducing notifications
- Content felt generic/preachy
- Required too much time
- App felt dated

## Design implications

1. **Micro-content** - 3-5 minute devotionals max
2. **Resumable** - No shame for missing days, easy to restart
3. **Contextual** - Content about work stress, parenting, marriage
4. **Kid mode** - Something to do with children
5. **Notifications** - Gentle, opt-in, no guilt
6. **Offline** - Works in airplane mode for commute
7. **Progress** - Celebrate consistency without punishment for gaps
