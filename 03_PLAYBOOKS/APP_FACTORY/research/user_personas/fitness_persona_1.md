# Fitness Persona 1: Sedentary Worker

## Quick profile

**Name:** David K.
**Age:** 38
**Occupation:** Software Engineer
**Location:** Seattle, WA
**Income:** $140,000/year
**Tech comfort:** Very high (builds software for a living)

---

## Core identity

David spends 10+ hours a day in front of screens. He knows he needs to move more but hasn't found a sustainable approach.

**Health status:**
- 25 lbs overweight (BMI 28)
- Back pain from sitting
- Low energy, especially afternoon
- Sleeps poorly
- Last physical: "Pre-diabetic, watch it"

---

## Daily life

**Morning (7:00-9:00 AM):**
- Wakes tired, hits snooze
- Coffee, checks Slack/email from bed
- Logs into work by 9
- No breakfast or quick processed food

**Workday (9:00-7:00 PM):**
- Continuous meetings and coding
- Forgets to stand up for hours
- Lunch at desk (delivery)
- Afternoon energy crash
- Often works late

**Evening (7:00-11:00 PM):**
- Too tired to cook, more delivery
- Netflix, gaming, browsing
- Doom-scrolls news before bed
- Sleeps at midnight, poorly

**Weekend:**
- Sleeps in to "recover"
- Some errands, mostly screens
- Occasional walk if weather's nice
- Monday arrives exhausted

---

## Pain points

### Primary frustrations

1. **Decision fatigue**
   - Spends mental energy all day on work
   - Zero willpower left for health decisions
   - Needs something that requires no thinking

2. **All-or-nothing mindset**
   - If can't do "proper" workout, does nothing
   - Gym feels intimidating, too much effort
   - Misses one day, quits for months

3. **Physical discomfort**
   - Back hurts, hard to start exercising
   - Gets winded quickly
   - Past workouts caused injuries

4. **Knowledge paralysis**
   - Has researched extensively
   - Too many conflicting approaches
   - Analysis paralysis prevents action

---

## What he actually needs

### Must-have
- Stupidly easy to start
- Works from home/desk
- Doesn't require equipment
- Builds slowly (no injuries)
- Zero decisions required

### Nice-to-have
- Integrates with calendar/work
- Accountability without judgment
- Back-pain specific exercises
- Quantified progress (engineer brain)

**Secondary JTBDs:**
- "Help me form a habit that actually sticks"
- "Don't require me to make decisions"
- "Start small enough that I won't get injured"

## Willingness to pay

- Currently pays: $50/month gym (unused), $15/month app subscriptions
- Would consider: $15-30/month for something effective
- Price sensitivity: Low (has disposable income)
- Value trigger: "If it actually got me moving consistently"

---

## Decision factors

**What would make him try:**
- Data-driven approach (speaks his language)
- Very low barrier to start
- Testimonials from other desk workers
- Free trial, skeptical of promises

**What would make him stay:**
- Actually did it for 2+ weeks
- Felt noticeably better
- Didn't require gym or equipment
- Fit into work schedule

**What would make him leave:**
- Required too much time
- Felt like a "fitness bro" app
- Missed few days, couldn't restart easily
- Aggravated back pain

---

## Messaging that resonates

**Yes:**
- "Designed for people who sit all day"
- "2 minutes to start"
- "No gym required"
- "Fix your back while you work"

**No:**
- "Transform your body"
- "Intense fat-burning workout"
- "Join the fitness community"
- "Push past your limits"

---

## User quotes (composite)

> "I've spent $500 on fitness stuff I don't use. I have the equipment. I have the knowledge. I just don't do it."

> "Every time I try to start exercising, my back flares up and I'm done for weeks."

> "The irony of being in tech is I can optimize anything except my own health."

> "I don't need another app that tells me to work out. I need something that makes it happen."

---

## Design implications

1. **Micro-movements** - 2-5 minute sessions max to start
2. **Desktop/calendar integration** - Meets him where he is
3. **Progressive difficulty** - Start embarrassingly easy
4. **Back-pain aware** - Exercises that help, not hurt
5. **Zero friction** - One tap to start
6. **Data/metrics** - Charts, progress, streaks
7. **Notification timing** - Work-schedule aware
8. **Recovery-friendly** - Easy to restart after breaks
