# Affiliate Best Practices

What separates top-earning affiliates from everyone else. Based on data from 500+ affiliates.

## The 80/20 of affiliate success

5% of affiliates generate 60% of revenue. Here's what they do differently.

### Top performer habits

1. **Post consistently** (minimum 2x/week)
2. **Show the app in action** (not just talk about it)
3. **Focus on one platform first** (master before expanding)
4. **Reply to every comment** (drives algorithm + conversions)
5. **Track what works** (double down on winners)

### Common mistakes

1. **One post and done** (affiliate marketing is a volume game)
2. **Generic "check out this app" content** (specific > vague)
3. **Hiding affiliate link** (make it obvious and easy to find)
4. **No disclosure** (FTC violation, trust killer)
5. **Ignoring analytics** (posting blind)

## Content that converts

### Best performing formats (ranked by conversion rate)

1. **Tutorial/how-to** (3.2% conversion rate)
   - Show yourself using a specific feature
   - Solve a real problem your audience has
   - 60-90 seconds ideal

2. **Before/after** (2.8% conversion rate)
   - Show the problem (frustration, manual process)
   - Show the solution (app in action)
   - End with result

3. **Day in my life** (2.4% conversion rate)
   - Natural integration into routine
   - Less "salesy" feel
   - Works best for lifestyle niches

4. **App comparison** (2.1% conversion rate)
   - "[App Name] vs [Competitor]"
   - Honest pros and cons
   - Builds trust through fairness

5. **List content** (1.8% conversion rate)
   - "5 apps I can't live without"
   - Position [App Name] in top 3
   - High engagement, moderate conversion

### Worst performing formats

1. **Static image with "link in bio"** (0.3% conversion rate)
2. **Generic "this app is great"** (0.4% conversion rate)
3. **Long-form without demonstration** (0.5% conversion rate)

### Hook formulas that work

**Curiosity gap:**
```
"This app does something no other [category] app does..."
"I've been using this for 3 months and here's what happened..."
"Most people don't know this exists..."
```

**Problem-focused:**
```
"Struggling with [problem]? This fixed it for me..."
"I wasted [X hours/dollars] before I found this..."
"If you [pain point], you need to see this..."
```

**Result-focused:**
```
"How I [achieved result] using one app..."
"This saved me [X hours] per week..."
"[Number]% improvement in [metric] since I started using this..."
```

**Contrarian:**
```
"I deleted [popular alternative] for this..."
"Unpopular opinion: [App Name] is better than [Competitor]..."
"Why I stopped using [old method]..."
```

## Platform-specific tactics

### TikTok

**What works:**
- Screen recordings with voiceover
- Face + screen split view
- Trending sounds (when relevant)
- 30-60 second videos
- Hook in first 2 seconds

**Post timing:**
- 7-9 AM, 12-3 PM, 7-9 PM local time
- Weekdays slightly better than weekends
- Post at least 1x/day during launch week

**Pro tips:**
- Reply to comments with video responses
- Use the "stitch" feature with related content
- Add text overlays (many watch without sound)
- End with soft CTA, don't beg for follows

### Instagram

**What works:**
- Reels (same content as TikTok)
- Carousels (educational content)
- Stories with poll/question stickers
- Pinned posts with link

**Post timing:**
- 11 AM - 1 PM, 7-9 PM
- Tuesday-Friday best engagement
- Stories: multiple per day

**Pro tips:**
- First line of caption = hook (cut off after ~125 chars)
- Include 3-5 relevant hashtags (not 30)
- Add link sticker to stories
- Create a highlight for "favorite apps"

### YouTube

**What works:**
- Full reviews (5-10 minutes)
- Comparison videos (vs competitor)
- "Best apps for [goal]" lists
- Tutorial walkthroughs

**Post timing:**
- Thursday-Sunday for upload
- 2-4 PM local time
- Consistency more important than perfect timing

**Pro tips:**
- Put affiliate link first in description
- Mention link verbally multiple times
- Use timestamps/chapters
- Strong thumbnail > clickbait title

### Twitter/X

**What works:**
- Thread breakdowns
- Quick tips with screenshot
- Hot takes on the niche
- Quote tweets of related content

**Post timing:**
- 8-10 AM, 12-1 PM, 5-6 PM
- Weekdays outperform weekends
- 3-5 tweets per day ideal

**Pro tips:**
- First tweet = complete thought + hook
- Include screenshot or video
- End thread with clear CTA
- Pin top-performing thread

### Blog/SEO

**What works:**
- "[App Name] review" posts
- "Best [category] apps" lists
- "[App Name] vs [Competitor]"
- "How to [goal] with [App Name]"

**Keywords to target:**
- [App Name] review
- [App Name] alternatives
- Best [category] apps [year]
- [Competitor] vs [App Name]
- Is [App Name] worth it

**Pro tips:**
- Target long-tail keywords (less competition)
- Include comparison tables
- Update posts annually
- Internal link to multiple app posts

## Optimization tactics

### A/B testing content

Test one variable at a time:

**Hooks:**
- Problem-focused vs result-focused
- Question vs statement
- Curiosity vs direct

**Format:**
- Face on camera vs screen only
- Voiceover vs text overlay
- Short (30s) vs long (60s)

**CTA:**
- "Link in bio" vs promo code
- Soft vs direct ask
- End of video vs mid-roll

Track clicks and conversions to find winners.

### Improving conversion rate

If you have clicks but no conversions:

1. **Check link is working** (sounds obvious, but test it)
2. **Audience mismatch** (are viewers actually in the target demo?)
3. **Weak CTA** (did you clearly tell them what to do?)
4. **Landing page issue** (screenshot and send to us)

If you have signups but no paid conversions:

1. **Free trial users need time** (wait 7-14 days)
2. **App/audience fit** (your audience may not be the paying type)
3. **Pricing objection** (use promo code more prominently)

### Scaling what works

When you find content that converts:

1. **Make variations** (same concept, different hook)
2. **Cross-post** (TikTok to Reels to Shorts)
3. **Respond to comments** (boosts algorithm, answers objections)
4. **Create series** (part 2, part 3, etc.)
5. **Update and repost** (after 3-6 months, same content can work again)

## Mindset and consistency

### Volume matters

Reality: Most content won't go viral. That's normal.

Top affiliates post 50-100 pieces before finding what works. Then they scale the winners.

**Numbers game:**
- 100 posts
- 10 get decent traction
- 3 drive significant clicks
- 1 becomes your "template" for future content

### Long-term thinking

Affiliate income compounds:

- Month 1: Learning, minimal earnings
- Month 3: Found what works, $100-300
- Month 6: Scaled winners, $500-1,000
- Month 12: Consistent, $1,000-3,000

Plus: recurring commissions stack. A customer you referred 6 months ago still earns you money.

### Staying motivated

When results are slow:

1. Check your analytics (small wins exist even without sales)
2. Focus on content quality, not just affiliate content
3. Engage with community (our Discord, other affiliates)
4. Set process goals (X posts/week) not just outcome goals

## Common questions

**Q: How long until I make my first sale?**
Average: 2-4 weeks of consistent posting. Some get it in days, some take 6 weeks.

**Q: Should I promote on multiple platforms?**
Start with one. Master it. Then expand. Spreading thin = mediocre results everywhere.

**Q: Can I be an affiliate for competing products?**
Yes. Comparison content works well. Be honest about pros/cons.

**Q: How do I handle negative comments?**
Respond professionally. Acknowledge concerns. Don't delete (looks bad).

**Q: What if my content flops?**
Normal. Post again. Iterate. 90% of content underperforms, 10% makes up for it.
