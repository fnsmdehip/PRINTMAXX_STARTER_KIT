# YouTuber Pitch Templates

Email templates for recruiting YouTube creators as affiliates. Focus on reviewers and niche educators.

---

## Why target YouTubers

- Video content is highly persuasive
- Long-form reviews = high intent viewers
- Evergreen content (videos rank for years)
- Multiple monetization touchpoints (description, pinned comment, end screen)
- Shorts for additional reach
- Often have cross-platform presence

---

## Finding YouTuber contacts

### Search strategies

1. **YouTube search:**
   - "best [category] apps"
   - "[competitor] review"
   - "[niche] tutorial"
   - Filter by upload date (recent = active creator)

2. **Target channel sizes:**
   - 1k-50k subscribers (responsive, not overloaded)
   - Check recent videos for views (active audience)
   - Look for consistent upload schedule

3. **Finding email:**
   - About page > Business inquiries email
   - Description box > Often includes email
   - Social links > Check Twitter/IG bio
   - Website link > Contact page

### Qualification checklist

- [ ] Uploads at least 2x per month
- [ ] Videos get 500+ views minimum
- [ ] Content relevant to your niche
- [ ] Has business email listed
- [ ] Recent sponsorships (knows the model)
- [ ] Professional quality content

## Video format suggestions

### When they ask "what should I make?"

Suggest based on their channel style:

**Review channels:**
```
Full review video (5-10 min)
- Overview of what [App Name] does
- Key features walkthrough
- Pros and cons
- Who it's for
- Verdict
```

**Tutorial channels:**
```
How-to video (5-15 min)
- "How to [achieve goal] with [App Name]"
- Step-by-step walkthrough
- Tips and tricks
- Results
```

**Comparison channels:**
```
Comparison video (8-12 min)
- "[App Name] vs [Competitor]"
- Side-by-side features
- Pricing comparison
- Which to choose
```

**Shorts/quick content:**
```
30-60 second demo
- Hook: Problem or curiosity
- Demo: App in action
- Result: Outcome
- CTA: Link in description
```

## What to provide YouTubers

### Assets package

- High-res app icon and logo
- Screenshots of key features
- Screen recordings (if allowed for B-roll)
- Feature list with descriptions
- Talking points (key benefits, differentiators)
- Comparison data vs competitors
- FAQ answers

### Sponsorship package (if paying flat fee)

Include:
- Talking points (required mentions)
- Link/code requirements
- Disclosure requirements
- Draft approval process (if needed)
- Payment terms

### Affiliate-only package

Include:
- Affiliate link
- Custom promo code
- Commission rate confirmation
- Cookie duration
- Asset links
- Support contact

## Negotiating rates

### If they ask for flat fee

**Budget option:**
```
"We're early stage, so doing affiliate-only right now. But 30% commission with no cap means unlimited upside for you. Some of our YouTubers make $[X]/month consistently."
```

**Hybrid option:**
```
"How about $[X] flat fee + 20% affiliate ongoing? Covers your production costs and you keep earning as long as the video is up."
```

**Full sponsorship:**
```
"What's your typical rate for a dedicated video? We can discuss."

Typical ranges (rough):
- 1k-10k subs: $100-500
- 10k-50k subs: $500-2,000
- 50k-100k subs: $2,000-5,000
- 100k+ subs: $5,000+
```

### Rate negotiation tips

- Ask for their rate card first
- Factor in CPM (cost per thousand views)
- Consider affiliate potential over time
- Smaller channels often negotiate more
