# Podcast Pitch Templates

Outreach templates for recruiting podcasters as affiliates. Focus on niche shows with engaged audiences.

---

## Why target podcasters

- Highly engaged, loyal audiences
- Host endorsements carry weight (parasocial relationship)
- Ad reads/mentions feel native
- Evergreen content (episodes live forever)
- Often undermonetized (open to affiliate deals)
- Cross-promotion potential (newsletter, social)

---

## Finding podcaster contacts

### Discovery methods

1. **Apple Podcasts / Spotify:**
   - Search [niche] keywords
   - Check "You might also like" suggestions
   - Look at chart rankings by category

2. **Podcast directories:**
   - Listen Notes (podcast search engine)
   - Podchaser (contact info, stats)
   - Rephonic (audience data)

3. **Target show sizes:**
   - 500-10k downloads/episode (responsive, not oversaturated)
   - Check social following as proxy
   - Look for consistent episode schedule

4. **Finding contact:**
   - Show website > Contact/Sponsor page
   - Show notes > Often include email
   - Social media bios
   - LinkedIn (search host name)

### Qualification checklist

- [ ] Active (new episodes in last month)
- [ ] Relevant niche to your app
- [ ] Has sponsor/contact page
- [ ] Some social following
- [ ] Professional audio quality
- [ ] Accepts sponsors (check show notes)

## Email templates

### Template 1: Sponsorship inquiry

**Subject:** Sponsorship inquiry for [Podcast Name]

```
Hi [Host Name],

I'm a regular listener of [Podcast Name] - especially enjoyed your recent episode with [guest] about [topic]. [Specific detail].

I'm reaching out about a potential sponsorship for [App Name], a [category] app that [one-line description].

Given your audience of [their audience description], I think there's a strong fit here.

Options I'm considering:

1. Mid-roll ad read (30-60 seconds)
2. Host endorsement (you actually try the app)
3. Affiliate-only (commission per signup, no upfront cost)

Would love to hear about your sponsorship packages and find something that works for both of us.

[Your name]
Founder, [App Name]
[Link]
```

### Template 2: Affiliate-only pitch

**Subject:** Affiliate partnership - [App Name] x [Podcast Name]

```
Hi [Host Name],

Love [Podcast Name]. Your episode on [topic] was exactly what I needed to hear.

Quick pitch: I run [App Name], and I think your listeners would genuinely benefit from it.

Here's what I'm proposing:

- You get free lifetime premium access
- Mention [App Name] when relevant (natural integration)
- Custom code/link for your listeners (they save 10%)
- You earn 30% commission on every signup

No upfront cost to you. Authentic mention only if you actually like the app.

Interested in trying it?

[Your name]
[App Name]
[Link]
```

### Template 3: Guest appearance pitch

**Use when:** You have expertise to share as a guest

**Subject:** Guest pitch: [Topic you can speak on]

```
Hi [Host Name],

[Podcast Name] is one of my favorites for [reason]. Your conversational style makes complex topics accessible.

I'd love to be a guest on the show to discuss [topic you can speak on]. Quick background: I'm [Your name], founder of [App Name], and I've [relevant credential or experience].

Topics I can cover:
- [Topic 1]
- [Topic 2]
- [Topic 3]

I come prepared, keep it conversational, and happy to promote the episode to my audience of [your reach if notable].

Would this be a fit for your show?

[Your name]
[One-line bio]
[Link]
```

### Template 4: Existing sponsor replacement

**Use when:** They've had sponsors in your category before

**Subject:** Re: [Category] sponsor for [Podcast Name]

```
Hi [Host Name],

Noticed you've worked with [previous sponsor in your category] before. If you're open to new sponsors in that space, I'd like to throw [App Name] in the ring.

Quick comparison:

[App Name] vs [Previous Sponsor]:
- [Advantage 1]
- [Advantage 2]
- [Advantage 3]

Same commission structure or happy to discuss what worked before.

Worth a conversation?

[Your name]
[App Name]
```

## Ad read scripts

Provide these to podcasters who ask "what should I say?"

### Host-read ad (60 seconds)

```
Before we continue, quick word from [App Name].

If you struggle with [problem], you'll want to hear this. [App Name] is a [category] app that [main benefit].

I've been using it for [timeframe] and here's what I like: [specific feature]. It's replaced [old solution] for me.

[Personal anecdote if genuine - 1-2 sentences]

Try it free at [URL] or use code [CODE] for 10% off. Link in the show notes.

Back to the show.
```

### Host-read ad (30 seconds)

```
Quick shout-out to [App Name], a [category] app I've been using.

If you deal with [problem], check it out. [Key feature] has been a game-changer for me.

Try it free at [URL]. Use code [CODE] for 10% off. Link in the show notes.
```

### Native mention (baked into content)

```
[During relevant discussion]

"...speaking of [topic], I've been using [App Name] for this exact thing. It's a [description] and it [benefit]. I'll put a link in the show notes if you want to check it out."
```

## Podcast sponsorship pricing

### Industry benchmarks

Pricing typically based on downloads per episode:

| Downloads/episode | CPM (cost per thousand) | Episode rate |
|-------------------|-------------------------|--------------|
| 500-2k | $15-25 | $50-250 |
| 2k-5k | $20-30 | $200-500 |
| 5k-10k | $20-30 | $400-800 |
| 10k-25k | $25-40 | $800-1,500 |
| 25k-50k | $25-40 | $1,500-3,000 |
| 50k+ | $30-50 | $3,000+ |

### Negotiation tips

**If they quote high rates:**
```
"That's a bit above our budget right now. Would you consider a hybrid - lower upfront + 25% affiliate commission? Could work out better long-term."
```

**If they don't know their rate:**
```
"What are you thinking? We typically do $[X] per episode plus affiliate, but open to discussing."
```

**Test episode offer:**
```
"How about we try one episode at [reduced rate] and if it performs well, we lock in a longer deal at your full rate?"
```

## Podcast categories to target

### High-fit categories

**Fitness apps:**
- Health & fitness podcasts
- Running/cycling shows
- Nutrition podcasts
- Personal development (overlaps)

**Faith apps:**
- Christian podcasts
- Faith & spirituality
- Marriage/family (if faith-based)
- Pastoral leadership

**Productivity/AI apps:**
- Business podcasts
- Entrepreneurship shows
- Tech commentary
- Work-life balance
- Solopreneur focused

### Finding niche shows

Use Listen Notes search:
1. Enter niche keywords
2. Filter by recent activity
3. Sort by relevance
4. Check episode counts (50+ = established)
5. Note contact info

## What to provide podcasters

### Sponsor package

- Talking points (key benefits, differentiator)
- Custom URL and promo code
- Product access for personal use
- Asset files (logo for show notes)
- Approval process (if needed)
- Payment terms

### For host endorsements

- Free premium access
- Onboarding call (show them the app)
- FAQ document for questions
- Direct support contact
- Flexibility on messaging (authentic > scripted)

## Measuring podcast performance

### Attribution challenges

Podcast listeners often don't click immediately. Track:
- Unique promo codes
- Custom URLs (yourapp.com/podcastname)
- "How did you hear about us?" survey
- Spike correlation with episode release

### Success metrics

- Promo code usage within 7 days of episode
- Landing page visits from podcast URL
- Long-term brand recall (survey)
- Multi-touch attribution if available
