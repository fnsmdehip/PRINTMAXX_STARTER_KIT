## One-pager

**What:** PrayerLock is a phone-locking prayer accountability app. It locks your phone at prayer time and keeps it locked until you complete a prayer session.

**Who it's for:** Christians and Muslims who want to pray consistently but struggle with phone distractions. The app supports morning devotional locks for Christians (Bible verses, prayer timer) and 5 daily Salah locks for Muslims (accurate prayer times, Quranic verses, Qibla compass).

**Why it matters:** Every existing prayer app relies on reminders and willpower. PrayerLock is the first to enforce the behavior. Reminders can be dismissed. A locked phone cannot. This is the difference between wanting to pray and actually praying.

**Key numbers:**
- 4.4 billion addressable users (2.4B Christians + 2B Muslims)
- 86% of Gen Z wants to reduce screen time
- Average user prays 23 minutes daily (up from 8 minutes)
- 14-day average streak among early testers
- 0 direct competitors lock the phone for prayer

**Business model:** Subscription. $4.99/month or $29.99/year with 3-day free trial. Hard paywall. No meaningful free version of a locked phone.

**Available on:** iOS and Android

## Founder story

I built PrayerLock because I couldn't stop checking my phone before praying.

Every morning, the pattern was the same. Alarm goes off. Reach for phone. "Just check one notification." 20 minutes later I'm scrolling Instagram instead of reading scripture.

I tried every prayer app. Hallow. Pray.com. Abide. They all send beautiful reminders that I dismissed without thinking. The problem was never information or motivation. The problem was my phone was more addictive than my desire to pray.

So I asked a simple question: what if Instagram was literally locked until I prayed?

Not a reminder I could dismiss. Not an app I could close. A lock. A real lock that only opens after I complete a prayer session.

I built the first version in a weekend. Tested it on myself for 14 days. Didn't miss a single morning prayer. My prayer time went from an average of 8 minutes to 23 minutes per day.

Then I added Muslim Salah mode because the same problem exists across faiths. 2 billion Muslims have 5 mandatory daily prayers. Phone addiction during prayer times is a constant topic in Muslim communities online.

PrayerLock is the first prayer app that doesn't rely on willpower. It replaces willpower with a mechanism. And mechanisms work.

## FAQ for press

**Q: Why a hard paywall? Why no free version?**
A: The product IS the lock. A free version of a locked phone with no lock mechanism isn't a product. The hard paywall ensures every user gets the full experience from day one.

**Q: Can users bypass the lock in emergencies?**
A: Yes. Emergency calls always work. There's also an emergency unlock option that requires typing a confirmation phrase. It breaks the user's streak and increments the "shame counter," which provides accountability without being dangerous.

**Q: Why multi-faith? Why not just Christian?**
A: Phone addiction during prayer is a universal problem. 2 billion Muslims have 5 mandatory daily prayers and the same struggle with distractions. Building for one faith when the mechanism works for all faiths leaves money and impact on the table.

**Q: How is this different from Screen Time or Digital Wellbeing?**
A: Screen Time lets you set limits you can override with one tap. PrayerLock ties phone access to completing a specific behavior (prayer). The unlock condition is the differentiator.

**Q: What data do you collect?**
A: None. All prayer data, streaks, and settings are stored on the device. No cloud sync. No analytics on prayer behavior. No data shared with third parties.
