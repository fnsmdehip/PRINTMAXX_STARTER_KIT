```
Download link in comments.
```

### LinkedIn 2 (behavioral design)

```
Most surprising finding from WalkToUnlock's first week:

Users who set the LOWEST step goals (200 steps) have the HIGHEST retention.

Why? Low barrier = they actually start. Once they start, they increase the goal themselves.

Day 1: 200 steps (2 minutes to complete)
Day 7: 500 steps
Day 14: 1,000 steps
Day 30: 3,000 steps

The habit scales because the phone addiction provides the motivation. You don't need to "want to walk." You need to want your phone. The walk is the price.

This is behavioral design 101: make the desired behavior the path of least resistance. Don't compete with the addiction. Use it.
```

### LinkedIn 3 (market)

```
The walking app market is about to get interesting.

Sweatcoin: 170M users, token crashed 83%, downloads fell 60%. Users leaving.
Strava: Great for runners. Walkers aren't runners.
Fitbit: Hardware-dependent. Data-focused. Not behavior-changing.

The gap: an app that CREATES walking behavior instead of tracking it.

WalkToUnlock fills this gap. Your phone locks until you walk. The incentive is your phone, not points or tokens or badges.

Walking is the lowest-barrier fitness activity. No gym. No equipment. Any age. Any location. Any fitness level.

3.1 billion smartphone users worldwide. All of them pick up their phone 80+ times per day. That's 80 potential micro-walks.

We don't need everyone to walk 10,000 steps. We need everyone to walk 2,000 more steps than they currently do. That's 20 minutes.

WalkToUnlock makes those 20 minutes non-optional.
```
