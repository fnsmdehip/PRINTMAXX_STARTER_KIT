## FAQ section

**Section headline:**
Common questions.

**Q1: What if I can't walk due to injury or disability?**
The app tracks steps from any movement your phone detects. If you use a wheelchair or have limited mobility, the step count adapts. You can also set a very low goal. The principle still works: move to unlock.

**Q2: Can I bypass the lock if there's an emergency?**
Phone calls and texts always work. Essential apps (maps, banking, email) can be excluded from the block. Only entertainment apps get locked.

**Q3: What stops me from just deleting the app?**
Nothing. But if you're the type to delete it to scroll, you probably wouldn't download it in the first place. The app works for people who want accountability.

**Q4: Does it sync with my fitness tracker?**
Yes. WalkToUnlock syncs with Apple Health and Google Fit. Steps from your Apple Watch, Fitbit, or Garmin count automatically.

**Q5: What if I want to use an app before I walk?**
You can't. That's the point. If you could bypass it, the app wouldn't work. The block is absolute until you hit your goal.

**Q6: How much does it cost?**
The app is free with basic features (3 app blocks, 1 step goal). WalkToUnlock Pro is $4.99/month or $39.99/year and includes unlimited blocks, multiple goals, and family accounts.

**Q7: What if I miss my goal one day?**
Your streak resets, but you start fresh the next day. One missed day doesn't erase progress. Set realistic goals you can actually hit.

**Q8: Can I set different goals for weekdays vs weekends?**
Yes. Pro users can set different step goals for each day of the week. Lower on rest days. Higher on active days.

**Q9: Is there a family plan?**
Yes. The Family Plan ($5.99/month) covers up to 6 people. Parents can manage settings for kids.

**Q10: How is this different from Apple's Screen Time?**
Screen Time lets you ignore the limit. WalkToUnlock doesn't. There's no "ignore for 15 minutes" button. Walk or stay locked.
