## Thread 2: Why app limits don't work

**Post 1 (Hook):**
You've tried app limits.

They don't work.

Here's why (and what actually does):

**Post 2:**
App limits give you a warning.

Then you press "Ignore Limit."

Then you feel guilty.

Repeat tomorrow.

I did this for 2 years.

**Post 3:**
The problem: your future self doesn't care about your past self's goals.

When you set the limit, you're motivated.

When you hit the limit, you're in a different mental state.

**Post 4:**
This is called "hot-cold empathy gap."

Cold state: "I should scroll less"
Hot state: "I need to check this one thing"

Your hot state always wins.

**Post 5:**
Apple and Google know this.

They designed limits to feel like accountability.

But they made them easy to bypass.

Because they make money when you keep scrolling.

**Post 6:**
What actually works: removing the option.

Not a warning. A full block.

You literally cannot access the app.

No button to ignore.

**Post 7:**
This is what PrayerLock does.

You set which apps get blocked.

You set what action unlocks them (prayer, devotional reading).

The apps stay locked until you complete it.

**Post 8:**
The first 3 days are uncomfortable.

You reach for apps that aren't available.

Your brain is looking for the dopamine hit.

It won't find it.

**Post 9:**
After about a week, something shifts.

The urge to check decreases.

You start to enjoy the quiet.

The prayer becomes the reward.

**Post 10:**
If app limits worked, you wouldn't still be struggling.

Try removing the option entirely.

Even just for a week.

See what happens.

**Post 1 (Hook):**

**Post 2:**

Wife on phone.

Everyone in the same room, no one present.

Lasted 2 days.

We ALL have PrayerLock installed.

Morning devotional required before any entertainment apps.

- Family actually talks at breakfast

- Morning chaos reduced

If your family mornings are screen-dominated, try this:
