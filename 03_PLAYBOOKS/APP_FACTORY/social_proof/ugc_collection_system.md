# UGC collection system

How to encourage, collect, and repurpose user-generated content.

---

## Why UGC matters

**Benefits:**
- More authentic than brand content
- Costs nothing to produce (users create it)
- Builds community and loyalty
- Provides endless marketing material
- Social proof at scale
- Better engagement than polished brand content

**Types of UGC:**
- Screenshots of results
- Before/after comparisons
- Workflow demonstrations
- Creative uses of product
- Testimonial posts
- Tutorial content
- Reaction videos
- Memes (yes, really)

---

## How to encourage user content

### 1. Make it easy to share

**In-app share features:**
```
[Achievement screen]

You just [accomplished X]!

[Share to Twitter] [Share to LinkedIn] [Copy image]
```

**Pre-written share text:**
```
Just hit [milestone] with @[AppHandle]!

[Metric/achievement]

#[AppHashtag]
```

**Shareable assets:**
- Auto-generate result graphics
- Create branded templates users can fill
- Provide high-quality screenshots
- Make achievements visually shareable

### 2. Celebrate users publicly

**When users share organically:**
- Like and comment immediately
- Repost to your Stories
- Quote tweet with gratitude
- Feature in your newsletter
- Add to "Wall of Love" page

**Monthly/weekly features:**
- "User of the week" spotlight
- "Best use case" roundup
- "Community wins" post
- Newsletter section for user content

### 3. Prompt specific content

**Ask for specific formats:**
- "Share your setup/workspace"
- "Show us your before/after"
- "What's your workflow?"
- "Screen record how you use [feature]"

**Template prompts:**
```
Fill in the blank:

"Before [App Name]: ___________
After [App Name]: ___________"

Tag us @[handle] with your answer!
```

### 4. Lower the barrier

**Make it feel small:**
- "Takes 30 seconds"
- "Just screenshot and post"
- "One sentence is enough"

**Provide structure:**
- Pre-written templates
- Fill-in-the-blank formats
- Specific questions to answer
- Clear instructions

## Hashtag strategies

### Primary brand hashtag

**Format:** #[AppName] or #[AppName]Users

**Requirements:**
- Short enough to type easily
- Not used by other brands
- Not a common word
- Easy to spell

**Promote it:**
- Include in bio
- Add to email signatures
- Use in all your posts
- Include in automated share text

### Campaign hashtags

**Format:** #[AppName][Campaign]

**Examples:**
- #[AppName]Results
- #[AppName]Challenge
- #[AppName]Tips
- #[AppName]Story

**Use cases:**
- Limited-time campaigns
- Contest entries
- Specific content themes
- Feature launches

### Community hashtags

**Format:** #[Community/Audience]

**Examples:**
- #SolopreneurLife (if your users are solopreneurs)
- #IndieHackers
- #DesignerProblems

**Why:** Tap into existing communities your users belong to.

## Contest ideas

### Results contest

**Format:** Best results wins a prize

**Rules:**
- Share your [metric] improvement
- Include before/after if possible
- Tag @[handle] and use #[hashtag]
- Must be real, verifiable results

**Prize ideas:**
- 1 year free subscription
- Lifetime deal
- Feature on your homepage
- 1-on-1 call with founder
- Merch bundle

### Creative use contest

**Format:** Most creative/unexpected use wins

**Rules:**
- Show how you use [App Name] differently
- Video or screenshot
- Explain your workflow

**Why it works:** Surfaces use cases you didn't know about

### Challenge campaign

**Format:** Complete a specific challenge

**Example:**
```
7-Day [App Name] Challenge

Day 1: Set up your dashboard
Day 2: Complete 10 tasks
Day 3: Share your progress
...
Day 7: Post your results with #[Challenge]
```

**Why it works:** Engagement + habit building + content generation

### Referral contest

**Format:** Most referrals wins

**Structure:**
- Track referrals with unique codes
- Weekly/monthly leaderboard
- Prizes for top referrers
- Everyone gets something small

## Content repurposing rights

### Getting permission

**Explicit opt-in (best):**
```
In-app or email:

"Can we share your post on our channels? We'd love to feature your results."

[ ] Yes, with my name
[ ] Yes, but anonymously
[ ] No thanks
```

**Terms of service approach:**
Include in ToS that user-generated content shared publicly with your hashtag may be reposted. BUT always ask anyway for goodwill.

**Reply/DM approach:**
```
Hey [Name]! Love this post. Mind if we share it to our followers? Happy to credit you (or keep you anonymous if you prefer).
```

### Permission tracking

Keep a database:
| Content | User | Date | Permission | Type | Used |
|---------|------|------|------------|------|------|
| Screenshot | @user | 1/15 | Yes, named | Tweet | Yes |
| Video | @user2 | 1/18 | Yes, anon | TikTok | No |

### What permission covers

**Ask specifically for:**
- Social media reposting
- Website/landing page use
- Paid advertising
- Email marketing
- Sales materials
- Case studies

Different uses may need different permissions. Don't assume one "yes" covers everything.

**How to encourage:**
- Make results visually appealing
- Add "Share" button next to achievements
- Create branded screenshot templates

**Collection:**
- Monitor hashtag

- "Show your workflow" prompts
- Reaction video requests

**Collection:**
- Monitor TikTok/Reels/YouTube

- Post-milestone email request

**Collection:**
- Support conversations
- Social media monitoring

- Offer affiliate/referral credit

**Collection:**

- Monitor community forums

## Organizing UGC

### Tagging system

Tag each piece of content:
- Content type (screenshot, video, quote)
- User tier (beta, free, paid, enterprise)
- Use case (specific feature or workflow)
- Permission level (social, website, ads, all)
- Quality (A, B, C)
- Platform source (Twitter, TikTok, email)

### Storage

**Simple:**
- Folder structure by type
- Spreadsheet for tracking
- Cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox)

**Advanced:**
- Notion database
- Airtable base
- Dedicated UGC platform (Yotpo, TINT)

### Refresh cycle

- Review UGC library monthly
- Archive outdated content (old UI, irrelevant)
- Identify gaps (need more video? enterprise users?)
- Plan collection campaigns for gaps
- Rotate featured content quarterly

## Using UGC in marketing

### Social media

**Best practices:**
- Credit original creator
- Get permission first
- Use consistent visual treatment
- Mix UGC with original content (60/40)
- Respond to comments tagging creator

### Landing pages

**Placement:**
- Hero section (short, punchy testimonial)
- Feature sections (relevant use case)
- Pricing page (value testimonials)
- Footer (trust signals)

**Format:**
- Quote cards with photos
- Video embeds
- Screenshot galleries
- Scrolling testimonial wall

### Ads

**UGC performs better because:**
- Looks native/organic
- More trustworthy
- Less "ad-like"
- Higher engagement

**Ad formats:**
- Screenshot of positive tweet/review
- User video clips
- Quote over product demo
- Before/after comparisons

**Requirements:**
- Must have explicit ad permission
- Disclose if paid/incentivized
- Keep original authentic feel

### Email marketing

**Uses:**
- Welcome sequence (social proof)
- Feature announcements (user reactions)
- Re-engagement (what others achieved)
- Sales emails (testimonials)

## FTC compliance for UGC

### Required disclosures

**If user received anything:**
- Free product
- Discount
- Payment
- Entry in contest
- Featured on your platform

**Disclosure format:**
Must be clear and conspicuous. "Thanks [Name] for sharing! [Name] received a free account."

### When repurposing

- Keep original context intact
- Don't edit to change meaning
- Include any disclosures from original
- Add your own disclosure if incentivized

### Contest rules

- Clear entry requirements
- Prizes disclosed upfront
- Selection criteria transparent
- No purchase necessary (where legally required)
- Follow platform-specific contest rules

## Measuring UGC success

### Metrics to track

| Metric | How to measure |
|--------|----------------|
| UGC volume | Monthly count of tagged content |
| Hashtag usage | Track via social monitoring |
| Repurpose rate | % of collected UGC used |
| Permission rate | % who say yes when asked |
| Engagement | Likes/comments on shared UGC |
| Conversion | Sales attributed to UGC content |

### A/B testing

Test UGC vs. brand content:
- Ad creative performance
- Landing page conversion
- Email click rates
- Social engagement

UGC usually wins. Measure by how much.

## UGC collection calendar

**Weekly:**
- Monitor hashtag and mentions
- Respond to and save new content
- Request permissions for best pieces
- Feature 1-2 pieces on social

**Monthly:**
- Outreach campaign to power users
- Update UGC library
- Refresh featured testimonials
- Analyze performance metrics

**Quarterly:**
- Major collection campaign or contest
- Audit permissions and renewals
- Plan next quarter's UGC themes
- Update UGC strategy based on data
