# Case study template

Framework for creating detailed customer success stories.

---

## When to create a case study

Good candidates:
- Users with measurable results
- Interesting or relatable use cases
- Users willing to be public
- Results you can replicate for others
- Notable companies or roles (adds credibility)

Skip if:
- Results aren't impressive enough to publish
- User wants to stay anonymous
- Use case is too niche to be relatable
- You can't verify the claims

---

## Case study structure

### 1. The hook (headline + summary)

**Headline formula:**
"How [Customer] [achieved result] with [Product]"

**Examples:**
- "How Sarah reduced her weekly admin time from 8 hours to 2"
- "How a solo founder scaled to $10k MRR without hiring"
- "How TechCorp cut onboarding time by 60%"

**Summary box:**
```
CUSTOMER: [Name/Company]
INDUSTRY: [Their industry]
USE CASE: [Primary use case]
KEY RESULT: [One headline metric]
```

### 2. The challenge (the before)

What problem were they facing?

**Write about:**
- Their situation and context
- The specific pain point
- What it was costing them (time, money, frustration)
- What they tried before (and why it didn't work)

**Example:**
```
Sarah Chen runs a freelance design studio. Between client work and admin tasks, she was working 60-hour weeks.

"I was spending almost a full day every week just on invoicing, scheduling, and follow-ups. That's billable time I was losing."

She tried spreadsheets. She tried other tools. Nothing stuck because the setup time was too high.
```

### 3. The solution (the discovery)

How did they find and implement your product?

**Write about:**
- How they discovered your product
- What made them decide to try it
- The implementation/onboarding experience
- Any initial obstacles and how they overcame them

**Example:**
```
Sarah found [App Name] through a recommendation on Twitter.

"I was skeptical because I'd tried other tools. But the setup took 15 minutes instead of a weekend. That was the difference."

She started with just invoicing. Within a week, she was using it for scheduling and client communication too.
```

### 4. The results (the after)

What changed? Be specific with metrics.

**Write about:**
- Quantifiable results (hours saved, money earned, efficiency gains)
- Qualitative improvements (less stress, better work-life balance)
- Unexpected benefits
- Timeline (how long to see results)

**Example:**
```
After 3 months with [App Name]:

- Weekly admin time: 8 hours → 2 hours
- Invoices sent on time: 60% → 100%
- Client response time: 48 hours → 12 hours

"I got 6 hours of my week back. That's an extra client project per week, which pays for the tool 10x over."

The unexpected benefit: better client relationships. "Clients started commenting that I seemed more organized. They were right."
```

### 5. Key features used

What specific features drove the results?

**Format:**
```
FEATURE: [Feature name]
HOW THEY USE IT: [Specific workflow]
RESULT: [What it changed]
```

**Example:**
```
FEATURE: Automated invoicing
HOW THEY USE IT: Invoices auto-generate when projects are marked complete
RESULT: Zero late invoices, no manual data entry

FEATURE: Client portal
HOW THEY USE IT: Clients self-schedule and access project updates
RESULT: 80% fewer scheduling emails
```

### 6. The recommendation (the testimonial)

Let them speak directly to potential customers.

**Pull quote:**
```
"If you're losing time to admin work, try [App Name]. Setup is quick, and you'll see results in the first week."
- Sarah Chen, Freelance Designer
```

## Interview questions for case studies

### Background
1. Tell me about your role and what you do day-to-day.
2. What does your team/company look like?

### The problem
3. What problem were you trying to solve?
4. How long had this been an issue?
5. What did this problem cost you? (Time, money, frustration)
6. What did you try before?
7. Why didn't those solutions work?

### Discovery and decision
8. How did you find [App Name]?
9. What made you decide to try it?
10. Were there any hesitations? What overcame them?

### Implementation
11. What was the setup process like?
12. How long until you were fully up and running?
13. Any challenges during implementation?

### Results
14. What's different now compared to before?
15. Can you quantify the impact? (Hours saved, money saved, etc.)
16. Were there any unexpected benefits?
17. How long did it take to see results?

### Features
18. What features do you use most?
19. What's your favorite thing about [App Name]?
20. What would you miss most if you had to stop using it?

### Recommendation
21. Who would you recommend [App Name] to?
22. What would you tell someone considering it?
23. Anything else you'd want potential users to know?

## Writing guidelines

### Voice
- Write in third person (about them, not "you")
- Use their actual words in quotes
- Be specific, not vague
- Focus on results, not features
- Make it about their story, not your product

### Length
- Short: 200-300 words
- Medium: 500-800 words
- Long: 1000-1500 words

### Formatting
- Use headers to break up sections
- Bold key metrics
- Pull quotes for emphasis
- Include photos where possible
- Add a summary box at the top

### What to avoid
- Promotional language
- Exaggerated claims
- Feature dumps without context
- Generic praise ("It's great!")
- Unverifiable statements

## Approval process

1. **Interview** - Record the conversation
2. **Draft** - Write first version from transcript
3. **Internal review** - Check accuracy and claims
4. **Customer review** - Send to customer for approval
5. **Revisions** - Make requested changes
6. **Final approval** - Get written sign-off
7. **Publish** - Only after written approval

### Approval email

```
Subject: Your [App Name] case study - review needed

Hey [Name],

Here's the draft of your case study. Please check:

- Are all facts accurate?
- Are you comfortable with the quotes used?
- Is your name/title/company correct?
- Anything you'd like added or removed?

[Link to draft]

Let me know any changes, or reply "approved" if it's good to go.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

## Distribution

### On your site
- Dedicated case study page
- Link from relevant feature pages
- Include in testimonials section
- Add to sales/pricing page

### In marketing
- Email to prospects considering purchase
- Share on social media
- Include in sales decks
- Reference in blog posts

### In ads
- Pull key metrics for ad copy
- Use quotes in social ads
- Create video versions for YouTube/social

---

## FTC compliance

- All claims must be accurate and verifiable
- Disclose any material connection (free product, payment, referral relationship)
- Results should be typical OR include disclaimer
- Get written permission before publishing
- Disclose if case study subject is an investor, employee, or has other relationship
