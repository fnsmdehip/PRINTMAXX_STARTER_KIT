## The 3-Minute Ad Framework

### Why Speed Wins

1. **Ugly ads perform best** - "Those ugly ads you could design yourself in 3 minutes are usually the ones that perform best. Not bc their design is so great, but bc they simply don't look like ads. People like that."

2. **Testing velocity beats polish** - Create 10 ugly ads in 30 minutes instead of 1 polished ad in 5 hours. More tests = faster winning creative discovery.

3. **Native = invisible** - "It's not about design here. The ad is supposed to look 'native'."

### The Workflow

1. Write the copy first (see principles below)
2. Find/generate a simple image (Nano Banana Pro, stock photos, AI)
3. Add headline text overlay
4. Launch and test
5. Total time: 3 minutes per creative

## Core Copywriting Principles

### Framework 1: The Third-Person Story Method
**From the $45k/7 days ad post (2K views)**

**1. Third-person storytelling**
- No command. No promise. Just a story about someone else.
- Stories lower resistance. We lean in instead of defend.
- Example: "She Added Gelatin to Her Routine - Now Her Clothes Don't Fit the Same"

**2. Tiny input, big output**
- The ad doesn't say she went on the treadmill for 2h per day. She just ate gelatin.
- The smaller the ask, the lower the resistance to try it yourself.
- Minimize the perceived effort required.

**3. Implied transformation**
- "Clothes don't fit the same" doesn't say she lost weight. Doesn't give a number.
- Makes your brain fill in the result.
- Let imagination do the selling.

**Key insight:** "Stop selling the product. Sell someone else's result. This will build trust and authority. Bc if they were able to do it, why can't you??"

---

### Framework 2: The Pattern Disruption Method
**From the $45k ad analysis**

**1. Pattern disruption**
- "Blood sugar is not from sweets!" challenges what everyone believes.
- When you break an assumption, you own their attention.
- Question their beliefs, and you'll have their attention.

**2. Enemy creation**
- Not a list of causes why you're at fault. One villain.
- Our brains love a single target to blame.
- Give them an enemy, then offer the weapon.

**3. Curiosity through the unknown**
- The ad could imply cashews are the reason for high blood sugar.
- But does it actually? To find out, you gotta click...
- Leave the loop open.

**Key insight:** "To be successful on any kind of advertising platform, we need to hook the audience. This is best done by challenging common beliefs."

---

### Framework 3: The Callout + Number + Loop Method
**From "Native Ads are the most overlooked advertising channel in 2026"**

**1. Callout targeting**
- "Attention, diabetics!" No guessing who this is for.
- The right people stop. Everyone else scrolls.
- Be specific about WHO this is for.

**2. Specific number = believability**
- "Below 4.9", not "lower blood sugar."
- A precise target. Specificity builds trust before they even click.
- Numbers feel real. Vague claims feel like marketing.

**3. Open loop**
- The sentence isn't finished. Their brain needs closure.
- Only way to get it? Click.
- Never resolve the curiosity in the ad itself.

**Key insight:** "Forget trying to appeal to everyone. The best native ads feel like a personal message to one person. Call them out. Give them a number. Leave them hanging. Resolve in the advertorial."

---

### Framework 4: The AI-Generated Ad Method
**From "This AI ad makes more profit per week than you take home after taxes"**

**1. Hyper-specific callout**
- "Side sleepers" - A specific group who immediately thinks "that's me."
- The more specific, the more they feel seen.

**2. Problem they accepted as normal**
- "Achy shoulders" - Side sleepers often accept this as the normal.
- This ad tells them it doesn't have to be.
- Highlight pain they've normalized.

**3. Exclusive knowledge**
- "Few know this trick" - Most people are suffering.
- You're about to not be one of them.
- Position as insider information.

**Key insight:** "No mattress brand. No pillow product. No sleep science. The ad is simply capturing the attention of exactly their ideal customer. The advertorial will then explain everything else and does the selling."

```

[background details], [emotional expression], [lighting/time of day]

```

```
gripping his knee, joggers passing by, trees and benches in background,
```

**Prompt template for credibility:**

## Testing Protocol

### How to Test Ads Quickly

1. **Create 5-10 variations** of each winning hook concept
2. **Run each for 24-48 hours** with $10-20 budget
3. **Kill anything under [target CTR]** immediately
4. **Scale winners** by creating more variations of that angle

### Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Target | Action if Below |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| CTR | >1.5% | Kill or rewrite hook |
| CPC | <$0.50 | Acceptable for testing |
| Landing CVR | >3% | Fix landing page, not ad |
| CAC | <30% of LTV | Scale aggressively |

### When to Kill vs Scale

**Kill immediately:**
- CTR < 0.5% after 1000 impressions
- No conversions after $50 spend
- CPC > 3x your target

**Scale aggressively:**
- CTR > 2%
- CAC < 50% of target
- Positive ROAS after 72 hours

**Iterate:**
- Everything in between
- Create new variations of the angle
- Test different images with same copy
