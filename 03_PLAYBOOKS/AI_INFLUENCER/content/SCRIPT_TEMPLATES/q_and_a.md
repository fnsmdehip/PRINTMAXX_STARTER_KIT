# Q&A Script Template

Answering audience questions and building community through dialogue.

---

## Format specs

- Length: 30-90 seconds per question
- Structure: Question > Context > Answer > Application
- Tone: Conversational, helpful
- Source: Real questions from comments/DMs (with permission)

---

## Template 1: Single question deep dive

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"Someone asked: [Question]"

CONTEXT (10-15 sec):
"This is a great question because [why it matters].

[Background if needed]"

ANSWER (30-50 sec):
"Here's my take:

[Core answer]

[Supporting explanation]

[Nuance or exception]"

APPLICATION (10-15 sec):
"Practically, this means:
[What to actually do]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Have a question? Drop it in comments."
```

**Example:**
```
"Someone asked: How do I stay consistent when motivation disappears?

This is a great question because everyone faces this. Motivation is unreliable.

Here's my take:

Stop relying on motivation. Build systems instead.

Motivation is an emotion. It comes and goes like any other feeling. Waiting for it is like waiting to feel like going to work. You go anyway.

Instead: Make the behavior easier than avoiding it. Put workout clothes out. Open the document before closing your laptop. Reduce friction.

Exception: If you consistently hate something, maybe it's the wrong goal.

Practically, this means:
Tonight, set up tomorrow's behavior. Remove one barrier."

"Have a question? Drop it in comments."
```

## Template 4: "Which should I choose" questions

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"[Option A] or [Option B]?"

SETUP (10-15 sec):
"This question comes up a lot.
[Why people struggle with this choice]"

ANALYSIS (40-60 sec):
"Choose [Option A] if:
- [Criterion 1]
- [Criterion 2]
- [Criterion 3]

Choose [Option B] if:
- [Criterion 1]
- [Criterion 2]
- [Criterion 3]"

RECOMMENDATION (15 sec):
"If I had to pick one for most people:
[Choice + brief reason]

But context matters more than generic advice."

CTA (5 sec):
"Which situation fits you? Comment."
```

## Sourcing questions

### Where to find questions:
- Comments on your posts
- DMs (with permission to share)
- Stories Q&A stickers
- Polls about what to cover
- Common questions in your niche
- Questions from other creators' comments (general topics)

### Permission practices:
- Ask before using someone's question
- Offer to keep them anonymous
- Don't share identifying details without consent
- General questions can be paraphrased

### Question selection criteria:
- Relevant to your niche
- Others likely wondering the same
- You can actually answer helpfully
- Not requiring individual professional advice

---

## Questions to avoid answering

### Medical/legal/financial advice:
- "Should I take [medication]?"
- "Is this legal?"
- "Should I invest in [specific thing]?"

**Instead:** "I can't give [type] advice. Please consult a [professional]."

### Personal relationship specifics:
- "Should I break up with my partner?"
- "Is my boss being unfair?"

**Instead:** Provide frameworks, not specific decisions.

### Questions that require knowing them:
- "What should I do about my exact situation?"

**Instead:** Offer general principles they can apply.

---

## AI-specific Q&A considerations

### Common questions about being AI:
- Answer honestly and completely
- Don't get defensive
- Use as transparency opportunity

### Questions requiring lived experience:
- Be honest about limitations
- "I can share what research shows and what I've seen work for others, but I don't have personal experience with this."
- Redirect to frameworks and data

### Parasocial questions:
- Gently maintain boundaries
- "I appreciate the connection, but remember I'm AI. For [personal matter], talking to people in your life will be more helpful."

---

## Q&A engagement tips

### Before the Q&A:
- Collect questions in advance
- Curate for quality and variety
- Have backup questions ready

### During response:
- Use the questioner's name if appropriate
- Acknowledge the question genuinely
- Don't rush the answer

### After posting:
- Respond to follow-up questions
- Ask if the answer helped
- Note questions for future content

---

## Building a question pipeline

### Regular prompts:
- End posts with "Questions?"
- Weekly "ask me anything" prompt
- Stories stickers for casual questions
- Poll for topics to cover

### Documenting questions:
- Keep a running list
- Note frequency of similar questions
- Use for content planning
- Track which Q&A content performs best
