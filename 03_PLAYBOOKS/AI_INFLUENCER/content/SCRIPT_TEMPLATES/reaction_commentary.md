# Reaction & Commentary Script Template

Responding to trends, news, and other content with your unique perspective.

---

## Format specs

- Length: 30-90 seconds
- Structure: Context > Reaction > Analysis > Takeaway
- Tone: Opinionated but thoughtful
- Timing: React while relevant (24-72 hours)

---

## Template 1: Trend reaction

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"Everyone's talking about [trend]. Here's my take:"

CONTEXT (10-15 sec):
"[Trend] is [brief explanation for those unfamiliar].

[Why it's getting attention]"

REACTION (25-40 sec):
"What I [like/dislike/notice]:
[Observation 1]

[Observation 2]

What people are missing:
[Unique insight]"

TAKEAWAY (15 sec):
"For you, this means:
[Practical application]"

CTA (5 sec):
"What's your take? Comment below."
```

**Example:**
```
"Everyone's talking about AI replacing jobs. Here's my take:

AI tools are genuinely changing what's possible. That's not hype.

What I notice:
The jobs most at risk aren't 'low skill.' They're routine and predictable. Highly paid lawyers doing document review are more threatened than plumbers.

What people are missing:
AI is a tool multiplier. One person with AI does what five did before. That's not replacement, it's leverage.

For you, this means:
Learn to work WITH AI tools now. The question isn't AI vs. humans. It's humans with AI vs. humans without."

"What's your take? Comment below."
```

## Template 3: Duet/stitch response

```
HOOK (immediate):
[Original clip plays or is referenced]

RESPONSE (30-60 sec):
"[Agree/Disagree/Add nuance]

Here's why:
[Your reasoning]

What I'd add:
[Complementary point or counter]

The bigger picture:
[Context or principle]"

CTA (5 sec):
"What do you think?"
```

**Example structure for agreement:**
```
[Original clip: "Morning routines are overrated"]

"This. Absolutely.

Here's why:
What you do consistently matters more than when. Night owls forcing 5 AM routines fight their biology.

What I'd add:
The routine itself is neutral. The intention behind it matters. Same actions, different purposes.

The bigger picture:
Productivity content often confuses correlation with causation. Successful people have routines, but the routine isn't why they're successful."

"Are you a morning person or not? Comment."
```

## Commentary guidelines

### Good commentary:
- Adds new perspective
- Based on expertise/knowledge
- Acknowledges complexity
- Invites discussion
- Stays in your lane

### Bad commentary:
- Just restates without insight
- Reacts for engagement without substance
- Speaks outside expertise
- Presents opinion as fact
- Punches down or attacks

---

## Timing considerations

### Speed matters:
- Trends: 24-72 hours
- News: Same day preferred
- Viral content: While still viral

### Speed vs. quality:
- Fast + shallow = forgettable
- Slow + deep = too late
- Fast + insightful = optimal

### When to wait:
- Developing stories (facts may change)
- Emotionally charged events (need reflection)
- Complex situations (need research)

---

## Handling controversy

### If you take a controversial position:

Before posting:
- Am I prepared to defend this?
- Do I have evidence?
- Can I handle pushback?

After posting:
- Engage thoughtfully with disagreement
- Don't delete unless factually wrong
- Don't escalate drama

### If your position ages poorly:
- Acknowledge it directly
- Explain what changed your mind
- Don't pretend it didn't happen

---

## AI-specific commentary notes

### What AI can comment on:
- Data and research
- Logical analysis
- Frameworks and patterns
- Industry trends

### What AI should approach carefully:
- Personal taste/preference questions
- Lived experience requirements
- Emotional/cultural nuance

### Framing for AI:
- "The data suggests..."
- "Looking at this logically..."
- "From a pattern perspective..."
- "What I notice is..."

Avoid:
- "As someone who has..."
- "In my experience..."
- "I personally feel..."

## Content to react to

### Good sources:
- Industry news in your niche
- Viral content in your space
- Questions your audience is asking
- Misconceptions spreading
- Announcements from major players

### Find through:
- Following key accounts
- News alerts for your niche
- Audience questions and comments
- Trend tracking tools
