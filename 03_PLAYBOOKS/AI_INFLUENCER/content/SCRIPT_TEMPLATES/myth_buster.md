# Myth Buster Script Template

Content that challenges common misconceptions and provides accurate information.

---

## Format specs

- Length: 30-90 seconds
- Structure: Myth > Why people believe it > Truth > Proof
- Tone: Authoritative but not condescending
- Hook: Controversial or surprising

---

## Template 1: Single myth takedown

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"This [topic] advice is wrong:"

MYTH (10-15 sec):
"You've probably heard: [Common belief]

[Why it sounds reasonable]"

TRUTH (20-40 sec):
"Here's what's actually true:
[Correct information]

The evidence:
[Data, research, or logic]

Why the myth persists:
[Brief explanation]"

TAKEAWAY (10 sec):
"What to do instead:
[Actionable replacement]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Share this with someone who needs to hear it."
```

**Example:**
```
"This productivity advice is wrong:

You've probably heard: Wake up at 5 AM if you want to be successful.

It sounds reasonable. Early risers get more done, right?

Here's what's actually true:
Sleep timing matters less than sleep quality and consistency.

The evidence:
Chronotypes are genetic. About 25% of people are naturally night owls. Forcing yourself into an unnatural schedule reduces cognitive performance.

Why the myth persists:
Survivorship bias. We hear from successful early risers, not successful night owls.

What to do instead:
Find your natural rhythm and protect 7-8 hours of sleep. When you wake matters less than how well you sleep."

"Share this with the 5 AM guilt club."
```

## Template 2: Myth vs fact rapid fire

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"[Number] [topic] myths debunked:"

MYTHS (40-70 sec):
"Myth 1: [Belief]
Fact: [Truth]

Myth 2: [Belief]
Fact: [Truth]

Myth 3: [Belief]
Fact: [Truth]

Myth 4: [Belief]
Fact: [Truth]

Myth 5: [Belief]
Fact: [Truth]"

WRAP (10 sec):
"Bonus myth: [Extra one]
Fact: [Truth]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Which one surprised you? Comment below."
```

**Example:**
```
"5 fitness myths debunked:

Myth 1: You need to feel sore for a good workout.
Fact: Soreness indicates unfamiliar stress, not effectiveness.

Myth 2: Cardio is the best way to lose fat.
Fact: Diet controls weight. Strength training preserves muscle while losing fat.

Myth 3: You need to eat protein within 30 minutes of working out.
Fact: Total daily protein matters more than timing.

Myth 4: Lifting heavy will make you bulky.
Fact: Building significant muscle takes years of dedicated training and nutrition.

Myth 5: Abs are made in the gym.
Fact: Everyone has abs. Visibility is body fat percentage, controlled by diet.

Bonus myth: Stretching prevents injury.
Fact: Warm-up prevents injury. Static stretching before lifting can reduce performance."

"Which one surprised you? Comment below."
```

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"[Common advice], but actually..."

SETUP (10-15 sec):
"Everyone says [common belief].

And it makes sense because [reasoning]."

TWIST (25-40 sec):
"But actually:
[The nuance or reversal]

Here's why:
[Explanation]

[Support]"

NUANCE (15 sec):
"When the original advice IS right:

```

## Evidence standards

### For myth-busting content, you need:

**Strong evidence:**
- Peer-reviewed research
- Expert consensus
- Replicated studies
- Clear data

**Acceptable evidence:**
- Multiple credible sources agreeing
- Logical analysis
- Expert interviews
- Case studies

**Weak evidence (avoid):**
- Single study
- Anecdotes alone
- "Common sense"
- Unverified claims

### How to cite in short-form:
- "Research from [Institution] shows..."
- "A meta-analysis of [X] studies found..."
- "[Credible source] concluded..."
- "The data is clear: [finding]"

### What to do if unsure:
- Hedge: "Evidence suggests" instead of "It's proven"
- Invite discussion: "The research points to X, though debate continues"
- Don't mythbust: If evidence is weak, don't make the content

---

## Tone guidelines

### Do:
- State facts clearly
- Acknowledge why myth exists
- Show empathy for believers
- Provide actionable alternatives

### Don't:
- Mock people who believe myth
- Be condescending
- Oversimplify complex topics
- Ignore legitimate nuance

### Example bad tone:
"Anyone who believes this is an idiot."

### Example good tone:
"This belief makes sense on the surface. Here's what the research actually shows."

## Topics that work well

- Productivity myths
- Health and fitness myths
- Business/career myths
- Relationship myths
- Money myths
- Learning myths
- Success myths
- Technology myths

---

## Topics to approach carefully

- Medical myths (stay general, don't give medical advice)
- Political myths (high risk of backlash)
- Religious myths (respect belief systems)
- Cultural myths (be sensitive to context)

When in doubt: Add disclaimers, stick to your expertise, recommend professionals.
