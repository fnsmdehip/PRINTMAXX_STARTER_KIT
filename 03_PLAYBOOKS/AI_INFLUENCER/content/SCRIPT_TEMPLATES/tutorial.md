# Tutorial Script Template

Step-by-step instructional content that teaches a specific skill.

---

## Format specs

- Length: 60-180 seconds (short-form) or 3-10 min (long-form)
- Structure: Hook > Context > Steps > Result > CTA
- Focus: One specific outcome
- Pace: Clear, not rushed

---

## Template 1: Quick how-to (short-form)

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"How to [specific result] in [timeframe/steps]:"

CONTEXT (5-10 sec):
"This works for [situation]. You'll need [requirements if any]."

STEPS (30-60 sec):
"Step 1: [Action]
[Brief explanation or tip]

Step 2: [Action]
[Brief explanation or tip]

Step 3: [Action]
[Brief explanation or tip]"

RESULT (5-10 sec):
"Now you have [the outcome]. [Quick benefit statement]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Save this for when you need it."
```

**Example:**
```
"How to write a cold email that gets responses in 4 steps:

This works for reaching out to anyone you don't know. No templates needed.

Step 1: First line about them, not you
Find something specific. Their recent post, their company, their work.

Step 2: One clear reason you're reaching out
Not 'pick your brain.' Specific: 'Question about X you mentioned.'

Step 3: Make it easy to say yes
Ask for 15 minutes, not an hour. Suggest times. Remove friction.

Step 4: Keep it under 100 words
If it's longer, you're explaining too much. Cut it.

Now you have an email that respects their time and shows you did homework."

"Save this for your next outreach."
```

## Template 2: Tool tutorial

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"How to use [Tool] for [specific outcome]:"

CONTEXT (5-10 sec):
"This feature helps you [benefit]. Most people miss it."

SCREEN/VISUAL (40-90 sec):
"First, go to [location].
Click [button/menu].
Select [option].
Adjust [setting] to [recommendation].
[Explain why this setting matters]
Click [save/confirm]."

RESULT (10 sec):
"Now [tool] will [do the thing]. This saves [time/effort]."

CTA (5 sec):
"Try this and comment how it goes."
```

## Template 3: Framework tutorial

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"The [Name] framework for [outcome]:"

OVERVIEW (10 sec):
"[Number] steps that work for [situation]. Here's how:"

FRAMEWORK (60-120 sec):
"[Letter/Number 1]: [Word]
[What this means]
[How to apply]

[Letter/Number 2]: [Word]
[What this means]
[How to apply]

[Letter/Number 3]: [Word]
[What this means]
[How to apply]"

APPLICATION (15 sec):
"Example: [Quick real application]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Screenshot this framework."
```

**Example:**
```
"The ACE framework for making decisions faster:

3 steps that work for any tough choice. Here's how:

A: Accept the downside
What's the worst that happens? Can you live with it? If yes, proceed.

C: Cap the deliberation
Set a deadline. 24 hours for small decisions. 1 week for big ones. Decide by then.

E: Execute and evaluate
Make the choice. Review after 30 days. Adjust if needed.

Example: Job offer decision. Worst case: you don't like it (you can leave). Cap: decide by Friday. Execute: take it and evaluate in 90 days."

"Screenshot this for your next decision."
```

## Template 4: Problem-solution tutorial

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"Fix [common problem] with this:"

PROBLEM (10-15 sec):
"[Problem description]
[Why it happens]
[Why common solutions fail]"

SOLUTION (45-90 sec):
"Instead, try [approach].

Here's exactly how:
[Detailed steps]

Why this works:
[Brief explanation]

Common mistakes to avoid:
[1-2 pitfalls]"

RESULT (10 sec):
"[Problem] solved. [Benefit of solution]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Comment 'fixed' when you try this."
```

## Template 5: Before/after tutorial

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"Turn [bad version] into [good version]:"

BEFORE (15-20 sec):
"Here's what most people do:
[Example of common approach]

The problem:
[Why this doesn't work]"

TRANSFORMATION (30-60 sec):
"Here's the fix:

Change [element 1]:
From [bad] to [good]

Change [element 2]:
From [bad] to [good]

Change [element 3]:
From [bad] to [good]"

AFTER (10-15 sec):
"Now you have [improved version].
The difference: [Key improvement]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Share your before/after in comments."
```

**Example:**
```
"Turn a weak bio into one that converts:

Here's what most people do:
'Passionate entrepreneur | Helping businesses grow | DM for collab'

The problem:
It's generic. Everyone says this. No reason to follow.

Here's the fix:

Change the opener:
From vague 'passionate' to specific result: 'Grew 3 brands to 6 figures'

Change the middle:
From 'helping businesses' to exact help: 'I share weekly case studies on content that works'

Change the CTA:
From 'DM for collab' to clear value: 'Free strategy template in link'

Now you have a bio that tells people exactly what they get.
The difference: Specificity over positivity."

"Drop your bio for feedback in comments."
```

## Long-form tutorial structure (3-10 min)

```
INTRO (30-60 sec):
- Hook with end result
- Who this is for
- What you'll learn
- Why it matters

OVERVIEW (30 sec):
- Preview the steps
- Set expectations

STEP 1 (60-120 sec):
- What to do
- How to do it
- Common mistakes
- Example

STEP 2 (60-120 sec):
- What to do
- How to do it
- Common mistakes
- Example

[Continue for each step]

RECAP (30 sec):
- Summary of steps
- Expected outcome

CTA (30 sec):
- What to do next
- Where to get resources
- Follow prompt
```

## Production notes

### Visual requirements:
- Screen recording for tool tutorials
- Text overlays for steps
- Numbered steps visible
- Highlight key areas

### Pacing:
- Slower than tips
- Pause after each step
- Allow processing time

### Accessibility:
- Clear verbal explanation
- Captions for all steps
- Contrast in visuals

---

## Checklist before publishing tutorial

- [ ] Outcome clearly stated in hook
- [ ] All steps are complete
- [ ] No assumed knowledge unexplained
- [ ] Examples provided
- [ ] Common mistakes addressed
- [ ] Works for stated audience
- [ ] Tested the steps yourself (or clearly noted if not)
