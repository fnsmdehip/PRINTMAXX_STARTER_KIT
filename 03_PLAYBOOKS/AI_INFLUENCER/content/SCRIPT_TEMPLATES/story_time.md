# Story Time Script Template

Narrative content that teaches through examples and scenarios.

---

## Important note for AI influencers

AI characters cannot share authentic personal experiences. These templates use:
- Hypothetical scenarios (clearly labeled)
- User stories (with permission)
- Historical/public examples
- "Imagine" framing

Never present fictional stories as personal experience.

---

## Format specs

- Length: 45-90 seconds
- Structure: Setup > Tension > Resolution > Lesson
- Hook: Curiosity-driven
- CTA: Reflection or application

---

## Template 1: The user story (with permission)

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"A follower messaged me about [situation]..."

BODY (45-60 sec):
"[Name] was [struggling with specific problem].

They had tried [common approaches].

Nothing worked until they [specific action/shift].

Within [timeframe], [specific result].

What made the difference: [the lesson]

Here's the thing - [universal application]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Comment if this sounds familiar."
```

**Example:**
```
"A follower messaged me about their workout consistency...

Sarah had started and stopped the gym six times in two years.

She'd tried motivation, accountability partners, new programs.

Nothing worked until she stopped aiming for the gym and aimed for the parking lot.

Within 3 months, she hadn't missed a week.

What made the difference: The goal became showing up, not performing.

Here's the thing - the smallest commitment is the hardest to break."

"Comment if this sounds familiar."
```

## Template 2: The hypothetical

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"Imagine this scenario..."

BODY (45-70 sec):
"Imagine you [relatable situation].

You feel [relatable emotion].

Your instinct is to [common reaction].

But what if instead, you [alternative approach]?

[Walk through what happens]

The difference isn't the situation. It's the response.

[Lesson distilled]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Save this for when you need it."
```

**Example:**
```
"Imagine this scenario...

Imagine you've worked on a project for months. You finally share it. Nobody engages.

You feel defeated. Like it was all a waste.

Your instinct is to quit. Delete everything. Move on.

But what if instead, you asked one person for honest feedback?

They tell you the title confused them. They didn't know what it was.

You change the title. Reshare. It works.

The difference isn't the failure. It's what you do next.

Most 'failures' are feedback waiting to be collected."

"Save this for when you need it."
```

## Template 3: The historical example

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"[Famous person/company] almost [didn't succeed]..."

BODY (50-70 sec):
"[Brief setup of the story]

At this point, they could have [quit/given up].

Instead, they [what they did differently].

[The result]

The lesson that applies to you:
[Universal takeaway]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Follow for more like this."
```

**Example:**
```
"Airbnb almost shut down three times...

In 2008, they were drowning in debt. Nobody wanted to rent strangers' couches.

At this point, they could have called it.

Instead, they went door to door taking better photos of listings themselves.

Bookings doubled. Then kept growing.

The lesson that applies to you:
Sometimes the problem isn't your idea. It's the execution. Before you quit, try fixing one specific thing."

"Follow for more like this."
```

## Template 4: The "what people don't see"

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"What people see: [surface result]
What they don't see:"

BODY (45-60 sec):
"[The visible success/result]

But behind that:
- [Hidden effort 1]
- [Hidden effort 2]
- [Hidden effort 3]

[The invisible work adds up to the visible result]

Reminder: [Lesson about not comparing to visible results]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Share with someone who needs perspective."
```

**Example:**
```
"What people see: The 'overnight' success.
What they don't see:

The viral post that took 3 minutes to write.

But behind that:
- 200 posts that got 12 likes
- 18 months of consistency
- Countless ideas that flopped

The visible moment sits on invisible years.

Reminder: You're seeing their highlight reel. You're living your behind-the-scenes. Not the same movie."

"Share with someone who needs perspective."
```

## Template 5: The contrast story

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"Two people started [same situation]. Different outcomes."

BODY (50-70 sec):
"Person A: [Approach one]
Person B: [Approach two]

Same starting point. Same opportunity.

Person A: [Result]
Person B: [Result]

The difference wasn't talent or luck.

It was [the key variable].

[How to apply this]"

CTA (5 sec):
"Which one are you being right now?"
```

**Example:**
```
"Two people started the same workout program. Different outcomes.

Person A: Followed it perfectly for 2 weeks. Got bored. Quit.
Person B: Modified it constantly. Stuck with it imperfectly for a year.

Same program. Same starting fitness.

Person A: Back to square one.
Person B: Transformed.

The difference wasn't the program.

It was sustainability over perfection.

Imperfect consistency beats perfect inconsistency every time."

"Which one are you being right now?"
```

## Template 6: The "I asked [X] people" format

```
HOOK (3 sec):
"I asked [number] [people] about [topic]. Here's what they said:"

BODY (45-65 sec):
"Most common answer: [Response 1]
Second most common: [Response 2]
Surprising answer: [Response 3]

What this tells us:
[Insight from the data]

The actionable takeaway:
[How to apply this]"

CTA (5 sec):
"What would your answer be? Comment below."
```

**Example:**
```
"I asked 50 people what held them back from starting. Here's what they said:

Most common answer: Fear of failure.
Second most common: Not knowing where to start.
Surprising answer: Fear of success and the pressure that comes with it.

What this tells us:
The barrier is rarely resources. It's psychology.

The actionable takeaway:
Stop solving for money or time. Solve for fear. Start smaller than you think you should."

"What would your answer be? Comment below."
```

## Attribution guidelines

### For user stories:
- Get explicit permission
- Use first names only (or pseudonyms if preferred)
- Don't share identifying details without consent
- Document permissions in your files

### For historical examples:
- Use publicly known information
- Cite sources when possible
- Don't embellish facts
- Stick to documented stories

### For hypotheticals:
- Frame clearly as hypothetical ("Imagine...")
- Don't present as your experience
- Make universal enough to apply broadly

---

## Production notes

### Pacing:
- Slower than tips content
- Build tension before resolution
- Pause after lesson reveal

### Visuals:
- Face-to-camera works well
- B-roll for historical examples
- Text overlays for key points

### Engagement:
- Stories drive comments
- Ask reflective questions in CTA
- Respond to people sharing their versions
