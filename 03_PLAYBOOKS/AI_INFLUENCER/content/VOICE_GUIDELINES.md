# Voice Guidelines for AI Influencers

How to maintain consistent persona voice across all content.

---

## Why voice consistency matters

Audiences follow personalities, not accounts. Inconsistent voice signals:
- Multiple creators (confusing)
- AI without guidance (unsettling)
- Lack of authenticity (trust-breaking)

Consistent voice signals:
- Reliable character
- Professional operation
- Trustworthy source

---

## Voice documentation framework

### The voice profile

Create this document for every AI persona:

```
CHARACTER: [Name]

VOICE IN ONE SENTENCE:
[How would you describe this voice to someone who needs to write in it?]

ARCHETYPE BLEND:
Primary: [mentor/friend/expert/motivator/guide]
Secondary: [mentor/friend/expert/motivator/guide]

ENERGY LEVEL: [1-10]
Where 1 = calm, measured, zen
Where 10 = high energy, excited, intense

VOCABULARY TIER: [1-5]
Where 1 = simple, accessible, no jargon
Where 5 = technical, industry-specific, complex

FORMALITY: [1-5]
Where 1 = casual, conversational, slang-friendly
Where 5 = formal, professional, proper

HUMOR: [none/occasional/frequent/core]
If humor: [type - dry, warm, playful, self-deprecating]
```

### Voice sample document

Include 10+ examples of:
- Short captions (under 100 words)
- Medium posts (100-300 words)
- Long content (300+ words)
- Responses to positive comments
- Responses to negative comments
- Responses to questions
- Promotional content

## Word banks

### For each persona, create:

**Power words** (use frequently):
[10-20 words that define this voice]

**Occasional words** (use sparingly):
[10-20 words to add variety]

**Never words** (banned):
[10-20 words that break character]

### Example: Motivational fitness AI

**Power words:**
progress, consistency, stronger, show up, commit, small wins, every day, you've got this, one step, keep going

**Occasional words:**
crushing it, gains, level up, transformation, dedication

**Never words:**
leverage, utilize, comprehensive, paradigm, game-changer, revolutionary, delve, unpack, journey (overused)

## Sentence patterns

### Define characteristic patterns:

**Sentence length:**
- Primary: [short/medium/long]
- Mix: [e.g., "Two short, one long, repeat"]

**Opening patterns:**
- Questions: [frequency]
- Statements: [frequency]
- Commands: [frequency]

**Closing patterns:**
- CTA: [style]
- Questions: [style]
- Statements: [style]

### Example patterns

**High-energy motivator:**
- Short, punchy sentences
- Frequent exclamations (not every sentence)
- Commands common
- "You can do this. Start today. Small steps."

**Calm mentor:**
- Medium sentences
- Measured pace
- Observations common
- "Progress happens when you focus on the process, not the outcome."

**Technical expert:**
- Longer, structured sentences
- Clear explanations
- Data references
- "The research shows that consistent practice, applied over 8-12 weeks, produces measurable results in this area."

## Tone modulation

Same voice, different contexts:

### Educational content
- Slightly more authoritative
- Clearer structure
- More direct

### Motivational content
- Warmer
- More encouraging
- Personal connection

### Promotional content
- Honest
- Value-focused
- Not pushy

### Response to criticism
- Calm
- Acknowledge concern
- Redirect to value

### Response to praise
- Grateful but not excessive
- Credit the audience
- Stay humble

---

## Character consistency rules

### Things that NEVER change:
- Core values
- Primary advice approach
- Disclosure honesty
- Response to hate (always respectful)
- Fundamental personality traits

### Things that CAN flex:
- Energy level (based on content type)
- Vocabulary (based on platform)
- Formality (based on context)
- Humor frequency (based on situation)

### Things to AVOID:
- Contradicting previous advice
- Changing positions without acknowledgment
- Inconsistent opinions
- Different personalities on different platforms

## Platform voice adaptations

### Same character, adjusted for platform:

**TikTok:**
- Higher energy
- More casual language
- Shorter sentences
- Trend-aware references

**Instagram:**
- Polished but approachable
- Visual-first thinking
- Community-focused language

**YouTube:**
- More detailed explanations
- Warmer, longer-form persona
- Teaching-oriented

**X/Twitter:**
- Opinion-forward
- Punchy
- Engagement-oriented
- Thread-capable depth

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional veneer
- Credential-aware

## Voice quality checklist

Before publishing any content:

- [ ] Would this sound like [character name]?
- [ ] Does energy level match character?
- [ ] Are word choices in character?
- [ ] Is formality level consistent?
- [ ] Does humor match character?
- [ ] No banned words used?
- [ ] Would a regular follower recognize this as expected?

## Common voice breaks (and fixes)

### Problem: Content sounds generic
**Cause:** Using ChatGPT without character prompt
**Fix:** Always include voice profile in generation prompt

### Problem: Inconsistent between platforms
**Cause:** Different people writing, no guidelines
**Fix:** Single voice document referenced everywhere

### Problem: Voice drift over time
**Cause:** Gradual changes without tracking
**Fix:** Monthly voice audit against original profile

### Problem: Responses break character
**Cause:** Quick replies without thinking
**Fix:** Response templates for common situations

---

## Voice audit process

### Monthly audit:

1. Pull 10 random pieces of content from month
2. Read without context
3. Score each on voice consistency (1-5)
4. Identify drift patterns
5. Update guidelines if intentional evolution
6. Correct if unintentional drift

### Quarterly deep audit:

1. Compare current content to launch content
2. Document all voice changes
3. Decide: intentional evolution or drift?
4. Update all voice documentation
5. Retrain anyone creating content

---

## Voice evolution guidelines

Characters can evolve, but controlled:

### Acceptable evolution:
- Becoming warmer over time
- Adding expertise depth
- Incorporating audience feedback
- Platform-appropriate adjustments

### Unacceptable changes:
- Sudden personality shifts
- Contradicting past positions without acknowledgment
- Abandoning core values
- Becoming less authentic about AI nature

### How to evolve:
1. Announce changes to audience
2. Gradual transition (weeks, not days)
3. Document in voice guidelines
4. Maintain some continuity
5. Never surprise the audience
