# FTC Requirements for AI Influencers

Complete guide to Federal Trade Commission compliance for AI-generated content creators.

---

## Core FTC principles

### The fundamental rule:

Advertisements must be truthful and not misleading, and endorsements must reflect honest opinions and experiences.

For AI influencers, this means:
- Disclose AI nature of content creator
- Disclose material connections (sponsorships, affiliates)
- Don't make claims you can't substantiate
- Don't deceive audiences about content origin

---

## Material connection disclosure

### What requires disclosure:

**Paid sponsorships:**
- Brand pays for content
- Brand pays for mention
- Any financial compensation

**Free products:**
- Products received for review
- Gifts from brands
- Press samples

**Affiliate relationships:**
- Commission-based links
- Referral programs
- Revenue share arrangements

**Employer relationships:**
- Content about your own products
- Content about employer's products

### How to disclose material connections:

**Clear language:**
- "Ad" or "Advertisement"
- "Sponsored by [Brand]"
- "Paid partnership with [Brand]"
- "I received this product free"
- "Affiliate link - I earn commission"

**Placement:**
- Before the promotion starts
- Visible without scrolling/clicking
- In the same medium (video = verbal + visual)
- Not buried at the end

**Avoid unclear disclosures:**
- "#sp" (not clear enough)
- "Thanks [Brand]" (sounds like gratitude, not payment)
- Disclosure only in small text
- Disclosure after "see more" fold

## AI-specific disclosure requirements

### Why AI nature matters:

The FTC requires disclosure of facts that would affect consumer decisions. An AI creator is a material fact because:
- Consumers may evaluate recommendations differently
- AI can't have genuine personal experience
- Authenticity assumptions may be wrong

### How to disclose AI nature:

**Bio/profile:**
- "AI-generated content creator"
- "AI-powered [role]"
- "[Name] is an AI"

**Periodic in-content:**
- "I'm [Name], an AI..."
- "As an AI..."
- Visual labels: "AI Content Creator"

**For new audience members:**
- First video/post someone sees should make AI nature clear
- Pinned content explaining AI nature
- Welcome messages with disclosure

### Combined disclosure (AI + sponsored):

When content is both AI-generated AND sponsored:
```
"This content is sponsored by [Brand]. I'm [Name], an AI - my avatar, voice,
and content are AI-generated. Here's what I think about [product]..."
```

## Endorsement guidelines

### What counts as an endorsement:

Any statement about a product or service that:
- Reflects the endorser's opinion
- Is used in advertising or promotion
- Could influence consumer decisions

### AI influencer endorsement rules:

**Can endorse:**
- Products researched and evaluated
- Products tested by operator/team
- Products with verifiable claims
- Products fitting your niche/audience

**Cannot claim:**
- Personal experience using products (AI doesn't "use" things)
- Results you personally achieved
- Feelings or emotions about products

**How to frame AI endorsements:**
- "Based on my research..."
- "Users report that..."
- "The specifications show..."
- "Data indicates..."

### Testimonials:

If featuring user testimonials:
- Must be real (not fabricated)
- Results must be typical OR disclose atypicality
- Get permission to share
- Disclose any compensation for testimonials

## Health, financial, and performance claims

### Health claims:

**Don't claim:**
- "This supplement cures [condition]"
- "This diet will make you lose 30 pounds"
- "This product treats [disease]"

**Safe approaches:**
- Share what manufacturer states (if accurate)
- Cite published research
- Include appropriate disclaimers
- Don't give medical advice

### Financial claims:

**Don't claim:**
- "You'll make $10,000/month"
- "Guaranteed returns"
- "I made [amount] with this system" (AI didn't make anything)

**Safe approaches:**
- Cite verifiable income data (with disclaimers)
- Share typical results, not outliers
- Include income disclaimers
- Don't promise specific results

### Performance claims:

Any claim about product performance must be:
- Truthful
- Substantiated by evidence
- Not misleading in context

## Platform-specific FTC compliance

### Instagram:

- Use Paid Partnership label when available
- Disclosure in caption visible before "more"
- Stories: Clear disclosure visible in each story
- Reels: Verbal + visual disclosure early

### TikTok:

- Use Content Disclosure feature
- Verbal disclosure in first 5-10 seconds
- Caption disclosure (won't be seen without clicking)
- Branded content toggle

### YouTube:

- Verbal disclosure in video
- Description disclosure
- Paid promotion checkbox in YouTube Studio
- Pinned comment for additional clarity

### X/Twitter:

- Begin tweet with #ad or #sponsored
- Disclosure visible without expanding
- Retweets of sponsored content need disclosure

## Record keeping

### What to document:

**For each sponsored post:**
- Contract/agreement
- Compensation amount
- Content requirements
- Approval communications
- Published content
- Disclosure used

**For affiliate content:**
- Affiliate program terms
- Products promoted
- Disclosure methods
- Commission records

**For AI disclosure:**
- Where AI is disclosed
- How often disclosed
- Bio text
- Pinned content

### Why documentation matters:

- Proves compliance if questioned
- Protects against disputes
- Shows good faith effort
- Helps with audits

## FTC enforcement

### How enforcement works:

1. FTC investigates (often from complaints)
2. Warning letter or civil investigation
3. Potential consent order
4. Fines and penalties if violated

### Potential consequences:

- Warning letters (common first step)
- Required corrective advertising
- Civil penalties ($50,000+ per violation)
- Consent decrees (ongoing oversight)
- Reputation damage

### How to avoid enforcement:

- Disclose clearly and consistently
- Don't make misleading claims
- Document compliance efforts
- Respond to any FTC contact immediately
- Consult attorney if unsure

## Frequently asked questions

### "Do I need a lawyer?"

For ongoing AI influencer business, consulting a lawyer is recommended for:
- Initial setup review
- Contract review
- Complex sponsorship deals
- Any FTC contact

### "What if I make a mistake?"

1. Correct it immediately
2. Document the correction
3. Review processes to prevent repeat
4. If contacted by FTC, respond promptly and honestly

### "Are small accounts exempt?"

No. FTC rules apply regardless of follower count.

### "Does AI need to disclose in every post?"

AI nature should be easily discoverable:
- Bio disclosure (always present)
- Periodic in-content reminders
- New follower onboarding
- When directly relevant

### "What about user-generated content?"

If your AI promotes UGC:
- Verify it's real
- Get permission
- Don't fake UGC
- Disclose if compensated
