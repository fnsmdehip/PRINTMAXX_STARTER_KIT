# Disclosure Templates for AI Influencers

Copy-paste templates for compliant AI disclosure across platforms and content types.

---

## The disclosure requirement

**Why disclose?**
- FTC requires clear disclosure of material facts
- Platform TOS require synthetic media labeling
- Audience trust requires honesty
- Legal protection requires documentation

**What to disclose:**
- AI-generated avatar/visual
- AI-generated voice
- AI-assisted script writing
- AI nature of "character"

## FAQ response templates

### "Are you real?"

```
I'm an AI! My avatar, voice, and content are AI-generated. I'm designed to help you [benefit]. What's real: the information I share and the value you get from it. Any other questions?
```

### "Who's behind this?"

```
Great question. I'm operated by [name/company]. They created my character, generate my content using AI tools, and manage this account. Want to know anything specific about how I'm made?
```

### "Is this all fake?"

```
My visuals and voice are AI-generated - that's clearly disclosed. The information and value I share is researched and curated to actually help you [benefit]. You can verify anything I share.
```

### "Why should I trust an AI?"

```
Fair question. You shouldn't trust me blindly - that applies to any content creator. What I offer: consistent, researched information on [topic]. Verify what resonates, ignore what doesn't. I'm upfront about what I am so you can make informed choices about following.
```

## Platform-specific disclosure formats

### TikTok
- Bio: Include "AI" explicitly
- Use TikTok's AI content label when posting
- Add #AIgenerated or similar

### Instagram
- Bio: Include "AI" or "AI-powered"
- Use Instagram's AI label feature
- Caption: Include AI mention

### YouTube
- Channel About: Full disclosure paragraph
- Video descriptions: Standard disclosure
- Pinned comment: Detailed breakdown

### X/Twitter
- Bio: Include "AI" indicator
- Pinned tweet: Longer explanation
- Replies: Direct when asked

## Disclosure audit checklist

For each platform, verify:

- [ ] Bio contains AI disclosure
- [ ] Pinned/featured content explains AI nature
- [ ] Standard content includes periodic disclosure
- [ ] Sponsored content has double disclosure (ad + AI)
- [ ] FAQ/response templates address AI questions
- [ ] Link destination has disclosure info

For each piece of content:

- [ ] Is AI nature clear to new viewers?
- [ ] Would FTC consider this adequately disclosed?
- [ ] Does platform TOS compliance check out?
- [ ] Is sponsorship disclosed separately from AI disclosure?

## What NOT to do

**Don't hide disclosure:**
- Tiny text
- After "read more" fold
- Only accessible via multiple clicks
- Only in terms nobody reads

**Don't use ambiguous language:**
- "Virtual" (unclear)
- "Digital creator" alone (insufficient)
- "Computer-generated" (confusing)
- "Not human" (weird)

**Don't disclosure-dump:**
- Don't make every post about being AI
- Don't over-explain constantly
- Balance: Clear disclosure + normal content

**Don't forget updates:**
- When tools change, update disclosure
- When platforms update policies, comply
- Annual review of all disclosure language
