# Brand Deals for AI Influencers

Securing and executing sponsored content partnerships.

---

## The AI influencer brand deal opportunity

### Why brands work with AI influencers:

- Novelty and attention
- Consistent content quality
- Predictable posting schedule
- Unique positioning opportunity
- Growing market interest

### Challenges to address:

- Authenticity questions
- New territory for brands
- Rate card uncertainty
- Educational barrier
- Trust building required

---

## Brand deal types

### Sponsored content

Single post or series featuring brand/product.

**Typical structure:**
- Flat fee per post
- Content requirements
- Usage rights
- Timeline

**AI consideration:** Disclose both sponsorship AND AI nature.

### Brand ambassadorship

Ongoing relationship with regular mentions.

**Typical structure:**
- Monthly/quarterly fee
- Content minimum
- Exclusivity (often)
- Longer commitment

**AI consideration:** Long-term partnerships build credibility.

### Affiliate sponsorship

Sponsored content plus affiliate commission.

**Typical structure:**
- Lower flat fee
- Commission on sales
- Tracking link
- Performance bonuses

**AI consideration:** Good starting point for proving value.

### Product integration

Brand naturally fits into regular content.

**Typical structure:**
- Per-mention fee
- Natural placement
- Less scripted
- Multiple touchpoints

**AI consideration:** Works well with AI consistency.

### Licensing

Brand licenses your character/content for their use.

**Typical structure:**
- Licensing fee
- Usage terms
- Territory/duration
- Your content on their channels

**AI consideration:** Unique to AI - your character can scale.

## Building brand appeal

### What brands look for:

**Audience metrics:**
- Follower count (awareness)
- Engagement rate (quality)
- Demographics (fit)
- Growth trajectory

**Content quality:**
- Production value
- Consistency
- Brand safety
- Professionalism

**Brand fit:**
- Niche alignment
- Values match
- Audience overlap
- Voice compatibility

### Making your AI influencer attractive:

1. **Professional media kit**
2. **Consistent content track record**
3. **Clear engagement metrics**
4. **Demonstrated brand safety**
5. **Unique value proposition**

## Media kit for AI influencers

### Essential sections:

**1. Introduction**
```
[Character Name]
[Tagline]

[Character Name] is an AI-powered [role] helping [audience] with [topic].

Operated by: [Your name/company]
Contact: [email]
```

**2. Platform stats**
```
Platform | Followers | Avg Engagement | Avg Views
---------|-----------|----------------|----------
TikTok   | 50,000    | 8%             | 25,000
Instagram| 25,000    | 5%             | 5,000
YouTube  | 10,000    | 4%             | 3,000
```

**3. Audience demographics**
- Age breakdown
- Gender split
- Location (top countries)
- Interests

**4. Content examples**
- Screenshots of best posts
- Performance metrics
- Brand collaboration examples (if any)

**5. Partnership options**
- What you offer
- Content types
- Pricing tiers (optional)
- Contact for custom

**6. Transparency section**
```
About AI Creation:
[Character Name] is fully AI-generated. Avatar created with [tool],
voice with [tool], content AI-assisted with human oversight.

All sponsored content clearly discloses:
1. The sponsorship relationship
2. The AI nature of the creator

We prioritize FTC compliance and platform guidelines.
```

## Pricing your services

### Pricing factors:

- Follower count
- Engagement rate
- Platform (TikTok > Instagram > YouTube for per-post)
- Niche (B2B pays more than B2C)
- Content complexity
- Usage rights

### Rough pricing guide:

**Micro (1K-10K followers):**
- $50-200 per post
- $200-500 per campaign

**Small (10K-50K followers):**
- $200-1,000 per post
- $500-2,500 per campaign

**Medium (50K-250K followers):**
- $1,000-5,000 per post
- $2,500-15,000 per campaign

**Large (250K+ followers):**
- $5,000+ per post
- $15,000+ per campaign

### AI-specific pricing considerations:

**Potential discount factors:**
- New space, less proven
- Authenticity questions
- Brand uncertainty

**Potential premium factors:**
- Novelty value
- Content consistency
- Unique angle
- Scalability

**Recommendation:** Start 20-30% below comparable human influencers, prove value, raise rates.

**Enable discovery:**
- Business email in bio
- "Open for collaborations" statement
- Professional profile
- Contact page on link-in-bio

**Content that attracts brands:**
- Product reviews (unpaid)
- Industry commentary
- Professional aesthetic
- Engaged audience

**Research targets:**
- Brands your audience uses
- Brands in your niche
- Brands with influencer programs
- Brands competitors work with

- LinkedIn (marketing/partnerships teams)

## Negotiating deals

### What to negotiate:

- Flat fee
- Content requirements
- Timeline
- Revision rounds
- Usage rights
- Exclusivity
- Performance bonuses

### Usage rights considerations:

**Limited use (lower fee):**
- Posts only on your channels
- No repurposing rights
- Time-limited use

**Extended use (higher fee):**
- Brand can repost
- Use in ads (requires premium)
- Unlimited duration

### Exclusivity:

- Category exclusivity (can't work with competitors)
- Should increase fee 25-50%
- Time-limited (3-6 months typical)

### Red flags:

- Full buyout of content rights
- Unlimited revisions
- Payment after 60+ days
- No clear contract
- Unrealistic expectations

## Executing brand deals

### Before agreeing:

1. Understand deliverables completely
2. Review brand guidelines
3. Confirm timeline
4. Get contract signed
5. Receive payment (or payment terms)

### Creation process:

1. Review brief and brand guidelines
2. Create draft content
3. Submit for approval
4. Incorporate feedback
5. Finalize and schedule
6. Publish and confirm

### Post-publish:

1. Share performance metrics
2. Archive content
3. Send final report
4. Maintain relationship

## Disclosure requirements

### FTC compliance (non-negotiable):

- Clear disclosure of sponsorship
- "Ad," "Sponsored," "Paid partnership"
- Early in content (not buried)
- Verbal + visual in video

### AI disclosure (required):

- AI nature mentioned
- Not buried or hidden
- Combined with sponsorship disclosure

### Combined disclosure example:

```
"This video is sponsored by [Brand]. Quick reminder: I'm [Character Name],
an AI, so my avatar and voice are AI-generated. Now let me tell you about [product]..."
```

### Caption format:

```
#ad #sponsored #AIcreator

[Caption content]

[AI disclosure if not in bio/content]
```

## Building long-term brand relationships

### From one-off to ongoing:

1. **Over-deliver** on first campaign
2. **Share results** proactively
3. **Propose next steps**
4. **Stay in touch** between campaigns
5. **Prioritize** repeat partners

### Value adds:

- Early access to metrics
- Suggestions for content
- Going above deliverables
- Quick turnaround
- Easy to work with

### Becoming brand ambassador:

After 2-3 successful campaigns:
- Propose ongoing relationship
- Offer package discount
- Discuss exclusivity
- Create dedicated content pillar

## Handling objections

### "We don't work with AI influencers"

Response:
"I understand it's new territory. What I've found is [specific benefits].
Would you be open to a small test campaign to see results?"

### "How can AI authentically endorse products?"

Response:
"My recommendations are based on thorough research and audience fit, not
personal experience. I'm transparent about being AI, and my audience values
the curated, consistent recommendations. Here's engagement data showing
audience trust..."

### "How do we know the audience is real?"

Response:
"Great question. Here's my analytics showing engagement patterns, audience
demographics, and comment quality. I'm happy to provide platform screenshots
or third-party verification."

### "What about authenticity?"

Response:
"I'm fully transparent about being AI, which actually creates a unique
authenticity. Audiences know what they're getting. My job is delivering
valuable content, and the engagement shows the audience finds value."

## Red flags for AI influencers

### Avoid brands that:

- Want to hide AI nature
- Ask for fake testimonials
- Require claims you can't substantiate
- Push products misaligned with audience
- Have unclear contracts
- Have payment terms beyond 30 days

### Protect your reputation:

- Only work with brands you'd recommend anyway
- Maintain editorial standards
- Don't over-promote
- Keep audience trust primary

## Case study: Cluely approach (analysis)

### What they do right:

1. **Full transparency** - AI nature is front and center
2. **Clear value proposition** - Not pretending to be human
3. **Consistent character** - Recognizable across content
4. **Quality production** - Professional output
5. **Audience-first content** - Value before promotion

### Lessons for your AI influencer:

- Lean into AI identity, don't hide it
- Build character consistency
- Maintain production quality
- Prioritize audience value
- Be straightforward about partnerships

### Why this approach works with brands:

- No authenticity crisis risk
- Clear differentiation
- Novelty still drives attention
- Trust through transparency
- Predictable partnership experience
