# VA cold calling script: App development for businesses

For VAs calling businesses that don't have a mobile app to sell custom app development services. Target: founders, owners, marketing directors at companies with repeat customers.

---

## Pre-call prep

Before calling:
1. Search for their company in App Store and Google Play (confirm no app)
2. Check if competitors in their space have apps
3. Visit their website on mobile (note the experience)
4. Check their industry for app adoption trends

---

## The call script

### Opener (10 seconds)

```
Hi, is this {{firstName}}?

Hey {{firstName}}, this is {{vaName}} with [Company Name].

Quick call. I searched for {{company}} in the App Store and noticed you don't have a mobile app. Given that 85% of consumers prefer apps over mobile websites, I was curious if that's something you've thought about.
```

**If gatekeeper:**
```
Hi, I'm looking for whoever makes decisions about digital strategy or technology at {{company}}. Would that be {{firstName}} or someone on their team?
```

**If asked "What's this about?":**
```
We build custom mobile apps for businesses. Fast and affordable. Noticed {{company}} doesn't have one yet and wanted to see if it's something you're considering.
```

---

### Qualifying questions (30 seconds)

Ask 2-3:

```
1. "Has {{company}} ever looked into building a mobile app? What held you back?"

2. "How do your customers currently interact with you digitally? Website only, or do you use email, SMS?"

3. "Do you have a lot of repeat customers? People who come back regularly?"

4. "Have you noticed any of your competitors launching apps?"

5. "What's your biggest challenge with customer retention or engagement right now?"
```

**Listen for buy signals:**
- "We've thought about it but it seemed expensive"
- "Our competitors have apps"
- "Customers keep asking for one"
- "We struggle with repeat business"
- "We want to do push notifications"
- "Our website isn't great on mobile"

---

### Value pitch (30 seconds)

```
Here's why I'm calling:

Most businesses think building an app costs $50,000 to $100,000 and takes 6 months. That used to be true. Not anymore.

We build custom mobile apps for businesses like {{company}} in 4-6 weeks starting at $5,000. You get:

- A native app on iPhone and Android
- Push notifications to stay in front of customers
- In-app purchases or booking functionality
- Analytics so you can see what's working

We just built an app for {{similarCompany}} in your space. They got 4,200 downloads in the first month and their customer retention went up 40%.
```

---

### Objection handling

**"Too expensive"**
```
What budget range were you thinking? Our projects start at $5k which is 90% less than a traditional agency. And we offer payment plans: 50% upfront, 50% on delivery.

Here's the thing: if an app brings in even 10 new customers per month, it pays for itself in the first few months. After that, it's pure profit.
```

**"We don't need an app"**
```
I hear that a lot. Quick question: how many repeat customers does {{company}} have? [Listen] Those repeat customers would use an app. Push notifications get 3-5x the engagement of email. An app gives you a direct line to your best customers without competing with their inbox.

It's not about whether you need it today. It's about whether your competitors will have one tomorrow.
```

**"We tried building one before and it didn't work out"**
```
What happened? [Listen] That's actually pretty common with traditional development. Projects go over budget and over time.

We use React Native which means one codebase for both iPhone and Android. We scope everything upfront with a fixed price. No surprises. And we guarantee delivery in 4-6 weeks or we extend support for free.
```

**"Our website works fine on mobile"**
```
Your mobile site might be good, but here's what it can't do:
- Send push notifications (3-5x higher engagement than email)
- Be on their home screen as a daily reminder
- Work offline
- Access native phone features like camera, GPS, contacts

Apps create loyalty. Mobile sites are for discovery. They serve different purposes.
```

**"I need to think about it / talk to my partner"**
```
Totally understand. This is a big decision. Here's what I can do:

Let me put together a free 3-screen mockup of what {{company}}'s app could look like. No cost, no obligation. You can show it to your partner and see if it resonates.

Can I get your email to send it over? And what day next week works to follow up?
```

**"Can you email me?"**
```
Sure thing. I'll send over:
- A mockup concept for {{company}}
- A case study from a business in your industry
- Our pricing

Best email? And is {{dayOption1}} or {{dayOption2}} better to follow up?
```

---

### Close

**Option A: Meeting close**
```
Here's what I suggest: let me schedule a 15-minute screen share where I show you 2-3 apps we've built for businesses like {{company}}. I'll give you a rough estimate on what your app would cost and what it would look like.

Does {{dayOption1}} or {{dayOption2}} work better?
```

**Option B: Free mockup close**
```
Tell you what: let me create a free mockup of {{company}}'s app. 3-5 screens showing the home screen, key feature, and user flow. Takes us about 2 hours. No cost to you.

If you love it, we can discuss building it. If not, you keep the mockup. Fair?

What's the best email to send it to?
```

**Option C: Competitor close**
```
I should mention: {{competitor}} in your space just launched an app. They're already collecting push notification subscribers and running in-app promotions.

Every month without an app, you're giving customers a reason to go to the competitor with the better mobile experience.

Can we grab 15 minutes to at least explore what {{company}}'s app could look like?
```

## Call metrics targets

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Calls per hour | 12-15 |
| Connect rate | 20-30% |
| Conversation rate | 55-65% |
| Interest rate | 15-25% |
| Meeting booked rate | 5-12% |

## Best calling times

- Small businesses: 10am-12pm (before afternoon rush)
- Medium businesses: 9-11am or 2-4pm
- Avoid calling during typical customer rush hours for their industry
- Tuesday-Thursday are best days

## Industries to target (highest app adoption need)

| Industry | Pain point | App feature focus |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Restaurants | Repeat orders, loyalty | Online ordering, rewards |
| Gyms/fitness | Member retention | Workout tracking, booking |
| Salons/spas | Scheduling, rebooking | Appointment booking, reminders |
| Dental/medical | Patient communication | Appointment booking, records |
| Real estate | Lead nurture | Property search, saved listings |
| E-commerce | Repeat purchases | Mobile shopping, push sales |
| Education | Student engagement | Course access, notifications |
