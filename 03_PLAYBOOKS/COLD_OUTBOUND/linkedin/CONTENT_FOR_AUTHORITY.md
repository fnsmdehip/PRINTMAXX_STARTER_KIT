# LinkedIn content for authority building

Posts that make your cold DMs warmer by establishing credibility before you pitch.

## Why content matters for outbound

1. **Profile visitors check your posts** - Active profile = credible
2. **Engagement increases visibility** - Your name shows up more
3. **Builds trust before outreach** - They've "seen you around"
4. **Creates inbound opportunities** - People reach out to you
5. **Makes cold DMs warmer** - "Saw your post about X" is easy opener

## Posting frequency

**Minimum viable:** 2-3 posts per week
**Optimal:** Daily (weekdays)
**Diminishing returns:** More than 1 per day

**Best times to post:**
- 7-9 AM (before work starts)
- 12-1 PM (lunch scrolling)
- 5-7 PM (commute/wind-down)

**Consistency matters more than frequency.** 3 posts every week beats 10 posts one week and none the next.

- Mistakes and failures
- Behind-the-scenes

### 4. Opinions and takes (15%)
- Contrarian views
- Industry commentary
- Predictions

### 5. Engagement bait (5%)
- Questions for your audience
- Polls
- Hot takes for discussion
- "What would you do?" scenarios

## Post formats that work

### Format 1: Hook + Story + Lesson

```
[Hook: Surprising statement or question]

[Story: What happened]

[Lesson: What you learned]

[CTA or question]
```

**Example:**
```
I lost a $50K client last month.

Here's what happened:

They asked for a proposal. I sent a generic one.
They went with a competitor who customized everything.

Lesson: Generic proposals lose to personalized ones every time.

Now I spend 2x longer on proposals. Win rate is up 40%.

What's the most painful business lesson you've learned?
```

### Format 2: List post

```
[Number] things I learned about [topic]:

1. [Point 1]
2. [Point 2]
3. [Point 3]
...

[Closing thought or CTA]
```

**Example:**
```
5 things I learned from 100 cold email campaigns:

1. Subject lines matter more than you think
2. Short emails outperform long ones 3:1
3. Following up 5x is not annoying (it's normal)
4. Personalization needs to be visible in first 2 lines
5. Timing matters less than relevance

The biggest lever? Your list quality.

Bad list = bad results, no matter how good your copy.
```

### Format 3: Contrarian take

```
Unpopular opinion: [Your take]

Here's why:

[Reasoning]

[Evidence or example]

[Acknowledge the other side]

[Conclusion]
```

**Example:**
```
Unpopular opinion: Cold email isn't dead.

Here's why people think it is:
- Their emails go to spam (deliverability problem)
- Their copy sounds like spam (writing problem)
- Their lists are garbage (targeting problem)

But when you get these right:
- 50%+ open rates are normal
- 5-10% reply rates are achievable
- Meetings show up on your calendar

Cold email isn't dead. Bad cold email is.
```

### Format 4: Framework/How-to

```
How I [achieve result]:

Step 1: [Action]
Step 2: [Action]
Step 3: [Action]

[Why this works]

[Results or proof]
```

**Example:**
```
How I book 10+ meetings per week from cold outreach:

Step 1: Build a list of 500 targeted prospects (Apollo)
Step 2: Verify emails (ZeroBounce)
Step 3: Write 5-email sequence with personalized first lines
Step 4: Send 100/day from 3 warmed inboxes
Step 5: Follow up with LinkedIn to non-responders

This consistently converts at 2% meeting rate.

500 emails = 10 meetings = 2 clients (at 20% close rate)

Repeatable. Predictable. Boring. Effective.
```

### Format 5: Before/After

```
[Time period] ago: [Old situation]

Today: [New situation]

What changed:

[Key changes]

[Insight or lesson]
```

**Example:**
```
6 months ago: Struggling to book meetings, sending 1000 emails for 2 calls

Today: 10+ meetings per week, consistent pipeline

What changed:

- Stopped sending from one inbox (now using 10)
- Started verifying every email before sending
- Focused on ICP instead of "anyone with a pulse"
- Rewrote all copy to be shorter and more direct

Same effort. 5x the results. Process matters.
```

## Writing tips for LinkedIn

### Hook strategies:
- **Surprising stat:** "90% of cold emails never get read."
- **Contrarian:** "LinkedIn DMs work better than email."
- **Question:** "How do you respond to a prospect who ghosts you?"
- **Story start:** "I made a $10K mistake last week."
- **Personal:** "I almost quit my business in 2022."

### Formatting for mobile:
- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences)
- Line breaks between paragraphs
- Use white space liberally
- No walls of text

### What to avoid:
- Obvious humble brags
- Constant self-promotion
- Generic motivational content
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver
- Posting about LinkedIn itself

## Engagement strategy

### Before posting:
Engage with others' content for 10-15 minutes first. This:
- Puts you in the algorithm's good graces
- Increases visibility of your upcoming post
- Builds relationships with your network

### After posting:
- Reply to every comment in first 2 hours
- Ask follow-up questions to commenters
- Like all comments
- Don't leave the app (activity = reach)

### Daily engagement habit:
- Like 10-15 relevant posts
- Comment on 5-10 posts (meaningful comments, not "Great post!")
- Reply to DMs
- Accept relevant connection requests

## Content repurposing

### From email campaigns:
- Turn cold email copy into posts about what works
- Share anonymized results from campaigns
- Lessons from A/B tests

### From client work:
- Results and case studies (with permission)
- Frameworks you use
- Problems you solve

### From conversations:
- Questions prospects ask
- Objections you hear
- Insights from sales calls

### From other content:
- Podcast clips → Written posts
- Blog posts → LinkedIn posts
- Twitter threads → LinkedIn carousels

## Content calendar structure

### Weekly rhythm:
- **Monday:** Industry insight/trend
- **Tuesday:** How-to or framework
- **Wednesday:** Case study or results
- **Thursday:** Personal story or lesson
- **Friday:** Engagement post (question or poll)

### Monthly themes:
Pick one topic per month to go deep on. This:
- Establishes expertise in specific area
- Creates content clusters
- Builds authority on that topic

## Building a content engine

### Capture ideas:
- Keep running notes file of content ideas
- Screenshot interesting posts for inspiration
- Note questions from sales calls
- Save client feedback and results

### Batch creation:
- Write 5 posts in one session
- Schedule for the week ahead
- Leaves room for timely/reactive posts

### Tools:
- LinkedIn native scheduler
- Taplio (LinkedIn-specific)
- Notion or Google Docs (drafts)
- Canva (if doing images/carousels)

## Content → DM transition

### When someone engages:

**They like your post:**
- Visit their profile
- Connect if not connected
- Note them as warm lead

**They comment:**
- Reply to comment publicly
- Follow up in DM if relevant
- Add to warm outreach list

**They DM you:**
- Respond quickly
- Start conversation
- Transition to call if there's fit

### Cold outreach to engaged contacts:

```
Hey {{firstName}},

Noticed you engaged with my post about [topic].

Curious: is [problem] something {{company}} is dealing with?

We've been helping companies like yours with exactly that.
```

This converts 2-3x better than truly cold outreach.
