# LinkedIn connection strategy

Who to connect with, how to connect, and why it matters for cold outbound.

## Why LinkedIn connections matter

1. **Warm up cold outreach** - Connected prospects are 2-3x more likely to respond
2. **Expand your network** - 2nd-degree connections are visible and more accessible
3. **Build credibility** - Prospects check your profile before responding to emails
4. **Create touchpoints** - LinkedIn + email = multi-channel outreach

## Connection limits

**Free LinkedIn:**
- ~100 connection requests per week (LinkedIn doesn't publish exact limits)
- Lower limit if acceptance rate drops
- Can get restricted for 2-7 days if flagged

**Sales Navigator:**
- Same connection limits (it's still LinkedIn)
- Better targeting to find the right people
- InMail as backup for non-connections

**Safe daily limits:**
- 20-30 connection requests per day
- Higher acceptance rate = more connections allowed
- Stop if you get warnings

## Who to connect with

### Primary targets (highest priority)

**Your ICP decision makers:**
- People you're actively emailing
- People you plan to email
- Past clients/customers

**Why:** Direct path to business conversations.

### Secondary targets (network building)

**Adjacent professionals:**
- People in similar roles at different companies
- Peers in your industry
- Potential referral partners

**Why:** They can introduce you to your ICP.

### Tertiary targets (authority building)

**Thought leaders and influencers:**
- Popular voices in your niche
- Conference speakers
- Podcast hosts

**Why:** Engagement with them increases visibility.

## Connection request types

### No note (often works best)

Just click connect. No message.

**When to use:**
- 2nd-degree connections
- Mutual connections visible
- Your profile clearly explains what you do

**Why it works:**
- Less friction to accept
- No salesy message to trigger rejection
- LinkedIn limits note length anyway (300 chars)

### With note (when you have a reason)

Short, personalized connection request.

**When to use:**
- 3rd-degree connections
- You have a specific reason to connect
- Referral or mutual connection to mention

**Template examples:**

**Mutual connection:**
```
Hi {{firstName}}, saw we're both connected to [Name]. Would love to connect.
```

**Same industry:**
```
Hi {{firstName}}, noticed we're both in [industry]. Always good to connect with others in the space.
```

**Relevant content:**
```
Hi {{firstName}}, enjoyed your post about [topic]. Would love to connect.
```

**Same event/group:**
```
Hi {{firstName}}, saw you're also in [group/attended event]. Would be great to connect.
```

**Do NOT:**
- Pitch in the connection request
- Write more than 2 sentences
- Ask for anything

## Acceptance rate optimization

**Target acceptance rate:** 50%+ (below 30% = too aggressive)

### Improve acceptance rate:

**Profile optimization:**
- Professional headshot (clear face, good lighting)
- Headline that explains what you do (not just job title)
- Banner image that's professional
- Complete profile (experience, about section)

**Target quality:**
- Connect with people in your industry
- Prioritize 2nd-degree connections
- Avoid C-suite at huge companies (low acceptance)
- Target mid-level at mid-size companies (higher acceptance)

**Request quality:**
- Personalize when you have a reason
- Don't pitch
- Don't sound desperate

### Warning signs:

- Acceptance rate below 30% = review targeting
- LinkedIn warning message = pause for 1-2 weeks
- "We noticed unusual activity" = stop immediately

## Daily connection workflow

### Manual process (10-15 min/day):

1. **Review accepted connections** (2 min)
   - Note who accepted
   - Add to "new connections" list for follow-up

2. **Send new requests** (10 min)
   - 20-25 requests to targeted prospects
   - Mix of ICP targets and network builders
   - Personalize when you have a reason

3. **Accept incoming requests** (2 min)
   - Review who's connecting with you
   - Accept relevant connections

### Tracking:
- Log date, number sent, acceptances
- Calculate weekly acceptance rate
- Adjust targeting if rate drops

## Post-connection strategy

### Don't immediately pitch

Wait at least 24-48 hours before messaging.

### Engagement sequence:

**Day 1:** Connection accepted
- Like one of their recent posts (if any)
- No message yet

**Day 2-3:** Light engagement
- Comment on a post (if relevant)
- Or send a simple "thanks for connecting" message

**Day 4+:** Start conversation
- Personalized message (see MESSAGE_TEMPLATES.md)
- Reference something from their profile/content
- Don't pitch yet, start conversation

### The goal:
Move from stranger to familiar face before you pitch anything.

## Segmenting your connections

### Create tags/lists for:

**ICP prospects:**
- Decision makers at target companies
- People you want to sell to
- Organized by industry/company size

**Referral partners:**
- Complementary service providers
- People who serve your ICP
- Potential partners

**Influencers/thought leaders:**
- People with large followings
- Industry voices
- Potential for collaboration

**Past clients/colleagues:**
- People who know your work
- Testimonial sources
- Referral sources

### Tools for organization:
- LinkedIn Tags (basic, built-in)
- Sales Navigator lists
- CRM integration (HubSpot, etc.)
- Simple spreadsheet

## Connection request sequence with email

### Multi-channel approach:

**Day 1:** Send cold email #1
**Day 2:** Send LinkedIn connection request (if email opened)
**Day 3:** Cold email #2
**Day 5:** LinkedIn message (if connected)
**Day 7:** Cold email #3

**Why this works:**
- Multiple touchpoints increase recognition
- LinkedIn humanizes you (they see your face)
- Different channels for different preferences
- Shows persistence without being annoying

## Building a valuable network

### Long-term network strategy:

**Target mix:**
- 40% ICP prospects (direct business)
- 30% peers/similar roles (referrals, learning)
- 20% adjacent industries (cross-pollination)
- 10% thought leaders (visibility)

**Engagement habits:**
- Post 2-3x per week (see CONTENT_FOR_AUTHORITY.md)
- Comment on others' posts daily
- Share valuable content from others
- Celebrate others' wins

**Network value compounds:**
- Connections lead to introductions
- Engagement leads to visibility
- Visibility leads to inbound

## Common mistakes

1. **Pitching in connection request** - Instant reject for most people
2. **Connecting then immediately pitching** - Feels like a trap
3. **Mass connecting without strategy** - Dilutes network quality
4. **Ignoring accepted connections** - Wasted opportunity
5. **Only connecting when you need something** - Looks transactional
6. **Incomplete profile** - Lowers acceptance rate
7. **No engagement after connecting** - Forgettable

## Metrics to track

| Metric | Target | Action if below target |
|--------|--------|----------------------|
| Connection requests sent | 100-150/week | Send more |
| Acceptance rate | 50%+ | Better targeting |
| Messages sent to new connections | 50%+ | Follow up more |
| Reply rate on messages | 30%+ | Better messages |
