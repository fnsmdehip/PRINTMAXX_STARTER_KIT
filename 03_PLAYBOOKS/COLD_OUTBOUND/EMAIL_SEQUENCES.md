# PRINTMAXX cold email sequences

Four battle-tested sequences using @pipelineabuser's 6 questions framework.

## The 6 questions framework

Every cold email should answer these in under 100 words:

1. **What you do** - the service/product
2. **Who for** - the target
3. **How** - the mechanism
4. **Problem solved** - the pain point
5. **Proof** - specific numbers
6. **ROI** - what they get

Stack all 6. No fluff. Consequence-first.

## Sequence 1: App promotion (partner/affiliate)

**Use for:** Getting influencers, content creators, and niche accounts to promote your apps for rev share or flat fee.

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Comp + opportunity |
| 2 | Day 2-3 | Earnings clarity |
| 3 | Day 5-7 | Breakup |

Expected reply rate: 12-20%

---

- C: Quick collab?

## A/B test priorities

For each sequence, test in order:

1. **Subject lines** - biggest impact on open rate
2. **First line** - specific observation vs generic opener
3. **CTA** - "quick call" vs "try it" vs "interested?"
4. **Send time** - 8am vs 11am vs 2pm

## Why clipper/streamer sequences convert higher

You're offering something, not selling. Clippers get paid work. Streamers get free distribution. Agency emails ask for money. Different dynamics.

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Too long** - keep under 100 words
2. **No numbers** - always include specific proof
3. **Weak CTA** - ask a clear yes/no question
4. **Generic opener** - reference something specific they did
5. **Multiple CTAs** - one ask per email
6. **Begging language** - "would you be so kind" energy is cringe
