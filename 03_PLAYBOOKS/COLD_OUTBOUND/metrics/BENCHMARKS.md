# Cold outbound benchmarks

Industry benchmarks for email, LinkedIn, and multi-channel outbound campaigns.

## Email benchmarks by stage

### Open rates

| Performance | Rate | What it means |
|-------------|------|---------------|
| Poor | < 30% | Deliverability issues or bad subject lines |
| Below average | 30-40% | Room for improvement |
| Average | 40-50% | Normal performance |
| Good | 50-60% | Strong subject lines, good deliverability |
| Excellent | 60%+ | Exceptional targeting and copy |

**Factors affecting open rates:**
- Subject line quality (biggest factor)
- Sender reputation
- Deliverability (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
- List quality
- Time of day

### Reply rates

| Performance | Rate | What it means |
|-------------|------|---------------|
| Poor | < 1% | Major issues with targeting or copy |
| Below average | 1-3% | Needs improvement |
| Average | 3-5% | Normal performance |
| Good | 5-10% | Strong campaign |
| Excellent | 10%+ | Exceptional targeting and copy |

**Factors affecting reply rates:**
- Relevance to recipient
- Value proposition clarity
- Personalization quality
- Offer/CTA
- Follow-up sequence

### Positive reply rates

(Replies that express interest, not objections/unsubscribes)

| Performance | Rate | What it means |
|-------------|------|---------------|
| Poor | < 0.5% | Likely targeting issues |
| Below average | 0.5-1% | Needs work |
| Average | 1-2% | Normal |
| Good | 2-4% | Strong campaign |
| Excellent | 4%+ | Exceptional |

### Meeting/call booking rates

| Performance | Rate | What it means |
|-------------|------|---------------|
| Poor | < 0.5% | Broken funnel somewhere |
| Below average | 0.5-1% | Needs optimization |
| Average | 1-2% | Normal |

## Multi-channel benchmarks

### Email + LinkedIn combined

Running email and LinkedIn in parallel typically yields:

| Single channel | Multi-channel | Lift |
|----------------|---------------|------|
| 3% reply (email only) | 5-6% reply | +67-100% |
| 5% reply (email only) | 8-10% reply | +60-100% |

### Optimal multi-channel sequence

| Day | Channel | Expected cumulative reply |
|-----|---------|-------------------------|
| 1 | Email #1 | 1.5% |
| 2 | LinkedIn connect | 1.8% |
| 3 | Email #2 | 2.5% |
| 5 | LinkedIn message | 3.5% |
| 7 | Email #3 | 4.5% |
| 10 | LinkedIn follow-up | 5.5% |
| 14 | Email #4 (breakup) | 6.5% |

## Funnel conversion benchmarks

### Full funnel (cold outreach to customer)

| Stage | Benchmark | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Emails sent → Reply | 3-5% | |
| Reply → Positive reply | 50-70% | Half of replies are objections |
| Positive reply → Meeting | 60-80% | |
| Meeting → Opportunity | 40-60% | Depends on qualification |
| Opportunity → Close | 15-30% | Depends on sales process |

### Example funnel math

**To get 1 customer:**
- Send 1,000 emails
- Get 40 replies (4%)
- 25 positive responses
- Book 18 meetings
- Create 9 opportunities
- Close 2-3 customers

**Revenue impact:**
- Customer value: $5,000
- Customers from 1,000 emails: 2.5
- Revenue: $12,500
- Revenue per email: $12.50

## What good looks like

### Healthy campaign metrics:
- Open rate: 50%+
- Bounce rate: < 2%
- Reply rate: 5%+
- Positive reply rate: 2%+
- Meeting booking rate: 1.5%+
- Spam complaints: < 0.1%

### Scaling targets:
If you can achieve "good" metrics consistently:
- Scale volume while maintaining rates
- Typical degradation: 10-20% per 2x volume
- If metrics hold, keep scaling

## Using benchmarks

### Don't:
- Compare to outlier success stories
- Expect immediate great performance
- Ignore your own baseline

### Do:
- Track your metrics consistently
- Compare to your previous performance
- Use benchmarks as directional guidance
- Optimize one variable at a time
- Celebrate improvement, even if below "average"

### Improvement priorities:
1. Fix bounce rate first (< 2%)
2. Then fix deliverability (50%+ opens)
3. Then fix copy (5%+ replies)
4. Then scale volume
