# Fractional CXO 5-touch sequence

Use this for: Fractional CMOs, CFOs, CTOs, COOs offering part-time executive services.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Stage-specific problem |
| 2 | Day 4 | Full-time vs fractional |
| 3 | Day 8 | What you actually do |
| 4 | Day 13 | Client transformation |
| 5 | Day 19 | Advisory call offer |

Expected reply rate: 6-12%
Expected meeting rate: 3-6%

---

## Email 1: Stage-specific problem

**Subject line variants:**
- A: {{company}}'s [function] at this stage
- B: [Function] gap at {{company}}?
- C: Noticed something about {{company}}

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Companies at {{company}}'s stage usually face a specific [function] challenge:

[1-2 sentences describing common problem for their stage/size]

They need executive-level [function] leadership but can't justify or afford a full-time [C-title].

That's exactly what I help with. I serve as a fractional [C-title] for [X] companies similar to {{company}}, giving you the strategy and leadership without the $[X]k+ salary.

Worth a conversation to see if there's a fit?

[Your name]
Fractional [C-title]
```

**Why it works:**
- Stage-relevant (shows you understand their situation)
- Frames the problem clearly
- Clear value proposition

## Email 2: Full-time vs fractional

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Re: {{company}}'s [function] (reply thread)
- B: Full-time [C-title] vs fractional
- C: $300K vs $[your rate]

**Timing:** 4 days after Email 1

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Quick comparison I share with companies in your position:

**Full-time [C-title]:**
- Salary: $200-350K+ per year
- Benefits, equity, overhead
- Recruiting takes 3-6 months
- Risk: What if they're not the right fit?

**Fractional [C-title]:**
- $[X-Y]K/month (you choose the hours)
- No benefits, no equity required
- Start within 2 weeks
- Risk: Low. If it's not working, easy to adjust.

Most companies at {{company}}'s stage don't need full-time executive bandwidth. They need the right strategy and someone to execute it part-time.

That's where I come in.

Interested in learning more?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Clear cost comparison
- Addresses risk concerns
- Positions fractional as smart choice

## Fractional role positioning

### Fractional CMO:
**Target:** Companies with $1-20M revenue, no marketing leadership
**Pain points:**
- Marketing team with no direction
- Founder doing marketing (poorly)
- Random marketing tactics, no strategy
- Ready to hire but don't know who

**What you do:**
- Marketing strategy and roadmap
- Team hiring and management
- Vendor/agency oversight
- Channel prioritization
- Metrics and reporting

### Fractional CFO:
**Target:** Companies with $2-30M revenue, outgrowing bookkeeper
**Pain points:**
- No financial visibility
- Cash flow surprises
- Can't make data-driven decisions
- Preparing for funding or exit

**What you do:**
- Financial planning and analysis
- Cash flow management
- Investor relations support
- Financial systems and reporting
- Budgeting and forecasting

### Fractional CTO:
**Target:** Non-technical founders, companies with dev team but no leadership
**Pain points:**
- Technical debt piling up
- Dev team with no direction
- Security/compliance concerns
- Scaling technical infrastructure

**What you do:**
- Technical strategy and roadmap
- Dev team hiring and management
- Architecture decisions
- Vendor evaluation
- Technical due diligence

### Fractional COO:
**Target:** Founder-led companies, ops chaos
**Pain points:**
- Founder doing everything
- No systems or processes
- Team growing but no structure
- Customer experience suffering

**What you do:**
- Process design and implementation
- Team structure and hiring
- Vendor and systems management
- Operational metrics
- Founder time liberation

## Pricing guidance

### Common structures:

**Retainer (most common):**
- $3-10K/month for small companies
- $8-15K/month for larger companies
- Defined hours/week (typically 8-20)

**Day rate:**
- $1,500-3,000/day
- Good for project work
- Less commitment from client

**Equity + cash:**
- Lower cash retainer
- 0.25-1% equity
- For early-stage, high-potential

### Pricing positioning:

```
Full-time CMO: $250,000/year salary + benefits = ~$300K total
Fractional CMO at 20 hrs/week: $120,000/year

Client gets 80% of the value at 40% of the cost.
```

## Objection handlers

**"We're not ready for this level"**
```
That's actually when fractional works best. I can help you build the
foundation so when you do need full-time, you hire the right person
into the right role with the right systems already in place.
```

**"We can't afford it"**
```
Understand. Let's look at it differently. What's the cost of NOT having
[function] leadership?

For most companies I talk to, it's:
- [Specific cost 1]
- [Specific cost 2]
- [Opportunity cost]

At $[X]/month, if I prevent even one of those, the ROI is there.
```

**"We want to hire full-time"**
```
Good goal. Fractional can actually help you get there faster:
1. I build the function while you search for the right person
2. I help you write the job description and interview candidates
3. I train the new hire and transition out

Some fractional engagements last 6 months. Some last 2 years. It's flexible.
```

**"How do you split time across clients?"**
```
I typically work with [X] clients simultaneously. Here's how I manage it:
- Dedicated days/hours for each client
- Clear communication expectations
- Async updates between meetings
- You're never waiting on me

This model actually works better than full-time for most companies at
your stage because you're paying for my 20 hours of focused work, not
40 hours of meetings and busywork.
```
