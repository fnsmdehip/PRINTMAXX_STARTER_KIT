# Accounting services 5-touch sequence

Use this for: Bookkeeping services, CFO services, tax preparation, accounting firms.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Financial visibility problem |
| 2 | Day 3 | What you're missing |
| 3 | Day 7 | Tax savings angle |
| 4 | Day 12 | Clean books client story |
| 5 | Day 17 | Free book review |

Expected reply rate: 5-10%
Expected meeting rate: 2-5%

## Email 1: Financial visibility

**Subject line variants:**
- A: {{company}} financials
- B: Quick question about your books
- C: Financial clarity

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Quick question: Do you know exactly how much {{company}} made last month? Not roughly. Exactly.

Most founders at your stage can't answer that. The books are "probably fine" but no one really knows.

We help companies like {{company}} get financial clarity so you can make decisions based on real numbers, not guesses.

Recent client went from "I think we're profitable" to "I know we're profitable by $X and here's why."

Worth a conversation?

[Your name]
[Your firm]
```

**Why it works:**
- Direct question creates reflection
- Common problem resonates
- Specific transformation

**Body:**

```

**With clean books:**
- Know your exact margins by product/service
- Investment-ready financials
- Tax planning throughout the year (fewer surprises)

The founders who understand their numbers make better decisions. Period.

Want to see what {{company}}'s numbers actually look like?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Clear before/after
- Multiple pain points
- Decision-making angle

## Service positioning

### Monthly bookkeeping:
- $300-1,500/month depending on volume
- Bank reconciliation
- Transaction categorization
- Monthly reports
- Best for: Ongoing need, don't want to hire

### Catch-up bookkeeping:
- $500-5,000 depending on how behind
- One-time cleanup
- Historical reconciliation
- Best for: Messy books, need to start fresh

### CFO services:
- $1,000-5,000/month
- Financial strategy
- Forecasting and budgeting
- Investor relations support
- Best for: Growth stage, raising capital

### Tax preparation:
- $500-5,000 depending on complexity
- Business returns
- Personal returns (for owners)
- State filings
- Best for: Annual need

### Full-service (book + tax + CFO):
- $2,000-8,000/month
- Everything above
- Integrated approach
- Best for: Want one partner for all finance

## Target segments

### Startups (funded):
**Pain:** Investor reporting, burn rate tracking
**Angle:** "Clean books make fundraising easier"
**Trigger:** Just raised, preparing to raise

### Professional services:
**Pain:** Time better spent on clients
**Angle:** "Focus on what you do, we'll handle books"
**Trigger:** Growing firm, new partners

### Ecommerce:
**Pain:** Inventory, COGS, multi-channel complexity
**Angle:** "Ecommerce books are uniquely complex"
**Trigger:** Crossing $500K, adding channels

### Agencies:
**Pain:** Project profitability, contractor payments
**Angle:** "Know your margins by client and project"
**Trigger:** Adding team, not sure about profitability

### Local businesses:
**Pain:** Don't want to think about books
**Angle:** "Set it and forget it bookkeeping"
**Trigger:** Tax season pain, looking for accountant

## Objection handlers

**"We use QuickBooks/Xero, we're fine"**
```
Great tools. The question is: who's actually running them?

Most companies have a tool but:
- Transactions aren't categorized properly
- Reports don't match reality
- No one's reconciling regularly

The tool is just a tool. You need someone managing it.
```

**"We have a bookkeeper"**
```
How current are the books? Are they closing monthly? Do you get
reports you actually understand?

Many companies have someone doing data entry but no one doing
strategic bookkeeping or advising on the numbers.

We can work alongside them or take over completely.
```

**"We're too small"**
```
This is actually the best time to start.

Fixing messy books later costs way more than keeping them clean
from the start.

Plus, for companies your size, our rates are [affordable option].
```

**"I'll deal with it at tax time"**
```
That's what most people say. Then tax time comes and it's:
- Rush to find receipts
- Pay premium rates for emergency cleanup
- Miss deductions because books weren't organized
- Stress for a month

Proactive bookkeeping costs less than reactive tax prep panic.
```
