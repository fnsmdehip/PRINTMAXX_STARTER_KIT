# Legal services B2B 5-touch sequence

Use this for: Corporate lawyers, IP attorneys, employment lawyers targeting businesses as clients.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Risk/compliance trigger |
| 2 | Day 4 | What proper counsel provides |
| 3 | Day 9 | War story (anonymized) |
| 4 | Day 14 | Free consultation offer |
| 5 | Day 20 | Last touch |

Expected reply rate: 4-8%
Expected meeting rate: 2-4%

---

## Email 1: Risk trigger

**Subject line variants:**
- A: {{company}} [legal area] compliance
- B: Quick question for {{firstName}}
- C: [Industry] legal update

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Quick question: Does {{company}} have proper [legal area] coverage in place?

Companies at your stage often don't until something goes wrong. By then it's expensive.

Common issues I help companies like yours prevent:
- [Issue 1 relevant to their stage/industry]
- [Issue 2]
- [Issue 3]

I work with [X] companies similar to {{company}} to get these things right before they become problems.

Worth a 15-minute call to see if you're covered?

[Your name]
[Your firm]
```

**Why it works:**
- Risk framing gets attention
- Stage-appropriate concerns
- Prevention positioning

```
[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**

- Cost comparison
- Predictable pricing angle

## Practice area positioning

### Employment law:
**Target:** Companies with 10-500 employees
**Key concerns:**
- Employee handbook
- Hiring/firing practices
- Non-compete agreements
- Discrimination claims
- Wage and hour compliance

### Corporate/startup law:
**Target:** Funded startups, growing companies
**Key concerns:**
- Entity formation/structuring
- Cap table management
- Funding documentation
- Shareholder agreements
- M&A preparation

### Intellectual property:
**Target:** Tech companies, creative businesses
**Key concerns:**
- Trademark protection
- Patent filings
- Copyright issues
- Trade secret protection
- Licensing agreements

### Contracts/commercial:
**Target:** B2B companies, service businesses
**Key concerns:**
- Client contracts
- Vendor agreements
- Partnership deals
- Terms of service
- Privacy policies

## Pricing models for legal services

### Hourly (traditional):
- $250-600/hour depending on experience
- Unpredictable for clients
- Better for complex litigation

### Fixed fee (per project):
- Contract review: $500-2,000
- Employment handbook: $2,000-5,000
- Entity formation: $1,500-3,000
- Good for defined scope work

### Monthly retainer:
- $1,500-5,000/month
- Covers ongoing needs
- Predictable for clients
- Builds long-term relationship

### Fractional GC:
- $3,000-10,000/month
- Part-time general counsel
- For companies not ready for in-house
- Comprehensive coverage

## Finding legal service leads

### Triggers that create need:
- Recent funding (need proper docs)
- Hiring milestone (10, 50, 100 employees)
- New product launch (IP protection)
- Contract disputes (visible in public records)
- Regulatory changes in their industry

### Observable signals:
- No legal page on website (no terms, privacy policy)
- Recent bad reviews mentioning legal issues
- Hiring for legal/compliance roles
- Industry facing regulatory pressure

## Objection handlers

**"We already have a lawyer"**
```
Good to hear. What do they specialize in?

Most companies have a generalist but lack specialists for specific areas
like [your specialty].

It's not about replacing. It's about having the right expertise for
the right issues.
```

**"We can't afford a lawyer right now"**
```
Understand. Most companies feel that way.

But here's the math: spending $X now on proper contracts and compliance
prevents spending $10-100X later on disputes and penalties.

We have options for smaller companies. Flat-fee basics that fit most
budgets. Worth discussing what those look like?
```

**"We'll deal with it when we need to"**
```
That's what most companies say. And it works... until it doesn't.

The challenge is that by the time you "need to," you're already in
reactive mode. Costs more. Stress more. Worse outcomes.

A 30-minute call now could save you all of that later.
```

**"We use online legal services (LegalZoom, etc.)"**
```
Those work for basic templates.

But generic documents don't account for:
- Your specific business model
- State-specific requirements
- Industry-specific regulations
- What actually happens when there's a dispute

You get what you pay for. Sometimes that's fine. Sometimes it's a
ticking time bomb. Worth knowing which you have.
```

- Open rate: 45-55%

- Legal not always top of mind
