# Ecommerce services 5-touch sequence

Use this for: Email marketing agencies, Shopify developers, conversion optimization, paid ads services targeting DTC brands.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Store-specific observation |
| 2 | Day 3 | Revenue opportunity |
| 3 | Day 7 | Quick audit offer |
| 4 | Day 11 | Case study match |
| 5 | Day 16 | Breakup |

Expected reply rate: 5-10%
Expected meeting rate: 2-4%

---

## Email 1: Store observation opener

**Subject line variants:**
- A: {{company}} [store observation]
- B: Quick thought on your store
- C: [Product category] + [your service]

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Checked out {{company}} and noticed [specific observation about their store]:

[1-2 sentence observation about something you could improve]

For stores doing your volume, this typically means [X revenue opportunity or problem].

We've helped [similar brand] fix exactly this and they saw [specific result].

Worth a quick call to see if we can do the same for {{company}}?

[Your name]
[Your company]
```

**Why it works:**
- Shows you visited their store
- Specific observation, not generic pitch
- Clear revenue angle

---

## Email 2: Revenue opportunity

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Re: {{company}} (reply thread)
- B: $[X]k opportunity
- C: Quick math on {{company}}

**Timing:** 3 days after Email 1

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Did some quick math on {{company}}.

Based on [observable metric: traffic, reviews, social following], you're likely doing [estimated revenue or order volume].

Stores at this level typically leave [X%] on the table by [problem you solve].

That's potentially $[X]/month in additional revenue.

Here's how we found that for [similar brand]: [1-2 sentence explanation]

Would it be worth 15 minutes to see if the same applies to you?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Specific numbers get attention
- Revenue opportunity is compelling
- Shows you understand their business

---

## Email 3: Free audit offer

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Free [audit type] for {{company}}
- B: Quick audit offer
- C: [Specific deliverable]

**Timing:** 4 days after Email 2

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Want to offer you a free [audit type: email audit, CRO audit, ads audit].

What you'd get:
- Analysis of your current [area]
- Top 3 opportunities we'd prioritize
- Rough revenue impact estimate
- Action plan whether you work with us or not

Takes me [X] minutes to put together. Yours to keep either way.

Interested?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Free value reduces friction
- Clear deliverable
- No risk for them

---

## Email 4: Case study match

**Subject line variants:**
- A: How [similar brand] did it
- B: [Category] case study
- C: {{company}} reminded me of this

**Timing:** 4 days after Email 3

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Wanted to share a case study since [similar brand] reminds me of {{company}}.

**The brand:** [Similar company in same space]
**The problem:** [Problem they had]
**What we did:** [Brief explanation]
**The result:** [Specific metrics]

Full case study here: [link]

If {{company}} is dealing with similar challenges, we should talk.

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Relevant proof
- Specific results
- Easy next step

---

## Email 5: The breakup

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Should I close this out?
- B: Last note from me
- C: Moving on

**Timing:** 5 days after Email 4

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Haven't heard back, so I'll assume the timing isn't right.

Before I go, one last thought:

Peak season is [X months] away. The brands that crush Q4 start optimizing [their thing] now.

If you want to revisit before then, just reply to this email.

Either way, wishing {{company}} a strong year.

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Creates gentle urgency
- Seasonal hook is relevant
- Professional close

## Customization by service

### Email marketing (Klaviyo, etc.)

**Observation examples:**
- "Signed up for your list and noticed [observation about welcome flow]"
- "Your pop-up is [observation about conversion optimization]"
- "Didn't see an abandoned cart email after leaving items"

**Metrics to use:**
- Email revenue as % of total (benchmark: 25-40%)
- Flow revenue vs campaign revenue
- List growth rate

### CRO/Conversion optimization

**Observation examples:**
- "Your mobile checkout is [X] steps, which usually causes [problem]"
- "Your product pages are missing [social proof element]"
- "Noticed your site speed is [X] seconds on mobile"

**Metrics to use:**
- Conversion rate by device
- Cart abandonment rate
- Average order value

### Paid ads (Meta, Google)

**Observation examples:**
- "Seeing your ads on [platform] with [observation about creative/targeting]"
- "Your competitor [X] is running [type of campaign] you might want to consider"
- "Noticed you're not running [campaign type] which usually converts well for [category]"

**Metrics to use:**
- ROAS benchmarks for category
- Spend efficiency
- New customer acquisition cost

### Shopify development

**Observation examples:**
- "Your site loads in [X] seconds, which costs you [estimated conversion loss]"
- "Your theme is [observation about limitations]"
- "Your checkout doesn't have [feature that improves conversion]"

**Metrics to use:**
- Page speed scores
- Mobile vs desktop conversion gap
- Checkout completion rate

## Ecommerce-specific research

### Before sending, check:
- [ ] Their Shopify store (can often see theme, apps)
- [ ] SimilarWeb for traffic estimates
- [ ] Facebook Ad Library for their ads
- [ ] Their email signup flow
- [ ] Reviews on product pages
- [ ] Mobile experience

### Tools:
- **SimilarWeb:** Traffic estimates
- **BuiltWith:** Tech stack
- **Facebook Ad Library:** Active ads
- **Google PageSpeed:** Site speed
- **Shopify Inspector (Chrome):** Theme and apps

---

## Performance benchmarks for ecom outreach

**Ecommerce tends to convert well:**
- Open rate: 50-60%
- Reply rate: 6-12%
- Meeting rate: 3-5%

**Why:** DTC founders are metrics-driven, always looking for growth levers, used to working with agencies.

---

## Timing considerations

**Best times to reach out:**
- Post-holiday (January-February): Reviewing performance
- Pre-Q4 (July-September): Preparing for peak
- After funding: Recently raised brands have budget

**Avoid:**
- November-December: Too busy with holiday
- Right after bad quarters: Defensive, cutting costs
