# Investor outreach 3-touch sequence

Use this for: Startups raising capital, reaching out to VCs, angels, and strategic investors.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Thesis fit + traction |
| 2 | Day 5 | News/milestone update |
| 3 | Day 12 | Simple ask |

Expected reply rate: 10-25% (highly dependent on traction)
Expected meeting rate: 5-12%

---

## Email 1: Thesis fit opener

**Subject line variants:**
- A: [Company name] - [one-line description] [raising $X]
- B: Intro: [Company name] - [metric/traction]
- C: [Investor's portfolio company] competitor

**Body:**

```
Hi [Investor first name],

[One sentence that shows why you're emailing THEM specifically]:
- "Saw you led [relevant portfolio company]'s seed round..."
- "Your thesis on [topic] resonated..."
- "Given your investments in [category]..."

**[Company name]:** [One sentence description - what you do]

**Traction:**
- [Key metric 1 - revenue, users, growth rate]
- [Key metric 2]
- [Optional: Key metric 3]

**Raising:** $[X] to [what you'll do with the money]

[Founder credibility: 1-2 sentences on why you're the right team]

Worth a 20-minute call to share more?

[Your name]
Founder, [Company]
[Link to deck or website]
```

**Why it works:**
- Shows why THEM specifically
- Metrics upfront
- Clear ask

---

## Email 2: Milestone update

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Re: [Company name] (reply thread)
- B: [Company name] update: [new milestone]
- C: Quick update

**Timing:** 5 days after Email 1

**Body:**

```
Hi [Investor first name],

Quick update since my last note:

[New milestone or traction point]:
- "Hit $[X] MRR this week"
- "Signed [notable customer]"
- "Closed [other investor] in the round"
- "Launched [feature] with [result]"

The round is coming together. [X%] committed so far.

Would love to include you in this round if there's interest.

Open to a quick call this week?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Shows momentum
- Creates urgency
- Round progress signals social proof

---

## Email 3: Simple final ask

**Subject line variants:**
- A: One more try
- B: Still open
- C: Final note

**Timing:** 7 days after Email 2

**Body:**

```
Hi [Investor first name],

Last follow-up.

If [Company name] isn't a fit for [Fund name], totally understand. But wanted to give it one more shot.

[One compelling reason to meet]:
- "We're now at $[X] MRR and growing [Y%] MoM"
- "Just closed [notable customer] as a customer"
- "[Relevant investor] just committed"

If there's any interest, I'd love 15 minutes.

If not a fit, would you mind pointing me toward anyone in your network who might be interested?

Thanks either way.

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Low pressure
- Referral ask if not interested
- Professional close

## Investor targeting

### Before emailing, verify:

**Thesis fit:**
- Do they invest in your stage (pre-seed, seed, Series A)?
- Do they invest in your category/vertical?
- Have they invested in similar companies?
- What's their typical check size?

**Sources:**
- Fund website (thesis, portfolio)
- LinkedIn (past investments)
- Crunchbase (portfolio, recent deals)
- Twitter (what they're interested in)

### Red flags (don't email):
- Wrong stage (Series B fund, you're pre-seed)
- Portfolio conflict (invested in direct competitor)
- Not actively investing
- No clear thesis that includes you

## Personalization tactics

### Why them specifically (use one):
- "You led [portfolio company]'s round in [similar space]"
- "Your post about [topic] resonated with our thesis"
- "Given your background in [industry]..."
- "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out"
- "Your investment in [company] showed you understand [market]"

### Connection warmth levels:

**Warm intro (best):**
- Have a mutual connection make the intro
- 2-3x more likely to get a meeting

**Referral mention (good):**
- "[Person] from [company] suggested I reach out"
- Use only if you have permission

**Cold but researched (acceptable):**
- Show clear thesis fit
- Mention specific portfolio companies
- Reference their content/posts

## Traction framing

### What to lead with (pick strongest):

**Revenue/MRR:**
- "$[X] MRR, growing [Y%] MoM"
- "$[X] ARR, [Y]x YoY growth"

**Users/customers:**
- "[X] paying customers"
- "[X] DAUs, [Y]% WoW growth"

**Retention:**
- "[X]% monthly retention"
- "[Y]% NRR"

**Notable customers:**
- "[Logo] and [Logo] as customers"
- "[X] Fortune 500 companies"

**Waitlist/interest:**
- "[X] on waitlist"
- "[Y] beta users"

### If pre-traction:
- Lead with team credibility
- Prior exits
- Domain expertise
- Early signals (design partners, LOIs)

## Deck attachment strategy

### Options:

**Link to deck (recommended):**
- Docsend or Notion link
- Can track opens
- Can update easily

**Attach PDF:**
- More direct
- Some investors prefer
- Can't track or update

**No deck, offer to send:**
- Creates conversation
- Can be seen as playing hard to get
- Only if deck isn't ready

### Docsend tips:
- See who viewed and how long
- Require email for access (know who's viewing)
- Update deck even after sending link

## Timing considerations

### Best times to raise:
- **Q1 (Jan-Mar):** New fund years, fresh budgets
- **Q4 (Oct-Nov):** Before holiday slowdown

### Avoid:
- **Late December:** Holiday mode
- **August:** Vacation season
- **Mid-July:** Partner meetings, summer lull

### Day/time:
- Tuesday-Thursday mornings
- Avoid Monday (inbox flood) and Friday (weekend mode)

## Following up after meeting

### If positive:
```
[Investor name],

Great meeting today.

As discussed:
- [Key point 1]
- [Key point 2]
- [Next step you agreed on]

Happy to provide any additional materials. Looking forward to next steps.

[Your name]
```

### If they passed:
```
[Investor name],

Thanks for considering [Company name]. I appreciate your time.

Would you be open to reconnecting in 6 months when we've hit [milestone]?

Also, is there anyone in your network you'd recommend we talk to?

[Your name]
```

## Common reasons for no response

1. **Wrong fit:** Stage, sector, or check size mismatch
2. **Weak traction:** Not enough to warrant meeting
3. **Bad timing:** Fund not actively deploying
4. **Lost in inbox:** Happens, worth following up
5. **No differentiation:** Email looks like every other pitch

### How to improve:
- Better targeting (right investors)
- Stronger traction (build more first)
- Warmer intros (leverage network)
- Better subject lines (stand out)
- Cleaner ask (make it easy)

## Metrics for investor outreach

**Response rate benchmarks:**
- Cold email: 10-20% response rate
- Warm intro: 30-50% response rate
- Post-meeting interest: 5-20%

**Typical funnel:**
- 100 emails sent
- 15 responses
- 10 first meetings
- 3 second meetings
- 1 term sheet

This means: Plan to contact 100+ investors for a seed round.

## Quick reference: What to include

**Must have:**
- [ ] Why them specifically
- [ ] What you do (one sentence)
- [ ] Key traction metrics (2-3)
- [ ] What you're raising and for what
- [ ] Clear ask

**Optional but helpful:**
- [ ] Team credibility
- [ ] Notable customers/logos
- [ ] Round progress
- [ ] Deck link

**Avoid:**
- Generic mass email feel
- Too much text
- Asking for intro meeting before they know anything
- Overconfidence without proof
- Underselling (be proud of what you've built)
