# Event/podcast sponsorship outreach 3-touch sequence

Use this for: Selling sponsorships, advertising packages, or partnership opportunities to brands.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Audience match pitch |
| 2 | Day 4 | Social proof + ROI |
| 3 | Day 10 | Limited availability |

Expected reply rate: 10-20%
Expected sponsorship rate: 3-8%

---

## Email 1: Audience match opener

**Subject line variants:**
- A: [Event/Show name] + {{company}} sponsorship
- B: Reaching [audience description] through [Event/Show]
- C: Partnership opportunity

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

[Event/Show name] reaches [X] [audience description].

Looking at {{company}}, your customers are [similar audience description]. That's a strong overlap.

**About us:**
- [What it is: podcast, newsletter, event, etc.]
- [Size: X listeners, attendees, subscribers]
- [Audience: who they are, demographics, interests]

**Sponsorship includes:**
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]
- [Benefit 3]

Previous sponsors include [Names if impressive].

Would {{company}} be interested in reaching this audience?

[Your name]
[Event/Show name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Clear audience match
- Specific numbers
- Shows value upfront

---

## Email 2: Social proof + ROI

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Re: Sponsorship (reply thread)
- B: What sponsors get from [Event/Show]
- C: Results from previous sponsors

**Timing:** 4 days after Email 1

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Quick follow-up with some sponsor results:

**[Sponsor 1]:**
- [What they sponsored]
- [Result: leads, signups, traffic, etc.]
- "[Quote if available]"

**[Sponsor 2]:**
- [What they sponsored]
- [Result]

**Why it works:**
- Our audience is [specific characteristic that makes them valuable]
- They trust our recommendations
- They take action (conversion rate, engagement stats)

**Investment:** $[X] for [sponsorship package]

For {{company}}, this could mean [estimated result based on their business].

Worth discussing?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Proof of results
- Specific numbers
- ROI framing

---

## Email 3: Limited availability

**Subject line variants:**
- A: [X] sponsor spots left
- B: Last call for [Event/Show name]
- C: Final availability

**Timing:** 6 days after Email 2

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Last note on sponsorship.

We have [X] spots remaining for [time period/event].

[Optional: Name drop] - [Other company] just confirmed, so we're down to [X] remaining slots.

If {{company}} wants to reach [audience], this is the window.

After these spots fill, next availability is [future date].

Interested in one of the remaining spots?

[Your name]

P.S. Happy to send a quick media kit with full details if helpful.
```

**Why it works:**
- Real scarcity
- Social proof from confirmed sponsors
- Clear deadline

## Sponsorship package positioning

### Podcast sponsorship:

**What to include:**
- Pre-roll, mid-roll, or post-roll spots
- Host-read vs produced ad
- Show notes link
- Social media mentions
- Newsletter inclusion

**Pricing benchmarks:**
- $15-50 CPM (cost per 1,000 downloads)
- Small podcasts (<5K downloads): $200-500/episode
- Mid-size (5-25K): $500-2,500/episode
- Large (25K+): $2,500-10,000+/episode

### Newsletter sponsorship:

**What to include:**
- Featured sponsor spot
- Banner/image placement
- Dedicated send option
- Click tracking

**Pricing benchmarks:**
- $10-50 CPM
- Small lists (<5K): $100-300/send
- Mid-size (5-25K): $300-1,500/send
- Large (25K+): $1,500-10,000+/send

### Event sponsorship:

**What to include:**
- Logo placement
- Speaking opportunity
- Booth/table
- Attendee list access
- Social media promotion

**Pricing:**
- Virtual events: $1,000-10,000
- Local events: $500-5,000
- Major conferences: $5,000-100,000+

## Finding sponsor prospects

### Who to target:
- Companies whose target customer = your audience
- Competitors of current sponsors
- Companies advertising in similar channels
- Brands that sponsor similar properties
- Companies with marketing budget (funded, growing)

### Where to find them:
- LinkedIn (marketing leaders)
- Similar podcasts/newsletters (who sponsors them?)
- Conference sponsor lists
- Industry publications (who advertises?)
- Crunchbase (recently funded companies)

### Research before pitching:
- What do they sell?
- Who is their customer?
- What other sponsorships do they do?
- Who makes sponsorship decisions?

---

## Targeting the right contact

### Job titles to target:
- VP of Marketing
- Director of Marketing
- Head of Growth
- Brand Marketing Manager
- Partnership Manager
- Media Buyer

### For smaller companies:
- Founder/CEO
- Head of Marketing
- Growth Lead

### Finding contact info:
- LinkedIn
- Company website (team page)
- Apollo, Hunter.io
- Podcast interviews (founders often mention marketing team)

## Sponsorship deck essentials

### Must include:
1. **About [Property]** - What it is, history
2. **Audience** - Size, demographics, interests
3. **Sponsorship options** - Tiers and pricing
4. **What's included** - Detailed benefits
5. **Previous sponsors** - Logos, results
6. **Testimonials** - Quotes from sponsors
7. **Next steps** - How to get started

### Design tips:
- Clean, professional design
- Real data and numbers
- Show ad placements visually
- Keep it under 10 pages

## Objection handlers

**"We don't have budget for sponsorships"**
```
Understand. Marketing budgets are tight.

Two options:
1. Smaller commitment - We have a [smaller package] at $[X]
2. Performance-based - We can discuss a model where you pay based on results

What would need to be true for sponsorship to make sense for {{company}}?
```

**"We've tried podcast/newsletter sponsorship before and it didn't work"**
```
That happens. Usually it's one of three things:
1. Wrong audience match
2. Wrong creative/message
3. Not enough frequency

Our audience is specifically [description]. Let me share how it's different
from what you've tried before.
```

**"Send me the rate card"**
```
Happy to send the full media kit.

Quick question: What's {{company}} trying to achieve with sponsorships?
Brand awareness, lead gen, or something else?

That way I can highlight the most relevant options.
```

**"We'll think about it"**
```
Of course. What information would help you decide?

I should mention we have [X] spots available for [time period] and
[other sponsors] are currently in conversations.

Would it help to do a quick call to answer any questions?
```

## Following up after sponsorship

### During campaign:
- Share early results
- Send listener/reader feedback
- Over-communicate

### After campaign:
```
{{firstName}},

Now that [campaign/episode] has aired, wanted to share results:

- [Metric 1: impressions, downloads, opens]
- [Metric 2: clicks, conversions if trackable]
- [Engagement: any notable responses]

How does this compare to what you expected?

If results are positive, we'd love to discuss [next opportunity].

[Your name]
```

### For renewal:
```
{{firstName}},

Coming up on [X months] since {{company}}'s last sponsorship.

We've got [new opportunity/upcoming event/special placement] that might be a fit.

Based on your last results, this could drive [estimated result].

Interested in round two?

[Your name]
```

## Performance benchmarks

**Sponsorship outreach:**
- Open rate: 45-55%
- Reply rate: 12-22%
- Sponsorship close rate: 5-12%

**Why higher rates:**
- Transactional relationship (they pay, you deliver)
- Clear value exchange
- Marketing budget dedicated for this

**Key success factor:** Audience match. If your audience is clearly their customer, it's an easy conversation.
