# IT managed services 5-touch sequence

Use this for: MSPs, IT support, cybersecurity services, cloud management.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Security/downtime risk |
| 2 | Day 3 | Cost of current approach |
| 3 | Day 7 | What managed IT covers |
| 4 | Day 11 | Client case study |
| 5 | Day 16 | Free assessment |

Expected reply rate: 4-8%
Expected meeting rate: 2-4%

## Email 1: Risk opener

**Subject line variants:**
- A: {{company}} IT security
- B: Quick IT question
- C: Downtime risk

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Quick question: What would happen to {{company}} if your systems went down for 24 hours?

For most businesses your size:
- Lost revenue from inability to serve customers
- Productivity loss across all employees
- Customer trust damage

Companies like {{company}} typically don't have dedicated IT... until something breaks.

We help [X] businesses like yours prevent these problems with proactive IT management.

Worth a conversation before something goes wrong?

[Your name]
[Your company]
```

**Why it works:**
- Fear-based but realistic
- Quantifies the risk
- Proactive vs reactive framing

## MSP service tiers

### Basic (monitoring only):
- $30-50/user/month
- Remote monitoring
- Help desk
- Antivirus
- Basic backups

### Standard (full managed):
- $75-125/user/month
- Everything in Basic
- On-site support
- Security training
- Vendor management

### Premium (full + security):
- $125-200/user/month
- Everything in Standard
- Advanced security (SIEM, MDR)
- Compliance support
- vCIO services

### Enterprise:
- Custom pricing
- Dedicated resources
- 24/7/365 support
- SLA guarantees

## Target industries for MSPs

### High-value targets:
- **Healthcare:** HIPAA compliance needs
- **Financial services:** Security requirements
- **Legal firms:** Data protection critical
- **Accounting firms:** Tax season reliability
- **Manufacturing:** OT/IT convergence

### Pain point triggers:
- Recent security incident in their industry
- New compliance requirements
- Growing headcount (scaling IT)

- Key IT person leaving

## Objection handlers

**"We have an IT guy"**
```
Great. What happens when they're sick? On vacation? Leave the company?

Most businesses with one IT person are one resignation away from chaos.
We don't replace your IT person. We augment them, giving them support
and filling gaps.
```

**"We use cloud/SaaS, don't need IT"**
```
Cloud shifts where IT happens, not whether you need it.

You still have:
- Endpoints to secure (laptops, phones)
- User management
- Integration challenges
- Data backup needs
- Security threats targeting your users

Cloud actually increases complexity in some ways.
```

**"Too expensive"**
```
What are you spending now on IT issues?

Add up:
- Break-fix calls (hourly)
- Lost productivity during outages
- Owner/employee time spent on IT
- Risk of major incident

Most companies find managed IT costs the same or less with way less risk.
```

**"We've never had a problem"**
```
That's lucky. But [X%] of small businesses experience a significant
cyber incident each year.

It's not if, it's when.

The question is: do you want to be prepared, or scrambling?
```

## Performance benchmarks

**MSP outreach:**
- Open rate: 45-55%
- Reply rate: 5-10%
- Meeting rate: 2-5%

**Why moderate:**
- IT is seen as cost center
- Often owner isn't feeling pain (yet)
- Competitive market in most regions

**Best results:** Targeting companies with recent triggers (growth, incident, compliance change)
