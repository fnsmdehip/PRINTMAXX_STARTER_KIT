# Local business services 4-touch sequence

Use this for: Marketing, web design, or other services targeting local businesses (restaurants, retail, professional services).

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Local observation |
| 2 | Day 3 | Competitor comparison |
| 3 | Day 7 | Local success story |
| 4 | Day 12 | Simple offer |

Expected reply rate: 6-12%
Expected meeting rate: 3-6%

---

## Email 1: Local observation

**Subject line variants:**
- A: [Business name] + [city]
- B: Quick thought about [Business name]
- C: Saw [Business name] on [street/platform]

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

I'm based in [city/area] and came across [Business name].

Noticed [specific observation]:
- "Your Google listing doesn't have updated photos"
- "Your website isn't showing up for [local keyword]"
- "Your Yelp reviews mention [issue you could help with]"
- "Your Instagram hasn't been updated in [X] months"

For local businesses like yours, [what you do] can [specific benefit].

I've helped [X] businesses in [city/area] with similar challenges.

Worth a quick call to see if I can help?

[Your name]
[Your company]
[Optional: Local address/phone]
```

**Why it works:**
- Local angle builds trust
- Specific observation shows effort
- Relatable to small business owners

---

## Email 2: Competitor angle

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Re: [Business name] (reply thread)
- B: How you compare locally
- C: [Competitor] is doing this

**Timing:** 3 days after Email 1

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Did some research on [your service area] in [city].

Here's how [Business name] compares to competitors:

**[Business name]:**
- [Metric 1: reviews, search ranking, etc.]
- [Metric 2]

**[Competitor 1]:**
- [Their metrics]

**[Competitor 2]:**
- [Their metrics]

[Competitor] is [doing X], which is why they're [winning in Y way].

We can help [Business name] catch up (or get ahead).

Want to see how?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Competitive data gets attention
- Shows you understand local market
- Creates urgency

---

## Email 3: Local success story

**Subject line variants:**
- A: How [Local business] did it
- B: [City] success story
- C: Local business results

**Timing:** 4 days after Email 2

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Quick story about a business like yours in [city/nearby area].

**[Business name] - [Business type]**

Where they started:
- [Challenge 1]
- [Challenge 2]

What we did:
- [Solution 1]
- [Solution 2]

Where they are now:
- [Result 1 with specific number]
- [Result 2]
- [Impact on their business]

They're [X blocks/miles] from you. Similar market, similar challenges.

Want to see if we can do the same for [Business name]?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Local proof is most relevant
- Specific results
- "They're near you" resonates

## Email 4: Simple offer

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Free [deliverable] for [Business name]
- B: Last thought
- C: Simple offer

**Timing:** 5 days after Email 3

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Last email from me.

Simple offer: I'll [free deliverable] for [Business name].

Examples:
- "I'll audit your Google Business profile and show you 3 quick fixes"
- "I'll review your website and share what's hurting your search rankings"
- "I'll analyze your social presence and give you a 30-day content plan"

No charge. No obligation. Takes 20 minutes on a call.

If you find it valuable, we can discuss working together.
If not, you still get free advice.

Interested?

[Your name]

P.S. I'm local, so we could even meet for coffee if you prefer face-to-face.
```

**Why it works:**
- Free value reduces friction
- Local option builds trust
- Easy yes

## Finding local business leads

### Where to find them:
- Google Maps (search by category + city)
- Yelp (filter by location, look for gaps)
- Facebook (local business pages)
- Local business associations
- Chamber of Commerce directories
- LinkedIn (owner/manager of local business)

### Signals they need help:
- Few/no Google reviews
- Old website or no website
- No social media presence
- Negative reviews mentioning fixable issues
- Competitors dominating local search

### Research checklist:
- [ ] Google Business profile (completeness, reviews)
- [ ] Website (mobile-friendly, speed, SEO basics)
- [ ] Social media (active, engagement)
- [ ] Yelp (reviews, claimed profile)
- [ ] Competitors (what they're doing better)

## Local business objections

**"We get enough business from referrals"**
```
Referrals are great. Question is: are you leaving money on the table?

[X%] of people search online before visiting local businesses.
If they can't find you (or find competitors first), you're losing potential customers.

Think of [what you offer] as an addition to referrals, not a replacement.
```

**"We tried marketing before, it didn't work"**
```
What did you try?

A lot of local businesses get burned by:
- Generic agencies that don't understand local
- One-time fixes without ongoing work
- Wrong channel for their business

I work exclusively with [city/area] businesses, so I know what works here.
```

**"Too expensive"**
```
I hear you. Budgets are real.

Let's talk about what you can afford. I have options at different price points:
- [Smaller package]
- [Starter option]
- [One-time vs ongoing]

What matters most is getting you results that pay for themselves.
```

**"I don't have time to deal with this"**
```
That's exactly why you hire someone.

Here's what working with me looks like:
- Initial setup: [X] hours of your time (onboarding)
- Ongoing: [X] minutes per week (approvals, quick updates)
- I handle everything else

You're too busy running the business to worry about [your service].
```

## Local outreach tips

### Stand out as local:
- Mention specific landmarks, streets, neighborhoods
- Reference local events, weather, news
- Offer to meet in person
- Use local phone number
- Include local address in signature

### Build local reputation:
- Join Chamber of Commerce
- Attend local business events
- Get testimonials from local businesses
- Feature local clients on your website
- Sponsor local events/teams

### Multi-channel approach:
- Email (this sequence)
- LinkedIn connection request
- Google Maps message (for businesses with messaging enabled)
- Walk-in (if appropriate for the business type)
- Direct mail (stands out in digital world)

## Performance benchmarks

**Local business outreach:**
- Open rate: 50-65%
- Reply rate: 8-15%
- Meeting rate: 4-8%

**Why higher rates:**
- Local angle builds trust
- Less competition in their inbox
- Business owners are accessible
- Problems are visible and specific

**Best approach:** Hyper-local focus. "I work with [neighborhood/city] businesses" beats "I work with local businesses."
