# SEO services 5-touch sequence

Use this for: SEO agencies, content marketing agencies, link building services.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Traffic/ranking observation |
| 2 | Day 3 | Competitor comparison |
| 3 | Day 7 | Low-hanging fruit audit |
| 4 | Day 11 | Case study with numbers |
| 5 | Day 16 | Last chance |

Expected reply rate: 4-8%
Expected meeting rate: 2-4%

---

## Email 1: Traffic/ranking observation

**Subject line variants:**
- A: {{company}} SEO opportunity
- B: Your rankings vs [competitor]
- C: [Keyword] traffic potential

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Ran a quick analysis on {{company}}'s site and found some opportunities:

[Specific observation from your research]

Example: You're ranking #12 for "[keyword]" which gets [X] searches/month. Moving to page 1 could mean [X] additional visitors/month.

We've helped [similar company] improve their organic traffic by [X%] in [timeframe].

Worth a 15-minute call to share what we found?

[Your name]
[Your agency]
```

**Why it works:**
- Shows you did real research
- Specific keyword data
- Clear opportunity quantified

## SEO research before outreach

### Tools to use:

**Free:**
- Google search: Check rankings manually
- Ubersuggest: Basic traffic/keyword data
- Google PageSpeed: Technical issues

**Paid:**
- Ahrefs: Traffic, keywords, backlinks
- SEMrush: Competitive analysis
- Moz: Domain authority, link profile

### Research checklist:

- [ ] Current organic traffic estimate
- [ ] Top ranking keywords
- [ ] Keywords ranking #4-#20 (quick win opportunities)
- [ ] Technical SEO issues (speed, mobile, etc.)
- [ ] Content gaps vs competitors
- [ ] Backlink profile vs competitors
- [ ] Domain authority comparison

### Time investment:

10-15 minutes per prospect. Worth it because:
- Personalization dramatically improves replies
- Shows you know what you're doing
- Creates instant credibility

## Service positioning

### Monthly retainer (ongoing SEO)
- $2K-$10K/month depending on scope
- Content + technical + links
- 6-12 month commitments typical
- Best for: Serious about organic growth

### Project-based (audit + strategy)
- $3K-$10K one-time
- Comprehensive audit
- Prioritized roadmap
- Best for: Companies who want to execute themselves

### Link building only
- $1K-$5K/month
- Focus on authority building
- Best for: Good content, need more links

### Content only
- $2K-$8K/month
- Blog posts, landing pages
- Best for: Need volume of optimized content

## Objection handlers

**"SEO takes too long"**
```
Fair concern. Here's the reality: most of our clients see measurable
improvements in 3-4 months, significant results in 6-12.

But here's the thing: your competitors who started 6 months ago are
now on page 1. Every month you delay, you're that much further behind.

SEO compounds. The best time to start was a year ago. The second best
time is now.
```

**"We tried SEO before and it didn't work"**
```
That happens. Usually it's one of three reasons:
1. Wrong strategy (focusing on wrong keywords)
2. Wrong tactics (outdated or spammy techniques)
3. Not enough time (gave up before results materialized)

What did you try before? Happy to diagnose what went wrong.
```

**"Can you guarantee results?"**
```
I can't guarantee specific rankings. No legitimate SEO can. Google
controls that, not us.

What I can guarantee:
- Deliverables (X posts, X links, X optimizations per month)
- Transparency (monthly reporting on everything we do)
- Our track record (X% of clients see Y improvement in Z months)

If you find an SEO who guarantees page 1 rankings, run. That's a red flag.
```

**"Too expensive"**
```
Let's look at the math. If we get you [X] additional visitors/month,
and your conversion rate is [Y%], that's [Z] new customers.

At your average customer value of $[X], that's $[Y] in new revenue.

Compared to our fee of $[Z], that's a [X]x return.

The question isn't cost. It's ROI.
```

## Performance benchmarks

**SEO outreach:**
- Open rate: 45-55%
- Reply rate: 5-10%
- Meeting rate: 2-5%

**Why moderate:**
- Lots of competition (every agency emails about SEO)
- Long sales cycles
- Hard to prove ROI upfront

**How to beat benchmarks:**
- Better research = more personalization
- Specific data beats generic claims
- Lead with opportunities, not services
