# PR and media services 5-touch sequence

Use this for: PR agencies, media placement, podcast booking, thought leadership services.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Visibility gap |
| 2 | Day 4 | What coverage does |
| 3 | Day 8 | Recent wins |
| 4 | Day 13 | Specific placement idea |
| 5 | Day 18 | Last chance |

Expected reply rate: 6-10%
Expected meeting rate: 3-5%

---

## Email 1: Visibility gap

**Subject line variants:**
- A: {{company}} in the press
- B: Getting {{company}} on the radar
- C: Visibility for {{firstName}}

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Quick observation: {{company}} is doing interesting work, but I don't see much press coverage.

That's a missed opportunity. Companies like [competitor] are getting featured in [publications], which drives credibility and leads.

We help founders like you get featured in [types of publications/podcasts].

Recent placements:
- [Client] in [Publication]
- [Client] on [Podcast]
- [Client] featured in [Outlet]

Worth a call to see what we could do for {{company}}?

[Your name]
[Your agency]
```

**Why it works:**
- Observational (you did research)
- Competitive angle
- Proof of placements

## Email 2: What coverage does

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Re: {{company}} press (reply thread)
- B: What PR actually does
- C: Beyond vanity metrics

**Timing:** 4 days after Email 1

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Quick follow-up on the PR conversation.

Some founders think press is just ego. Here's what it actually does:

**Immediate:**
- Traffic spike from publication audiences
- Social proof for your website
- "As seen in" badges for sales materials

**Medium-term:**
- SEO boost from backlinks
- Credibility with investors/partners
- Inbound leads from people who read the coverage

**Long-term:**
- Authority position in your space
- First search result for your name
- Platform for future opportunities

One good placement can outperform months of ads.

Want to explore what placements would make sense for {{company}}?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Educates on value
- Beyond vanity metrics
- Clear benefits

## Email 5: Last touch

**Subject line variants:**

- B: Your competitors are getting coverage
- C: Final thought

**Timing:** 5 days after Email 4

**Body:**

```

Last email from me.

Here's what I'll leave you with: Every day without press coverage is a day your competitors are building authority you'll have to catch up to.

I looked at [competitor]. They've been featured in [X, Y, Z] in the last year.

When you're ready to change that, we'll be here.

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Competitive urgency
- Easy micro-commitment
- Professional close

## PR service positioning

### Full-service PR retainer:
- $5-15K/month
- Media relations + content
- Guaranteed placements (usually)
- Ongoing coverage campaign

### Project-based (launch/campaign):
- $10-25K one-time
- Specific event or launch
- Concentrated outreach
- Defined timeline

### Podcast booking:
- $3-8K/month
- Focus on podcast appearances
- Thought leadership positioning
- Great for founders who speak well

### Thought leadership content:
- $3-10K/month
- Ghostwritten articles
- Bylines placed in publications
- LinkedIn content support

### Media training:
- $2-5K one-time
- Interview preparation
- Message development
- Camera/speaking coaching

## What makes a company PR-ready

### Good fit for PR:
- Interesting story (founding story, contrarian view, unique approach)
- Founder willing to be visible
- Something newsworthy (growth, milestone, trend tie-in)
- Budget for 3-6 month commitment
- Patience (PR takes time)

### Not ready for PR:
- Nothing newsworthy to say
- Founder won't do interviews
- Expects immediate ROI
- Budget for only 1 month
- Already in crisis mode

---

## Story angles that work

### For startups:
- Founder story (unusual background, pivots)
- Contrarian takes on industry
- Growth milestones
- Funding announcements
- Industry trend commentary

### For established companies:
- Major pivot or transformation
- Culture/workplace innovations
- Customer success stories (with permission)
- Research or data releases
- Awards and recognition

### For executives/founders:
- Thought leadership on industry trends
- Career lessons and advice
- Behind-the-scenes of building company
- Predictions for the future
- Response to industry news

## Objection handlers

**"PR is too expensive"**
```
I understand. Let's think about it differently.

What's the value of being seen as the authority in your space?
What would you pay for a backlink from Forbes?
What's the credibility worth when closing a major deal?

If one piece of coverage helps you close one deal or raise funds at
a better valuation, the ROI is massive.

We can also start smaller. Podcast placements or one-off projects
are lower investment entry points.
```

**"We tried PR before, nothing happened"**
```
That's frustrating. Usually that means one of three things:
1. Wrong agency (not connected to your publications)
2. Wrong story angle (didn't resonate with journalists)
3. Wrong timing (no news hook)

What did you try? I'd be curious to diagnose what went wrong.
```

**"We can do it ourselves"**
```
Definitely possible. Question is: do you have the time and relationships?

Getting coverage requires:
- Journalist relationships (built over years)
- Understanding what they're looking for
- Time to pitch and follow up consistently

Most founders try for a few months, then realize the opportunity cost
of their time isn't worth it.
```

**"We're not ready for PR"**
```
What would need to happen for you to be ready?

For most companies, "ready" just means:
- You have a story to tell
- You're willing to be visible
- You can commit to 3-6 months

If those boxes are checked, you're ready.
```
