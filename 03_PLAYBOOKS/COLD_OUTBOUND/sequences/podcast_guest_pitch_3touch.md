# Podcast guest pitch 3-touch sequence

Use this for: Getting booked on podcasts to build authority and generate leads.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Pitch guest appearance |
| 2 | Day 5 | Add value, different angle |
| 3 | Day 10 | Simple ask |

Expected reply rate: 15-30%
Expected booking rate: 8-15%

---

## Email 1: The guest pitch

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Guest pitch for [Podcast Name]
- B: Topic idea: [Specific topic]
- C: [Your credential] + [Podcast Name]

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Been listening to [Podcast Name] for a while. Really liked the episode with [recent guest] about [topic from that episode].

Quick pitch to be a guest:

**Who I am:**
[Your name], [your role/company]. [1 sentence about what you do and credibility]

**Topic I'd bring:**
[Specific topic that fits their show]

**What makes it interesting:**
- [Angle 1: unique perspective, contrarian take, or specific expertise]
- [Angle 2: stories, data, or examples you can share]
- [Angle 3: why their audience would care]

**Previous appearances:**
[List 2-3 podcasts you've been on, or skip this if none]

Worth considering?

[Your name]
[LinkedIn or website]
```

**Why it works:**
- Shows you listen to their show
- Specific topic, not vague "I could talk about anything"
- Clear value for their audience

---

## Email 2: Add value + different angle

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Re: Guest pitch (reply thread)
- B: Another angle for [Podcast Name]
- C: [Different topic idea]

**Timing:** 5 days after Email 1

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Following up on my guest pitch.

Had another idea that might work better:

**Alternative topic:** [Different topic that fits their show]

This one might resonate because [reason it fits their audience/current themes].

I could share:
- [Specific story or example]
- [Framework or methodology]
- [Actionable takeaways for listeners]

Also, if you're looking for guests for future episodes, I'd be happy to introduce you to people in my network who'd be great fits.

Let me know either way.

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Shows flexibility
- Offers value beyond yourself
- Positions you as helpful, not desperate

---

## Email 3: Simple ask

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Quick question
- B: Should I follow up later?
- C: One more idea

**Timing:** 5 days after Email 2

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Last one from me on this.

If the timing isn't right, no worries at all. Just let me know:

1. Not interested (totally fine, I'll stop emailing)
2. Maybe later (I'll follow up in a few months)
3. Let's talk (I'll send my calendar)

Either way, keep up the great work with [Podcast Name].

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Easy to respond to
- Shows respect for their time
- Often converts fence-sitters

## Finding the right podcasts

### Where to search:
- **Listen Notes:** Best podcast search engine
- **Apple Podcasts:** Browse by category
- **Spotify:** Search by topic
- **Rephonic:** Podcast analytics and discovery

### Qualification criteria:
- **Audience fit:** Their listeners = your potential customers
- **Guest format:** They actually have guests (not solo shows)
- **Active:** Released episode in last 30 days
- **Accessible:** Under 50K downloads (easier to get booked)

### Red flags:
- No guest episodes
- Hasn't published in 3+ months
- Obvious pay-to-play (they want you to buy ads)
- Wrong audience entirely

---

## Research before pitching

### Listen to at least 1 episode:
- How do they format interviews?
- What topics have they covered recently?
- What's their host style (casual vs formal)?
- What guests do they typically have?

### Find specific hooks:
- Recent episode you can reference
- Gap in their content you can fill
- Trend they're covering that you know about
- Contrarian take on something they've discussed

---

## Topic ideas that get booked

### Formats that work:
1. **The "How I Did X" story** - Personal journey with specific results
2. **The contrarian take** - Why conventional wisdom is wrong
3. **The framework** - Step-by-step methodology listeners can use
4. **Behind the scenes** - How something actually works
5. **Predictions** - Where the industry is heading

### Examples by niche:

**Marketing:**
- "How I grew [X] from 0 to 10K subscribers in 6 months"
- "Why most content strategies fail (and what works instead)"
- "The exact outbound process that books 10 meetings/week"

**Business/entrepreneurship:**
- "How I built a $X business while working full-time"
- "The 3 systems that let me run my company in 10 hours/week"
- "What I learned from [number] failed businesses"

**Tech/SaaS:**
- "How we got our first 100 customers with $0 marketing budget"
- "The technical decisions that saved us 6 months of development"
- "Why most startups over-engineer (and what to do instead)"

## What hosts actually want

Based on booking 50+ podcast appearances:

1. **Easy to work with:** Respond quickly, be flexible on timing
2. **Prepared:** Know their show, come with talking points
3. **Promotional:** Will share the episode with your audience
4. **Entertaining:** Have stories, not just information
5. **Unique:** Something their audience hasn't heard

---

## Pre-interview checklist

When you get booked:

**Before recording:**
- [ ] Re-listen to their most recent episode
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 key talking points
- [ ] Have 2-3 stories ready to share
- [ ] Test audio/video setup
- [ ] Prepare your "pitch" for end of episode

**Questions to ask host:**
- What topics do you want to focus on?
- Any topics I should avoid?
- How do you typically end episodes?
- Will you share questions in advance?

---

## Maximizing episode value

### During the episode:
- Mention your lead magnet or resource naturally
- Give actionable advice (makes you memorable)
- Tell stories (more engaging than information)

### After the episode airs:
- Share to all your channels
- Tag the host and show
- Clip best moments for social content
- Add to your "As Seen On" page

### Long-term:
- Stay in touch with host (good relationship = future invites)
- Ask if they know other hosts who'd be a fit
- Repurpose content from the episode

## Performance benchmarks

**Podcast pitching typically converts well:**
- Open rate: 55-70%
- Reply rate: 18-30%
- Booking rate: 10-18%

**Why high rates:**
- Hosts need guests (mutual value)
- Less competitive than sales outreach
- You're offering content, not asking for money

---

## Volume needed

**To get 1 booking per week:**
- Pitch 10-15 podcasts per week
- At 15% booking rate = 1-2 bookings
- Allow 2-4 weeks from pitch to air

**To get 1 booking per month:**
- Pitch 5-8 podcasts per week
- Lower effort, sustainable long-term
