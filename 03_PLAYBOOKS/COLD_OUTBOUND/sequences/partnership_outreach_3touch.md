# Partnership outreach 3-touch sequence

Use this for: Affiliate partnerships, integration partnerships, co-marketing, reseller agreements.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Propose mutual value |
| 2 | Day 4 | Specific partnership idea |
| 3 | Day 9 | Low-commitment ask |

Expected reply rate: 10-20%
Expected partnership rate: 3-8%

---

## Email 1: Mutual value opener

**Subject line variants:**
- A: {{company}} + [Your company] partnership?
- B: Idea for {{company}}
- C: Partnership opportunity

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

I run [Your company]. We [what you do] for [who you serve].

There's a natural fit between what you do and what we do:

- You help customers with [their thing]
- We help customers with [your thing]
- Together, we could [combined value]

A few partnership ideas I had in mind:
1. [Partnership type 1]
2. [Partnership type 2]

Interested in exploring this?

[Your name]
[Your company]
```

**Why it works:**
- Shows you understand their business
- Clear value for both sides
- Specific ideas, not vague "let's partner"

---

## Email 2: Specific proposal

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Re: Partnership (reply thread)
- B: [Specific partnership type]
- C: How this would work

**Timing:** 4 days after Email 1

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Following up on the partnership idea.

Here's specifically what I'm thinking:

**The partnership:**
[One paragraph explaining the specific arrangement]

**What's in it for {{company}}:**
- [Benefit 1]
- [Benefit 2]
- [Benefit 3]

**What's in it for us:**
- [What you get]

**Similar partnerships we have:**
- [Partner 1]: [Brief description of arrangement]
- [Partner 2]: [Brief description]

Worth a 20-minute call to see if there's a fit?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Concrete proposal, not abstract concept
- Transparent about mutual benefit
- Social proof from existing partners

---

## Email 3: Lower the bar

**Subject line variants:**
- A: Quick question
- B: Before I move on
- C: Simpler option

**Timing:** 5 days after Email 2

**Body:**

```
{{firstName}},

Haven't heard back, so maybe a full partnership isn't the right fit right now.

Here's a simpler option: [lower-commitment alternative]

Examples:
- I add you to our [resource/directory/newsletter] for free
- We share your content with our audience
- A single co-marketing experiment

No formal agreement needed. Just a test to see if there's value.

Interested in something smaller?

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:**
- Reduces friction to "yes"
- Still opens relationship
- Can expand later

## Partnership type variations

### Integration partnership

**Email 1 angle:**
```
Our customers keep asking for [integration with their product].

We'd love to build a native integration that benefits both our user bases.

Here's what I'm thinking:
- We build the integration
- You feature us in your integrations directory
- We promote the integration to our [X] users

Would {{company}} be open to exploring this?
```

### Affiliate partnership

**Email 1 angle:**
```
We pay [commission rate]% for referred customers.

Given your audience of [their audience type], this could be a solid revenue stream with minimal effort.

Current affiliate partners earn $[X-Y] per month on average.

Happy to set you up with a custom tracking link and see how it performs.

Interested?
```

### Co-marketing partnership

**Email 1 angle:**
```
I think there's a great co-marketing opportunity here.

Ideas:
- Joint webinar on [topic relevant to both audiences]
- Co-authored guide on [topic]
- Guest post swap

Your audience of [their audience type] + our audience of [your audience type] = bigger reach for both.

Worth exploring?
```

### Reseller partnership

**Email 1 angle:**
```
Looking for companies like {{company}} to resell [Your product].

The arrangement:
- You sell [product] to your clients
- You keep [X]% of each sale
- We handle fulfillment/support

Current resellers are adding $[X]/month to their revenue.

Is this something {{company}} offers?
```

### Referral partnership (informal)

**Email 1 angle:**
```
Simple proposal: We refer customers to each other when there's a fit.

No formal agreement needed. Just an understanding:
- When we have a client who needs [what they do], we send them your way
- When you have a client who needs [what you do], you send them ours

I'm already doing this informally with [other partners]. Works well for everyone.

Open to being added to our referral network?
```

## Partnership pitch research

### Before reaching out, answer:
1. What does their customer actually need that we provide?
2. What can we offer them that they can't easily get elsewhere?
3. Who at their company handles partnerships?
4. Do they already have similar partnerships?
5. What's the revenue/value potential for them?

### Finding the right contact:
- **Integration partnerships:** Product team, Head of Integrations
- **Affiliate partnerships:** Marketing, Partnership Manager
- **Co-marketing:** Head of Marketing, Content Lead
- **Reseller:** Sales leadership, Business Development

---

## What to prepare for the call

When they reply interested:

**Partnership one-pager:**
- Your company overview (2-3 sentences)
- Partnership proposal specifics
- What you each contribute
- Revenue/benefit split
- Success metrics
- Next steps to get started

**Questions to ask them:**
1. Have you done similar partnerships before?
2. What would make this a win for you?
3. Who else needs to be involved to move forward?
4. What's your typical timeline for partnership decisions?

---

## Performance benchmarks

**Partnership outreach typically converts better than sales:**
- Open rate: 50-65%
- Reply rate: 12-22%
- Partnership rate: 4-10%

**Why:** Less competitive, mutual benefit clear, often reaching partnership-minded people.

---

## Nurturing partnerships long-term

After partnership established:

**Monthly:**
- Check in on performance
- Share any customer feedback
- Discuss optimization opportunities

**Quarterly:**
- Review metrics together
- Identify expansion opportunities
- Plan co-marketing activities

**Annually:**
- Full partnership review
- Renegotiate terms if needed
- Discuss deeper integration

---

## Common objections and responses

**"We don't do partnerships"**
Response: "Totally understand. Would you be open to a one-time co-marketing experiment? No formal partnership needed. If it works, we can revisit. If not, no hard feelings."

**"We need to think about it"**
Response: "Of course. What information would help you decide? Happy to put together a more detailed proposal."

**"We already have a partner for this"**
Response: "Good to know. A few of our partners started as a second option before becoming primary. Would it make sense to connect in 6 months in case things change?"

**"What's in it for us?"**
Response: "Great question. Based on what I know about {{company}}, here's what I think you'd get: [specific benefits with numbers if possible]"
