# Follow-up call script

Phone scripts for calling prospects who have engaged with your email or shown interest.

## When to use follow-up calls

### High-priority triggers:
1. **Email opened multiple times** - They're interested but not responding
2. **Clicked link in email** - Strong intent signal
3. **Positive email reply** - But didn't book meeting
4. **LinkedIn accepted connection** - Warm touchpoint
5. **Visited your website** - Intent data signal

### Timing:
- Call within 1-4 hours of engagement (if possible)
- Same day if not immediate
- Within 24 hours maximum
- Strike while iron is hot

## Follow-up call framework

### Opening (acknowledge the context)

**After email engagement:**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your name] from [Company].

I sent you an email [yesterday/earlier today] about [topic], and I
wanted to follow up quickly. Did you get a chance to see it?"
```

**After positive reply:**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your name].

Thanks for your reply to my email! I figured it'd be easier to
connect by phone. Do you have a couple minutes?"
```

**After LinkedIn connection:**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your name]. We just connected on LinkedIn.

Thought I'd give you a quick call since we're now connected.
Is this a good time?"
```

## Scripts by scenario

### Scenario 1: Multiple email opens, no reply

**Context:** They've opened your email 3+ times but haven't responded.

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your name] from [Company].

I sent you an email about [topic] and noticed you might have
been looking at it. Figured I'd call to see if you had questions.

Is [topic] something you're thinking about right now?"
```

**If they say yes:**
```
"Great. What specifically caught your attention?"

[Listen]

"Got it. Here's what typically makes sense at this point...
[Brief value prop]

Would it be helpful to schedule 15 minutes to dig into
how this would work for [Company]?"
```

**If they say no/not right now:**
```
"Totally understand. What's the main priority for you right now?"

[Listen - might uncover other opportunity]

"Got it. I'll let you go. Mind if I follow up in [timeframe]
in case things change?"
```

### Scenario 2: Clicked link in email

**Context:** They clicked your calendar link or content link but didn't take action.

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your name].

I sent you an email with [link description] and noticed you
checked it out. Wanted to see if you had any questions before
booking time.

What did you think?"
```

**If positive:**
```
"Glad it resonated. What would be helpful to cover on a call?"

[Listen]

"Perfect. I have [time] available [day]. Does that work?"
```

**If hesitant:**
```
"Totally fair. What's holding you back from a quick call?

[Address objection]

Even if it's just 10 minutes to see if there's a fit - sound fair?"
```

### Scenario 3: Positive email reply, no meeting booked

**Context:** They replied positively but didn't take the next step.

```
"Hi [Name], [Your name] here.

Thanks so much for your reply! You mentioned [what they said].

I'd love to hear more about that. Do you have a few minutes now,
or should we schedule something?"
```

**If they have time:**
```
"Perfect. Tell me more about [what they mentioned].

[Discovery conversation]

Based on what you're sharing, I think [your solution] could help.
Let's schedule a proper call to dive deeper. How's [time]?"
```

**If they don't have time:**
```
"No problem. Let's get something on the calendar then.
I'm looking at [specific times]. What works for you?"
```

### Scenario 4: After LinkedIn connection

**Context:** They just accepted your connection request.

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your name].

We just connected on LinkedIn - thanks for accepting!

I saw [something relevant about them] and thought it'd be
worth a quick conversation.

Do you have 2 minutes?"
```

**If yes:**
```
"Great. I help [type of company] with [problem].

Given what I see at [Company], I thought there might be a fit.

Is [problem area] something you're focused on right now?"
```

**If no:**
```
"Totally understand. When would be a better time?
I can call back or send you some times to meet."
```

### Scenario 5: After website visit (intent data)

**Context:** They visited your website after receiving your email.

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your name] from [Company].

Wanted to follow up on my email - I noticed you might have
visited our site. Did you find what you were looking for?"
```

**If they admit visiting:**
```
"Great! What caught your attention?

[Listen]

That's exactly what we specialize in. Would it make sense
to do a quick call to see if we can help [Company]?"
```

**If they deny or seem surprised:**
```
"No worries - just wanted to make sure you had my info.

In case it's relevant: we help [type of company] with [outcome].

Is that something worth a quick conversation?"
```

## Handling common follow-up objections

### "I was just looking, not really interested"
```
"Totally fair. Out of curiosity, what made you take a look?

[Listen]

Got it. Even if the timing isn't right, would it make sense
to connect briefly so you know what we do for when it is?"
```

### "I haven't had time to look closely"
```
"No problem at all. Want me to give you the 60-second version
right now? Then you can decide if it's worth more time."

[If yes, give brief pitch]

"Does that sound relevant enough for a proper call?"
```

### "I'm not the right person"
```
"Thanks for telling me. Who should I be talking to about this?

Would you be willing to make an intro, or should I mention
you pointed me in their direction?"
```

### "Can you just send me more information?"
```
"Happy to. What specifically would be helpful?

Actually, it might be easier if I walk you through it briefly.
Most people find that more useful than reading docs.

Can we do 10 minutes [time]?"
```

### "I need to think about it / talk to my team"
```
"Completely understand. What are the main things you need to think through?

[Listen and address concerns]

How about this: Let's schedule a follow-up call for [day].
That gives you time to think/discuss, and I can answer any
questions that come up. Fair?"
```

## Follow-up call cadence

### After email open/click (no reply):
- **Call 1:** Within 4 hours of engagement
- **Call 2:** Next day (different time of day)
- **Email follow-up:** If no answer on calls

### After positive reply (no meeting):
- **Call 1:** Immediately after seeing reply
- **Call 2:** Same day if no answer
- **Email follow-up:** "Tried calling, here's my calendar"

### After LinkedIn connection:
- **Wait 24-48 hours** (don't seem desperate)
- **Call 1:** 24-48 hours after connection
- **LinkedIn message:** If no answer

## Tracking follow-up calls

### Log:
- What triggered the call
- Outcome (connected, voicemail, meeting)
- Notes from conversation
- Next steps

### Metrics:
- Follow-up call connect rate (higher than cold calls)
- Conversion to meeting (should be 20-40%)
- Time from trigger to call

## Email follow-up after missed call

**Template:**
```
Hi [Name],

Just tried calling you about [topic].

I noticed [what triggered your call - opened email, visited site, etc.]
and wanted to follow up.

Here's my calendar if a call makes sense: [link]

Or just reply with a few times that work.

[Your name]
[Phone number]
```

## Quick reference

### Warm follow-up (they engaged):
- Connect rate: 30-40% (vs 10-20% cold)
- Meeting rate: 25-40% (vs 10-15% cold)
- Best timing: Within 4 hours of engagement

### What to prepare:
- [ ] Their email thread open
- [ ] Their LinkedIn profile
- [ ] Calendar ready
- [ ] Notes on what they engaged with

### Opening formula:
"Hi [Name], this is [Your name]. [Context for why you're calling].
Do you have a moment?"

### Closing formula:
"Based on what you've shared, let's schedule [time frame].
Does [specific time] work?"
