# Voicemail drop script

Voicemail templates that get callbacks. Keep them short and intriguing.

## Voicemail fundamentals

### Rules:
1. **Under 30 seconds** - No one listens to long voicemails
2. **Say your name twice** - Once at start, once at end
3. **Say your number slowly** - Twice if possible
4. **One clear point** - Don't try to explain everything
5. **Create curiosity** - Give them a reason to call back

### What NOT to do:
- Don't leave a sales pitch
- Don't ramble
- Don't sound robotic
- Don't apologize for calling
- Don't leave multiple voicemails same day

## Voicemail templates

### Template 1: The basic

```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your name] from [Company].

Calling about [one sentence - what you help with].

Would love to connect briefly.

My number is [number, slowly].

Again, [Your name] at [number].

Talk soon."
```

**Example:**
```
"Hi Sarah, this is Mike from GrowthEngine.

Calling about your outbound pipeline.

Would love to connect briefly.

My number is 555-123-4567.

Again, Mike at 555-123-4567.

Talk soon."
```

### Template 2: The referral/trigger

```
"Hi [Name], [Your name] here.

[Trigger or reason]:
- "Saw [Company] just raised a round..."
- "Noticed you're hiring for [role]..."
- "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out..."

Wanted to share something relevant.

Call me at [number].

[Your name], [number]."
```

**Example:**
```
"Hi Sarah, Mike here.

Saw Acme just closed your Series B. Congrats.

I work with companies at exactly this stage on scaling outbound.

Wanted to share something that might help.

Call me at 555-123-4567.

Mike, 555-123-4567."
```

### Template 3: The curiosity gap

```
"Hi [Name], [Your name] calling.

Had a quick thought about [Company]'s [area].

Nothing urgent, but worth a few minutes.

Give me a ring at [number].

Thanks, [Name]. Talk soon."
```

**Example:**
```
"Hi Sarah, Mike calling.

Had a quick thought about Acme's lead generation.

Nothing urgent, but worth a few minutes.

Give me a ring at 555-123-4567.

Thanks, Sarah. Talk soon."
```

### Template 4: The peer drop

```
"Hi [Name], [Your name] here.

I just finished working with [similar company] on [outcome].

Made me think of [Company].

Would love to share what worked for them.

[Number].

[Your name], [number]."
```

**Example:**
```
"Hi Sarah, Mike here.

I just finished working with a similar SaaS company on doubling their outbound meetings.

Made me think of Acme.

Would love to share what worked for them.

555-123-4567.

Mike, 555-123-4567."
```

### Template 5: The follow-up (after email)

```
"Hi [Name], [Your name].

Following up on the email I sent about [topic].

Not sure if you caught it, but I think it's worth a quick chat.

Call me at [number] or I'll try you again [day].

[Your name], [number]."
```

### Template 6: The breakup

```
"Hi [Name], [Your name].

I've tried reaching you a few times.

I'll assume the timing isn't right and won't keep calling.

If [topic] does become relevant, you have my number: [number].

Best of luck with [Company]. Take care."
```

## Voicemail sequence

### Call attempt cadence:

**Attempt 1:**
Use Template 1 (basic) or Template 2 (trigger)

**Attempt 2 (3 days later):**
Use Template 3 (curiosity) or Template 5 (follow-up)

**Attempt 3 (7 days later):**
Use Template 4 (peer drop)

**Attempt 4 (14 days later):**
Use Template 6 (breakup)

### Don't:
- Leave voicemails every day
- Leave the same voicemail twice
- Give up after one attempt

## Voicemail delivery tips

### Voice quality:
- Stand up while recording
- Smile (it changes your voice)
- Speak clearly, not too fast
- Project confidence
- Vary your tone (don't be monotone)

### Phone number delivery:
- Say it slowly
- Pause slightly between groups
- Say it twice
- Example: "five-five-five... one-two-three... four-five-six-seven"

### Energy:
- Match the energy of someone calling a friend
- Not over-the-top enthusiastic
- Not boring or flat
- Natural, human, professional

## Voicemail + email combo

### Best practice:
1. Call first
2. Leave voicemail
3. Send email immediately after

**Email reference:**
```
"Hi [Name],

Just left you a voicemail about [topic].

[Brief email content]

Let me know if you're open to a quick call.

[Your name]
[Phone number]"
```

**Why this works:**
- Some people check email faster than voicemail
- Shows you made effort to reach them
- Double touchpoint increases recognition

## Callback expectations

### Realistic expectations:
- Callback rate: 3-5% (that's normal)
- Most won't call back (and that's okay)
- The goal is awareness, not just callbacks
- Voicemails prime them for future touches

### When they do call back:
- Answer professionally
- Have their info pulled up
- Don't be surprised (have your pitch ready)
- Thank them for returning the call
- Get to the point quickly

### Callback answer template:
```
[Phone rings, unknown number]

"This is [Your name]."

[They identify themselves]

"[Name]! Thanks so much for calling me back. I appreciate it.

I called because [brief reason - same as voicemail].

Do you have a couple minutes now, or should we schedule a quick call?"
```

## Voicemail automation (ringless voicemail)

### What it is:
Technology that drops voicemails without ringing the phone.

### Tools:
- Slybroadcast
- Drop Cowboy
- VoiceShotVoice

### Pros:
- Scale (hundreds of voicemails per hour)
- Consistent delivery
- Less time per lead

### Cons:
- Less personal
- Legal gray area in some states
- Lower callback rates than real calls

### Best use:
- Lead nurture sequences
- Event invitations
- Follow-ups at scale

### Not recommended for:
- First touch to cold prospects
- Enterprise targets
- Highly personalized outreach

## Tracking voicemails

### Log:
- Date/time of voicemail
- Which template used
- Callback received? Y/N
- Notes

### Metrics:
- Voicemails left per day
- Callback rate per template
- Best days/times for callbacks

### Optimization:
- Track which templates get callbacks
- Test different approaches
- Kill what doesn't work
