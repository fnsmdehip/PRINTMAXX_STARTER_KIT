# 30-day inbox warmup protocol

New inboxes have zero reputation. Send cold emails day 1 and you land in spam. Warmup fixes this.

## What warmup does

1. Your inbox sends emails to a network of other inboxes
2. Those inboxes open, reply, mark as important
3. Gmail/Outlook sees positive engagement signals
4. Your sender reputation builds
5. After 30 days, you can send cold emails

## Warmup schedule

### Week 1 (Days 1-7)
- Warmup emails per day: 10-15
- Reply rate target: 30-40%
- No cold emails yet
- Just let the warmup run

### Week 2 (Days 8-14)
- Warmup emails per day: 20-30
- Reply rate target: 30-40%
- Can start 5-10 cold emails/day (optional)
- Monitor for bounces

### Week 3 (Days 15-21)
- Warmup emails per day: 30-40
- Cold emails: 15-20/day
- Check deliverability daily

### Week 4 (Days 22-30)
- Warmup emails per day: 40-50
- Cold emails: 20-30/day
- Should see 95%+ inbox placement

### After warmup (Day 31+)
- Keep warmup running: 20-30/day (maintenance)
- Cold emails: 30-50/day per inbox
- Never exceed 50 cold emails per inbox per day

## Warmup settings (Instantly example)

**Recommended settings:**
- Daily warmup limit: Start 10, increase by 5 every 3 days
- Reply rate: 30-40%
- Read emulation: ON
- Mark important: ON
- Warmup tag: Use custom tag like "acme-warmup"

**What these mean:**
- Reply rate: % of warmup emails that get auto-replied
- Read emulation: Opens email, scrolls, waits before closing
- Mark important: Marks some emails as important in Gmail

## Manual warmup (if no budget)

If you can't afford a warmup tool:

1. **Friend network method**
   - Find 10-20 friends with Gmail/Outlook accounts
   - Send them real emails
   - Ask them to reply, mark as not spam, star the email

2. **Newsletter method**
   - Subscribe to 5-10 newsletters from your inbox
   - Receive, open, sometimes reply

3. **Self-reply method**
   - Send emails between your own inboxes
   - Reply to yourself
   - Mark as important

This works but is slower and less reliable than paid warmup.

## Signs warmup is working

**Good signs:**
- mail-tester.com score improving (target 9+/10)
- Warmup emails landing in inbox (not spam)
- No blocks or warnings from Google

**Bad signs:**
- Warmup emails going to spam
- Google security alert about "suspicious activity"
- Bounce rate over 5%

## After warmup: Maintaining reputation

Warmup is not "set and forget." Keep it running forever.

**Maintenance mode:**
- 20-30 warmup emails/day per inbox
- Keeps engagement signals flowing
- Protects against reputation decay

**If you stop warmup:**
- Reputation drops within 2-4 weeks
- Deliverability suffers
- Takes 2-3 weeks to recover

## Inbox limits (post-warmup)

**Gmail via Google Workspace:**
- Sending limit: 500/day (new accounts) to 2,000/day (mature)
- Safe cold email limit: 30-50/day per inbox
- Wait time between emails: 60-120 seconds

**Outlook/Microsoft 365:**
- Sending limit: 300/day (free) to 10,000/day (enterprise)
- Safe cold email limit: 30-50/day per inbox
- More lenient than Gmail but smaller market share

## Multi-inbox strategy

For 500 cold emails/day:
- 10 domains x 2 inboxes each = 20 inboxes
- 25 emails/inbox/day = 500 total
- Leaves headroom for warmup emails

**Rotation:**
- Spread sends across all inboxes
- Use your email tool's inbox rotation
- Never blast from one inbox

## Emergency: Inbox went to spam

If your cold emails start hitting spam:

1. **Stop all cold sending immediately**
2. Keep warmup running
3. Check mail-tester.com score
4. Check mxtoolbox.com for blacklists
5. If blacklisted: burn the inbox, start fresh
6. If not blacklisted: wait 2 weeks, warmup only, then slowly resume
