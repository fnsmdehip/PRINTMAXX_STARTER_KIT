# Trigger event prospecting

Trigger events are changes in a company that create buying windows. New funding, new hire, bad review, product launch. Timing your outreach to these events can 2-3x response rates.

## Why trigger events work

**Standard cold email:** "Hey, want to buy my thing?"
**Trigger-based cold email:** "Saw you just raised Series A. Companies at this stage usually need X."

The second email:
- Shows you did research
- Has a reason for reaching out now
- Hits when they're actively thinking about change
- Feels less random

## Top trigger events

### 1. Funding announcements

**Signal:** Company raised Seed, Series A, B, C
**Why it works:** They have money to spend and pressure to grow
**Best timing:** 1-4 weeks after announcement
**Where to find:** Crunchbase, TechCrunch, PitchBook, LinkedIn news

**Email angle:**
```
Congrats on the Series A. When companies hit this stage,
they usually need to scale [area]. We help with that.
```

### 2. New executive hires

**Signal:** New CMO, VP Sales, CTO, etc.
**Why it works:** New leaders want quick wins, willing to try new approaches
**Best timing:** 30-90 days after start date
**Where to find:** LinkedIn job changes, press releases, company blogs

**Email angle:**
```
Saw you recently joined as VP Marketing. New marketing
leaders often inherit messy systems. We help clean that up fast.
```

### 3. Job postings

**Signal:** Company hiring for specific roles
**Why it works:** Hiring = growth area + budget allocated
**Best timing:** While posting is live
**Where to find:** LinkedIn, Indeed, company careers page, HiringSignal.io

**Email angle:**
```
Noticed you're hiring 3 SDRs. Before you scale the team,
we should talk about your outbound infrastructure.
```

### 4. Product launches

**Signal:** New product, feature, or service announced
**Why it works:** Launch = marketing push = need for support
**Best timing:** 1-2 weeks before or after launch
**Where to find:** Product Hunt, press releases, social media

**Email angle:**
```
Saw you just launched [product]. Launches like this usually
need [what you sell] to maximize the momentum.
```

### 5. Bad reviews

**Signal:** Negative G2/Capterra reviews, Glassdoor complaints
**Why it works:** They know there's a problem
**Best timing:** Within 2 weeks of review
**Where to find:** G2, Capterra, Glassdoor, Trustpilot, Google reviews

**Email angle:**
```
Saw some recent G2 reviews mentioning [pain point].
We specialize in fixing exactly that.
```

### 6. Technology changes

**Signal:** Added/removed a tool from their stack
**Why it works:** They're evaluating solutions in that category
**Best timing:** Within 30 days of change
**Where to find:** BuiltWith alerts, Datanyze, SimilarTech

**Email angle:**
```
Noticed you recently added Salesforce. Companies at this
stage usually need help with implementation.
```

### 7. Office moves/expansion

**Signal:** New office location, expansion announcement
**Why it works:** Growth indicator, new budget cycles
**Best timing:** Before or shortly after move
**Where to find:** LinkedIn, press releases, commercial real estate news

**Email angle:**
```
Congrats on the new Austin office. Expanding companies
usually need [what you sell] dialed in.
```

### 8. Awards and recognition

**Signal:** Made Inc 5000, won industry award, featured in press
**Why it works:** They're growing and visible, flattery works
**Best timing:** Within 2 weeks of announcement
**Where to find:** Inc, Forbes lists, industry publications

**Email angle:**
```
Congrats on making the Inc 5000. Companies growing that
fast usually hit [pain point]. We help with that.
```

### 9. Competitor customer churn

**Signal:** Company stopped using competitor product
**Why it works:** They need a replacement
**Best timing:** Immediately when detected
**Where to find:** BuiltWith alerts, social listening, industry intel

**Email angle:**
```
Noticed you moved off [Competitor]. If you're looking for
a better option, worth comparing us.
```

### 10. Seasonal/cyclical events

**Signal:** Industry-specific timing (Q4 budget, January planning, etc.)
**Why it works:** Predictable buying windows
**Best timing:** Aligned with planning/budget cycles
**Where to find:** Industry knowledge, calendar

**Email angle:**
```
Q4 planning season is coming up. This is when [ICP]
usually lock in [what you sell] budgets.
```

## Finding trigger events at scale

### Funding: Crunchbase

**Free:** Limited searches, no alerts
**Pro ($29/month):** Saved searches, daily alerts

**Setup:**
1. Filter: Funding Date = Last 30 days
2. Filter: Funding Amount = $1M - $50M (your ICP range)
3. Filter: Industry = Your targets
4. Save search, get daily email alerts
5. Export leads weekly

### Job changes: LinkedIn Sales Navigator

**Filter:** Changed jobs in past 90 days
**Combine with:** Your ICP filters

This is gold for "new executive" trigger events.

### Hiring: HiringSignal.io

**Price:** $49/month
**What it does:** Tracks job postings across companies

**Use case:** "Show me marketing agencies posting SDR jobs" = agencies investing in outbound.

### Technology changes: BuiltWith alerts

**Setup:**
1. Create account
2. Add technologies to monitor
3. Get alerts when target companies add/remove

### Reviews: Manual + Google Alerts

**G2/Capterra:**
1. Check competitor product reviews monthly
2. Note companies leaving negative reviews
3. Add to outreach list

**Google Alerts:**
- Set alert: "[Competitor name] review"
- Get notified of new content

### News: Google Alerts + RSS

**Google Alerts:**
- "[Company name] funding"
- "[Company name] expansion"
- "[Industry] award"

**RSS feeds:**
- TechCrunch funding category
- Industry publication newsrooms

## Building a trigger event workflow

### Weekly process

**Monday:**
1. Check Crunchbase alerts for new funding
2. Check LinkedIn for job changes in saved searches
3. Review G2 for new competitor reviews

**Tuesday:**
1. Export trigger leads
2. Research each company (5 minutes max)
3. Categorize by trigger type

**Wednesday:**
1. Write personalized first lines
2. Assign to trigger-specific sequences
3. Queue for sending

**Thursday-Friday:**
1. Send trigger emails (higher priority than standard cold)
2. Monitor for faster response times
3. Follow up on warm responses quickly

### Trigger event CRM tags

Track triggers in your CRM/spreadsheet:
- `trigger_funding`
- `trigger_new_exec`
- `trigger_hiring`
- `trigger_bad_review`
- `trigger_tech_change`
- `trigger_award`

This lets you analyze which triggers convert best for your offer.

## Trigger event email templates

### Funding trigger

**Subject:** {{company}} growth

```
{{firstName}},

Saw the Series A news. Congrats.

At this stage, most companies realize their [what you sell]
needs to scale with the team. We help with that.

Helped 3 other post-Series A companies hit [specific result]
in the first 90 days.

Worth 15 minutes to see if we can do the same?
```

### New exec trigger

**Subject:** Re: your new role

```
{{firstName}},

Noticed you just took over as {{title}} at {{company}}.

New [title]s usually inherit a mess and need quick wins.
We specialize in [what you do] and can show you what we've
done for other companies in your position.

Open to a quick call?
```

### Hiring trigger

**Subject:** Scaling {{company}}'s team

```
{{firstName}},

Saw you're hiring [role] on LinkedIn.

Companies scaling [that function] usually hit [pain point].
We help fix that before it becomes a bottleneck.

Mind if I share how we've helped similar companies?
```

### Bad review trigger

**Subject:** Re: your G2 reviews

```
{{firstName}},

Came across some recent reviews mentioning [specific pain point].

That's exactly what we fix. We've helped [X] companies
turn that around.

Would it be worth showing you how?
```

## Conversion benchmarks

**Standard cold email:**
- Open rate: 40-50%
- Reply rate: 2-5%
- Meeting rate: 1-2%

**Trigger-based cold email:**
- Open rate: 50-60%
- Reply rate: 8-15%
- Meeting rate: 3-6%

Trigger events don't triple your response rates, but they do double them. And the quality of conversations is higher because the timing is right.
