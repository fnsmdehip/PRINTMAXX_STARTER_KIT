# BuiltWith guide for tech stack targeting

BuiltWith shows you what technology websites use. This lets you find leads based on their tools. Selling Shopify apps? Find Shopify stores. Selling HubSpot services? Find HubSpot users.

## Why tech stack targeting works

1. **Pre-qualified interest** - They already use similar tools
2. **Pain point precision** - "I see you use X, companies using X often struggle with Y"
3. **Competitor displacement** - Find users of competitor products
4. **Budget signals** - Enterprise tools = enterprise budget

## Building your tech stack ICP

### Example: Shopify app seller

**Primary technology:** Shopify

**Refining signals:**
- Also uses Klaviyo = Email-focused, has marketing budget
- Also uses Recharge = Subscriptions, recurring revenue
- Also uses Gorgias = Customer service focus, scaling
- Traffic rank < 500K = Getting decent traffic
- Country = United States

**Search:** Shopify + Klaviyo + US + Traffic < 500K

### Example: Marketing agency

**Primary technology:** HubSpot (Marketing Hub)

**Refining signals:**
- Company employee count: 50-200 (from LinkedIn data overlay)
- Also uses Google Ads = Running paid campaigns
- Also uses WordPress = Needs content work
- Traffic rank < 1M = Established presence

**Search:** HubSpot Marketing + Google Ads + WordPress + US

### Example: Competitor displacement

Selling alternative to Mailchimp? Find Mailchimp users who might be outgrowing it.

**Search:** Mailchimp + Shopify + Traffic rank < 100K

Logic: High-traffic Shopify stores using basic Mailchimp probably need Klaviyo.

## Using BuiltWith

### Basic workflow

1. Go to builtwith.com
2. Search for a technology (e.g., "Shopify")
3. See list of websites using it
4. Apply filters (country, traffic, company size)
5. Export list
6. Enrich with contact data (Apollo, Hunter)

### Advanced filters

**Traffic filters:**
- Top 10K: Major brands
- Top 100K: Established businesses
- Top 1M: Growing companies
- 1M+: Smaller sites, less budget

**Technology combinations:**
- "Uses Shopify AND Klaviyo"
- "Uses HubSpot BUT NOT Salesforce"
- "Uses React AND TypeScript"

**Historical data:**
- Recently added technology (they're implementing new tools)
- Recently removed technology (they might need replacement)

### Export format

BuiltWith exports:
- Domain
- Company name
- Technologies detected
- Traffic rank
- Social profiles
- Sometimes email/phone (hit or miss)

**You still need:** Contact enrichment via Apollo/Hunter

## Workflow: Tech stack to email campaign

### Step 1: BuiltWith export
Export 500 Shopify stores using Klaviyo in US.

### Step 2: Enrich with Apollo
Upload domains to Apollo. Find marketing decision makers.
- Titles: "Marketing Director" OR "Ecommerce Manager" OR "Head of Marketing"

### Step 3: Verify emails
Run through ZeroBounce. Target < 3% bounce rate.

### Step 4: Craft tech-specific copy

**Subject:** Your Klaviyo setup

**Body:**
```
Hey {{firstName}},

Saw you're running Klaviyo on {{company}}.

Most Shopify stores using Klaviyo leave 15-20% of revenue on the table with suboptimal flows.

We've helped 30+ stores like yours add $50K+/month in email revenue.

Worth a 15-min call to see if we can do the same for you?
```

This works because:
- Shows you did research (you know their stack)
- Specific to their situation
- Concrete numbers
- Low-commitment ask

### Step 5: Send and track
- Load into Instantly
- Send 100/day across 3 inboxes
- Target: 50%+ open rate (tech-relevant subject lines perform)
- Target: 8-12% reply rate

## Use case playbook

### If you sell marketing services

**Stack:** HubSpot Marketing Hub
**Angle:** "Your HubSpot instance might be leaving money on the table"
**Pain:** Most companies use 20% of HubSpot's features

### If you sell development services

**Stack:** WordPress + WooCommerce
**Angle:** "Your store is running on [theme]. We've migrated 50+ stores to headless with 40% speed improvement"
**Pain:** Slow sites hurt conversion

### If you sell SaaS

**Stack:** Competitor technology
**Angle:** "Saw you're using [Competitor]. Companies usually switch to us when they hit [pain point]"
**Pain:** Specific limitation of competitor

### If you sell consulting

**Stack:** Complex tool like Salesforce
**Angle:** "Your Salesforce could probably use an audit"
**Pain:** Enterprise tools are always misconfigured
