## Market Overview

The prompt engineering market sits at the intersection of:
- Massive AI adoption (ChatGPT 180M+ users, Claude growing fast)
- Knowledge gap (most users write bad prompts)
- Time savings demand (good prompts = hours saved)
- Commoditization pressure (prompts getting cheaper, but quality premium remains)

**Market reality:** Individual prompts are commoditizing ($1-5). The money is in:
1. Niche-specific prompt packs with proven workflows
2. High-ticket consulting for enterprises
3. Building prompt-powered tools/apps (MM001 + MM033 stack)

## Product Types and Pricing

### Tier 1: Individual Prompts ($1-10)

**Marketplaces:** PromptBase, Gumroad, Etsy

**What sells:**
- Midjourney/DALL-E prompts with proven outputs (show the result)
- ChatGPT prompts that solve specific problems
- Claude prompts for developers/writers

**Pricing psychology:**
- $1.99-2.99: Impulse buy, high volume needed
- $4.99-9.99: Requires proof of value (screenshot, video demo)

**Reality check:** Volume game. Need 100+ prompts or traffic arbitrage to make real money here. Better as traffic builder to higher-ticket offers.

### Tier 2: Prompt Packs ($20-100)

**Best format:** 10-30 prompts around a specific workflow

**Examples that sell:**
| Pack Type | Price Range | Target Buyer |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Real estate listing descriptions | $27-47 | Realtors |
| Legal contract review prompts | $47-97 | Paralegals, small law firms |
| E-commerce product descriptions | $27-47 | Shopify sellers |
| Marketing copy pack (ads, email, landing) | $37-67 | Marketers, agencies |
| Developer debugging prompts | $27-47 | Software engineers |
| Content repurposing workflow | $37-57 | Content creators |
| Cold email generation pack | $47-97 | Sales teams, founders |

**Pack structure (what sells):**
```
Prompt Pack: [NICHE] AI Workflow
├── Core prompts (10-15)
├── Variation templates (5-10)
├── Output examples (screenshots, text samples)
├── Use case guide (when to use which prompt)

└── Bonus: prompt customization tips
```

**Pricing factors:**
- Higher price = higher perceived value (test $27 vs $47 vs $67)
- Video demo increases conversions 40%+
- Screenshots of actual outputs are table stakes

## Marketplace Comparison

### PromptBase

**Pros:**
- Dedicated prompt marketplace
- Built-in audience of prompt buyers
- Handles payments
- SEO for prompt searches

**Cons:**
- 20% commission
- Race to bottom on pricing
- Limited branding/customer data
- Lots of commodity prompts

**Best for:** Testing market demand, volume strategy, Midjourney/image prompts

**Strategy:** Use for validation and traffic, funnel to own site for high-ticket

### Gumroad

**Pros:**
- 10% + $0.50 per sale (lower than PromptBase)
- Own your customer emails
- Build brand, not platform
- Flexible pricing, bundles, subscriptions

**Cons:**
- No built-in prompt buyer traffic
- Need own marketing
- Payment processing fees

**Best for:** Prompt packs, libraries, recurring products

**Strategy:** Main sales channel after validation

### Etsy (Digital Downloads)

**Pros:**
- Massive buyer traffic
- Search-based discovery
- Trust built in

**Cons:**
- Heavy fees (6.5% transaction + 3% payment + listing fees)
- Not optimized for digital prompts
- Buyers expect lower prices

**Best for:** Midjourney/image prompt packs, creative prompts

**Strategy:** Side channel for specific niches (wedding invites, logos, etc.)

### Own Website

**Pros:**
- 100% margin (minus payment processing)
- Full brand control
- Customer email list
- Upsell/cross-sell freedom

**Cons:**
- Need traffic
- Need trust-building
- Tech setup

**Best for:** High-ticket offers, consulting, authority play

**Strategy:** Ultimate destination. Build traffic via content, convert on site.

### Comparison Matrix

| Platform | Commission | Traffic | Branding | Email List | Best Price Range |
|----------|------------|---------|----------|------------|------------------|
| PromptBase | 20% | Built-in | Low | No | $1-20 |
| Gumroad | 10% + $0.50 | None | Medium | Yes | $20-200 |
| Etsy | ~12% | High | Low | No | $5-50 |
| Own Site | 3% (Stripe) | None | Full | Yes | $50-500+ |

## Risk Mitigation

### Commoditization Risk
**Problem:** Prompts becoming cheaper, AI improving at understanding bad prompts
**Solution:**
- Focus on workflow systems, not individual prompts
- Build authority (expertise sells when prompts commoditize)
- Stack with services (consulting doesn't commoditize as fast)

### Platform Risk
**Problem:** PromptBase could disappear, Gumroad could raise fees
**Solution:**
- Build email list from day 1
- Own website for high-ticket
- Multiple channels, not single platform dependency

### AI Improvement Risk
**Problem:** Better AI needs fewer prompts
**Solution:**
- Move toward AI workflow consulting
- Focus on enterprise (they'll always need optimization)
- Build prompt-powered tools (MM001 stack)

## Bottom Line

Prompt products are real money but commoditizing fast. The play:

1. **Short-term:** Sell prompt packs ($27-97) on Gumroad
2. **Medium-term:** Build consulting arm ($1-5k/project)
3. **Long-term:** Build prompt-powered tools/apps (MM001 stack)

Don't try to win on individual prompt prices. Win on:
- Workflow systems (multiple prompts working together)
- Niche expertise (faith prompts, legal prompts, etc.)
- Authority building (expert persona sells)
- Stacking with other methods (prompts + courses + apps)

Individual prompts = commodity. Prompt systems + expertise = premium.
