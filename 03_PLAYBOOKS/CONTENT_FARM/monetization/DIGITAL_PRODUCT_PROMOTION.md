## Content-to-product strategy

### Phase 1: Identify what converts (Weeks 1-4)

**Test content themes:**
- Post about different topics
- Track engagement per topic
- Note what gets saves and shares
- Read comments for pain points

**Questions to answer:**
- What do people ask you about most?
- What content gets saved/shared most?
- What would people pay to learn?
- What can you teach better than others?

### Phase 2: Create lead magnet (Week 5)

**Purpose:** Build email list and validate interest

**Best lead magnets:**
- Checklist or cheatsheet
- Template (Notion, spreadsheet)
- Quick-start guide
- Mini course (email series)
- Tool comparison

**Promotion:**
- Pinned post: "Free [resource]"
- Regular CTA in content
- DM funnel for delivery
- Link in bio

### Phase 3: Create mini product (Weeks 6-8)

**Purpose:** Generate first revenue, build confidence

**Characteristics:**
- Solves one specific problem
- Can be consumed in 1-2 hours
- Price: $9-29
- Easy to create and deliver

**Examples:**
- 10 premium templates
- 30-minute masterclass
- Advanced guide/ebook
- Resource bundle

### Phase 4: Create core product (Weeks 9-16)

**Purpose:** Main revenue driver

**Characteristics:**
- Comprehensive solution
- Multiple modules/components
- Price: $97-197
- 4-8 weeks to create

**Components:**
- Video lessons
- Written guides
- Templates/resources
- Community access (optional)
- Support mechanism

## Launch strategy

### Pre-launch phase (2-4 weeks before)

**Week 1-2: Build anticipation**
```
Content focus:
- Behind-the-scenes of creation
- Sneak peeks
- Problem-focused content
- Success stories (past results)
```

**Week 3-4: Build waitlist**
```
Content focus:
- Announce upcoming launch
- DM funnel for early access
- Count down content
- Testimonials/social proof
```

### Launch phase (5-7 days)

**Day 1: Open cart**
```
Posts:
- Launch announcement (all platforms)
- What's included breakdown
- Early bird offer (if any)
- Story/Reel showing product

DM to waitlist:
- "It's live! Here's your link"
- Highlight exclusive bonuses
```

**Day 2-3: Social proof**
```
Posts:
- First customer testimonials
- Sales milestone updates
- FAQ content
- Use case examples

DM follow-up:
- Check if questions
- Remind about deadline
```

**Day 4-5: Overcome objections**
```
Posts:
- Address common concerns
- "Is this for me?" content
- Comparison to alternatives
- ROI breakdown

DM to non-buyers:
- "Any questions I can answer?"
- Share relevant testimonial
```

**Day 6-7: Urgency**
```
Posts:
- Deadline reminders
- Last chance content
- Final testimonials
- Countdown posts

DM final push:
- "Closing in X hours"
- Last call with link
```

### Post-launch (Week after)

- Thank customers publicly
- Share early wins
- Gather testimonials
- Plan next promotion
- Analyze what worked

## Evergreen promotion

### Always-on promotion system

**Bio link:**
```
Structure:
1. Free lead magnet
2. Mini product
3. Core product
4. Email signup
```

**Pinned content:**
```
X: Thread about product + results + link
IG: Carousel showing product benefits
TikTok: Video demo + "link in bio"
YT: Video review of own product
```

**Weekly content mix:**

| Day | Content type | Promotion level |
|-----|--------------|-----------------|
| Mon | Educational | 0% (pure value) |
| Tue | Educational | 0% (pure value) |
| Wed | Behind-scenes | 10% (soft mention) |
| Thu | Educational | 0% (pure value) |
| Fri | Case study | 20% (results + link) |
| Sat | Personal | 0% (connection) |
| Sun | Value + CTA | 30% (promotional) |

### Content templates for evergreen

**Template 1: Problem-solution**
```
The problem: [Pain point]
What most people try: [Common approach]
Why it doesn't work: [Limitation]
What works instead: [Your solution]
I built [product] to make this easy.
Link in bio if you want to [achieve result].
```

**Template 2: Results showcase**
```
[Customer name] was struggling with [problem].
After using [product], they [specific result].
Key insight: [Lesson]
Want similar results? [CTA]
```

**Template 3: Behind-the-scenes**
```
I just updated [product] with [new feature].
Here's why I added it:
[Reason based on customer feedback]
If you already bought: [How to access]
If you haven't: [Link/CTA]
```

**Template 4: FAQ content**
```
"Is [product] right for me?"
It's perfect if:
• [Ideal customer trait 1]
• [Ideal customer trait 2]
• [Ideal customer trait 3]
It's NOT for you if:
• [Wrong fit trait]
[Link for those it's perfect for]
```

### X/Twitter

**Thread structure:**
```
Tweet 1: Hook about result/problem
Tweet 2-5: Value and insights
Tweet 6: How you systematized this
Tweet 7: Product mention + results
Tweet 8: CTA with link
```

**Ongoing promotion:**
- Pinned thread about product
- Regular value threads with soft CTA
- Engage with comments about product
- Quote tweet testimonials

### TikTok

**Video types:**
- Day-in-life using product
- Quick tip from product
- Customer transformation
- "What's inside" reveal
- Before/after using product

- "Link in bio"
- "Comment [word] for link"


- Product link in first 3 lines

## Testimonial collection

### When to ask

**Best moments:**
- After purchase (immediate reaction)
- After consumption (specific feedback)
- After results (transformation story)

### How to ask

**Simple ask:**
```
"Hey [Name]! Quick favor -

If you've found [product] helpful, would you mind sharing a quick testimonial?

Just 2-3 sentences about:
• Your situation before
• What you got from [product]
• Any specific results

Would mean a lot!"
```

**Specific ask:**
```
"Could you answer these 3 questions?

1. What was your situation before [product]?
2. What specific result did you get?
3. What would you tell someone on the fence?

Screenshot of DM or voice memo works!"
```

### Using testimonials

**Content formats:**
- Quote graphic (carousel slide)
- Screenshot of DM (Story)
- Video testimonial (Reel)
- Thread of customer results
- Case study post

**Placement:**
- Sales page
- Launch content
- Pinned posts
- Bio link page
- Email sequences

## Common mistakes

### What kills product sales

1. **Promoting before value**
   - Too promotional too early
   - No trust built
   - No proof of expertise

2. **Wrong product-audience fit**
   - Built what you wanted, not what they need
   - Price point mismatch
   - Problem not painful enough

3. **Weak positioning**
   - Not clear who it's for
   - Not clear what problem it solves
   - Not differentiated from alternatives

4. **Poor launch execution**
   - No waitlist
   - No anticipation building
   - No urgency
   - Weak sales content

5. **Abandoned after launch**
   - No evergreen promotion
   - No new testimonials
   - No product updates
   - No ongoing content

### What drives product sales

1. **Massive upfront value**
2. **Clear transformation promise**
3. **Social proof everywhere**
4. **Consistent evergreen promotion**
5. **Engaged community**
6. **Multiple touchpoints**
7. **Strong launch events**
