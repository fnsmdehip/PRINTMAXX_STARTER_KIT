**Finding contacts:**
- LinkedIn (search "[Brand] influencer marketing")
- Brand website (partnership page)
- Instagram (DM brand account)
- Email (partnerships@brand.com)

```

My audience is [description of audience] - which aligns well with [Brand product].

• [Link to relevant post]
• [Link to relevant post]

I'd love to discuss a potential partnership. Are you the right person to talk to?

Best,
[Your name]
[Links to profiles]
```

### Method 3: Inbound attraction

**Make brands find you:**
- Tag brands in genuine content
- Create spec content (unpaid brand content)
- Join brand-specific communities
- Attend industry events
- Get featured in "top creators" lists

**What attracts brands:**
- High engagement rates (3%+)
- Quality content production
- Brand-safe content
- Clear niche focus
- Professional communication

## Negotiation strategies

### Know your worth

**Calculate your rate:**
```
Base rate formula:
Followers × Engagement rate × Industry multiplier = Base rate

Example:
50,000 followers × 4% engagement × 2.0 (tech niche) = $4,000

Adjust for:
+ Complex deliverables (+20-50%)
+ Exclusivity (+25-100%)
+ Usage rights (+25-50%)
+ Fast turnaround (+25%)
- Multiple posts (-10-20% per additional)
```

### Negotiation scripts

**When rate is too low:**
```
"Thanks for the offer. Based on my engagement rates and audience alignment, my standard rate for this type of content is [X].

I could do [slightly lower rate] if we can [add something: longer partnership, fewer revisions, flexible timeline].

Would that work for your budget?"
```

**When asking for more:**
```
"I'm excited about this partnership! A few questions:

1. Are you open to a package deal? I find multi-post campaigns perform better for brands.

2. What's the exclusivity period? My rate is [X] for 30 days, [higher rate] for 90+ days.

3. Will you be running paid ads with my content? That would be [higher rate] for whitelisting rights.

Let me know your thoughts!"
```

**When brand says no budget:**
```
"I understand budget constraints. Here are some options:

1. Product trade: I'd love [specific product] in exchange for [specific deliverable]

2. Affiliate: I can promote with my affiliate link and we both win when it converts

3. Smaller scope: For [lower budget], I could do [reduced deliverable]

4. Future partnership: Let's stay in touch for when budget opens up

Which of these might work?"
```

### Red flags to watch

**Avoid if:**
- No contract provided
- "Exposure" as payment
- Demanding all rights in perpetuity
- Unrealistic deadlines
- Too many revision rounds
- Brand has reputation issues
- Product is sketchy

**Get in writing:**
- Exact deliverables
- Payment amount and timing
- Usage rights and duration
- Exclusivity terms
- Revision limits
- Kill fee if cancelled

## Creating sponsored content

### Content that converts

**The 80/20 rule:**
- 80% value for audience
- 20% brand message

**Structure that works:**
```
1. Hook (your style, not brand speak)
2. Problem/situation
3. Natural product introduction
4. Product in action
5. Results/benefit
6. Soft CTA (not pushy)
```

### Script template (video)

```
[HOOK - 0-3 seconds]
"Here's something I've been using lately..."
or
"I finally solved my [problem]..."

[SETUP - 3-10 seconds]
"You know how [relatable problem]?
I've tried [other solutions] but nothing worked until..."

[PRODUCT INTRO - 10-20 seconds]
"[Brand] sent me their [product] and honestly, I was skeptical.
But after [time period], here's what I noticed..."

[DEMONSTRATION - 20-40 seconds]
[Show product in use]
[Highlight 2-3 key features]
[Be specific about what you like]

[RESULTS - 40-50 seconds]
"The biggest difference for me was [specific result].
I've been using it for [time] and [honest assessment]."

[CTA - 50-60 seconds]
"If you want to try it, they gave me a code [CODE] for [discount].
Link's in my bio."

[AUTHENTIC CLOSER]
"Honestly, I'll keep using this regardless of the partnership."
```

### Post template (X/Twitter)

```
I've been testing [Brand Product] for [time period].

Here's my honest take:

What I love:
• [Specific benefit 1]
• [Specific benefit 2]
• [Specific benefit 3]

What could be better:
• [Minor criticism - shows honesty]

Who it's for:
[Ideal user description]

Get [discount]% off with code [CODE]: [link]

#ad
```

### Post template (Instagram carousel)

```
Slide 1: Hook headline
"My [result] setup revealed"

Slide 2: The problem
"I used to struggle with [problem]..."

Slide 3-6: The solution (product features)
[One feature per slide with visual]

Slide 7: Results
"After [time period]: [specific results]"

Slide 8: Discount/offer
"[Brand] gave me [X]% off for you"
"Code: [CODE]"

Caption:
[Natural caption about experience]
...
Use my code [CODE] for [discount]% off [link in bio]

Paid partnership with @[brand]
```

## Disclosure requirements

### FTC guidelines (USA)

**Required disclosure when:**
- You received payment
- You received free product
- You have business relationship
- Affiliate link is present

**Acceptable disclosures:**
- #ad
- #sponsored
- "Paid partnership with [brand]"
- "Thanks to [brand] for sponsoring"
- [Brand] partner

**Placement rules:**
- Must be clear and conspicuous
- Before or with the endorsement
- Can't be buried in hashtags
- Must be visible without expanding
- For video: verbal + visual

### Platform-specific

**Instagram:**
- Use "Paid partnership" label (required by Meta)
- Add #ad in caption
- Mention in Stories text

**TikTok:**
- Use "Sponsored" tag if available
- Say "This is sponsored by [brand]"
- Include #ad in caption

**YouTube:**
- Check "Includes paid promotion" box
- Verbal disclosure in first 30 seconds
- Add to description

**X/Twitter:**
- Include #ad or "ad" in tweet
- Can be at end of tweet
- Each sponsored tweet needs disclosure

## Managing brand relationships

### Before the campaign

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Contract signed
- [ ] Payment terms confirmed
- [ ] Brief received and understood
- [ ] Key messages noted
- [ ] Dos and don'ts clear
- [ ] Assets received (if any)
- [ ] Deadline confirmed
- [ ] Revision process agreed

### During the campaign

**Best practices:**
- Submit for approval before posting
- Keep communication professional
- Meet deadlines
- Ask questions early
- Document everything
- Save all content

**Follow-up:**
- Send performance report
- Thank them for partnership

- Request testimonial

## Long-term partnerships

### Benefits of retainers

**For you:**
- Predictable income
- Deeper brand knowledge
- Less outreach needed
- Better content
- Higher total value

**For brand:**
- Consistent presence
- Better rates per post
- Audience familiarity
- Stronger association

### Pitching a retainer

```
"Hi [Name],

Thank you for the great results from our recent campaign!

I wanted to propose a longer-term partnership.

Based on [performance metrics], I think we could achieve even better results with consistent presence.

Here's what I'm thinking:

3-month retainer:
• [X] posts per month
• [X] stories per month
• Priority scheduling
• Exclusive (no competing brands)

Investment: $[X]/month (vs $[X] for same content one-off)

This would give [Brand] regular visibility with my audience and help build authentic association.

Interested in discussing?

Best,
[Name]"
```

### Maintaining relationships

- Deliver exceptional work every time
- Over-communicate
- Meet all deadlines
- Provide performance data
- Suggest improvements
- Remember key contacts
- Send occasional value (no ask)
- Be professional always

## Revenue optimization

### Maximize earnings per brand

**Package deals:**
```
Single post: $500
3-post package: $1,200 (save $300)
6-post package: $2,000 (save $1,000)
```

**Add-ons to offer:**
- Story additions: +$100-300
- Comment responses: +$50-100
- Content repurposing: +$100-200
- Whitelisting: +20-50%
- Exclusivity: +25-100%
- Rush delivery: +25%

### Diversify brand income

**Monthly targets:**
- 2-3 smaller deals ($500-2,000 each)
- 1 medium deal ($2,000-5,000)
- 1 larger deal every quarter ($5,000+)

**Mix platforms:**
- Different brands for different platforms
- Cross-platform packages for same brand
- Platform exclusives for premium rates

### Track your deals

**Spreadsheet columns:**
```
Brand | Platform | Deliverables | Rate | Status | Due date | Paid | Notes
```

**Metrics to track:**
- Revenue per platform
- Revenue per content type
- Average rate per follower
- Close rate on outreach
- Repeat brand percentage

## Common mistakes

### What kills brand deals

1. **Unprofessional communication**
   - Slow responses
   - Unclear expectations
   - Missing deadlines

2. **Poor content quality**
   - Too promotional
   - Off-brand voice
   - Low production value

3. **Audience mismatch**
   - Promoting irrelevant products
   - Too many ads
   - Lost audience trust

4. **Business issues**
   - No contract
   - Unclear pricing
   - Poor negotiation

### What wins brand deals

1. **Professional approach**
   - Quick, clear communication
   - Contracts and invoices
   - Meets all deadlines

2. **Quality content**
   - Authentic integration
   - High production value
   - Your unique style

3. **Results focus**
   - Track and report metrics
   - Optimize based on data
   - Suggest improvements

4. **Relationship building**
   - Follow up after campaigns
   - Provide value beyond contract
   - Long-term mindset
