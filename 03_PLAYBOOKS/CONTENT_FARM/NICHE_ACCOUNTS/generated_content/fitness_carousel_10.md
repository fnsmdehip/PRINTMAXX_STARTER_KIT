## Carousel 5: "Progressive Overload - The Complete Guide"

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
Progressive overload explained in 6 slides. This is the only concept that matters for muscle growth.

**Slide 2:**
What it is: doing slightly more than last time. Every session. That's it. More weight, more reps, more sets, or more time under tension.

**Slide 3:**
Method 1: Add weight. Add 5 lbs to barbell movements every week. Add 2.5 lbs to dumbbell movements. When you can complete all prescribed reps, go up.

**Slide 4:**
Method 2: Add reps. If you can't add weight yet, add 1 rep per set. Week 1: 3x8. Week 2: 3x9. Week 3: 3x10. Then add weight and go back to 3x8.

**Slide 5:**
Method 3: Add sets. Week 1: 3 sets. Week 4: 4 sets. Increase total volume over time. This works especially well for accessories.

**Slide 6:**
How to track: use a notes app, spreadsheet, or workout journal. Log every exercise, weight, sets, and reps. Compare week to week. If numbers aren't going up, something needs to change (sleep, food, or intensity).

**Slide 7 (CTA):**
This concept is responsible for 80% of muscle growth results. Save this. Apply it Monday. Comment OVERLOAD for a tracking template.

## Carousel 7: "12-Week Training Periodization"

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
How to structure 12 weeks of training for maximum results.

**Slide 2:**
Weeks 1-4 (Foundation): moderate weight, higher reps (8-12). Focus on form and mind-muscle connection. Build work capacity. Progressive overload starts here.

**Slide 3:**
Weeks 5-8 (Growth): increase weight, moderate reps (6-10). Push intensity. This is where most visible changes happen. Aim for RPE 8-9 on working sets.

**Slide 4:**
Weeks 9-11 (Peak): heaviest weights, lower reps (4-8). Test your limits. This is where PRs happen. Recovery becomes critical.

**Slide 5:**
Week 12 (Deload): reduce volume by 40-50%. Same exercises, lighter weights, fewer sets. Your body supercompensates during this week. Many people see their best visual changes during deloads.

**Slide 6:**
Why this works: prevents plateaus. manages fatigue. creates natural peaks and recovery. this is what actual strength programs use.

## Carousel 10: "The Recovery Checklist"

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
Your recovery is probably worse than your training. here's the checklist.

**Slide 2:**
Sleep: 7-8 hours minimum. Dark room. No phone 30 min before bed. Same bedtime within 30 minutes every night.

**Slide 3:**
Nutrition: protein within 2 hours post-workout. Total daily calories matching your goal (surplus for growth, deficit for fat loss). Hydration: half your bodyweight in ounces of water minimum.

**Slide 4:**
Movement: 10-15 minute walk after training. Light stretching or foam rolling on off days. Don't sit for 8 hours after a hard session.

**Slide 5:**
Stress: high cortisol kills recovery. 5 minutes of deep breathing daily. Walk outside. Get sunlight first thing in the morning. These aren't "wellness" tips, they directly impact muscle growth.

**Slide 6:**
What to skip: ice baths (blunt the inflammatory response needed for growth). Heavy stretching before training (reduces force output). NSAIDs after training (same issue as ice baths).

**Slide 7 (CTA):**
Recovery is where gains happen. The gym is the stimulus. Food and sleep are the response. Save this checklist.
