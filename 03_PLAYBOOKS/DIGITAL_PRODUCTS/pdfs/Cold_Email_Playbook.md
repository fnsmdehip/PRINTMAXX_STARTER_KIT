## 1. The 6-question framework

Every cold email you send should answer these 6 questions in under 100 words. No exceptions.

| # | Question | What It Does |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **What you do** | The service or product, stated simply |
| 2 | **Who for** | The target customer, specifically |
| 3 | **How** | The mechanism (how it actually works) |
| 4 | **Problem solved** | The pain point you address |
| 5 | **Proof** | Specific numbers, case studies, results |
| 6 | **ROI** | What they get in concrete terms |

Stack all 6. No fluff. Lead with the consequence, not the explanation.

**Example (bad):**
```
Hi [Name],

I hope this email finds you well. I wanted to reach out because
I believe there could be a mutually beneficial opportunity for
us to explore together. Our company specializes in providing
comprehensive digital marketing solutions that can help businesses
like yours achieve unprecedented growth...
```

87 words. Zero questions answered. Straight to trash.

**Example (good):**
```
[Name],

we help fitness brands post to 6 platforms in 10 minutes.
one piece of content, 6x distribution.

last month we helped FitLife Gym go from 12 posts/month to 90.
their reach went up 340%.

worth a 15-min call?

[Your name]
```

43 words. All 6 questions answered. Reply rate: 4-8%.

### Research checklist (per prospect)

Before sending, spend 2-3 minutes finding:

- [ ] Their recent content (reference a specific post or video)
- [ ] Their company size (adjust language: "your team" vs "you")
- [ ] A specific metric (reviews, followers, posts/week, traffic)
- [ ] A specific problem you noticed (inconsistent posting, bad SEO, missing reviews)
- [ ] A competitor doing better (creates urgency without being aggressive)

**The personalized first line is the single highest-impact variable in cold email.** Generic opens like "I noticed your company" get ignored. Specific observations like "saw your TikTok reel about protein timing, solid engagement" get read.

## 10. A/B testing priorities

Test in this order (highest impact first):

| Priority | What to Test | Impact Level |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | Subject lines | Biggest impact on open rate |
| 2 | First line | Specific observation vs generic opener |
| 3 | CTA | "Quick call" vs "try it" vs "interested?" |
| 4 | Send time | 8am vs 11am vs 2pm |
| 5 | Sequence length | 3 emails vs 5 emails |
| 6 | Social proof format | Numbers vs testimonial quotes |

**How to A/B test:**
- Send variant A to 50% of your list, variant B to the other 50%
- Minimum 100 sends per variant before making decisions
- Track open rate AND reply rate (high opens + low replies = bad body, good subject)
- Run one test at a time (changing two variables = no clean data)

### Why some sequences convert higher

Clipper and streamer sequences convert highest because you're OFFERING something, not selling. Clippers get paid work. Streamers get free distribution. Agency and SaaS emails ask for money. Different dynamics.

**Rule of thumb:** The more you give first, the higher the reply rate. Structure your offer so the recipient gets value before you ask for anything.

## 13. Common mistakes (and how to fix them)

| Mistake | Why It Kills Replies | Fix |
|---------|---------------------|-----|
| Too long (200+ words) | Attention gone after sentence 3 | Keep under 100 words |
| No numbers | No proof = no credibility | Always include 1-2 specific results |
| Weak CTA | "Let me know what you think" = no urgency | Ask a clear yes/no question |
| Generic opener | "I hope this finds you well" = instant delete | Reference something specific they did |
| Multiple CTAs | "Call me or email me or visit our site" = decision paralysis | One ask per email |
| Begging language | "Would you be so kind" = low status | State the offer. Let them decide. |
| Selling in the first email | Pricing and features before they care | Sell the meeting, not the product |
| No follow-up | 80% of deals come after email 3+ | Always send the full sequence |

### Sending infrastructure

For cold email at scale, you need:

1. **3+ domains** (not your main domain) for sending
2. **Warmed inboxes** (2-week warmup minimum, or buy pre-warmed)
3. **SPF/DKIM/DMARC** configured on all sending domains

5. **Rotation**: Rotate subject lines, first lines, and sender names

## 15. Deliverability fundamentals

### The basics

| Factor | What To Do | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Domain warmup | Send 5/day, increase by 5 daily for 2 weeks | New domains get flagged |
| Authentication | SPF + DKIM + DMARC on all domains | Gmail/Outlook require this |
| Sending limits | 30-50/day per inbox | Over 100/day = spam folder |
| List quality | Verify emails before sending | Bounces kill sender reputation |
| Content | No spammy words, no HTML-heavy emails | Plain text converts better anyway |
| Unsubscribe | Include one-click unsubscribe | Required by law (CAN-SPAM, GDPR) |

### If your emails land in spam

1. Check domain reputation: mail-tester.com (score should be 9+/10)
2. Check blacklists: mxtoolbox.com/blacklists
3. Reduce sending volume by 50%
4. Wait 7 days, then slowly increase
5. If still in spam: new domain, fresh warmup
