## N003 - Fitness Men

**Webinar Title:** "From Skinny-Fat to Shredded in 12 Weeks: The Body Recomposition Blueprint That's Actually Scalable"

**Target Audience:** Men 25-45, beginner-intermediate, physique-conscious

### Section 1: Why Most Plans Fail (8 min)

**Title:** "The 3 Reasons You're Stuck in Skinny-Fat (It's Not Your Fault)"

1. **Reason 1: Workout selection is backwards**
   - Men do bicep curls + cardio (looks good, doesn't work)
   - Need: Compound lifts that signal your body to build muscle
   - Bench press, squats, deadlifts, rows (boring but they work)

2. **Reason 2: Progressive overload is missing**
   - You do same workout every week (plateau by week 3)
   - Your body adapts, stops growing
   - Need: Add weight or reps every single week

3. **Reason 3: Nutrition is guessed, not tracked**
   - "Eat clean" is vague
   - Don't know if you're in calorie deficit (fat loss) or surplus (muscle gain)
   - Need: Protein targets + calorie math

**Proof:** Show before/after photo (your own or documented transformation)

### Section 2: The 3-Phase Recomposition System (20 min)

#### Phase 1: Foundation Building (Weeks 1-4) - 6 min
**Goal: Establish habit + baseline strength**

- **Workout:** Full-body, 3x/week
  - Day 1: Squat + rows + accessories
  - Day 2: Bench + deadlifts + accessories
  - Day 3: Mix of both
  - Each session: 45 minutes
- **Progression rule:** +2.5-5 lbs per week on main lifts
- **Nutrition:** Maintenance calories + high protein (0.8g per lb bodyweight)

**Why:** Boring is good. You're building the habit before adding complexity.

#### Phase 2: Progressive Overload (Weeks 5-8) - 7 min
**Goal: Force muscle growth through controlled difficulty**

- **Workout:** Still 3x/week, but add one exercise per session
  - Day 1: Squat (main), rows (main), leg press, leg curl, core
  - Day 2: Bench (main), deadlift (main), flyes, bent rows, core
  - Day 3: Upper/lower hybrid, higher reps (12-15)
  - Each session: 60 minutes

- **Progression rule:**
  - Add 1 rep until you hit target range (8-12 reps)
  - When you hit 12, increase weight +5-10 lbs
  - Drop back to 8 reps, repeat

- **Nutrition:** Slight deficit (-300 calories) but high protein (1g per lb)
  - This is where fat burns while muscles grow

**Why:** You're forcing your muscles to adapt under load while slowly creating a calorie deficit.

#### Phase 3: Hypertrophy Push (Weeks 9-12) - 7 min
**Goal: Maximize muscle gain, dial in final physique**

- **Workout:** Upper/lower split, 4x/week
  - Day 1: Upper (heavy)
  - Day 2: Lower (heavy)
  - Day 3: Upper (hypertrophy, higher reps)
  - Day 4: Lower (hypertrophy, higher reps)
  - Each session: 45 minutes (you're now strong, less rest needed)

- **Progression rule:** Add volume (more sets/reps) not just weight
  - Heavy days: 3x3-5 reps
  - Hypertrophy days: 4x8-12 reps

- **Nutrition:** Maintenance calories + high protein + slight carb increase
  - You've built muscle, can eat normal now without gaining fat

**Live demo:** Show the app/spreadsheet you use to track workouts (makes it real)

### Section 3: The Tracking System (12 min)

**Title:** "How to Stay Accountable (The Unsexy Part That Matters)"

#### Workout Tracking
- **Tool:** Simple spreadsheet or app (I use StrongLifts)
- **What to log:** Weight, reps, date
- **Review weekly:** Did you hit progression targets?
  - Yes → Keep going
  - No → Adjust (eat more, sleep more, or deload)

#### Nutrition Tracking
- **Tool:** MyFitnessPal (free) or Cronometer
- **Weekly check:** Did you hit protein/calorie targets?
- **Metric that matters:** Scale weight (track weekly average, not daily)
  - Week 1-2: Might go UP (muscle + water)
  - Week 4: Should see scale stabilize while looking better
  - Week 8+: Should see gradual downward trend (-1-2 lbs/week)

#### Biometric Tracking
- **Take photos:** Front, side, back (every 4 weeks)
- **Photos matter more than scale** (your real transformation is visible here)
- **Measurements:** Chest, arms, waist, thighs (every 4 weeks)

**Why:** What gets measured gets managed. You can't optimize what you don't track.

### Section 1: Why Normal Productivity Fails ADHD Brains (9 min)

**Title:** "The ADHD Productivity Problem Isn't Laziness (It's Architecture)"

1. **Issue 1: Executive function taxes**
   - You have 3 "mental power-ups" per day (real brain science)
   - Neurotypical people: Can spend one on planning, one on work, one on random stuff
   - ADHD people: Spend all 3 on "should I work now or later?" anxiety
   - By 2 PM, you're cognitively exhausted without doing actual work

2. **Issue 2: Time blindness**
   - "Just work for 30 minutes" doesn't work
   - Suddenly it's 3 hours or 15 minutes—no in-between
   - Normal time-management assumes you can *feel* time. You can't.

3. **Issue 3: Hyperfocus is a trap**
   - You hyperfocus on email (fun, immediate dopamine)
   - Skip actual work (boring, delayed reward)
   - Then feel guilty and do nothing

4. **Issue 4: Decision paralysis**
   - Too many options = frozen
   - "What should I work on?" becomes unsolvable
   - Defaulting to YouTube is easier than choosing

**Proof:** Show ADHD brain neuroscience (executive function graph that shows the gap)

**Key insight:** You're not broken. Systems are built wrong for your neurology.

### Section 2: The Now/Next/Later System (18 min)

**Title:** "The 3-Box Framework That Matches How Your Brain Works"

#### Box 1: NOW (What you're doing right now) - 5 min
**Rules:**
- Only ONE item in NOW at any time
- That's it. Not three. Not two. One.
- Written as a verb: "Write proposal email" not "Work on proposal"

**Why it works:**
- ADHD brains hyperfocus on one thing
- Removes "what should I do?" decision
- Clear done state (check off = dopamine)

**Implementation:**
- Phone home screen: Widget showing current NOW task
- Timer on wall (visual reminder)
- Reward: Check off = 5-min break (ADHD needs breaks)

#### Box 2: NEXT (What you'll do after NOW) - 6 min
**Rules:**
- Max 5 items in NEXT
- Ordered by urgency + energy level
- Written as small verbs: "Send 3 emails" not "Email people"

**Why it works:**
- You can see what's coming (removes anxiety)
- Limited options (no decision paralysis)
- When you finish NOW, just move to NEXT (no planning needed)

**Task sizing principle:**
- Small tasks (5-15 min) for low-energy days
- Medium tasks (15-30 min) for medium-energy
- Big tasks (30-60 min) only when hyperfocused

**Implementation:**
- Daily NEXT list (refresh every morning)
- Sticky note or app you see constantly

#### Box 3: LATER (Everything else) - 7 min
**Rules:**
- Dump everything else here
- Don't organize it by project/date (wastes mental power)
- Just: "Add item, move on"

**Why it works:**
- Clears your mental RAM immediately
- You're not forgetting, it's recorded
- Removes "I should remember" anxiety

**Review:**
- Weekly: Pick 5 items from LATER → Move to NEXT for next week
- Monthly: Delete 20% (honestly, you won't do most of this stuff)

**Implementation:**
- Digital list (Keep, Todoist, or Notes app)
- One long list, no sub-folders (organization = paralysis)

### Section 3: Real Implementation + Examples (15 min)

**Title:** "What Your Actual Day Looks Like"

#### Example: Writer/Content Creator
**8 AM startup:**
- NOW: "Write 500-word email"
- NEXT: ["Send 3 outreach emails", "Review competitor content", "Create content calendar", "Post thread", "Fix website copy"]
- LATER: [50 items dumped]

**8:40 AM (email done):**
- Check off email → Dopamine hit

- 5-min walk, water, snack

**8:50 AM:**
- NOW: "Send 3 outreach emails"
- NEXT: ["Review competitor content", "Create content calendar", "Post thread", "Fix website copy"]

**9:25 AM (emails done):**
- NOW: "Create content calendar (30-min task)" → Longer task because you're energized
- Work until 10 AM (hyperfocus locked in)

**10 AM break (FORCED):**
- You've done 3 items, made progress visible
- Show photos of check-offs to client/accountability partner

#### Example: Service Business Owner
**Morning (high executive function):**
- NOW: "Review client proposals" (15 min)
- NEXT: ["Respond to 5 emails", "Update project tracker", "Call vendor quote", "Invoice client"]

**After proposal review:**
- NOW: "Respond to 5 emails" (batched, removes task-switching)

**Later (afternoon, low energy):**
- NOW: "Update project tracker" (admin, low-thinking task)
- NEXT: Tasks moved to tomorrow

#### Example: Student
**Study session:**
- NOW: "Math problems, sections 1-3 (20 min)"
- NEXT: ["Write essay outline", "Review chemistry notes", "Read chapter 5", "Do practice test"]
- LATER: [All other classes/assignments]

**After 20 min:**
