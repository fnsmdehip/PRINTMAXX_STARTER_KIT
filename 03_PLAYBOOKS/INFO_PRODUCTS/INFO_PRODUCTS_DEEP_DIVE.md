## Executive Summary

Info products are the highest-margin money method in the PRINTMAXX stack. Zero marginal cost, infinite scalability, and perfect synergy with content farm and AI influencer operations.

**Key Numbers:**
- $97-$197 price points convert best for solopreneurs
- 2-5% conversion rate at $100 is healthy
- Email list with 200 subscribers can generate $10k+ flash sale drops
- Payment plans increase conversion 50-300% on $497+ products
- Middle tier in 3-tier pricing converts best (price anchoring)

## Pricing Strategy

### Psychological Price Points (Memorize These)

| Price | Use Case | Conversion Rate |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| $7-$19 | Tripwire, impulse buy | 5-10% from warm traffic |
| $27 | Entry product, low commitment | 3-5% |
| $47-$49 | Mid-tier entry | 2-4% |
| $97 | Sweet spot #1 | 2-3% |
| $197 | Sweet spot #2 | 1.5-2.5% |
| $297 | Premium entry | 1-2% |
| $497 | Comprehensive course | 0.5-1.5% |
| $997 | High-ticket | 0.3-1% |
| $1,997+ | Premium/certification | 0.1-0.5% |

**Why These Numbers Work:**
- Consumers conditioned to these prices from years of digital products
- $97 and $197 hit the sweet spot: high enough for perceived value, low enough for impulse purchase
- Under $200 = no spouse/boss approval needed

### Good-Better-Best Tiering

**Standard Template:**
| Tier | Multiplier | Features |
|------|------------|----------|
| Basic | 1x ($97) | Core video course |
| Standard | 2x ($197) | Course + templates + workbook |
| Premium | 5x ($497) | Everything + group coaching |

**Key Insight:** Middle tier converts best due to price anchoring. Highlight it as "Most Popular" or "Best Value."

### Payment Plans (For Products $297+)

**Structure:**
- Full pay: $497
- 3 payments: $197/mo = $591 total (19% premium)

**Why This Works:**
- $197 feels more accessible than $497
- You earn 19% more on payment plan customers
- Reduces "need to ask spouse" friction
- Commitment bias locks them in after first payment

**Payment Plan Conversion Lift:**
| Price Point | Without Plan | With Plan | Lift |
|-------------|--------------|-----------|------|
| $297-$497 | 2% | 3-4% | 50-100% |
| $997+ | 0.5% | 1-2% | 100-300% |

### Detailed Platform Breakdown

#### Gumroad (Start Here)

**Pros:**
- Zero upfront cost
- Built-in audience discovery
- Simple product setup
- Handles global payments
- PDF, video, membership support

**Cons:**
- 10% + $0.50 per sale (expensive at scale)
- Limited customization
- Basic analytics

**Use When:** Testing new products, under $5k/mo revenue

**Breakeven Point:** After $5k in sales, ThriveCart ($495 one-time) pays for itself

**Gumroad Fee Analysis:**
| Monthly Revenue | Gumroad Fees | ThriveCart (Stripe 3%) | Savings |
|-----------------|--------------|------------------------|---------|
| $1,000 | $100 | $30 | -$70 (stay on Gumroad) |

### The Flash Sale Method (From @knoxtwts Alpha)

**The Pattern:**
1. Build email list (even 200 subscribers works)
2. Send 15-30 days of pure value (zero pitching)
3. Drop one flash sale email
4. Pull $10-45k in 48 hours
5. Return to value mode
6. Repeat quarterly

**Why It Works:**
- List is primed with value
- Single sale creates urgency
- No pitch fatigue
- Can "cash out" list whenever needed

**Email Structure for Flash Sale:**
```
Subject: [First Name], 48 hours only

Hey [Name],

For the next 48 hours, I'm opening [Product] at [X]% off.

[1 sentence on what it is]
[1 sentence on the result]
[1 sentence on why now]

[Link]

Price goes back to [full price] Thursday at midnight.

[Your name]
```

### Upsell/Downsell Strategy

**Order Bump (At Checkout):**
- Complementary product: "Add the workbook for $27"
- Conversion: 2.5-3%
- Keep it simple, checkbox format

**Post-Purchase Upsell (One-Click):**
- Higher-tier product: "Upgrade to include coaching calls"
- Conversion: 8-15%
- Show immediately after purchase
- No re-entering payment info

**Downsell (If Upsell Declined):**
- Lighter version: "Not ready for coaching? Get the templates only"
- Conversion: 20-30% of decliners
- Lower price point, still valuable

**AOV Impact:**
| Element | Acceptance | AOV Lift |
|---------|------------|----------|
| Order Bump | 2.5-3% | +$0.75-$1.50 avg |
| Post-Purchase Upsell | 8-15% | +$16-$75 avg |
| Downsell | 20-30% of declines | +$5-$15 avg |

**Combined Effect:** 10-30% total AOV increase

### List Building

**Lead Magnet Ideas by Niche:**

| Niche | Lead Magnet | Expected Opt-in Rate |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| Faith | 7-day prayer challenge | 35-45% |
| Fitness | Free workout PDF | 30-40% |

| Finance | Budget template | 30-40% |

### Email Sequence Framework

**Welcome Sequence (7 emails, 7 days):**

**Email 1 (Immediate):** Deliver lead magnet + introduce yourself
**Email 2 (Day 2):** Your story + why this matters
**Email 3 (Day 3):** Quick win (implement this today)
**Email 4 (Day 4):** Case study or success story
**Email 5 (Day 5):** Common mistake + solution (soft pitch)
**Email 6 (Day 6):** Main pitch + urgency
**Email 7 (Day 7):** Last chance + testimonials

**Conversion Rates by Sequence Position:**
- Email 1-3: 0.5-1% (trust building)
- Email 4-5: 1-2% (warming up)
- Email 6-7: 2-4% (urgency phase)

### Email Copy Framework

**Subject Line Formulas:**
- Question: "Are you making this prayer mistake?"
- Number: "5 workouts that burn fat 2x faster"
- Personal: "[First Name], quick question"
- Curiosity: "The weird thing about..."
- Urgency: "Closing in 3 hours"

**Body Structure:**
```
Hook (1-2 sentences)
↓
Story or proof (2-3 sentences)
↓
Main point (1 paragraph)
↓
CTA (1 link, clear action)
↓
P.S. (secondary hook or deadline)
```

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Pricing too low:** $7-$27 for comprehensive course = undervalued
2. **Too many tiers:** 3 max, don't offer 6 options
3. **Random pricing:** $83 instead of $97 looks unprofessional
4. **No payment plans:** Kills conversion on high-ticket
5. **Cost-based pricing:** Ignore market value
6. **Crippled base tier:** Core value must be in lowest tier
7. **No urgency:** Evergreen without any scarcity underperforms
8. **Complex checkout:** More than 2 clicks = lost sales
9. **No email follow-up:** Single email vs sequence = 3-5x difference
10. **Ignoring refunds:** Bad reviews cost more than refund
