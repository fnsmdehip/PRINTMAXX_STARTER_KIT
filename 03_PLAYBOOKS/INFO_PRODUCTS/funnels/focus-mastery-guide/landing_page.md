## Body copy

You sit down to work.

15 minutes later you've checked email, scrolled Twitter, texted someone back, and Googled something random.

The task you sat down to do? Barely touched.

Sound familiar?

Your focus isn't broken. It's just never been trained.

**The math:**

2 hours of deep focus per day = 10 hours per week = 500+ hours per year of real work.

Most people get about 15 minutes of deep work between interruptions. That's 1.25 hours per week.

The difference? Everything.

**What's in the protocol:**

- The 4-step deep work framework (how to actually do it)
- "Phone jail" setup (10-second change that removes the temptation)
- The 90-minute rhythm (why it works better than Pomodoro for deep work)
- Environment design checklist (your space is probably sabotaging you)
- The "focus recovery" technique (what to do when you get distracted)

**This is for you if:**

- You know you should focus but can't seem to do it
- You've tried Pomodoro and it felt too choppy
- Your job requires thinking, not just task completion
- You want to ship projects, not just stay busy

**This is NOT for you if:**

- Your job is 100% reactive (support, customer service, etc.)
- You can already focus for 2+ hours easily
- You think focus is just "willpower"
