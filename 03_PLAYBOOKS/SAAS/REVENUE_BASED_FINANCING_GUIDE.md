# Revenue-Based Financing Guide for Bootstrapped Startups

Non-dilutive capital for SaaS and tech founders. Keep your equity, scale your business.

---

## What Is Revenue-Based Financing (RBF)?

Revenue-based financing is a funding model where you receive capital in exchange for a fixed percentage of your future monthly revenue until a predetermined amount is repaid.

**Key characteristics:**
- Non-dilutive (you keep 100% equity)
- Repayment fluctuates with your revenue
- Fixed repayment cap (typically 1.1x to 2x principal)
- No personal guarantees in most cases
- Faster than traditional loans or VC

**How it differs from other funding:**

| Funding Type | Equity Given | Repayment | Speed | Best For |
|--------------|--------------|-----------|-------|----------|
| VC | 15-25% per round | None (exit event) | 3-6 months | Hypergrowth plays |
| Bank Loan | 0% | Fixed monthly + interest | 2-8 weeks | Asset-heavy businesses |
| RBF | 0% | % of revenue | 1-4 weeks | Predictable revenue |
| Angel | 10-20% | None (exit event) | 1-3 months | Pre-revenue/early |

## Major RBF Providers

### 1. Lighter Capital

**Overview:** One of the largest RBF providers focused on B2B tech/SaaS companies.

**Terms:**
- Funding: $50K to $4M
- Minimum requirement: $15K+ MRR ($180K ARR)
- Repayment: 2-8% of monthly revenue
- Cap: 1.35x to 1.65x principal (depending on risk)
- Term: 3-5 years (typical payback 2-3 years)
- Fees: No equity, no warrants, no board seats

**Requirements:**
- B2B software or tech-enabled services
- At least 6 months of revenue history
- U.S. or Canadian company
- Reasonable unit economics (CAC payback < 18 months preferred)

**Best for:** Established SaaS with $15K-$200K MRR wanting growth capital without dilution.

**Application:** Online application, connect financials (QuickBooks, Stripe), decision in 2-4 weeks.

**Perks beyond capital:**
- Founder community access
- Partner discounts (AWS, HubSpot, etc.)
- CFO advisory services

---

### 2. Clearco (formerly Clearbanc)

**Overview:** Originally e-commerce focused, now also serves SaaS. Known for very fast funding decisions.

**Terms:**
- Funding: $10K to $20M
- Minimum requirement: $10K+ monthly revenue
- Repayment: 6-12% of revenue
- Cap: 1.06x to 1.12x principal (lower than most)
- No fixed term (pay as you earn)

**Requirements:**
- E-commerce, SaaS, or subscription business
- 6+ months of revenue
- Connect ad accounts (Facebook, Google) and payment processor
- Primarily funds marketing/inventory spend

**Best for:** E-commerce and D2C brands with proven ad spend ROI. Fast capital for scaling ads.

**Application:** Connect accounts, AI-driven analysis, offers in 24-48 hours.

---

### 3. Pipe

**Overview:** Not traditional RBF - Pipe is a trading platform where you sell your future recurring revenue at a discount for immediate cash.

**How it works:**
- Connect your billing system (Stripe, Chargebee, etc.)
- Pipe shows your tradeable contracts
- Sell monthly/annual subscriptions for upfront cash
- Buyers (institutional investors) purchase at a discount

**Terms:**
- Funding: Based on your ARR (can access up to 100% of annual contracts)
- No minimum (but practical minimum ~$50K ARR)
- Discount rate: 1-5% per trade (varies by contract quality)
- No cap - you sell the exact contract value

**Requirements:**
- Subscription-based business model
- Recurring revenue with low churn
- Compatible billing system

**Best for:** SaaS with annual contracts wanting to pull forward cash without debt or dilution. Great for smoothing cash flow.

**Unique advantage:** You can choose exactly which contracts to trade. No blanket revenue share.

---

### 4. Capchase

**Overview:** Focused on B2B SaaS with strong ARR metrics. Offers both revenue advances and expense management.

**Terms:**
- Funding: $50K to $50M
- Minimum requirement: $100K+ ARR (some flexibility at $50K+)
- Repayment: Fixed monthly payments (not pure revenue share)
- Fee: 6-18% flat fee on capital drawn
- Term: 6-18 months

**Requirements:**
- B2B SaaS with subscription revenue
- Operating in US, UK, or select EU countries
- Minimum 6 months operating history
- Net revenue retention > 80% preferred

**Products:**
- **Capchase Grow:** Revenue financing
- **Capchase Extend:** Extend runway, pay vendors over time
- **Capchase Pay:** Let customers pay annually while you get cash upfront

**Best for:** Growth-stage SaaS with strong retention wanting flexible capital.

---

### 5. Uncapped

**Overview:** UK/Europe-focused revenue-based financing for digital businesses.

**Terms:**
- Funding: $10K to $10M (GBP/EUR available)
- Minimum requirement: $10K+ monthly revenue
- Repayment: 6-12% of revenue
- Cap: 1.06x (6% flat fee)
- Term: No fixed term

**Requirements:**
- E-commerce, SaaS, apps, or digital services
- 6+ months trading history
- UK or EU registered company
- Must connect data sources (Stripe, Shopify, ad accounts)

**Best for:** European founders who need non-dilutive capital without US entity complications.

---

### 6. Wayflyer

**Overview:** E-commerce specialist with very competitive rates. Strong in DTC/retail.

**Terms:**
- Funding: $10K to $20M
- Minimum requirement: $20K+ monthly revenue
- Repayment: Fixed percentage (varies)
- Fee: 2-8% of capital (competitive rates)
- Term: 6-12 months typical

**Requirements:**
- E-commerce or consumer products business
- Minimum 6 months sales history
- Connect Shopify, Amazon, or similar platform
- International (US, UK, EU, AU, NZ)

**Best for:** D2C and e-commerce brands needing inventory or marketing capital.

## When to Use Revenue-Based Financing

**Good use cases:**
- Scaling paid acquisition with proven CAC/LTV
- Hiring to accelerate growth (sales, engineering)
- Inventory financing for seasonal demand
- Bridge to profitability (not bridge to next round)
- International expansion
- Product development with clear revenue timeline

**Prerequisites for success:**
- $15K+ MRR (most providers require this minimum)
- Positive unit economics (CAC payback < 18 months)
- Predictable, recurring revenue
- Clear ROI on capital deployment
- Low churn (<5% monthly for most providers)

**Math that works:**
```
Example: $100K RBF at 1.35x cap, 5% revenue share

MRR: $30K
Monthly payment: $1,500 (5%)
Payback: 90 months worst case
Actual payback (growing): 24-36 months
Total repayment: $135K

ROI needed: >35% over payback period
If you can 2x revenue with the $100K, you pay $135K to make $360K+
```

## When NOT to Use RBF

**Red flags - avoid RBF if:**
- No proven revenue model yet (pre-PMF)
- Revenue is declining or flat
- You need capital for runway, not growth
- Churn is high (>8% monthly)
- No clear ROI on capital use
- You're considering it as "cheap VC" (it's debt, treat it as such)

**Better alternatives:**
- Pre-revenue: Angel/friends and family
- Pre-PMF: VC or incubator
- Cash crunch: Cost cutting first
- High growth potential: VC may be better (dilution vs. debt burden)

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

**Predatory terms:**
- Uncapped repayment multipliers (>2x is aggressive)
- Personal guarantees on large amounts
- Warrants or equity kickers hidden in terms
- Prepayment penalties
- Revenue covenants that trigger default
- UCC liens on all assets (standard for some, negotiate if possible)

**Questions to ask:**
1. What's the total repayment cap?
2. Is there a prepayment discount?
3. What happens if revenue drops 50%?
4. Are there any equity components?
5. What triggers a default?
6. Can I take additional financing?

## PRINTMAXX Applications

### Which money methods could qualify?

**Ready for RBF (with $15K+ MRR):**
- APP_FACTORY - Subscription apps with consistent IAP revenue
- SAAS - Any micro-SaaS hitting revenue threshold
- CONTENT_FARM - Monetized channels with stable ad revenue
- INFO_PRODUCTS - Recurring membership/course revenue
- AGENCY_SERVICES - Retainer-based revenue qualifies

**Timeline to RBF eligibility:**

| Method | Path to $15K MRR | Estimated Timeline |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| APP_FACTORY | 3 apps at $5K MRR each | 6-12 months |
| SAAS | Single product, 150 users at $99/mo | 4-8 months |
| INFO_PRODUCTS | Membership at $47/mo, 320 members | 6-12 months |
| AGENCY | 3 retainer clients at $5K/mo | 3-6 months |

### How to prepare for RBF

**6 months before applying:**
- Clean up financials (QuickBooks, Xero)
- Track MRR, churn, CAC in a dashboard
- Build 6+ months of revenue history
- Document how you'll deploy capital

**3 months before:**
- Calculate unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback)
- Project revenue scenarios (base, upside, downside)
- Identify specific growth initiatives (ads, hiring, product)
- Get customer contracts in order

**Application prep:**
- Connect Stripe, billing systems
- Prepare 12-month P&L
- Document revenue sources
- Have deployment plan ready

## RBF Stacking Strategy

Some founders use multiple RBF sources:

**Example stack:**
1. Pipe for trading annual contracts (immediate cash)
2. Lighter Capital for growth capital (longer term)
3. Clearco specifically for ad spend (short term)

**Considerations:**
- Most providers don't prohibit stacking
- Total debt service shouldn't exceed 20-25% of revenue
- Prioritize lowest-cost capital first
- Keep buffer for revenue dips

## Action Items for PRINTMAXX

1. **Track MRR religiously** - Can't get RBF without clean revenue data
2. **Build to $15K MRR milestone** - This unlocks most providers
3. **Focus on retention** - Low churn = better terms
4. **Document unit economics** - Know your CAC/LTV cold
5. **Create deployment plan** - "I'll use $X for Y to generate Z"

**First provider to apply to:**
- If SaaS: Lighter Capital or Founderpath
- If E-commerce: Clearco or Wayflyer
- If European: Uncapped
- If annual contracts: Pipe
