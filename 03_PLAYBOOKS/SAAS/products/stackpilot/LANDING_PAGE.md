## FAQ section

### Q1: Why not just use ChatGPT's memory?
ChatGPT memory is conversation-based. You can't search it, organize it, or add variables. It doesn't work across models. StackPilot is a dedicated prompt library with structure.

### Q2: I already save prompts in Notion. Why switch?
Notion doesn't have variables, one-click run, or workflow automation. You still copy-paste. StackPilot saves 5+ minutes per prompt use. If you use prompts 10 times per day, that's an hour back.

### Q3: Which AI models do you support?
ChatGPT (GPT-4, GPT-4o), Claude (Sonnet, Opus, Haiku), and Gemini. We add new models as they become popular. Your prompts work across all of them.

### Q4: Can I import my existing prompts?
Yes. Upload a CSV, paste from Notion, or use our Chrome extension to save prompts from your chat history. Most users migrate in under 30 minutes.

### Q5: What's a workflow?
A sequence of prompts where output flows to input. Example: Prompt 1 generates an outline. Prompt 2 takes that outline and writes a first draft. Prompt 3 edits for tone. Click once, get a finished draft.

### Q6: Do you store my AI outputs?
Only if you choose. By default, outputs are shown but not saved. Turn on history to keep outputs. Delete anytime.

### Q7: Is my data private?
Yes. We don't train models on your prompts. We don't sell data. You can export or delete everything at any time.

### Q8: What's the variable syntax?
Use {{variable_name}}. When you run the prompt, StackPilot shows a form with each variable as a field. Fill it in, click run.

### Q9: Can I share prompts publicly?
Yes. Make any prompt public to share a link. Or keep it private. Team plan lets you share within your team only.

### Q10: What if I need more team members?
Team plan includes 5 members. Each additional member is $10/month. No limit on total team size.
