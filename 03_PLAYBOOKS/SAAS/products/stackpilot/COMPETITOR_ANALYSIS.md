# StackPilot Competitor Analysis

**Last updated:** 2026-01-20

---

## Summary

StackPilot wins on simplicity and workflow automation. Competitors either focus on prompt sharing (marketplaces) or are feature-bloated productivity suites. We're the best tool for individual power users who want to organize and automate their prompts.

Our positioning: "Your AI prompts in one place. Variables, multi-model, workflows."

---

## Primary competitors

### 1. PromptBase

**What they are:** Prompt marketplace. Buy and sell prompts.

**Pricing:**
- Free to browse
- Prompts: $2-10 each
- Seller takes 80%, PromptBase takes 20%

**Strengths:**
- Large prompt library
- Monetization for creators
- SEO for prompt discovery
- Established marketplace

**Weaknesses:**
- No personal library
- No variables
- No workflow automation
- Prompts are static (no personalization)
- Focus on selling, not using

**Our advantage:**
- Personal library (save YOUR prompts)
- Variables for reuse
- Workflow automation
- Run in multiple models

**Sales angle:**
"PromptBase is for buying generic prompts. StackPilot is for organizing and automating YOUR prompts. The ones you write, the ones that work for your specific use case."

---

### 2. AIPRM (Chrome extension)

**What they are:** ChatGPT prompt library and community

**Pricing:**
- Free: Limited templates
- Plus: $9/month (more templates)
- Pro: $29/month (team features)
- Enterprise: $69/month

**Strengths:**
- 2M+ users
- Huge template library
- ChatGPT integration
- Community-driven

**Weaknesses:**
- ChatGPT only (no Claude, no Gemini)
- No personal prompt library
- No workflow automation
- Cluttered interface
- Templates, not your own prompts

**Our advantage:**
- Multi-model support
- Personal library focus
- Workflow builder
- Cleaner interface
- Your prompts, not templates

**Sales angle:**
"AIPRM gives you community templates. StackPilot lets you save and automate your own prompts. If you write custom prompts for your work, you need a library, not a template marketplace."

## Positioning by customer type

### Individual creators (writers, marketers)
**Primary competitors:** AIPRM, Notion
**Our angle:** "Variables save you 5+ minutes per prompt use. Workflows chain prompts together. Worth it if you use AI daily."

### Developers
**Primary competitors:** Typingmind, custom solutions
**Our angle:** "Code review prompts, documentation generators, debugging assistants. Save them once, run with one click. Chain into automated workflows."

### Small teams
**Primary competitors:** Dust, Notion
**Our angle:** "Shared prompt library without per-seat enterprise pricing. Everyone uses the same prompts. $49/month for 5 people."

### Content agencies
**Primary competitors:** Custom workflows, Notion
**Our angle:** "Build content pipelines. Research > Outline > Draft > Edit. Run the whole chain with one click. Consistent output across all clients."

## Objection handling

### "I already use Notion for prompts"
"How much time do you spend copy-pasting? With StackPilot, you click Run and the prompt goes straight to the AI with your variables filled in. That's 5 minutes saved per prompt, per use."

### "AIPRM is free/cheaper"
"AIPRM is ChatGPT only. If you use Claude or Gemini, you need separate solutions. StackPilot works with all of them. Plus we have workflow automation."

### "I don't have that many prompts"
"Start with 5 prompts you use weekly. Add variables. That's 5 prompts x 5 uses x 5 minutes saved = 125 minutes per week. More prompts = more savings."

### "Can't I just remember my prompts?"
"The best prompts are specific and detailed. Hard to remember a 200-word prompt perfectly. Miss one detail and you get worse output. StackPilot means perfect prompts every time."

### "What if you raise prices?"
"Current users get grandfathered in. We're not VC-funded, so we don't need to jack up prices to hit growth targets."
