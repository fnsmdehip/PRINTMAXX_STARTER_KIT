## General Upwork Proposal Best Practices

### Pre-Proposal Phase
1. **Message on job posting first:** "Hi [CLIENT_NAME], I've helped [#] similar businesses with [SPECIFIC_OUTCOME]. Happy to discuss—let me know your timeline."
2. **Ask clarifying questions:** Scope, timeline, budget range, decision makers
3. **Mention specific experience:** Name similar projects/companies
4. **Send within 24-48 hours:** Fresh proposals get better response

### Proposal Structure (Use Above Templates)

**Opening (First 2 Sentences):**
- Lead with client problem
- Mention specific outcome you've delivered

**Body (Services, Timeline, Pricing):**
- 2-3 service options (Starter/Standard/Premium)
- Clear timeline
- Specific deliverables (not vague promises)

**Closing (Call to Action):**
- "Let's talk about your timeline" (not "Are you interested?")
- Calendar link for 15-min call
- Email for detailed questions

### Pricing Psychology for Upwork

| Client Size | Typical Budget | Pricing Approach |
|-------------|----------------|------------------|
| Solo/Startup | $2K-$5K | Offer "Starter" tier at lower end |
| Small Business (5-20 people) | $5K-$15K | Standard tier, position as investment |
| Mid-market (20-100 people) | $15K-$50K+ | Premium tier, tie to ROI |
| Enterprise | $50K+ | Quote custom based on scope |

### Portfolio Pieces to Include

For each niche proposal, include 1-2 of:
1. **Screenshot of live product/system** (your work)
2. **Case study with numbers** ("Reduced costs by 40%")
3. **Before/after comparison** (visual)
4. **User testimonial** (authentic quote)
5. **Video demo** (3-5 min Loom walkthrough)

## Quick Reference: When to Customize Pricing

**Increase price if:**
- Client mentioned large budget/enterprise
- Multiple locations/departments mentioned
- Long-term/ongoing work implied
- High-stakes outcomes (revenue-dependent)

**Decrease price if:**
- Startup/early stage
- Limited scope (single feature)
- First-time engagement (good portfolio value)
- Competition is intense

**Add premium tier if:**
- Client asks "Can you do more than that?"
- Scope creep visible
- Client hints at larger budget

## Post-Proposal Follow-up

**If no response in 3 days:** "Hi [NAME], checking if you had a chance to review. Happy to clarify anything or adjust scope. Available for a quick call [CALENDAR_LINK]."

**If no response in 7 days:** "One last check—still interested in discussing this project? If timing isn't right, no worries. Feel free to reach out when you're ready."

**After 14 days:** Proposal expires. Don't re-send. If they come back, send fresh proposal with updated pricing and examples.
