# Discovery Call Script

Framework for 30-minute sales calls that qualify prospects and close deals.

---

## Pre-call preparation (5 min)

Before every call:
- [ ] Review their website/LinkedIn
- [ ] Note 2-3 specific observations
- [ ] Check how they found you
- [ ] Review any previous messages
- [ ] Have pricing sheet ready
- [ ] Prepare questions specific to their situation

---

## Call structure

| Phase | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Opener | 2 min | Build rapport, set agenda |
| Discovery | 12 min | Understand situation and pain |
| Qualify | 5 min | Budget, timeline, decision |
| Present | 5 min | Solution overview |
| Close | 6 min | Handle objections, next steps |

---

## Phase 1: Opener (2 min)

**Goal:** Set tone, establish agenda, build quick rapport.

---

"Hey [NAME], thanks for jumping on. How's your day going?"

[Brief small talk, max 30 seconds]

"Cool. So here's what I'm thinking for the next 25 minutes:

First, I want to understand what's going on in your business and what you're trying to solve. Then, if it seems like I can help, I'll share how I'd approach it. If not, I'll tell you that too.

Sound good?"

[Wait for yes]

"Perfect. Let's start with you. Tell me a bit about your business and what made you reach out."

---

## Phase 2: Discovery (12 min)

**Goal:** Understand their situation deeply. Let them talk 80% of the time.

---

### Situation questions

"Walk me through your current process for [RELEVANT TASK]. What does that look like day-to-day?"

"How much time does that take you per week?"

"What tools are you using right now?"

"Who else is involved in this process?"

---

### Pain questions

"What's the biggest frustration with the current setup?"

"What happens when things slip through the cracks?"

"How does this affect [revenue/time/stress/growth]?"

"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be?"

---

### Impact questions

"If you got those [X hours] back, what would you do with them?"

"What would it mean for your business to have this running smoothly?"

"What's the cost of not fixing this? Continuing as-is for another 6 months?"

---

### History questions

"Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"

"What's stopped you from fixing this until now?"

"Why now? What made this a priority?"

---

**Active listening cues:**
- "Tell me more about that."
- "What do you mean by [specific word they used]?"
- "How did that make you feel?"
- "Interesting. Why do you think that is?"

---

## Phase 3: Qualify (5 min)

**Goal:** Determine if they're a fit (budget, timeline, authority, need).

---

### Timeline

"If we got started, when would you want this done by?"

"Is there a specific deadline or event driving this?"

"How urgent is this? Scale of 1-10?"

---

### Decision process

"Besides you, who else would need to be involved in this decision?"

"What would you need to see to feel confident moving forward?"

---

### Budget

"Have you set aside budget for this, or are you still exploring what it might cost?"

"To give you context, projects like this typically range from $[LOW] to $[HIGH] depending on scope. Does that ballpark work for you?"

**If they push back on price:**
"I hear you. What were you expecting?"

---

### Summary check

"Let me make sure I understand:

You're currently [THEIR SITUATION], which takes [TIME] and causes [PAIN]. You want to [DESIRED OUTCOME], ideally by [TIMELINE]. And you're looking at investing around [BUDGET RANGE].

Did I miss anything?"

---

## Phase 4: Present solution (5 min)

**Goal:** Connect your solution to their specific pains.

---

"Based on what you've told me, here's how I'd approach this:

**The core problem:** [RESTATE THEIR PAIN IN THEIR WORDS]

**My approach:**
1. [STEP 1] - This addresses [THEIR SPECIFIC PAIN]
2. [STEP 2] - This gives you [SPECIFIC BENEFIT THEY MENTIONED]
3. [STEP 3] - This ensures [OUTCOME THEY WANT]

**What you'd get:**
- [DELIVERABLE 1]
- [DELIVERABLE 2]
- [DELIVERABLE 3]

**Timeline:** [X] weeks from kickoff

**Investment:** Based on what you need, this would fall into my [TIER] package at $[PRICE].

That includes [KEY DELIVERABLES]. After that, it's yours. You own everything."

---

### Provide social proof

"I did something similar for [CLIENT/INDUSTRY]. They were dealing with [SIMILAR PAIN]. After we set this up, they [SPECIFIC RESULT]."

---

## Phase 5: Close (6 min)

**Goal:** Handle objections and establish clear next steps.

---

### Trial close

"Based on everything we discussed, does this feel like it would solve the problem?"

[Listen carefully to response]

---

### Address hesitation

If they seem hesitant:

"I'm sensing some hesitation. What's on your mind?"

[Let them articulate concerns, then address specifically]

---

### Common objections

**"I need to think about it."**
"Totally fair. What specifically do you want to think through? Maybe I can help clarify."

**"It's more than I expected."**
"I hear you. What were you expecting? Let's see if we can adjust scope to fit your budget."

**"I need to talk to [partner/team]."**
"Makes sense. What would they need to see? I can put together something you can share with them."

**"Can you send me a proposal?"**
"Absolutely. I'll send it today. Before I do, just so I nail it: is there anything else you'd want me to include?"

---

### Close options

**Direct close:**
"Ready to get started? I have availability starting [DATE]."

**Two-choice close:**
"I can start next week or the week after. Which works better for you?"

**Summary close:**
"So here's where we are: You need [SOLUTION], and I can deliver it in [TIMELINE] for [PRICE]. The next step is a 50% deposit and we kick off [DATE]. Sound good?"

---

### If yes:

"Great. Here's what happens next:
1. I'll send over the proposal today for your records
2. Once you approve, I'll send the invoice for the deposit
3. As soon as payment hits, we schedule kickoff

Any questions before we wrap?"

---

### If not yet:

"No problem. What would help you get to a decision?

Here's what I'll do: I'll send over a proposal with everything we discussed. Take a look, and let's touch base on [SPECIFIC DATE] to see where you're at.

Does [DAY/TIME] work for a quick follow-up?"

---

## Post-call actions

Immediately after:
- [ ] Send proposal (same day, within 2 hours)
- [ ] Log notes in CRM
- [ ] Schedule follow-up task
- [ ] Send thank you message

---

**Proposal email:**

Subject: Proposal: [PROJECT NAME]

---

Great chatting today. As promised, here's the proposal:

[PROPOSAL LINK OR ATTACHMENT]

Quick summary:
- What: [ONE SENTENCE]
- Timeline: [X] weeks
- Investment: $[PRICE]

Let me know if you have any questions. Otherwise, just reply "approved" and I'll send the invoice to get started.

Talk soon,
[YOUR NAME]

---

## Follow-up cadence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 0 | Send proposal |
| 2 | Check-in email: "Any questions on the proposal?" |
| 5 | Value-add: "Thought of one more thing that might help..." |
| 7 | Direct ask: "Where are you on this?" |
| 14 | Last call: "Wanted to check in one more time..." |

After day 14 with no response: move to nurture sequence.
