## Model 1: AI Automation Agency

### What You Sell

Build AI-powered workflows and automations for businesses that don't have technical staff.

**Core services:**
- Zapier/Make/n8n workflow implementation
- ChatGPT/Claude API integrations
- Document processing automation
- Customer service chatbots
- Data pipeline automation
- Custom AI agents for specific tasks

### Pricing Structure

**Project-based pricing:**

| Service | Price Range | Delivery Time |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| Simple workflow (3-5 steps) | $500-1,500 | 1-3 days |
| Complex workflow (10+ steps) | $2,000-5,000 | 1-2 weeks |
| Full process automation | $5,000-15,000 | 2-4 weeks |
| Custom AI agent build | $3,000-10,000 | 1-3 weeks |

**Retainer pricing:**
- Maintenance + support: $500-1,500/mo
- Ongoing optimization: $1,500-3,000/mo
- Full automation team: $5,000-10,000/mo

**Revenue share (advanced):**
- 10-20% of documented time savings
- 5-10% of increased revenue from automation
- Hybrid: lower project fee + performance share

### Service Delivery

**Phase 1: Discovery (Day 1-3)**
```
1. Audit current manual processes
2. Document time spent per task
3. Identify automation candidates
4. Calculate ROI potential
5. Prioritize by impact/difficulty ratio
```

**Phase 2: Build (Day 4-14)**
```
1. Set up accounts (client or yours)
2. Build workflows incrementally
3. Test each trigger/action
4. Document everything for handoff
5. Create training videos (Loom)
```

**Phase 3: Handoff (Day 15-21)**
```
1. Live walkthrough with client
2. Provide written documentation
3. 2-week "bug fixing" support
4. Offer ongoing maintenance retainer
```

### Stack

**Tools you need to master:**
- **No-code:** Make.com, Zapier, n8n (self-hosted)
- **AI APIs:** OpenAI, Claude, Gemini
- **Integration:** Airtable, Notion, Google Sheets
- **Deployment:** Railway, Render, Vercel

**Learning curve:** 2-4 weeks to be competent, 2-3 months to be expert.

### Target Clients

**Best fit:**
- SMBs with 5-50 employees
- $500k-10M revenue range
- Non-technical founders/ops managers
- Manual data entry or repetitive processes
- Customer service bottlenecks

**Verticals that pay well:**
- Real estate (lead processing)
- E-commerce (order management)
- Healthcare (patient intake)
- Legal (document processing)
- Recruiting (candidate screening)

### Client Acquisition

**Cold email (primary):**
```
Subject: {{company}} - question about [manual process]

{{first_name}},

saw your job posting for [admin role/data entry/customer service].

we automate that work for companies like yours. last month we helped
[similar company] cut 20 hours/week from their [process].

cost: $2,500 one-time. ROI in 30 days.

worth a 15-min call to see if it fits?

[Your name]
```

**Expected metrics:** 45-55% open rate, 3-6% reply rate, 1-2% conversion.

**LinkedIn (secondary):**
- Connect with ops managers, founders, COOs
- Comment on posts about "hiring" or "scaling"
- DM after 3-5 touches

**Content marketing (long-term):**
- Document automations you build (anonymized)
- Post before/after comparisons
- Share specific time/cost savings

## Model 2: Cold Email Agency

### What You Sell

Done-for-you lead generation and appointment setting via cold email.

**Core services:**
- Email infrastructure setup
- List building and enrichment
- Sequence writing and optimization
- Campaign management
- Appointment booking

### Pricing Structure

**Per-lead pricing:**

| Lead Type | Price | Volume Discount |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| Raw reply | $5-15 | $3-10 at 50+/mo |
| Qualified interested | $15-35 | $10-25 at 30+/mo |
| Booked meeting | $30-75 | $25-50 at 20+/mo |
| Sales qualified | $75-200 | $50-150 at 10+/mo |

**Retainer model:**

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|----------|
| Starter | $1,500-2,500/mo | 5 inboxes, 1,000 sends/day, 10-20 leads |
| Growth | $3,000-5,000/mo | 15 inboxes, 3,000 sends/day, 30-50 leads |
| Scale | $7,500-15,000/mo | 50+ inboxes, 10,000+ sends/day, 100+ leads |

**Hybrid model (recommended):**
- Base retainer ($1,500-3,000/mo) covers infrastructure
- Per-lead fee ($20-50) for qualified responses
- Bonus ($100-200) for closed deals

### Infrastructure Costs

Your costs to deliver the service:

| Item | Monthly Cost | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Domains (10) | $10-15 | Namecheap bulk |
| Email hosting | $30-50 | Google Workspace or Zoho |
| Warmup tool | $100-200 | Instantly, Lemwarm |
| Sending tool | $100-300 | Instantly, Smartlead |
| List building | $50-200 | Apollo, Snov.io |
| Enrichment | $50-100 | Clearbit, Hunter |
| **Total** | **$340-865/mo** | |

**Margin calculation:**
- $3,000/mo retainer - $500 costs = $2,500 profit per client
- 5 clients = $12,500/mo profit
- 10 clients = $25,000/mo profit

### Service Delivery

**Week 1: Setup**
```
1. Onboard client (ICP, value prop, objections)
2. Purchase and configure domains (3-5 per client)
3. Set up email accounts
4. Start warmup (10-14 days)
5. Build initial lead list (500-1,000 contacts)
```

**Week 2-3: Launch**
```
1. Write 3 email sequences (test variations)
2. Load sequences into sending tool
3. Start campaigns at 50/day per inbox
4. Monitor deliverability daily
5. Ramp to 100/day per inbox
```

**Ongoing: Optimize**
```
1. A/B test subject lines weekly
2. Update lists monthly
3. Add new domains quarterly
4. Report results weekly
5. Adjust ICP based on responses
```

### Deliverability Fundamentals

**Non-negotiables:**
- SPF, DKIM, DMARC configured properly
- 14-21 day warmup before sending
- Max 50-100 emails per inbox per day
- Rotate domains every 3-6 months
- Never send from primary domain

**Warning signs:**
- Open rate below 40% = inbox issues
- Bounce rate above 5% = list quality issues
- Spam rate above 0.5% = content issues

### Target Clients

**Best fit:**
- B2B companies with long sales cycles
- Average deal size $5,000+
- Founders who hate outbound
- Companies with proven offer but no pipeline

**Red flags:**
- Consumer businesses
- Deal size under $1,000
- No case studies or proof
- Unrealistic expectations (100 leads in week 1)

### Client Acquisition

**Meta approach:** Use cold email to sell cold email services.

```
Subject: {{company}}'s outbound

{{first_name}},

noticed {{company}} is hiring [SDR/BDR]. that tells me outbound is a priority.

we run cold email for companies like yours. fully done-for-you.

what we do:
- build and manage infrastructure
- write and test sequences
- deliver qualified leads

last month we generated 47 meetings for [similar company] in
[industry]. their pipeline grew 340%.

worth a 15-min call to see if this fits?

[Your name]
```

**Referrals (highest quality):**
- Ask happy clients for intros
- Offer 10-20% referral fee
- Join B2B communities and provide value first

## Model 6: Clipper Network

### What You Sell

Operate a network of content clippers creating short-form videos from long-form content.

**Two business models:**

1. **Service model:** Streamers pay you to clip and distribute
2. **Network model:** You own channels, monetize views, recruit clippers

### Pricing Structure

**Service model pricing:**

| Service | Price | Volume |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Basic clipping | $10-25/clip | 10-50 clips/mo |
| Full distribution | $15-35/clip | Includes posting |
| Exclusive network | $500-2,000/mo retainer | Unlimited clips + distribution |

**Network model economics:**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Clipper pay | $5-15/clip |
| Views per clip (average) | 50k-200k |
| RPM (TikTok Creativity) | $0.50-1.00 |
| Revenue per clip | $25-200 |
| Margin after clipper pay | 50-70% |

### Building the Network

**Phase 1: Prove concept (Month 1-2)**
```
1. Clip 50-100 videos yourself
2. Post to your own channels
3. Track performance by clip type
4. Build SOPs for clipping
5. Identify winning formats
```

**Phase 2: Recruit clippers (Month 2-4)**
```
1. Post on r/TwitchClippers, r/YouTubeClippers
2. DM clippers making good content
3. Offer $5-10/clip for trial period
4. Train on your style guide
5. Scale to 5-10 clippers
```

**Phase 3: Scale (Month 4+)**
```
1. Add more channels (different niches)
2. Recruit more clippers (20-50)
3. Build quality control process
4. Negotiate streamer partnerships
5. Explore brand sponsorships
```

### Clipper Recruitment

## Scaling Strategy

### Growth Stages

**Stage 1: Foundation ($0-10k MRR)**
```
Focus: Get first 3-5 clients
Pricing: Lower to get wins
Delivery: Do everything yourself
Sales: All outbound
```

**Stage 2: Validation ($10k-30k MRR)**
```
Focus: Prove repeatable delivery
Pricing: Raise to market rate
Delivery: Hire first specialist
Sales: Mix outbound + referrals
```

**Stage 3: Scale ($30k-100k MRR)**
```
Focus: Build systems
Pricing: Value-based, tiered
Delivery: Team handles most work
Sales: Hire SDR, add content marketing
```

**Stage 4: Optimize ($100k+ MRR)**
```
Focus: Increase profit margin
Pricing: Premium positioning
Delivery: Productize where possible
Sales: Inbound-heavy, partnerships
```

### Productization Path

**Move from custom to productized:**

1. **Identify repeatable work** - What do you do for every client?
2. **Create packages** - Standard deliverables, fixed price
3. **Build templates** - Reduce custom work
4. **Automate onboarding** - Self-serve where possible
5. **Consider SaaS** - Turn service into software

**Example productization:**
- Custom automation ($5k project) to
- Packaged automation ($2k fixed scope) to
- Automation templates ($500 DIY) to
- Automation SaaS ($50/mo self-serve)

## Financial Benchmarks

### Agency Economics

**Healthy metrics:**
- Gross margin: 60-80%
- Net margin: 20-40%
- Client retention: 70-85% annually
- Average contract: 6-12 months
- Revenue per employee: $100k-200k

**Warning signs:**
- Margin below 50%
- Churn above 40%
- Single client >40% of revenue
- Constant scope creep
- Delivery always late

### Cash Flow Management

**Invoice timing:**
- Retainers: 100% upfront monthly
- Projects: 50% upfront, 50% on delivery
- Large projects: 33% upfront, 33% midway, 33% final

**Expense management:**
- Keep fixed costs low until $50k MRR
- Variable costs should scale with revenue
- Build 3-month runway before hiring
- Don't buy annual tools until proven need

## Risk Factors and Mitigation

### Client Concentration

**Risk:** Single client is 30%+ of revenue
**Mitigation:** Always be prospecting. Cap any client at 25%.

### Scope Creep

**Risk:** Projects expand without price increase
**Mitigation:** Document scope clearly. Change orders for additions.

### Key Person Dependency

**Risk:** You or one team member handles all work
**Mitigation:** Document everything. Cross-train team.

### Platform Changes

**Risk:** Tool or platform you rely on changes terms
**Mitigation:** Diversify tool stack. Build relationships, not just integrations.

### Delivery Quality

**Risk:** Quality drops as you scale
**Mitigation:** Build QC process early. Don't scale before systems ready.
