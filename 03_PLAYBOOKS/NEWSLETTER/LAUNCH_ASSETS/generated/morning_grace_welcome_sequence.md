## Email 2 - Day 1

**Subject:** the prayer habit that changed my year (2-min read)

**Body:**

Quick question: when's the last time you looked back at an answered prayer and actually remembered asking for it?

Most people can't remember. Not because God didn't answer. But because we forget what we asked for.

Here's what I started doing: writing every prayer request in a journal. Date. Request. Status.

90 days in, I went back and counted. 31 answered prayers. Things I'd completely forgotten I'd asked for.

Your brain forgets. Paper doesn't.

**Today's verse: Philippians 4:6**
"Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God."

Notice it says "present your requests." Plural. Specific. Written down so you can present them again and again.

**Prayer prompt:** Write down 3 things you're praying for right now. Put a date next to each one. Come back to this list in 30 days.

## Email 3 - Day 3

**Subject:** what happens when prayer feels empty (honest answer)

**Body:**

Some mornings you'll sit down to pray and feel absolutely nothing.

No warmth. No peace. No "God moment." Just silence.

This is normal. And it's not a sign that something is wrong.

Faith isn't a feeling. It's a decision. The most faithful people I know have more "dry" prayer times than mountaintop experiences. They show up anyway.

Here's what helped me: instead of trying to manufacture a feeling, I started praying with honesty.

"God, I don't feel You right now. I'm showing up because I said I would. Help me want to be here."

That prayer, prayed honestly, did more for my faith than 100 polished devotionals.

**Today's verse: Psalm 46:10**
"Be still, and know that I am God."

Being still doesn't mean feeling something. It means choosing to stop and trust even when it's quiet.

**Prayer prompt:** If prayer feels empty today, say this: "I'm here. I don't have the right words. But I'm here." That counts. God knows.

Grace,
Morning Grace

## Email 4 - Day 5

**Subject:** the accountability trick I stole from a gym buddy

**Body:**

My friend lost 30 pounds because his gym partner texted him every morning at 5am: "you up?"

No judgment. No lecture. Just a check-in.

I applied the same principle to prayer.

Found one friend. We text "prayed" when we're done each morning. If one of us misses 2 days, the other calls.

That's it. No weekly Bible study. No group meeting. Just one person who keeps you honest.

We're 200+ days in. I've never been this consistent in my life.

**Today's verse: James 5:16**
"Therefore confess your sins to each other and pray for each other so that you may be healed."

Notice it says "each other." Faith was never designed to be solo.

**Prayer prompt:** Think of one person you trust. Text them right now and ask: "Would you be open to daily prayer accountability? We just text 'prayed' when we're done."

If they say yes, your consistency just doubled.
