**the opportunity nobody's talking about: niche unlocks**

every screen blocker on the market is generic. "set a timer, block apps, done." they compete on features.

but the real play is niche unlock mechanics. instead of "tap a button to unlock your phone," make the user DO something meaningful:

- pray for 5 minutes to unlock (faith audience)
- walk 2,000 steps to unlock (fitness audience)
- complete a study session to unlock (student audience)

same core mechanic. 3 different audiences. 3 different App Store listings. 3 different content strategies. 3 different SEO keywords. 3 different affiliate angles.

that's 3 apps from 1 codebase, each targeting a specific emotional trigger instead of competing in the generic "screen time blocker" category.

**the pricing data**

screen blocker apps with hard paywalls generate 8x more revenue than those with freemium models (Piano/Adapty data). the psychology is simple: if someone downloads a phone-blocking app, they already decided they have a problem. they're at peak motivation. hit them with the paywall NOW, not after a 7-day trial they'll forget about.

the numbers per app:

|--------|-------------|----------|------------|
| installs/month | 500 | 2,000 | 10,000 |
| conversion rate | 3% | 4.5% | 6% |
| annual price | $49.99 | $49.99 | $49.99 |
| monthly revenue | $750 | $4,500 | $30,000 |
| x3 apps | $2,250 | $13,500 | $90,000 |

the top end is unrealistic without paid ads. the conservative end is achievable with organic content + ASO.

**what I'm building**

3 Lock Apps:
1. **PrayerLock** - lock phone until you pray (faith niche, zero direct competitors with pray-to-unlock)
2. **WalkToUnlock** - lock phone until you walk 2K steps (fitness niche)
3. **StudyLock** - lock phone until you study (student niche)

shared codebase. hard paywall from day 1. RevenueCat for subscription management. testing subscription vs lifetime purchase (43% of consumers now prefer hybrid pricing per SYN034).

**the subscription fatigue angle**

43% of consumers say they prefer hybrid monetization over pure subscription. screen blockers that offer a lifetime purchase option at 3x annual price capture the "I don't want another subscription" segment without cannibalizing annual subscribers.

we're testing 3 pricing variants:
- subscription only (control)
- subscription + lifetime (expected winner)
- lifetime-first with higher subscription prices

if lifetime captures 15-25% of buyers at $149.99 while annual holds at $49.99, the revenue per install increases 8-15%. RevenueCat experiments handle the split testing.
