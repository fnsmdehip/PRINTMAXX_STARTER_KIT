**Body:**

Here's my actual daily content workflow. Takes 15 minutes per day for 4 platforms.

**Step 1 (3 min):** Open Claude. Give it a topic and ask for 5 post variants. Pick the best one. Edit for 2 minutes.

**Step 2 (2 min):** Paste into Typefully. Schedule for X/Twitter at optimal time (Typefully tells you when).

**Step 3 (3 min):** Copy the post. Reformat for LinkedIn (single line breaks, question at end). Paste into Buffer. Schedule.

**Step 4 (5 min):** Take the core insight. Turn it into a 3-slide carousel summary. Schedule on Instagram via Buffer.

**Step 5 (2 min):** If the post is good enough for newsletter, copy to Beehiiv draft folder for next issue.

15 minutes. 4 platforms. 1 piece of original content repurposed.

**Subject:** MCP servers are the biggest AI unlock nobody talks about

**Body:**

If you haven't heard of MCP (Model Context Protocol), pay attention. This changes how AI works.

**What it is:** An open standard that lets AI models like Claude connect to external tools. Databases. APIs. Browsers. File systems. Spreadsheets.

**Why it matters:** Instead of copy-pasting data into ChatGPT, Claude can directly read and write to your tools. Automatically.

**Real examples I've built:**

1. Competitive price monitor: Claude checks 200+ competitor pages daily. Logs changes to a spreadsheet. Alerts me on Slack.

2. Research agent: Claude searches the web, finds relevant articles, summarizes them, and appends findings to my research database. Runs on a schedule.

3. Content pipeline: Claude reads my content calendar, generates drafts, and saves them to the right folder. I just review and publish.

**How to start:** Search "MCP servers" on GitHub. Hundreds of pre-built connectors. Google Sheets, Slack, file systems, browsers. Most install in under 5 minutes.

This is the infrastructure layer that makes AI agents practical for business. Not theoretical. Practical.
