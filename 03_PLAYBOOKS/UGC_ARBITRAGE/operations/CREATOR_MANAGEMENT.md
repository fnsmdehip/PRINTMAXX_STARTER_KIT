# Creator management

How to manage multiple creators efficiently at scale.

---

## Creator roster structure

### Ideal roster composition

**Primary creators (3-5)**
- Your best, most reliable
- Consistent quality
- Fast turnaround
- Priority for best projects
- Worth paying slightly more

**Secondary creators (5-10)**
- Good quality, reliable
- Different looks/demographics
- Fill capacity gaps
- Backup when primaries busy

**Development pool (3-5)**
- Testing new creators
- Lower rates while proving
- Potential to move up
- Accept some risk

**Specialty creators (as needed)**
- Specific demographics
- Niche expertise
- Higher rates, specific use
- Project-by-project basis

## Creator tiers and treatment

### Tier A: Primary creators

**Characteristics:**
- Quality score 5
- Reliability score 5
- 10+ successful projects
- Minimal revisions needed

**Treatment:**
- First choice for premium projects
- Better rates (10-15% premium okay)
- Priority communication
- Relationship building
- Referral incentives

---

### Tier B: Reliable creators

**Characteristics:**
- Quality score 4+
- Reliability score 4+
- 3-9 successful projects
- Occasional revisions

**Treatment:**
- Regular project flow
- Standard rates
- Good communication
- Development opportunities
- Path to Tier A

---

### Tier C: Development creators

**Characteristics:**
- Quality score 3-4
- Reliability unproven
- <3 projects
- Testing phase

**Treatment:**
- Lower-stakes projects first
- More detailed briefs
- Closer monitoring
- Clear feedback for improvement
- Evaluate after 3 projects

---

### Tier D: Probation/exit

**Characteristics:**
- Missed deadlines
- Quality issues
- Poor communication
- Not improving

**Treatment:**
- No new projects
- Complete current work
- Exit from roster
- Document issues

## Onboarding new creators

### Onboarding checklist

1. **Initial vetting**
   - [ ] Review portfolio
   - [ ] Assess quality level
   - [ ] Verify availability
   - [ ] Discuss rates
   - [ ] Check communication style

2. **Test project**
   - [ ] Small, low-risk project first
   - [ ] Clear brief
   - [ ] Standard timeline
   - [ ] Evaluate results

3. **Documentation**
   - [ ] Get payment details
   - [ ] Add to database
   - [ ] Sign agreement (if applicable)
   - [ ] Note preferences

4. **First project debrief**
   - [ ] Review quality
   - [ ] Assess communication
   - [ ] Note any issues
   - [ ] Decide tier placement

## Day-to-day management

### Communication best practices

**Clarity:** Be specific in briefs
**Timeliness:** Respond within 24 hours
**Professionalism:** Keep it business-appropriate
**Appreciation:** Acknowledge good work

### Communication channels

| Channel | Use for |
|---------|---------|
| Email | Formal briefs, contracts, invoices |
| WhatsApp/Telegram | Quick questions, updates |
| Instagram DM | Initial contact, casual check-ins |
| Slack | Volume creators, ongoing relationships |

### Check-in schedule

**Per project:**
- Brief acknowledgment (Day 1)
- Midpoint check (Day 2-3)
- Delivery reminder (Day before due)

**Ongoing relationships:**
- Weekly check-in if active
- Monthly touch base if dormant
- Quarterly roster review

## Handling common issues

### Issue: Missed deadline

**First occurrence:**
```
"Hey [Name], just checking in - the content was due today. Is everything okay? When can I expect delivery?"
```

**Pattern emerging:**
```
"Hi [Name], I noticed the last few projects have had timeline issues. To continue working together, I need to be able to rely on deadlines. Can you commit to on-time delivery going forward?"
```

**Action:** Document, adjust expectations, consider tier downgrade.

---

### Issue: Quality below expectations

**First occurrence:**
```
"Thanks for the delivery! A few things need adjustment:
1. [Specific issue]
2. [Specific issue]

Please reference [example] for what we're looking for."
```

**Ongoing:**
```
"Hi [Name], I want to help you succeed, but I'm consistently seeing quality issues. Let's discuss what would help you deliver at the level we need."
```

**Action:** Provide clearer briefs, examples, consider if fit exists.

---

### Issue: Communication problems

**Slow response:**
```
"Hey [Name], I need quicker responses during active projects. Can you commit to responding within 24 hours?"
```

**Defensive about feedback:**
```
"I understand feedback can be tough, but it helps us deliver what the client needs. I appreciate your professionalism in implementing revisions."
```

**Action:** Set clear expectations, evaluate if relationship works.

---

### Issue: Rate negotiation

**Creator asks for raise:**
```
"Thanks for bringing this up. Based on our history:
- You've done [X] projects
- Quality has been [assessment]
- Current rate is [X]

I can offer [modest increase] given [justification]. Does that work?"
```

**When to increase:**
- Consistently excellent work
- Growing with you long-term
- Market rates have shifted
- Volume commitment from you

## Capacity planning

### Know your capacity

Track per creator:
- Projects/month they can handle
- Current workload
- Upcoming availability
- Lead time needed

### Capacity spreadsheet

```
| Creator | Max/month | Current | Available | Notes |
|---------|-----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| Jane D. | 10 videos | 6 | 4 | Free after 1/20 |
| John S. | 8 videos | 8 | 0 | Fully booked |
| Maria K. | 12 videos | 3 | 9 | Looking for more |
```

### Scaling decisions

When at capacity:
1. Recruit new creators
2. Increase capacity of existing
3. Raise prices (demand > supply)
4. Turn down lower-value work

## Performance reviews

### Quarterly review process

1. **Pull data**
   - Projects completed
   - On-time rate
   - Revision rate
   - Quality scores

2. **Assess performance**
   - Meeting expectations?
   - Improving or declining?
   - Communication quality
   - Value delivered

3. **Take action**
   - Tier upgrades/downgrades
   - Rate adjustments
   - Feedback conversations
   - Roster additions/removals

## Building long-term relationships

### Relationship investments

- Pay on time, every time
- Provide helpful feedback
- Share when content performs well
- Offer volume when available
- Remember personal details
- Be professional and kind

### Loyalty incentives

- Rate increases for longevity
- First choice on good projects
- Referral bonuses ($25-50 per successful referral)
- Holiday bonuses for top performers
- Public credit/testimonials (if they want)

### When to invest more

- Reliable over many projects
- Quality consistently high
- Good cultural fit
- Growing with you
- Hard to replace
