# Script template: Storytime

Narrative-driven content. Highly engaging, builds connection.

---

## Structure

```
[HOOK - 3-5 seconds]
Tease the story outcome

[SETUP - 10-15 seconds]
Set the scene, introduce characters

[CONFLICT - 15-20 seconds]
The problem or challenge

[TURNING POINT - 10-15 seconds]
Discovery of solution (product)

[RESOLUTION - 10-15 seconds]
How it worked out

[CTA - 3-5 seconds]
Takeaway and link
```

---

## Example script: Wellness product (60 seconds)

```
HOOK:
"Let me tell you about the worst night of my life... and what changed everything."

SETUP:
"So last year, I was working 60-hour weeks. Stressed out of my mind. I thought I was handling it fine."

CONFLICT:
"Then one night I'm lying in bed and my heart is racing. Can't breathe. I thought I was having a heart attack. Went to the ER. Turns out - panic attack. Doctor basically said 'you need to manage your stress or this is your life now.'"

TURNING POINT:
"A friend gave me [Product]. I was skeptical but desperate. Started using it before bed, then again when I felt overwhelmed during the day."

RESOLUTION:
"That was 8 months ago. Haven't had another panic attack. My baseline stress is just... lower. I can actually think clearly now."

CTA:
"It's not magic. But it helped me. Link if you want to try it."
```

## Example script: Productivity tool (45 seconds)

```
HOOK:
"I almost got fired last year. Seriously."

SETUP:
"I was that person who said yes to everything. Always taking on more projects, promising tight deadlines."

CONFLICT:
"Then I missed a major deadline. A big one. Client was furious. My boss pulled me aside and basically said 'figure it out or you're done.'"

TURNING POINT:
"That weekend I overhauled my entire system. Found [Product] and spent Sunday setting it up."

RESOLUTION:
"Since then? Zero missed deadlines. I actually know what's on my plate. I can say no because I can see my capacity clearly."

CTA:
"Saved my job. Maybe it'll help you too. Link below."
```

## Story elements

### The hook
- Tease the outcome
- Create curiosity
- Emotional pull
- "Wait for it" energy

### The setup
- Relatable situation
- Enough context
- Establish stakes
- Build empathy

### The conflict
- Clear problem
- Emotional weight
- Consequences shown
- Tension building

### The turning point
- Discovery moment
- Product introduction
- Hope appears
- Change begins

### The resolution
- Problem solved
- Transformation shown
- Current state
- Lessons learned

---

## Story formulas

### The struggle story
1. Life was hard because [problem]
2. I tried [failed solutions]
3. Found [product]
4. Now [better state]

### The failure story
1. I made a mistake / hit rock bottom
2. Everything seemed bad
3. This one thing helped
4. Turned it around

### The discovery story
1. I didn't know about [issue]
2. Something bad happened
3. Learned about [solution]
4. Changed my approach

### The transformation story
1. I was [before state]
2. I wanted to be [after state]
3. [Product] helped me get there
4. Now I'm [transformed]

---

## Emotional beats

### Opening (curiosity)
- "Let me tell you about..."
- "This happened to me..."
- "I never share this but..."
- "The story of how..."

### Rising action (tension)
- "And then it got worse..."
- "I didn't know what to do..."
- "Everything was falling apart..."
- "I was desperate..."

### Turning point (hope)
- "Then I discovered..."
- "That's when everything changed..."
- "Someone told me about..."
- "I finally found..."

### Resolution (satisfaction)
- "Now I..."
- "That was [time] ago and..."
- "Looking back..."
- "I wish I had found this sooner..."

---

## Story authenticity markers

### Specific details
- Dates and timeframes
- Names (first names)
- Places
- Numbers

### Vulnerability
- Admitting failure
- Showing emotion
- Honest struggle
- Imperfect resolution

### Sensory details
- What you saw/heard/felt
- Physical sensations
- Emotional reactions
- Environmental details

### Imperfection
- Things that still aren't perfect
- Ongoing challenges
- Realistic expectations

---

## Visual storytelling tips

### Match visuals to story
- Setup: Normal life footage
- Conflict: Show the struggle
- Turning point: Product introduction
- Resolution: Happy/improved state

### Pacing
- Slow down at emotional moments
- Speed up during less critical parts
- Pause for impact
- Let reactions breathe

---

## Creator brief notes

For storytime content:
- Can provide story outline or let creator use their own
- Authentic stories work best
- Allow personal details
- Product should fit naturally into narrative
- Longer format (45-60s) acceptable
- Emotional delivery encouraged
