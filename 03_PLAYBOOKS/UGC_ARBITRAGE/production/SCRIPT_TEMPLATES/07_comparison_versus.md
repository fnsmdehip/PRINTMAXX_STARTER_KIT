# Script template: Comparison/versus

Product comparison content. Shows why your product is better.

---

## Structure

```
[HOOK - 2-3 seconds]
Set up the comparison

[COMPETITOR PROBLEMS - 8-12 seconds]
What's wrong with alternatives

[PRODUCT INTRODUCTION - 5-8 seconds]
Enter the better option

[KEY DIFFERENCES - 15-20 seconds]
Side-by-side comparison points

[VERDICT - 5-8 seconds]
Clear winner declaration

[CTA - 3-5 seconds]
Where to get it
```

---

## Example script: Productivity app (45 seconds)

```
HOOK:
"I tried both [Competitor] and [Product]. Here's the honest difference."

COMPETITOR PROBLEMS:
"[Competitor] looks great but it's way too complicated. I spent more time organizing my system than actually getting work done. And it's $15/month."

PRODUCT INTRODUCTION:
"Then someone recommended [Product]."

KEY DIFFERENCES:
"Setup took 10 minutes vs the whole afternoon with [Competitor].
The interface is simple - no 50 different views to learn.
It does one thing really well: keeps my tasks organized.
And it's half the price."

VERDICT:
"For 90% of people, [Product] is the better choice. You don't need all those extra features."

CTA:
"Link to try it free below."
```

## Comparison frameworks

### Side-by-side
Direct feature comparison
"[Product] has X. [Competitor] doesn't."

### My journey
Personal experience switching
"I used [Competitor] for 6 months, then tried [Product]."

### Problem-focused
What competitors get wrong
"Most [product type] products have this problem..."

### Results-focused
Outcome comparison
"With [Competitor], I got X results. With [Product], I got Y."

---

## Tone guidelines

### Do
- Be specific with comparisons
- Use personal experience
- Acknowledge competitor strengths
- Focus on fit, not absolute better

### Don't
- Bash competitors aggressively
- Use competitor trademarked names in ads
- Make false claims about competitors
- Be mean-spirited

### Softer language options
- "More intuitive" vs "easier"
- "Better fit for me" vs "better"
- "I prefer" vs "objectively superior"
- "Works better for my needs" vs "competitors suck"

---

## Legal considerations

### Trademark issues
- Don't use competitor logos
- Be careful with trademarked names in paid ads
- "Leading brand" or "[category] products" as alternatives

### Claim substantiation
- Comparison claims must be true
- Price comparisons must be verifiable
- Feature comparisons must be accurate

### Platform rules
- Some platforms restrict competitor mentions in ads
- Check platform ad policies
- Organic content has more flexibility
