# Script template: Objection handling

Address common concerns proactively. Removes purchase barriers.

---

## Structure

```
[HOOK - 2-3 seconds]
State the common objection

[ACKNOWLEDGE - 5-8 seconds]
Validate the concern

[COUNTER - 15-20 seconds]
Address with evidence/experience

[REFRAME - 8-10 seconds]
New perspective on the concern

[CTA - 3-5 seconds]
Encourage action despite objection
```

---

## Example script: Price objection (30 seconds)

```
HOOK:
"'[Product] is too expensive.' I used to think that too."

ACKNOWLEDGE:
"I get it. $[Price] feels like a lot upfront."

COUNTER:
"But here's the math I did. I was spending $[Amount] per month on [alternatives that don't work]. Over a year, that's $[Total]. [Product] is $[Price] once, and it actually works."

REFRAME:
"The expensive option is the one that doesn't solve the problem. [Product] has paid for itself [X] times over."

CTA:
"Stop paying for things that don't work. Link below."
```

---

## Example script: Skepticism objection (30 seconds)

```
HOOK:
"'Does [Product] actually work or is it just marketing?' Fair question."

ACKNOWLEDGE:
"There's so much garbage out there. I've wasted money on products that promised everything and delivered nothing."

COUNTER:
"What convinced me about [Product]: [specific evidence]. [Number] reviews, [specific result], and I've personally used it for [time] with [result]."

REFRAME:
"I'm not saying trust blindly. But the proof is there if you look."

CTA:
"They have a [guarantee/trial]. Try it risk-free. Link in bio."
```

## Common objections by category

### Price objections
- "It's too expensive"
- "I can't afford it"
- "There are cheaper options"
- "Is it worth the money?"

### Effectiveness objections
- "Does it actually work?"
- "I've tried similar things"
- "Sounds too good to be true"
- "How is this different?"

### Timing objections
- "I don't have time"
- "I'll do it later"
- "Not the right time"
- "I'm too busy"

### Trust objections
- "Is this legit?"
- "Seems like a scam"
- "Why haven't I heard of this?"
- "How do I know it's real?"

### Need objections
- "I don't really need this"
- "I'm fine without it"
- "My current solution works"
- "Is this necessary?"

### Complexity objections
- "Looks complicated"
- "I'm not tech-savvy"
- "Will I actually use it?"
- "Seems like too much effort"

## Objection response frameworks

### Feel-Felt-Found
"I felt the same way. Many people felt that too. What I found was..."

### Acknowledge-Bridge-Counter
"That's a valid concern. Here's what I discovered..."

### Agree-Reframe
"You're right that [partial truth]. The way to think about it is..."

### Evidence-based
"I thought that too until I saw [proof]."

---

## Counter-argument types

### Math/logic
- Cost per use breakdown
- ROI calculation
- Comparison math
- Time savings calculation

### Social proof
- Number of customers
- Reviews and ratings
- Expert endorsements
- Before/after results

### Personal experience
- Your own results
- Timeline of experience
- Specific outcomes
- Honest assessment

### Risk reversal
- Money-back guarantee
- Free trial
- No commitment option
- Easy cancellation

### Scarcity/urgency
- Limited time pricing
- Stock limitations
- Price increase coming
- Seasonal relevance

## Objection-specific counters

### "Too expensive"
- Cost per use/day breakdown
- Compare to alternatives
- Long-term value
- What you save/gain

### "Doesn't work"
- Specific mechanism
- Clinical/research backing
- Personal results
- Social proof

### "No time"
- How little time it takes
- Time it saves
- Integration ease
- Quick start option

### "Don't need it"
- Hidden cost of status quo
- Future benefit
- Prevention angle
- Quality of life improvement

### "Seems sketchy"
- Company background
- Transparency points
- Guarantee/protection
- Real user evidence

## Tone guidance

### Empathetic, not defensive
- "I totally get why you'd think that"
- "That's actually a smart question"
- "I had the same concern"
- NOT "You're wrong because..."

### Confident, not pushy
- "Here's what I found"
- "The data shows"
- "In my experience"
- NOT "You NEED to buy this"

### Helpful, not salesy
- "Let me share what helped me decide"
- "Here's how I thought about it"
- NOT "This is the best thing ever"

## Creator brief notes

For objection-handling content:
- Specify exact objection to address
- Provide counter-arguments/evidence
- Note any guarantees/trials to mention
- Include personal experience talking points
- Clarify any claims that need substantiation
