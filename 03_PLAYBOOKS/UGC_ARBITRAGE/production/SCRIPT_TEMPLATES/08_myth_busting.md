# Script template: Myth busting

Challenge common misconceptions. Positions product as the truth.

---

## Structure

```
[HOOK - 2-3 seconds]
State the myth provocatively

[MYTH EXPLANATION - 8-10 seconds]
What people commonly believe

[WHY IT'S WRONG - 10-12 seconds]
The truth/reality

[PRODUCT TRUTH - 10-15 seconds]
How product aligns with reality

[CTA - 3-5 seconds]
Where to learn more/try it
```

---

## Example script: Skincare (30 seconds)

```
HOOK:
"Stop believing this skincare myth."

MYTH EXPLANATION:
"Everyone says you need a 10-step routine for good skin. More products, more results, right?"

WHY IT'S WRONG:
"Actually, over-complicating your routine can damage your skin barrier. Most dermatologists recommend 3-4 products max."

PRODUCT TRUTH:
"That's why I use [Product]. It combines [ingredient 1] and [ingredient 2] in one step. No layering 7 different serums."

CTA:
"Simple works better. Link below."
```

## Common myths by category

- Results should be instant

### Productivity

- Multitasking works
- Wake up early to succeed
- More hours = more output
- Willpower is unlimited

## Myth-busting formula

### The pattern
1. State widely believed myth
2. Explain why people believe it
3. Present the counter-evidence
4. Show how product aligns with truth
5. Position viewer as now enlightened

### Authority sources
- "Research shows..."
- "According to [expert type]..."
- "Studies have found..."
- "The data actually says..."

Note: If citing research, it should be real. Vague claims need substantiation.

---

## Hook formulas

### Provocative statements
- "Stop believing this [category] myth."
- "This popular advice is actually wrong."
- "Everyone gets this wrong about [topic]."
- "The [industry] doesn't want you to know this."

### Question hooks
- "Think you need [myth]? Think again."
- "Still doing [common practice]? Here's why you should stop."
- "Why does everyone still believe [myth]?"

### Contrarian hooks
- "Hot take: [contrarian view]"
- "Unpopular opinion about [topic]"
- "I'm going to say what others won't"

---

## Transition phrases

### From myth to truth
- "But here's the thing..."
- "The reality is..."
- "What actually works is..."
- "The science shows..."
- "Here's what they don't tell you..."

### From truth to product
- "That's why I switched to..."
- "That's exactly what [Product] does..."
- "[Product] gets this right..."
- "Finally, something that [aligns with truth]..."

"[X]% of people who try this see no results..."

## Creator brief notes

For myth-busting content:
- Specify exact myth to debunk
- Provide talking points for truth
- Note any claims that need substantiation
- Include sources if referencing research
- Clarify tone (educational vs. provocative)
