# Script template: POV/scenario

Role-play format. High relatability and entertainment value.

---

## Structure

```
[POV SETUP - 2-3 seconds]
Set the scenario

[SCENE - 20-30 seconds]
Play out the situation

[PRODUCT MOMENT - 10-15 seconds]
Product saves the day or enhances

[PAYOFF - 5-8 seconds]
Resolution and CTA
```

---

## Example script: Skincare (30 seconds)

```
POV SETUP:
[Text on screen: "POV: You finally found a sunscreen that doesn't make you look like a ghost"]

SCENE:
[Shows applying sunscreen]
"Okay, let's see..."
[Rubs in]
"Wait... it's actually... blending?"
[Checks mirror]
"No white cast??"
[Multiple angles]
"I can actually wear this to work??"

PRODUCT MOMENT:
[Shows product]
"[Product Name]. I'm literally never going back."

PAYOFF:
"Link in bio. You need this."
```

## Example script: Productivity (30 seconds)

```
POV SETUP:
[Text: "POV: Your to-do list when you actually use a system that works"]

"Monday morning. Here's everything I need to do."

"Check. Check. Check."
[End of day]
"Done by 4 PM with time to spare."

PRODUCT MOMENT:
"This is [Product]. Setup took 20 minutes."

PAYOFF:
"Link below. Your future self will thank you."
```

## Example script: Food/supplement (30 seconds)


## POV formats

### The discovery POV
"POV: You find [solution] for [problem]"
Focus: First time experiencing the benefit

### The comparison POV
"POV: You finally switch from [old way] to [new way]"
Focus: Contrast between old and new

### The relatable struggle POV
"POV: [Common frustrating situation]"
Focus: Validation + solution

### The fantasy POV
"POV: [Aspirational situation made possible]"
Focus: Desired outcome achieved

### The reaction POV
"POV: You try [product] for the first time"
Focus: Genuine reactions and surprise

## Scene-building elements

### Visual cues
- Outfit/setting matches scenario
- Props that support the story
- Before/after comparison
- Reactions visible

### Audio cues
- Internal monologue
- Surprised sounds
- Satisfaction sounds
- Music matching mood

### Text overlays
- POV statement at start
- Thoughts as text
- Key moments highlighted
- Product name reveal

---

## Reaction types to capture

### Surprise
Wide eyes, raised eyebrows
"Wait..."
"What?"
"No way..."

### Relief
Relaxed shoulders, exhale
"Finally..."
"This is it..."
"Why didn't I find this sooner?"

### Satisfaction
Smile, nod
"Yes..."
"Perfect..."
"This is exactly what I needed"

### Disbelief
Head shake, double-take
"I can't believe..."
"Is this real?"
"How have I lived without this?"

## Timing breakdown

| Section | Duration | Focus |
|---------|----------|-------|
| POV setup | 2-3s | Text/context |
| Scene | 20-25s | Acting/scenario |
| Product moment | 10-12s | Reveal |
| Payoff | 5-8s | Resolution + CTA |
| **Total** | ~40s | |

---

## Acting tips for creators

### Stay in character
- React naturally to scenario
- Don't break character
- Let emotions show

### Exaggeration (appropriate)
- Slightly bigger reactions
- Clear facial expressions
- Voice matches emotion

### Timing
- Don't rush the reveal
- Let surprise moments land
- Pace for watchability

---

## Text overlay best practices

### POV setup text
- Clear and concise
- Sets scene immediately
- Visible for full duration

### Thought text (optional)
- Short phrases
- Matches internal monologue
- Appears at right moment

### Product reveal text
- Product name clear
- Key benefit if space
- Link reminder

---

## Creator brief notes

For POV content:
- Specify exact POV text
- Describe scenario clearly
- Note key reaction moments
- Allow creator personality
- Product integration should feel natural
- Trending audio optional but effective
