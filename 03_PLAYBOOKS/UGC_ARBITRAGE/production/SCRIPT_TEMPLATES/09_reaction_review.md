# Script template: Reaction/review

Authentic first impressions and honest reviews. High trust content.

---

## Structure

```
[INTRO - 3-5 seconds]
What they're reviewing and why

[FIRST IMPRESSIONS - 10-15 seconds]
Initial reactions, expectations

[TESTING/USE - 15-20 seconds]
Actually using the product

[HONEST ASSESSMENT - 10-15 seconds]
What they liked, any critiques

[VERDICT - 5-8 seconds]
Overall recommendation

[CTA - 3-5 seconds]
Where to get it
```

---

## Example script: Tech gadget (60 seconds)

```
INTRO:
"I've been seeing [Product] everywhere so I finally ordered one to see if it lives up to the hype."

FIRST IMPRESSIONS:
"Out of the box, first thing I notice is [observation]. It feels [quality assessment]. I was expecting [expectation] so this is [reaction]."

TESTING/USE:
[Uses product]
"Let me try [primary function]..."
"Okay so [observation about performance]."
"Let me also test [secondary function]..."
"That works [assessment]."

HONEST ASSESSMENT:
"Things I love: [positive 1], [positive 2], and [positive 3].
One thing that could be better: [honest critique].
But for the price point, [value assessment]."

VERDICT:
"Overall? I'm actually impressed. I'd recommend this for [who it's for]."

CTA:
"Link below if you want to try it."
```

- Results after
- Would repurchase?

### Comparison review
- vs. previous product
- vs. expectations
- vs. price point
- vs. competitors

---

## Authenticity markers

### What makes reviews believable

**Specific observations**
- "The click feels solid but not too loud"
- "Absorbs in about 30 seconds"
- "Lasts about 6 hours on my skin type"

**Honest critiques**
- "The app could be better"
- "I wish it came in more colors"
- "Packaging is kind of wasteful"

**Personal context**
- "For reference, I have [skin type/use case]"
- "I've been using [competitor] for 2 years"
- "I'm pretty picky about [category]"

**Nuanced opinions**
- "Great for [use case], not ideal for [other use]"
- "Worth it at [price] but maybe not at [higher price]"
- "Perfect for beginners, power users might want more"

---

## Testing demonstrations

### Physical products
- Hold and examine
- Test primary function
- Test secondary features
- Durability/quality check
- Size/fit verification

### Software/Apps
- Navigate interface
- Complete key task
- Show unique features
- Test speed/performance
- Demonstrate workflow

### Beauty/Skincare
- Texture demonstration
- Application process
- Immediate results
- Hours later update
- Before/after (if applicable)

### Food/Beverages
- Visual inspection
- First taste reaction
- Texture/consistency
- Flavor breakdown
- Overall satisfaction

---

## Honest review language

### Positive without sounding paid
- "Actually impressed" vs "amazing"
- "This works well" vs "life-changing"
- "Would recommend" vs "everyone needs this"
- "Good value" vs "incredible deal"

### Critiques that build trust
- "Not perfect, but..."
- "One thing I'd change..."
- "Fair criticism:..."
- "Room for improvement:..."

### Balanced verdicts
- "For [audience], this is great. If you're [different audience], maybe look elsewhere."
- "At [price], worth it. At full price, depends on your needs."
- "Solid [rating]/10. Does what it promises."

## Camera/visual tips

### For physical products
- Good lighting on product
- Multiple angles
- Close-ups of details
- Show scale (hand, common objects)
- In-use footage

### For apps/software
- Clean screen recording
- Zoom on key features
- Show actual workflow
- Highlight unique elements

### For beauty products
- Clean skin/face visible
- Natural lighting preferred
- Show texture clearly
- Before/after same conditions

---

## Creator brief notes

For review content:
- Provide product in advance for genuine experience
- Note must-mention features
- Allow honest critiques (builds trust)
- Specify any claims to avoid
- Include key selling points to hit
- Let personality come through
