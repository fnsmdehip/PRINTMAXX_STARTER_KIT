# Script template: Testimonial story

Personal story arc. Builds trust through authentic experience.

---

## Structure

```
[HOOK - 2-3 seconds]
Relatable situation or result

[BACKSTORY - 10-15 seconds]
Where they were before, what they struggled with

[DISCOVERY - 5-8 seconds]
How they found the product

[EXPERIENCE - 10-15 seconds]
What using it was like, specific details

[TRANSFORMATION - 8-10 seconds]
Results, how life is different now

[RECOMMENDATION - 3-5 seconds]
Why they'd tell others to try it
```

---

## Example script: Fitness supplement (45 seconds)

```
HOOK:
"6 months ago I couldn't get through a workout without crashing."

BACKSTORY:
"I'd been going to the gym for years but my energy was always inconsistent. Some days I felt great, other days I'd burn out 20 minutes in. I tried pre-workouts but they made me jittery."

DISCOVERY:
"My trainer recommended [Product]. I was skeptical because I'd tried so many things before."

EXPERIENCE:
"The first time I used it, I noticed the difference immediately. Clean energy, no crash, no jitters. I could actually finish my workouts."

TRANSFORMATION:
"Now I have consistent energy every single session. I've hit PRs I never thought possible. My recovery is better too."

RECOMMENDATION:
"If you're struggling with workout energy, just try it. Link's below."
```

```
DISCOVERY:

"I actually have free time now because I'm not scrambling last minute."

```

## Story arc framework

### The journey pattern
1. **Before state**: Life with the problem
2. **Catalyst**: What made them seek a solution
3. **Discovery**: Finding the product
4. **Skepticism**: Initial doubts (adds authenticity)
5. **Experience**: Using the product
6. **After state**: Life after transformation
7. **Recommendation**: Why others should try

---

## Authenticity markers

Include these to sound real:

### Specific details
- "Every Tuesday and Thursday" not "often"
- "$47 a month on supplements" not "too much money"
- "3 weeks in" not "after a while"

### Honest admissions
- "I was skeptical at first"
- "I almost didn't try it"
- "It's not magic, but..."
- "I still have to [effort], but..."

### Personal quirks
- "I'm the kind of person who..."
- "My [friend/family] always said..."
- "I know it sounds crazy, but..."

---

## Emotional beats

### Frustration (backstory)
"I was so tired of..."
"It was embarrassing when..."
"I felt like giving up on..."

### Hope (discovery)
"Then I saw..."
"Someone mentioned..."
"I figured I'd give it one more try..."

### Surprise (experience)
"I didn't expect..."
"Within [timeframe] I noticed..."
"The first time I used it..."

### Relief (transformation)
"Finally..."
"I can actually..."
"I don't have to worry about..."

### Confidence (recommendation)
"Trust me on this one."
"I tell everyone about it now."
"This is the real deal."

## Timing breakdown (45 second version)

| Section | Duration | Word count |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Hook | 3-4s | 8-12 words |
| Backstory | 12-15s | 35-45 words |
| Discovery | 6-8s | 18-25 words |
| Experience | 12-15s | 35-45 words |
| Transformation | 8-10s | 25-30 words |
| Recommendation | 4-5s | 12-15 words |
| **Total** | ~45s | ~130-170 words |

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't make claims you can't substantiate
- Don't use "cure" or "guarantee" language
- Don't bash competitors by name
- Don't sound like you're reading a script
- Don't oversell - some skepticism adds credibility

---

## FTC compliance notes

For testimonial content:
- Results must be genuine and typical
- Material connections must be disclosed
- Claims must be substantiated
- Consider adding "Results may vary" language

Include disclosure instruction in brief if needed.
