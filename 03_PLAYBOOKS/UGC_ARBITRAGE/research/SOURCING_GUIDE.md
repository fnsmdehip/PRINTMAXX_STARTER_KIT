## UGC Sourcing Overview

### The Arbitrage Opportunity

```
Source creators at: $3-50/video
Sell to clients at: $100-500/video
Margin: 50-90%
```

**Why this works:**
- Geographic wage arbitrage (Eastern Europe, Southeast Asia, Latin America)
- Direct sourcing vs platforms (skip middlemen)
- Volume discounts from reliable creators
- Building creator roster = competitive moat

## Direct Sourcing Strategies

### Strategy 1: X/Twitter Outreach

**Process:**
1. Search: "UGC creator" + [location]
2. Search hashtags: #ugccreator, #ugcvideos
3. Find creators in target price range
4. DM with professional inquiry
5. Request portfolio and rates

**Outreach template:**
```
Hey [Name]!

I run a content agency and I'm building a roster
of UGC creators. Saw your work - really like your style!

Would you be interested in ongoing projects?
- Product categories: [list]
- Typical turnaround: 5-7 days
- Volume: 5-10 videos/month

What are your current rates for 30-60 second videos?
```

### Strategy 2: Instagram Discovery

**Process:**
1. Search hashtags: #ugccreator, #ugcartist, #ugccontentcreator
2. Add location filters where possible
3. Check engagement and quality
4. DM creators who match criteria
5. Build relationship before bulk orders

**Hashtags to search:**
- #ugccreator
- #ugccontentcreator
- #ugcvideo
- #ugccommunity
- #[country]ugc (e.g., #ukraineugc)

### Strategy 3: TikTok Creator Mining

**Process:**
1. Find UGC-style content in target niches
2. Check if creator does UGC (often in bio)
3. Note creators from target regions
4. Reach out via TikTok DM or linked socials
5. Move conversation to email/WhatsApp

### Strategy 4: Facebook Groups

**Groups to join:**
- "UGC Creators Community"
- "UGC Jobs & Collaborations"
- "[Country] UGC Creators"
- "Social Media Content Creators"

**How to use:**
1. Join and observe (don't spam immediately)
2. Post that you're looking for creators
3. Specify: location preference, rate range, niche
4. Respond to creators who comment
5. Move to DMs for negotiation

### Strategy 5: Cold Outreach to Small Creators

**Process:**
1. Find creators with 1K-10K followers
2. Check if they do UGC or seem interested
3. Offer opportunity: "We pay $X for videos"
4. Provide clear brief and expectations
5. Test with small order first

**Why small creators:**
- More affordable
- More responsive
- Eager for work
- Can develop long-term relationships

## Vetting Process

### Step 1: Initial Screen

**Check:**
- Portfolio quality
- English language skills
- Response time
- Professionalism in communication
- Rate alignment

### Step 2: Test Order

**First order:**
- Single video (not bulk)
- Clear brief
- Reasonable deadline
- Pay agreed rate
- Evaluate quality + process

### Step 3: Quality Assessment

| Factor | Weight | Questions |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Video quality | 30% | Lighting, audio, framing? |
| Delivery | 25% | Natural, engaging, authentic? |
| Following brief | 20% | Did they hit all points? |
| Turnaround | 15% | On time? Communication? |
| Revisions | 10% | Responsive to feedback? |

### Step 4: Ongoing Evaluation

**Track per creator:**
- Quality consistency
- Reliability (deadlines)
- Revision rate
- Communication
- Rate changes

## Building Your Creator Roster

### Recommended Roster Size

| Business Stage | Roster Size | Regions |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| Starting | 3-5 creators | 1-2 regions |
| Growing | 10-15 creators | 2-3 regions |
| Established | 20-30 creators | 3-4 regions |

### Roster Categories

**By price tier:**
- Budget ($3-20): 5-10 creators
- Mid-range ($20-50): 5-10 creators
- Premium ($50-100): 3-5 creators

**By niche:**
- Beauty/skincare specialists
- Tech/gadget reviewers
- Fitness/wellness
- Food/cooking
- General/versatile

**By demographics:**
- Age ranges (18-25, 25-35, 35+)
- Ethnicities (for diverse campaigns)
- Genders

### Creator Relationship Management

**Monthly:**
- Check in with top performers
- Offer consistent work
- Pay on time (always)
- Provide feedback

**Quarterly:**
- Review performance
- Adjust rates if needed
- Trim non-performers
- Recruit new talent

## Red Flags to Avoid

### During Vetting

- Won't show portfolio
- Prices too good to be true ($1-2/video)
- Poor communication
- Demands full payment upfront
- No social media presence
- Stock photos as "portfolio"

### During Work

- Misses deadlines without communication
- Quality drops after first video
- Constant excuses
- Tries to change terms mid-project
- Argues about revisions

### General Red Flags

- Stolen content (reverse image search)
- AI-generated "portfolio"
- New accounts with no history
- Won't do video call
- Asks for unusual payment methods

## Payment Methods by Region

### Eastern Europe

**Preferred:**
- Wise (TransferWise) - low fees
- PayPal (widely used)
- Payoneer

**Avoid:**
- Bank transfer (high fees)
- Crypto (unless they prefer)

### Southeast Asia

**Preferred:**
- PayPal
- Wise
- Payoneer
- GCash (Philippines)

### Latin America

**Preferred:**
- PayPal
- Wise
- Payoneer
- Local transfer (if high volume)

### Payment Best Practices

- 50% upfront, 50% on delivery (new creators)
- Net 7 payment terms (established creators)
- Use Wise for best rates
- Document all payments
- Keep receipts for accounting

## Legal Considerations

### Usage Rights

**Always get in writing:**
- Full commercial usage rights
- No time limit on usage
- Rights to edit/modify
- Rights to use in ads
- Rights to resell to clients

**Template clause:**
```
Creator grants Client full, perpetual, worldwide rights
to use, modify, and distribute the delivered content
for any commercial purpose, including advertising and
resale, without additional compensation.
```

### Contracts

**For regular creators, include:**
- Scope of work
- Deliverables
- Usage rights
- Payment terms
- Confidentiality
- Revision policy

**Template recommendation:**
- Use simple 1-page agreement
- Get digital signature (DocuSign, HelloSign)
- Keep records of all agreements
