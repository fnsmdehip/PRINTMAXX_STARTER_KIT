# UGC Client Acquisition Guide

**Updated:** 2026-01-20
**Purpose:** How to find and close UGC buyers consistently

---

## Client Acquisition Overview

### Target Client Profile

**Best clients:**
- DTC (direct-to-consumer) brands
- E-commerce businesses
- Funded startups
- Brands running paid ads
- Agencies needing overflow capacity

**Client signals (ready to buy):**
- Running Facebook/TikTok ads
- Active social presence
- E-commerce site live
- Recently funded
- Hiring for marketing

**Client red flags:**
- No website/product yet
- "Exposure" offers
- Very small budget expectations
- Unclear on what they need
- No existing marketing presence

## Acquisition Channels

### Channel 1: Cold Outreach

**Effectiveness:** High (if done well)
**Cost:** Low (time only)
**Scale:** Medium (30-50/day sustainable)

#### Finding Prospects

**Facebook Ads Library Method:**
1. Go to facebook.com/ads/library
2. Search product categories
3. Filter: Active ads, United States
4. Find brands running video ads
5. Note: If running ads, they have budget

**TikTok Creative Center:**
1. Go to ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter
2. Browse top ads
3. Find brands in your target categories
4. Note what's working (can offer similar)

**Product Hunt:**
1. Browse recent launches
2. Find funded/growing products
3. Especially: Consumer products, apps
4. Great for timing (just launched = need content)

**LinkedIn:**
1. Search: "DTC" + "Marketing Manager"
2. Search: "E-commerce" + "Brand Manager"
3. Filter by company size (11-200)
4. Connect and nurture

#### Outreach Templates

**Cold Email - Direct:**
```
Subject: UGC for [Company Name] ads?

Hey [Name],

Noticed [Company] is running video ads on [Facebook/TikTok].

We make UGC content for brands like yours - the authentic
creator videos that perform well in paid social.

Recent clients: [Relevant example] - saw [X% improvement]
in their ad performance after switching to UGC.

Would it be worth a 15-min call to see if we can help?

[Your Name]
[Link to portfolio]
```

**Cold Email - Value-First:**
```
Subject: Quick idea for your [TikTok/FB] ads

[Name],

Was browsing your Facebook ads and had a thought.

Your current creative is [observation - polished, product-focused, etc.].
What's working really well right now is authentic UGC-style content.

I've seen brands in [their industry] get [X% better ROAS/lower CPA]
just by testing a few UGC videos.

Happy to share some examples if useful.

[Your Name]
```

**LinkedIn Message:**
```
Hey [Name] - saw [Company] is scaling up marketing.

I run a UGC studio that makes creator-style video content
for paid social. Brands like [examples] see [result].

Worth connecting? Happy to share portfolio.
```

#### Follow-Up Sequence

```
Day 1: Initial outreach
Day 3: Short bump
Day 7: Value add (share relevant UGC example)
Day 14: Different angle
Day 21: Breakup ("Closing your file unless...")
```

### Channel 2: Marketplace/Platform Presence

**Effectiveness:** Medium (passive)
**Cost:** Low-Medium (listing fees)
**Scale:** Low (dependent on platform traffic)

#### Platforms to List On

| Platform | Cost | Competition | Notes |
|----------|------|-------------|-------|
| Upwork | 20% fee | High | Volume but race to bottom |
| Fiverr | 20% fee | High | Good for portfolio building |
| Contra | 0-5% | Medium | More professional |
| Toptal | $500 screening | Low | Premium clients |
| 99designs | Entry fee | Medium | Design-adjacent |

#### Optimizing Listings

**Title formula:**
"[Type] UGC Content for [Platform/Result]"
Examples:
- "Authentic UGC Videos for TikTok Ads"
- "High-Converting UGC Content for DTC Brands"

**Portfolio must include:**
- 5+ example videos
- Before/after results (if available)
- Different styles/tones
- Various product categories

**Reviews strategy:**
- Start with lower prices for reviews
- Ask every satisfied client
- Respond to all reviews
- Feature best reviews prominently

### Channel 3: Inbound Content Marketing

**Effectiveness:** High (long-term)
**Cost:** Time-intensive
**Scale:** High (compounds)

#### Content Strategy

**Platforms (prioritized):**
1. TikTok (showcase your work)
2. Instagram (portfolio)
3. LinkedIn (B2B clients)
4. YouTube (tutorials, education)

**Content pillars:**
- Examples of your work (portfolio showcase)
- UGC tips for brands (education)
- Behind-the-scenes of creation
- Results/case studies
- Industry trends/news

**Posting cadence:**
- TikTok: 1-2x/day
- Instagram: 1x/day + stories
- LinkedIn: 3-5x/week
- YouTube: 1x/week

#### Lead Magnets

**Options:**
- "UGC Script Templates" PDF
- "What Makes UGC Convert" guide
- "UGC Checklist for Brands"
- Free UGC audit offer
- Sample video (for select brands)

#### Capture Flow

```
Content → Profile visit → Bio link → Email capture → Nurture → Sale
                                          ↓
                              Direct inquiry → Discovery call → Sale
```

### Channel 4: Referral System

**Effectiveness:** Highest (warm leads)
**Cost:** Revenue share
**Scale:** Medium (dependent on relationships)

#### Building Referral Network

**Who to partner with:**
- Media buyers (need creative)
- Marketing agencies (overflow work)
- Ad agencies (UGC not their specialty)
- Other UGC creators (different niches)
- E-commerce consultants (client connections)

#### Referral Structure

**Option 1: One-time fee**
- Pay $50-100 per referred client
- Simple and clean
- Works for casual referrers

**Option 2: Revenue share**
- 10-15% of first project
- Or 5-10% ongoing
- Better for consistent referrers

**Option 3: Reciprocal referrals**
- You send them clients too
- No money changes hands
- Best for complementary services

#### Referral Outreach

```
Hey [Name],

I run a UGC studio making creator content for paid ads.

I know you work with brands who run ads. If any of them
need video content, I'd love to be your go-to.

I offer [referral structure] for any clients you send.

Open to chatting about how we could work together?
```

## Sales Process

### Step 1: Discovery Call (15-30 min)

**Goals:**
- Understand their needs
- Qualify budget/fit
- Build rapport
- Set up proposal

**Questions to ask:**
1. "What's driving your interest in UGC right now?"
2. "What have you tried before? What worked/didn't?"
3. "What does success look like for this project?"
4. "What's your timeline?"
5. "What's your budget range for content?"
6. "Who else is involved in this decision?"

### Step 2: Proposal

**Include:**
- Understanding of their needs (shows you listened)
- Recommended package/scope
- Pricing (clear and simple)
- Timeline
- What's included (and what's not)
- Next steps

**Proposal template:**
```markdown
# UGC Proposal: [Company Name]

## Understanding Your Needs
[Summary of what they said they need]

## Recommended Package
[Package name]: [X] videos
- [Spec 1]
- [Spec 2]
- [Spec 3]

## Investment
$[Amount]

Includes:
- [What's included]
- [What's included]
- [Revisions]

## Timeline
- Kick-off: [Date]
- Scripts: [Date]
- First drafts: [Date]
- Final delivery: [Date]

## Next Steps
1. Sign off on this proposal
2. 50% deposit ($[X])
3. Kickoff call scheduled
4. We begin!

Ready to move forward? Reply to this email or sign below.
```

### Step 3: Close

**Closing questions:**
- "Does this match what you had in mind?"
- "Any questions before we move forward?"
- "What would you need to see to say yes?"
- "Is there anything holding you back?"

**If objections:**
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | "What's your budget? Let me see if we can adjust scope." |
| "Need to think" | "Of course. What specifically do you need to consider?" |
| "Need approval" | "Who else needs to sign off? Can we include them?" |
| "Not the right time" | "When would be better? I'll follow up then." |

### Step 4: Onboarding

**After signing:**
1. Send welcome email
2. Collect payment (50% upfront)
3. Schedule kickoff call
4. Send onboarding form (product info, brand guidelines, etc.)
5. Begin production

## Pricing Presentation

### How to Present Pricing

**Anchoring:**
- Start with highest package
- Then show mid-tier
- Then starter
- They'll often choose middle

**Package structure:**
```
SCALE: 20 videos/month - $2,500
- Perfect for brands testing at scale
- Variety of creator styles
- Priority turnaround

GROWTH: 10 videos/month - $1,400
- Most popular choice
- Mix of styles and formats
- Standard turnaround

STARTER: 5 videos - $750
- Great for testing
- See what works
- One-time purchase
```

**When asked for discount:**
```
"I can't lower the price, but I could:
- Add 2 more videos at the same rate
- Include raw footage (usually extra)
- Expedite delivery
- Add [other value]

Would any of those work?"
```

## Client Retention

### Keeping Clients Long-Term

**After first project:**
- Deliver on time (or early)
- Exceed quality expectations
- Ask for feedback
- Suggest next steps

**Ongoing relationship:**
- Check in monthly (even if no project)
- Share industry insights
- Offer loyalty pricing
- Ask for referrals
- Feature them in case studies

### Upsell Opportunities

| Timing | Upsell |
|--------|--------|
| After first project | Retainer package |
| After good results | More volume |
| Seasonal | Holiday content push |
| Product launch | Launch content package |
| Underperforming ads | Creative refresh |

```
Glad the content is performing well!

If you know any other brands who could use UGC,
I'd really appreciate an intro.

Happy to offer [referral incentive] for anyone
you send our way.
```

## Tracking & Pipeline

### CRM Setup

**Track for each lead:**
- Company name
- Contact name + email
- Source (how they found you)
- Stage (lead → qualified → proposal → closed)
- Value (estimated project size)
- Next action (follow up date/task)

- Notion (free)

```
1. Lead (just identified)
2. Contacted (reached out)
3. Responded (they replied)
4. Discovery (call scheduled/completed)
5. Proposal (sent proposal)
```

**Every week, review:**
- New leads added
- Leads contacted
- Calls completed
- Proposals sent
- Deals closed
- Revenue generated

## Outreach Targets

### Weekly Goals (Starting)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Cold emails sent | 50-100 |
| LinkedIn connections | 25-50 |
| Discovery calls | 3-5 |
| Proposals sent | 2-3 |
| Deals closed | 1-2 |

### Conversion Benchmarks

| Stage | Expected Conversion |
|-------|---------------------|
| Email → Reply | 5-15% |
| Reply → Call | 30-50% |
| Call → Proposal | 50-70% |
| Proposal → Close | 30-50% |

### Math

```
If you want 5 clients/month:
5 clients ÷ 0.3 (proposal close rate) = 17 proposals
17 proposals ÷ 0.5 (call to proposal) = 34 calls
34 calls ÷ 0.3 (reply to call) = 113 replies
113 replies ÷ 0.1 (email to reply) = 1,130 emails

~300 emails/week = ~5 clients/month
```
