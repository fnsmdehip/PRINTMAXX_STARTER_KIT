## Pricing Framework Overview

### The Arbitrage Model

```
Creator Cost + Overhead + Profit Margin = Client Price

Where:
- Creator Cost: What you pay for content
- Overhead: Script writing, revisions, management (10-20%)
- Profit Margin: Your take (50-70%)
```

### Margin Targets

| Business Stage | Target Margin | Notes |
|----------------|---------------|-------|
| Starting | 50%+ | Build portfolio, gain experience |
| Growing | 60-70% | Optimize operations |
| Established | 70%+ | Volume + efficiency |

## Pricing Models

### Model 1: Per-Video Pricing

**Best for:** One-off projects, testing clients

```
Base price: $XXX per video
+ Add-ons as needed
```

**Pros:**
- Simple to understand
- Easy to quote
- Flexible

**Cons:**
- Less predictable revenue
- More admin per sale

### Model 2: Package Pricing

**Best for:** Most clients

```
3 videos: $400 ($133/video)
5 videos: $600 ($120/video)
10 videos: $1,100 ($110/video)
```

**Pros:**
- Higher average order value
- Encourages volume
- Simpler operations

**Cons:**
- Need to commit to deliverables
- Less flexibility

### Model 3: Retainer Pricing

**Best for:** Agency clients, ongoing relationships

```
5 videos/month: $750/mo
10 videos/month: $1,400/mo
20 videos/month: $2,500/mo
```

**Pros:**
- Predictable revenue
- Stronger relationships
- Easier planning

**Cons:**
- Need to deliver consistently
- Less flexibility

### Model 4: Revenue Share (Advanced)

**Best for:** Performance-focused clients

```
Base fee: $XXX
+ % of ad spend or sales generated
```

**Pros:**
- Higher upside potential
- Aligned incentives
- Premium positioning

**Cons:**
- Complex tracking
- Delayed payment
- Risk if content underperforms

## Negotiation Guidelines

### When Clients Push Back

**"That's expensive"**
```
Response: "I understand. Let me show you what goes into this:
- Professional creators (not random TikTokers)
- Script development
- Quality review process
- Revision rounds
- Commercial usage rights

What's your budget? I might be able to adjust the scope."
```

**"Can you do cheaper?"**
```
Response: "I can offer better per-unit pricing with volume:
- 5 videos: 10% off
- 10 videos: 15% off
- Monthly retainer: 20% off

What volume are you looking at?"
```

**"Competitor is cheaper"**
```
Response: "There are definitely cheaper options. A few questions:
- Do they include commercial usage rights?
- What's their revision policy?
- What's the creator quality like?
- What's the turnaround?

Happy to compete on value, not just price."
```

### When to Discount

**Discount for:**
- Volume (10+ videos)
- Retainers (monthly commitment)
- Case study permission
- Testimonial in exchange
- Long-term contract

**Don't discount for:**
- "First-time customer" without commitment
- "I'll refer others" (get referrals first)
- Price matching competitors
- Sob stories

### Discount Structure

| Trigger | Discount |
|---------|----------|
| 5+ videos | 5-10% |
| 10+ videos | 10-15% |
| Monthly retainer | 15-20% |
| 6-month contract | 20-25% |
| Case study + testimonial | 10% |

## Calculating Your Rates

### Step 1: Calculate Creator Cost

```
Base video cost: $30
+ Revisions estimate: $5
+ Platform fees: $2
= Total creator cost: $37
```

### Step 2: Add Overhead

```
Creator cost: $37
+ Script writing time: $10
+ Management time: $8
+ Tools/software: $5
= Total cost: $60
```

### Step 3: Apply Margin

```
Total cost: $60
Target margin: 65%
Price = Cost / (1 - Margin)
Price = $60 / 0.35 = $171

Round to: $175 or $200
```

### Quick Pricing Formula

```
Client Price = Creator Cost × 3 to 4

Example:
$40 creator cost × 3.5 = $140 client price
$75 creator cost × 3 = $225 client price
```

## Price Increase Strategy

### When to Raise Prices

- After 10+ satisfied clients
- When demand exceeds capacity
- After adding new capabilities
- Annually (inflation adjustment)
- When creator costs increase

### How to Raise Prices

**For new clients:**
- Simply quote new rates
- No explanation needed

**For existing clients:**
```
"Hi [Name],

Just letting you know that starting [date],
our rates will be increasing by [X%].

This reflects [reason - quality improvements,
creator cost increases, demand, etc.].

Your current rate of $X will move to $Y.

Projects booked before [date] will honor
current pricing.

Thanks for your continued partnership!"
```

### Price Increase Schedule

| Situation | Increase | Frequency |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| New service | Set higher | One-time |
| Demand > Supply | 10-20% | As needed |
| Cost increases | Passthrough | As needed |
| Annual adjustment | 5-10% | Yearly |

## Competitive Positioning

### Budget Positioning ($75-150/video)

**Target:** Small businesses, startups
**Value prop:** Affordable quality
**Competition:** Fiverr, cheap creators

**Pros:**
- High volume potential
- Easy to sell
- Large market

**Cons:**
- Low margin
- High churn
- Price-sensitive clients

### Mid-Market Positioning ($150-300/video)

**Target:** Growing DTC brands, SMBs
**Value prop:** Quality + reliability
**Competition:** Other agencies, platforms

**Pros:**
- Good margins
- Reasonable client quality
- Sustainable

**Cons:**
- Competitive market
- Need to differentiate

### Premium Positioning ($300-750/video)

**Target:** Funded startups, enterprise
**Value prop:** Results-focused, full service
**Competition:** Large agencies

**Pros:**
- High margins
- Better clients
- Less price sensitivity

**Cons:**
- Smaller market
- Higher expectations
- Longer sales cycle
