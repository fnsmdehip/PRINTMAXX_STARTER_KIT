## Proposal do's and don'ts

### Do:
- Customize for each client (reference their call)
- Include clear pricing with options
- Show social proof
- Make next steps obvious
- Follow up systematically

### Don't:
- Send generic templates
- Overwhelm with options (3 max)
- Hide pricing
- Include too much jargon
- Forget to follow up

---

## Handling objections in proposals

### "It's too expensive"

Include this in proposal:
"Consider this: A single video that performs well can generate thousands in revenue. The ROI on quality UGC typically pays for itself within the first month of running ads."

Or offer smaller starter package.

### "We need to think about it"

In follow-up:
"Totally understand - what specific questions can I help answer to make the decision easier?"

### "We're comparing options"

In proposal:
"Unlike agencies that charge $500+ per video, we deliver quality content at a fraction of the cost while maintaining the same professional standards."

### "Can we do a test first?"

Have a starter package ready:
"Absolutely - our Starter Package ($500) is designed exactly for this. Three videos to test the approach before committing to more."
