# UGC portfolio building guide

Your portfolio is your sales tool. Here's how to build it from scratch.

---

## Starting from zero

### Challenge: No clients yet

You need samples to get clients, but need clients to get samples.

### Solution: Create spec work

**Option 1: Partner with small brands**

1. Find brands with no UGC (look for product-shot-only ads)
2. Offer 2-3 free videos in exchange for testimonial
3. Pick brands with good products you'd actually use
4. Get permission to use in portfolio

**Approach:**
"Hey [Brand], I'm a UGC producer building my portfolio. I love your product and would create 2 free videos for you - just need permission to show them as samples. Interested?"

---

**Option 2: Create for products you own**

1. Buy products you already use/like
2. Create UGC for them (even without brand permission)
3. Marked as "spec work" in portfolio
4. Shows your capabilities

**Products to consider:**
- Skincare you use
- Tech gadgets you own
- Supplements you take
- Apps you like

---

**Option 3: Hire creators for spec projects**

1. Pick 2-3 product categories you want to work in
2. Hire 1-2 creators to make sample content
3. Use generic/white-label products if needed
4. Invest $100-200 in samples

This demonstrates:
- You can direct creators
- You understand quality
- You have a process

## Portfolio structure

### What to include

**Category 1: Best work (Hero pieces)**
- 3-5 absolute best videos
- Highest quality
- Variety of styles
- Lead with these

**Category 2: By content type**
- Testimonials
- Tutorials
- Unboxings
- Before/after
- Shows range

**Category 3: By niche (optional)**
- Beauty/skincare
- Tech/gadgets
- Food/supplements
- Fitness
- Helps clients envision their content

**Category 4: Results/case studies**
- Performance metrics
- Client testimonials
- Before/after stats
- Proves value

- [ ] Natural delivery

- [ ] Different content types
- [ ] Different niches

- [ ] Brief description of each piece

## Building portfolio over time

### Phase 1: Starting (0-3 clients)
- 5-10 spec samples
- Focus on quality over quantity
- Get first client testimonials
- Document everything

### Phase 2: Growing (3-10 clients)
- Add client work (with permission)
- Start collecting results data
- Build case studies
- Expand niche variety

### Phase 3: Established (10+ clients)
- Curate best work only
- Strong case studies with metrics
- Multiple client testimonials
- Niche-specific portfolios

## Getting permission to use client work

### In your contract

Include clause:
"[Your Company] may use deliverables in portfolio and marketing materials unless specifically restricted by Client in writing."

### After project completion

Email:
"Thanks for the great project! Quick request - may I feature this work in my portfolio? I'll include [Brand Name] or keep it anonymous, whichever you prefer."

### What to ask permission for
- Using the video itself
- Mentioning brand name
- Sharing performance results
- Using as case study

## Case study format

### Simple case study structure

```
CLIENT: [Brand Name or "DTC Skincare Brand"]

CHALLENGE:
[What they needed]
"Brand X was running the same product photos for 6 months. CTR had dropped to 0.8% and CPAs were rising."

SOLUTION:
[What you did]
"We created 6 UGC testimonial videos with 3 hooks each, giving them 18 ad variations to test."

RESULTS:
[Metrics]
- CTR increased from 0.8% to 2.1%
- CPA decreased 35%
- 3 videos still running after 60 days

CLIENT QUOTE:
"[Testimonial from client]"

SAMPLE WORK:
[Video embed or link]
```

## Collecting testimonials

### When to ask
- Right after successful delivery
- When client expresses satisfaction
- After sharing positive results
- During project wrap-up

### How to ask

```
"Hey [Name], I'm so glad the project went well! Would you mind sharing a quick testimonial about working together? Just 2-3 sentences about your experience. I'd really appreciate it!"
```

### What to ask for

Good testimonial prompts:
- What was your experience working with us?
- What results did you see?
- Would you recommend us to others?
- What was the best part of working together?

### Testimonial formats
- Written quote (easiest)
- Video testimonial (most powerful)
- LinkedIn recommendation (public proof)
- Case study participation (detailed)

## Portfolio mistakes to avoid

### Common mistakes

1. **Too much content** - Quality over quantity. 10 great samples beat 50 mediocre ones.

2. **No organization** - Make it easy to find relevant work.

3. **No context** - Explain what each piece was for.

4. **Outdated work** - Remove old/lower quality samples as you improve.

5. **No results** - Include metrics wherever possible.

6. **Hard to access** - Don't require login or downloads.

7. **Wrong format** - Vertical videos should be viewable on mobile.

## Portfolio maintenance

### Monthly review
- Remove underperforming samples
- Add new best work
- Update case studies
- Refresh testimonials

### Quarterly update
- Review overall positioning
- Check all links work
- Update services/pricing if changed
- Add new niches/capabilities

### Track what works
- Note which samples get mentioned
- Ask clients what caught their attention
- Double down on what resonates
