# UGC pricing strategy

What to charge clients and how to think about markup.

---

## Pricing philosophy

Your price = Creator cost + Your markup + Value delivered

**The arbitrage math:**
- Buy content at $20-50
- Sell content at $100-250
- Keep the spread
- Scale with volume

**Value-based pricing:**
- You're not just selling videos
- You're selling time saved (client doesn't source creators)
- You're selling expertise (you know what converts)
- You're selling reliability (you deliver on time)
- You're selling results (ads that work)

## Pricing by client type

### Small DTC brands ($0-$1M revenue)
- Price: Entry to standard tier
- Package: Starter or Growth
- Payment: 50% upfront
- Typical project: $500-1,500

### Growth DTC brands ($1M-$10M revenue)
- Price: Standard to premium tier
- Package: Growth or Scale
- Payment: Net 15-30
- Typical project: $1,000-3,000

### Established brands ($10M+ revenue)
- Price: Premium tier
- Package: Scale or custom
- Payment: Net 30
- Typical project: $2,500-5,000+

### Agencies
- Price: Wholesale (15-20% discount)
- Volume: High
- Relationship: Ongoing
- Typical monthly: $3,000-10,000

## Price positioning

### Don't compete on price

**Race to bottom:**
- "We're cheaper than agencies"
- "Budget UGC"
- "$50 videos"

**Compete on value:**
- "UGC that converts"
- "Performance creative"
- "Done-for-you UGC system"

### How to justify higher prices

1. **Speed** - "We deliver in 5 days, not 3 weeks"
2. **Quality** - "Vetted creators, consistent results"
3. **Expertise** - "We know what performs on paid"
4. **Reliability** - "We've delivered 500+ videos"
5. **Volume** - "Scale without hiring"

## Discounting strategy

### When to discount

- First project with promising client (10-15% max)
- Volume commitment (5%+ for 10+ videos)
- Long-term retainer (10-15% monthly rate)
- Referral incentive

### When NOT to discount

- To win a price-sensitive client
- When they "need to check budget"
- To match cheaper competitors
- For "exposure" or "future work promise"

### How to present discounts

Always frame as value-add, not price reduction:

```
"Standard rate for 6 videos is $1,200. For your first project, I can do $1,000 to get us started and prove the value."

"If you commit to 10 videos, I can drop the per-video rate by 15%."
```

## Payment terms

### New clients
- 50% upfront, 50% on delivery
- Payment before work begins
- Net 7 on final invoice

### Established clients
- Net 15-30
- Invoiced on delivery
- Monthly invoicing for retainers

### Payment methods
- Stripe (cards)
- PayPal
- Wire transfer (large amounts)
- ACH

### Late payment policy
- Include in contract
- Follow up at 7 days
- Pause work at 30 days
- Collections process if needed

## Price increase strategy

### When to raise prices

- After 5-10 successful projects
- When demand exceeds capacity
- When you have strong portfolio
- Annually (inflation + experience)

### How much to raise

- Small increase: 10-15%
- Significant increase: 20-30%
- Market repositioning: 50%+

### How to communicate

```
"Hi [Client],

I wanted to give you advance notice that our rates are increasing on [Date]. New pricing reflects increased demand and continued investment in quality.

Current clients locked in at current rates through [Date].

New rates will be:
[New pricing]

Happy to discuss any questions.
```

Grandfather existing clients temporarily (3-6 months).
