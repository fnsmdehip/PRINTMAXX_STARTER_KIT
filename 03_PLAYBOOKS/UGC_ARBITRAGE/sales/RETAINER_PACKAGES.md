# Monthly UGC retainer packages

Recurring revenue is the goal. Here's how to structure retainers.

---

## Why retainers beat one-offs

### Benefits for you
- Predictable monthly income
- Consistent workload
- Deeper client relationships
- Less sales time needed
- Higher lifetime value

### Benefits for clients
- Consistent creative refresh
- Priority treatment
- Better rates
- Streamlined process
- Dedicated attention

## Retainer structure options

### Option A: Fixed volume

Set number of videos per month at fixed price.
- Simple to understand
- Clear expectations
- Predictable costs

Best when: Client has consistent needs

---

### Option B: Credit system

Client buys credit pool, uses as needed.

```
CREDIT RETAINER - $2,500/month

Credits included: 25 credits

Credit values:
- 15s video: 1 credit
- 30s video: 1.5 credits
- 60s video: 2 credits
- Additional hook: 0.5 credits
- Rush delivery: +1 credit

Unused credits: Roll over (max 10)
```

Best when: Client needs flexibility

---

### Option C: Hybrid model

Base retainer + usage pricing.

```
BASE RETAINER - $1,000/month

Includes:
- Account management
- Creative strategy
- Priority treatment

PLUS per-video rates:
- 30s video: $100 (vs. $150 standard)
- Additional hooks: $30 (vs. $50 standard)
```

Best when: Variable volume, established relationship

## Converting one-off clients to retainers

### When to pitch retainer

- After successful first project
- When they come back for seconds
- When they mention ongoing needs
- During project wrap-up

### How to pitch

```
"Hey [Name], glad the first batch went well! I noticed you're probably going to need fresh creative regularly for ads.

I offer monthly retainers that give you:
- Better per-video rates (saves 15-20%)
- Priority turnaround
- Dedicated attention

Want me to put together a retainer proposal for your needs?"
```

### Conversion email

```
Subject: Ideas for ongoing UGC

Hi [Name],

Now that we've established what works, I wanted to share some options for ongoing partnership.

Most brands I work with find they need fresh creative every 2-4 weeks to combat ad fatigue. Running the same ads too long tanks performance.

I put together a few retainer options that would:
- Lock in better rates for you
- Ensure consistent creative flow
- Give you priority delivery

[Link to retainer proposal]

Happy to jump on a call to discuss what makes sense.

[Your name]
```

## Retainer onboarding process

### Week 0: Kickoff

1. Sign contract
2. Collect brand assets
3. Review ad account (if shared)
4. Document brand guidelines
5. Align on content strategy

### Week 1: First batch

1. Develop initial briefs
2. Assign creators
3. Production begins
4. First delivery by end of week

### Ongoing: Monthly rhythm

```
MONTHLY CALENDAR:

Week 1:
- Review previous month performance
- Plan content focus
- Assign to creators

Week 2:
- First batch delivery
- Revisions if needed
- Begin second batch

Week 3:
- Second batch delivery
- Revisions
- Begin third batch if applicable

Week 4:
- Final batch delivery
- Performance review prep
- Plan next month
```

## Retainer pricing math

### Example: $3,000/month retainer

Client pays: $3,000
Videos delivered: 16

Your cost per video: ~$30
Total creator cost: ~$480

Other costs:
- Tools/software: ~$50
- Your time (10 hrs): ~$500 (at $50/hr equivalent)
- Total costs: ~$1,030

Gross profit: ~$1,970/month (66% margin)

### Scaling with efficiency

As you systematize:
- Briefs become templates
- Creator management streamlines
- Less time per client
- Margin improves to 70-80%

## Retainer contract terms

### Key terms to include

**Commitment period**: 3-6 months minimum
**Payment terms**: Monthly, due on 1st
**Scope**: Specific volume and deliverables
**Overages**: Rate for additional content
**Unused volume**: Rollover policy (limited)
**Termination**: 30 days notice after minimum
**Rate increases**: Annual review clause

### Rollover policy options

1. **No rollover**: Use it or lose it (simplest)
2. **Limited rollover**: 20% max carries forward
3. **Credit system**: Full rollover with expiration

Recommendation: Limited rollover (20%) prevents scope creep while showing flexibility.

## Retainer red flags

### Clients to avoid putting on retainer

- Constantly late on payments
- High-maintenance, low-budget
- Unclear on what they need
- No consistent ad budget
- Decision by committee (slow)

### Warning signs during retainer

- Consistently not using full volume
- Frequent scope expansion requests
- Late payments
- Difficult feedback cycles

### When to end a retainer

- Consistently unprofitable
- Taking too much time vs. revenue
- Relationship has soured
- Better opportunities available

Give 30-day notice, be professional, offer transition support.
