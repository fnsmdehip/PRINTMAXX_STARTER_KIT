# UGC client acquisition

How to find clients who will pay for UGC content.

---

## Target client profiles

### Tier 1: Best clients (prioritize)

**DTC e-commerce brands ($1M-$20M revenue)**
- Running paid ads (Meta, TikTok)
- Selling physical products
- Need constant creative refresh
- Have budget, want results
- Decision maker: Founder or Marketing Manager

**Signs they need UGC:**
- Running the same 3 ads for months
- Static product photos only
- No customer testimonials
- Competitors using UGC successfully
- Growing but creative-constrained

**Where to find:**
- Shopify store directories
- Facebook Ad Library (see who's advertising)
- Product Hunt launches
- Instagram brand accounts
- LinkedIn (search "DTC founder")

---

### Tier 2: Good clients

**Marketing agencies**
- Handle multiple clients
- Need reliable UGC sourcing
- Volume potential
- Can become long-term partner

**Signs they need you:**
- Agency with e-commerce clients
- No in-house UGC team
- Running performance ads
- Looking to expand creative services

**Where to find:**
- LinkedIn (search "performance marketing agency")
- Agency directories
- Marketing Twitter
- Clutch.co listings

---

**Funded startups (Seed-Series B)**
- Have money to spend
- Need to grow fast
- Marketing experiments encouraged
- Usually have gaps in creative

**Where to find:**
- Crunchbase (recent funding)
- TechCrunch funding announcements
- Product Hunt
- Y Combinator portfolio

---

### Tier 3: Entry-level clients (for portfolio building)

**Small e-commerce stores**
- Lower budgets ($500-2000)
- Fewer ongoing needs
- Good for starting out
- Building case studies

**Solopreneurs/creators**
- Small projects
- Quick turnaround
- Lower rates acceptable
- Relationship potential

## Outreach channels

### Cold email

**Pros:** Scalable, professional, trackable
**Cons:** Lower response rate, spam filters
**Response rate:** 2-5% (good list, good copy)

**Finding emails:**
- Hunter.io
- Apollo.io
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Company website contact pages

**Email sequence:**
```
Email 1 (Day 1): Introduction + value prop
Email 2 (Day 4): Case study or social proof
Email 3 (Day 8): Final follow-up
```

---

### LinkedIn outreach

**Pros:** Professional context, can see their content
**Cons:** Connection limits, can feel salesy
**Response rate:** 5-15% (warm approach)

**Strategy:**
1. Connect with relevant people (founders, marketing managers)
2. Engage with their content first (genuine comments)
3. After acceptance, send personalized message
4. Don't pitch immediately - start conversation

**Who to target:**
- Founder / CEO (small companies)
- Marketing Manager / Director
- Growth Lead
- Performance Marketing Manager
- Creative Director

---

### Twitter/X

**Pros:** Public conversations, casual approach
**Cons:** Takes time to build presence
**Works best:** For personal brand + inbound

**Strategy:**
1. Follow people in your target market
2. Reply to their tweets with value
3. Share UGC tips and insights
4. Build relationships before pitching
5. DMs once relationship exists

---

### Referrals

**Pros:** Highest conversion, pre-qualified
**Cons:** Need existing clients first
**Conversion rate:** 30-50%

**How to generate:**
- Ask happy clients for referrals
- Offer referral incentive ($50-100 per new client)
- Stay in touch with past contacts
- Network in marketing communities

---

### Communities and groups

**Where to be:**
- Slack communities (Marketing, E-commerce)
- Facebook groups (DTC brands, Shopify sellers)
- Discord servers (marketing focused)
- Reddit (r/ecommerce, r/marketing)

**Approach:**
- Provide value first
- Answer questions
- Share knowledge
- Position as expert
- Soft pitch when appropriate

**Groups to join:**
- DTC Twitter community
- Shopify entrepreneurs groups
- Facebook advertiser groups
- E-commerce marketing Slack channels

## Qualifying leads

### Questions to ask

**Budget qualification:**
- What's your current ad spend?
- What's your budget for creative?
- How much are you spending on content currently?

**Need qualification:**
- How are you creating ad content now?
- How often do you refresh creative?
- What's performing best for you right now?

**Timing qualification:**
- When are you looking to test new creative?
- Do you have upcoming launches?
- What's your timeline?

**Decision qualification:**
- Who else is involved in this decision?
- What's your process for bringing on vendors?

---

### Good lead signals

- Currently running paid ads
- Spending $10K+/month on ads
- Creative refresh every 2-4 weeks
- Expressed frustration with current content
- Competitor using UGC
- Recent funding or growth

### Red flags

- "We do everything in-house"
- No paid ad budget
- Looking for "partnership" (free work)
- Decision maker unavailable
- "We'll reach out when ready" (never)
- Asking for free samples before any commitment

## Building pipeline

### Outreach volume

Starting out:
- 50 cold emails per week
- 20 LinkedIn connections per day
- 5 warm introductions per week

As pipeline builds:
- Reduce cold volume
- Increase referral focus
- Content marketing for inbound

### CRM tracking

Track for each lead:
- Company name
- Contact name + title
- Email/LinkedIn
- Source (how you found them)
- Status (contacted, replied, called, proposal sent, closed)
- Notes
- Follow-up date

Simple tools: Notion, Airtable, Google Sheets
CRM tools: HubSpot (free tier), Pipedrive

## Converting leads to clients

### Discovery call structure (15-20 min)

1. **Intro** (2 min)
   - Thank them for time
   - Briefly introduce yourself
   - Agenda for call

2. **Understand their situation** (8 min)
   - What are you selling?
   - How are you advertising now?
   - What creative are you using?
   - What's working/not working?
   - What would better creative do for you?

3. **Position your solution** (5 min)
   - How you can help
   - Brief process overview
   - Social proof/examples

4. **Next steps** (3 min)
   - If interested: Send proposal
   - If not ready: Follow up timeline
   - Clear next action

### Proposal to close

After call:
- Send proposal within 24 hours
- Include pricing options
- Follow up at 3 days if no response
- Second follow up at 7 days
- Move on after 2 weeks
