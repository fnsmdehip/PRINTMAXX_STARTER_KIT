# Affiliate Tactic: DTC First-Purchase Bonus Structure

## Source
- DTC affiliate commission benchmarks 2026
- E-commerce affiliate trends

## Tactic
Promote DTC brands offering 10-15% on first-time customer orders with tier bonuses for volume.

## Specific Numbers
- **DTC standard rate: 10-15%** for new customer orders
- **Flat bonus alternative: $10-15** for entry SKUs
- **Tier bump: 2-5%** additional for top performers
- **Apparel & accessories: 8-15%** first-time orders
- **Beauty & personal care: 10-18%** or flat $10-15
- **Health & fitness: 3-20%** depending on product
- **E-commerce average: 5%** (baseline)
- **Digital products: 20%+** (higher margins)

## How It Works (Tiered Structure)
**Tier 1 (0-9 sales/month):**
- 10% commission on first-time customer orders
- 0% on repeat customer orders

**Tier 2 (10-49 sales/month):**
- 12% commission on first-time orders
- 2% on repeat orders

**Tier 3 (50+ sales/month):**
- 15% commission on first-time orders
- 5% on repeat orders

## Why First-Purchase Focus?
- **Brands pay for customer acquisition** (CAC), not retention
- **Affiliates incentivized to find new customers** vs recycling existing
- **Prevents cannibalization** of organic/repeat traffic
- **2026 standard** in DTC affiliate platforms

## Commission Structure Examples
**Example 1: Apparel Brand**
- Product: Hoodie ($60)
- First-time customer: 12% = $7.20
- Repeat customer: 2% = $1.20

**Example 2: Beauty Brand**
- Product: Skincare set ($45)
- First-time customer: Flat $15 bonus
- Repeat customer: $0 (or 2%)

**Example 3: Supplement Brand**
- Product: Protein powder ($80)
- First-time customer: 15% = $12
- Repeat customer: 5% = $4

## Why Flat Bonuses Work for Low AOV
For products under $50:
- Flat $10-15 bonus > percentage commission
- Easier to communicate to affiliates
- Predictable payout for affiliates

**Math:**
- 10% of $30 product = $3 (not motivating)
- Flat $15 for same $30 product = better incentive

## Best DTC Categories for Affiliates (2026)
1. **Beauty/skincare**: 10-18% + high AOV
2. **Fitness supplements**: 10-20% + subscription potential
3. **Apparel**: 8-15% + seasonal spikes
4. **Home/lifestyle**: 5-12% + high AOV items
5. **Pet products**: 8-15% + recurring potential

## Strategic Focus
**Prioritize brands with:**
- Strong organic demand (easy to convert)
- Subscription options (recurring revenue potential)
- High AOV ($80+ average order)
- Good retention (repeat customers count after tier bonus)
- Brand recognition (trust = conversions)

## Tier Progression Strategy
**Month 1-3**: Hit 10 sales/month (Tier 2)
- Focus on easy wins (gift guides, seasonal content)
- Promote 3-5 brands max (don't spread thin)

**Month 4-6**: Scale to 50 sales/month (Tier 3)
- Invest in SEO content
- Build email list for promotions
- Add more products from same brands

**Month 7+**: Maintain Tier 3, add new brands
- Negotiate custom rates with top brands
- Get early access to product launches
- Lock in higher rates before scaling

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Promoting only repeat purchase brands (low commissions)
- Ignoring tier structure (leaving money on table)
- Promoting too many brands (dilutes authority)
- Only focusing on low AOV products (more work, less payout)

## DTC vs SaaS Comparison
| Factor | DTC Affiliates | SaaS Affiliates |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Commission | 10-15% | 30-70% |
| Recurring? | Rare | Common |
| Conversion | Easier | Harder |
| AOV | $50-150 | $99-999/year |
| Market Size | Larger | Smaller |

**Strategy**: Do both. SaaS for recurring, DTC for volume.
