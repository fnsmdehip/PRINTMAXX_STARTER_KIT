# Info Product Pricing: Good-Better-Best Tiered Structure

## Source
- Tiered pricing strategy research 2026
- Digital product pricing optimization
- SaaS pricing page best practices

## Tactic
Offer 3 pricing tiers with middle option optimized for highest conversion (price anchoring).

## Specific Numbers
- **3-4 pricing tiers recommended** (not more)
- **2-3x price jumps between tiers** (e.g., $97 → $197 → $497)
- **Middle tier converts best** (price anchoring effect)
- **Too many tiers confuse customers** (max 4 options)

## How It Works
1. **Create 3 tiers** based on value delivered (not cost)
2. **Price at 2-3x increments**:
   - Basic: $97 (core value)
   - Standard: $197 (2x - most popular)
   - Premium: $497 (2.5x - premium experience)
3. **Highlight middle tier** as "Most Popular" or "Best Value"
4. **Add features progressively** (don't remove from lower tiers)

## Good-Better-Best Examples

### Example 1: Course + Coaching
| Tier | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Basic** | $97 | Video course only |
| **Standard** (MOST POPULAR) | $197 | Course + workbook + templates |
| **Premium** | $497 | Everything + 4 group coaching calls |

### Example 2: Template Products
| Tier | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Starter** | $27 | 10 templates |
| **Pro** (BEST VALUE) | $49 | 50 templates + video tutorials |
| **Complete** | $97 | Everything + quarterly updates |

### Example 3: Membership/Community
| Tier | Price/Year | Features |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Community** | $297 | Forum access + monthly Q&A |
| **Insider** (RECOMMENDED) | $597 | Everything + weekly workshops |
| **VIP** | $1,297 | Everything + 1-on-1 quarterly calls |

## Why Middle Tier Converts Best
- **Price anchoring**: High tier makes middle feel reasonable
- **Feature comparison**: Has most valuable features without premium extras
- **Decision simplification**: Not cheapest (low quality signal) or most expensive
- **Visual emphasis**: Highlighted, enlarged, or badged as "Most Popular"

## Pricing Tier Progression Rules

**Rule 1: Core value in lowest tier**
- Never cripple the base product
- It must deliver transformation/outcome promised
- Upsells should be acceleration, not completion

**Rule 2: 2-3x price increases between tiers**
- Not 1.2x (too small, confusing)
- Not 10x (too big, feels disconnected)
- Sweet spot: 2x to 3x

**Rule 3: Features add progressively**
- Lower tier: Core product
- Middle tier: Core + convenience/speed
- Upper tier: Core + convenience + access/community

- Visual hierarchy (middle tier prominent)

## Feature Differentiation Strategies

### What to Tier:
**Implementation speed:**
- Basic: DIY templates
- Standard: Done-with-you templates + support
- Premium: Done-for-you + 1-on-1 calls

**Access level:**
- Basic: Self-paced course
- Standard: Course + community forum
- Premium: Course + community + group coaching

**Update frequency:**
- Basic: One-time purchase
- Standard: Quarterly updates for 1 year
- Premium: Lifetime updates

**Support:**
- Basic: Email support
- Standard: Priority email + community
- Premium: 1-on-1 support + Slack access

### What NOT to Tier:
- Core transformation/outcome
- Critical features needed for success
- Access to majority of content

## Conversion Optimization

**Visual hierarchy:**
1. Make middle tier larger or elevated
2. Use color contrast (highlight middle)
3. Add "Recommended" badge
4. Show "X% of customers choose this"

**Messaging:**
- Basic: "Get Started"
- Standard: "Most Popular" (social proof)
- Premium: "Best Value" or "Premium Experience"

**Feature listing:**
- List all features for each tier
- Use checkmarks, not text
- Highlight exclusive features per tier
- Keep under 7 features per tier (cognitive load)

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- **Too many tiers** (4+ confuses customers)
- **Tiny price gaps** ($97 → $107 → $117 = confusing)
- **Removing features** from lower tiers (feels like punishment)
- **No visual emphasis** on recommended tier (equal presentation kills anchoring)
- **Complex feature matrices** (keep it simple, under 7 features)

## A/B Testing Strategy
Test these variations:
1. **Price points**: $97/$197/$497 vs $99/$199/$499
2. **Middle tier emphasis**: Badge vs color vs size
3. **Feature count**: 5 features vs 7 features vs 10 features
4. **Naming**: Basic/Standard/Premium vs Starter/Pro/Complete
5. **Annual vs monthly**: Show both, default to annual

## Membership Tier Pricing Example (Annual)
| Tier | Monthly Equivalent | Annual Price | Savings |
|------|-------------------|--------------|---------|
| Community | $29/mo | $297/year | 15% off |
| Insider | $59/mo | $597/year | 15% off |
| VIP | $129/mo | $1,297/year | 16% off |

**Strategy:** Show monthly pricing, offer annual discount, middle tier converts best.
