## Voice Message Best Practices

### 1. Keep It Concise (30 Seconds Max)

**Why:** Attention span is short. Longer = higher drop-off.

**Structure:**
- 0-5 sec: Intro ("Hey [Name], this is [You]")
- 5-20 sec: Value prop (why you're reaching out)
- 20-30 sec: Clear CTA ("Would you be open to a quick 15-min chat?")

**Example:**
> "Hey Sarah, this is Mike from [Company]. I noticed you recently posted about [specific pain point]. We've helped 3 companies in your space reduce [metric] by 40%. Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call to see if there's a fit? Either way, appreciate your time!"

### 2. Ask for Permission Before Sending

**Pattern:** Send text DM first, offer voice message

**Example Text DM:**
> "Hey [Name], I have some specific insights about [their pain point]. Would you be open to a quick voice message explaining? (30 seconds, promise!)"

**Why:**
- Reduces friction
- Primes them to listen
- Shows respect for their time

### 3. Focus on Value, Not Pitch

**Good:** "I noticed X, here's an insight about Y"
**Bad:** "Let me tell you about our amazing product"

**Formula:**
- Reference specific observation about them/their company
- Share insight or pattern you've seen
- Invite conversation (don't hard sell)

## When to Use Voice vs Text

**Use Voice When:**
- High-value target (decision maker)
- Complex offer (needs nuance)
- Want to differentiate from competitors
- Already have some connection/context

**Use Text When:**
- Initial connection request
- Quick follow-up
- Sharing link/resource
- Low-friction ask

**Omnichannel Approach:** Text → Voice → Text (follow-up)

## LinkedIn Cold Outreach Funnel (2026)

**Step 1: Personalized Connection Request**
- Reference mutual connection, post, or company
- 45% acceptance rate if personalized
- No pitch in connection request

**Step 2: Welcome Message (Text)**
- Send within 24 hours of acceptance
- Thank them for connecting
- Ask permission for voice message

**Step 3: Voice Message**
- 30 seconds max
- Value-focused
- Clear CTA

**Step 4: Text Follow-Up (If No Response)**
- 5-7 days later
- Different angle or resource
- Low-pressure

**Expected Results:**
- Connection acceptance: 45%
- Voice message listen rate: 60-70% (estimated)
- Response to voice: 8-9%

## Technical Setup

**How to Send Voice Message on LinkedIn:**
1. Open conversation with connection
2. Click microphone icon in message box
3. Record (30 sec max recommended)
4. Review and send

**Requirements:**
- Must be connected first (can't send to non-connections)
- Desktop or mobile app
- Microphone access enabled

**Tip:** Test audio quality first. Bad audio = instant delete.

## Script Template (30-Second Voice)

**Intro (5 sec):**
"Hey [Name], this is [You] from [Company]."

**Context (10 sec):**
"I saw your post about [specific topic] and noticed you're dealing with [pain point]. We recently helped [similar company] solve this exact issue."

**Value Prop (10 sec):**
"We reduced their [metric] by [number]% using [brief method]. I think there might be a similar opportunity for [their company]."

**CTA (5 sec):**
"Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call to explore if there's a fit? Either way, thanks for your time!"

**Total:** ~30 seconds

## Compliance & Best Practices

**LinkedIn Terms:**
- Don't spam (max 10-15 voice messages/day to be safe)
- Personalize each message
- Respect opt-outs immediately

**Anti-Spam Tips:**
- Rotate between text and voice
- Don't send multiple voice messages to same person
- Quality > quantity

**Safety Limits (2026):**
- Connection requests: 100/week max
- Messages: 150-200/week max (combined text + voice)
- If using automation tools, stay under platform limits

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**DON'T:**
- Send voice message without connecting first
- Ramble for 60+ seconds
- Pitch products in first voice message
- Send same voice to everyone
- Follow up with another voice if they didn't respond to first

**DO:**
- Reference specific details about them
- Keep it under 30 seconds
- Focus on value/insight
- Ask permission when possible
- Follow up with text, not another voice
