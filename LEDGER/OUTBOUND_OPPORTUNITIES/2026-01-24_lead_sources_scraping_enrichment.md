**Pricing:**

### LinkedIn Scraping: Safety Considerations

**Why Third-Party Tools Are Necessary:**
- Sales Navigator doesn't allow CSV/Excel export
- No official API for company data
- Manual export is time-prohibitive at scale

**Safety Best Practices:**
- Use proxies (rotate IPs)
- Simulate human-like behavior (delays, randomization)
- Respect daily activity limits (typically 2,500-15,000/day max)
- Use tools designed to avoid detection

**Risk Without Safety Measures:**
- Account bans
- Temporary restrictions
- Profile flagging

**Recommended Approach:**
- Use tools with built-in safety (Vayne, GiveMeLeads)
- Don't exceed tool's recommended daily limits
- Avoid scraping from same IP repeatedly

## Compliance & Legal Considerations

### Data Privacy Laws

**GDPR (EU):**
- B2B cold email: Generally OK under "legitimate interest"
- B2C cold email: Requires consent
- Right to deletion: Must honor within 30 days
- Data processing records: Keep logs of data sources

**CAN-SPAM (US):**
- Include physical address
- Accurate From/Subject lines
- Unsubscribe mechanism required
- Honor unsubscribe within 10 days

**CCPA (California):**
- Similar to GDPR for California residents
- Right to know what data you have
- Right to deletion

### Tool-Specific Compliance

**LinkedIn Scraping:**
- Violates LinkedIn Terms of Service
- Use at own risk
- Safety tools reduce but don't eliminate risk
- Consider: Is the data available elsewhere legally?

**Email Verification:**
- Generally legal (you're checking deliverability)
- Don't verify lists you purchased (spam trap risk)

**Contact Databases:**
- Apollo, Hunter, RocketReach claim compliance
- They scrape public sources
- You're responsible for how you use the data
