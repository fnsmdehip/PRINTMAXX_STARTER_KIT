### SECTION 2: The Training Program (Pages 16-40)

**Page 16-18: Program Overview**

**Option A: Full Body (3x/week)**
Best for: Complete beginners, maximum time efficiency
- Mon/Wed/Fri: All Big 3

Best for: Intermediate, want more volume

We'll focus on Option A (3x/week full body).

---

**Page 19-25: Session Template (Full Body)**

**Warmup (10 min):**
- 5 min cardio (bike, row, jog)
- Dynamic stretches:
  - Leg swings (10 each)
  - Arm circles (10 each)
  - Hip openers (10 each)
  - Cat-cow (10 reps)
- Empty bar practice (10 reps each: squat, bench, deadlift)

**Main Work (40 min):**

**Exercise 1: Squat**
- Sets: 3
- Reps: 5-8
- Rest: 3 min
- Progression: +5 lbs/week

Form cues:
- Bar on upper back (not neck)
- Chest up, core tight
- Depth: Hip crease below knee
- Drive through heels

**Exercise 2: Bench Press OR Overhead Press** (alternate each session)
- Sets: 3
- Reps: 5-8
- Rest: 3 min
- Progression: +5 lbs/week

Bench form:
- Feet flat, tight core
- Retract shoulder blades
- Bar touches chest, press up

OHP form:
- Feet hip-width
- Bar path vertical
- Lock out overhead

**Exercise 3: Deadlift OR Barbell Row** (alternate each session)
- Sets: 3
- Reps: 5-8
- Rest: 3 min
- Progression: +5 lbs/week

Deadlift form:
- Feet hip-width
- Neutral spine (no rounding)
- Push through floor
- Hinge at hips

Row form:
- Bent over 45°
- Pull to sternum
- Control the negative

**Accessory (optional, 10 min):**
Pick 1-2:
- Pull-ups (3x max reps)
- Dips (3x max reps)
- Planks (3x 60 sec)
- Face pulls (3x 15)

**Cooldown (5 min):**
- Static stretches
- Foam rolling (quads, hamstrings, back)

---

**Page 26-30: Week-by-Week Progression**

**Week 1-2: Form Focus**
- Use light weight (50-60% max)
- Perfect the movement
- End feeling like you could do 3 more reps
- Log all sets/reps/weight

**Week 3-4: Find Your Baseline**
- Test your 5-rep max on each lift
- Use 80% of that for working sets
- Track recovery

**Week 5-12: Linear Progression**
- Add 5 lbs every week you complete all sets
- If you miss reps 2 weeks in a row, deload 10%
- Focus: Consistency > PRs

**Page 36-40: Troubleshooting**

**"I can't squat deep"**
→ Mobility work: Goblet squats, ankle stretches

**"Bench hurts my shoulders"**
→ Form check: Retract scapula, don't flare elbows

**"Deadlifts hurt my back"**
→ Form issue: Film yourself, check neutral spine

**"I'm not gaining strength"**
→ Check: Sleep? Protein? Progressive overload?

**"I missed a week"**
→ Come back, reduce weight 10%, resume

### SECTION 3: Nutrition (Pages 41-55)

**Page 41-43: The Stack Method**

```
Don't count calories.
Stack habits instead.
```

**Habit 1: Protein First**
- Target: 0.8-1g per lb bodyweight
- Every meal starts with protein
- Sources: Chicken, beef, fish, eggs, whey

**Habit 2: Veggies + Carbs**
- Veggies: Fill half the plate
