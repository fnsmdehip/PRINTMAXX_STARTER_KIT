## FTC Testimonial Rules Overview

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) requires that testimonials and endorsements be truthful, not misleading, and properly disclosed.

**Key Principles:**
1. Testimonials must reflect honest opinions
2. Results must be typical OR atypical results must be disclosed
3. Material connections must be disclosed
4. You cannot make claims through testimonials that you could not make directly

---

## What Counts as a Testimonial?

**Testimonials include:**
- Customer reviews and quotes
- Case studies with specific results
- Before/after stories
- Video testimonials
- Social media posts about your product
- Influencer endorsements
- Expert endorsements

**Also applies to:**
- Reposting customer content
- Screenshots of messages/DMs
- Star ratings with comments
- User-generated content you share

---

## Core Requirements

### 1. Truthfulness

**Required:**
- Testimonial must reflect the person's genuine experience
- Claims must be accurate and verifiable
- Person must have actually used the product/service

**Prohibited:**
- Fake testimonials (even if the claims are plausible)
- Testimonials from people who have not used the product
- Exaggerated or embellished results
- Testimonials taken out of context

### 2. Typicality of Results

**Old Rule (No Longer Valid):**
> "Results not typical" is NOT sufficient.

**Current Rule:**

If you feature a testimonial with specific results:

**Option A:** Results are typical
- You can show the testimonial without disclaimer
- You must have evidence that results are generally achievable

**Option B:** Results are not typical
- You MUST disclose what typical results actually are
- Use clear, specific language about expected outcomes

**Example:**

*Testimonial: "I made $10,000 in my first month!"*

If typical results are $500/month:
> **"Results not typical. Most customers earn between $200-$800 in their first month."**

### 3. Material Connections

**Disclose if the person:**
- Received free product/service
- Was paid for the testimonial
- Has a business relationship with you
- Is related to you or an employee
- Received discount or special treatment
- Has equity or financial interest

**Disclosure Examples:**

> "[Name] received a free copy of our course in exchange for their honest review."

> "Paid testimonial"

> "[Name] is an affiliate partner"

## Testimonial Collection Process

### Step 1: Request Permission

Before using any testimonial, get written permission.

**Permission Form Template:**

```
TESTIMONIAL RELEASE FORM

I, [CUSTOMER NAME], grant [COMPANY NAME] permission to use my testimonial
in marketing materials, including but not limited to:
- Website
- Social media
- Advertising
- Email marketing
- Print materials

I confirm that:
[ ] My testimonial reflects my honest opinion and genuine experience
[ ] The results I describe are accurate to the best of my knowledge
[ ] I have actually used [PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME]

Testimonial text:
[TESTIMONIAL]

[ ] I give permission to use my full name
[ ] I give permission to use my first name only
[ ] I give permission to use my photo/video
[ ] I prefer to remain anonymous (first name and last initial only)

Signature: _________________
Date: _________________
Email: _________________
```

### Step 2: Verify the Testimonial

Before publishing:

- [ ] Confirm the person actually purchased/used the product
- [ ] Verify any specific claims or numbers mentioned
- [ ] Check that results align with your records
- [ ] Assess if results are typical or atypical
- [ ] Identify any material connections to disclose

### Step 3: Document Everything

Keep records of:

- Original testimonial text
- Signed permission form
- Verification of purchase/use
- Evidence supporting any claims
- Any modifications made (and approval for changes)

**Retention period:** Keep for [3-7] years after last use.

## Editing Testimonials

### Allowed Edits

You may edit for:
- Spelling and grammar
- Length (with permission)
- Clarity (without changing meaning)
- Removing irrelevant information

**Always get approval for any edits.**

### Prohibited Edits

You may NOT:
- Change the meaning or sentiment
- Add claims the person did not make
- Remove context that changes meaning
- Combine multiple testimonials into one
- Improve results or outcomes mentioned

### Edit Disclosure

If you make substantial edits, consider noting:
> *Edited for length and clarity*

## Specific Scenarios

### Case Studies

Case studies with specific metrics require:

1. **Permission** from the subject
2. **Verification** of all numbers and claims
3. **Typicality disclosure** if results are exceptional
4. **Context** about factors that contributed to success

**Example Structure:**
```
CASE STUDY: [COMPANY/PERSON NAME]

Results: [SPECIFIC OUTCOMES]

Disclosure: [Name] is a [customer since DATE / affiliate partner / etc.].
Their results may not be typical. Factors contributing to their success
include [RELEVANT FACTORS]. See our income disclaimer for more information.
```

### Before/After Claims

For transformation testimonials:

1. Both before and after must be accurate
2. Timeframe must be disclosed
3. Other factors must be mentioned if relevant
4. Typical results must be disclosed if atypical

**Example:**
> "[Name] lost 20 pounds in 3 months using our program. Individual results vary.
> Most participants lose 5-10 pounds in 3 months when following the program
> consistently."

### Expert Endorsements

If an expert endorses your product:

- Disclose any payment or material connection
- Expert must actually use/evaluate the product
- Their expertise must be relevant to the endorsement
- Claims must be within their area of expertise

### Influencer Testimonials

Influencers must disclose:
- #ad, #sponsored, or similar
- Material connection in own words
- Disclosure must be clear and conspicuous

**You are responsible** for ensuring influencers disclose properly.

## What You Cannot Do

### Prohibited Practices

1. **Create fake testimonials**
   - Even if claims are plausible
   - Even using AI-generated personas
   - Even for "illustrative purposes"

2. **Cherry-pick only best results**
   - Without disclosing they are not typical

3. **Use outdated testimonials**
   - If results are no longer achievable
   - If product has changed significantly

4. **Pressure customers**
   - Offering payment/incentives requires disclosure
   - Cannot require positive reviews only

5. **Suppress negative reviews**
   - While featuring only positive ones
   - FTC has taken action against review gating

6. **Make claims via testimonial**
   - That you could not make directly
   - Example: Health claims without evidence

## Disclosure Templates

### Standard Testimonial

```
"[TESTIMONIAL TEXT]"
— [Name], [Location/Title if relevant]

[If material connection exists:]
*[Name] received [describe what they received] in exchange for their honest feedback.*
```

### Results-Based Testimonial

```
"[TESTIMONIAL WITH SPECIFIC RESULTS]"
— [Name]

*Individual results vary. [Name]'s results are [not typical / among our top performers].
Most customers experience [TYPICAL RESULTS]. [Additional context if needed.]*
```

### Paid/Incentivized Testimonial

```
"[TESTIMONIAL TEXT]"
— [Name]

*[Name] was compensated for this testimonial. Their views are their own.*
```

### Affiliate Testimonial

```
"[TESTIMONIAL TEXT]"
— [Name]

*[Name] is an affiliate partner and may earn commissions from purchases made
through their links. Results described are their personal experience.*
```

## Red Flags Checklist

Before publishing a testimonial, check for these issues:

**Testimonial Content:**
- [ ] Claims seem exaggerated or too good to be true
- [ ] Specific numbers that cannot be verified
- [ ] Health or medical claims without evidence
- [ ] Financial results without context
- [ ] Timeframes that seem unrealistic

**Source Issues:**
- [ ] Cannot verify person actually used product
- [ ] Person has undisclosed material connection
- [ ] Testimonial is significantly old
- [ ] Person cannot be contacted to verify

**Compliance Issues:**
- [ ] No written permission on file
- [ ] Testimonial edited without approval
- [ ] No typicality disclosure when needed
- [ ] Material connection not disclosed

## Testimonial Audit

Regularly audit your testimonials:

**Quarterly Review:**
- [ ] All testimonials still have valid permissions
- [ ] Results claimed are still achievable
- [ ] Disclosures are present and visible
- [ ] Typicality data is still accurate
- [ ] No outdated information

**When Product Changes:**
- [ ] Review if existing testimonials still apply
- [ ] Update or remove testimonials for old versions
- [ ] Re-verify claims if significant changes made

## Quick Reference

| Situation | Required Disclosure |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Customer testimonial (no payment) | None, unless results atypical |
| Free product provided | Disclose free product |
| Paid testimonial | Disclose payment |
| Affiliate testimonial | Disclose affiliate relationship |
| Employee testimonial | Disclose employment |
| Results are atypical | Disclose typical results |
| Expert endorsement | Disclose any payment + their credentials |
