## Overview

As AI tools become more prevalent in content creation and marketing, transparency is increasingly important and may be legally required in some jurisdictions.

This document covers disclosure requirements for:
- AI-generated content (text, images, video)
- AI influencers and personas
- AI-assisted products and services
- AI in advertising and marketing

---

## AI-Generated Content

### When to Disclose

**Disclose AI involvement when:**

- Content is primarily generated by AI
- AI images are used that could be mistaken as real photos
- AI video or deepfakes are used
- AI voices are used in audio content
- AI chatbots interact with customers
- AI is used to create personalized recommendations
- Content that appears to be from a human is AI-generated

### Disclosure Formats

**For Written Content:**

> **AI Disclosure:** This [article/post/email] was [created with / assisted by / generated using] AI tools. A human reviewed and edited the final version.

> **Note:** AI was used to help draft this content.

**For Images:**

> **AI-Generated Image:** This image was created using AI.

> *Created with [AI Tool Name]*

**For Video:**

> **AI Notice:** This video contains AI-generated [elements/visuals/voice].

> Verbally: "Just so you know, this video uses AI-generated [describe what]."

**For Audio:**

> **AI Voice Notice:** This audio uses AI-generated voice technology.

### Where to Place Disclosures

| Content Type | Placement |
|--------------|-----------|
| Articles/blog posts | Top of post or footer |
| Social media posts | In post text or first comment |
| Images | Caption or watermark |
| Videos | On-screen text at start and/or end |
| Podcasts | Verbal at start |
| Emails | Header or footer |
| Chatbots | At start of conversation |

## AI Influencers and Personas

### Virtual Influencers

If you create or use virtual/AI influencers:

**Required Disclosure:**
- Clearly identify the persona as AI/virtual/computer-generated
- Do not represent AI personas as real humans
- Include disclosure in bio/about section
- Disclose in sponsored content

**Disclosure Example:**

Bio:
> [Name] is a virtual influencer created by [COMPANY NAME]. [He/She/They] is not a real person.

In posts:
> Virtual creator. AI-generated content.

### AI Chatbots

**When chatbots could be mistaken for humans:**

Opening message:
> Hi! I'm [Name], an AI assistant for [COMPANY NAME]. I'm here to help answer your questions. For complex issues, I can connect you with a human team member.

> Just so you know, you're chatting with an AI, not a human. How can I help you today?

**Do NOT:**
- Claim chatbots are human
- Design chatbots to deceive users about their nature
- Use human names without AI disclosure
- Imply emotional connections that AI cannot have

## AI in Products and Services

### AI-Assisted Products

If your product uses AI:

**On sales page:**
> **How It Works:** [PRODUCT NAME] uses artificial intelligence to [describe function]. AI analyzes [input] and provides [output]. Results are generated by algorithms, not humans.

**In terms of service:**
> Our service uses AI technology to [describe]. AI-generated outputs should be reviewed for accuracy. We do not guarantee AI outputs are error-free.

### AI Limitations Disclosure

When AI could make errors:

> **AI Limitations:** This tool uses AI which may occasionally produce inaccurate results. Always verify important information. Not a substitute for professional [legal/medical/financial] advice.

### Training Data Disclosure

If relevant:
> Our AI was trained on [describe data sources]. It may reflect biases present in training data.

## AI in Advertising

### FTC Guidelines for AI in Ads

**Current FTC position (subject to change):**

1. Ads cannot use AI to make deceptive claims
2. AI-generated testimonials may violate FTC rules
3. Deepfakes in ads may be deceptive
4. Materially AI-generated content may require disclosure

### AI-Generated Ad Content

**Disclosure needed when:**
- Images show results that are AI-generated (not real)
- Testimonials are AI-generated or simulated
- Before/after images are AI-enhanced
- Voices impersonate real people
- Celebrities' likenesses are used without permission

**Disclosure format:**
> This advertisement contains AI-generated imagery.

> Simulated results. Actual outcomes may vary.

### AI Targeting Disclosure

If using AI for ad targeting:
> We use automated systems to help determine which content you see.

## Jurisdictional Requirements

### United States

**Current federal rules:**
- FTC: AI cannot be used to deceive consumers
- Deepfakes: Some states have specific laws
- California: Bot disclosure laws for certain contexts
- Evolving legislation at state and federal level

### European Union

**EU AI Act (when fully effective):**
- Requires disclosure for certain AI systems
- High-risk AI systems have specific requirements
- Emotion recognition and biometric systems regulated
- Deepfakes must be disclosed

**Check current status of EU AI Act implementation.**

### Other Jurisdictions

Requirements vary. Check local laws for:
- Canada
- UK
- Australia
- Your specific target markets
