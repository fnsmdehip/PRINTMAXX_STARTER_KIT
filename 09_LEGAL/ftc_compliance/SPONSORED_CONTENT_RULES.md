## What Is Sponsored Content?

Sponsored content includes any material where you receive something of value in exchange for creating or sharing content.

**Examples:**

- Paid posts or articles
- Free products received for review
- Affiliate commissions
- Brand partnerships
- Gifted items
- Event attendance (travel, tickets)
- Exclusive access or early release
- Discounts or special treatment
- Employment relationships

---

## FTC Requirements

### Core Principle

The FTC requires that **material connections** between endorsers and advertisers be **clearly and conspicuously** disclosed.

**Material connection:** Any relationship that might affect the credibility of an endorsement.

### What "Clear and Conspicuous" Means

**Clear:**
- Plain language, easy to understand
- Not buried in hashtags or long text
- Not in fine print
- Cannot be missed

**Conspicuous:**
- Visible before engagement with the content
- Same format as the content (visual for visual, audio for audio)
- Not requiring clicks, scrolls, or additional actions to see

## Disclosure Requirements by Platform

### Instagram

**Feed Posts:**
- Disclosure in the caption, before "more" cutoff
- First three lines visible
- Not buried in hashtags
- Use: #ad, #sponsored, "Paid partnership with [brand]"
- Platform's "Paid Partnership" tool is helpful but may not be sufficient alone

**Stories:**
- Text overlay saying "Ad" or "Sponsored"
- Superimposed text must be readable (size, duration, contrast)
- Must appear long enough to read
- Cannot rely on swipe-up link alone

**Reels:**
- Disclosure in video and caption
- On-screen text at beginning
- Verbal disclosure if speaking

**Example Disclosure:**
> #ad | This post is sponsored by [BRAND NAME]. I received [product/payment] in exchange for sharing my honest opinion.

### TikTok

**Requirements:**
- Disclosure visible in the video itself
- Text overlay at the start
- Verbal disclosure within first few seconds
- Caption disclosure alone may not be sufficient
- Use TikTok's branded content toggle

**Example:**
> "This video is sponsored by [BRAND]" - spoken at start
> Text overlay: "#ad" or "Sponsored"

### YouTube

**Short-Form (Shorts):**
- On-screen disclosure at start
- Verbal disclosure
- Description disclosure

**Long-Form Videos:**
- Verbal disclosure at the beginning (within first 30 seconds)
- On-screen text/graphic
- Use YouTube's "includes paid promotion" checkbox
- Description disclosure

**Example Script:**
> "Before we start, I want to let you know that [BRAND] sponsored this video. They sent me [product] to try, but all opinions are my own."

### X (Twitter)

**Requirements:**
- Disclosure in the tweet itself
- Not just a hashtag at the end of a long thread
- Clear language like #ad, #sponsored

**Example:**
> #ad Trying out [PRODUCT] from [BRAND]. [Content]

### Facebook

**Requirements:**
- Use the Branded Content tool when available
- Disclosure in post text
- Visible without clicking "see more"

### Podcasts

**Requirements:**
- Verbal disclosure at or near the beginning
- Repeat disclosure if sponsor mentioned later
- Written disclosure in show notes

**Example Script:**
> "This episode is brought to you by [BRAND]. [Brief description]. They're sponsoring today's show, but my opinions are my own."

### Blog Posts / Articles

**Requirements:**
- Disclosure at the beginning of the article
- Clear heading or highlighted box
- Before any product discussion or links

**Example:**
```
---
**Disclosure:** This post is sponsored by [BRAND]. I received [compensation/product]
in exchange for this review. All opinions expressed are my own.
---
```

### Email Marketing

**Requirements:**
- Disclosure near the beginning of the email
- Before any sponsored content or links
- Clear labeling

**Example:**
> **Sponsor Spotlight:** Today's newsletter is brought to you by [BRAND].

## Disclosure Language Options

### Paid Sponsorship

**Acceptable:**
- "#ad"
- "#sponsored"
- "Paid partnership with [BRAND]"
- "Sponsored by [BRAND]"
- "[BRAND] paid me to create this content"
- "Ad: [content]"

**NOT Acceptable:**
- "#sp" (too unclear)
- "Thanks to [BRAND]" alone (doesn't indicate payment)
- "#partner" alone (ambiguous)
- Disclosure only in tags/metadata

### Free Products / Gifts

**Acceptable:**
- "Gifted"
- "[BRAND] sent me this product"
- "PR sample"
- "I received this product for free in exchange for my honest review"

**NOT Acceptable:**
- No disclosure at all
- Only "#collab" (ambiguous)

### Affiliate Links

**Acceptable:**
- "This post contains affiliate links"
- "I may earn a commission if you purchase through my link"
- "(affiliate link)"

### Employee / Ambassador

**Acceptable:**
- "I work for [BRAND]"
- "[BRAND] Ambassador"
- "Employee of [BRAND]"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Placement Issues

**Wrong:**
- Disclosure buried in hashtags
- Disclosure at the end of a long caption
- Disclosure requiring "see more" click
- Disclosure in video description only (not the video)
- Disclosure in bio only

**Right:**
- Disclosure at the beginning
- Disclosure visible without scrolling or clicking
- Disclosure in the same format as content (video = verbal + visual)

### Language Issues

**Wrong:**
- Ambiguous terms only: "#partner," "#collab," "thanks to"
- Industry jargon: "#spon," "#sp"
- Disclosure that could be missed
- Disclosure in a different language than the content

**Right:**
- Clear terms: "#ad," "#sponsored," "paid partnership"
- Explanation when needed: "I received this product for free"

### Assuming Audience Knowledge

**Wrong assumption:** "Everyone knows I get free products"
**Reality:** Each piece of content needs its own disclosure

**Wrong assumption:** "My followers know what #sp means"
**Reality:** Use universally understood terms

## Templates by Scenario

### Paid Brand Deal

**Instagram/Facebook:**
> #ad | Partnered with [BRAND] on this post. [Content]

**YouTube intro:**
> "Today's video is sponsored by [BRAND]. They [brief description of product/service]. I'll share my thoughts after trying it, and yes, they paid me to make this video, but all opinions are my own."

**Blog:**
```
**Sponsored Post**

This article is sponsored by [BRAND]. I was compensated to share my experience
with [PRODUCT]. All opinions expressed are my own.
```

### Product Review (Free Product)

**Instagram:**
> [BRAND] sent me [PRODUCT] to try! #gifted Here's my honest review...

**YouTube:**
> "Full disclosure: [BRAND] sent me this [PRODUCT] for free. They didn't pay me and didn't require me to say anything positive. Here's what I actually think..."

**Blog:**
```
**Disclosure:** I received [PRODUCT] free from [BRAND] for review purposes.
I was not required to post or write a positive review. All opinions are my own.
```

### Affiliate Content

**Social media:**
> Found the perfect [product category]! Link in bio (affiliate link - I may earn a small commission)

**Blog post intro:**
```
**Affiliate Disclosure:** This post contains affiliate links. If you make a purchase
through these links, I may earn a commission at no additional cost to you.
See my [full disclosure](/affiliate-disclosure).
```

### Brand Ambassador

**Instagram bio:**
> [BRAND] Ambassador

**In posts:**
> #brandambassador #ad | As a [BRAND] ambassador, I get to share [content]...

### Event Coverage

**For events where brand paid travel/attendance:**
> [BRAND] invited me to attend [EVENT] and covered my travel expenses. #ad

## Influencer Agreements

### What to Include in Contracts

If you hire influencers, your contracts should specify:

1. **Disclosure requirements:**
   > Influencer agrees to include clear disclosure of the sponsored relationship in all content. Disclosure must comply with FTC guidelines and appear [specify placement: at beginning of post, in video verbally and visually, etc.].

2. **Specific language:**
   > Acceptable disclosure language includes: [#ad, #sponsored, "Paid partnership with [BRAND]"]. Influencer may not use ambiguous terms like "#partner" or "#collab" alone.

3. **Review rights:**
   > [BRAND] may review content before posting to ensure compliance with disclosure requirements.

4. **Responsibility:**
   > Influencer is responsible for maintaining disclosures and may be liable for FTC violations.

### Sample Contract Clause

```
DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS

Influencer agrees to clearly and conspicuously disclose the sponsored nature
of all Content created under this Agreement. Disclosures must:

a) Appear at the beginning of the content (before any promotional content)
b) Use clear language such as "#ad," "Sponsored by [BRAND]," or "Paid partnership"
c) Be visible without scrolling, clicking, or additional action
d) Comply with all applicable FTC guidelines and platform policies
e) Be in the same language as the content

For video content, disclosures must be both verbal (spoken within the first
30 seconds) and visual (on-screen text).

Failure to comply with disclosure requirements may result in content removal
and [specify consequences].
```

## Monitoring and Enforcement

### If You're a Brand

**Monitor sponsored content for:**
- [ ] Proper disclosure present
- [ ] Disclosure placement (beginning, visible)
- [ ] Disclosure language (clear, unambiguous)
- [ ] Platform-specific requirements met

**Response to violations:**
1. Contact influencer to request correction
2. Document the issue
3. If not corrected, consider removing content
4. Note for future partnership decisions

### If You're a Creator

**Self-audit checklist:**
- [ ] Did I receive anything of value?
- [ ] Is my disclosure clear?
- [ ] Is my disclosure visible immediately?
- [ ] Would a viewer/reader understand the nature of the relationship?
- [ ] Did I disclose in all formats (video + caption, etc.)?

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

### FTC Actions

The FTC can take action against:
- Brands that don't ensure influencer compliance
- Influencers who fail to disclose
- Agencies that arrange non-compliant deals

**Potential consequences:**
- Warning letters
- Consent orders
- Civil penalties (up to $50,000+ per violation)
- Required compliance monitoring
- Public disclosure of violations

### Platform Consequences

Platforms may:
- Remove non-compliant content
- Reduce reach/shadowban
- Suspend or terminate accounts
- Deny monetization
