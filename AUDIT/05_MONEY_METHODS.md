### 3. MM046 - Notion Templates -- Viability: 7/10

### 5. MM002 - Info Products -- Viability: 7/10

## Critical Gaps

### 1. Execution Gap (Severity: 10/10)
70 methods documented. 0 methods generating revenue. The project has more documentation about making money than most small businesses have total documentation. The ratio of planning to execution is approximately infinity.

### 2. Account Gap (Severity: 10/10)
Surge.sh (free static hosting) is the only platform account. Stripe, Gumroad, Fiverr, Upwork, Etsy, Apple Developer, email providers, social media accounts: all zero. This blocks literally everything.

### 3. Coverage Gap (Severity: 7/10)
40 of 70 methods (57%) have no dedicated directory, no playbook, and no executable assets. They exist only as rows in a CSV. Having a method ID without execution infrastructure is not a method.

### 4. Revenue Model Validation Gap (Severity: 8/10)
Every revenue estimate in the master CSV is theoretical. None have been validated by even $1 of actual revenue. The $68K/mo newsletter ceiling and $50K/mo app factory ceiling are cited from others' results, not the project's.

### 5. Method Sprawl (Severity: 7/10)
70 methods for a solo operator with $0 revenue is 65 too many. Focus on 5 and execute them before adding more. Every new method added is another thing competing for attention without generating returns.
