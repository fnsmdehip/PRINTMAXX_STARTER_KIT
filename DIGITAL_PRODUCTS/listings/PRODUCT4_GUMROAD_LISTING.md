## Full Description

what you get:

667 lines. 35 pages. the entire business model documented from finding your first creator to running a clipping network.

**what's inside:**

**the clipping business model**
- why creators need clippers (4-8 hrs raw content, no time to clip)
- the value proposition: 10-50x their content output for $500-$2K/mo
- your actual job: find moments, edit clips, distribute across platforms
- time per client: 5-10 hours/week at start, 2-3 hours with systems
- solo operator: 3-5 clients = $1,500-$10K/month
- network operator: 10-30 clients, team of clippers, $5K-$30K/month

**finding creators who need clippers**
- Twitch streamers doing 4+ hours/day with low YouTube presence
- YouTube creators who don't post Shorts
- podcast hosts with no clip strategy
- where to find them (Twitch directory, SullyGnome, Social Blade)
- red flags: creators who already have a clipper, low engagement, inconsistent streaming

**pricing and packaging**
- starter package: $500/month (10 clips/week, 2 platforms)
- growth package: $1,000/month (25 clips/week, 4 platforms)
- premium package: $2,000/month (50 clips/week, 6 platforms + custom thumbnails)
- per-clip pricing for smaller creators: $15-$30/clip
- retainer vs per-clip: when to offer which

**10x distribution without ads**
- one stream = 20-50 potential clips
- each clip posted to: TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, X, Facebook Reels, LinkedIn (if B2B)
- 25 clips x 6 platforms = 150 content pieces per week per creator
- scheduling: batch edit Monday, schedule Tuesday, monitor rest of week
- hashtag and description optimization per platform

**the network operator model**
- you: sales, client management, quality control
- clippers: editing, captioning, posting
- hiring clippers: Fiverr, OnlineJobs.ph, Discord communities
- clipper pay: $3-$5/hr (Philippines, Eastern Europe)
- your margin: 40-60% per client
- example: 15 clients at $1K avg, 8 clippers at $400/mo = $15K rev - $3.2K cost = $11.8K profit
- quality control: review 20% of clips before posting, rating system, bonuses for top performers

**revenue math**
- solo operator (month 1-3): 2-3 clients, $1K-$3K/month, 15-25 hrs/week
- solo operator (month 3-6): 4-5 clients, $3K-$5K/month, 25-35 hrs/week
- network operator (month 6-12): 10-15 clients, $8K-$15K/month, 15-20 hrs/week (delegated)
- cost breakdown: tools ($50-$150/mo), clippers ($3-$5/hr), your time
- break-even: first client covers tool costs

**cold outreach templates**
- DM template for Twitch streamers (Discord)
- email template for YouTube creators
- LinkedIn template for podcast hosts
- follow-up sequence (3 messages, 5-day spacing)
- the free audit hook: "i found 12 clips in your last stream. here's 3 of them edited."
- conversion rate benchmark: 5-10% reply rate, 20-30% of replies to call

**tool stack**
- editing: CapCut (free), DaVinci Resolve (free), Premiere Pro ($22/mo)
- captions: CapCut auto-captions (free) or Opus Clip ($19/mo for auto-clipping)
- scheduling: Buffer ($6/mo/channel), Publer ($12/mo), or Later ($25/mo)
- storage: Google Drive (free 15GB) or Dropbox ($12/mo)
- project management: Notion (free) or Trello (free)
- total cost: $0-$75/month depending on tier

**VA hiring and training**
- where to hire: OnlineJobs.ph ($3-$5/hr), Fiverr ($5-$10/task), Discord clipping communities
- job post template (copy-paste ready)
- training document template (send to every new clipper)
- quality standards checklist (hook in first 1s, captions, aspect ratio, audio levels)
- payment setup: Wise for international transfers (low fees)
- management: weekly 1-on-1, clip review queue, performance tracking spreadsheet

**bonus: the free audit hook**
the single best tactic for landing your first client. find a streamer. watch their last VOD. clip 3 moments. edit them. send the finished clips as a DM. "found these in your last stream. this is what i do."

conversion rate on the free audit hook: 25-40% to a call. because you already did the work.
