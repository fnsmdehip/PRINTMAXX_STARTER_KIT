you have 23 app ideas in your notes app. you started building 4 of them. you shipped 1 six months ago and haven't checked the analytics since. you have no idea which apps are worth your time and which are dead weight.

App Factory fixes this. one workspace. every app tracked from idea to revenue. with built-in decision logic that tells you when to kill a loser and when to double down on a winner.

what you get:

**idea vault**
- dump every app idea with structured fields: problem, audience, monetization model, estimated build time, competition level
- idea scoring formula: (market size x monetization potential x build speed) / competition
- "top ideas" view sorted by score
- idea-to-app conversion: promote an idea to active build with one click
- tag by category: SaaS, mobile app, Chrome extension, API, marketplace, tool
- "graveyard" for ideas you evaluated and passed on (with reasons, so you don't re-evaluate them)

**build tracker**
- 8-stage pipeline: idea > spec > design > build > test > ship > grow > evaluate
- per-app fields: tech stack, repo link, deploy URL, build start date, ship date, hours logged
- daily build log: what you worked on today, blockers, next steps
- milestone tracking: MVP features checklist per app
- dependency tracker: what's blocking this app from shipping
- time tracking: hours per app, so you know your true cost

**deployment checklist**
- pre-launch: domain, hosting, SSL, analytics, error tracking, legal (privacy policy, terms)
- launch day: submit to stores, post on Product Hunt/Indie Hackers/X, email list announcement
- post-launch: monitor errors, respond to feedback, first iteration
- platform-specific checklists: App Store, Google Play, Chrome Web Store, web deployment
- each checklist item has a "done" toggle and notes field

**ASO tracker (App Store Optimization)**
- keyword tracking per app: target keywords, current rank, search volume
- competitor apps: what they rank for, their ratings, their pricing
- screenshot and icon spec tracking
- A/B test log for titles, descriptions, screenshots
- rating/review monitoring with response templates

**revenue dashboard**
- per-app: MRR, total revenue, LTV, churn rate, daily active users
- portfolio view: all apps side by side, sorted by revenue
- monthly trend charts (formula-driven, not manual)
- revenue by monetization type: subscription, one-time, ads, freemium, in-app purchases
- total portfolio MRR with month-over-month growth rate
- cost tracking per app: hosting, tools, marketing spend
- profit margin per app: revenue minus costs

**kill/double-down triggers (this is the differentiator)**

most app builders hold onto dead apps for months because they "might still work." and they ignore winners because they're too busy building the next thing.

built-in decision triggers:

KILL signals (any 2 = kill the app):
- less than $100 MRR after 60 days live
- less than 50 DAU after 30 days
- negative growth for 3 consecutive weeks
- customer acquisition cost > lifetime value
- you haven't touched it in 2 weeks (if you're not excited, users won't be either)

DOUBLE DOWN signals (any 2 = pour resources in):
- MRR growing 20%+ month-over-month at $500+
- organic user growth without paid acquisition
- users asking for features (demand signal)
- retention rate above 40% at 30 days
- positive unit economics (LTV > 3x CAC)

each app has a "decision status" field: BUILD, GROW, EVALUATE, KILL, DOUBLE DOWN
- formula auto-calculates based on your numbers
- forces you to make decisions instead of letting apps rot

**why this works:**
- most indie hackers build 1 app, launch it, and pray. the portfolio approach (10 apps, 90 days) means your odds of hitting a winner are 10x higher.
- the kill triggers prevent you from wasting 6 months on an app making $12/month.
- the double-down triggers prevent you from ignoring your winners.
- the idea vault prevents you from starting apps that score low before you've evaluated them.
- time tracking shows you which apps eat your hours vs which apps earn your money.

setup: 15 minutes. duplicate, add your current apps and ideas, start tracking.

$22. the price of a mediocre app purchase. except this one helps you build 10 that generate revenue.
