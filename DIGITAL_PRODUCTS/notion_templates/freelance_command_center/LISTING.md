your freelance business right now: client info in email threads you can't find. invoices in a spreadsheet you opened twice. proposals you copy-paste from old ones and forget to change the client name. project deadlines in your head. no idea what your actual hourly rate was last month.

Freelance Command Center puts your entire freelance operation in one workspace.

what you get:

**gig tracker**
- 6-stage pipeline: Lead > Proposal Sent > Negotiating > Active > Delivered > Paid
- kanban view: drag gigs between stages
- per-gig fields: client, project type, quoted price, actual hours, hourly rate, deadline, platform/source
- gig history: every completed gig with client, revenue, and rating
- "pending payment" filter: gigs delivered but not yet paid (the ones you forget to chase)
- monthly/quarterly revenue rollups
- gig source tracking: where your best gigs come from (Upwork, referral, cold outreach, X, LinkedIn)

**proposal templates**
- 5 proposal templates by project type:
  - web development / design
  - content / copywriting
  - consulting / strategy
  - marketing / social media
  - general services
- each template: intro, scope of work, deliverables, timeline, pricing, terms
- pricing section with 3-tier options (basic, standard, premium)
- copy-paste ready with [CLIENT NAME] and [PROJECT] fields
- proposal-to-gig conversion: accepted proposals auto-create gig entries
- win rate tracking: proposals sent vs proposals accepted

**client onboarding**
- onboarding checklist template for new clients:
  - welcome message sent
  - contract/agreement signed
  - payment method confirmed
  - project brief received
  - access/credentials collected
  - kickoff call scheduled
  - project timeline shared
- client info database: contact details, communication preferences, timezone, decision maker
- past project history per client
- client satisfaction scoring (after each project)
- "repeat client" flag with total lifetime value

**invoice tracker**
- per-invoice: client, amount, date sent, date due, date paid, status (draft, sent, overdue, paid)
- "overdue invoices" view: sorted by days overdue
- monthly revenue summary: invoiced vs received vs outstanding
- payment method tracking: which clients pay fast, which ones drag
- auto-calculated: average days to payment per client
- late payment follow-up templates (polite nudge, firm reminder, final notice)

**portfolio showcase**
- project portfolio database for case studies
- per-project: title, client (optional), description, results, images/links, testimonials
- "public portfolio" view: what you'd show to prospective clients
- category tags for filtering by project type
- "featured" flag for your best work
- testimonial collection: client quotes linked to projects

**freelance analytics**
- monthly revenue trend
- average project value
- actual hourly rate (revenue / hours worked, not quoted rate)
- utilization rate: billable hours / available hours
- client concentration risk: % of revenue from top client (if >50%, you're one email from broke)
- gig source ROI: which lead sources produce the most revenue per hour spent sourcing
- best month / worst month comparison

**why this and not a spreadsheet:**
- spreadsheets don't have kanban views for your pipeline
- spreadsheets don't auto-link clients to invoices to projects
- spreadsheets don't calculate your real hourly rate across all projects
- spreadsheets don't flag when one client is 60% of your income
- this does. for $15. one time.

**why this and not a SaaS tool:**
- HoneyBook: $16/month. Dubsado: $20/month. And.co: $18/month.
- this is $15 once. same features. you own the data. no monthly bleeding.

setup: 10 minutes. duplicate, add your current clients and active gigs, start tracking.

$15. less than one hour of your billable rate.
