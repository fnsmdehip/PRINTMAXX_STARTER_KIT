## Competitor Analysis (Top 5 in Category)

### 1. Ultimate Brain by Thomas Frank ($129)
- 60K+ copies sold. The gorilla in the room.
- Strengths: Massive brand, GTD methodology baked in, YouTube tutorial ecosystem
- Weaknesses: Overkill for solopreneurs. Designed for personal productivity, not business operations. No revenue tracking. No CRM. $129 is steep for someone at $0 revenue.
- Our edge: Business-first. Revenue tracker, client CRM, and content calendar are front and center, not buried under personal task management.

### 2. Notionery Solopreneur OS ($39)
- Solid template, ~2K copies
- Strengths: Clean design, good database relations
- Weaknesses: Weak content calendar (no multi-platform support), no habit tracker, generic project views
- Our edge: 6-platform content calendar with repurposing tracker. Habit tracker tied to business outcomes, not just personal habits.

### 3. Gridfiti Business Hub ($19)
- High volume, lower price
- Strengths: Cheap, clean, good for beginners
- Weaknesses: Too simple. No formulas, no automations, no cross-database relations. Feels like a starter template.
- Our edge: Real formulas (revenue calculations, goal progress %), proper relations between databases, views that actually help you make decisions.

### 4. Easlo Second Brain Business ($49)
- Strong brand in Notion template space
- Strengths: Beautiful design, good documentation, active community
- Weaknesses: "Second brain" focus means lots of knowledge management, less operational. No weekly review structure. CRM is basic.
- Our edge: Operations-first. Weekly review template that forces you to look at numbers, not just journal about feelings.

### 5. IndieKit Solopreneur Dashboard ($24)
- Closest direct competitor
- Strengths: Price point similar, targets same audience
- Weaknesses: No content calendar, no habit tracker, limited views, basic CRM
- Our edge: More complete. 7 modules vs their 4. Content calendar alone justifies the $3 price difference.
