Payments & Monetization

In-app purchases use Apple's IAP system (not Stripe, PayPal, or external links)

Rule 3.1.1 — digital goods sold inside the app MUST use IAP. Stripe is only allowed for physical goods, services, or B2B. Violations cause instant rejection.

Paywall does not block all app functionality for free users

Apple requires minimum useful functionality at zero cost. An app that shows a paywall immediately on launch with no usable free features gets rejected under Rule 3.1.2.

Subscription terms are clearly displayed (price, duration, renewal)

Must be visible before purchase. Monthly/annual price and auto-renew disclosure required per Apple's subscription guidelines.

No external links to buy premium (no "Visit our website to upgrade")

Directing users to an external site to avoid Apple's cut is an immediate 3.1.3 violation. All upgrade CTAs must go through IAP.

Content & Functionality

No placeholder text, dummy content, or "TODO" strings visible in the UI

Reviewers tap through every screen. Placeholder copy or lorem ipsum is a 2.1 completeness rejection.

App is meaningfully different from other apps in the same category

Guideline 4.3 — duplicate apps and template apps with zero differentiation are rejected at scale in 2026. Especially religious streak apps and generic AI wrappers.

All features described in App Store listing actually work

Reviewers test against your description. "AI-powered" with a random number generator gets a 2.1 rejection.

No exaggerated health, medical, or performance claims

"Medical grade" or "clinically proven" require peer-reviewed citations. Phone camera PPG cannot match dedicated medical equipment.

Privacy & Permissions

Privacy policy URL exists, resolves, and covers the app's actual data usage

Broken link = auto-reject. Generic text without specifics gets rejected on subsequent review. URL must be in App Store Connect AND in the app itself.

Each permission request has a specific purpose string (camera, mic, contacts, location)

"For a better experience" is rejected. Must say specifically why: "Camera is used to scan nutrition labels."

ITSAppUsesNonExemptEncryption is set in Info.plist

Missing this flag causes App Store Connect submission failure before the app even reaches review. Set to NO if you only use standard HTTPS/SSL.

Privacy nutrition labels accurately reflect data collected

Overstating or understating data collection triggers review flags, especially post-ATT enforcement in 2024+.

Technical Quality

App doesn't crash on launch on a clean device (no pre-existing data)

Reviewers use fresh devices. If your app assumes existing localStorage/AsyncStorage data and crashes without it, that's an instant 2.1 crash rejection.

All navigation flows complete (no dead ends, back button works everywhere)

Every non-root screen must have a way back. Tapping into a screen with no exit = trap = 2.1 rejection.

Bundle ID is unique and not previously associated with a rejected submission

Reusing a bundle ID from a previously rejected app carries rejection history. Use a new bundle ID for major resubmissions.

App name doesn't conflict with existing popular apps (App Store search test)

Similar names to top apps trigger trademark/confusion rejections. Search your exact app name before finalizing.

App Store Listing

Screenshots show actual app UI (not stock images or mockups that don't match)

Screenshots must accurately represent what reviewers see. Fancy mockups that don't match the real UI = 2.3 rejection.

App description doesn't mention competitor names or make comparative claims

"Better than Duolingo" or "Like Headspace but free" in the description is a rejection risk under accurate metadata guidelines.

Age rating is correct and content matches the rating selected

Selecting 4+ for an app with mature content causes immediate rejection when reviewers encounter that content.

No references to other platforms (Android, Google Play, "available on web") in the UI

"Also on Android" buttons or Google Play badges inside the iOS app = 3.2.1 rejection.

Onboarding & User Experience

Sign in with Apple is implemented if you offer any other social login

Rule 4.8 — if you offer "Login with Google" or "Login with Facebook," Sign in with Apple is mandatory. No exceptions.

Users can delete their account from within the app

Required since June 2023. Any app with account creation must offer in-app account deletion that actually deletes data.

App works on the oldest iOS version in the minimum deployment target

If your app.json says iOS 15 but you use iOS 16+ APIs without version guards, it crashes for a subset of users Apple may test.

Restore Purchases button exists and actually works

Rule 3.1.2 — apps with subscriptions MUST include a visible Restore Purchases button. Fake restore flows that do nothing = rejection.
