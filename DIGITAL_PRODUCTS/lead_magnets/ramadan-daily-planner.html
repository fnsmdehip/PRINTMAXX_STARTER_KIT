suhoor strategy

  • eat slow-digesting carbs: oats, sweet potato, whole grain bread. they hold you 12+ hours.
  • 2 glasses of water minimum. add electrolytes if you're in a hot climate or exercising.
  • set your alarm 25 minutes before fajr, not 5. rushed suhoor means a worse fast.
  • protein + fat slows glucose absorption. eggs + cheese + whole grain beats cereal.
  • avoid salty foods. they pull water out of cells and you'll feel thirsty by noon.

staying productive while fasting

  • schedule deep work 2-3 hours after fajr. energy peaks here before hunger hits hard.
  • 2pm to 4pm is the danger zone. use it for admin, emails, calls. not creative work.
  • naps are sunnah. 20 minutes after dhuhr makes the rest of the day manageable.
  • caffeine withdrawal hits days 1-3. wean down the week before Ramadan starts.
  • break large tasks into 25-minute blocks. fasting reduces working memory, smaller chunks help.
  • keep a notepad nearby. capture thoughts, then return to the task. don't chase ideas mid-fast.

quran completion plan

  • the Quran is 604 pages. 20 pages a day = full completion by day 30.
  • split it: 5 after fajr, 5 after dhuhr, 5 after asr, 5 after taraweeh.
  • if you miss a day, don't try to double-up next day. add 2-3 pages across the following week instead.
  • listening while commuting counts. follow along with audio for passive progress.
  • understanding one ayah deeply beats reading 20 pages you don't process.

iftar done right

  • break fast with 1-3 dates and water. simple sugar signals the body to start digestion.
  • wait 10-15 minutes before the main meal. your stomach shrinks during the fast. give it time.
  • avoid deep-fried foods right at iftar. they sit heavy and wreck taraweeh concentration.
  • soup or broth before the meal helps hydration and primes the stomach for solid food.
  • stop at 2/3 full. the sunnah is 1/3 food, 1/3 water, 1/3 air. it's physiologically correct.

the last 10 nights

  • days 21-30 are the high-value nights. laylatul qadr is worth 1,000 months of worship.
  • odd nights (21, 23, 25, 27, 29) are most likely. if you can only go all-in on one, go on the 27th.
  • plan now: request PTO, prep easy batch meals, tell your family your intentions.
  • sleep 6pm to midnight. pray midnight to fajr. nap after fajr. repeat.
  • simple duas repeated with full attention beat elaborate ones you don't fully understand.
