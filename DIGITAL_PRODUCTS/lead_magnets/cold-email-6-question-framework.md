# Cold Email 6-Question Framework
## The exact method behind $22k/mo service businesses built with no ads, no followers, no content

---

## What this is

6 questions. Answer all 6 in under 100 words. That's your cold email.

Most people write emails about themselves. This framework forces you to write emails about the prospect's problem. That's the only difference between a 0.3% reply rate and a 12% reply rate.

---

## The 6 Questions (answer each in 1-2 sentences max)

**Q1: What do you do?**
State the outcome you deliver, not the service you provide.

- BAD: "I'm a web developer who builds React apps."
- GOOD: "I build tools that cut manual reporting time by 80%."

**Q2: Who is it for?**
Name the exact buyer. Job title + company type + revenue range.

- BAD: "I work with businesses."
- GOOD: "I work with operations managers at logistics companies doing $5M-$50M in revenue."

**Q3: How does it work?**
One sentence. No jargon. No process diagrams.

- BAD: "Through our proprietary 12-step methodology..."
- GOOD: "I audit your current workflow, identify the 3 biggest time drains, and automate them in 2 weeks."

**Q4: What problem does it solve?**
Name the specific pain, with a number if possible.

- BAD: "Helping businesses scale."
- GOOD: "Most ops teams spend 8+ hours/week on reports that could run automatically."

**Q5: What's the proof?**
One result from one client. Real name optional. Numbers required.

- BAD: "Our clients love us."
- GOOD: "Last client cut reporting time from 9 hours to 45 minutes. Same data, zero manual work."

**Q6: What's the ROI?**
Make it easy to say yes. Frame the value in their terms.

- BAD: "Pricing starts at $2,500."
- GOOD: "If your team earns $60/hour, 8 hours/week saved = $2,000/month. We charge less than that to set it up once."

---

## The Assembly Rule

Take your 6 answers. Cut to 80-100 words total. That's the email body.

Add one line at the end: "Worth a 15-minute call this week?"

That's it. No "I hope this finds you well." No three-paragraph company history.

---

## Full Example Email (94 words)

```
Subject: cutting your reporting time

Hi [Name],

I build automation tools that cut manual reporting time by 80% for ops teams.

I work with operations managers at logistics companies doing $5M-$50M in revenue.

Here's how it works: I audit your current workflow, find the 3 biggest time drains, and automate them in 2 weeks.

Most ops teams I talk to spend 8+ hours/week on reports. My last client went from 9 hours to 45 minutes.

If your team earns $60/hour, that's $2,000/month saved. We charge less than that to set it up once.

Worth a 15-minute call this week?

[Name]
```

## Common mistakes (and fixes)

**Mistake: Writing about features.**
Fix: Every sentence must describe the prospect's world, not yours.

**Mistake: Too long.**
Fix: If it's over 120 words, cut. People don't read. They scan.

**Mistake: Weak subject line.**
Fix: Use the outcome or the problem. "cutting your reporting time" beats "quick intro from [company]" every time.

**Mistake: Sending to bad lists.**
Fix: Verify emails before sending. NeverBounce or ZeroBounce. Bounce rate over 3% kills your domain.

**Mistake: One email and done.**
Fix: 3-email sequence. Email 1: the pitch. Email 2 (3 days later): "did this land in spam?" Email 3 (5 days later): "last try." Sequence 3x's reply rate.

