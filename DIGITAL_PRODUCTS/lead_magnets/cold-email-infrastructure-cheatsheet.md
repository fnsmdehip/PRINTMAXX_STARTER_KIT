## THE 7-DAY WARMUP PROTOCOL

**Why warmup:** Gmail flags new accounts that jump to 100+ sends/day. Warmup avoids spam folder.

**Day 1-2:** Send 5-10 normal emails to friends/colleagues. Reply rate needed.
**Day 3-4:** Increase to 20-25 outbound. Mix cold + warm.
**Day 5-6:** Ramp to 50/day. Start first cold sequence.
**Day 7+:** Full 100/day per account. Track open rates (target >40%).

**Account math:**
- 1 Gmail = 500 sends/day
- 3 Gmails = 1,500 sends/day
- 30-day total = 45,000 touches
- Cost = $0

## THE 6-QUESTION FRAMEWORK (100 words max)

Every cold email answers all 6 in this order:

1. **What you do** — 1 sentence, no jargon
2. **Who you do it for** — specific job title or industry
3. **How you do it** — your method, briefly
4. **Problem you solve** — their pain, not your feature
5. **Proof** — 1 real number (client, result, time)
6. **ROI** — what they gain, quantified if possible

**Example (92 words):**
> I build automated lead pipelines for B2B service businesses. Specifically for agency owners billing $10K-50K/mo. I set up a 3-tool stack (Apollo + Gmail + Streak) that runs 1,500 daily touches with zero manual work. Most agencies spend 10-15 hours/week on outreach. I get that to 30 min/week. Did this for a 12-person agency last month — they booked 14 qualified calls in 21 days. Worth a 15-min call to see if the numbers work for you?

## SUBJECT LINE FORMULAS (ranked by open rate)

| Formula | Example | Avg Open Rate |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| [Name], quick question | "Sarah, quick question" | 52% |
| [Company] + observation | "Noticed [Company] just hired 3 SDRs" | 48% |
| Referral hint | "[Mutual contact] said to reach out" | 61% |
| Specific trigger | "Saw your post about [topic]" | 44% |
| Direct benefit | "14 calls in 21 days — here's how" | 41% |

**Never use:**
- "Touching base"
- "Following up" (as subject line)
- "I hope this finds you well"
- Question mark in subject (tanks open rates 18%)

## THE FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

**Email 1 (Day 0):** Full 6-question pitch
**Email 2 (Day 3):** 1-sentence bump. "Did this get buried?"
**Email 3 (Day 7):** New angle. Lead with proof or case study.
**Email 4 (Day 14):** Breakup email. "Closing your file — unless this is still relevant?"

**Why the breakup email works:** Scarcity triggers response. 30-40% of replies come on email 4.

**Total sequence = 4 emails over 14 days. If no reply, move on.**

## REPLY TRIGGERS (copy these)

When someone replies "not interested":
> "Totally fair. One last question — is the timing off, or is this just not a fit at all? Would help me know whether to reach back out in Q3."

When someone asks for more info:
> "Happy to. Can I send a 2-min Loom instead of a wall of text? Easier to see the setup."

When someone ghosts after showing interest:
> "Hey [name] — last note from me. I held a slot open through Friday. If not a fit, no worries — just let me know so I can close the loop."
