## Description (paste exactly)

Most genealogy resources give you a list of names and dates.

This gives you the story behind them.

**Irish Heritage: A Complete Narrative History** covers 40,000 years of Irish history in four editorial-quality chapters — written for people who want to understand what their ancestors actually lived through, not just when they were born and where they died.

---

**What's inside:**

**Chapter 1: The Ice and the Refuge** (~40,000 BC – 2500 BC)
How Ireland emerged from the ice sheets, who the first Irish people were (DNA evidence from Trinity College Dublin, 2020), the mystery of Newgrange, and the Bell Beaker migration that established the genetic foundation of the modern Irish population.

**Chapter 2: Saints, Scholars, and the Sword** (~400 AD – 1500 AD)
Ireland's golden age of monasticism, the Book of Kells, the Viking invasion and the founding of Dublin, Brian Boru and the Battle of Clontarf, and the Norman arrival that began eight centuries of English entanglement.

**Chapter 3: Conquest, Plantation, and the Penal Dark** (1500 – 1845)
The Nine Years War and Hugh O'Neill's near-victory, the Flight of the Earls, the Plantation of Ulster, Cromwell's massacres, the collapse of Catholic land ownership from 59% to 14%, and the Penal Laws that tried — and failed — to extinguish Irish culture.

**Chapter 4: The Hunger, the Ships, and the New World** (1845 – 1920)
The Great Famine in full detail (what actually caused it, why food continued to be exported), the coffin ships, the death statistics, the Irish-American experience in Boston and New York, the Irish Brigade at Fredericksburg, the Land League, the Easter Rising, and Independence.
