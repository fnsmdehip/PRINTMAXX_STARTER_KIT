## Full Description

**Every family holds thousands of years of history. Most of it will never be written down — unless you write it.**

Wars survived. Oceans crossed. Names changed at borders. Languages traded for opportunity. The decision your great-great-grandmother made to get on a boat with nothing but the name of a cousin in a foreign city — that decision echoes in everything you are today.

The Family Story Workbook is your structured guide to capturing that history before it's gone.

---

### What's inside:

**Part 1 — Your Family Tree**
Worksheets for 3 generations of both sides of your family. Fields for names, dates, places, origins, and the one thing you know about each person. Designed to be filled in progressively as you discover more.

**Part 2 — The Relative Interview Guide**
12 carefully crafted questions designed to draw out real stories, not just dates. Includes questions about childhood homes, migration decisions, war experiences, financial hardship, what was kept and what was lost. The most important section. Do this first — while the people who remember are still here.

**Part 3 — Heritage Region Guides**
In-depth historical context for the most common American ancestry origins: Ireland, Italy, England, Germany, plus blank pages for other regions. Understand *why* your ancestors left, what they survived, and what shaped them — so you can understand what shaped you.

**Part 4 — Your Family Timeline**
A structured timeline from medieval roots to the present day. Maps your family against major world events to show how history touched your specific lineage.

**Part 5 — Writing Your Family Narrative**
10 narrative writing prompts to transform your notes into actual prose. Includes the golden rule of family narrative writing and examples of how to turn facts into stories.

**Part 6 — Photo & Document Vault**
Photo slots, a digitization priority guide, a document inventory checklist (14 document types), and a research tracking table. Everything you need to organize the physical evidence of your family's existence.

**Part 7 — Research Resources**
A curated guide to free and paid genealogy resources: FamilySearch, Ellis Island records, Ancestry.com, DNA testing comparison (AncestryDNA vs 23andMe vs MyHeritage vs FamilyTreeDNA). A 10-item research checklist to guide your next steps.

**Part 8 — Turn This Into a Before You Website**
How to use your completed workbook data to generate a full Before You narrative website — a scroll-driven, AI-personalized site tracing your family from the ice age to today.
