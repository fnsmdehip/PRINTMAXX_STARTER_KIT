# Chapter 4: Seven Proven Email Sequences With Templates

## What You'll Have After This Chapter

Seven complete email sequences you can deploy today — each with the initial email and 3-4 follow-ups. Pick the sequence that matches your situation, fill in the variables, and start sending.

## How Sequences Work

A sequence is a series of emails sent to the same prospect over time. Here's why sequences matter:

- Email 1: 2-5% reply rate
- Email 2: Additional 3-4% reply rate
- Email 3: Additional 2-3% reply rate
- Email 4: Additional 1-2% reply rate

Total sequence reply rate: 8-14%. That's 3-5x higher than sending a single email.

The key insight: **Most replies come from follow-ups, not the first email.** The first email gets their attention. The follow-ups get their response.

### Sequence Timing

- Email 1: Day 0 (initial send)
- Email 2: Day 3 (first follow-up)
- Email 3: Day 7 (second follow-up)
- Email 4: Day 14 (breakup email)

All follow-ups should be replies to the original thread (same subject line, same email chain). This pushes the whole conversation up in their inbox.

## Sequence 1: The Problem Spotter

**Best for:** Service providers who can identify a specific, fixable problem on the prospect's website or in their marketing.

### Email 1 — The Observation

```
Subject: {{COMPANY}}'s [specific thing]

{{FIRST_NAME}},

I was looking at {{COMPANY}}'s [website/ads/email flow] and noticed
[SPECIFIC PROBLEM — be precise, not vague].

This usually costs companies like yours [SPECIFIC CONSEQUENCE — e.g.,
"15-30% of mobile visitors" or "$2K-5K/month in lost revenue"].

I fixed this exact issue for {{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} and they saw
[SPECIFIC RESULT] within [TIMEFRAME].

Want me to send over what we did? Takes 2 minutes to read.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 2 — Day 3 Follow-Up

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Quick follow-up on the [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] I mentioned.

Here's a 60-second breakdown of the fix:
[2-3 BULLET POINTS explaining the solution at a high level]

Happy to walk through how this would apply to {{COMPANY}} specifically.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 3 — Day 7 Follow-Up

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Wanted to share a quick case study that's relevant to {{COMPANY}}.

{{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} had the same [PROBLEM] I noticed on your
[website/funnel/ads]. After [WHAT WE DID], they went from
[BEFORE METRIC] to [AFTER METRIC] in [TIMEFRAME].

If you're interested, I can put together a similar breakdown for
{{COMPANY}} — no charge, no commitment.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 4 — Day 14 Breakup

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

I've reached out a few times about the [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] on
{{COMPANY}}'s [website/funnel]. Totally understand if the timing
isn't right.

I'll leave this here — if [PROBLEM] becomes a priority down the
road, happy to pick this back up.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

## Sequence 2: The Social Proof Lead

**Best for:** When you have a strong case study in the prospect's exact industry.

### Email 1

```
Subject: How {{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} [achieved result]

{{FIRST_NAME}},

{{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} was [SITUATION SIMILAR TO PROSPECT'S] before we
started working together.

In [TIMEFRAME], we helped them [SPECIFIC RESULT WITH NUMBER] by
[ONE-SENTENCE DESCRIPTION OF WHAT YOU DID].

{{COMPANY}} is in a similar position. Would it be worth 15 minutes
to walk through exactly what we did?

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 2 — Day 3

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

One more detail on the {{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} project I mentioned.

The biggest win wasn't [OBVIOUS METRIC]. It was [UNEXPECTED SECONDARY
BENEFIT — e.g., "they cut their agency spend by 40% while getting
better results" or "their team went from 12 hours/week on X to 2"].

Is this the kind of outcome that would be useful for {{COMPANY}}?

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 3 — Day 7

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

I put together a 1-page breakdown of the {{SIMILAR_COMPANY}} project:
- Where they started: [METRIC]
- What we changed: [ACTION]
- Where they are now: [RESULT]
- Timeline: [DURATION]

Happy to send it over if you're curious — or if you know someone
else at {{COMPANY}} this would be relevant for, feel free to forward.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 4 — Day 14

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Closing the loop on this — seems like the timing might not be right
for {{COMPANY}}, and that's completely fine.

If [SERVICE AREA] becomes a focus later this year, I'm an easy
person to ping.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

## Sequence 3: The Value-First (Free Audit)

**Best for:** When you can provide tangible value before asking for anything. Works exceptionally well for SEO, web design, email marketing, and conversion optimization.

### Email 1

```
Subject: {{COMPANY}} audit

{{FIRST_NAME}},

I did a quick [SEO/conversion/email/ad] audit of {{COMPANY}}'s
[website/funnel/campaigns] and found 3 things worth looking at:

1. [SPECIFIC FINDING #1]
2. [SPECIFIC FINDING #2]
3. [SPECIFIC FINDING #3]

I recorded a 3-minute video walking through each one with
suggestions. Want me to send it over?

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 2 — Day 3

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Following up on the audit findings. Here's a quick preview of
finding #1:

[2-3 SENTENCES explaining the most impactful finding and the fix]

This alone could [ESTIMATED IMPACT]. The other 2 findings are
in the video — just say the word and I'll send the link.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 3 — Day 7

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

I'll leave the video link here in case it's useful:

[LOOM/VIDEO LINK]

No strings attached — these are fixes your team can implement
without my help. If you want a deeper dive after watching,
happy to chat.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 4 — Day 14

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Last note on this. The video covers 3 quick wins for {{COMPANY}}.
Even if we never work together, the fixes are worth implementing.

Link is above if you want to check it out. Either way, all the best
with {{COMPANY}}.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

**Why this sequence works:** You've already done the work. Most prospects feel a combination of curiosity and reciprocity — they want to see what you found, and they feel a natural obligation to at least respond because you invested effort.

## Sequence 4: The Referral Ask

**Best for:** When your contact isn't the right person but might know who is.

### Email 1

```
Subject: Quick question

{{FIRST_NAME}},

I help [TYPE OF COMPANY] with [SPECIFIC OUTCOME]. I've been trying
to connect with whoever handles [FUNCTION — marketing, web
development, growth, etc.] at {{COMPANY}}.

Would you be the right person to talk to about this, or could you
point me to who is?

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 2 — Day 3

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Just a quick bump on this — trying to find the right person at
{{COMPANY}} who handles [FUNCTION].

If that's not you, would you mind pointing me in the right direction?
Takes 10 seconds.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 3 — Day 7

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Last try on this. Would [PERSON'S NAME — guess based on LinkedIn
research] be the right contact for [FUNCTION] at {{COMPANY}}?

If so, I'll reach out to them directly.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

**Why this works:** Asking "are you the right person?" has a surprisingly high response rate because it's a low-effort question. People either say "yes" (and now you're in a conversation) or they forward you to the right person (warm intro).

## Sequence 5: The Trigger Event

**Best for:** When something just happened at the company that makes your service immediately relevant — new funding, new hire, product launch, expansion.

### Email 1

```
Subject: Congrats on [TRIGGER EVENT]

{{FIRST_NAME}},

Saw the news about {{COMPANY}} [SPECIFIC TRIGGER — "raising your
Series A," "launching the new product line," "expanding to the
UK market"].

When [SIMILAR COMPANY] hit the same milestone, they ran into
[SPECIFIC CHALLENGE THAT COMES NEXT]. We helped them [SPECIFIC
SOLUTION] and they [SPECIFIC RESULT].

Is [CHALLENGE] on your radar right now?

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 2 — Day 3

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

One thing I didn't mention — after [TRIGGER EVENT], the biggest
time-sensitive issue is usually [SPECIFIC URGENT CHALLENGE].

If you wait [TIMEFRAME], [CONSEQUENCE]. But if you address it now,
[POSITIVE OUTCOME].

Worth a 10-minute conversation to see if this applies to
{{COMPANY}}'s situation?

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 3 — Day 10

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Quick check-in. I know post-[TRIGGER EVENT] things move fast.

If [CHALLENGE] becomes a priority, I've got a framework that's
worked for 3 other companies in your exact situation. Happy to
share it — takes 5 minutes to walk through.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

## Sequence 6: The Mutual Connection

**Best for:** When you share a connection, community, or event with the prospect.

### Email 1

```
Subject: [MUTUAL CONNECTION'S NAME] / {{COMPANY}}

{{FIRST_NAME}},

[MUTUAL CONNECTION] and I were talking about [TOPIC], and your name
came up. [He/She] mentioned you're [SPECIFIC CONTEXT — "scaling
{{COMPANY}}'s marketing," "rebuilding your website," "looking for
ways to reduce churn"].

I've helped [SIMILAR COMPANIES] with exactly that — specifically,
[ONE-SENTENCE RESULT].

Worth connecting? I'll keep it short — 15 minutes max.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}

P.S. If [MUTUAL CONNECTION] didn't mention me, I promise this
isn't as random as it looks. [BRIEF CONTEXT].
```

### Email 2 — Day 4

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Following up on the note from last week. [MUTUAL CONNECTION] thought
we'd be a good fit to connect around [TOPIC].

I put together a quick overview of what we did for [SIMILAR COMPANY]
— happy to share it if useful.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 3 — Day 10

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Last note on this. If the timing isn't right, no worries at all.

If [CHALLENGE] becomes a priority later, I'm easy to find — feel
free to circle back anytime.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

## Sequence 7: The Content-First Approach

**Best for:** Prospects who are active content consumers (they read blogs, engage on LinkedIn, subscribe to newsletters).

### Email 1

```
Subject: Thought you'd find this useful

{{FIRST_NAME}},

I saw your [LinkedIn post/tweet/comment] about [TOPIC]. It reminded
me of a piece I wrote on [RELATED TOPIC]:

[LINK TO YOUR ARTICLE/VIDEO/RESOURCE]

The key insight: [ONE-SENTENCE SUMMARY OF THE CONTENT].

Curious if you've tried [SPECIFIC APPROACH FROM THE CONTENT] at
{{COMPANY}}.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 2 — Day 4

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

Another resource related to what you posted about [TOPIC]:

[DIFFERENT PIECE OF CONTENT — someone else's, not yours]

The data in here about [SPECIFIC DATA POINT] is worth looking at,
especially for companies at {{COMPANY}}'s stage.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 3 — Day 8

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

One more thing on [TOPIC]. I've been working with
[SIMILAR COMPANIES] on this exact challenge, and the pattern I keep
seeing is [INSIGHT].

If you're currently tackling [CHALLENGE] at {{COMPANY}}, I'd be
happy to share what's working for others in your space.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

### Email 4 — Day 14

```
{{FIRST_NAME}},

I'll stop filling your inbox. If [TOPIC] stays on your plate
and you want to compare notes with someone who works on this
daily, I'm around.

{{YOUR_FIRST_NAME}}
```

**Why this works:** You're not selling. You're sharing value, building familiarity, and positioning yourself as an expert. By email 3, when you mention working with similar companies, it's a natural transition — not a cold pitch.

## Which Sequence to Use

| Situation | Use Sequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| You found a specific problem on their website/marketing | 1: Problem Spotter |
| You have a strong case study in their industry | 2: Social Proof Lead |
| You can provide a free audit or video review | 3: Value-First |
| You're not sure who the decision-maker is | 4: Referral Ask |
| They just raised funding, launched, or hit a milestone | 5: Trigger Event |
| You share a connection, community, or event | 6: Mutual Connection |
| They're active on social media and engage with content | 7: Content-First |

## Sequence Rules

1. **Never send more than 4 emails in a sequence.** After 4, you're annoying, not persistent.
2. **Always reply to the same thread.** New subject lines for follow-ups get lower response rates.
3. **Each follow-up must add new information.** Don't just say "following up." Add a case study, an insight, a question — something new.
4. **Remove anyone who replies negatively.** "Not interested" means stop. Immediately.
5. **Track replies, not opens.** Open tracking kills deliverability. Reply rate is the only metric that matters.
