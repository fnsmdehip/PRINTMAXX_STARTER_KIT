# Section 5: Business Strategy — 40 Production Prompts

## Pricing & Positioning Prompts (161-175)

### Prompt 161: Value-Based Pricing Calculator
```
Help me set a value-based price for [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

What my customer gets:
- Time saved: [HOURS PER WEEK/MONTH]
- Money saved: [$AMOUNT PER MONTH]
- Revenue generated: [$AMOUNT PER MONTH]
- Risk reduced: [WHAT RISK AND ESTIMATED COST IF IT HAPPENS]

Their current alternative: [WHAT THEY DO NOW AND WHAT IT COSTS]

Calculate:
1. Total value delivered per month (quantified in dollars)
2. Recommended price at 10% of value delivered (standard value-based pricing)
3. Price range: "floor" (minimum viable) to "ceiling" (maximum the market bears)
4. How to frame the price in terms of ROI ("pays for itself in X days")
5. Pricing objection responses for: "too expensive," "competitor is cheaper," "can I get a discount?"
```

### Prompt 162: Positioning Statement Generator
```
Create a positioning statement for [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

Framework: "For [TARGET CUSTOMER] who [NEED/PAIN], [PRODUCT] is the [CATEGORY] that [KEY BENEFIT], unlike [COMPETITOR/ALTERNATIVE] which [COMPETITOR LIMITATION]."

Inputs:
- Target customer: [WHO — be specific]
- Their main need: [PAIN POINT]
- My product: [NAME]
- Category: [WHAT TYPE OF PRODUCT IS THIS]
- Key benefit: [THE ONE THING THAT MATTERS MOST]
- Main competitor: [WHO]
- Their limitation: [WHAT THEY DO POORLY]

Generate:
1. The positioning statement (following the framework above)
2. A 5-word tagline derived from the positioning
3. An elevator pitch (30 seconds, spoken — not written)
4. A one-paragraph product description for the website
5. Three headlines for the landing page hero section (test all three)
```

```
My product features:
[LIST ALL FEATURES]

- Anchor on the [MIDDLE/HIGHEST] tier

Also provide:
- FAQ section (6 questions, focus on objection-handling)

- "Not sure which plan?" comparison table
```

### Prompt 164: Freemium Strategy Design
```
Design a freemium strategy for [PRODUCT].

All features: [LIST EVERY FEATURE]

Decide which features go where:

Free tier:
- Must include: enough value that users keep the product
- Must exclude: enough value that power users NEED to upgrade
- Usage limits: [SUGGEST LIMITS — e.g., "3 projects," "100 API calls/month"]

Paid tier ($[PRICE]):
- The "unlock" that justifies the price
- The emotional trigger for upgrading (what moment makes someone say "I need this")

Rules:
- Free tier users must be able to experience the core value within 5 minutes
- The free-to-paid upgrade path must feel natural, not forced
- The limit should hit when users are MOST engaged (not when they're still evaluating)

Include the upgrade modal copy: headline, 3 bullet points, CTA button text, and a one-line objection handler.
```

### Prompt 168: Discount Strategy
```
Design a discount strategy for [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

- Never discount the core product (it devalues the brand)
- Discounts should create urgency, not expectation
- Every discount must have a clear business goal

Create discount strategies for these scenarios:
1. Black Friday / seasonal sale
2. First-time customer incentive
3. Annual plan promotion
4. Win-back churned customers
5. Referral reward

For each:

```

### Prompt 169: Loss Leader Strategy
```
Design a loss leader strategy for my business.

My products/services:
[LIST ALL OFFERINGS WITH PRICES AND MARGINS]

A loss leader is a product sold at or below cost to attract customers who then buy higher-margin products.

Questions to answer:
1. Which product should be the loss leader? (criteria: low cost to deliver, high perceived value, naturally leads to upsell)
2. What should I charge for it? ($0? $1? Below cost?)
3. What's the upsell path from the loss leader to my core offering?
4. What conversion rate do I need from loss leader buyer to core product buyer to break even?
5. How do I prevent people from buying ONLY the loss leader?

Provide the math: If I sell [X] loss leaders at $[PRICE] and [Y]% convert to $[CORE PRODUCT PRICE], my net revenue is $[CALCULATE].
```

### Prompt 174: Revenue Goal Reverse Engineering
```

My product: [DESCRIPTION]

Current customers: [NUMBER]

1. How many customers do I need at my current price?
```

### Prompt 181: Soft Launch Checklist
```

- [ ] Payment flow works (test with Stripe test cards)
- [ ] Email notifications work (signup, purchase, password reset)

- [ ] Error handling (what happens when things break — graceful errors, not white screens)
- [ ] Analytics tracking (what events am I tracking, confirm they fire)
- [ ] Legal: Privacy policy and Terms of Service pages exist
- [ ] Support channel set up (email or chat)

Soft launch (Days 1-7):
- [ ] Invite [NUMBER] hand-picked users
- [ ] Monitor error logs daily
- [ ] Respond to all feedback within [HOURS]
- [ ] Document all bugs and prioritize fixes
- [ ] Track: signups, activation rate, retention at Day 1/3/7

Go/No-Go for full launch:
- Activation rate above [%]
- No critical bugs unresolved
- [NUMBER]+ positive testimonials collected
- Payment flow has processed [NUMBER]+ real transactions
```

### Prompt 184: Viral Loop Design
```
Design a viral loop for [PRODUCT].

A viral loop is: a mechanism where using the product naturally leads to inviting others.

Examples of viral loops:
- Dropbox: "Invite friends, get free storage"
- Calendly: "Every meeting invite shows the product"
- Loom: "Every video shared shows a Loom watermark"

My product: [DESCRIPTION]
The core action: [WHAT USERS DO IN THE PRODUCT]

Design:
1. The trigger: What natural behavior leads to sharing?
2. The share mechanism: How does the product get exposed to non-users?
3. The incentive: What motivates users to share? (intrinsic or extrinsic)
4. The conversion: How do non-users become users after seeing the product?
5. The loop: How does the new user repeat the cycle?

Calculate:
- If each user shares with [X] people, and [Y]% convert, and each new user shares with [X] people...
- Viral coefficient (K-factor): If K > 1, the product grows on its own
- What K-factor is realistic for my product?
```
