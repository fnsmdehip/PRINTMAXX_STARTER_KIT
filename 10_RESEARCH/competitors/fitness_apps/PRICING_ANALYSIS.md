## Free Tier Strategies

### Generous Free (Growth-focused)
**Habitica approach:**
- Full functionality free
- Premium = cosmetic perks
- Goal: Massive user base, conversion on engagement

**Pros:** High downloads, strong reviews, community
**Cons:** Low conversion (1-3%), reliant on volume

### Limited Free (Conversion-focused)
**Opal approach:**
- Core feature free (limited apps)
- Premium = full blocking suite
- Goal: Demonstrate value, convert power users

**Pros:** Higher conversion (8-12%), clear upgrade path
**Cons:** Lower initial engagement, reviews mention limitations

### Trial Only (Premium-focused)
**Freedom approach:**
- 7-session trial
- No permanent free tier
- Goal: Qualify serious users

**Pros:** Higher ARPU, serious users
**Cons:** Lower downloads, harder growth

## Conversion Optimization

### Trial Lengths

| Length | Apps | Conversion Impact |
|--------|------|-------------------|
| 3 days | AppBlock | Quick decision, lower conversion |
| 7 days | Opal, Freedom | Standard, balanced |
| 14 days | Fabulous | Higher engagement, higher conversion |
| 30 days | Special promos | Event-based |

### Best Practices

1. **7-day trial with payment upfront** - Industry standard
2. **Remind 1 day before charge** - Reduces churn and negative reviews
3. **Annual discount 40-60%** - Strong conversion driver
4. **Lifetime at 2-3x annual** - Captures high-intent users

## Revenue Model Comparison

### One-Time Purchase (Forest, Streaks)

**Pros:**
- Low friction to purchase
- No subscription fatigue
- Good App Store reviews
- Simple accounting

**Cons:**
- No recurring revenue
- Must constantly acquire new users
- Hard to fund ongoing development
- Lower LTV

**Best for:** Simple apps, indie developers

### Subscription (Opal, Fabulous)

**Pros:**
- Predictable recurring revenue
- Higher LTV
- Can fund ongoing development
- Aligns incentives with user success

**Cons:**
- Subscription fatigue in market
- Higher churn
- App Store reviews mention cost
- Complex accounting

**Best for:** Feature-rich apps, VC-backed companies

### Freemium with Stakes (Beeminder)

**Pros:**
- Unique positioning
- Strong accountability
- Users self-select (serious only)
- Mission-aligned revenue

**Cons:**
- Niche appeal
- Complex to explain
- User resentment when charged
- Limited market size

**Best for:** Unique positioning, committed users

## Pricing Recommendations

### For New Entrant

**Recommended Model:** Freemium with annual subscription

**Pricing:**
- Free tier: 3 habits OR 3 apps blocked
- Monthly: $6.99
- Annual: $49.99 (40% discount)
- Lifetime: $99.99

**Rationale:**
- Below Opal/Fabulous (not competing on premium)
- Above AppBlock (quality positioning)
- Accessible entry point
- Strong annual incentive

### Differentiation Opportunities

1. **Financial accountability (mainstream Beeminder)**
   - Stake money on goals
   - Lose $5-20 for failures
   - Novel, generates PR

2. **Group/couple accountability**
   - Shared goals with partner
   - $9.99/month for 2 users
   - Underserved market

3. **ADHD premium tier**
   - Specialized features
   - Can charge 50% more
   - Strong niche positioning
