# Info Products Pricing Strategies

## Price Point Psychology

### Impulse Purchase Zone ($5-29)
**Characteristics:**
- No consultation with spouse/partner
- Credit card out immediately
- Decision in <2 minutes
- Low buyer's remorse risk

**Best Products:**
- Single templates
- Short ebooks
- Basic prompt packs
- Simple printables

**Conversion Rate:** 3-8% of traffic

---

### Considered Purchase Zone ($29-97)
**Characteristics:**
- Brief consideration (1-24 hours)
- May check reviews
- Compare alternatives
- Look for social proof

**Best Products:**
- Template bundles
- Comprehensive guides
- Basic courses
- Toolkits

**Conversion Rate:** 1-3% of traffic

---

### Significant Purchase Zone ($97-297)
**Characteristics:**
- Days to decide
- Research alternatives
- Seek testimonials
- May need payment plan

**Best Products:**
- Full courses
- Complete systems
- Premium bundles
- Membership access

**Conversion Rate:** 0.5-2% of traffic

---

### Investment Zone ($297-997)
**Characteristics:**
- Weeks to decide
- Discuss with others
- Need guarantee
- Expect transformation

**Best Products:**
- Premium courses
- Coaching programs
- Certification programs
- Enterprise licenses

**Conversion Rate:** 0.1-0.5% of traffic

---

## Pricing Strategies

### 1. Value-Based Pricing

**Formula:** Price = (Value Delivered) x (Conversion Factor)

**Example:**
- Product saves 10 hours/month
- User's time worth $50/hour
- Value = $500/month
- Price at 10-20% = $50-100

**When to Use:**
- Clear ROI can be demonstrated
- B2B or professional audiences
- Premium positioning

---

### 2. Competitor-Based Pricing

**Formula:** Position vs. alternatives

**Options:**
- **Premium:** 20-50% above market
- **Competitive:** Within 10% of market
- **Undercut:** 20-40% below market
- **Disruptive:** 50%+ below market

**When to Use:**
- Established market exists
- Clear alternatives available
- Need quick market entry

---

### 3. Cost-Plus Pricing

**Formula:** Price = (Cost to Create + Deliver) x Margin

**Typical Margins:**
- Digital products: 70-90%
- Physical products: 40-60%
- Services/coaching: 60-80%

**When to Use:**
- Unique products (no alternatives)
- High production costs
- Physical component

---

### 4. Penetration Pricing

**Strategy:** Launch low, raise later

**Example:**
- Launch at $27
- Raise to $47 after 100 sales
- Raise to $67 after 500 sales

**When to Use:**
- New market entry
- Building social proof
- Creating urgency

---

### 5. Premium Pricing

**Strategy:** Price high, justify with value

**Tactics:**
- Comprehensive bonuses
- Personal access/support
- Exclusive community
- Limited availability

**When to Use:**
- Established authority
- Clear differentiation
- Premium audience

## Bundle Pricing Strategies

### The Value Stack

**Structure:**
- Core product: $97 value
- Bonus 1: $47 value
- Bonus 2: $27 value
- Bonus 3: $27 value
- Total value: $198
- **Your price: $67** (66% off)

**Psychology:** Feels like a deal

---

### Tiered Bundles

| Tier | Contains | Price |
|------|----------|-------|
| Basic | Core product only | $47 |
| Standard | Core + 2 bonuses | $67 |
| Premium | Everything + support | $97 |

**Psychology:** Anchors to Premium, most buy Standard

---

### The Decoy Effect

| Option | Contains | Price |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Single | 1 product | $29 |
| Bundle | 3 products | $47 |
| Premium | 3 products + coaching | $197 |

**Psychology:** Bundle looks like obvious choice

---

## Launch Pricing Strategies

### Early Bird Pricing

**Structure:**
- First 48 hours: 40% off
- First week: 25% off
- Full price after

**Tactics:**
- Countdown timer
- Limited quantity
- Email sequence

---

### Founding Member Pricing

**Structure:**
- First 100 customers: Special rate
- Locked in forever
- VIP perks

**Psychology:** Exclusivity + loyalty

---

### Launch Week Bonuses

**Structure:**
- Product same price
- Bonuses expire

**Example:**
- Buy this week: Get bonus workshop ($197 value)
- After launch: Product only

## Discount Strategies

### When to Discount

**Good Reasons:**
- Launch/early bird
- Seasonal (Black Friday, etc.)
- Bundle vs. individual
- Annual vs. monthly
- Loyalty (existing customers)

**Bad Reasons:**
- Not selling well
- Competitor lowered price
- Customer complained
- Feels too expensive

### Discount Limits

| Discount | Perception |
|----------|------------|
| 10-15% | Appreciated |
| 20-30% | Good deal |
| 40-50% | Great deal |
| 60%+ | Suspicious quality |

### Alternative to Discounts

Instead of lowering price:
- Add bonus content
- Extend access period
- Include support
- Add community access

## Testing and Optimization

### A/B Testing Pricing

**What to Test:**
- Price points ($27 vs $37)
- Display format ($27 vs $27.00)
- Payment plans (1x $97 vs 3x $37)
- Bundle composition

**Minimum Sample:**
- 100+ sales per variant
- 2+ weeks per test
- Same traffic source

### Price Increase Strategy

**When to Raise:**
- After hitting sales milestone
- After adding features
- At product anniversary
- Seasonally (January)

**How to Communicate:**
- Announce in advance
- Grandfather existing customers
- Add value simultaneously
- Make it about growth
