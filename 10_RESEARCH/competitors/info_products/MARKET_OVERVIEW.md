## Pricing Psychology

### Sweet Spots by Product Type

| Product | Impulse | Standard | Premium |
|---------|---------|----------|---------|
| Ebook | $9-17 | $27-37 | $47-67 |
| Template | $7-15 | $27-47 | $67-97 |
| Course | $27-47 | $97-197 | $297-997 |
| Membership | $9-19/mo | $29-49/mo | $67-97/mo |
| Toolkit | $17-27 | $47-97 | $147-297 |

### Pricing Strategies

1. **Charm Pricing:** $27, $47, $97 (not $30, $50, $100)
2. **Anchor High:** Show $497 → discounted to $197
3. **Bundle Value:** "$500 value for $97"
4. **Scarcity:** Limited time, limited quantity
5. **Tiered:** Basic/Standard/Premium options

## Success Factors

### What Works

1. **Solve specific problem** - Not "fitness" but "30-day home workout for busy moms"
2. **Clear outcome** - Before/after, measurable result
3. **Professional presentation** - Design matters
4. **Social proof** - Testimonials, numbers
5. **Urgency/scarcity** - Limited offers
6. **Strong guarantee** - Reduces risk

### Common Failures

1. Too broad/generic
2. Poor design/presentation
3. No audience before product
4. Wrong price point
5. No marketing system
6. No differentiation
