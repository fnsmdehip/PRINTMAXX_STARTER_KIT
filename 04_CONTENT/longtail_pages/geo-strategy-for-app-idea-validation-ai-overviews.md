# GEO strategy for app idea validation how to get cited by AI overviews

## Quick Answer

Write 5 posts about your app idea on your own site. Optimize for specific queries about the problem it solves. Claude/Gemini will cite your posts in AI overviews. You get warm traffic with almost no effort. This works because AI reads fresh content first.

## Why This Works

AI overviews pull from 3-5 sources per query. They cite blogs that:
1. Have recent content (last 30 days)
2. Rank on first page of Google
3. Answer the exact question asked

You can jump all three by writing targeted content.

## The GEO Strategy

### Step 1: Identify Your Query

What do people Google before they want your app?

Example:
- App idea: Task management for ADHD
- Query: "How to stay productive with ADHD"
- AI overview currently cites: Psychology Today, Healthline, Reddit
- Opportunity: Write better post, get cited

### Step 2: Write the Definitive Post

1200-1500 words. Answer the question completely. Include:
- Research data
- Actionable steps
- Real examples
- Your unique angle

Your angle: "Here's how app XYZ helps"

### Step 3: Optimize for AI Overviews

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Question in headline (exact query)
- [ ] Answer in first paragraph (2-3 sentences)
- [ ] Lists and tables (AI loves structured data)
- [ ] Multiple formats (text + code blocks + tables)
- [ ] External links (2-3 reputable sources)
- [ ] Your app mentioned 1-2 times naturally

Example structure:
```
# How to stay productive with ADHD

[Answer in 2 sentences]

## Main strategies
1. Time blocking
2. External brain systems
3. ...

[Details for each]

## Tools that help
[Mention your app here naturally]

## Next steps
```

### Step 4: Publish and Promote

1. Publish on your site
2. Share to relevant subreddits (2-3)
3. Share to Twitter/X (relevant communities)
4. Email any existing list

That's it.

## Real Example: AI Overviews

Google searches: "Best project management for remote teams"

Current AI overview (January 2026):
- Asana blog post
- Monday.com case study

Your window: Write "Project management for remote teams without legacy tools." Get cited next month.

## Timeline

- Day 1: Research + write (2 hours)
- Day 2: Publish + promote (30 min)
- Day 5: First mentions in search (if you're lucky)
- Day 10-30: Cited in AI overviews (usually)
- Day 30+: Warm traffic arriving

Most posts see traffic after 2 weeks.

## How Much Traffic?

"Productive with ADHD" = 500 searches/month

AI overview gets 10-20% of clicks (vs top 3 organic results get 50%)

So: 50-100 monthly visitors from AI overview

If you write 5 posts:
- 250-500 warm visitors/month
- 2-5% convert (app signups)
- 5-25 new users per month

Not huge. But warm and free.

## The 5-Post Strategy

Post 1: Main pain point
Post 2: Comparing solutions
Post 3: How to evaluate (your criteria)
Post 4: Common mistakes
Post 5: The right tool for you

Together: They dominate the topic. Get cited multiple times.

## Queries to Target

Pick 5 queries from:
- "How to..." (top of funnel)
- "Best... for..." (comparison)
- "[Problem] with [adjective]" (specific pain)

Example queries:
- "How to manage remote team productivity"
- "Best project management for distributed teams"
- "Project management for ADHD"
- "Comparing Asana vs Monday for remote teams"
- "How to build communication systems for remote work"

## Tools to Check Rankings

**Check if you can rank:**
1. Google search the query
2. Are top results 1-2 years old? Opportunity.
3. Is Reddit top 3? Easy to beat.
4. Are top results thin (< 500 words)? Easy to beat.

**Check if AI overviews exist:**
1. Search on Google
2. Look for "AI overview" box
3. If it exists, you can get cited

## Content Format That Gets Cited

AI overviews like:
- Bulleted lists
- Tables with comparisons
- Step-by-step guides
- Data with sources
- Specific numbers

Avoid:
- Long paragraphs (hard to cite)
- Opinion without data
- Fluff (AI skips it)

## Mentioning Your App

Don't sell. Don't hype. Just mention:

"Some teams use custom tools built specifically for remote ADHD-friendly workflows, combining async video updates with weekly sprints."

That's enough. AI readers will cite the sentence.

## Common Mistakes

**Don't:** Write about your app directly (too salesy, won't rank)
**Do:** Write about the problem, mention your app once

**Don't:** Copy Reddit posts (duplicate content)
**Do:** Add original research or unique angle

**Don't:** Publish then ignore (need backlinks eventually)
**Do:** Promote for 2-3 weeks, then move to next post

**Don't:** Change content after publishing (hurts rankings)
**Do:** Write well the first time, minor tweaks only
