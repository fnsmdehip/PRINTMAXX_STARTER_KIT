# Social scheduling SOP template for solopreneurs

Social media burns solopreneurs. Most quit because it's daily. Here's the SOP that makes it manageable.

Use this exact system.

## Daily 30-minute system

**9 AM: Check and reply** (10 minutes)
- Reply to top 3 comments from yesterday's posts
- Like 20 posts from your niche
- Done. Move on.

**12 PM: Batch create** (15 minutes)
- Write 3 posts for next week
- Don't edit yet
- Dump them in Notion

**4 PM: Batch schedule** (5 minutes)
- Edit 5 posts from your backlog
- Schedule them in Buffer/Later
- Next 5 days covered

Total: 30 minutes daily. Results: consistent presence.

## The weekly batch day

**Monday 2 hours:**
- Review last week's data (impressions, replies)
- Identify top 3 performing post types
- Outline 15 posts for next 2 weeks using those types
- Create titles/hooks only (save body for daily)

This sets direction. Rest of week just executes.

## The template

Use this structure for all posts:

**Hook:** Problem or question (first line)
**Detail:** One specific number or fact
**CTA:** Question or "RT if you agree"

Character count: 120-180 for Twitter, 200-300 for LinkedIn.

Example:
"Most solopreneurs spend 4+ hours on cold email weekly. 80% get zero replies. I found the ones who get replies focus on research first, templates second. What's your biggest cold email problem?"

Specific. Helpful. Drives engagement.

## The posting schedule

**Twitter/X:** 5 posts/week, Tuesday-Saturday, 9 AM ET

**LinkedIn:** 3 posts/week, Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday, 8 AM ET

**Email newsletter:** 1 email/week, Wednesday, 10 AM ET

Consistency beats frequency. 5 weak posts daily loses to 3 strong posts weekly.

## The tools (minimal)

1. **Buffer ($5/month)** - Schedule and track
2. **Notion ($0)** - Content ideas
3. **Google Sheets ($0)** - Track what works

No need for Hootsuite, Sprout, or anything else.

## The metrics you track

- Impressions (weekly)
- Replies/engagement rate (weekly)
- Clicks to site (if you add links)
- Email signups (if you include link)

Track in one Google Sheet. Update every Monday. That's it.

## The content pillars

Stick to 4 topics:

1. **How-tos:** Step-by-step for your niche
2. **Data:** Specific numbers and findings
3. **Opinions:** What you disagree with (polarizing works)
4. **Tools:** Recommendations or comparisons

Rotate through these. Don't vary. Consistency in format = audience learns what to expect.

## What kills momentum

- Posting sporadically (kills algorithm)
- Deleting old posts (looks unstable)
- Over-editing every post (kills speed)
- Responding to every comment (time sink)
- Chasing trends (confuses your audience)

Avoid all 5. You'll survive the long term.

## Scaling when it works

After 2 months:

- If engagement is 2%+: increase to 8 posts/week
- If clicks are steady: add email sequence to link
- If DMs are coming: build reply templates

Don't add tools. Add content volume.

## The SOP in writing

**Weekly (Monday 2 hours):**
1. Audit last week's performance
2. Outline 15 posts for next 2 weeks
3. Save to Notion template

**Daily (30 minutes):**
1. 9 AM: Reply to top comments (10 min)
2. 12 PM: Write 3 posts for next week (15 min)
3. 4 PM: Edit + schedule 5 posts (5 min)

**Monthly (1 hour):**
1. Review analytics
2. Adjust topics if needed
3. Plan new content angle if old one failed

Total monthly time: ~40 hours. Sustainable for solo founder.

Solopreneur in cold email niche.

- 800 impressions

## Common mistakes

**Mistake 1: Trying all platforms.** You post to Twitter, LinkedIn, TikTok, Instagram. You burn out. Pick one. Master it. Add others after 3 months.

**Mistake 2: Posting without a schedule.** You post when you remember. Algorithms see this as inconsistent. Followers don't know when to check.

**Mistake 3: Generic content.** "Just shipped new feature" with no detail. No engagement. Be specific. Numbers. Problems. Real examples.

Social scheduling is a system. This SOP makes it work.
