# How to do AI influencer marketing legally for sales follow-ups

## Quick Answer

You can create AI avatars and use them for marketing. But you MUST disclose: "This is an AI-generated avatar." Never impersonate a real person. Never use someone's face without permission. Follow FTC guidelines. Cost: $20/mo for AI avatar tools.

## What's Legal

Legal ways to use AI for marketing:
1. Create fictional AI character, disclose clearly
2. Use AI to draft emails (you review/send)
3. Use AI voiceovers with disclosure
4. Create AI video with "AI-generated" watermark
5. Use chatbots labeled "Chatbot" or "AI assistant"

All require one thing: Transparency.

## What's Illegal

Never do this:
1. Deepfake a real person's voice/face without consent
2. Impersonate a real influencer
3. Create fake testimonials from fake people
4. Use someone's photo + AI voice without permission
5. Hide that content is AI-generated

These violate FTC rules and potentially criminal impersonation laws.

## FTC Rules (Must Follow)

**Disclosure requirement:**
If it's AI-generated and used for marketing, say so.

**Where to disclose:**
- In the bio/header ("AI-generated content")
- At the start of video ("This is AI-generated")
- In the caption of images

**What to say:**
- "This video features an AI-generated avatar"
- "AI-generated voiceover"
- "Synthetic media"

**Not acceptable:**
- Tiny print at the end
- Hidden in comments
- Not mentioned at all

## Safe AI Influencer Setups

### Setup 1: Fictional AI Character

Create a brand character (not a real person):
- Name it something obvious: "Alex AI" or "Bot Bailey"
- Give it a profile pic (use AI generator)
- Disclose clearly: "I'm an AI assistant created by [Your Company]"
- Use for educational content, sales follow-ups, etc.

Example:
```
Avatar: "Bailey Bot"
Bio: "I'm an AI assistant created by [Company].
I'm here to help you with [product]."

Sales follow-up email:
"Hi [Name], Bailey here (I'm an AI assistant created by [Company]).

I noticed you checked out our product yesterday.
Questions? Happy to help."
```

Fully legal. Clear disclosure.

### Setup 2: AI Voiceover (With Disclosure)

Use tools like:
- ElevenLabs: AI voices ($11/mo)
- Natural Reader: Text-to-speech ($10/mo)
- Google Wavenet: AI voices (free)

For sales follow-ups:
- Record voicemail using AI voice
- Include: "[Your company]'s AI assistant speaking"
- Message: "Hi [name], we noticed..."

**Required disclosure:** "This message was generated by AI"

### Setup 3: AI Video (With Watermark)

Tools:
- Synthesia: AI avatar videos ($30/mo)
- D-ID: Digital humans ($20/mo)
- HeyGen: AI video tool ($15/mo)

For sales video:
- Create 30-second video with AI avatar
- Add watermark: "AI-GENERATED" or "SYNTHETIC MEDIA"
- Never use real person's face

**Required:** Watermark + disclosure in description

## Sales Follow-Up Example (Legal)

Email with AI-generated subject line:

```
Subject: [AI-generated subject line]
From: Bailey Bot (AI Assistant) <sales@company.com>

Hi Sarah,

I'm Bailey, an AI assistant at [Company].
(I'm here to help with questions about our product)

I noticed you downloaded our guide yesterday.
A few quick questions:

1. What problem are you trying to solve?
2. What tool are you currently using?
3. When do you need a solution by?

Reply with a few words, and I'll get you in touch
with [Your name] to chat.

Best,
Bailey Bot
AI Assistant, [Company]

P.S. - I'm powered by Claude API. For privacy terms, see: [link]
```

This is fully legal because:
- Clearly identifies as AI
- Honest disclosure upfront
- Offers human follow-up
- No impersonation

## What NOT to Do

Bad example (illegal):
```
Subject: Quick question from Sarah
From: Sarah <sales@company.com>

Hey! I'm Sarah from our sales team...

[But "Sarah" is actually an AI avatar, not disclosed]
```

This violates FTC guidelines. Don't do it.

## Compliance Checklist

Before using AI for marketing:

- [ ] Is it labeled "AI-generated"?
- [ ] Is the disclosure prominent (not hidden)?
- [ ] Am I impersonating anyone? (If yes, delete)
- [ ] Did I use anyone's real face/voice without permission? (If yes, delete)
- [ ] Is this disclosed in the right place? (Video: watermark, email: text)
- [ ] Would a reasonable person know this is AI? (If no, add more disclosure)

## When Disclosure Is Needed

**Always disclose if:**
- Video features AI avatar (watermark)
- Voicemail/voice is AI (state in message)
- Email is AI-generated (mention in signature)
- Content claims real person's endorsement (if AI)

**Don't need disclosure if:**
- You're using AI to draft, then you send with your name
- You mention "AI-powered recommendations" generically
- It's obvious from context (chatbot on website)
