# How to do AI influencer marketing legally for cold outreach

## Quick Answer

Use AI to draft emails. Never fake testimonials or results. Disclose affiliates. Keep records for 3+ years. That's it. Everything else is legal.

## What's Legal

### Using AI to Write Cold Outreach Messages

Completely legal. Claude writing personalized emails = fine.

The claim inside the email must be true, but the AI writing it is irrelevant.

### AI-Generated Images in Ads

Legal if disclosed. If the image looks photorealistic but AI-generated, say so.

Example disclosure: "Photo is AI-generated for illustration purposes."

### Personalizing at Scale

Legal. Using AI to customize templates is standard practice.

Example:
- Base template: "Hi [NAME], I noticed you..."
- AI fills in: NAME, company, specific achievement
- You send personally signed email

All legal. 100% normal.

### Affiliate Links in Outreach

Legal with proper disclosure.

Example email ending:
"P.S. - Disclosure: I may earn a commission if you buy through my link. No extra cost to you."

## What's Illegal

### Fake Testimonials

Illegal. Creating fictional reviews violates FTC Act Section 5.

Example illegal: "I made $10k in 30 days using this tool" (if you didn't)

**Fine:** Up to $50,000 per violation

### Fabricated Results

Illegal. Claiming 100% success rate when you haven't tried it = fraud.

Example illegal: "This system works for everyone" (when you haven't tested)

### Undisclosed Material Connections

Illegal. Getting paid without disclosure = deception.

Example illegal: Affiliate promoting product without saying "I earn commission"

### Impersonation

Illegal. Using AI voice/image to impersonate real person.

Example illegal: "Send AI-generated voice from influencer to promote product"

## Safe Cold Outreach Checklist

- [ ] All claims are true (you've tested or verified)
- [ ] Affiliate disclosures in email
- [ ] No fake testimonials (use real customer names or none)
- [ ] No impersonation (you sign your real name)
- [ ] Unsubscribe link in email footer
- [ ] Records saved (dates, recipients, disclosures)

## Real Examples

### Good Email

"Hi John,

I noticed you're building software for solopreneurs. I built a tool that helps with [specific pain point] and it saved our team 5 hours/week.

Thought you might find it useful.

Link: [affiliate link]

P.S. - Full disclosure: I earn a commission if you buy. No extra cost to you.

[Your signature]"

This is legal. Honest. Personalized. Clear disclosure.

### Bad Email

"Hi John,

I made $100k using this secret tool! You can too!

[Link to tool]"

Why it's bad:
- Unsubstantiated claim ($100k)
- No disclosure if affiliate link
- Vague ("this tool")
- Implies everyone succeeds (they don't)

## Platform-Specific Rules

| Platform | Key Rule |
|----------|----------|
| Email | Unsubscribe link, physical address in footer |
| LinkedIn | No bot automation (manual or rate-limited only) |
| X/Twitter | #AD or #Sponsored for paid posts |
| SMS | Opt-in required, unsubscribe option |

## Timeline: How Long to Keep Records

**Minimum:** 3 years from date sent
**Recommended:** 5+ years
**Best practice:** Keep forever in case of audit

Store in folder with date: `/disclosures/2026-01/`

## Red Flags That Get You in Trouble

- "Everyone succeeds with this"
- Income claims without proof
- No unsubscribe option
- Promising guaranteed results
- Fake testimonials
- Celebrity endorsements you didn't pay for

## FTC Gets You on 2 Things

1. **Deception:** Untrue claims
2. **Non-disclosure:** Hidden affiliate connection

Avoid both and you're safe.

## Insurance Against Trouble

1. Document everything (save emails sent, dates, disclosures)
2. Use honest language (avoid "guarantee," "everyone")
3. Disclose clearly and early
4. Test what you claim
5. Keep unsubscribe working

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to say "AI-written"?**

A: No. The email being AI-written doesn't matter. The claims inside matter.

**Q: Can I use AI voice for cold calls?**

A: No. Impersonation is illegal. Be clear you're AI or hire human.

**Q: How clear must affiliate disclosure be?**

A: Clear enough that average reader understands. "I earn commission" works. "Affiliate" works. Hiding it doesn't.

**Q: What if I get audited?**

A: Show your records. Honest disclosures. You're fine.

## Real Risk vs Hype Risk

Low risk (probably safe):
- Honest claims about product you tested
- Clear affiliate disclosure
- Opt-in email list

High risk (FTC notices):
- Income claims without proof
- Fake testimonials
- Undisclosed sponsors
- Impersonation
