# Hard paywall vs freemium: which makes more money for indie apps

## Quick answer

Hard paywalls generate 8x more revenue than freemium for indie apps with under 100K users. Freemium only wins at massive scale (1M+ users) because the 1-3% conversion rate needs volume. If you are an indie developer shipping your first 5 apps, use hard paywalls.

## The numbers

| Metric | Hard paywall | Freemium | Difference |
|--------|-------------|----------|------------|
| Trial start rate | 15-25% | N/A | - |
| Trial-to-paid | 40-60% | N/A | - |
| Overall conversion | 8-15% | 1-3% | 5-8x |
| Revenue per install | $1.20-$4.50 | $0.15-$0.45 | 8-10x |
| LTV (annual) | $18-$45 | $2-$8 | 6-9x |
| Monthly churn | 5-8% | 8-15% | 40% lower |

## When hard paywalls win

- Apps with fewer than 100K downloads
- Apps solving a specific daily problem (timers, trackers, habits)
- Apps with strong onboarding demonstrating value in 60 seconds
- Niche apps (prayer, fitness, student tools)
- Apps where the core feature IS the product

## When freemium wins

- Social/network apps needing critical mass
- Apps targeting 1M+ users
- Apps where free users create value for paid users
- Apps with strong viral loops
- VC-funded apps absorbing years of free users

## How to implement correctly

### The wrong way

User downloads, uses for 3 days free, gets value. Day 4 popup says "Subscribe." User already got what they wanted. They uninstall.

### The right way

User downloads. 3-screen onboarding shows value. Hits paywall. Starts 3-day trial. Uses app during trial. Converts on day 3.

The difference: paywall BEFORE value, not after.

## A/B test framework

Test 1 (week 1-2): Paywall timing. After onboarding vs after first session.
Test 2 (week 3-4): Trial length. 3-day vs 7-day.
Test 3 (week 5-6): Pricing display. Annual first vs weekly first.
Test 4 (week 7-8): Design. Feature list vs before/after comparison.

Need 500+ installs per variant.

## FAQ

### Will I get bad reviews?

Some. But paying users leave better reviews than free users. Average rating often improves after switching to hard paywall.

### What if no competitors use hard paywalls?

The market is undertested. Run a 30-day experiment. Revenue per install over $1 means keep it.

### Can I use both models?

Worst of both worlds. Free users never upgrade. Pick one. Commit fully.

### How do I handle Apple's 30% cut?

Build a web-to-app funnel. 82% of top apps use external payment links.
