# Best content production templates for solo founders

## Quick Answer

Save 30 hours/month using templates. Use the same hook formula 5 times. Reuse outline structure. Fill in data. Done.

Don't write from scratch. Copy structure. Change details.

## The Core Problem

Solo founders waste time:
- Staring at blank page (1 hour)
- Finding structure (30 minutes)
- Writing first draft (2 hours)
- Editing (1 hour)

Total: 4.5 hours per piece.

Templates cut this to 1 hour.

## Template 1: Hook Formula

Use this for social posts, email subject lines, blog intros.

**Structure:**
[PROBLEM] + [DATA] + [SOLUTION]

**Examples:**

"I wasted $5k on tools I don't use. Here's the stack that actually works. Total: $50/mo."

"Most cold emails fail at 'Hi there.' Here's the line that works. 35% open rate."

"Everyone's building SaaS. Here's what actually makes money. Numbers inside."

**Formula to copy:**
"I [PROBLEM]. Here's [SOLUTION]. [PROOF/DATA]."

That's it. Plug in your problem, solution, data. Takes 5 minutes.

## Template 2: Email Sequence

Use for follow-ups, onboarding, nurture sequences.

**Email 1 (Hook):**
Subject: [PROBLEM I solve]
Body: "Hi [Name],

[Pain point specific to them]

[Solution I have]

[Link]"

**Email 2 (Social proof):**
Subject: [SOCIAL PROOF]
Body: "[Customer name] saved X hours using this.

You can too.

[Link]"

**Email 3 (Urgency):**
Subject: [LAST CHANCE]
Body: "This offer ends Friday.

[Link]"

Copy this 3-email structure. Change details. Use forever.

## Template 3: Blog Post Outline

Use for long-form content.

**Structure:**
1. Intro (answer in first paragraph)
2. Why it matters (1 section)
3. How to do it (3-5 steps)
4. Tools/templates (comparison table)
5. Common mistakes (FAQ section)
6. Next steps (action items)

**Time to write:** 2 hours instead of 4 hours

Just follow structure. Fill in examples. Done.

## Template 4: Comparison Table

Use for tool recommendations, product reviews.

**Structure:**
| Feature | Option A | Option B | Option C |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|
| Cost | $ | $$ | $$$ |
| Ease | Easy | Medium | Hard |
| Best for | Beginners | Growing | Enterprise |

That's all. Copy structure. Change tools. Insert your data.

Readers love tables. Fast to make.

## Template 5: Case Study

Use to show results.

**Structure:**
1. Problem (client/you faced)
2. Solution (what you tried)
3. Results (what happened)
4. Numbers (percentage, dollar amount, time saved)
5. Lesson (takeaway)

**Example:**
"I was spending 10 hours/week on email. I built an automation. Now it's 30 minutes. Here's the setup (open source, costs nothing)."

Simple. Proven. Repeatable.

## Template 6: FAQ Section

Use to handle objections, show credibility.

**Format:**
Q: [Real question from customers]
A: [Direct answer, then example]

Examples:
- "Q: Does this actually save time? A: Yes. 5 hours/week. Here's proof."
- "Q: Is it expensive? A: No. $27/month."
- "Q: Can I do this without coding? A: Yes. I have a no-code version."

Don't invent questions. Use real ones from customers.

## How to Build Your Template Library

**Month 1:** Collect 5 posts you love (not your own)

**Month 2:** Reverse-engineer their structure

**Month 3:** Create your own templates

**Month 4+:** Use templates, add to library

Example reverse-engineering:
- What's the headline structure?
- How many sections?
- Where's the call-to-action?
- How long is intro?
- What format is proof (numbers, quotes, links)?

Now make a template from it.

## Common Template Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Template too rigid**

Bad: "You must use exact same words"

Good: "Here's structure, adapt language"

Flexibility required.

**Mistake 2: Template too generic**

Bad: "Use [ADJECTIVE] [NOUN]"

Good: "Use specific data (e.g., '$500/month', '40 hours/week')"

Specificity wins.

**Mistake 3: Not testing template**

Bad: Create template, never use it

Good: Use template 5 times, iterate based on results

Test everything.

## Template Customization

**For cold outreach:**
- Use personal details (company name, person's name)
- Adjust tone (less formal)
- Lead with benefit to them

**For sales:**
- Use social proof (numbers, names)
- Lead with outcome (not features)
- Multiple CTAs

**For education:**
- Use steps + examples
- Lead with learning objective
- End with action

Adapt templates to context.
