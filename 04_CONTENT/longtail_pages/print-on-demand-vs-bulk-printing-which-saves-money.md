# Print on demand vs bulk printing: which saves money

## Quick answer

Print on demand costs more per unit ($8-15/shirt) but has zero upfront investment. Bulk printing costs less per unit ($3-5/shirt at 500+) but requires inventory cash. POD wins for testing. Bulk wins for proven sellers.

## Cost breakdown

| Order Size | POD Cost/Unit | Bulk Cost/Unit | Total POD | Total Bulk |
|------------|---------------|----------------|-----------|------------|
| 10 shirts | $12 | $15 | $120 | $150 |
| 50 shirts | $11 | $8 | $550 | $400 |
| 100 shirts | $10 | $6 | $1,000 | $600 |
| 500 shirts | $9 | $4 | $4,500 | $2,000 |

The crossover point is around 50-100 units. Below that, POD wins on total cost.

## When to use print on demand

**Use POD when:**
- Testing new designs (0 risk if it flops)
- Running limited editions (under 50 units)
- Offering many design variations
- You have no storage space
- Cash flow is tight

**Real numbers:**
- Test 10 designs at $0 upfront
- Sell each for $25
- POD cost: $10-12
- Profit per sale: $13-15
- If design fails: $0 lost

## When to use bulk printing

**Use bulk when:**
- Design has proven sales (50+ units sold via POD)
- You can afford $1,000+ inventory investment
- You have storage space
- You want higher margins
- You need faster shipping

**Real numbers:**
- Order 500 shirts at $4 each = $2,000 upfront
- Sell each for $25
- Profit per sale: $21
- vs POD profit: $13
- Extra profit: $8 x 500 = $4,000

## Hidden costs to consider

### POD hidden costs
- Higher shipping to customer ($5-8)
- Limited customization options
- Longer production time (3-5 days)
- Quality varies by provider

### Bulk hidden costs
- Storage space ($50-200/month)
- Unsold inventory (if design flops)
- Shipping to your location ($100-300)
- Quality control time (checking each unit)
- Packaging materials ($0.50-1 per unit)

## Hybrid approach

Best strategy: Start POD, graduate to bulk.

**Month 1-2:** List 10 designs on POD
**Month 3:** Identify winner (20+ sales)
**Month 4:** Order 100 bulk of winner
**Month 5+:** Scale bulk orders for proven sellers

This way you test cheap and scale smart.

## FAQ

**Can I switch from POD to bulk mid-campaign?**

Yes. Most sellers do this. Run POD for 30-60 days, identify winners, then bulk order those designs only.

**What's the minimum order for bulk savings?**

Usually 50+ units for meaningful savings. At 100+ units you see real margin improvement.

**Does bulk quality beat POD?**

Not always. Bulk gives you more control over blanks and print quality, but good POD providers match bulk quality.

**Should I use both simultaneously?**

Yes. Use POD for new designs and long-tail products. Use bulk for bestsellers. Most successful sellers do both.
