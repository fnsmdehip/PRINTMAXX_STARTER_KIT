# How to clone a successful app and build a niche version

## Quick answer

Find apps making $100K+/month (check App Store revenue estimates on AppMagic or Sensor Tower). Build a version targeting a specific demographic: faith, fitness, students, women, seniors. Niche versions convert 2-5x better because the marketing speaks directly to the audience. Use Expo + React Native. Ship in 1-2 weekends.

## The app cloning playbook

### Step 1: Find a proven app (1 hour)

Sources for finding apps to clone:
- appkittie.com (trending app movers)
- AppMagic (revenue estimates)
- Product Hunt (new launches with traction)
- App Store top charts by category

Look for:
- Revenue $50K+/month (proven market)
- Simple core feature (can be rebuilt quickly)
- No strong network effects (not social apps)
- Generic positioning (room for niche versions)

### Step 2: Identify the niche angle (30 min)

Take the generic app and make it specific:

| Generic app | Niche version | Target audience |
|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| Habit tracker | PrayerLock (faith habits) | Christians, Muslims |
| Step counter | WalkToUnlock (gamified walking) | Fitness beginners |
| Study timer | StudyLock (screen time for students) | College students |
| Supplement tracker | BioMaxx (biohacking tracker) | Health optimizers |
| Budget app | Halal Finance (Islamic finance) | Muslim community |
| Meditation app | Christian Meditation | Faith community |

### Step 3: Research the competition (1 hour)

Search the App Store for your niche version. If zero or few results: huge opportunity. If many results: check their ratings and reviews for complaints (build what they are missing).

### Step 4: Find MIT-licensed repos (30 min)

Search GitHub: `"{app type} clone" license:mit stars:>50`

Allowed licenses: MIT, Apache 2.0, BSD (commercial use OK).
Avoid: GPL, AGPL (requires releasing your source code).

Using an existing repo saves 50-80% of development time.

### Step 5: Build the niche version (1-2 weekends)

Keep 80% of the original feature set. Change: branding, color scheme, copy, onboarding to speak to your niche. Add 1-2 niche-specific features that the generic app does not have.

### Step 6: Monetize and ship

Hard paywall with RevenueCat. Annual-first pricing. Submit to App Store.

## Revenue expectations

| Niche | Competition level | Revenue potential (month 6) |
|-------|------------------|---------------------------|

| Fitness niche | Medium | $1,000-5,000/month |

Low competition niches convert better because users feel the app was "made for them."

## FAQ

### Is cloning legal?

You cannot copy code, design assets, or exact UI. You can build an app with similar functionality and different branding. Functionality is not copyrightable. Design is. Make it your own.

### How do I find revenue estimates for apps?

AppMagic, Sensor Tower, and AppFigures provide revenue estimates. Free alternatives: check App Store ratings count and multiply by estimated download-to-rating ratio (typically 1-3% of users rate).

### What if the original app is huge?

Good. Huge apps serve everyone, which means they serve nobody perfectly. Your niche version serves one audience perfectly. You do not need 1% of their market. You need 100% of your niche.

### Should I build for iOS or Android first?

iOS. Higher revenue per user. Easier to get featured in niche categories. Ship Android after proving the concept on iOS.
