# Best workout tracking apps vs Notion templates in 2026

Dedicated workout apps (Strong, JEFIT, Hevy) are better for in-gym logging with timers and exercise demos. Notion templates are better for comprehensive program management including nutrition, progress photos, and periodization planning. Best approach: use both. Log workouts in an app, plan programs in Notion.

## Quick answer

If you just need to log sets and reps at the gym: use the Strong app (free tier). If you want a complete training system with meal plans, progress tracking, and periodized programming: use a Notion workout template ($12-27 one-time). The ideal setup uses both: Strong for quick gym logging, Notion for the big picture.

## When to use each

Use a workout app when you need quick in-gym logging, automatic rest timers, exercise form references, and workout history at a glance.

Use a Notion template when you need a complete 12-week program structure, meal planning with macro calculations, progress photo comparison views, and weekly check-in systems.

## FAQ

### Can Notion replace a workout app?
For program planning and comprehensive tracking, yes. For real-time gym logging with timers and exercise demos, apps are more practical. Most serious lifters use both.

### Which workout app has the best free tier?
Strong. The free tier allows unlimited exercises and workout logging. The paid tier ($5/month) adds charts and routine management.

### Are Notion workout templates worth paying for?
If you want a pre-built 12-week system with meal plans, exercises, and progress tracking, yes. Building the same system from scratch takes 8-15 hours. A $17 template saves that time immediately.

### How do I track progressive overload?
Both apps and Notion can track this. Log your weight and reps each session. Compare week over week. If you completed all prescribed reps, add 5 lbs next session for barbell movements, 2.5 lbs for dumbbells.
