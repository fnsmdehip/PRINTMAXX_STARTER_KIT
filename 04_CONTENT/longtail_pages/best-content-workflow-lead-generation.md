# Best content workflow to post daily for lead generation

Daily content for lead gen doesn't work if you're inconsistent. Most founders post for 2 weeks, quit for a month, post again.

Here's the system that creates consistency.

## The 30-minute daily system

**9 AM: Check yesterday's results (5 minutes)**
- Open Buffer/Later
- Note which posts got engagement
- Screenshot top performer

**10 AM: Write 1 post (15 minutes)**
- Use template (hook + detail + CTA)
- Target different angle than yesterday
- Schedule for tomorrow

**4 PM: Reply to all comments (10 minutes)**
- Go through all platforms
- Reply to every meaningful comment
- Thank people for engagement

Total: 30 minutes daily. Guaranteed consistency.

## Why this beats batch content

**Batch approach:**
- Week 1: Write 20 posts
- Week 2-3: Post daily
- Week 4: Burnout, quit

**Daily approach:**
- Every day: write 1 post
- Every day: reply to comments
- Sustainable forever

The daily approach wins.

## Content pillars for lead gen

Rotate through 4 types daily (M-F):

**Monday: How-to**
- "Here's how we generate leads in 3 steps"

**Tuesday: Data**
- "80% of solopreneurs don't follow up. Here's why."

**Wednesday: Tool**
- "We switched from X to Y. ROI improvement: 3x."

**Thursday: Opinion**
- "Most lead gen advice is wrong. Here's what works."

**Friday: Case study**
- "We generated 100 leads. Cost per lead: $X. Here's how."

Rotate these 5 types. Your audience learns what to expect. Engagement increases.

## The template (that converts)

**Hook:** Problem or controversial statement (first line)
**Detail:** One specific number or finding
**CTA:** Question or "RT if you agree"

Character count: 150-200

Example:
"Most lead gen strategies fail because founders target everyone. I narrowed my ideal customer profile to 3 properties. Lead quality went up 5x. Conversion rate: 8%.

What's one property you'd test?"

Specific. Actionable. Drives engagement.

## Measuring what works

Track weekly:
- Impressions (target: growing)
- Engagement rate (target: 2%+)
- Clicks (if including link)
- Email signups (if including CTA)

Which post types get most engagement?
- If data wins: post more data
- If opinions win: post more opinions

Lean into what works. Kill what doesn't.

**Week 1-2:** Crickets. Keep posting anyway.
**Week 3-4:** First real engagement (2-3 replies per post)
**Week 5-8:** Momentum. 10+ replies per post.
**Week 9-12:** First inbound lead from content
**Month 4+:** Lead flow becomes predictable

Most quit week 2. Don't.

## Common failure patterns

**Failure 1: Inconsistency.** Post for 5 days. Skip 3 days. Algorithm sees you as inactive. Engagement tanks.

Fix: 30-minute system. No excuses. Every day.

**Failure 2: Vague content.** "Tips for success" with no specifics. No engagement.

Fix: One number per post. One specific finding.

**Failure 3: No replies.** You post. Ignore comments. People see you as broadcast-only, not conversational.

Fix: Reply to every comment. 10 minutes daily.

## Email capture from content

Don't hard-sell. Soft-sell:

In post: "I've tested 50 lead gen strategies. Results vary. [Link to my email list]"

Not: "SIGN UP FOR MY EMAIL"

More conversational. Better conversion.

By week 12: 5-10 email signups daily from content.

## Scaling after traction

After 3 months:
- If 2% email conversion: hire VA to help with replies
- If 5% email conversion: consider full-time content creator
- If 10% email conversion: build paid course around content

But: don't scale until you see results.

## Platform-specific tweaks

**Twitter:** Shorter, snappier, daily

Post on multiple platforms but optimize for each.

Each post targets different angle. All same niche. All drive traffic/leads to your funnel.
