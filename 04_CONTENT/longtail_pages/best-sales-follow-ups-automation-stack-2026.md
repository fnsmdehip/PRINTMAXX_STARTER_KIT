# Best sales follow-ups automation stack in 2026

Sales teams won't close deals without follow-ups. Most fail because they're manual or broken.

Here's the stack that works in 2026.

## The core stack

**Email platform:** SendGrid ($0-50/month)
**Sequence builder:** Zapier ($25) or n8n ($0 self-hosted)
**CRM:** Airtable ($0) or Pipedrive ($50)
**Tracking:** Mailgun ($0) for open/click tracking
**Analytics:** Google Sheets ($0) for manual tracking

Total: $25-75/month

## How it works together

1. Lead enters Airtable (CRM)
2. Zapier triggers first email (SendGrid)
3. Tracking happens in Mailgun
4. After 48 hours, Zapier sends email 2
5. After 3 days, email 3 (if no reply)
6. After 7 days, email 4 (if no reply)
7. Analytics dashboard shows results

No manual work after setup. Runs forever.

## Real example (cold outreach SOP)

You have 50 prospects.

**Day 1:** Zapier adds them to Airtable. SendGrid sends email 1.

**Day 3:** Zapier auto-sends email 2 (if no reply).

**Day 5:** Zapier auto-sends email 3 (if no reply).

**Day 8:** Zapier auto-sends email 4 (if no reply).

**Day 10:** You manually check Airtable. Mark replies + non-replies.

**Results:** 50 prospects, 5-7 replies (10-14% from automation).

Without automation: 0 replies (you forgot to follow up).

## Email sequence templates

**Sequence 1: Simple cold email**

Email 1 (Day 0): Intro + value prop
Email 2 (Day 3): Different angle (social proof)
Email 3 (Day 7): Last attempt (urgency)
Stop.

**Sequence 2: Sales follow-up**

Email 1 (Day 0): Call to action
Email 2 (Day 1): Objection handling
Email 3 (Day 3): Social proof
Email 4 (Day 7): Final nudge
Stop.

**Sequence 3: Long-form nurture**

Email 1 (Day 0): Intro
Email 2 (Day 3): Education
Email 3 (Day 7): Case study
Email 4 (Day 10): Different angle
Email 5 (Day 14): Final offer
Stop.

Use these. Don't create custom sequences until you know what works.

Check weekly. Modify based on data.

## Common mistakes

**Mistake 1: 10+ email sequence.** Most prospects ignore after email 3. 80% who reply do so in first 2 emails. Shorten sequences.

**Mistake 2: Generic emails.** "Checking in!" converts at 1%. Specific emails ("I saw your post on X, relevant because Y") convert at 8%.

**Mistake 3: No segmentation.** Same email to everyone. Modify for role (CEO vs SDR), industry, company size.
