# Facebook Reels pays 440x more than TikTok. same video.

Facebook Reels: $4.40 per 1,000 views.
TikTok: $0.01-$1.00 per 1,000 views.
YouTube Shorts: about $0.04 per 1,000 views.

Same video. Three platforms. One pays 4-440x more than the others.

If you're posting short-form content anywhere and NOT cross-posting to Facebook Reels, you're leaving the majority of your revenue on the table.

## The numbers

Let's run the math on a video that gets 100,000 views:

| Platform | RPM | Revenue |
|----------|-----|---------|
| Facebook Reels | $4.40 | $440 |
| TikTok (high end) | $1.00 | $100 |
| TikTok (low end) | $0.01 | $1 |
| YouTube Shorts | $0.04 | $4 |

At 100K views, Facebook Reels pays $440. TikTok at its best pays $100. At its worst, $1.

This is the single biggest platform arbitrage in short-form content right now.

## Why nobody talks about this

Three reasons:

**1. Facebook isn't "cool."** Creators under 30 ignore Facebook because it feels like their parents' platform. That's exactly why it pays more. Less creator supply, more advertiser demand. Supply-demand imbalance = higher CPMs.

**2. TikTok has better distribution.** TikTok's algorithm is better at going viral. But viral with $0.01 RPM is worse than moderate reach with $4.40 RPM. A video that gets 10K views on Facebook Reels ($44) beats a video that gets 100K views on TikTok at the low end ($1-100).

**3. Cross-posting feels like "extra work."** It isn't. Download your TikTok video. Upload to Facebook. Same video. Same caption. 60 seconds of extra work per video for potentially 4-440x more revenue.

## The cross-posting workflow

**Step 1:** Create your video natively on TikTok (or wherever you edit).

**Step 2:** Download without watermark. Use SnapTik.app or download before posting.

**Step 3:** Upload to Facebook Reels. Adjust caption for Facebook's audience (slightly more professional, slightly older demographic).

**Step 4:** Upload to YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels while you're at it.

Total extra time: 3-5 minutes per video. Four platforms instead of one.

**Automation option:** Publer ($12/month) or Repurpose.io ($25/month) can auto-cross-post to multiple platforms. Set it once and every video goes everywhere.

## The Facebook Reels algorithm

Facebook Reels is currently in a growth phase. Meta is pushing Reels hard to compete with TikTok. This means:

- Higher organic reach than Instagram Reels
- Better RPMs (they're paying creators more to attract content)
- Less competition (fewer creators posting)
- Older, higher-income audience (advertisers pay more for this demographic)

The high RPM won't last forever. Meta will eventually adjust as more creators join. The window is now.

## What content works on Facebook Reels

Facebook's audience skews older (25-55) and more mainstream than TikTok.

**What performs well:**
- How-to content (practical tips, life hacks)
- Before/after transformations
- Motivational content with text overlays
- Finance and business tips
- Health and fitness advice
- Faith and family content

**What doesn't work as well:**
- Gen Z humor and references
- Dance trends
- Ultra-fast editing with Gen Z pacing
- Niche internet culture

Without Facebook Reels, that's $524/month. With it, $2,284. Facebook Reels accounts for 77% of the revenue despite having the lowest view count.

## Who should care about this

Anyone creating short-form video content:
- Faceless channels (text-on-screen, slideshow, AI narration)
- UGC creators

If you're already creating the content, cross-posting is almost free incremental revenue.
