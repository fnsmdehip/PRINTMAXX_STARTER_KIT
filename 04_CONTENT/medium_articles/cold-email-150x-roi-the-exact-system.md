# Cold email: 150x ROI on $50/month. the exact system.

I spent $50 on cold email infrastructure this month. Generated $7,500 in closed deals.

That's 150x ROI. No paid ads. No content marketing. No referrals. Just emails to strangers who became customers.

Here's every piece of the system.

## The infrastructure

Most people fail at cold email because they skip infrastructure. They send from their main domain, get flagged as spam, and conclude "cold email doesn't work."

Cold email works. Your setup didn't.

**Domain setup:**
Buy 3 domains similar to your main domain. If your business is printmaxx.com, buy printmaxx.io, getprintmaxx.com, printmaxxhq.com. $4/month each through Namecheap or Porkbun.

**DNS records (non-negotiable):**
- SPF record on each domain
- DKIM configured
- DMARC policy set
- Custom tracking domain (not Instantly's default)

**Warmup:**
Instantly handles this automatically. 2-3 weeks of sending fake emails back and forth between inboxes to build sender reputation. Don't skip this. Don't rush it.

**Sending limits:**
30-40 emails per inbox per day. 3 inboxes per domain. 9 inboxes total = ~300 emails per day. Scale slowly. If bounce rate exceeds 3%, slow down.

## The targeting

Bad targeting kills everything. If you email the wrong people, your open rates collapse and your domain reputation tanks.

**Lead sourcing:**
Apollo.io (free tier) for finding contacts. Filter by:
- Company size: 10-200 employees (big enough to have budget, small enough to read cold email)
- Job title: Founder, CEO, Head of Marketing, VP Growth
- Industry: whatever your service solves for
- Recently hired in marketing (trigger event = they're investing in growth)

**List quality:**
Verify every email with NeverBounce or ZeroBounce before sending. Costs $3-5 per 1,000 emails. Worth every cent. One bad bounce rate destroys your domain.

**Lead enrichment:**
Clay ($149/month at scale, but start manual). Add context: recent funding, job changes, company news. The more context, the more personal the email reads.

## The sequence (4 emails over 14 days)

**Email 1 (Day 1) - The Opener**

Subject: quick question about {company_name}

Body:
Hey {first_name},

Saw {company_name} is {relevant observation from their site/LinkedIn}.

We help {similar companies} {specific result with number}. Last client went from {before} to {after} in {timeframe}.

Worth a 15-minute call to see if this fits?

{signature}

**Email 2 (Day 3) - The Value Add**

Subject: re: quick question about {company_name}

Thought this might be useful. We put together a {relevant resource} for companies doing {what they do}.

{link to free resource}

No strings. If it's helpful, happy to walk through how we'd apply it to {company_name}.

**Email 3 (Day 7) - The Social Proof**

Subject: {similar company} got {result}

{first_name}, quick follow-up.

{similar_company_name} was in a similar position. We helped them {specific result}. Took {timeframe}.

Happy to share the full case study if relevant.

**Email 4 (Day 14) - The Breakup**

Subject: should I close your file?

{first_name}, I've reached out a few times. I'll assume the timing isn't right.

If {the problem you solve} becomes a priority, my calendar's always open: {booking link}

No hard feelings either way.

## The subject line that gets 52% open rate

"quick question about {company_name}"

That's it. No tricks. It works because it looks like a real email from someone who actually looked at your company. Because it is.

Other subject lines that work:
- "{mutual_connection} suggested I reach out" (only if true)
- "{first_name}, quick thought on {company}"
- "idea for {company_name}"

Subject lines that don't work:
- Anything with "boost" or "scale" or "10x"
- Anything that reads like marketing
- Anything longer than 6 words

## Reply management

This is where most people drop the ball. Getting replies is step 1. Converting replies to meetings is step 2.

**Interested reply:** Book the call within 4 hours. Send calendar link immediately. Every hour of delay reduces booking rate by ~10%.

**Question reply:** Answer concisely. Don't write a novel. End with a question that moves toward a call.

**Not interested reply:** Thank them. Remove from sequence. Never argue.

**No reply after sequence:** Wait 30 days. Add to a different sequence with different angle.

## What I'd do differently

1. Start with 5 domains instead of 3. More sending capacity from day 1.
2. Use Clay from the start for enrichment. Manual research is slow.
3. Focus on ONE industry first. Trying to sell to "all small businesses" dilutes your messaging.
4. Write the breakup email first. It's the easiest to write and sets the tone for the whole sequence.
