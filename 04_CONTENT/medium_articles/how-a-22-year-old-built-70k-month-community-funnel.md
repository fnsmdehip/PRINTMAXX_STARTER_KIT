*The distribution model nobody talks about, and the 3 things you can steal today*

## The distribution model nobody talks about

His name is Clavvicular. He runs a looksmaxxing community. 1,400 members paying $49/month. That's $68K/month maximum. Add coaching at $3-5K per close and he's clearing $70-100K.

But here's the thing: I've almost never seen his actual TikTok or Instagram posts in the wild.

700K TikTok followers. 237K on Instagram. Decent numbers. But that's not where his reach comes from.

He runs a clipping army.

750 people. Separate platform from his main community. They take his content and repost it to their own accounts. Some of those clipper pages have 70K+ followers each.

Do the math. 750 accounts, each posting his content daily. Multiple clipping pages with massive followings. Every video gets 750 chances to go viral instead of 1.

This is the Andrew Tate distribution model applied to a $49/month Skool community. And it costs him $1,700/month in clipper payments. Compare that to what $1,700 buys you in TikTok ads. Maybe 500K impressions if you're lucky.

750 accounts posting organically? 10x that reach. Minimum.

The audience sees content from third-party pages. They think the clipper IS the creator. Then they click through and find the real account. Perceived omnipresence.

**What this means for you:** Stop thinking about growing YOUR account. Think about building an army of accounts that grow FOR you. Clipping communities, affiliate programs, referral incentives. Your follower count becomes almost irrelevant.

## Why "new mechanism" naming changes everything

Here's the most important copywriting move in his entire funnel.

He doesn't call his program "looksmaxxing." He calls it "Ascension."

Why does this matter?

If someone already tried looksmaxxing and it didn't work, offering them "better looksmaxxing" or "looksmaxxing 2.0" won't convert. They already have a mental model. Looksmaxxing = drink water, do facial exercises, basic tips that didn't transform them.

"Ascension" is positioned as fundamentally different. A NEW mechanism. Not an improvement on the old thing. An entirely new category.

Same outcome (look better), completely different framing.

His VSSL script does this explicitly: "You've tried looksmaxxing. That's the basic approach. Ascension goes beyond surface-level tactics into dimorphism, pharmacology, compound stacking."

For his 15-22 year old audience, that terminology creates massive perceived authority. Medical language. Scientific framing. The analyst who broke this down compared it to "making teenagers think this kid is Carl Sagan."

**The pattern applies everywhere:**

- Fitness: Don't sell "workout plans." Sell "Metabolic Override" or "Physical Ascension Protocol"
- Faith: Don't sell "prayer routines." Sell "Divine Protocol" or "Covenant System"
- Productivity: Don't sell "time management." Sell "Output Architecture"
- Finance: Don't sell "budgeting." Sell "Capital Genesis"

New mechanism = new buying decision. Old mechanism = "I already tried that."

This single reframe is the difference between a $49/month community that struggles to convert and one that pulls 1,400 paying members.

## The real money isn't the community

$49/month times 1,400 members is $68K. Nice MRR. But that's not where the real money is.

The community is lead generation for $3-5K coaching.

Here's the funnel:

1. Free content on TikTok/IG attracts them
2. Clipping army amplifies reach 750x
3. VSSL converts them to $49/month Skool
4. Timed content unlocks nurture them for 14 days
5. High-ticket coaching application becomes the next step

The 14-day gate is deliberate. Best content unlocks at day 14. Billing cycle is 30 days. By the time they access the premium stuff, they're 2 weeks invested. Canceling feels like quitting right when things got good.

And during those 2 weeks? They're being nurtured toward the $3-5K coaching application.

TypeForm survey. Budget question asks "$3K-$5K" and "credit card vs cash." Qualification built into the application.

Close 2-3 coaching clients per month at $4K average and you've doubled the community revenue.

**The pattern:** Community at $49-99/month pays for itself while filtering for high-intent buyers. Then you ascend the serious ones to $3-5K+ coaching. The community is the lead magnet for the real offer.

## The 3 things you can steal today

### 1. Build your clipping army first

Don't wait until you have 100K followers. Start with 10-20 people who will repost your content. Give them affiliate links. Pay per clip. Whatever works.

750 accounts times daily posts equals 750 chances at virality per piece of content. That's not a growth hack. That's a distribution infrastructure.

You can start this with $500/month. Maybe less if you do rev-share on affiliate commissions.

### 2. Name your mechanism

Whatever you're teaching, stop using the generic category name. Create a proprietary term.

Bad: "I teach email marketing"
Good: "I run the Pipeline Protocol"

Bad: "This is my productivity system"
Good: "This is Output Architecture"

The new name creates a new buying decision. People who "already tried email marketing" haven't tried your specific mechanism.

### 3. Gate your best content at day 14

If you have any kind of community or course, don't give everything away on day 1.

Day 0: Basic modules. Enough for a quick win.
Day 7: Next tier unlocks. They're invested.
Day 14: Best content unlocks. Inside the first billing cycle.

By the time they access the premium stuff, they've already decided to stay. And they're primed for the high-ticket upsell.
