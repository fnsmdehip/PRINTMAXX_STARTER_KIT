# I replaced a 5-person marketing team with 3 AI tools. $52/month vs $35,000.

Previously: 5 people, 160 combined hours/month, $35,000/month in salaries.
Now: me, 3 AI tools, 150+ posts/month across 6 platforms, $52/month.

Before you hire another content person, read this.

## The old setup

I ran a content operation that required:
- 1 content strategist ($6,500/month)
- 2 writers ($5,000/month each)
- 1 social media manager ($4,500/month)
- 1 designer for social graphics ($4,000/month)

Total: $25,000 in salaries. Plus management overhead, tools, and coordination time. Real cost: closer to $35,000/month.

Output: ~60 posts/month across 3 platforms. Decent quality. Slow iteration. 2-3 revision cycles per piece.

## The new setup

**Tool 1: Claude ($40/month)**
Content generation. I describe the topic, tone, and format. It produces drafts that need 10-15 minutes of editing vs 2-3 hours of writing from scratch.

What it handles:
- Blog posts (first draft in 5 minutes vs 4 hours)
- Social media posts across all platforms
- Email sequences
- Landing page copy
- Newsletter issues

**Tool 2: Buffer (free tier)**
Scheduling across 3 platforms. Queue posts, set times, auto-publish. The free tier handles 3 channels with 10 posts per queue. Enough for starting.

At scale: Publer ($12/month) for more channels and bulk scheduling.

**Tool 3: Typefully ($12/month)**
X/Twitter analytics. Shows what's working, optimal posting times, engagement patterns. The data feedback loop is what makes everything else better over time.

**Total: $52/month.**

## The workflow

Morning (45 minutes):
1. Check Typefully analytics for yesterday's performance
2. Identify what formats/topics performed best
3. Use Claude to draft 5-8 posts based on what's working
4. Edit for voice, add specific numbers, remove AI-sounding language
5. Queue in Buffer for the day

Weekly (2 hours):
1. Write one long-form piece (blog post or newsletter)
2. Break it into 10-15 social posts
3. Create platform-specific versions (Twitter short-form, LinkedIn professional, Instagram visual)
4. Schedule the full week

That's it. 45 minutes daily + 2 hours weekly = ~6 hours/week for 150+ posts/month.

## What AI doesn't do well

I'm not saying AI replaces everything. Here's where I still do the work manually:

**Strategy.** AI can generate content but can't tell you what to talk about. You need to understand your audience, track what resonates, and adjust. That's human judgment.

**Voice editing.** Every AI draft needs human editing. Removing AI vocabulary, adding specific numbers, injecting personality. The draft is 80%. The editing is what makes it 100%.

**Community engagement.** Replying to comments, DMs, building relationships. AI can draft responses but authentic community building requires a human.

**Crisis response.** When something goes wrong or a hot topic breaks, you can't queue-schedule your way through it. Real-time response needs a human.

## The quality question

"But is the quality as good?"

Honestly? The quality is better. Not because AI writes better than professional writers. Because the volume allows more iteration.

When you publish 150 posts/month instead of 60, you get 2.5x more data on what works. You can test more formats, more hooks, more topics. The feedback loop is faster.

The best-performing content is always the one that got tested and refined, not the one that was "perfect" on first draft.

## Who this works for

Solo founders and small teams (<5 people) who need content distribution but can't justify a full marketing team.

This doesn't work for:
- Enterprise companies with compliance requirements (every piece needs legal review)
- Highly technical content that needs domain expertise AI doesn't have
- Brand-sensitive content where voice consistency is critical and you haven't trained AI on your brand

## The tools I evaluated but didn't use

**Jasper ($49/month):** Too expensive for what it does. Claude handles everything Jasper does at a fraction of the cost.

**Hootsuite ($99/month):** Overkill for a solopreneur. Buffer's free tier is enough to start.

**Canva Pro ($13/month):** Useful if you need graphics. I focus on text-based content so it's unnecessary for my workflow.

3. Spend 1 hour creating your first week of content
4. Post consistently for 30 days
5. Review analytics. Double down on what works.
