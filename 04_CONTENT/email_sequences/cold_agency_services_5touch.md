# Agency services cold outreach (5 emails)

Use this for: Cold outreach to businesses for agency services (marketing, design, development, etc.).

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Value-first opener |
| 2 | Day 4 | Case study proof |
| 3 | Day 7 | Different angle |
| 4 | Day 11 | Simple question |
| 5 | Day 15 | Break-up email |

Expected reply rate: 8-15%
Expected meeting booking rate: 3-7%

---

## Email 1: Value-first opener

**Subject:** Quick idea for [Company name]
**Subject B:** [Specific observation] at [Company name]
**Preview:** Noticed something about your [channel]. 2-minute fix.

---

{{firstName}},

Saw your [LinkedIn/website/content channel] yesterday.

Quick observation: [Specific thing you noticed that could be improved]

Easy fix that might help:
[One actionable tip they can implement today]

Try it and see if it moves the needle.

If you want help with more [service area] work, I can share what's worked for companies like [similar company].

Worth a conversation?

[Your name]
[Your company]
[Link to portfolio or case study]

---

**Why it works:**
- Gives value before asking
- Shows you actually looked at their business
- Specific, not generic
- Easy to respond to

---

## Email 2: Case study

**Subject:** Re: Quick idea (reply to thread)
**Subject B:** How we helped [Similar company]
**Preview:** [Similar company] had same issue. Here's what we did.

---

{{firstName}},

Following up on my email from last week.

Not sure if you tried the [thing you suggested], but wanted to share a relevant case study.

**Client:** [Similar company name]
**Challenge:** [Problem they had]
**What we did:** [Solution you provided]
**Results:** [Specific metrics]

Timeline: [How long it took]

If [Company name] is dealing with similar [challenge], we could probably help.

Open to a quick call?

[Your name]
[Calendar link]

---

**Why it works:**
- Shows you can deliver results
- Specific numbers, not vague claims
- Similar company helps them visualize fit

---

## Email 3: Different angle

**Subject:** Different approach
**Subject B:** Another angle for [Company name]
**Preview:** The [original suggestion] might not fit. Here's another option.

---

{{firstName}},

Haven't heard back, so maybe [original service] isn't a priority right now.

Different idea: [Alternative service or approach]

This tends to work well for [their industry] companies when [specific situation].

Recent example:
[Company name] was [situation]. We [what you did]. Result: [metric].

Took 6 weeks start to finish.

Interested in something like that?

[Your name]

---

**Why it works:**
- Shows flexibility
- Offers alternative entry point
- Another proof point

---

## Email 4: Simple question

**Subject:** Quick question
**Subject B:** Should I follow up?
**Preview:** Not sure if this is relevant for you.

---

{{firstName}},

Last one from me on this.

Simple question: Is [service area] something you handle in-house, or work with an agency on?

If in-house, ignore this.

If agency, I'd love to show you our work. Might be a fit, might not.

Either way, let me know and I'll stop bothering you.

[Your name]
[Calendar link]

---

**Why it works:**
- Easy yes/no question
- Shows respect for their time
- Gives permission to say no

---

## Email 5: Break-up email

**Subject:** Calling it
**Subject B:** Last email (promise)
**Preview:** Probably not a fit. Let me know if that changes.

---

{{firstName}},

Haven't heard back, so I'm guessing [service area] isn't a priority for [Company name] right now.

Totally fine. I'll stop emailing.

If anything changes and you want to see our work, here's my calendar: [link]

Keep building.

[Your name]

P.S. If you know someone who needs [service], I'd appreciate the intro.

---

**Why it works:**
- Break-up emails get highest response rate
- Gives final easy out
- Opens door to referrals

## Finding prospects

**Where to find them:**
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Company websites
- Industry directories
- Twitter/X (search by keywords)
- Podcast guest lists
- Event attendee lists

**Qualification criteria:**
- Right company size (employees, revenue)
- In target industry
- Has budget for services
- Active online presence
- Growing (hiring, raising funds, launching products)

---

## Research before sending

**Minimum research per prospect:**
- Visit their website (5 minutes)
- Check their social presence (2 minutes)
- Find specific pain point to reference (3 minutes)

**Strong openers come from:**
- Recent company news
- Gaps in their current marketing/product
- Competitor doing something they're not
- Industry trend they could capitalize on

**Don't send without:**
- Correct first name
- Current job title
- Specific reason you're reaching out

---

## Personalization at scale

**Three levels:**

**Level 1: Basic (5 seconds per email)**
- First name
- Company name
- Job title

**Level 2: Medium (30 seconds per email)**
- + One specific observation about their business
- + Reference to recent activity (post, article, launch)

**Level 3: Deep (3-5 minutes per email)**
- + Custom value suggestion
- + Relevant case study match
- + Specific ROI projection

Use Level 3 for dream clients. Level 2 for most. Level 1 when testing messaging.

---

## Subject line testing

**Test these patterns:**

**Curiosity:**
- "Quick idea for [Company]"
- "Noticed something"
- "[Specific observation]"

**Direct:**
- "Help with [service area]?"
- "[Service] for [Company name]"
- "Work together?"

**Value:**
- "Free audit: [Company name]"
- "[Improvement] opportunity"
- "How [Similar company] grew [metric]"

Track which style gets best open rates for your industry.

---

## Timing and frequency

**Send times:**
- Best: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM or 1-3 PM
- Avoid: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons
- Test your audience's patterns

**Follow-up spacing:**
- Email 1: Day 0
- Email 2: Day 4 (business days)
- Email 3: Day 7
- Email 4: Day 11
- Email 5: Day 15

**Why this spacing:**
- Enough time to see and respond
- Not so frequent it feels spammy
- Catches them at different times

---

## Response handling

**Positive response:**
"Interested, tell me more"
→ Share one-pager and book call

**Neutral response:**
"Not right now"
→ Ask when to follow up, add to nurture list

**Negative response:**
"Not interested"
→ Thank them, ask for referrals

**Objection:**
"Too expensive/not a fit"
→ Address objection, offer alternative

**Question:**
"How does this work?"
→ Answer directly, offer call to go deeper

---

## Volume strategy

**To book 4 calls per week:**
- Send 100 emails per week
- At 8% reply rate = 8 replies
- At 50% positive replies = 4 calls

**To book 1 client per month:**
- Book 8-12 calls per month
- At 25% close rate = 2-3 clients

**Scale accordingly:**
- More clients needed = more outreach volume
- Better targeting = higher reply rate
- Better pitch = higher close rate

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Too salesy in email 1:** Give value before asking
2. **Generic templates:** Obvious copy-paste kills response
3. **No social proof:** Case studies matter
4. **Too long:** Keep under 150 words
5. **Weak call to action:** Be specific about next step
6. **Following up too fast:** Give them time to respond
7. **No break-up email:** Often gets best response

## Deliverability checklist

Before sending cold emails:

- [ ] Domain is warmed up (send from aged domain)
- [ ] SPF, DKIM, DMARC records set up
- [ ] Email volume increases gradually
- [ ] Personalization in every email
- [ ] No spam trigger words
- [ ] Unsubscribe link included
- [ ] Sending from real person, not noreply@
- [ ] Reply rate above 5%

Low reply rate = risk of spam folder.

## When to stop a sequence

**Stop and remove from sequence if:**
- They reply (even negative)
- They book a call
- They unsubscribe
- Email bounces
- They ask you to stop

**Never:**
- Keep emailing after they said no
- Send from different address after unsubscribe
- Buy email lists (terrible deliverability)

## A/B testing priorities

**Test in this order:**

**1. Subject lines**
- Biggest impact on opens
- Test curious vs direct vs value

**2. First line**
- Determines if they keep reading
- Test observation vs question vs value

**3. Call to action**
- Book call vs reply vs check website
- Hard ask vs soft ask

**4. Length**
- Short (75 words) vs medium (150 words)

**5. Social proof placement**
- Email 1 vs email 2
- Metric vs testimonial

## Legal and compliance

**CAN-SPAM compliance:**
- Include physical address
- Clear unsubscribe method
- Honor unsubscribes within 10 days
- Accurate from name and subject

**GDPR (if targeting EU):**
- Legitimate interest basis for B2B
- Easy opt-out
- Privacy policy available
- Data processing documentation

**Best practice:**
- Only email business addresses
- Clear about who you are and why you're emailing
- Professional tone
- Honor requests to stop immediately

## Scaling cold outreach

**At 50 emails/day:**
- Manually personalize each
- Track in spreadsheet
- Send from personal email

**At 100-200 emails/day:**
- Use mail merge tool
- Semi-automated personalization
- Dedicated sending domain

**At 500+ emails/day:**
- Multiple domains
- Full automation platform
- Dedicated team member
- Advanced deliverability monitoring

---

## Follow-up after reply

**They said yes:**
- Book call within 48 hours
- Send confirmation with agenda
- Prepare customized deck/proposal

**They said maybe:**
- Ask what info they need
- Add to nurture sequence
- Follow up in 30 days

**They said no:**
- Thank them professionally
- Ask for feedback (why not?)
- Request referrals
- Add to long-term nurture

---

## Converting calls to clients

**Call preparation:**
- Research their business (15 minutes)
- Prepare custom recommendations
- Have calendar ready to book next steps

**During call:**
- Listen more than talk (60/40 split)
- Ask about their goals and challenges
- Show relevant case studies
- Explain your process clearly

**After call:**
- Send proposal within 24 hours
- Follow up if no response in 3 days
- Be ready to negotiate

**Close rate should be:**
- 20-30% for qualified calls
- Higher if discovery process is good
- Lower means qualification or pitch needs work
