# SaaS founders cold email sequence

## Strategy notes

SaaS founders at $10K-100K MRR are data people. they track MRR, churn, CAC, LTV religiously. generic "grow your business" copy gets deleted instantly. what gets replies: showing you know THEIR specific numbers.

the $10K-$100K MRR range is where 70% of micro SaaS products stall (ALPHA972915). founders here face three simultaneous crises: churn eating growth (3-7% monthly for SMB), CAC rising 14% year over year, and solo founder overwhelm from wearing too many hats.

**Why this ICP works for cold outreach:**
- SaaS founders are online. their data is public (Product Hunt launches, G2 reviews, Crunchbase, LinkedIn activity)
- they buy tools. avg SaaS founder spends $2K-5K/mo on their own stack
- they respond to specificity. show them a churn number or feature gap and they'll reply
- SMB SaaS reply rates 10-18% baseline for sub-$50K ACV (highest B2B segment)
- founder-led outreach outperforms SDR-led by 30-50% in reply rates

**The angle:** you're not selling them marketing. you're solving churn. every SaaS founder who's hit $10K+ MRR knows churn is the thing killing them. existing customers generate 40% of new ARR. companies with NRR above 100% grow 1.5-3x faster. retention is the entire game.

**Personalization variables (AI-generated per prospect):**
- {{product_name}}: their SaaS product name
- {{founder_name}}: first name
- {{mrr_estimate}}: estimated MRR (from pricing page x customer estimate or public data)
- {{churn_signal}}: specific churn indicator (G2 negative reviews, feature requests, competitor mentions)
- {{recent_launch}}: recent feature, Product Hunt launch, or blog post
- {{competitor_name}}: direct competitor they're losing to
- {{integration_gap}}: missing integration their users request (from G2, Canny, changelog)
- {{growth_metric}}: specific growth data point (LinkedIn employee count change, web traffic trend, G2 review velocity)
- {{monthly_churn_cost}}: calculated: estimated churned customers x avg revenue per customer

**ICP scoring system (target 8+ out of 15):**

| Signal | Points | How to Check |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| $10K-100K MRR range (pricing x est. customers) | 3 | Pricing page + G2 company size filter |
| Solo founder or <5 person team | 2 | LinkedIn company page |
| Product Hunt launch in last 12 months | 2 | Product Hunt profile |
| G2 reviews mention competitor or missing feature | 2 | G2 review analysis |
| Active on Twitter/X or LinkedIn (posts 2x/week+) | 2 | Social profiles |
| Churn signal visible (negative G2, feature requests) | 2 | G2, Canny, public roadmap |
| B2B SaaS (not B2C) | 1 | Product positioning |
| Bootstrapped or seed stage | 1 | Crunchbase or self-reported |

**Lead sourcing workflow:**
1. G2 category pages: filter by company size (1-10 employees), rating (3.5-4.5 = room to improve), review count (10-100)
2. Product Hunt: browse recent launches, filter by "SaaS" + upvotes 100-500 (enough traction, not massive)
3. LinkedIn: "Founder" + "SaaS" + employee count 1-10 + posted in last 30 days
4. Indie Hackers: revenue milestones, product updates
5. Twitter/X: bio contains "building" + "SaaS" + "MRR" or "$" + follower count 500-10K
6. Apollo: SaaS industry + company size 1-10 + decision maker titles

**Campaign size: <50 recipients per batch.** Instantly benchmark: <50 recipients = 5.8% reply rate (69% higher than campaigns >50). Quality targeting > volume.

**Multichannel sequence:**
1. Day -2: LinkedIn connection request (personalized note referencing their product)
2. Day 0: Email 1 (45-word opener with specific churn/growth signal)
3. Day 3: Email 2 (retention case study with before/after metrics)
4. Day 5: LinkedIn DM referencing email
5. Day 7: Email 3 (different angle: competitor gap or integration opportunity)
6. Day 14: Email 4 (proof stack: 3 SaaS companies)
7. Day 21: Email 5 (breakup with free resource)

**Subject lines (A/B test):**
- A: {{product_name}} retention
- B: quick question about {{product_name}}
- C: noticed something about {{product_name}} on G2

**Body (45 words):**

```
{{founder_name}},

saw {{churn_signal}} on G2 for {{product_name}}.

at your pricing, losing even 5 customers a month probably costs ${{monthly_churn_cost}}.

we've helped 3 SaaS companies in {{their_category}} cut churn 40-60% with automated retention flows.

worth 15 minutes?
```

**Why this works:** opens with a verifiable data point (their G2 reviews). quantifies the cost in their own numbers. positions solution as specific to their category. one simple CTA.

**RETVec safety:** plain text only, no HTML, no images, no urgency words, conversational tone, unique body per recipient. passes all 9 RETVec avoidance criteria.

## A/B test plan (8-week progressive)

**Phase 1 (Week 1-2): Subject lines**
- Variant A: product name in subject ("{{product_name}} retention")
- Variant B: question format ("quick question about {{product_name}}")
- Variant C: competitor mention ("{{competitor_name}} just shipped something")
- Winner criteria: highest open rate with 200+ sends

**Phase 2 (Week 3-4): First email length**
- Variant A: 45-word opener (current)
- Variant B: 80-word opener (add one more data point)
- Winner criteria: highest reply rate

**Phase 3 (Week 5-6): Multichannel vs email-only**
- Variant A: full 7-touch multichannel (email + LinkedIn)
- Variant B: email-only 5-touch
- Winner criteria: meeting booked rate (not just reply rate)

**Phase 4 (Week 7-8): Angle testing**
- Variant A: churn-first angle (current Email 1)
- Variant B: competitor-first angle (current Email 3 as opener)
- Variant C: NRR-first angle ("your NRR is probably under 100%. here's what that costs")
- Winner criteria: qualified meeting rate

**Minimum sample:** 200 sends per variant. run one test at a time.

## Compliance and deliverability notes

**CAN-SPAM compliance:**
- Physical business address in signature
- Clear unsubscribe mechanism
- Accurate "From" and "Reply-To"
- No deceptive subject lines

**Gmail RETVec avoidance (ALPHA429):**
- Plain text only. no HTML formatting
- No images, no tracking pixels
- No urgency language ("limited time", "act now")
- No link shorteners
- Unique body per recipient (AI personalization handles this)
- Under 100 words per email
- Conversational tone, not marketing copy
- Send from warmed domain (14+ day warmup minimum)
- Monitor complaint rate: MUST stay under 0.1%

**Domain strategy:**
- Never cold email from primary domain
- Use 2-3 secondary domains
- Each domain: 2 email accounts, max 25 sends/account/day
- Rotate domains if any approach 0.05% complaint rate
