# Info product launch sequence (5 emails)

Use this for: Launching courses, ebooks, templates, or any digital info product. Creates urgency without being pushy.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Announcement + early bird |
| 2 | Day 2 | Value and benefits |
| 3 | Day 4 | Social proof |
| 4 | Day 6 | Urgency (last 24 hours) |
| 5 | Day 7 | Final call |

Expected open rate: 40-60%
Expected conversion rate: 3-8% of list

---

[Your name]

## What makes this work

**Email 1: Excitement + discount**
- Clear announcement
- Early bird creates urgency
- Specific about what's included

**Email 2: Value justification**
- Module breakdown shows depth
- Total value calculation
- Addresses common objection

**Email 3: Social proof**
- Real testimonials
- Shows others are buying
- Reinforces guarantee

**Email 4-5: Deadline urgency**
- Clear countdown
- No manipulation, just facts
- Final reminder

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Too many emails:** 5 is enough. More feels pushy.
2. **Fake urgency:** Don't lie about deadlines or scarcity.
3. **No guarantee:** Reduces risk with money-back promise.
4. **Weak benefits:** Show specific outcomes, not features.
5. **Forgetting non-buyers:** Have plan for people who don't buy.

---

## Performance benchmarks

**Expected metrics:**
- Email 1: 50-70% open, 40% of total sales
- Email 2: 40-55% open, 20% of total sales
- Email 3: 35-50% open, 15% of total sales
- Email 4: 40-55% open, 15% of total sales
- Email 5: 45-60% open, 10% of total sales

**Red flags:**
- High opens, low clicks: Offer isn't compelling or price is wrong
- Low opens after email 2: Subject lines are boring or you're burning the list
- Sales spike only on last day: Consider shorter launch window

## Pricing strategy

**Early bird structure:**
- Day 0-2: 40% off ($X)
- Day 3-5: 25% off ($Y)
- Day 6+: Full price ($Z)

Or simpler:
- Early bird (Days 0-6): $X
- Regular price (Day 7+): $Y

Test both approaches. Simpler often converts better.

---

## Post-launch sequence

**For buyers:**
- Welcome email with login details
- Onboarding sequence (how to get started)
- Check-in at 7, 14, 30 days
- Request testimonial at 30 days

**For non-buyers:**
- Wait 2-3 days (let them breathe)
- Send survey: "What stopped you from buying?"
- Continue regular content
- Offer different product or payment plan later

---

## Customization by price point

**Under $50:**
- Shorter launch (3-5 days)
- Less social proof needed
- Impulse buy territory

**$50-$200:**
- 5-7 day launch works well
- Need strong social proof
- Emphasize guarantee

**$200-$500:**
- 7-10 day launch
- Multiple testimonials
- Consider payment plans
- May need discovery calls

**Over $500:**
- Longer launch (10-14 days)
- High-touch approach
- Application or call required
- Detailed case studies

## Bonus tactics

**Bonus stacking:**
Email 2 or 3: Add limited bonuses
- "First 50 buyers get [bonus]"
- "Buy today, get [bonus] free"

Creates additional urgency without being sleazy.

**Live element:**
- "Join me for live Q&A on [date]"
- "First 25 buyers get 1-on-1 call"

Increases perceived value.

**Payment plans:**
Email 3 or 4: Offer alternative
- "$197 today or 3 payments of $75"
- Captures people who want it but can't pay upfront

## Subject line testing

Test these patterns:

**Curiosity:**
- "Something new"
- "Built this for you"
- "You asked for this"

**Direct:**
- "[Product name] is live"
- "New course just launched"
- "Early access: [Product name]"

**Urgency:**
- "Ends Friday"
- "48 hours left"
- "Last call"

Track which style gets best opens for your audience.

---

## When to launch

**Best days:** Tuesday-Thursday
**Best times:** 10 AM or 2 PM in subscriber's timezone

**Avoid:**
- Mondays (inbox overload)
- Fridays (weekend mode)
- Major holidays
- When competitor is launching

---

- [ ] Sales page is live and tested

- [ ] Email sequence is scheduled

- [ ] Support system ready for questions

## Post-launch analysis

Track:
- Total revenue
- Conversion rate (% of list)
- Email open and click rates
- Revenue per email
- Refund rate
- Support ticket volume

Use this data to improve next launch.
