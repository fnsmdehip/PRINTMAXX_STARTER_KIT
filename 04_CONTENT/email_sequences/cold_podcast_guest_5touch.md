# Podcast guest pitch (5 emails)

Use this for: Getting booked on podcasts to build authority and generate leads. Focus on value for their audience.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Guest pitch with topic |
| 2 | Day 5 | Different topic angle |
| 3 | Day 10 | Add value + flexibility |
| 4 | Day 15 | Media kit share |
| 5 | Day 20 | Simple ask |

Expected reply rate: 15-30%
Expected booking rate: 8-18%

---

## Email 1: The guest pitch

**Subject:** Guest pitch for [Podcast name]
**Subject B:** Topic idea: [Specific topic]
**Preview:** Been listening to your show. Have a topic your audience might like.

---

{{firstName}},

Been listening to [Podcast name]. Really liked your episode with [recent guest] about [specific topic].

Quick pitch to be a guest:

**Who I am:**
[Your name], [your role/company]. [One sentence about what you do and key credential]

**Topic I'd bring:**
[Specific topic that fits their show theme]

**Why it's relevant:**
- [Angle 1: unique perspective or contrarian take]
- [Angle 2: specific stories or data you can share]
- [Angle 3: actionable takeaways for listeners]

**Previous appearances:**
[List 2-3 podcasts, or say "Happy to send media kit if interested"]

Worth considering?

[Your name]
[Website or LinkedIn]

---

**Why it works:**
- Shows you actually listen
- Specific topic (not vague "I could talk about anything")
- Clear value for their audience
- Proven track record

---

## Email 2: Different topic angle

**Subject:** Re: Guest pitch (alternative topic)
**Subject B:** Another angle for [Podcast name]
**Preview:** Original topic might not fit. How about this?

---

{{firstName}},

Following up on my guest pitch.

Had another topic idea that might work better for [Podcast name]:

**Alternative topic:** [Different angle or subject]

**Why this might resonate:**
[Reason it fits their audience or recent show themes]

**What I could share:**
- [Specific story or case study]
- [Framework or methodology]
- [Surprising data or contrarian insight]

Recently discussed this on [other podcast]. Got great feedback from listeners dealing with [challenge].

If either topic interests you, I'm flexible on approach and timing.

[Your name]

---

**Why it works:**
- Shows flexibility
- Gives second option
- Proof other hosts valued it
- Easy for them to say yes to either

## Email 3: Add value + offer help

**Subject:** Guest pitch + guest referrals
**Subject B:** Can help with future guests
**Preview:** Still interested if you are. Also happy to make intros.

---

{{firstName}},

Still interested in being a guest on [Podcast name] if you're looking for [topic area] episodes.

Also, if you're looking for other guests, I'd be happy to introduce you to people in my network who'd be great fits:
- [Type of person 1: role/expertise]

Let me know if either interests you.

[Your name]

P.S. Here's my media kit if you want more background: [link]

---

**Why it works:**
- Offers value beyond yourself
- Shows you're helpful, not desperate
- Media kit makes their job easier
- Positions you as networked

## Email 5: Simple ask

**Subject:** Last follow-up
**Subject B:** Should I stop following up?
**Preview:** Not sure if this is a fit.

---

{{firstName}},

Last one from me on this.

Not sure if having me as a guest on [Podcast name] is something you're interested in.

Simple ask: Let me know:
1. Not interested (totally fine, I'll stop emailing)
2. Maybe later (I'll follow up in a few months)
3. Let's talk (I'll send my calendar)

Either way, keep up the great work with [Podcast name].

[Your name]

---

**Why it works:**
- Break-up emails get responses
- Easy multiple choice
- Shows respect for their time
- Compliments the show

## Finding podcasts to pitch

**Where to search:**
- Listen Notes (best podcast search engine)
- Apple Podcasts (browse by category)
- Spotify (search by topic)
- Rephonic (podcast analytics)
- Chartable (podcast rankings)

**Search strategies:**
- Keywords in your niche
- Competitor mentions
- Industry terms
- Job titles of ideal guests

---

## Qualification criteria

**Good fit podcasts:**
- Active (episode in last 30 days)
- Has guests (not solo format)
- Right audience (your potential customers)
- Right size (under 50K downloads easier to book)
- Consistent release schedule

**Red flags:**
- No episodes in 3+ months
- Pay-to-play (charges guests)
- Wrong audience completely
- Only interviews celebrities
- Poor production quality (reflects on you)

---

## Research before pitching

**Minimum research (10 minutes per podcast):**
- Listen to 1-2 recent episodes
- Check episode format and length
- Note typical guest credentials
- Review recent topics covered
- Find host on LinkedIn or Twitter

**Strong pitches reference:**
- Specific recent episode
- Gap in their content
- Trend they're covering
- Guest type they feature

## Topic ideas that get booked

**Format types:**

**1. The "How I did X" story**
- Personal journey with specific results
- "How I grew from 0 to 10K subscribers in 6 months"

**2. The contrarian take**
- Challenge conventional wisdom
- "Why most content strategies fail"

**3. The framework**
- Step-by-step methodology
- "The 3-step process that books 10 meetings per week"

**4. Behind the scenes**
- How something actually works
- "What really happens in the first year of building a SaaS"

**5. Future predictions**
- Where industry is heading
- "The AI tools that will matter in 2026"

## Personalization tactics

**Level 1: Basic (2 minutes)**
- Host first name
- Podcast name
- General compliment

**Level 2: Researched (5 minutes)**
- Reference specific recent episode
- Comment on guest or topic
- Show you understand their audience

**Level 3: Deep (15 minutes)**
- Suggest topic that fills content gap
- Reference multiple episodes
- Connect your expertise to their show arc
- Offer specific guest value

Use Level 3 for dream podcasts. Level 2 for most.

## Subject line testing

**Test these patterns:**

**Direct:**
- "Guest pitch for [Podcast name]"
- "Topic idea: [Specific topic]"
- "[Your credential] on [Podcast name]"

**Specific:**
- "Episode idea: [Exact topic]"
- "[Topic] guest for [Podcast]"
- "How to [specific outcome]"

**Curious:**
- "Idea for your show"
- "Thought for [Podcast name]"
- "Episode concept"

**Avoid:**
- "You'll love this guest idea!"
- "Perfect guest for your podcast"
- Generic templates
- All caps or excessive punctuation

## What hosts actually want

**Based on booking 50+ podcasts:**

**1. Easy to work with**
- Respond quickly
- Flexible on scheduling
- Show up prepared

**2. Prepared guest**
- Know the show
- Have stories ready
- Ask good questions

**3. Promotional support**
- Share episode to your audience
- Tag the show
- Provide clips and quotes

**4. Good content**
- Entertaining stories (not just facts)
- Unique perspective
- Actionable advice

**5. Professional setup**
- Good audio quality
- Quiet space
- Stable internet
- Know your recording platform

## Response handling

**"Send me your info"**
→ Send media kit link
→ Offer to jump on intro call

**"What else can you talk about?"**
→ Send 3-5 additional topics
→ Ask which fits their upcoming schedule

**"Not taking guests right now"**
→ "When should I check back?"
→ Add to follow-up calendar

**"We only feature [criteria]"**
→ If you don't meet it, thank and move on
→ If you do, clarify how you qualify

**"Can you talk about [different topic]?"**
→ "Absolutely. Here's my angle on that..."
→ Shows flexibility

## Pre-interview checklist

**Before recording:**
- [ ] Re-listen to recent episode
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 key talking points
- [ ] Have 2-3 stories ready
- [ ] Test audio/video setup
- [ ] Review host's questions (if sent)
- [ ] Prepare your CTA/pitch for end

**Questions to ask host beforehand:**
- What topics should we focus on?
- Any topics to avoid?
- How do you typically end episodes?
- Can I mention my product/service?
- When will episode air?

---

## During the interview

**Best practices:**
- Be conversational (not robotic)
- Tell stories (more engaging than facts)
- Give actionable advice
- Reference the host's show naturally
- Mention your offer subtly (not sales pitch)
- Ask the host questions too
- Don't ramble (respect their editing time)

**What to avoid:**
- Reading from script
- Going on tangents
- Arguing with host
- Selling too hard
- Forgetting the audience

**Day 1:**

**First week:**
- Create clips for social media

- Celebrate their show milestones
- Offer to return as guest

- Good hosts become friends
- They refer you to other shows
- May invite you back
- Can lead to other opportunities

## Volume strategy

**To get 1 booking per week:**
- Pitch 10-15 podcasts per week
- At 15% booking rate = 1-2 bookings
- Allow 2-4 weeks from pitch to air

**To get 1 booking per month:**
- Pitch 5-8 podcasts per week
- More sustainable long-term
- Focus on higher-quality targets

**Quality vs quantity:**
- Better to pitch 50 perfect fits deeply researched
- Than 500 random shows with templates

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Generic pitch:** Clear you're mass emailing
2. **Wrong fit:** Pitching podcasts with wrong audience
3. **Too salesy:** Treating it like ad spot
4. **No prep:** Don't know the show format
5. **Bad audio:** Using laptop mic
6. **No promotion:** Don't share the episode
7. **One and done:** Don't build relationship with host

## Scaling podcast appearances

**At 5-10 appearances:**
- Document what works
- Create standard prep process
- Build media kit

**At 20-50 appearances:**
- Hire VA to find and pitch shows
- Create clips library
- Build relationships with hosts

**At 50+ appearances:**
- Hosts reach out to you
- Create your own podcast
- Train others on your process
- Write book about your journey

Goal: Get to point where you're turning down shows, not pitching.
