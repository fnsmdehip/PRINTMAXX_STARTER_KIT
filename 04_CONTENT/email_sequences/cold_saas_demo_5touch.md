# SaaS demo cold outreach (5 emails)

Use this for: Getting product demos booked for B2B SaaS. Focus on specific pain point and quick value demonstration.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Problem-focused opener |
| 2 | Day 3 | Quick value demo |
| 3 | Day 7 | Social proof |
| 4 | Day 10 | Different use case |
| 5 | Day 14 | Break-up with trial offer |

Expected reply rate: 10-18%
Expected demo booking rate: 4-9%

---

## Email 1: Problem-focused opener

**Subject:** [Problem] at [Company]?
**Subject B:** Quick question about [their process]
**Preview:** 2-minute fix for [specific pain point].

---

{{firstName}},

Quick question: How does your team handle [specific workflow/problem]?

Most [their role] at [their industry] companies tell me [common pain point].

We built [Product name] to fix this. Cuts [task time] from [X] to [Y].

Worth a 15-minute demo?

[Your name]
[Calendar link]

P.S. If [problem] isn't an issue for you, ignore this.

---

**Why it works:**
- Leads with their problem, not your product
- Specific about time savings
- Short and scannable
- Easy opt-out

---

## Email 2: Video demo

**Subject:** Re: [Problem] (see 2-min demo)
**Subject B:** How [Product] works
**Preview:** Screen recording. Shows the core workflow.

---

{{firstName}},

Haven't heard back, so I recorded a quick demo of [Product name].

[Link to Loom or demo video]

What you'll see:
1. [Step 1 of solving their problem]
2. [Step 2 of solving their problem]
3. [Step 3 of solving their problem]

Takes 2 minutes to watch. Might save your team [X hours] per [timeframe].

If it looks useful, let's jump on a call and I'll show you how it works for [their specific situation].

[Calendar link]

[Your name]

---

**Why it works:**
- Video is easier to consume than text
- Shows product in action
- Self-qualifies (if interested, they'll watch)
- Reduces demo call time

---

## Email 3: Social proof

**Subject:** How [Similar company] uses this
**Subject B:** [Similar company] cut [task] time by 60%
**Preview:** Real customer. Similar to your situation.

---

{{firstName}},

Following up because this seems relevant for [Company name].

**Customer:** [Similar company in same industry]
**Problem they had:** [Pain point]
**What they use [Product] for:** [Specific use case]
**Result:** [Metric improvement]

Setup took them 20 minutes. Saw results first week.

Their [role] said: "[Short testimonial quote]"

If [Company name] is dealing with [same problem], we can probably help.

15-minute demo: [Calendar link]

[Your name]

---

**Why it works:**
- Social proof from similar company reduces risk
- Specific metrics make it credible
- Fast setup time addresses objection
- Clear relevance to their situation

---

## Email 4: Different use case

**Subject:** Another way to use [Product]
**Subject B:** Different angle
**Preview:** May not be for [original use case], but what about this?

---

{{firstName}},

Not sure if [original use case] is a priority for you right now.

Different angle: A lot of [their role] teams use [Product] for [alternative use case].

Example:
[Company] was struggling with [different problem]. Used our [feature] to [solution]. Cut [metric] by [X%].

Might be a better fit for what [Company name] needs.

Worth exploring?

[Calendar link]

[Your name]

P.S. We also integrate with [tools they likely use]. Makes setup even faster.

---

**Why it works:**
- Shows product versatility
- Gives second chance with different angle
- Integration mention increases relevance

---

## Email 5: Break-up with trial

**Subject:** Last one (trial offer inside)
**Subject B:** Calling it + free trial
**Preview:** Haven't heard back. Here's a no-meeting option.

---

{{firstName}},

Haven't heard back, so I'm guessing a demo isn't a priority.

Last offer: Skip the demo. Try [Product] free for 14 days.

No credit card. Full access. Cancel anytime.

[Link to free trial signup]

If it helps with [problem], great. If not, no hard feelings.

[Your name]

P.S. Setup takes under 5 minutes. Here's a guide: [link to quick start doc]

---

**Why it works:**
- Break-up emails get high response
- Trial offer is low-commitment
- Respects their time (no meeting needed)
- Self-service option for busy people

## Finding SaaS prospects

**Best sources:**
- LinkedIn (search by job title + company size)
- Company tech stacks (BuiltWith, Wappalyzer)
- Industry forums and communities
- G2/Capterra competitor reviews
- Job boards (companies hiring = growing = buying tools)

**Ideal prospect signals:**
- Recently raised funding
- Hiring for roles that use your tool
- Posting about the problem you solve
- Using competitor or outdated tool
- Company size matches your ICP

## SaaS-specific personalization

**Level 1: Basic**
- {{firstName}} and {{Company}}
- Reference their industry

**Level 2: Researched**
- Mention specific tool they use
- Reference recent company news
- Call out likely workflow bottleneck

**Level 3: Custom**
- Record personalized video demo
- Show how you'd solve their specific problem
- Reference their actual content/product

Use Level 3 for enterprise deals. Level 2 for most. Level 1 for testing.

## Demo booking best practices

**Make it easy:**
- Use calendar tool (Calendly, Cal.com)
- Multiple time options
- Automatic reminders
- Zoom link auto-generated

**Set expectations:**
- "15-minute demo" (not "exploratory call")
- "I'll show you X, Y, Z"
- "Come with questions"

**Reduce friction:**
- No forms to fill out
- No qualification hoops
- Book directly from email

## Subject line testing

**Test these patterns:**

**Question:**
- "[Problem] at [Company]?"
- "How do you handle [workflow]?"
- "Dealing with [pain point]?"

**Outcome:**
- "Cut [task] time by 60%"
- "Faster [workflow] for [Company]"
- "[Result] in [timeframe]"

**Social proof:**
- "How [Similar company] solved [problem]"
- "[Company] uses this for [use case]"
- "[Number] [industry] teams use this"

Avoid:
- All caps
- Multiple exclamation points
- "Revolutionary" or hype words
- Tricks or clickbait

---

## Timing strategy

**Send schedule:**
- Email 1: Tuesday or Wednesday
- Email 2: Friday or Monday (catches different days)
- Email 3: Mid-week again
- Email 4: Different day than previous
- Email 5: Tuesday (highest open rates)

**Time of day:**
- 8-9 AM (before meetings start)
- 1-2 PM (after lunch, before afternoon grind)
- Avoid: Early morning Monday, Friday after 3 PM

**Why spacing matters:**
- 3-4 days between = enough time to see email
- Too fast = annoying
- Too slow = they forget who you are

## Response handling

**"Not interested"**
→ "No problem. Can I ask what you're using instead?"
→ Learn about competition, move to long-term nurture

**"Send me more info"**
→ Send one-pager and book demo
→ "Best way to show this is 15-min demo: [calendar]"

**"What's the price?"**
→ "Depends on [variable]. Quick demo and I'll send custom quote."
→ Don't give price without context

**"Too busy right now"**
→ "When should I follow up?"
→ Set reminder, add to nurture list

**"We use [Competitor]"**
→ "How's that working? Happy to show differences if you're ever reviewing options."
→ Add to competitive tracking list

## Demo call conversion

**Pre-call prep:**
- Research their company (10 minutes)
- Prepare customized demo flow
- Have pricing calculator ready
- Check if they're in your sweet spot

**During demo (15 minutes):**
- Minutes 0-3: Discovery (what's their current process?)
- Minutes 3-12: Demo focused on their use case
- Minutes 12-15: Q&A and next steps

**Post-call:**
- Send recording + trial link within 1 hour
- Follow up in 2 days if no activity
- Propose implementation call if interested

---

## Objection handling

**"Too expensive"**
→ Show ROI: "Your team spends [X hours] on [task]. At $[wage], that's $[cost] per month. We're $[lower price]."

**"Too complicated"**
→ "Setup takes 5 minutes. I'll do it with you on this call."

**"Already using [Competitor]"**
→ "What do you wish it did better?" (find gaps)

**"Need to check with team"**
→ "Who needs to be involved? Let's get them on a call."

**"Not the right time"**
→ "What needs to happen first?" (uncover real objection)

## Volume math

**To close 4 deals per month:**
- Need ~16 demos (at 25% close rate)
- Need ~160-320 emails sent (at 5-10% demo booking)
- Need ~20 emails per day (business days)

**To close 10 deals per month:**
- Need ~40 demos
- Need ~400-800 emails sent
- Need ~50 emails per day

**Scale factors:**
- Better targeting = fewer emails needed
- Better demo = higher close rate
- Better pricing fit = higher conversion

## Deliverability for SaaS cold email

**Critical setup:**
- Dedicated domain for outreach
- Warm up domain (start with 20 emails/day, increase slowly)
- SPF, DKIM, DMARC properly configured
- Professional email signature
- Real person sender name

**Avoid:**
- Sending from primary company domain
- Blasting 500 emails day one
- Generic noreply@ addresses
- Long URLs (use branded short links)
- Attachments in first email

**Monitor:**
- Reply rate (should be 8%+)
- Bounce rate (keep under 2%)
- Spam complaints (keep at 0%)

## A/B testing priority

**Test in this order:**

**1. Offer (biggest impact):**
- Demo vs trial vs "more info"
- 15-min vs 30-min demo
- Video demo vs written case study

**2. Subject line:**
- Question vs statement
- Problem-focused vs solution-focused
- With/without company name

**3. Email length:**
- 50 words vs 100 words vs 150 words

**4. Social proof:**
- Testimonial vs metric vs logo
- Email 1 vs email 3

**5. CTA:**
- Calendar link vs "reply to book"
- Hard ask vs soft ask

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Feature dumping:** Talk about their problem, not your features
2. **Too long:** SaaS buyers are busy, keep it under 100 words
3. **No demo video:** Video converts better than text
4. **Weak social proof:** Use specific companies and metrics
5. **Vague value prop:** "Save time" is weak. "Cut data entry from 2 hours to 10 minutes" is strong
6. **No trial offer:** Self-service trial is great fallback
7. **Pushy follow-up:** Respect their time and inbox

- Manually personalize
- Send from personal email
- Track in spreadsheet

- Semi-automated with mail merge

## Follow-up sequences

**After demo (no show):**
- Day 0: "Missed you on the call. Here's recording: [video]"
- Day 2: "Want to reschedule?"
- Day 5: Send trial link

**After demo (positive):**
- Hour 0: Send recording + trial access
- Day 1: "How's the trial going?"
- Day 3: Check in on specific feature
- Day 7: "Ready to go live?"
- Day 13: "Trial ends tomorrow"

**After demo (unsure):**
- Day 0: "What info do you need?"
- Day 3: Send case study
- Day 7: "Should I check back later?"

Keep people moving through the pipeline. Momentum matters.
