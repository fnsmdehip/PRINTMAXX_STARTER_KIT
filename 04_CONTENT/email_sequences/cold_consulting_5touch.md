# Consulting cold outreach (5 emails)

Use this for: Reaching out to potential consulting clients. Emphasizes expertise and specific ROI.

## Sequence overview

| Email | Timing | Goal |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Insight-based opener |
| 2 | Day 4 | ROI calculation |
| 3 | Day 8 | Case study |
| 4 | Day 12 | Simple ask |
| 5 | Day 16 | Break-up with resource |

Expected reply rate: 10-20%
Expected call booking rate: 5-12%

---

## Email 1: Insight-based opener

**Subject:** [Specific observation] at [Company]
**Subject B:** Quick thought on [their challenge]
**Preview:** Noticed something about your [situation]. May be helpful.

---

{{firstName}},

Been following [Company name]'s [recent activity: launch, funding, expansion].

Noticed [specific observation about their business or market position].

Based on what I've seen with [similar companies], this usually means [insight about their likely challenge].

I help [their industry] companies with [specific outcome]. Happy to share what's worked for similar situations.

Worth a conversation?

[Your name]
[Your credentials]
[Calendar link]

---

**Why it works:**
- Shows you did research
- Leads with insight, not pitch
- Demonstrates expertise without being pushy
- Relevant to their current situation

---

## Email 2: ROI calculation

**Subject:** Re: [Observation] (see math)
**Subject B:** The ROI on fixing [problem]
**Preview:** Ran some numbers. Might be interesting.

---

{{firstName}},

Following up on my email about [observation].

Did some quick math on the [problem] I mentioned:

**Current cost:**
[Specific calculation showing what the problem costs them]

**Typical improvement:**
[Metric improvement you help achieve]

**Net impact:**
[Financial benefit calculation]

Timeline: [How long it takes]

This is based on [number] similar engagements with [type of companies].

If the math is interesting, let's talk about whether it's achievable for [Company name].

[Calendar link]

[Your name]

---

**Why it works:**
- Concrete ROI makes decision easier
- Shows you understand their economics
- Specific numbers build credibility
- Low-pressure invitation

---

## Email 3: Case study

**Subject:** How [Similar company] achieved [result]
**Subject B:** Relevant case study for [Company]
**Preview:** [Similar company] faced same challenge. Here's what we did.

---

{{firstName}},

Wanted to share a relevant example.

**Client:** [Similar company]
**Their situation:** [Problem they faced]

**What we did:**
[High-level approach in 3-4 points]

**Results after [timeframe]:**
- [Metric 1]
- [Metric 2]
- [Metric 3]

Investment: [Project budget range]
ROI: [X]x in [timeframe]

If [Company name] is dealing with [similar challenge], we could map out something similar.

Open to a conversation?

[Calendar link]

[Your name]

P.S. Happy to send the full case study if you want more detail.

---

**Why it works:**
- Proof you can deliver results
- Similar company helps them visualize fit
- ROI makes business case
- Specific timeframe sets expectations

---

## Email 4: Simple question

**Subject:** Quick question about [topic]
**Subject B:** Should I keep following up?
**Preview:** Not sure if this is relevant for you right now.

---

{{firstName}},

Last follow-up from me on this.

Quick question: Is [challenge area] something you're actively working on, or not a focus right now?

If not a focus, totally fine. I'll stop emailing.

If it's on your radar, I'd be happy to share:
- How [similar company] approached it
- What typically works in [their industry]
- Ballpark timeline and investment

Either way, let me know.

[Your name]
[Calendar link]

---

**Why it works:**
- Easy yes/no question
- Gives them permission to say no
- Clear about what you'll share on call
- Respectful of their time

---

## Email 5: Break-up with resource

**Subject:** Last one (free resource inside)
**Subject B:** Calling it + something useful
**Preview:** Haven't heard back. Here's something that might help anyway.

---

{{firstName}},

Haven't heard back, so I'm guessing [challenge] isn't a priority for [Company name] right now.

No problem. I'll stop emailing.

One last thing: I put together a guide on [topic relevant to their challenge].

[Link to free resource]

It covers:
- [Key insight 1]
- [Key insight 2]
- [Key insight 3]

Based on what I've seen work for [number] companies in [industry].

Use it whether we work together or not.

[Your name]

P.S. If anything changes and you want to discuss [challenge], my calendar is here: [link]

---

**Why it works:**
- Break-up emails get responses
- Free resource demonstrates expertise
- Leaves door open for future
- Generous without being pushy

## Finding consulting prospects

**Where to look:**
- LinkedIn (job title + company signals)
- Company announcements (funding, expansion, leadership changes)
- Industry publications
- Conference speaker lists
- Portfolio companies of VCs in your space

**Good signals:**
- Recent funding (have budget)
- Hiring in areas related to your expertise
- Expansion into new markets
- New executive hire (often brings consultants)
- Competitors recently worked with consultants

---

## Research process

**Minimum research (10 minutes per prospect):**
- Read recent company news
- Check LinkedIn for relevant challenges
- Review their product/offering
- Identify specific problem you can solve
- Find mutual connections if possible

**Best insights come from:**
- Job postings (reveal challenges)
- Executive LinkedIn posts (priorities and pain points)
- Company blog (strategic initiatives)
- Glassdoor reviews (internal challenges)
- Competitor moves (pressure points)

---

## Consulting-specific personalization

**High-value approach:**
- Reference specific company milestone
- Call out strategic challenge they're facing
- Share insight about their market position
- Connect to similar client success

**Example:**
Generic: "I help companies with growth strategy"
Specific: "Saw you just raised Series B. Based on similar companies, scaling from 50 to 150 people typically creates [specific challenge]. I've helped [similar company] navigate that transition."

---

## Positioning your expertise

**Strong positioning includes:**
- Specific niche (industry, function, company stage)
- Measurable outcome you drive
- Credibility markers (clients, results, credentials)
- Unique methodology or approach

**Example:**
Weak: "I'm a business consultant"
Strong: "I help B2B SaaS companies (20-100 employees) build sales processes that scale. Worked with 40+ companies. Average result: 2.3x revenue growth in 12 months."

---

## Subject line patterns

**Test these:**

**Insight-based:**
- "[Observation] at [Company]"
- "Thought on [their challenge]"
- "Quick insight for [Company]"

**Outcome-focused:**
- "How to [achieve outcome]"
- "[Metric] improvement for [Company]"
- "[Result] in [timeframe]"

**Question:**
- "Question about [topic]"
- "Working on [challenge]?"
- "How do you handle [situation]?"

**Avoid:**
- "I can help you"
- "Consulting services"
- "Let's connect"
- Generic templates

---

## Timing and cadence

**Spacing:**
- Day 0: First email (insight)
- Day 4: ROI calculation
- Day 8: Case study
- Day 12: Simple question
- Day 16: Break-up with resource

**Best send times:**
- Tuesday-Thursday
- 7-9 AM or 2-4 PM
- Avoid Monday morning inbox crush

**Why 4-day gaps:**
- Executives are busy
- Gives time to see and consider
- Catches different days/moods
- Not aggressive

## Response handling

**"Tell me more"**
→ Send one-pager + book call
→ "Best way to explore fit is 20-min call: [calendar]"

**"What's your rate?"**
→ "Depends on scope. After a discovery call, I can send a custom proposal."
→ Never give rate without context

**"Not right now"**
→ "When should I check back?"
→ Add to nurture list, follow up in 3 months

**"Send me a proposal"**
→ "I need to understand your situation first. 20 minutes this week?"
→ Don't write blind proposals

**"We handle this in-house"**
→ "Makes sense. What would make you consider external help?"
→ Learn what would trigger need

---

## Discovery call framework

**Structure for 30-minute call:**

**Minutes 0-5: Rapport**
- Brief intro
- How they heard about you
- Set agenda

**Minutes 5-20: Discovery**
- Current situation
- What they've tried
- Why it hasn't worked
- Goals and timeline
- Budget expectations

**Minutes 20-28: High-level approach**
- How you'd approach their situation
- Similar client example
- Ballpark timeline
- General investment range

**Minutes 28-30: Next steps**
- If good fit: Propose written assessment or proposal
- If not: Refer to better fit
- Timeline for next step

---

## Proposal best practices

**Include:**
- Clear understanding of their problem
- Your approach (phases/milestones)
- Specific deliverables
- Timeline
- Investment
- Success metrics
- Terms and next steps

**Don't include:**
- Generic methodology descriptions
- Excessive credentials
- Multiple pricing options (confusing)
- Vague outcomes

**Follow up:**
- Day 0: Send proposal, schedule review call
- Day 3: "Have you had a chance to review?"
- Day 7: "Any questions I can answer?"
- Day 10: "Should I follow up later?"

## Pricing strategies

**Project-based:**
- Fixed fee for defined scope
- Clear deliverables
- Payment: 50% upfront, 50% on completion
- Best for: Well-defined problems

**Retainer:**
- Monthly fee for ongoing support
- Defined hours or outcomes
- Quarterly or annual commitment
- Best for: Ongoing needs

**Value-based:**
- Price tied to outcome
- Percentage of savings/revenue
- Requires clear measurement
- Best for: Financial outcomes

**Hourly:**
- Rarely best option
- Caps your earning potential
- Use only for small exploratory work

## Objection handling

**"Too expensive"**
→ Show ROI: "At $[rate], if we achieve [outcome], your return is [X]x in [timeframe]"

**"Need to think about it"**
→ "What specifically do you need to think about?" (uncover real objection)

**"Want to see if budget approves"**
→ "Who needs to approve? Happy to join that conversation."

**"Timing isn't right"**
→ "What needs to happen first?" (find real blocker)

**"Used a consultant before, didn't work out"**
→ "What went wrong?" (learn and differentiate)

## Volume math

**To close 2 consulting clients per month:**
- Need ~8-10 discovery calls (25% close rate)
- Need ~80-200 cold emails (5-10% call booking)
- Need ~15-20 emails per day

**To close 5 clients per month:**
- Need ~20-25 discovery calls
- Need ~200-500 cold emails
- Need ~40-50 emails per day

**Optimization levers:**
- Better targeting = fewer emails needed
- Stronger positioning = higher response rate
- Better discovery = higher close rate
- Referrals = warmer leads, higher conversion

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Generic outreach:** Consulting is personal, template kills credibility
2. **Feature dumping:** Talk about their outcomes, not your process
3. **No ROI:** Business case makes decision easier
4. **Too fast to proposal:** Need discovery first
5. **Weak case studies:** Vague results don't sell
6. **Wrong targets:** Send to decision makers, not researchers
7. **No follow-up:** Most deals come from persistence

## Deliverability

**For consulting cold email:**
- Personal domain works fine
- Send from your actual name
- 20-50 emails per day max
- High personalization = better deliverability
- Include signature with real info
- Reply to responses quickly

**Warning signs:**
- Emails going to spam
- Low open rates (under 30%)
- Bounces above 3%

If any of these, stop and fix before continuing.

## Tracking metrics

**Email metrics:**
- Open rate: Target 50-70%
- Reply rate: Target 10-20%
- Positive reply rate: Target 5-12%

**Sales metrics:**
- Calls booked
- Proposal sent
- Close rate: Target 25-40%
- Average deal size

**Time metrics:**
- Days from first email to call
- Days from call to proposal
- Days from proposal to close

## Building referral engine

**After successful project:**
- Ask for testimonial
- Request 2-3 referrals
- Offer referral fee if appropriate
- Stay in touch (quarterly check-in)

**Referrals convert at:**
- 30-50% (vs 25% cold)
- Close faster
- Higher trust
- Better clients

Goal: Get to 50%+ of clients from referrals within 18 months.

---

## Scaling beyond cold email

**Once you have 3-5 clients:**
- Launch newsletter (share insights)
- Post on LinkedIn (showcase expertise)
- Speak at events (build authority)
- Write case studies (social proof)

**Long-term (12-24 months):**
- Publish book or course
- Build community
- Train associates
- Move to inbound only

Cold email gets you started. Reputation gets you to scale.
