## Thread 3: Prompt engineering in 10 minutes

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
Prompt engineering isn't complicated.

Here's everything you need to know in 10 tweets:

**Tweet 2:**
The formula:

Role + Context + Task + Format + Example

That's it. Five variables.

**Tweet 3:**
Role: Tell the AI who to be.

"You are a senior copywriter with 10 years of experience."

Specificity matters. "Expert" is too vague.

**Tweet 4:**
Context: Give background info.

"I run a SaaS for project managers. My audience is non-technical."

AI can't read your mind. Tell it what you know.

**Tweet 5:**
Task: Be specific about output.

Bad: "Write me something about productivity."

Good: "Write a 200-word LinkedIn post about the 2-minute rule for task management."

**Tweet 6:**
Format: Define structure.

"Use short paragraphs. Include a hook, 3 bullet points, and a question at the end."

Structure removes guesswork.

**Tweet 7:**
Example: Show what good looks like.

Paste a sample output you like.

"Match this style: [example]"

AI learns patterns fast.

**Tweet 8:**
Pro tip: Chain prompts.

Don't ask for everything at once.

1. Generate ideas
2. Pick the best
3. Expand it
4. Edit for tone

**Tweet 9:**
Biggest mistake: accepting first output.

AI is a drafting tool, not a publishing tool.

Always edit. Add your voice.
