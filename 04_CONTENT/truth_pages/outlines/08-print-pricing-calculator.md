## Quick Answer

Minimum markup: 2.5x your total cost. Aim for 3x for healthy margins. Total cost = blank + print + shipping + platform fees + returns buffer. Example: $8 total cost should retail at $24-29. POD margins are tight (30-40%); screen print at volume gets 50-60%.

## H2 Sections

### 1. The pricing formula

**Basic formula:**
Retail Price = Total Cost x 2.5 to 3.0

**Total cost includes:**
- Blank garment cost
- Print cost (per unit)
- Shipping to customer
- Platform fees (Etsy 6.5%, Shopify 2.9%+, etc.)
- Payment processing (2.9% + $0.30)
- Returns buffer (3-5% of orders)

**Why 2x markup fails:**
- Platform fees eat 10-15%
- Returns/exchanges cost you
- Marketing costs not covered
- No profit for growth

### 3. POD pricing strategy
- Accept lower margins (30-40%)
- Focus on higher AOV (bundles, premium products)
- Price by perceived value, not just cost
- Premium blanks justify premium prices
- Don't compete on price with Amazon sellers
- Niche positioning > low prices

### 5. Pricing for different platforms

**Etsy:**
- Fees: 6.5% transaction + 3% payment + $0.20 listing
- Price 10-15% higher than Shopify to compensate
- Customers expect handmade premium

**Amazon:**
- Referral fee: 15%
- FBA fees if applicable
- Price competitive but protect margin
- High volume, lower margin model

**Shopify:**
- Payment fees only (2.9% + $0.30)
- Full pricing control
- Best margins when you drive traffic

**Wholesale:**
- Keystone pricing (50% of retail)
- Volume discounts from there
- Minimum order quantities protect you

### 6. Competitive pricing research
- Search your product on Etsy, Amazon
- Note price ranges
- Identify premium vs budget positioning
- Don't automatically match low prices
- Factor in your unique value

### 7. Premium pricing psychology
- Higher price = perceived higher quality
- Bundle products for higher AOV
- Anchor pricing (show "value" vs "price")
- Limited editions justify premium
- Founder pricing creates urgency
- Premium blanks = premium price justified

### 8. Common pricing mistakes
- Copying competitor prices without knowing their costs
- Forgetting platform fees in calculations
- Racing to bottom on price
- Pricing the same across all platforms
- Not testing different price points
- Discounting too frequently

### 9. Pricing calculator walkthrough

**Inputs:**
1. Blank cost: $____
2. Print cost: $____
3. Shipping cost: $____
4. Platform fee %: ____%
5. Payment processing: ____%
6. Returns buffer: ____%

**Calculation:**
- Base cost = Blank + Print + Shipping
- Fee-adjusted cost = Base cost / (1 - platform % - payment %)
- With returns = Fee-adjusted cost x 1.05
- **Minimum retail = With returns x 2.5**
- **Target retail = With returns x 3.0**

### 10. Adjusting prices over time

**When to raise prices:**
- After 100+ sales with no complaints
- When materials costs increase
- Adding value (better blanks, branding)
- Strong demand (selling out quickly)

**How to raise prices:**
- Small increments ($2-5 at a time)
- Announce improvements justifying increase
- Grandfather existing customers
- Test on new products first

### 11. B2B and wholesale pricing

**Keystone rule:**
- Wholesale price = 50% of retail
- Your cost should be max 25% of retail

**Volume discounts:**
| Units | Discount |
|-------|----------|
| 24-99 | 0% |
| 100-249 | 10% |
| 250-499 | 15% |
| 500+ | 20% |

**Minimum orders:**
- Set MOQ to cover handling time
- 12-24 units typical minimum
- Higher for custom/new designs

## FAQ Section

### What's a healthy profit margin for POD?
30-40% after all fees. Higher than 40% is excellent. Lower than 25% is unsustainable long-term. POD margins are tighter than self-fulfillment by design.

### Should I price the same on all platforms?
No. Adjust for platform fees. Etsy takes 10%+ total. Shopify takes 3%. Price accordingly. Many sellers price 10-15% higher on Etsy than their own store.

### How do I compete with $15 t-shirts on Amazon?
Don't compete on price. Compete on value: unique designs, niche appeal, quality blanks, brand story. Customers buying $15 Amazon shirts aren't your customers.

### When should I offer discounts?
Sparingly. Black Friday, launch promotions, clearance. Frequent discounts train customers to wait. Bundle discounts work better than straight percentage off.

### How do I price custom/one-off orders?
2-3x your standard markup for custom work. Custom takes more time, has higher error risk, and no reuse value. Minimum order fees protect your time.

### What's the minimum I should charge for a t-shirt?
With POD: $24.99 minimum for basic tee. With self-fulfillment: $19.99 minimum. Under these prices, margins collapse after fees. Better to sell fewer at higher margin.
