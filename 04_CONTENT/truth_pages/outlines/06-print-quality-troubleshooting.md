## Quick Answer

Most print issues trace to three causes: wrong temperature/time, poor file quality, or incorrect pretreatment. Quick fixes: Increase press time by 5 seconds (peeling). Reduce temperature 10 degrees (scorch marks). Check file resolution is 300 DPI minimum (blur).

## H2 Sections

### 1. Quick diagnosis chart

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Quick Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----------|
| Print peeling | Under-cured | More time, verify temp |
| Print cracking | Over-cured or old | Less time or fresh transfers |
| Fading after wash | Poor ink/cure | Check wash instructions, re-cure |
| Blurry print | Low resolution file | 300 DPI minimum |
| Color mismatch | Wrong color profile | Check RGB vs CMYK |
| Rough/plastic feel | Too much ink | Reduce coverage |
| Scorch marks | Too hot | Lower temperature |
| White spots | Air bubbles or debris | Clean press, degassing |

### 2. DTG-specific problems

**White ink washout:**
- Cause: Insufficient pretreatment or wrong pretreat type
- Fix: Apply even pretreatment, dry completely before printing

**Fibrillation (fuzzy print):**
- Cause: Loose fibers showing through ink
- Fix: Better pretreatment, second pass, different garment

**Fading after few washes:**
- Cause: Under-curing or low ink coverage
- Fix: Cure at 330F for 90+ seconds, increase ink density

**Rough texture:**
- Cause: Excessive pretreatment or white ink
- Fix: Reduce pretreatment, soften settings

### 3. DTF-specific problems

**Transfer not sticking:**
- Cause: Temperature too low, time too short, or adhesive issue
- Fix: Increase to 320-330F, 15-20 seconds, medium-firm pressure

**Edges lifting after washing:**
- Cause: Incomplete cure or peeled while warm
- Fix: Ensure full cold peel, press entire design area

**Cracking during stretch:**
- Cause: Over-cured transfer or too thick application
- Fix: Reduce cure time, check powder amount

**Colors not matching:**
- Cause: ICC profile mismatch
- Fix: Calibrate printer, use correct profile for film

### 4. Screen print-specific problems

**Ink not curing properly:**
- Cause: Wrong temperature or flash time
- Fix: Use cure tape test, adjust conveyor speed

**Ink bleeding under screen:**
- Cause: Mesh too coarse, off-contact wrong, ink too thin
- Fix: Use finer mesh, adjust off-contact, thicker ink

**Registration issues (colors misaligned):**
- Cause: Screen movement, garment shift
- Fix: Better screen adhesion, platen tacky spray

**Pinholes in print:**
- Cause: Emulsion breakdown, debris in ink
- Fix: Check screens for holes, strain ink

### 5. Sublimation-specific problems

**Ghosting (blurry shadow):**
- Cause: Paper moved during pressing
- Fix: Use heat-resistant tape, careful placement

**Faded or washed-out colors:**
- Cause: Wrong temperature, not enough dwell time, wrong substrate
- Fix: Increase temp/time, verify polyester content

**Color shift (wrong colors):**
- Cause: ICC profile mismatch, wrong paper
- Fix: Calibrate printer, use matched sublimation paper

### 6. File preparation issues

**Resolution problems:**
- Print: 300 DPI minimum
- Screen: 150 DPI minimum for separations
- Vector preferred for text and logos

**Color mode errors:**
- Design in RGB for DTG/DTF (CMYK conversion at RIP)
- Check Pantone matching for brand colors
- Test print before production runs

**File format best practices:**
- PNG with transparency for most POD
- AI/EPS for screen separations
- PSD for layer editing

### 7. Temperature and time guides

| Method | Temperature | Time | Pressure |
|--------|-------------|------|----------|
| DTF | 320-330F | 15-20 sec | Medium-firm |
| DTG cure | 330F | 90-180 sec | Light |
| Screen cure | 320F | 60-90 sec | - |
| Sublimation | 385-400F | 45-60 sec | Medium |
| Vinyl/HTV | 305-320F | 10-15 sec | Medium |

### 8. Fabric-specific considerations

**Cotton:**
- DTG: Needs pretreatment for darks
- DTF: Works great, no prep
- Screen: Best results, absorbs ink

**Polyester:**
- DTG: Risk of dye migration
- DTF: Works but may lift easier
- Sublimation: Best method for poly

**Blends (50/50):**
- DTG: May need adjusted settings
- DTF: Good results
- Screen: Ring-spun preferred

### 9. Prevention checklist
- [ ] File is 300 DPI minimum
- [ ] Correct color mode (RGB/CMYK)
- [ ] Heat press calibrated
- [ ] Fresh transfers/ink
- [ ] Clean garment surface
- [ ] Test print completed
- [ ] Cure temperatures verified
- [ ] Wash instructions provided to customer

### 10. When to reject a job
- Customer file under 150 DPI
- Requested colors impossible to match
- Fabric incompatible with method
- Timeline impossible for quality
- Previous quality issues unresolved

## FAQ Section

### Why do my prints crack after one wash?
Usually over-curing. Reduce press time by 5 seconds. Or transfer is old and adhesive degraded. Test with fresh transfers. Also check if customer dried on high heat.

### How do I prevent fading on dark shirts?
For DTG: More pretreatment, proper cure. For DTF: Correct cold peel, adequate cure time. For screen: Sufficient ink deposit, proper cure. Always provide wash care instructions (cold wash, inside out, no dryer).

### My colors don't match the design file. Why?
Color profiles differ screen to print. Calibrate your monitor. Use correct ICC profiles. Test print before full runs. Some colors (bright neon) are impossible to match in CMYK.

### What causes that rough/plastic feeling?
Excessive ink or adhesive. For DTG: Reduce ink density, less pretreatment. For DTF: Less powder, thin layer. For screen: Use softer hand additives, reduce ink deposit.

### How long should prints last with proper care?
DTG: 30-50 washes. DTF: 50+ washes. Screen: 100+ washes. Sublimation: Lifetime of garment. These assume cold wash, air dry, inside out.

### Can I fix a print that's already peeling?
Sometimes. Try repressing with heat (DTF/vinyl). For DTG/screen, print is permanent. Prevention is better. Offer replacement for quality issues.
