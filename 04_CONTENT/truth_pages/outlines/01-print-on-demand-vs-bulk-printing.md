## Quick Answer

Print on demand wins under 50 units or when testing designs. Bulk printing wins at 100+ units with proven sellers. Break-even point for most t-shirts: 75-100 units. POD costs $12-18/shirt; bulk drops to $4-7/shirt at 100+. Use POD to validate, bulk to scale.

## FAQ Section

### How many sales before switching to bulk?
Graduate to bulk after 50+ sales of the same design. This proves demand and reduces the risk of dead stock. Keep tracking post-switch.

### Can I use both POD and bulk simultaneously?
Yes. Run bestsellers on bulk for margins, new designs on POD for testing. Many successful sellers do 60% bulk / 40% POD split.

### What about print quality differences?
Screen printing (bulk) generally produces more vibrant, longer-lasting prints than DTG (most POD). However, DTG has improved significantly. DTF transfers offer a middle ground with excellent quality.

### How do I handle returns with bulk inventory?
Budget for 3-5% returns. Create a returns policy. Consider seconds sales or donations for unsellable returns. Factor return handling time into your pricing.

### Which is faster for customer delivery?
POD ships in 3-7 business days typically. Bulk that you fulfill ships same-day if you have inventory. Winner depends on whether you keep stock ready.
