# Thread: I took 52 digital sabbaths last year

## Tweet 1 (Hook)
Every Sunday for a year: no phone, no laptop, no screens.

24 hours offline, 52 weeks straight.

What I learned surprised me:

## Tweet 2
Week 1 was brutal.

I reached for my phone 30+ times out of habit. Checked an empty pocket.

The urge to "just look something up" was constant.

But I survived.

## Tweet 3
By week 4, something shifted.

I noticed things: birds outside, how my coffee actually tasted, my kid's questions.

All things that existed before. I just wasn't present for them.

## Tweet 4
Practical rules I followed:

- Phone goes in a drawer Saturday night
- Laptop stays closed
- TV is off (exception: family movie with kids)
- Emergencies only via landline or spouse's phone

Simple boundaries. No negotiating.

## Tweet 5
What I did instead:

- Long walks
- Actual board games
- Reading physical books
- Cooking without a recipe video
- Sitting on the porch doing nothing

Turns out, doing nothing is hard. And necessary.

## Tweet 6
The Monday after was always more productive.

Ideas came easier. Focus was sharper.

My brain had 24 hours to process instead of consume.

Rest isn't wasted time. It's preparation time.

## Tweet 7
You don't have to do 24 hours.

Start with a 4-hour block. Saturday morning. Sunday afternoon.

Put the phone in a drawer and see what happens.

The world will keep spinning. You'll be more present in it.
