# Thread: How I track answered prayers (and why it matters)

## Tweet 1 (Hook)
I've tracked 247 prayer requests over the past 2 years.

143 answered. 67 still waiting. 37 answered differently than expected.

Here's my simple system:

## Tweet 2
I use a basic spreadsheet. Nothing fancy.

Columns:
- Date requested
- What I prayed for
- Date answered
- How it was answered
- Notes

Takes 30 seconds to add a new entry.

## Tweet 3
Why track prayers?

Because my memory is terrible.

Last year I felt like "nothing was happening." Then I looked at my spreadsheet.

23 answered prayers that month alone. I just forgot them.

## Tweet 4
The "answered differently" column is important.

Example: Prayed for a job at Company X. Didn't get it.

6 months later, got a better role at Company Y. Company X had layoffs.

God's "no" was protection.

## Tweet 5
I review my prayer log monthly.

Usually Sunday evening. 20 minutes.

Questions I ask:
- What patterns do I see?
- What's been waiting longest?
- What did I stop praying for (and why)?

## Tweet 6
The "still waiting" list builds my faith more than the answered list.

Some requests have been there 18 months.

Seeing them reminds me: God's timing isn't my timeline. But He hears.

## Tweet 7
Start simple. You don't need an app.

A note on your phone works. A journal page works.

Write the date, the request, and leave space for the answer.

Future you will be grateful you did.
