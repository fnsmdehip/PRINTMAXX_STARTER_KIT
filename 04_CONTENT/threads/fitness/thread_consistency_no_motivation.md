# How I stay consistent when motivation disappears

Tweet 1:
I haven't missed a gym session in 43 weeks.

Not because I'm motivated.

Because I have systems that work when I feel like garbage.

Here's how.

Tweet 2:
1. Same time, same days

Monday, Wednesday, Friday at 6am.

Not "when I feel like it." Not "if my schedule allows."

Those 3 slots are blocked. Nothing else goes there.

Decide once. Execute forever.

Tweet 3:
2. Gym clothes by the bed

I lay out shorts, shirt, shoes the night before.

Wake up. Put them on. Half asleep.

By the time I'm dressed, I'm going. No decision to make.

Remove friction.

Tweet 4:
3. The 10-minute rule

On days I don't want to go, I commit to 10 minutes.

Show up. Do the warm-up. One set.

95% of the time I finish the workout.

Lower the barrier to starting.

Tweet 5:
4. Track the streak, not the performance

I have a calendar. X for every session.

Some days I lift 80%. Some days 100%. Doesn't matter.

The X goes on the calendar either way.

Showing up is the only metric that matters.

Tweet 6:
5. Forgive bad workouts immediately

Last week I felt terrible. Lifted 70% of normal weight.

Didn't beat myself up. Didn't skip the next session.

Bad days happen. They don't break the system.

One bad workout is fine. Two missed sessions starts a pattern.

Tweet 7:
6. No zero days

Sick? Do bodyweight squats at home.

Traveling? 20 push-ups in the hotel.

Can't make the gym? Something is better than nothing.

The habit matters more than the perfect workout.

Tweet 8:
Motivation gets you started.

Systems keep you going.

Build systems that work when you're tired, busy, unmotivated, or sick.

That's how you go from 6 weeks to 43 weeks to 2 years.
