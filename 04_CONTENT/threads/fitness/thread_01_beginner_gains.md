# Thread: How I gained 15 lbs of muscle in my first year

## Tweet 1 (Hook)
I was 145 lbs, couldn't do a pull-up.

12 months later: 160 lbs, doing sets of 8.

Here's exactly what worked (no supplements, no magic):

## Tweet 2
The program: Starting Strength for 3 months, then GZCLP.

Squats, bench, deadlift, overhead press, rows.

3-4 days per week. 45-60 minutes per session.

Nothing fancy. Just progressive overload.

## Tweet 3
Nutrition was 80% of the equation.

My mistakes early on:
- Eating "clean" but not enough
- Skipping breakfast
- Guessing at protein

Fixed by: tracking calories for 2 weeks to learn portion sizes.

## Tweet 4
My numbers:

Calories: 2,800-3,000/day (I'm 5'10")
Protein: 150g minimum
Everything else: didn't stress about it

Ate the same breakfast and lunch most days. Varied dinner.

## Tweet 5
Sleep was non-negotiable.

Before: 6 hours average, woke up groggy.
After: 8 hours, in bed by 10 PM.

My lifts stalled when sleep suffered. Every time.

Your body grows when you rest, not when you lift.

## Tweet 6
What I wish I knew earlier:

1. You'll feel small for a long time. That's normal.
2. Progress photos beat the scale.
3. Soreness isn't a sign of a good workout.
4. Consistency beats intensity.
5. One bad week doesn't erase three good months.

## Tweet 7
Total cost for the year:

- Gym membership: $400
- Protein powder: $300
- Food (extra calories): ~$1,200
- No trainer, no programs bought

Under $2k to add 15 lbs of muscle. Patience was the real cost.
