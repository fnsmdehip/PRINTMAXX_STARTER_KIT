# What 6 months of consistency actually looks like

Tweet 1:
6 months ago I committed to 3x per week training. No matter what.

Here's what actually happened.

Not a highlight reel. The real timeline.

Tweet 2:
Month 1:

Excited. Showed up 12 times.
Followed the program perfectly.
Weight on the scale went up 2 lbs (probably water).

Felt: pumped but looked the same.

Tweet 3:
Month 2:

Reality kicked in. Missed 2 sessions (work trip).
Had 3 workouts that felt terrible.
Scale weight bounced around randomly.

Felt: frustrated. Almost quit here.

Tweet 4:
Month 3:

Got sick for 5 days. Went anyway at 50% effort.
One person said I looked bigger (probably lying).
Lifts started going up consistently.

Felt: the routine was automatic now.

Tweet 5:
Month 4:

First month where I didn't think about quitting.
Clothes fit different. Shirts tight in shoulders.
Bench press up 30 lbs from Month 1.

Felt: this might actually work.

Tweet 6:
Month 5:

Someone asked what program I was on.
Old pants too loose. Had to buy new jeans.
Face looked leaner in photos.

Felt: confident. Wanted to keep going.

Tweet 7:
Month 6:

Looked in the mirror and didn't recognize my back.
Scale was only down 8 lbs but I looked 20 lbs lighter.
People who hadn't seen me in months did double takes.

Felt: wish I started 2 years ago.

Tweet 8:
Real talk:

The first 10 weeks looked like nothing was happening.
Weeks 10-16 had small changes.
Weeks 16-24 everything clicked.

If I quit in Month 2 like I wanted to, I'd never know what I was capable of.

Consistency is boring until it's not.
