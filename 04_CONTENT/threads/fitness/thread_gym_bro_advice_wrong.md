# The gym bro advice that's actually wrong

Tweet 1:
I followed standard gym advice for 18 months.

Made almost zero progress.

Then I tested what actually worked for me.

Here's the popular advice that's terrible.

Tweet 2:
"You need to train 6 days per week"

Wrong for most people.

I trained 6 days and got weaker. Always tired. Couldn't recover.

Switched to 3 days. Strength jumped 35% in 4 months.

More isn't better if you can't recover.

Tweet 3:
"You have to feel the burn"

Wrong.

Chasing the burn made me do light weight for high reps. Never got stronger.

Focused on adding weight to the bar. Progressive overload beats pump chasing.

The burn feels good. It doesn't build muscle.

Tweet 4:
"Eat 6 small meals per day"

Wrong.

I spent all day thinking about food. Preparing 6 meals. Never satisfied.

Switched to 3 big meals. Same calories. Same protein. Way easier.

Meal timing doesn't matter. Total intake does.

Tweet 5:
"Cardio kills gains"

Wrong.

I skipped cardio for a year. Got strong but my conditioning sucked.

Added 20 minutes of walking after lifting. Recovered faster. Leaned out.

Cardio helps if you don't overdo it.

Tweet 6:
"You need supplements to see results"

Wrong.

I spent $200 per month on pre-workout, BCAAs, creatine, greens.

Kept creatine. Dropped everything else. Saw the same results.

Food works. Most supplements are marketing.

Tweet 7:
Test advice on yourself.

What works for someone on gear won't work for you naturally.

What works for a 22-year-old won't work if you're 40.

Copy principles, not programs. Adjust based on your recovery and results.
