# Thread: My AI workflow saves 15 hours per week

## Tweet 1 (Hook)
I used to work 50-hour weeks.

Now I work 35 and get more done.

Here's my exact AI setup (no hype, just what works):

## Tweet 2
Email triage: AI sorts my inbox.

Rules I set up:
- Urgent (needs reply today)
- This week (can wait)
- FYI only (archive after reading)
- Spam/promo (auto-delete)

15 minutes of setup. Saves 30 minutes every morning.

## Tweet 3
Meeting prep: AI reads past notes and docs.

Before every call, I feed Claude:
- Last 3 meeting summaries
- Any relevant emails
- The agenda

It gives me a 1-page brief. 2 minutes instead of 20.

## Tweet 4
Content first drafts: Never from scratch.

My process:
1. Voice memo my rough thoughts (5 mins)
2. Transcribe with Whisper
3. Claude turns it into a draft
4. I edit for voice and facts

30-minute posts now take 10.

## Tweet 5
Research summaries: AI reads so I don't have to.

Example: Needed to understand a new API.

Fed Claude the docs (50 pages).

Asked specific questions. Got answers in minutes, not hours.

## Tweet 6
My tool stack:

- Claude: Long-form writing, analysis, coding
- ChatGPT: Quick questions, brainstorms
- Whisper: Voice transcription
- Notion AI: Meeting summaries
- Zapier: Connecting everything

Total cost: ~$60/month.

## Tweet 7
The mindset shift:

AI doesn't replace thinking. It replaces typing.

I still decide what to write, what to build, what matters.

AI handles the mechanical work. I handle the judgment.

15 hours back per week. That's the real ROI.
