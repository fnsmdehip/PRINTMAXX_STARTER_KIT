# Thread: 5 automations running while I sleep

## Tweet 1 (Hook)
I set up 5 automations last month.

They run overnight, every night, without me.

Combined: 8 hours of work automated. Here's each one:

## Tweet 2
Automation 1: Social media posting.

Buffer pulls from a content queue.
Posts go out at 8 AM, 12 PM, 6 PM.
I batch-write content on Sundays.

Time saved: 1.5 hours/week.

## Tweet 3
Automation 2: Lead capture to CRM.

Someone fills out my contact form.
Zapier creates a contact in my CRM.
Adds them to the right email sequence.
Notifies me on Slack.

No manual data entry. Time saved: 45 mins/week.

## Tweet 4
Automation 3: Invoice follow-up.

Invoice sent. No payment in 7 days.
Automatic reminder email goes out.
14 days: Second reminder.
21 days: Slack alerts me to follow up manually.

Time saved: 30 mins/week (and fewer awkward conversations).

## Tweet 5
Automation 4: Content research.

RSS feeds from 12 sources I trust.
Filtered by keywords I care about.
Summarized daily into a Notion page.

I spend 10 minutes reading summaries instead of 2 hours browsing.

Time saved: 1.5 hours/week.

## Tweet 6
Automation 5: Weekly reports.

Every Friday at 5 PM:
- Pulls my time tracking data
- Pulls my revenue numbers
- Creates a summary doc

Takes my assistant 5 minutes to review instead of 45 to compile.

Time saved: 2 hours/week.

## Tweet 7
Tools I used:

- Zapier: $20/month (connects everything)
- Buffer: $15/month (social scheduling)
- Make: Free tier (complex workflows)
- Google Sheets: Free (data storage)

Setup time: 6 hours total.
Monthly savings: 8 hours ongoing.

Pays for itself in week 1.
