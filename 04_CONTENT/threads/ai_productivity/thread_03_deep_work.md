# Thread: How I protect 4 hours of deep work every day

## Tweet 1 (Hook)
Most people get 30 minutes of uninterrupted work per day.

I get 4 hours. Every single day.

Here's my system (simple but strict):

## Tweet 2
Rule 1: The first 4 hours belong to me.

6 AM - 10 AM: No meetings. No calls. No Slack.

This is when my brain works best. I guard it like my salary depends on it. (It does.)

## Tweet 3
Rule 2: Phone goes in another room.

Not on silent. Not face down. Gone.

The mere presence of a phone reduces cognitive capacity by 10%, even when off.

Out of sight = out of mind.

## Tweet 4
Rule 3: One task per block.

I don't "work on multiple things."

6-8 AM: Writing.
8-10 AM: Building (code, systems, products).

Two blocks. Two tasks. No switching.

## Tweet 5
Rule 4: The shutdown ritual.

At 10 AM, I write tomorrow's deep work plan.

Two sentences:
- "Tomorrow's writing block: [specific piece]"
- "Tomorrow's building block: [specific task]"

Deciding in advance removes friction.

## Tweet 6
What changed after 90 days:

- Output doubled (measurable, not feeling)
- Stress dropped (less reactive firefighting)
- Ideas improved (time to think deeply)

4 hours of deep work beats 8 hours of scattered work.

## Tweet 7
Start smaller if you need to.

Block 1 hour tomorrow morning.
Turn off notifications.
Work on one thing.

See how it feels.

Then expand from there.

Your calendar is a budget. Spend it on what matters.
