# Thread: 5 AI Tools That Replaced My $800/Month VA

## Tweet 1
I paid a VA $800/month for admin tasks.

Now 5 AI tools do 90% of that work for $67/month total.

The 10% I still outsource is creative work that actually matters:

## Tweet 2
Email responses: TypeGenie ($0, Chrome extension)

My VA spent 8 hours per week answering customer emails.

Now TypeGenie suggests responses. I edit and send. Takes 2 hours per week instead.

Free for 100 suggestions per month. I use about 80.

## Tweet 3
Meeting scheduling: Cal.com ($0)

VA used to handle "find a time that works" back-and-forth.

Cal.com shows my availability, people book directly. Syncs with Google Calendar.

Free tier handles 20 meetings per month. I do 12.

## Tweet 4
Social media posting: Hypefury ($19/month)

VA would post my tweets at optimal times, do retweets, manage threads.

Hypefury does all of that. Plus it recycles top tweets automatically.

Saves 10 hours per week of VA time.

## Tweet 5
Transcription: AssemblyAI ($0.10 per hour)

VA transcribed my video calls and podcast interviews.

Now I upload audio, get transcript in 5 minutes. 94% accuracy.

Used to pay $60/month for VA transcription. Now it's $8/month.

## Tweet 6
Expense tracking: Expensify ($5/month)

VA would categorize receipts and update my spreadsheet.

Now I photograph receipts, Expensify extracts data and categorizes automatically.

Syncs to QuickBooks. Takes 30 seconds per receipt vs 5 minutes manual entry.

## Tweet 7
What I still pay humans for:

Podcast editing ($150/month)
Thumbnail design ($100/month)
Customer success for complex issues ($200/month)

Total: $450/month for creative work
vs $800/month for admin work that AI now handles

The difference: humans for judgment, AI for repetition.
