Karmic Mathematics: Distortion Energy, Self-Reinforcing Loops, and Why Patterns Repeat

2026-03-28 · UAF Series, Chapter 9
"Karma" is used here as a mathematical accounting metaphor: the system accumulates distortion energy when persistently driven away from baseline patterns. That energy changes the cost of future motion back toward alignment. No mysticism required.

Karma as accounting, not metaphysics

The objective in this chapter is not moral framing. It is: define a scalar functional E[s], derive its dynamics, and use it to explain (and constrain) persistent loops. "Karma" means the system accumulates distortion energy when it is persistently driven away from its baseline patterns. That energy changes the cost of future motion back toward alignment.

The field energy functional (distortion energy as accounting)

The distortion tensor

Let s(x,t) be the latent state used throughout UAF, defined on a manifold M with metric g. Let nabla be the covariant derivative and let nabla_0(s) denote a baseline/target gradient field (the "natural pattern" reference).

Define the distortion tensor (or distortion covector field, depending on s):

D(x,t) = nabla(s(x,t)) - nabla_0(s(x,t))

D measures the deviation of the field's gradient structure from its natural pattern at each point. When D is zero everywhere, the field perfectly matches its baseline.

E[s] definition

Define the distortion energy functional:

E[s] = integral_M ||D||_g^2 dV

     = integral_M inner(D(x), D(x))_g dV

     = integral_M inner(nabla(s) - nabla_0(s), nabla(s) - nabla_0(s))_g dV

This integrates the squared magnitude of the distortion tensor across the entire manifold, producing a single scalar value that represents total field distortion. It is the total "resistance" in the field, with higher values indicating greater deviation from natural equilibrium patterns.

Core properties (what is actually guaranteed)

Four properties are guaranteed by the definition:

1. Non-negativity: E[s] >= 0 for all s. The integral of a squared norm is always non-negative. There is no state with negative distortion energy.

2. Zero point: E[s] = 0 if and only if D is identically zero almost everywhere. This means the field gradient perfectly matches the baseline at every point. This is the mathematical definition of "perfect alignment."

3. Locality: energy density depends on local D(x), with any additional regularization you choose. You can analyze distortion region by region. This enables targeted intervention analysis.

4. Scaling: if s maps to lambda * s and nabla_0(s) is fixed, then E typically scales like lambda^2. This is useful for "amplitude" reasoning: doubling the deviation quadruples the distortion energy.

The energy is minimized when s belongs to the set of harmonic sections:

A = { s in Gamma(Omega) | nabla^2(s) + lambda * s = 0 }

Where Gamma(Omega) represents sections of the bundle, nabla^2 is the covariant Laplacian, and lambda represents natural eigenvalues.

Minimizers and Euler-Lagrange equation (what "alignment" means mathematically)

If nabla_0(s) is fixed and smooth, minimizing E[s] over a function space yields a variational condition. In the simplest flat case with nabla_0(s) = 0, minimizing integral(||nabla(s)||^2) yields the Laplace equation:

Delta(s) = 0

Harmonic functions, subject to boundary conditions. In the general case, the minimizer solves a driven harmonic condition:

Delta_g(s) = div(nabla_0(s))    (with boundary terms depending on how nabla_0(s) is defined)

Interpretation: "alignment" is not a vibe. It is solving the appropriate boundary-value problem.

1D standing wave example (energy decay under damping)

For a 1D pattern s(x,t) = A * sin(kx) * exp(-gamma * t) on a domain of length L with nabla_0(s) = 0:

E(s) = integral_0^L (ds/dx)^2 dx

     = integral_0^L k^2 * A^2 * cos^2(kx) * exp(-2*gamma*t) dx

     = (k^2 * A^2 * L / 2) * exp(-2*gamma*t)

Energy decays exponentially when damping is present. This is the cleanest "karmic clearance" toy model. No mysticism required. The distortion dissipates at rate 2*gamma, meaning the half-life of distortion energy is ln(2) / (2*gamma). Higher damping means faster clearance.

Self-reinforcing distortion loops (positive feedback)

A loop is a dynamical regime where distortion produces conditions that increase distortion. A minimal abstract form:

d_t(s) = L[s] + F(s)

F(s) proportional to G(s) * D(s)

Where G(s) is a gain/gating term (physiology, incentives, attention, protocol state) and D(s) is the distortion defined above.

Stability claim: if G(s) increases with ||D|| over some region (stress leads to worse decisions leads to worse environment leads to more stress), then the equilibrium D = 0 can lose stability and trajectories run to higher distortion basins. This is the mathematical formalization of vicious cycles.

Kill switches (self-reinforcing loops):
1. If G(s) is empirically bounded and negative feedback dominates, then "runaway karma" narratives are wrong. The system is self-correcting.
2. If loops only appear in language reports but not in measurable proxies (behavior + physiology), then the loop model is redundant.

Sources, sinks, and standing-wave persistence (why loops repeat)

Let the canonical UAF dynamics include sources J, observer coupling gamma*chi, diffusion alpha, and sinks sigma(x,t):

d_t(s) = -alpha * Delta(s) + beta * inner(D, nabla(s)) + gamma(t)*chi(t) + J(x,t) - kappa * sigma(x,t) * (s - s_0)

Then the distortion energy typically obeys an inequality of the form:

dE/dt  <=  -2*kappa * integral_M sigma(x,t) * ||D||^2 dV
           -2*alpha * integral_M ||nabla(D)||^2 dV
           + (source/gain injection terms)

Interpretation of each term:

  • If sinks are matched (sigma large where D is large), E decays fast. The distortion is being dissipated exactly where it concentrates
  • If sinks are mistuned or suppressed where distortion concentrates, standing-wave modes persist and reappear ("same loop, new day"). The system keeps encountering the same patterns because the dissipation mechanism is not aligned with where distortion lives

Sink matching operator

Operational proxy (coarse but useful):

C_sink(t) = integral_M sigma(x,t) dV

Gamma(t) = integral_M sigma * ||D||^2 dV  /  integral_M ||D||^2 dV

Where Gamma(t) measures whether sinks are geographically matched to distortion. C_sink measures total sink capacity. Gamma measures sink efficiency: how well the available dissipation aligns with where distortion actually lives.

If Gamma(t) is high, sinks are matched to distortion and energy decays efficiently. If Gamma(t) is low despite high C_sink, you have plenty of dissipation capacity but it is pointed at the wrong places.

Sink matching and lifestyle norms (behavior as energy injection schedule)

Social enforcement as a sink-activation gradient (formalized)

Treat "social backlash" as a thresholded response field A_s(t) triggered by deviation d(t) from the local norm/tide. A minimal model:

Deviation magnitude:

d(t) = ||x(t) - x_star(t)||

(or a projected mismatch along the dominant norm axis)

Response activation:

A_s(t) = sigma(beta * d(t) + gamma * d_dot(t) - theta)

Cost to the agent:

C_dot = A_s(t) * c_0

(piecewise or saturating)

Strategic indulgence is then a control policy that keeps d and d_dot below the activation boundary while still moving toward the target state. One cannot fully reject norm structures without triggering overwhelming resistance. Strategic indulgence involves engaging with the system just enough to maintain operational security while strengthening autonomy internally.

Strategic indulgence (clearance condition)

Model a behavior stream as impulsive injections into J:

J(t) = sum_i a_i * delta(t - t_i)

==>  Delta_E ~ sum_i Delta_E_i - integral(sink dissipation) dt

A "strategic indulgence" regime is periodic injections with a guaranteed clearance constraint:

sum_{t_i in [T, T+tau]} Delta_E_i  <=  integral_T^{T+tau} 2*kappa * integral_M sigma * ||D||^2 dV dt

This is the clearance condition. Indulgence is permitted if sink dissipation is sufficient to prevent accumulation. If the inequality fails for many cycles, accumulation is not a moral failure. It is a math outcome.

Extrapolations: attention economy, institutions, and collective loops

Let a population-level coarse state S(x,t) exist on a social manifold. Then "polarization" can be modeled as multimodal attractors plus energy injection:

d_t(S) = L[S] - sigma_S * (S - S_0) + J_media(t) + J_incentives(t) + eta

A norm or institution is "aligned" (in this technical sense) if it increases Gamma_S(t) (sink matching) or reduces persistent source injection that amplifies standing-wave social modes (fear loops, outrage loops, prestige spirals).

Discriminating predictions

What should be true if this chapter's mechanism is carrying real explanatory load:

  • "Karmic" variables should reduce to measurable feedback loops that constrain future action space
  • Norm enforcement should behave as sink activation: deviation increases dissipation cost, reducing control energy
  • Reinforcement regimes should increase stability margins and reduce variance over time

Breakpoints

Kill switches (Chapter 9):
1. If "karmic" terms cannot map to operational loops, they remain metaphor.
2. If enforcement does not increase costs / reduce degrees of freedom, the sink-gradient model is wrong.
3. If stability margins do not improve in reinforcement regimes, the control framing is misapplied.
4. If G(s) is empirically bounded and negative feedback dominates, "runaway karma" narratives are wrong.
5. If loops only appear in language reports but not in measurable proxies, the loop model is redundant.
