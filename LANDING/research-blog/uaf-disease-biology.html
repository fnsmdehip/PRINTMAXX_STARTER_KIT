Piezoelectric microcurrent generation (ordinary physics)

Certain crystals (e.g., quartz, tourmaline) exhibit the piezoelectric effect: mechanical stress induces electric polarization in non-centrosymmetric lattices. The standard constitutive relation:

P_i = d_ijk * S_jk

Where d_ijk is the piezoelectric tensor and S_jk the strain tensor.

Coupling path (mechanism pipeline): stress/micro-deformation, then polarization/charge separation, then surface voltage/current (bounded by contact impedance), then mechanosensitive channel bias + peripheral nerve firing probability shift, then perceptual + autonomic changes.

The non-bullshit boundary: the magnitude at skin contact depends strongly on surface finish, humidity, contact impedance, and mechanical load. The claim is not "huge voltage," but that small currents can be perceptible when combined with gain control (see gain control section below).
