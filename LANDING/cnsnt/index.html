Questions

Is this legally admissible?
Consent documentation is supporting evidence, similar to a signed contract or timestamped photo. While laws vary by jurisdiction, having a contemporaneous encrypted record is objectively stronger than having nothing. Consult a lawyer in your jurisdiction for specific legal advice.
Where is my data stored?
Web app: Your browser's localStorage on YOUR device. If you clear browser data, your vault is gone unless you exported a backup. Desktop app (recommended): An encrypted file on your hard drive at ~/.cnsnt/vault.enc. Survives browser resets, OS updates, everything short of formatting your drive. iOS app: Encrypted in the app's sandbox storage. In all cases: no server, no database, no account. Your PIN unlocks the vault locally. Stripe handles payment verification separately but your consent records never touch any server.
Can my partner see or access my records?
Records are encrypted on your device with your PIN. Nobody can access them without your PIN. After 5 failed attempts, the app locks. Both parties have their own copy of any mutual consent record.
How does video consent recording work?
Open the app, tap "Record Video Consent," and point your camera at both parties. The app captures video and audio while embedding a live timestamp and GPS coordinates. When you stop recording, the video is immediately encrypted with AES-256 on your device. Nobody else can access it. The recording is linked to the consent record as cryptographic evidence. Pro feature.
What if I lose my phone?
All users can export encrypted backups to iCloud, Google Drive, or Dropbox. The backup is encrypted before it leaves your device. If you lose your phone, restore from your cloud backup on a new device.
Can timestamps be faked?
Timestamps use cryptographic methods tied to the device clock and record content. Altering the timestamp would invalidate the HMAC integrity check, making tampering detectable.
Is this just for intimate situations?
No. cnsnt has 11 templates covering medical procedures, photo/video consent, business agreements, NDA acknowledgment, event participation, property access, and more. Anyone who needs to document that two parties agreed to something can use it.
Do you sell my data?
We don't have your data. Everything is encrypted and stored on your device. There is nothing to sell. We make money from Pro subscriptions, not your personal information.
Why should I trust this app?
You don't have to trust us. That's the point. Local-first architecture means you're trusting the encryption (AES-256, the same standard used by the US government) and your own device. Not us. Not our servers. We designed it so trust isn't required.
