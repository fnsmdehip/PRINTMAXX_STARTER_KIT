Treatment Options

Injection vs. Topical Gel

Both are included in your plan. Your doctor recommends one based on your situation, but you have input.

Testosterone Cypionate

Subcutaneous Injection

  • Most common TRT delivery method
  • Injected 1-2x per week (self-administered)
  • Steady blood levels with consistent dosing
  • Small insulin-type needle (27-29 gauge)
  • No transfer risk to partners/children
  • Precise dose control
  • Less daily hassle than gel

Best for

Men who want consistent levels, don't mind a weekly routine, and want zero transfer risk. Most patients choose injection.

Testosterone Gel

Topical Application

  • Applied daily to shoulders/upper arms
  • No needles required
  • Dries in 5-10 minutes
  • Absorption varies by individual
  • Transfer risk to others via skin contact
  • Must cover application site with clothing
  • May require dose titration over weeks

Best for

Men who strongly prefer no injections. Be aware: absorption can be unpredictable, and skin contact transfer is a real concern if you have a partner or children.
