The Process

How Dosewell works

From your first visit to ongoing care, here is exactly what to expect. No surprises, no fine print.

Day 1 — 10 minutes

Step 1: Complete your health questionnaire

You will answer questions about your medical history, current medications, allergies, previous weight loss attempts, and health goals. This is not a generic form. The questions are designed by physicians to gather the information needed for a safe prescribing decision. Be thorough and honest because your doctor needs accurate information to treat you safely.

Day 1-2 — Within 48 hours

Step 2: A licensed physician reviews your case

A board-certified physician licensed in your state reviews your entire health profile. They evaluate your BMI, medical history, contraindications, and drug interactions. This is a real medical evaluation, not a rubber stamp. If the doctor has additional questions, they will reach out to you directly. If GLP-1 medication is not safe or appropriate for you, the doctor will let you know and may suggest alternatives. You will not be charged for medication if you are not approved.

Day 2-5 — After approval

Step 3: Prescription filled at an FDA-registered pharmacy

Your prescription is sent to an FDA-registered 503B outsourcing facility (compounding pharmacy). The pharmacy prepares your compounded semaglutide or tirzepatide according to federal compounding standards. The medication is the same active ingredient as brand-name versions, prepared in an FDA-inspected facility.

Day 3-7 — Standard shipping

Step 4: Medication delivered to your door

Your medication ships in temperature-controlled, discreet packaging. No indication of the contents on the outside of the box. You will receive tracking information via email. The package includes your medication, injection supplies, and clear instructions for self-administration. If this is your first time using injectable medication, your care team can walk you through the process.

Ongoing — Monthly

Step 5: Regular check-ins and dose adjustments

Your prescribing physician monitors your progress with regular check-ins. GLP-1 medications are typically started at a lower dose and gradually increased over several weeks. Your doctor adjusts the dose based on your response, side effects, and weight loss progress. You can message your care team directly if you have questions or concerns between scheduled check-ins.

The Science

How GLP-1 medications work in your body

GLP-1 receptor agonists mimic a natural hormone. Here is what the research shows about how they affect weight.

🧠

Appetite regulation

GLP-1 acts on receptors in the brain that control hunger and satiety signals. Patients typically report feeling full sooner and thinking about food less frequently.

⏱

Slower gastric emptying

The medication slows how quickly food leaves your stomach, which helps you stay satisfied longer after meals. This is also why nausea is a common early side effect.

📈

Blood sugar regulation

GLP-1 helps regulate insulin release and blood sugar levels. Originally developed for type 2 diabetes, these medications improve metabolic health alongside weight loss.

💉

Clinically studied

Both semaglutide and tirzepatide have been studied in large randomized controlled trials with thousands of participants. The weight loss effects are well-documented in peer-reviewed medical journals.

💊

Weekly injection

GLP-1 medications are administered as a once-weekly subcutaneous injection, typically in the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. Most patients report the injection is nearly painless.

⚠

Not without risks

Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, and GI discomfort. Rare but serious risks include pancreatitis and thyroid tumors (observed in animal studies). Your doctor will discuss all risks.

Eligibility

Who qualifies for GLP-1 medication?

GLP-1 medications are prescription drugs with specific medical criteria. A doctor must evaluate your individual case to determine if this treatment is appropriate for you.

General criteria (your doctor makes the final decision):

  • ✓ BMI of 30 or above (obesity), OR
  • ✓ BMI of 27-29.9 with at least one weight-related condition (type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, sleep apnea)
  • ✓ 18 years or older
  • ✓ Not pregnant or planning to become pregnant
  • ✓ No personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2

Not everyone who applies will be approved. Your doctor may identify contraindications or risk factors that make GLP-1 medication unsuitable for you.

⚕

Licensed physicians only

Every prescription is written by a licensed, board-certified physician after a genuine medical evaluation. No auto-approvals.
