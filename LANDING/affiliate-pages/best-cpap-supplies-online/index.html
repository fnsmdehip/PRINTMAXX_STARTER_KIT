Best CPAP Supplies Online 2026 — Replacement Schedule + Where to Buy

📋 Medicare Covers 80% of Your CPAP Supplies

If you're on Medicare Part B, your CPAP supplies are covered as Durable Medical Equipment (DME) — you pay only 20% after your deductible. Most people dramatically under-claim what they're entitled to.

What Medicare allows per year:

  • Masks: 1 complete mask system every 3 months
  • Filters: 2 disposable filters per month; 1 non-disposable every 6 months
  • Hose/tubing: 1 per 3 months
  • Humidifier chamber: 1 every 6 months
  • Headgear/chinstrap: 1 every 6 months
  • CPAP machine: 1 every 5 years (full replacement)

Ordering Through Insurance vs. Paying Out of Pocket

If you have Medicare: Contact a Medicare-approved DME supplier. They bill Medicare directly and you pay only the 20% coinsurance. CPAP.com works with Medicare and many private insurers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know when my CPAP mask seal is failing?

Common signs: hissing air sounds during therapy, facial skin indentations in the morning, red marks that last more than 30 minutes after removing the mask, your machine data showing high leak rates (check the ResMed MyAir app or Philips DreamMapper), or waking up with a dry mouth despite using a humidifier. If you notice any of these, it's time to replace the cushion or full mask.

Can I clean CPAP supplies instead of replacing them?

Cleaning extends the life of supplies between replacement cycles, but it doesn't substitute for replacement. Silicone degrades from cleaning agents over time. The official Medicare replacement schedule exists because CPAP manufacturers and sleep medicine researchers have determined these intervals produce optimal therapy outcomes — not because the manufacturer wants to sell more product.

Does Medicare cover a new CPAP machine?

Yes. Medicare covers a new CPAP machine every 5 years. If your current machine is 5+ years old, you're likely entitled to an upgrade. ResMed AirSense 11 and Philips DreamStation 2 are the current top-tier machines. Your DME supplier will handle the Medicare billing.

Is CPAP.com or Amazon better for buying supplies?

For insurance billing: CPAP.com (they handle DME billing directly). For lowest cash price on individual items: Amazon (Prime shipping, easy returns). For full supply subscriptions and auto-reorder: CPAP.com has a better system. We recommend bookmarking both and checking prices per-item.
