Frequently Asked Questions

Do anti-snoring mouthpieces cause jaw pain?

A small percentage of MAD users experience temporary jaw soreness in the first 1–2 weeks as the jaw muscles adapt. This usually resolves on its own. If jaw pain persists past 2 weeks or worsens, reduce the advancement setting by 1mm (SnoreRx Plus allows this) or switch to a TSD like GMSS. Anyone with diagnosed TMJ disorder should consult their dentist before using an MAD.

How long do anti-snoring mouthpieces last?

Budget devices (boil-and-bite single-use): 3–6 months. Mid-range MADs (ZQuiet, AirSnore): 6–12 months. Precision-adjustable devices (SnoreRx Plus): 12–18 months. All of the products on this list come with a minimum 30-day money-back guarantee, so there's no risk in trying one.

Can I use a snoring device if I have dentures or dental implants?

If you wear full dentures, you cannot use a MAD (it requires natural upper and lower teeth). Use a TSD like Good Morning Snore Solution instead — it attaches to the tongue, not the teeth. If you have partial dentures or dental implants, MADs can usually be used, but check with your dentist first to ensure the anchor teeth are stable enough.

What's the fastest way to see if a device is working?

Ask your partner to check on the first night you wear it. Many couples report immediate or near-immediate improvement. More objectively: the SnoreLab app (free on iOS/Android) records snoring sounds during sleep and provides a "snore score" — compare one week before and one week after using the device.

Is snoring dangerous for men over 50?

Primary snoring (no breathing pauses) is mostly a quality-of-life issue for you and your partner. However, snoring that includes gasping, choking, or complete breathing pauses is a red flag for obstructive sleep apnea — a serious condition linked to increased cardiovascular risk, high blood pressure, and cognitive decline. If in doubt, a home sleep test (available online for $100–150) can screen for sleep apnea before you commit to a device.
