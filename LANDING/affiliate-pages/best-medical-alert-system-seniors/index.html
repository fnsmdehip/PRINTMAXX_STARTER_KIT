📄 Does Medicare Cover Medical Alert Systems?

Original Medicare (Parts A & B): Does not cover medical alert devices as a standard benefit. PERS (personal emergency response systems) are classified as "convenience items" under original Medicare.

Medicare Advantage (Part C) — check your plan: Many 2026 Medicare Advantage plans include medical alert devices as a supplemental benefit, especially if you've had a recent fall, hospitalization, or chronic condition diagnosis. Common benefit names: "Safety" / "Independence" / "Health and Wellness" extras.

Medicaid: Some states (Florida, Texas, California, New York) cover PERS through Medicaid waiver programs. Check your state's Medicaid HCBS (Home and Community-Based Services) waiver.

  • Call your Medicare Advantage plan: ask specifically about "PERS coverage" or "medical alert device benefit"
  • Check Medicare.gov/plan-compare for your 2026 plan's supplemental benefits
  • Most plans that offer this benefit cover $50-200 per year toward device/monitoring costs
  • If denied by insurance, medical alert costs are deductible as a medical expense if they exceed 7.5% of AGI

How to Choose: A 3-Question Framework

Before comparing prices, answer these three questions — they determine which system is actually right for your situation:

  1. Does the senior primarily stay home, or do they leave regularly? — Primarily home = in-home system ($25-35/mo) is sufficient. Active / leaves frequently = mobile GPS bundle essential (MobileHelp, Medical Guardian ActiveGuardian).
  2. Is there a landline in the home? — If yes, Bay Alarm Medical's no-monthly-fee landline plan can cut costs to near zero. If no (increasingly common), cellular-based plans are necessary.
  3. Are family members monitoring remotely, or is this self-monitored? — Remote family monitoring (adult children in other cities) benefits from real-time GPS apps — Medical Guardian and MobileHelp both offer family dashboards. Self-monitored = basic in-home coverage is sufficient.
The decision most families get wrong: Buying the cheapest plan and discovering the monitoring center outsources to an overseas call center where agents are reading from a script. The $10/month difference between LifeStation and Medical Guardian buys you a faster, better-trained US response center. In an actual emergency, that difference is real.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Medicare cover medical alert systems?
Original Medicare (Parts A and B) does not cover medical alert systems. However, many Medicare Advantage (Part C) plans include them as a supplemental benefit in 2026 — particularly after a fall or hospitalization. Check your plan's benefits under "Safety" or "Health and Wellness." Medicaid may also cover PERS in some states via Home and Community-Based Services waivers.
What is the best medical alert system with no monthly fee?
Bay Alarm Medical is the only major provider offering a true no-monthly-fee option — their landline SOS Home system has a one-time device cost with no ongoing fees. You only pay a monthly plan if you upgrade to cellular or GPS coverage. LifeStation and Medical Guardian both offer no-contract month-to-month plans (lowest financial lock-in), but both have monthly fees.
How accurate is automatic fall detection?
Fall detection accuracy averages 90-95% for hard falls in published testing. Medical Guardian's AutoAlert and Apple Watch Series 4+ are among the most accurate available. All systems generate occasional false positives during vigorous exercise, driving over bumps, or heavy housework. No system detects 100% of falls — the manual button is always the most reliable option.
Can I get a medical alert system for a parent who lives alone?
Yes — family members are the most common buyers. Medical Guardian and MobileHelp both offer family companion apps with real-time GPS, activity tracking, and instant alerts when the button is pressed or a fall is detected. You can add up to 5 emergency contacts to most systems. This is one of the most effective purchases an adult child can make for an aging parent.
What's the difference between in-home and mobile medical alert systems?
In-home systems use a base station connected by landline or cellular, with a wearable button that works within 600-1,400 feet of the base. They're best for seniors who primarily stay home. Mobile systems use built-in GPS and cellular — they work anywhere with cell service. The best value for active seniors is a hybrid bundle (MobileHelp Duo or Medical Guardian ActiveGuardian) that covers both home and away in one plan.
Do medical alert systems require a long-term contract?
The three systems we recommend (Medical Guardian, Bay Alarm, MobileHelp) all offer month-to-month plans with no cancellation fees. LifeStation has no contracts on any plan. Be cautious of annual contracts offered at a discount — the monitoring center quality, not the price, is the variable worth paying attention to.
