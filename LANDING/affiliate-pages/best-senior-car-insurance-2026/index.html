Senior Driver Discounts You May Be Missing

Insurance companies offer over a dozen discount types. Most seniors only know about 2 or 3. Here are the ones worth checking at your next renewal.

Defensive Driving Course Complete a state-approved safe-driver course (most take 6–8 hours online) and get 5–15% off. Many states require insurers to offer this. AARP and AAA both offer approved courses for under $30.
Low Mileage / Retiree Drive under 7,500 miles per year? That's half the national average and qualifies you for low-mileage discounts with most major insurers — up to 30% off. Pay-per-mile programs can save even more.
Bundling with Home/Life Insuring your home and auto with the same company saves an average $947/year. This is the single largest available discount for most seniors.
AARP Membership $16/year for AARP unlocks The Hartford's exclusive senior rates, plus discounts on hotels, restaurants, and Rx drugs. The auto insurance savings alone pay for membership dozens of times over.
Vehicle Safety Features Anti-lock brakes, airbags, anti-theft devices, and modern backup cameras each qualify for individual discounts. Many seniors drive newer vehicles with full safety packages — make sure these are credited.
Accident-Free History 5+ years without a claim puts you in "preferred driver" status with most carriers. Some offer 10–20% for clean records. If you have this and your current insurer hasn't adjusted your rate, they're taking advantage of your loyalty.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do seniors pay more for car insurance than younger drivers?

No — not until roughly age 75. Drivers 55–70 typically pay less than drivers 25–40 because they have more experience, drive more defensively, and have longer claim-free histories. Rates begin increasing around age 75 as accident frequency increases with age.

Is AARP car insurance worth it?

AARP partners with The Hartford and offers rates only available to AARP members (50+). The $16/year AARP membership cost is recouped many times over in premium savings — often $300–500/year. The lifetime renewability guarantee (they cannot cancel you based on age) is unique in the industry and alone makes it worth considering for anyone over 65.

How often should I shop for car insurance as a senior?

Every year at renewal is the standard recommendation. Also shop after any life event: retirement (lower mileage), moving to a different state, adding or removing a driver from your policy, or after your youngest driver turns 26 and leaves your policy.

What if I have had accidents or tickets?

Comparison tools like EverQuote still show options — they include insurers who specialize in imperfect records. The General accepts nearly any driving history. Completing a defensive driving course can offset recent tickets with most carriers.

Is it safe to use an online comparison tool?

Yes — reputable tools like EverQuote and Insurify do not sell your information to anyone beyond the insurers you select. They generate quotes using your zip code and a few basic details. You are never obligated to purchase. Reading the privacy policy before submitting is always a good idea.
