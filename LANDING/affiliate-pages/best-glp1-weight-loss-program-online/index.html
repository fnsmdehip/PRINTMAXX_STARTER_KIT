Why GLP-1 Programs Are Not Regular Diet Plans

GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide, liraglutide) work by mimicking a gut hormone called glucagon-like peptide-1. When injected weekly, they slow gastric emptying, reduce appetite signals in the brain, and improve insulin sensitivity. Clinical trials showed average weight loss of 15-21% of body weight depending on the drug and dose, which is in a different league from what diet pills or meal plans produce alone.

The online telehealth market has responded to this demand by offering GLP-1 prescriptions without in-person visits. The business model is usually: you fill out a health questionnaire, a licensed provider reviews it, and if you qualify, medication ships to your door. Programs differ in medication type (compounded vs brand-name), the level of medical oversight, included services (coaching, lab work, follow-ups), and price.

A Note on Compounded GLP-1 Medications: Many online programs use compounded semaglutide, which is produced by compounding pharmacies and is less expensive than brand-name Wegovy. The FDA has publicly noted that compounded versions have not undergone the same approval process. Some compounding pharmacies have received FDA warning letters. Compounded medications are legal under certain conditions, but they are not identical products. We note medication type for each provider below so you can make an informed choice.

None of these programs are magic. All GLP-1 medications come with side effects (primarily GI: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation), require ongoing injection, and research shows most people regain weight after stopping. These are prescription medications, not supplements. Approach them accordingly.

Compounded vs Brand-Name GLP-1 Medications: What You Need to Know

The single biggest decision when choosing an online GLP-1 program is whether you are comfortable with compounded semaglutide versus brand-name Wegovy or Zepbound. The price difference is significant, but so are the regulatory and quality differences.

Brand-name medications (Wegovy, Ozempic for off-label use, Zepbound) are manufactured under FDA-approved processes with standardized dosing, established safety profiles, and batch-by-batch quality testing. Wegovy specifically is approved for chronic weight management. These cost $1,300+ per month at retail but may be partially or fully covered by insurance.

Compounded semaglutide is produced by compounding pharmacies, which operate under different regulatory frameworks than traditional manufacturers. The active ingredient is the same, but the finished product has not undergone FDA approval as a specific drug product. Compounding pharmacies are regulated by state boards of pharmacy and, in some cases, the FDA under Section 503A or 503B of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act.

FDA Warnings: The FDA has issued warning letters to some compounding pharmacies related to GLP-1 medications for issues including sterility concerns and use of salt forms (semaglutide sodium) not proven to be bioequivalent to the base form used in approved products. This does not mean all compounded semaglutide is unsafe, but it means quality varies by pharmacy. Reputable programs use 503B-registered pharmacies with third-party testing.

Neither option is universally "better." If you have insurance that covers Wegovy, brand-name is the clear choice because the out-of-pocket cost may be comparable or less. If you are paying cash, compounded semaglutide from a reputable pharmacy is what makes GLP-1 treatment financially accessible for most people. Just know what you are getting and from whom.

Red Flags to Watch for When Choosing a GLP-1 Program

The online GLP-1 market is growing fast and attracting providers of varying quality. When evaluating any program, including the five we reviewed above, watch for:

  • Guaranteed weight loss claims. No legitimate medical provider guarantees a specific number of pounds lost. Individual response to GLP-1 medications varies considerably.
  • No mention of side effects. Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation are common, especially during dose escalation. Any program that does not discuss these is prioritizing sales over informed consent.
  • Prescribing without meaningful evaluation. If you can get a prescription in under 5 minutes with a questionnaire and no follow-up, the medical oversight is likely insufficient.
  • Unclear compounding pharmacy sourcing. Reputable programs will tell you which compounding pharmacy fills your prescription. If they will not disclose this, that is a concern.
  • Pressure to commit before you see pricing. If you have to go through a full intake before learning the actual monthly cost, the program is using a sunk-cost sales tactic.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about online GLP-1 weight loss programs.

What is a GLP-1 weight loss program?

GLP-1 weight loss programs combine prescription GLP-1 receptor agonist medications (like semaglutide or tirzepatide) with telehealth medical oversight. GLP-1 medications work by mimicking a hormone that regulates appetite and blood sugar, leading to reduced hunger and caloric intake. These programs typically include an online consultation, ongoing check-ins, and home-delivered injectable medication.

What is the difference between compounded semaglutide and brand-name Wegovy?

Brand-name Wegovy is manufactured by Novo Nordisk and FDA-approved for weight management. Compounded semaglutide uses the same active ingredient but is produced by compounding pharmacies in custom formulations. Compounded versions cost significantly less but have not undergone the same FDA approval process as finished drug products. The FDA has noted differences in manufacturing oversight between the two.

How much does semaglutide cost without insurance?

Brand-name semaglutide (Wegovy) lists at roughly $1,300-$1,700 per month without insurance. Online telehealth programs with compounded semaglutide typically run $129-$299 per month depending on the provider, dose, and included services. Some programs separate membership and medication costs, so always calculate the total monthly spend.

Are online GLP-1 weight loss programs safe?

Reputable programs employ licensed physicians or nurse practitioners who evaluate your medical history and contraindications before prescribing. Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation, especially during dose increases. Rare but serious risks include pancreatitis and gallbladder issues. Safety depends on medical oversight quality, which is why provider accessibility matters.

How much weight can you lose on semaglutide?

Clinical trials (STEP 1) showed average weight loss of about 15% of body weight over 68 weeks with brand-name semaglutide. Individual results range widely. Compounded semaglutide has not been studied in comparable large-scale trials. Weight regain after discontinuation is common and documented in follow-up studies.

Can I use insurance for online GLP-1 programs?

Some programs (Hims, Ro) offer insurance navigation tools for brand-name GLP-1 coverage. Many insurance plans still exclude weight loss medications or require prior authorization. Compounded semaglutide is generally not covered by insurance. If your plan covers Wegovy or Zepbound, an insurance-friendly provider could save you significant money.

What happens when you stop taking GLP-1 medications?

Research shows most people regain a significant portion of lost weight within 1-2 years of stopping. The appetite-suppressing effects wear off and hunger returns to baseline. This is why some providers (like Calibrate) emphasize building sustainable habits while on medication. Stopping does not cause medical emergency, but the weight management benefit is not permanent.

Is tirzepatide better than semaglutide for weight loss?

Clinical trials showed tirzepatide (Zepbound) produced about 21% average weight loss at the highest dose versus semaglutide's 15%. Tirzepatide targets both GIP and GLP-1 receptors. However, it is generally more expensive, has a shorter track record, and head-to-head data is limited. Both have similar gastrointestinal side effect profiles.

The Bottom Line

There is no single best GLP-1 weight loss program for everyone. The right choice depends on your insurance situation, budget, how much clinical support you want, and whether you care about compounded vs brand-name medication.

If you are paying out-of-pocket and want a straightforward, well-supported program, Medvi is the most balanced option. If your insurance might cover GLP-1 drugs, start with Hims & Hers to check your coverage. If budget is tight, Found has the lowest entry price. If you want the full behavioral and metabolic package and can afford it, Calibrate is the most thorough program on the market.

Whichever program you choose, know that GLP-1 medications are a tool, not a permanent solution for most people. The clinical data is clear that weight regain after stopping is the norm, not the exception. Any program that does not acknowledge that reality is selling you a version of the future that the science does not support.
