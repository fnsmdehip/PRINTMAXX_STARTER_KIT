What Affects the Price

Dose Escalation

Most providers start you at 0.25mg weekly and titrate up to 1.0mg or 2.4mg over 4-5 months. Some providers charge the same regardless of dose. Others increase the monthly fee as your dose increases. Verify whether the quoted price covers the maintenance dose or just the starter dose.

Compounded vs. Brand

Every provider on this list uses compounded semaglutide. Brand Ozempic from Novo Nordisk costs $900-1,100/month without insurance. The compounded version uses the same molecule but is produced by 503A or 503B compounding pharmacies. It exists because of the ongoing FDA shortage designation for semaglutide.

What to Watch For

  • Providers advertising "$99/month" that charge separately for consultation, shipping, or supplies
  • Prices that don't include dose increases — starting dose is cheap, maintenance dose costs more
  • Required upfront fees for "metabolic testing" or "initial evaluation" on top of monthly cost
  • Auto-renewal terms that make cancellation difficult

Red Flag: Prices Under $100/Month

If someone is offering injectable semaglutide for under $100/month, ask what you're actually getting. Is it a sublingual form (less studied)? A lower dose than standard? A shorter supply? Legitimate compounded semaglutide at therapeutic doses has production costs that make pricing under $100/month unsustainable for most pharmacies.
