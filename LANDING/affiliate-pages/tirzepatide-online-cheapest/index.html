Why Tirzepatide vs. Semaglutide

Tirzepatide is a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. Semaglutide targets only GLP-1. In the SURMOUNT trials, tirzepatide produced up to 22.5% body weight reduction at the highest dose, compared to ~15% for semaglutide in STEP trials. That's a meaningful clinical difference.

The dual mechanism means tirzepatide affects both incretin pathways, potentially offering better appetite suppression and metabolic improvement for some patients. However, not everyone responds better to tirzepatide - individual variation matters.
