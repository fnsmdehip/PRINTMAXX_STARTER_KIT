Common questions

How many email accounts do I need for cold email?

Start with 3-5 email accounts sending 30-50 emails each per day. That gives you 90-250 sends/day without hitting spam thresholds. Scale to 10-20 accounts as you validate your offer. Each account should be warmed up for at least 2 weeks before sending.

Which tool has the best deliverability?

Smartlead has the most comprehensive deliverability toolkit: inbox rotation, warmup, auto-reply detection, spam score testing, and dedicated IP options. Instantly and Lemlist are close seconds with built-in warmup and rotation. Apollo lacks warmup entirely, so you'll need a separate tool.

Can I use cold email tools for marketing emails?

No. Cold email tools are designed for 1-to-1 sales outreach. For marketing emails (newsletters, promotions), use tools like ConvertKit, Beehiiv, or Mailchimp. Using cold email tools for bulk marketing will get your domains blacklisted.

Do I need a separate tool for finding leads?

Depends on the tool. Instantly (160M+), Lemlist (450M+), Apollo (275M+), and Reply.io (140M+) have built-in lead databases. Smartlead, Saleshandy, and Woodpecker require external lead sources like Apollo, Clay, or LinkedIn Sales Navigator.

How long does it take to see results from cold email?

Expect 2-3 weeks for email warmup, then 1-2 weeks to validate your first campaign. Most senders see meaningful results (meetings booked) within 30-45 days of starting. The biggest factor isn't the tool, it's your offer and targeting.

Is cold email legal?

Yes, in most jurisdictions, B2B cold email is legal under CAN-SPAM (US), PECR (UK), and similar regulations. You must include an unsubscribe mechanism, your physical address, and honor opt-out requests within 10 days. GDPR (EU) requires "legitimate interest" which is generally accepted for relevant B2B outreach.
