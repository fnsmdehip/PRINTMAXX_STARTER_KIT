What BPC-157 Treats

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound-157) is a 15-amino-acid peptide. Animal research is extensive. Human clinical data is limited but growing.

  • Tendons and ligaments: Strongest evidence. Animal studies show accelerated healing. Primary reason athletes seek it.
  • Gut healing: Originally from gastric juice. Protective effects on GI tract in animal models. Used for inflammatory bowel conditions and NSAID damage.
  • Joint recovery: Anecdotal reports of reduced pain, likely from tendon/ligament healing properties.
  • Post-surgical recovery: Some providers prescribe for accelerated healing, though off-label.

Injectable vs. Oral

Injectable (subcutaneous) delivers directly and is more studied. Oral capsules are gaining traction for gut-specific use but have lower systemic bioavailability. For tendons: injectable. For gut: oral may suffice.

Legality in 2026

BPC-157 is not FDA-approved as a drug. It can be legally prescribed by physicians and dispensed by compounding pharmacies. The grey-market "research chemical" route has been increasingly shut down by FDA enforcement.
