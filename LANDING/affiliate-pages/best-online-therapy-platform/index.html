How to Pick the Right Platform — 3 Questions

Don't start with features. Start with your actual situation:

You have insurance that covers mental health telehealth

→ Use Talkspace or Brightside first. They accept major insurance plans. Your cost could drop to $0-30/session vs $65-100/week on BetterHelp.

You need both therapy AND medication

→ Use Brightside (anxiety/depression) or Cerebral (ADHD + anxiety). BetterHelp cannot prescribe anything.

You just need flexible, affordable therapy — no meds

→ BetterHelp has the largest network (35,000+ therapists) and the most flexible scheduling, including text/voice/video options.

You're price-sensitive (under $50/session)

→ Open Path Collective ($30-80/session) or BetterHelp financial aid (income-based discounts available, rarely advertised).

#1 Largest Network — Best for No-Insurance Users

BetterHelp

35,000+ Therapists $65-100/week No Insurance Accepted Text + Video + Phone

BetterHelp is the world's largest online therapy platform with 35,000+ licensed therapists across all 50 states. For anyone paying out-of-pocket, it consistently undercuts in-person rates by 30-50%. The subscription model includes unlimited messaging plus weekly live sessions — the messaging access alone is valuable for many people.

The biggest misconception: BetterHelp doesn't pay therapists less than in-person rates — it pays them less per session but more in volume. Quality is comparable to in-person therapy for most conditions. The platform has been used in 15+ peer-reviewed studies examining digital CBT outcomes.

What It Gets Right

  • 35,000+ licensed therapists (widest selection)
  • Unlimited messaging between sessions
  • Switch therapists anytime, no penalty
  • Financial aid available (ask at checkout)
  • iOS + Android apps, well-reviewed
  • Worksheet library for self-guided work

What It Gets Wrong

  • No insurance — cash-pay only
  • No medication management
  • Therapist matching isn't always right (switch if needed)
  • Not for crisis/suicidal ideation (crisis hotline instead)

Cost: $260-400/month (billed weekly at $65-100). Financial aid reduces this for qualifying incomes.

#2 Best for Insurance — Accepts Most Major Plans

Talkspace

Insurance Accepted $69-109/week cash-pay Therapy + Psychiatry Cigna, Aetna, BCBS

If you have insurance that covers mental health, Talkspace should be your first call — not BetterHelp. Talkspace accepts Cigna, Aetna, some Blue Cross Blue Shield plans, and many others. When insurance applies, your cost typically drops to your in-network specialist copay — often $10-40/session vs $65-100 out-of-pocket on BetterHelp.

Talkspace also offers psychiatry (medication management), which BetterHelp does not. For anxiety or depression that may eventually need medication, having therapy and psychiatry on the same platform is a significant clinical advantage.

What It Gets Right

  • Therapy + psychiatry on same platform
  • Psychiatry available without therapy subscription
  • HIPAA compliant, clinical notes available
  • Same messaging model as BetterHelp

What It Gets Wrong

  • Smaller therapist network than BetterHelp
  • Cash-pay pricing competitive with BetterHelp, not cheaper
  • App less polished than BetterHelp

Unlike platforms that bolt on psychiatry as an afterthought, Brightside's clinical model integrates therapy and prescriptions from day one, with outcomes monitoring built in.

Published clinical data: 86% of Brightside members report symptom improvement within 12 weeks. That's not a testimonial — it's tracked PHQ-9 and GAD-7 scores across their patient population. For people who want to know their therapy is actually working, this level of measurement is rare.

What It Gets Right

#4 Best for ADHD + Anxiety Combo

Cerebral

ADHD + Anxiety $85-325/mo Stimulants Available Insurance Accepted

Cerebral is the strongest choice for ADHD — particularly ADHD that presents with anxiety, which is the most common comorbidity (up to 50% of adults with ADHD also have anxiety). Cerebral can prescribe stimulants (Adderall, Ritalin) as well as non-stimulant options (Strattera, Wellbutrin) — a wider prescribing range than most telehealth platforms.

Note: Cerebral went through a highly-publicized DEA investigation in 2023 related to stimulant prescribing practices. The company has since implemented stricter protocols. The prescribing investigation concluded without criminal charges, and the platform remains available in all 50 states.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is BetterHelp worth it?

For people paying out-of-pocket who need flexible weekly therapy, yes. At $65-100/week it's 30-50% cheaper than in-person therapy. It's not worth it if you have insurance that covers mental health telehealth — use Talkspace or Brightside first and check your coverage.

Does insurance cover online therapy?

Talkspace and Brightside accept many major insurance plans. Call your insurance provider and ask: "Does my plan cover behavioral health telehealth?" Then ask Talkspace/Brightside directly if they're in-network. BetterHelp does not accept insurance under any circumstances.

Can online therapists prescribe medication?

No — therapy-only platforms like BetterHelp cannot prescribe. Platforms with psychiatry services (Talkspace, Brightside, Cerebral) can prescribe non-controlled medications. Controlled substances have restrictions under telehealth rules. For benzodiazepines, an in-person psychiatrist is typically required.

What is the cheapest online therapy option?

Open Path Collective offers therapy at $30-80/session for individuals below certain income thresholds. If you qualify for income-based aid, BetterHelp also offers financial aid that's rarely advertised but available on request at checkout. Insurance-covered Talkspace or Brightside sessions can cost $0-30 with the right plan.
