The Biggest Mistakes Men Make When Choosing Medigap

1. Picking the first plan the insurance agent recommends

Medicare supplement insurance agents work on commission. They earn 12-20% of your first year premium. An agent earning 15% commission on a $180/mo Plan G ($324/year) has incentive to sell you the most expensive plan available, not the right plan for your situation. Always get quotes from at least 3 providers before deciding.

2. Confusing Medicare Supplement with Medicare Advantage

These are completely different products. Medicare Advantage (Part C) replaces Original Medicare with a private plan — typically HMO or PPO with networks and referrals. Medicare Supplement (Medigap) works alongside Original Medicare and lets you use any Medicare-accepting doctor in the country. If you travel, have specialists you're attached to, or live part of the year in another state, Medigap is almost always the better choice.

3. Forgetting about Part D drug coverage

Medigap does NOT cover prescription drugs. You need a separate Medicare Part D plan. If you skip Part D when you first enroll and sign up later, you face a permanent premium penalty (1% per month you went without coverage). Get Part D when you get Medigap.

4. Not understanding the rating method

Three pricing structures exist. Community-rated (same price for everyone) often costs more upfront but stays stable. Attained-age rated (increases as you age) starts cheap at 65 but grows significantly by 75-80. Issue-age rated (based on enrollment age, then only CPI increases) is often the best long-term value if available in your state. Most people only compare today's premium, not 10-year cost.

State-Specific Notes

Three states (Massachusetts, Minnesota, Wisconsin) have standardized Medigap differently from the federal standard. If you live in one of these states, the plan letter system (G, N, etc.) doesn't apply the same way — you have a different standardized benefit structure. Contact your State Health Insurance Assistance Program (SHIP) for state-specific guidance: 1-877-839-2675.

Bottom Line: Which Plan Should You Choose?

  • Plan G — best for anyone who visits doctors regularly, has chronic conditions, or values predictable zero-surprise costs
  • Plan N — best for someone in good health who sees doctors a few times per year and wants to save $50-80/month on premium
  • HD Plan G — best for someone in excellent health who primarily wants catastrophic coverage and can self-insure the $2,870 annual deductible

In all cases: get 3+ quotes before you enroll. Premiums for identical Plan G coverage can vary by 40% across insurers in the same zip code. The plan letter is standardized — the price is not.
