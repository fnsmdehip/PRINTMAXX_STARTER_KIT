Migration

How to move from Klaviyo
without losing a subscriber

Takes 1-2 hours for most solopreneurs. Here's the exact process.

1
Export your Klaviyo list Klaviyo → Lists & Segments → select your main list → Export → CSV. Includes email, name, and any custom properties you've added.
2
Map your segments and tags List your Klaviyo segments. ConvertKit uses tags instead of segments. Create a tag for each segment you want to preserve. (e.g., "bought_course", "free_subscriber", "vip")
3
Import to your new tool ConvertKit: Subscribers → Import → upload CSV → map columns. Assign existing tags during import. Confirm double opt-in status is preserved (important for deliverability).
4
Recreate your key automations Copy your top 2-3 email sequences manually. Start with your welcome sequence and your main nurture. Leave advanced flows for after your first send proves deliverability.
5
Send a test to 500 subscribers first Don't blast your entire list on day 1 from a new sending domain. Warm up over 1-2 weeks. Send to engaged segments first, then expand. Check open rates before full send.
6
Cancel Klaviyo once confirmed working Wait until you've sent 3+ campaigns from the new tool with normal open rates. Then cancel Klaviyo. ConvertKit migration concierge is free for paid plans if you need help.

FAQ

Common questions

Is Klaviyo actually bad?
No - Klaviyo is genuinely great software. The problem is product-market fit. Klaviyo was built for high-volume ecommerce (Shopify stores doing $1M+/year). The features it does best - predictive CLV, Shopify product blocks, abandoned cart flows, SMS - are irrelevant to most solopreneurs. You're paying $45-175/mo for tools you'll never use.
Can I use ConvertKit for ecommerce?
ConvertKit has a commerce feature that lets you sell digital products directly. It works well for ebooks, courses, templates, and digital downloads. For physical products with Shopify, abandoned cart flows, and inventory tracking, Klaviyo is still the better tool. ConvertKit is not a Klaviyo replacement for ecom brands - it's a replacement for solopreneurs and creators.
Will I lose subscribers when migrating?
Not if you follow the migration process correctly. Export your Klaviyo list, import it to your new tool, and warm up your sending domain gradually (500 → 2K → 10K → full list over 1-2 weeks). Deliverability drop is common when switching email providers because your new sending domain has no reputation yet - sending slowly prevents this from tanking your open rates.
What happens to my Klaviyo automations?
You'll need to rebuild them in your new tool. Most solopreneurs only have 2-3 live automations (welcome sequence, nurture sequence, maybe a product upsell). That's a 2-4 hour job to recreate. ConvertKit's visual automation builder is fast to learn. The one-time migration cost is worth the $100-400/mo you'll save ongoing.
Does ConvertKit have a landing page builder?
Yes. ConvertKit includes unlimited landing pages and forms on the free plan. The templates are clean and conversion-optimized. You can use a custom domain (paid plan) or a kit.com subdomain (free). For simple opt-in pages and lead magnets, it's more than enough.
Is Beehiiv better than Substack?
Beehiiv gives you more control over your audience and monetization. Substack takes a 10% cut of paid subscription revenue forever. Beehiiv charges a flat monthly fee and keeps 0% of your subscriber revenue. At $1,000/mo in paid subscriptions, Substack costs you $100/mo extra on top of their plan. Beehiiv wins on economics past $500/mo in subscription revenue.
