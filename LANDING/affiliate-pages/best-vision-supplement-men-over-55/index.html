PreserVision AREDS2 is the most-recommended eye supplement by ophthalmologists in the United States for one reason: it is the exact formulation tested in the AREDS2 clinical trial. The trial enrolled over 4,000 participants with intermediate AMD and found a 25% reduction in the risk of progression to advanced AMD over 5 years.

The key difference versus MacuHealth is the addition of high-dose vitamin C (500mg), vitamin E (400 IU), and zinc (80mg), which were part of the original AREDS formula and retained in AREDS2. This makes it a more complete antioxidant package — but it does not include meso-zeaxanthin.

Note from our review: If your ophthalmologist has diagnosed intermediate AMD, PreserVision AREDS2 is the evidence-based choice — it is the only formula proven in a clinical trial to slow disease progression. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best eye supplement for men over 55?

MacuHealth Plus+ is our top pick based on ingredient completeness — it includes the meso-zeaxanthin component that AREDS2 did not test but that newer research supports. PreserVision AREDS2 is the better choice if your doctor has diagnosed intermediate AMD, as it is the exact formula tested in the largest clinical trial.

Do lutein and zeaxanthin supplements really protect eyesight?

For men with intermediate AMD, yes — AREDS2 showed a 25% reduction in progression to advanced disease. For men with healthy eyes, the prevention evidence is promising but less definitive. The supplements are safe, and the downside of supplementation is minimal.

How much lutein should a man over 55 take daily?

The AREDS2 formula uses 10mg lutein and 2mg zeaxanthin daily. This is the minimum clinically studied dose for macular protection. Products providing less than 10mg lutein are under the threshold used in the research.

Can vision supplements reverse macular degeneration?

No supplement reverses existing AMD damage. The clinical evidence supports slowing progression in men with intermediate AMD. Always consult an ophthalmologist before starting supplementation if you have an existing eye condition.
