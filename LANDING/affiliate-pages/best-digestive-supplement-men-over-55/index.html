Why Your Gut Changes After 55

Most doctors blame diet. The real culprit is biology. Four things happen to the male gut after 55 that don't respond to "eat more fiber":

  1. Enzyme decline. Amylase, lipase, and protease output falls 13% per decade after 40. By 55, you're digesting food 30% less efficiently than you did at 25. Result: gas, bloating, undigested food, and poor nutrient absorption.
  2. Microbiome aging. Bifidobacterium — the dominant beneficial bacteria in healthy adult guts — declines by 80% between ages 30 and 70. Harmful species fill the gap. This is the single biggest driver of age-related constipation and immune decline.
  3. Lower stomach acid (hypochlorhydria). HCl production falls ~25% by 55. Proteins aren't broken down properly, B12 absorption drops, and the gut becomes more vulnerable to small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO).
  4. Slower gut motility. Transit time increases with age. Food spends longer in the colon, fermenting. The result: bloating, constipation, and harder stools.

A single probiotic doesn't fix all four. The evidence-based approach uses a three-product stack: a high-CFU probiotic, a comprehensive enzyme complex, and a soluble fiber source. Here's what actually works.

How to Read Probiotic Labels (Without Being Fooled)

Four things supplement companies hide in plain sight:

  1. "At time of manufacture" vs "At time of expiry." Labels that say CFU "at time of manufacture" are meaningless — live cultures die in storage. You want CFU guaranteed "at time of expiry." VSL#3 and Garden of Life use the honest standard.
  2. Strain specificity. "Lactobacillus acidophilus" tells you nothing. The strain matters — L. acidophilus NCFM has clinical evidence; random acidophilus strains may not. Look for specific strain designations (letter + number codes after the species name).
  3. CFU inflation. "50 billion" sounds impressive. But if those are 50 billion dead cells in a poorly manufactured capsule that can't survive stomach acid, you've bought placebo. Refrigerated capsules or acid-resistant coating matters.
  4. Third-party testing. NSF, USP, or Informed Sport certifications mean an independent lab verified the label claims. Most supplement brands skip this.
