Common Questions

What is the best heart health supplement for men over 60?
CoQ10 (ubiquinol form, 200–300mg/day) has the strongest evidence for heart muscle function and is especially important if you take a statin. Omega-3 at 1–2g EPA+DHA daily reduces triglycerides and inflammation. Magnesium glycinate (300–400mg) supports blood pressure and reduces arterial stiffness. Together these address most of the modifiable cardiovascular risk factors accessible via supplementation.
Should men over 60 on statins take CoQ10?
Yes — statins inhibit the mevalonate pathway, which reduces both cholesterol AND CoQ10 synthesis. Studies show statin users have 16–54% lower plasma CoQ10 than non-users. Most integrative cardiologists recommend 100–200mg/day ubiquinol (the active form) for statin users over 55.
Does omega-3 actually help the heart?
At high doses (4g EPA/day), yes — the REDUCE-IT trial showed a 25% reduction in major cardiovascular events with EPA in high-risk patients. At standard supplemental doses (1–2g EPA+DHA), evidence is robust for triglyceride reduction (25–30%) and inflammation reduction. Key: most fish oil capsules contain 300mg EPA+DHA per pill. You need 3–6 capsules/day for meaningful cardiovascular benefit, or choose a concentrated formula.
Is berberine safe for heart health?
Berberine at 500–1,500mg/day has been shown to reduce LDL by 15–20% and lower triglycerides by 25–35%. However, berberine inhibits CYP3A4 and may interact with many prescription medications. Do not take without discussing with your prescribing physician if you take any prescription medications.
Can heart supplements replace prescription heart medications?
No. These supplements can support cardiovascular health and address specific risk factors, but cannot replace statins if your LDL is severely elevated, cannot replace antihypertensives if your blood pressure is dangerously high, and cannot replace anticoagulants if you have atrial fibrillation. They are adjuncts to — not replacements for — medical care.
What is nattokinase and is it safe?
Nattokinase is a proteolytic enzyme that breaks down fibrin (blood clot protein). Safety concern: it has significant fibrinolytic activity, so it should NOT be taken with warfarin, aspirin, clopidogrel, or any anticoagulant without medical supervision. Most cardiologists advise extreme caution if you are on any blood-thinning medication.
