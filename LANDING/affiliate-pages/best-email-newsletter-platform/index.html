ConvertKit — rebranded as Kit in 2024 — is the automation-focused choice. Its visual automation builder allows conditional branching based on opens, clicks, purchases, tags, and custom fields. If you are running a product launch with a "opened email 1 but not email 3" sequence, ConvertKit handles it without workarounds.

The free plan is generous at 10,000 subscribers — but it removes automations, which defeats the platform's main advantage. The Creator plan at $66/month for 5,000 subscribers is more expensive than Beehiiv's Grow plan ($42/month) for the same subscriber count.

MailerLite is the pragmatic choice for newsletters that need solid automations and a clean editor at the lowest possible price. At $32/month for 5,000 subscribers, it is the cheapest paid option of the five platforms, yet it includes automations, segmentation, and a landing page builder. Its deliverability rates are consistently near the top of independent tests.

What it lacks is the growth ecosystem of Beehiiv (no ad network, no referral marketplace) and the sophisticated automation branching of ConvertKit. But for a newsletter that does not need those specific tools, MailerLite is hard to beat on pure value.

Ghost ranks fourth despite having no affiliate program because it is the correct recommendation for a specific use case: long-form publications with paid memberships where 0% revenue share matters. At $25/month for managed hosting, Ghost takes nothing from your paid subscriber revenue. Substack takes 10%. On $10,000 MRR in paid subscriptions, that difference is $1,000 every month.

Ghost's weakness is its email delivery infrastructure — you will need to integrate a transactional email service (Mailgun, Postmark) for reliable delivery at scale, adding setup complexity. For non-technical newsletter operators, Beehiiv or ConvertKit are easier. For technical founders who want full control, Ghost is unmatched.

Substack is the right starting point for creators with zero audience who want to validate an idea at no cost. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Beehiiv better than ConvertKit in 2026?

For newsletter-first creators focused on growth and monetization, Beehiiv is better — built-in ad network, referral programs, and 0% revenue share on paid subscriptions. ConvertKit is better for complex automation sequences, product launches, and ecommerce integrations.

How much does Beehiiv cost for 5,000 subscribers?

$42/month on the Grow plan. The Scale plan at $99/month covers up to 100,000 subscribers. There is a free tier for up to 2,500 subscribers.

Does Substack take a percentage of paid subscriptions?

Yes — 10% of all paid subscription revenue. On $5,000 MRR, that is $500/month. Migration to Beehiiv or Ghost saves that cut entirely, paying for itself within the first month at that revenue level.

What is the best free email newsletter platform?

Beehiiv offers the best free plan for growth — up to 2,500 subscribers with unlimited sends. MailerLite's free plan is limited to 1,000 subscribers and 12,000 sends/month. Substack is free with no subscriber cap but takes 10% of paid revenue.
