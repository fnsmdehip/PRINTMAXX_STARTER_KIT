Finasteride vs. Minoxidil - What the Evidence Says

Finasteride (oral): Blocks DHT production by ~70%. Addresses the root cause of androgenetic alopecia. Takes 3-6 months for visible results, 12 months for full effect. About 90% of men maintain or regrow hair over 5 years. Side effects (sexual, ~2-5% incidence) are the main concern.

Minoxidil (topical/oral): Increases blood flow to follicles and extends the growth phase. Works regardless of DHT. Visible results in 4-6 months. Topical 5% foam/solution is standard. Oral low-dose minoxidil (2.5-5mg) is gaining traction for better results.

The evidence is clear: combination therapy (finasteride + minoxidil) outperforms either alone. Most men should be on both unless there's a specific reason to avoid one.
