Which Aid Is Right for You?

Post-surgery / maximum stability: Standard walker (no wheels). Daily walking outdoors / trips to store: 4-wheel rollator with brakes. Minor balance support / short distances: Quad cane or single-point cane. Knee or foot injury: Knee walker (scooter). Our top 5 covers all four categories.

Medicare Coverage for Rollators

Medicare Part B may cover a rollator walker as durable medical equipment (DME) if your doctor prescribes it as medically necessary. You typically pay 20% of the approved amount after your annual deductible. Purchase must be from a Medicare-enrolled DME supplier — Amazon purchases generally do not qualify for reimbursement. Ask your physician for a prescription and call 1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227) for a list of local suppliers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best rollator walker for seniors?

The Drive Medical Nitro Euro Style Rollator is our top pick for most seniors — its 10-inch all-terrain wheels handle outdoor use better than any competitor in its price range, and the fold-flat design fits easily in a car trunk.

Should I get a walker or rollator?

A rollator (4-wheel walker with brakes) allows a normal walking gait and is better for active seniors who walk outdoors. A standard walker (no wheels) provides maximum stability but forces a lift-and-step gait better suited to short distances and post-surgery recovery.

How do I choose the right rollator height?

Stand upright with your arms at your sides. Handle height should align with your wrist crease. Most rollators adjust from 32 to 38 inches, covering adults from 5'0" to 6'4". Look for tool-free height adjustment if multiple people will use it.

Are rollator walkers covered by Medicare?

Medicare Part B covers rollators with a doctor's prescription from a Medicare-enrolled DME supplier. You pay 20% after your deductible. Amazon purchases typically do not qualify for reimbursement.
